## Section 1 — The Body-Temple and the Original Battleground (6 min)

- 1 Corinthians 6:20 — "Ye are bought with a price"
  - Ownership transferred at the cross
  - Stewardship, not autonomy — your body is not your private property
- [RQ]: If the body belongs to God by purchase, whose rules govern its use? (1 Cor 6:20)
- **Appetite: where it all began — and where it begins again**
  - Genesis 3:6 — "she saw that the tree was good for food" — appetite was the entry point of sin
  - Matthew 4:3-4 — Christ's FIRST temptation: "command that these stones be made bread"
    - Christ answered: "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God"
    - What Adam failed on appetite, Christ conquered on appetite
  - [WB]: Write — EDEN: appetite → fall. WILDERNESS: appetite → victory (Gen 3:6; Matt 4:4)
  - [→] Appetite is not a minor issue. It was the entry point of sin — and the first place Christ reclaimed victory. What begins here shapes everything else.
- [EGW]: "The body is the only medium through which the mind and the soul are developed for the upbuilding of character." (MH 130)
- [EB]: 1 Corinthians 9:27 — "I keep under my body, and bring it into subjection" — Paul understood bodily discipline as essential

- **You already believe this — follow the principle**
  - Most agree: no smoking, no drinking, no pork. Why?
  - Surface answer: "It's bad for you" — health
  - Deeper: "My body is God's temple" — stewardship
  - Deepest: it clouds the mind, weakens self-control, and hinders the Spirit's sanctifying work
  - [RQ]: If you abstain from alcohol because it impairs judgment, what about anything else that impairs judgment? (Prov 20:1; 1 Pet 5:8)
  - The principle does not change — only the application widens
  - The same governing principle applies in different degrees — caffeine, excess sugar, chronic sleep deprivation, overeating, sedentary living all affect the mind's clarity
  - Not that coffee equals alcohol — but the _principle_ is the same: whatever clouds the mind hinders the Spirit's work
  - [→] We accept the principle where it is comfortable. God asks us to follow it where it is costly.

### Answering Common Objections

[Q] **"Plant-based isn't the best diet — you need meat for strength/performance"**
→ Optimal for _what_? Every diet is optimized for a goal. A warrior's diet builds muscle. An athlete's diet builds speed. God's diet is optimized for _spiritual clarity and resistance to sin_. It may not give you a bodybuilder's physique — but it gives you health of mind and body, and more importantly, strength to resist temptation. Daniel and his friends were "fairer and fatter in flesh" than those who ate the king's meat (Dan 1:15) — and God gave them understanding in all visions (Dan 1:17). What are you optimizing for?

[Q] **"Jesus declared all foods clean (Mark 7:19) / Peter's vision (Acts 10)"**
→ Mark 7: Jesus was addressing Pharisaic hand-washing traditions, not abolishing Leviticus 11. "All foods" meant all _lawful_ foods — no Jew considered pork "food" in the first place. Acts 10: Peter himself interprets the vision — "God hath shewed me that I should not call any _man_ common or unclean" (Acts 10:28). It was about Gentiles entering the church, not bacon entering the diet. Let Scripture interpret Scripture.

[Q] **"Isn't this legalism?"**
→ Is avoiding smoking legalism? Is not drinking legalism? If those are wisdom, then so is this. No one earns salvation by eating vegetables. But salvation comes _through_ sanctification (2 Thess 2:13), and anything that clouds the mind hinders the Spirit's sanctifying work. This is not legalism — it is cooperation with the Spirit. Legalism is trying to earn God's favor. This is removing obstacles to the favor He is already trying to give.

[Q] **"My body, my choice — God cares about the heart, not what I eat"**
→ "Ye are not your own... ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body" (1 Cor 6:19-20). It is not your body. And God does care about what you eat — He cared enough to give dietary instruction in Eden before sin even existed (Gen 1:29). The heart and the body are not separate systems — what you do with the body _shapes_ the heart. A clouded mind cannot discern a pure heart.

- The evidence that God is in us — and that He exists — is a transformed life
  - Not just character transformed, but the whole person: mind, body, habits, health
  - God grants health of mind and body through obedience to His laws — this is not incidental, it is _proof_
- John 13:35 — "By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples" — the world reads our lives before it reads our Bibles
- Deuteronomy 4:6 — "Keep therefore and do them; for this is your wisdom and your understanding in the sight of the nations, which shall hear all these statutes, and say, Surely this great nation is a wise and understanding people"
  - The nations were meant to look at Israel and see something _different_
  - A people whose laws produced visible wisdom, health, clarity, and moral strength
  - [→] This is the evangelistic power of the health message. When God's people live by His design — clear-minded, disciplined, healthy, temperate — the world sees the evidence and says: "Surely this is a wise and understanding people."
- [RQ]: If the world cannot see a difference in how we live, eat, think, and carry ourselves — what are we witnessing? (Deut 4:6)
- A transformed character that willfully neglects the body is incomplete testimony. God wants the _whole_ witness — spirit, soul, and body (1 Thess 5:23)
  - This is not about those who suffer illness beyond their control — it is about those who have light and choose not to walk in it
