## 1 — The Two Wounds (6 min)

**First, how does the Bible define sin?**

- 1 John 3:4 — "Whosoever committeth sin transgresseth also the law: for **sin is the transgression of the law**."
- Rom 3:20 — "By the law is the knowledge of sin."
- Rom 4:15 — "Where no law is, there is no transgression."
- Rom 7:7 — "I had not known sin, but by the law."
- James 2:9-10 — to fail at one point of the law "is convinced of the law as transgressors."
- [→] Hold that definition. Sin is not a vague badness. **Sin is breaking the Law.** Write it down — we come back to it when we find out _what_ the Law really is.

**Now — sin did two things. The whole gospel answers both.**

**Wound 1 — the image was marred.**

- Gen 1:26-27 — "Let us make man in our image, after our likeness... So God created man in his own image."
- Gen 5:3 — "Adam... begat a son in his own likeness, after his image."
- Read side by side: v.1 — image of _God_. v.3 — image of _Adam_. The portrait changed hands.
- Traces remain (Gen 9:6; James 3:9) — but the likeness is distorted.
- 1 Cor 15:49 — "As we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly."

**Wound 2 — we were separated from God.**

- Isa 59:2 — "Your iniquities have separated between you and your God."
- Why can't God just come close?
  - Isa 33:14 — "Who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire?"
  - Ex 33:20 — "No man see me, and live."
  - Heb 12:29 — "Our God is a consuming fire."
- His presence destroys sin. The distance is mercy, not rejection — it keeps the sinner alive while sin remains.

**Two wounds — one word: DEATH.**

- Image marred = the life of God _in_ you, lost.
- Separated from God = the life of God _with_ you, lost.
- Put them together and Scripture has one word for the result:
  - Gen 2:17 — "In the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die."
  - Rom 6:23 — "The wages of sin is death."
  - Rom 5:12 — "By one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men."
  - Eph 2:1 — "Dead in trespasses and sins."
  - Eze 18:4 — "The soul that sinneth, it shall die."
  - James 1:15 — "Sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death."
- [→] The image is how God makes you _alive_ (Gen 2:7 — His breath, His likeness). His presence is what _keeps_ you alive (Acts 17:28 — "in him we live, and move, and have our being"). Lose the image, lose the presence — you are **dead**, walking or not.
- That is why the gospel is described as resurrection (Eph 2:5 — "quickened us together with Christ"; John 5:24 — "passed from death unto life"). Salvation is not improvement of the living. It is **life given to the dead.**
- [→] Cure: **restore the image, and the separation ends — and the dead live.** Fix #1, #2 takes care of itself, and death itself is unmade.
- [WB]: Two wounds = one death — IMAGE LOST + SEPARATED → DEAD. Image restored + presence returned → ALIVE.

## 2 — What Is Eternal Life? (10 min)

- Salvation _is_ eternal life (John 3:16; 1 John 5:11-13). So we have to ask: **what is eternal life?**
- Scripture gives three answers. They look different. They are the same.

**Answer 1 — eternal life is keeping the Law.**

- Luke 10:25 — "Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?"
- Luke 10:27-28 — "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God... and thy neighbour as thyself... _This do, and thou shalt live._"
- Lev 18:5 / Neh 9:29 / Eze 20:11 — "which if a man do, he shall live in them."
- Rom 10:5 — "The man which doeth those things shall live by them."
- Matt 19:17 — "If thou wilt enter into life, keep the commandments."

**Answer 2 — eternal life is knowing God.**

- John 17:3 — "This is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ."
- Jer 9:23-24 — "Let him that glorieth glory in this, that he understandeth and knoweth me."
- Hosea 6:3 — "Then shall we know, if we follow on to know the LORD."
- Hosea 4:6 — "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge."
- Phil 3:8, 10 — "That I may know him, and the power of his resurrection."

**Answer 3 — Christ IS life.**

- John 14:6 — "I am the way, the truth, and **the life**." Not _gives_ life. _Is_ life.
- John 1:4 — "In him was **life**; and the life was the light of men."
- John 11:25 — "I am the resurrection, and the life."
- 1 John 5:11-12 — "God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. **He that hath the Son hath life;** and he that hath not the Son of God hath not life."
- Col 3:4 — "Christ, who is our life."

[→] **Three answers. One reality. Doing the Law = life. Knowing God = life. Christ IS life.** How are they the same?

- The bridge between these three is one word — **image.** So before we go further we have to define it.

## 3 — What Is An Image? (8 min)

- When Scripture says man was made "in the image of God" (Gen 1:27), what does _image_ actually mean? A face? A shape? A glow? Scripture defines it for us — twice.

**Definition 1 — Exodus 33-34. An image is a character.**

- Ex 33:18 — Moses: "I beseech thee, shew me thy glory."
  - Moses asks to _see_. He expects a vision, splendor, fire.
- Ex 33:19 — God: "I will make all my **goodness** pass before thee, and I will proclaim the **name** of the LORD before thee."
  - Moses asked for glory. God answered with goodness and name.
- Ex 34:5-7 — "The LORD descended in the cloud... and proclaimed the name of the LORD. And the LORD passed by before him, and proclaimed, The LORD, The LORD God, **merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth**, keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin."
  - No appearance. No form. **A list of character traits.** That is what God showed Moses when Moses asked to see His glory.
- [WB]: GLORY = NAME = GOODNESS = CHARACTER (on one line)
- 1 Cor 11:7 — man is "the image and glory of God." Image and glory are the same thing.
- [→] **An image is a visible character.** God's image is God's character made visible.

**Definition 2 — John 14 and Hebrews 1. Christ is the living Image.**

- John 14:8 — Philip: "Lord, shew us the Father, and it sufficeth us."
  - Same request as Moses. Show us what God looks like.
- John 14:9 — "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father."
  - Philip had been looking at the image of God for three years and not realized it.
- Heb 1:1-3 — "God... hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son... who being the **brightness of his glory**, and the **express image of his person**."
  - "Express image" (Greek _charaktēr_) — an exact imprint, like a seal pressed into wax.
  - "Brightness of his glory" — the same glory Moses asked to see (Ex 33:18).
- Col 1:15 — "Who is the image of the invisible God."
- 2 Cor 4:4 — "Christ, who is the image of God."
- 2 Cor 4:6 — "The light of the knowledge of the glory of God **in the face of Jesus Christ**."
- John 1:14 — "The Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory...) full of grace and truth."
  - "Full of grace and truth" — **Ex 34:6 walking among us.** The Exodus character became a Person.

**Two definitions. One point.**

- Ex 33-34: God's image = God's character (spoken).
- John 14 / Heb 1: God's image = God's character (embodied in Christ).
- [→] **An image of God is a visible expression of His character.**
- So if we find anything else in Scripture that expresses God's character — that too is an image of God.
- That is exactly what §4 is about to show.

## 4 — The Law Is An Image of God (10 min)

_[The spine. Build it slowly, on the board.]_

**Step 1 — God IS love.**

- 1 John 4:8 — "He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love."
- 1 John 4:16 — "God is love; and he that dwelleth in love dwelleth in God."
- [→] Not "God loves" — "God IS love." Love is His essence. To know Him is to share it. No love → no knowledge of Him.

**Step 2 — the Law IS love.**

- Matt 22:37-40 — "On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets." (Love God, love neighbor.)
- Rom 13:8-10 — "He that loveth another hath fulfilled the law... love is the fulfilling of the law."
- Gal 5:14 — "All the law is fulfilled in one word, even in this; Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself."
- 1 John 5:3 — "This is the love of God, that we keep his commandments."
- 2 John 1:6 — "This is love, that we walk after his commandments."
- John 14:15 — "If ye love me, keep my commandments."

**Step 3 — therefore the Law is an Image of God.**

- [WB]: Build it on the board —
  - GOD = LOVE (1 John 4:8, 16)
  - LAW = LOVE (Matt 22:40; Rom 13:10; 1 John 5:3)
  - **∴ LAW = THE CHARACTER OF GOD**
- Ps 19:7 — "The law of the LORD is perfect." Only _God_ is perfect (Matt 5:48). The Law is perfect because it describes Him.
- Rom 7:12 — "The law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good." Same words used of God.
- Ps 119:142 — "Thy righteousness is an everlasting righteousness, and thy law is the truth."
- [EGW]: "The law of God is a transcript of His character." (_ST_ Jan 14, 1897)

**Step 4 — Christ and the Law are the same portrait, two mediums.**

- §3 showed that an image of God is a visible expression of His character.
- Christ is that image **in flesh** (John 1:14; Heb 1:3).
- The Law is that image **in words** — the Ex 34:6-7 character distilled into ten commandments.
- Isa 42:21 — Christ came to "magnify the law, and make it honourable." He lived it out visibly. The words became a walk.
- Same portrait. Different mediums. You can read the Law and you are reading Christ.

**Step 4.5 — and now sin comes into focus.**

- At the start we said: **sin is the transgression of the Law** (1 John 3:4).
- But the Law is the image of God. So transgressing the Law is not just rule-breaking — it is **defacing the image of God in you.**
- Every sin is a stroke against the portrait. Every act of love is a brush restoring it.
- Rom 3:23 — "All have sinned, and _come short of the glory of God_." The word "glory" is Exodus 33 language — His character, His image. Sin = falling short of the image.
- [→] This is why sin separates (Isa 59:2). The image distorted cannot stand in the presence of the Image undimmed (Isa 33:14).

**Step 5 — the chain closes.**

- [WB]: Draw the chain, top to bottom —
  - Eternal life = knowing God (John 17:3)
  - Knowing God = sharing His love (1 John 4:8)
  - His love = His Law (Rom 13:10; 1 John 5:3)
  - His Law = His character = His image (Rom 7:12; Ex 34:6-7)
  - His image = Christ (Heb 1:3; John 14:9)
  - **∴ Eternal life = bearing the Image of God.**

- [→] Salvation is not a ticket. Salvation is **life**. And life is **having God's image** — the same image Gen 1:27 gave and Gen 5:3 lost.
- Rom 8:29 — "Predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son."
- Col 3:10 — "The new man, which is renewed in knowledge after the image of him that created him."
- 2 Cor 3:18 — "Changed into the same image from glory to glory."
- 1 John 3:2 — "We shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is."

## 5 — Justification — Legs Given (8 min)

- If the chain is true, then the practical question is: **how does the Law get from outside me to inside me?** How does an image on stone become an image on a heart?
- Answer: Jer 31:33 — "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts."
- Eze 36:26-27 — "A new heart also will I give you... I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes."
- Heb 8:10 / Heb 10:16 — the new covenant.
- That rewrite begins at justification. Christ showed the pattern twice.

**Mark 2 — faith, forgiveness, power.**

- Mark 2:5 — "When Jesus saw _their faith_... thy sins be forgiven thee." (Faith → forgiveness.)
- Mark 2:10-11 — "The Son of man hath _power_ on earth to forgive sins... Arise, take up thy bed, and _go thy way into thine house_." (Forgiveness → power → direction.)

**John 8 — the same pattern.**

- John 8:11 — "Lord" (faith) → "Neither do I condemn thee" (forgiveness) → "Go, and sin no more" (power).
- Phil 2:13 — "It is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure." When Christ commands, He enables.

**The anatomy.**

- [WB]: FAITH → FORGIVENESS → POWER
- Rom 5:1 — "Justified by faith, we have peace with God."
- Rom 3:24 — "Justified freely by his grace."
- Eph 1:13-14 — "Sealed with that holy Spirit of promise, which is the earnest of our inheritance."
- Titus 3:5-6 — "Saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost."
- [→] Justification puts you on your feet. It does not yet take you home.

## 6 — Sanctification — The Walk Home (10 min)

- Mark 2:11 — "Arise, and take up thy bed, and go thy way _into thine house_."
- Legs. Direction. **Home.** Sanctification is the walk.

**Justification is a gift. Sanctification is a choice.**

- Justification — what _God_ does: faith, forgiveness, power. **Given to you.**
  - Eph 2:8-9 — "By grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God."
  - Rom 5:15-17 — "the gift of righteousness... the gift by grace."
- Sanctification — what _you_ do with what God has given. **Worked out by you.**
  - Phil 2:12-13 — "**Work out your own salvation** with fear and trembling. For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure."
  - Read those two verses together. v.13: God works _in_. v.12: you work _out_.
- [WB]: GIFT (justification) → WORKED OUT (sanctification)
- Christ gave the paralytic legs. He did not drag him home. **Power does not remove the choice — power creates the freedom to make it.**

**Choosing Christ daily.**

- Every morning you wake up with the same choice Mark 2 put in front of the paralytic: arise and walk, or stay on the mat.
- Luke 9:23 — "If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross **daily**, and follow me."
- 1 Cor 15:31 — "I die daily."
- Josh 24:15 — "**Choose you this day** whom ye will serve."
- Deut 30:19 — "I have set before you life and death... **therefore choose life**."
- 2 Cor 4:16 — "The inward man is renewed day by day."
- [→] Sanctification is not one heroic decision. It is the small, repeated choice to pick Christ today. And tomorrow. And the day after that.

**This is what "abide" means — and the exchange that happens.**

- John 15:4 — "**Abide in me, and I in you.**" Two directions. You abide in Him — and He abides in you.
- **You choose Christ. He moves in.** That is the exchange.
  - Rev 3:20 — "If any man hear my voice, and open the door, **I will come in to him**, and will sup with him, and he with me."
  - Eph 3:17 — "That **Christ may dwell in your hearts** by faith."
  - John 14:23 — "If a man love me, he will keep my words... and **we will come unto him, and make our abode with him**."
  - Gal 2:20 — "**Christ liveth in me**."
  - Col 1:27 — "**Christ in you**, the hope of glory."
- Abiding in practice is not mystical. It looks like: open the Bible. Pray. Surrender the sin you were planning to keep. Say yes to the Spirit's nudge. Say no to the thing pulling you the other direction. Tomorrow — do it again.
- While you are choosing Him, He is _already inside_ doing what you could never do.

**While He abides, He transforms.**

- Phil 1:6 — "He which hath begun a good work in you **will perform it** until the day of Jesus Christ."
- 1 Thess 5:23-24 — "The very God of peace **sanctify you wholly**... Faithful is he that calleth you, who also will do it."
- Heb 13:20-21 — "Make you perfect in every good work to do his will, **working in you** that which is wellpleasing in his sight."
- 2 Cor 3:18 — "Changed into the same image, **from glory to glory**."
- [→] You don't sanctify yourself. You stay on the vine. _He_ does the growing. _He_ does the transforming.

**The law of liberty.**

- James 1:25 — "The perfect law of liberty."
- James 2:12 — "So speak ye, and so do, as they that shall be judged by the law of liberty."
- Rom 8:2 — "The law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus hath made me free from the law of sin and death."
- John 8:32, 36 — "The truth shall make you free... If the Son therefore shall make you free, ye shall be free indeed."
- The same Law that condemned the powerless _liberates_ the empowered. It is a law of liberty because the ability to keep it has been restored.

**How the image is restored — beholding.**

- 2 Cor 3:18 — "Beholding... changed into the same image from glory to glory."
- 2 Cor 4:6 — "The light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ."
- Heb 12:2 — "Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith."
- Col 3:1-2 — "Set your affection on things above."
- Ps 17:15 — "I shall be satisfied, when I awake, with thy likeness."
- You become what you gaze at. Rev 14:9 — worship the beast, bear his image. 2 Cor 3:18 — behold Christ, bear His.

**But the most important thing is that you GROW — more knowledge of Christ.**

- Recall §2: knowing God = eternal life (John 17:3). So if life is knowing Him, **more knowing = more life.**
- A Christian who is not growing is not a Christian — because sanctification, by definition, grows.
- 2 Pet 3:18 — "But **grow in grace, and in the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ**."
- 2 Pet 1:5-8 — "Add to your faith... for if these things be in you, and **abound**, they make you that ye shall neither be barren nor unfruitful **in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ**."
- Eph 4:13 — "Till we all come... unto a **perfect man**, unto the measure of the stature of the fulness of Christ."
- Eph 4:15 — "May **grow up into him in all things**, which is the head, even Christ."
- Col 1:10 — "Fruitful in every good work, and **increasing in the knowledge of God**."
- Col 2:19 — "Holding the Head... **increaseth with the increase of God**."
- Phil 3:8, 10 — "That I may **know him**" — Paul, at the end of his life, still reaching for more.
- Hosea 6:3 — "Then shall we know, if we **follow on** to know the LORD."
- [→] Every day, more of Him. More Bible. More time in His presence. More of His character you have actually seen and absorbed. This is not optional enrichment. **This is the life itself growing.**

**The purpose of it all — fruit.**

- Why does a plant exist? To **bear fruit.** The vine, the branch, the sap, the soil — all of it serves one end.
- John 15:8 — "Herein is my Father glorified, that **ye bear much fruit**; so shall ye be my disciples."
- John 15:16 — "I have chosen you, and ordained you, that **ye should go and bring forth fruit**, and that your fruit should remain."
- Matt 7:16-20 — "By their fruits ye shall know them... every good tree bringeth forth good fruit."
- Gal 5:22-23 — the fruit of the Spirit: love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance — **the character of God in a human life.**
- Rom 7:4 — "That we should **bring forth fruit unto God**."
- Phil 1:11 — "Filled with the fruits of righteousness, which are by Jesus Christ, unto the glory and praise of God."
- Col 1:6 — "the gospel... **bringeth forth fruit**... since the day ye heard of it."
- [→] Sanctification is not a private project. It has a purpose. The image restored in you becomes a **visible portrait** others can see — fruit hanging on the branch, the character of God on display.
- [EGW]: "The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace... and this fruit can never perish." (COL 68)

**The Law written within.**

- Jer 31:33 — "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts."
- Eze 11:19-20 — "I will give them one heart... that they may walk in my statutes."
- Ps 40:8 — "Thy law is within my heart."
- Ps 37:31 — "The law of his God is in his heart; none of his steps shall slide."
- [→] This is sanctification: day by day, the Spirit writes the Law — the Image — the character — into your life.

**Why it's the heart of salvation.**

- 2 Thess 2:13 — "Chosen you to salvation _through sanctification of the Spirit_."
- Heb 12:14 — "Follow... holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord."
- 1 Pet 1:15-16 — "Be ye holy; for I am holy."
- Matt 1:21 — "He shall save his people _from_ their sins." Not in them — from them.

## 7 — Appeal — Will You Bear the Image? (6 min)

- The chain, one more time:
  - Sin marred the image (Gen 5:3) and made separation necessary (Isa 59:2).
  - Salvation = eternal life (John 3:16; 1 John 5:11).
  - Eternal life = knowing God (John 17:3) = doing the Law (Luke 10:28) = **Christ IS life** (John 14:6; 1 John 5:12).
  - God is love (1 John 4:8). Law is love (Rom 13:10). Christ is the image (Heb 1:3).
  - **∴ Eternal life = bearing the Image of God.**
  - Justification gives you legs (Mark 2:11). Sanctification is the walk home (James 1:25).
  - The medium of restoration: the Law written on the heart (Jer 31:33).

- [RQ]: If the Law is the image of God, how have you been treating it? As a burden? A list? Or as the portrait of the One you love?
- [RQ]: If eternal life is bearing His image, how much of your life has been spent beholding Him — and how much has been spent beholding something else?

- The promises stand:
  - 2 Cor 3:18 — "Changed into the same image from glory to glory."
  - Rom 8:29 — "Conformed to the image of his Son."
  - 1 John 3:2 — "We shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is."

- The call is simple.
  - **Rise.** The forgiveness is real (Mark 2:5; John 8:11; 1 John 1:9).
  - **Take up thy bed.** The power is given (Mark 2:11; Phil 2:13; Eph 3:20).
  - **Go home.** The Way is Christ (John 14:6). The walk is daily (Luke 9:23). The destination is the image fully restored (1 John 3:2).

- "_Create in me a clean heart, O God._" (Ps 51:10) — the same word for "create" as Genesis 1. Ask Him. He does it.

- "_And the very God of peace sanctify you wholly._" (1 Thess 5:23) — He who calls you will do it (1 Thess 5:24).

- Behold Him. Bear His image. **That is what salvation really is.**
