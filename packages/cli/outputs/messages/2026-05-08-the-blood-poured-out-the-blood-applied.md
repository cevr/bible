## Introduction — The Sanctuary Story: Blood Applied, Blood Poured Out (5 min)

- Picture the courtyard. The sinner brings the lamb. He lays his hand on its
  head — confessing — and the knife falls (Lev 4:27–29).
- The priest catches the blood in a basin. Every drop is precious; not one is
  lost (Lev 4:30, 34).
- [WB]: Draw a basin at center. Above it: ONE LIFE POURED OUT. Around it,
  three arrows — ALTAR / VEIL / BASE.
- Then watch what the priest does with that single basin of blood:
  - **A little** is put on the horns of the altar of burnt offering — atonement
    accepted, the sinner forgiven (Lev 4:30)
  - **A little** is sprinkled before the veil — carried toward the presence of
    God in the Most Holy Place (Lev 4:6, 17)
  - **The rest** — the bulk of it — is **poured out at the base of the altar**
    (Lev 4:7, 18, 30)
- Stop on that last line. Most of the blood never touches the worshipper. Most
  of it never reaches the veil. Most of it runs out into the ground at the
  altar's base — given, but not claimed.
- [WB]: Underline POURED OUT AT THE BASE — and write beside it: _given for all,
  received by few._
- This is the shadow. The Lamb of God would do the same: His whole life caught
  in one basin, a measure applied to those who receive, the rest poured out
  for a world that would not.
- [RQ]: If the priest in the type poured out more than was applied, what does
  Calvary tell us about the love that was willing to be **mostly refused**?
  (Lev 4:7; John 1:11; Isa 53:12)
- John 15:13 — "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his
  life for his friends" — the whole basin, not a measured drop
- [WB]: Above the basin, write the equation — LOVE = WHAT IT IS WILLING TO
  LOSE (John 15:13)
- The cross is not a tragedy God endured; it is the **basin** God chose to
  pour out — knowing in advance how much would soak unclaimed into the ground.

## Section 1 — The Measure of What He Gave (7 min)

- Isaiah 53:11–12 — "He hath poured out his soul unto death"
  - Not a body only — His **soul**, His inner life, His Sonship
  - [WB]: Three columns — BODY / SOUL / GLORY — and underline that all three
    were given (Isa 53:11–12; Phil 2:6–8)
- Philippians 2:6–8 — He emptied Himself, becoming obedient unto death
  - The eternal Son chose self-emptying as His native posture
- 2 Corinthians 8:9 — Though He was rich, for our sakes He became poor
  - [RQ]: What did Christ surrender that He could never naturally have lost?
    (Phil 2:6–8)
- [EGW]: "He could have remained at the Father's side. He could have retained
  the glory of heaven, and the homage of the angels. But He chose to give back
  the scepter into the Father's hands... that He might bring many sons unto
  glory." (DA 22.2)
- [EB]: Hebrews 12:2 — "For the joy that was set before him **endured the
  cross**" — He counted the cost and paid it with joy
- This is not the careful giving of a man who counts his coins; it is the
  reckless generosity of perfect love

## Section 2 — He Knew, and He Gave Anyway (7 min) [*]

- Matthew 7:13–14 — "Few there be that find it"
  - Christ did not die under an illusion of mass acceptance
  - [WB]: Draw two roads — wide MANY / narrow FEW — and write above them: "He
    saw both before He came" (Matt 7:13–14)
- John 1:11–12 — "He came unto his own, and his own received him not"
  - Omniscient love still walked into a world that would not have Him
  - [RQ]: Knowing in advance how few would receive Him, what kind of love still
    chose Calvary? (John 1:11; Rom 5:8)
- Romans 5:8 — God commendeth His love toward us, in that **while we were yet
  sinners** Christ died
  - The death came **before** any response — unconditional in offer, even when
    most would never claim it
- Luke 19:41–42 — He wept over Jerusalem because they would not be gathered
  - The same blood that saves the few **grieves** over the many who refuse
- [EGW]: "Christ saw the suffering and woe that would result from the rejection
  of His mercy. He knew the doom of those who would refuse Him, yet He shrank
  not from the sacrifice. He chose to give His life for a world that would
  largely scorn it." (DA 753.2 — paraphrased; verify against context of
  Gethsemane)
- [Aside]: A merchant who knows most of his cargo will be thrown into the sea
  still loads the ship for the sake of the few crates that will reach harbor.
  Christ's love loaded the **whole** cargo of heaven for those who would
  receive even one crate.
- [EB]: 1 Timothy 2:4 — God "will have all men to be saved" — the offer is to
  all; the heartbreak is real

## Section 3 — Becoming Like the Giver (6 min)

- 1 John 3:16 — "Hereby perceive we the love of God... and we ought also to
  lay down our lives for the brethren"
  - The cross is not only a gift to receive — it is a **character to put on**
  - [WB]: Equation — RECEIVE His love → REFLECT His love (1 John 4:19)
- Ephesians 5:1–2 — "Be ye therefore followers of God... and walk in love, as
  Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us"
  - The Christian's measure of love is the same: **what am I willing to pour
    out?**
- Matthew 10:8 — "Freely ye have received, freely give"
  - [RQ]: If Christ gave knowing most would refuse, can I give only when I am
    sure of being received? (Matt 5:44–48)
- Matthew 5:44–48 — Love your enemies, do good to them that hate you — be
  perfect as your Father is perfect
  - The character of the Giver is reproduced in those who truly receive Him
- [EGW]: "The love of Christ in the heart will make us willing to be hewn and
  squared by the master Builder. ... It will make us willing to deny self,
  to lift the cross, to follow our Saviour, even to suffering and self-sacrifice."
  (5T 105.1)
- The poured-out love of Calvary is meant to flow **through** the receiver, not
  stop in him

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — Receive the Love That Was Poured Out for You (5 min)

- John 1:12 — "But as many as received him, to them gave he power to become
  the sons of God"
  - The whole sacrifice waits for one act: **receiving**
  - [WB]: Draw an open hand under a tilted vessel — empty hands are the only
    vessels mercy fills (John 1:12)
- Revelation 3:20 — "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock"
  - The Giver does not force what He paid everything to offer
- John 15:13 — Greater love hath no man than this
  - This love has already been spent — the only question is whether it has been
    **received**
- [RQ]: Christ poured out His soul knowing few would drink — will you be among
  the few who actually receive what He gave? (Isa 53:11; John 1:12)
- [EGW]: "It was the love manifested upon the cross that draws souls to
  Christ. ... Such love is without a parallel." (SC 13.1; DA 480.3)

### Call to Action — Receive Him, Then Pour Out Like Him

- Receive Christ today — open the heart fully to the love already poured out
  (John 1:12; Rev 3:20)
- Stop counting the cost of discipleship; the Giver did not count His
  (Phil 2:6–8; Luke 9:23)
- Surrender the reserved places — half-received love is unreceived love
  (1 John 3:16)
- Become a giver in the same pattern — pour yourself out for the few who will
  receive, and for the many who will not (Matt 5:44–48; Matt 10:8)
- Stand in the company of the few who **find** the narrow way, marked by the
  love of Christ flowing through them to a dying world (Matt 7:14; Rev 14:12)
