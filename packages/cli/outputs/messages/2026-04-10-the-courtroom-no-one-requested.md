## 1 — The Accusation in Heaven (5 min)

- Job 1:1 — A man named Job. "Perfect and upright, one that feared God and eschewed evil."
  - Hebrew _tam_ (תָּם) = complete, whole, having integrity
  - God looked at this man and said: _this is what My grace produces._
  - Not a sinner under judgment. A faithful man. God made him complete.
- Job 1:6–8 — A council convenes in heaven. God raises Job's name — unprompted.
  - [WB]: Draw courtroom — GOD (Judge) / SATAN (Accuser) / JOB (Exhibit)
- Job 1:9 — "Doth Job fear God for nought?"
  - Satan doesn't deny Job's obedience. He attacks the _reason_ behind it.
  - "You've built a hedge around him. Of course he worships You. He's _paid_ to."
  - Behind the motive question is a deeper claim: **God cannot make anyone truly complete. Loyalty is always purchased — never genuine.**
- Job 1:12; 2:6 — God permits the trial. Sets limits. Says nothing to Job.
- [EGW]: "Unselfishness, the principle of God's kingdom, is the principle that Satan hates; its very existence he denies... To disprove Satan's claim is the work of Christ and of all who bear His name." (Ed 154)
- Job never knows any of this. He never requested this trial.
- The book's question: **Can God make a person complete — and will that completeness hold under fire?**

---

## 2 — Name the Hedge (5 min)

- Satan's accusation didn't retire with Job. It is being leveled against every person in this room.
- [RQ]: What hedge would Satan say is buying _your_ loyalty? (Job 1:10)
  - Your health? Your marriage? Your children? Your reputation? Answered prayer? Church approval?
  - What is the one thing that — if God removed it — would make you question whether He is worth following?
- That thing is your hedge. And Satan knows it.
- Be honest: Is your faith transactional? "Lord, I will obey _if_..."
  - If You keep my family safe. If You bless my ministry. If You answer this prayer.
  - Job 1:9 stripped to its core: Do you serve God for what you GET — or for who He IS?
- God already knows your heart. He doesn't need the test. But the universe does. Angels cannot read the heart — only God can.
  - [EGW]: "We are made a theater unto the world, both to angels, and to men." (1 Cor 4:9, margin; Ed 154)
  - The trial is what reveals the real from the fake. Not for God — for everyone else.

## 3 — Pressure Exposes the Heart (10 min)

_[Walk through the trial. The congregation should see their own life in this — the voices telling them to quit, the silence, the mess.]_

### The Closest Voice Says Quit

- Job 2:9 — "Dost thou still retain thine integrity? curse God, and die."
  - The person closest to Job — his partner — counsels apostasy
  - She is making **the same claim as Satan** from a different angle
    - Satan says: God can't make anyone truly complete — it's always purchased
    - The wife says: even if He could, it's not worth the cost — just give up
  - You know this voice. It comes from the people nearest to you. Sometimes from inside the church.
    - "Just rest in grace. Stop striving. Nobody can really overcome."
    - It sounds loving. It sounds humble. But it is counseling you to let go of the very thing God is trying to complete.
- [RQ]: Who in your life is saying "just let go"? (Job 2:9)
- Job 2:10 — "Shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil?"
  - Job rejects the counsel. He will not trade his integrity for relief.

### The Religious Voice Says You've Already Failed

- Job's three friends assume suffering = hidden guilt
  - Eliphaz (Job 4:7) — "Who ever perished, being innocent?"
  - Bildad (Job 8:6) — "If thou wert pure..."
  - Zophar (Job 11:6) — "God exacteth less than thine iniquity deserveth"
- [EGW]: "His friends, seeing in adversity but the retribution of sin, pressed on his bruised and burdened spirit their accusations of wrongdoing." (Ed 155)
- These are not outsiders. These are fellow believers who become instruments of accusation.
- And they are making **the same claim as Satan** — from yet another angle
  - Satan says: God can't make anyone complete — it's always purchased
  - The wife says: it's not worth the cost — give up
  - The friends say: He clearly hasn't succeeded — you've already failed
  - [WB]: Three voices, one accusation — GOD CANNOT MAKE YOU COMPLETE
- You hear this too — the suggestion that real faithfulness under pressure is impossible, that claiming otherwise is presumption. But if that is true, God has no case. His grace doesn't finish what it starts.

### The Silence

- Job 3–37: Friends speak. Job protests. God says nothing.
- 37 chapters of heaven's silence. No explanation. No comfort. No voice from the whirlwind — not yet.
- You know this silence. The prayer that heaven doesn't answer. The season where God feels distant. The question you've carried for years with no resolution.
  - That silence is not punishment. It may be your trial.

### He Complained, But He Didn't Leave

- Job does not curse God. Satan's central prediction fails.
- But Job does not suffer cleanly. He is loyal — and he is a mess:
  - Job 3 — curses the day of his birth
  - Job 10:1–3 — "Is it good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress?"
  - Job 30:20–21 — "I cry unto thee, and thou dost not hear me... thou art become cruel to me"
- And yet — through all of it — he never leaves:
  - Job 13:15 — "Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him"
  - Job 27:5 — "Till I die I will not remove mine integrity from me"
  - [WB]: HE COMPLAINED, BUT HE DIDN'T LEAVE
- The distinction matters: Job murmurs _to_ God, not _away from_ God.

### God Speaks — Rebuke, Not Rejection

- God breaks the silence — with correction, not comfort.
  - Job 38:2 — "Who is this that darkeneth counsel by words without knowledge?"
  - Job 40:8 — "Wilt thou condemn me, that thou mayest be righteous?"
- God rebukes Job's _theology_ — but does not disqualify his _loyalty_
  - God's verdict: "ye have not spoken of me the thing that is right, _as my servant Job hath_" (Job 42:7)
- Job 42:5 — "I have heard of thee by the hearing of the ear: but now mine eye seeth thee"
  - Theological knowledge → personal encounter.
- Job 42:7–10 — Friends rebuked. Job intercedes for his accusers. The Lord restores him. Vindication first, blessing second.
- [EGW]: "By his patient endurance he vindicated his own character, and thus the character of Him whose representative he was." (Ed 156)

---

## 4 — The Redeemer Stands (5 min)

- In the deepest pit of his suffering — before God spoke, before vindication — Job reaches past his confusion and seizes something:
  - Job 19:25–27 — "For I know that my redeemer liveth, and that he shall stand at the latter day upon the earth: and though after my skin worms destroy this body, yet in my flesh shall I see God: whom I shall see for myself, and mine eyes shall behold, and not another."
- Job does not answer Satan alone. In his darkest hour, he points to _someone else_ — a living Redeemer who will stand and vindicate.
  - Job's loyalty is real. But Job's loyalty is not the final answer. **Christ is.**
- This is Job's fundamental belief about God: **He can finish what He started. He can make me complete. And I will see Him with my own eyes.**
  - Satan, the wife, and the friends all say otherwise. Job stakes everything on the Redeemer.
- Job is not a perfect picture of the finished work. He is the raw prototype — wounded, confused, loyal, still needing correction. He murmured under pressure. But he had less light than we do. Each succeeding generation bears a higher standard — not because God is harder on later people, but because more truth has been revealed. Job murmured with the light he had. We cannot afford to, because we have the cross, the sanctuary, the three angels' messages.
- But the direction of his heart is right. And his belief about God is right: the Redeemer lives, and He finishes what He starts.

---

## 5 — Christ Answered the Accuser (4 min)

- That Redeemer came. And He settled the question.
  - He kept the law from the heart — perfectly, under maximum pressure
  - He served God "for nought" — no hedge, no comfort, no explanation
  - Gethsemane: "Not my will, but thine be done" (Luke 22:42). No guarantee He would see through the tomb.
  - The cross: accused without cause, forsaken by friends, silent heaven — and He did not let go.
- Christ proved that God _can_ make a human being complete. It spelled the end for Satan.
  - [EB]: John 14:30 — "The prince of this world cometh, and hath nothing in me."
- But Satan does not surrender. He pivots.
  - "Fine — Christ could do it. But He was God. No _mere human_ can."
  - This is the post-cross version of Job 1:9. And every voice that tells you completeness is impossible — every version of the wife's counsel and the friends' theology — is echoing Satan's pivot.
- There is another man in Scripture who faced the same pattern as Job — crisis he didn't initiate, alone, stripped of support, wrestling through the night, vindicated after the ordeal. His name was Jacob. (Genesis 32:24–28)
  - [EGW]: "Jacob's night of anguish, when he wrestled in prayer for deliverance from the hand of Esau, represents the experience of God's people in the time of trouble." (GC 618)
- Each succeeding generation bears a higher standard — not because God is harder, but because more truth has been revealed.
  - **Job**: innocent, confused, murmured — but he had less light. He didn't leave.
  - **Jacob**: guilty but repentant, clinging to God's promises through an agonizing night. He had more light — Bethel, Mahanaim, the covenant. He wrestled not in self-reliance but in repentance, pleading the merits of his Redeemer: "I will not let Thee go, except Thou bless me." And he prevailed — through self-surrender and confiding faith.
  - **The final generation**: sins already confessed and blotted out, the fullness of light received — the cross, the sanctuary, the three angels' messages. No concealed wrongs. Standing on settled trust.
- And there will be that final generation — ordinary people who have received the fullness of light, made complete by the Redeemer, standing under pressure with no hedge and no mediator — proving that what Christ did in His own life, He can reproduce in theirs.
  - Daniel 7:22 — "Judgment was given to the saints." Not against — _for_. Vindication.
  - Revelation 14:5 — "Without fault before the throne of God."
- The question that separates you is what you believe about God: **Can He make you complete?**

## 6 — Will You Let Him Finish? (8 min)

### Where Are You?

- Every person in this room is somewhere in this story:
  - **Some are in Job 1** — the hedge is still up. You've never asked: _Would I still trust Him if it disappeared?_
  - **Some are hearing the wife's voice** — someone close to you is telling you to give up. Or — and be honest — you _are_ that voice for someone else.
  - **Some are in Job 3–37** — complaining, questioning, saying things heaven will correct. That does not mean Satan has won. Job murmured but did not leave. And God did not leave him there — He rebuked him and brought him higher.
  - **Some are in Job 13:15** — holding on. "Though he slay me." Your faithfulness is answering Satan right now. Don't let go.
- [RQ]: Where are you? (Pause.)

### The Mediator Still Stands

- [EGW]: "The 'time of trouble, such as never was,' is soon to open upon us; and we shall need an experience which we do not now possess... It is in this life that we are to separate sin from us." (GC 623)
- The character you refuse to surrender now will not be manufactured when the night comes.
- Hebrews 9:24 — Christ appears in God's presence for us. Now. The Mediator still stands.
- But He will not stand forever. Revelation 22:11 — "He that is filthy, let him be filthy still... he that is holy, let him be holy still."
- Now — _now_ — while Christ still intercedes — is the only time you are guaranteed.

### The Decision

- This is the question of your life. Not "Do you believe in God?" Satan believes. The question is:

- **Do you believe God can make you complete — and will you let Him?**
  - Satan says He can't. The wife says it's not worth it. The friends say it's already failed.
  - Will you agree with them — or will you believe what Job believed?
  - "I know that my redeemer liveth." He lives. He finishes what He starts.

- **Will you serve God for nothing?**
  - Not for the hedge. Not for comfort. Not for answered prayer. Not even for explanation.
  - For _nothing_ — except that He is God, He is worthy, and He can make you whole.

- If your answer is yes, then three things must happen _today_:
  1. **Name the hedge.** What makes your faith conditional? Name it before God.

  2. **Confess the bargain.** "Lord, I have served You for the hedge. Forgive me. I choose You for _nothing_."

  3. **Surrender known sin.** The sin you have planned to keep — give it to Christ _now_, while He can blot it out.

- You may be more like Job than like Revelation 14 today. That is not disqualifying. But it is not the destination. Job reached through his confusion and grabbed hold of a Redeemer: "I know that my redeemer liveth... in my flesh shall I see God" (Job 19:25–26). So can you.

- He wants to make you _tam_ — whole, complete, settled. Not in your own strength. In His.

- **If you want this settled today** — come forward now. The courtroom is open. Your name is in the record. The Accuser is watching. And the Redeemer lives.

- Job 13:15 — "Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him."

- Let that be your answer. Let it be the last word Satan ever hears about you.
