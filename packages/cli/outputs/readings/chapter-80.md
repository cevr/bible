# Reading 80: The Gospel in the Old Testament

Two men walking home from Jerusalem. Crushed. The One they thought was the Messiah — dead. Buried. It's over.

Then a stranger falls in step beside them.

- "What manner of communications are these that ye have one to another, as ye walk, and are sad?" (Luke 24:17)
- They pour it out: "We trusted that it had been He which should have redeemed Israel" (Luke 24:21)
- The stranger's response: "O fools, and slow of heart to believe all that the prophets have spoken: ought not Christ to have suffered these things, and to enter into His glory?" (Luke 24:25-26)

And then — the Bible study that changed everything:

- "And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, He expounded unto them in all the Scriptures the things concerning Himself" (Luke 24:27)

**Beginning at Moses.** Not skipping to the "relevant" parts. Not starting at Isaiah 53. Starting at the Pentateuch — because every tenet of the faith lives there. The gospel is not a New Testament invention. It was preached in Eden.

Their testimony afterward: "Did not our heart burn within us, while He talked with us by the way, and while He opened to us the Scriptures?" (Luke 24:32)

[→] Let's walk that same road. Book by book through the Law, prophet by prophet — and see what made their hearts burn.

---

## One Problem, One Solution

- **One man** broke it; **one Man** fixes it
  - "as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin: and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned" (Rom. 5:12)
  - "by the righteousness of one the free gift came upon all men unto justification of life" (Rom. 5:18)
- **No alternative name**
  - "Neither is there salvation in any other; for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved" (Acts 4:12)
- **Always grace, always faith** — God's method from the beginning
  - "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God" (Eph. 2:8)
  - "Therefore it is of faith, that it might be by grace; to the end the promise might be sure to all the seed" (Rom. 4:16)

But what _is_ salvation? What did Adam lose? What does Christ restore?

- **Image** — "Let Us make man in Our image, after Our likeness" (Gen. 1:26)
- **Image = character** — God's "name" _is_ His character:
  - "I beseech Thee, shew me Thy glory" (Ex. 33:18) → God's answer: "I will make all My goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the LORD before thee" (Ex. 33:19)
  - The proclamation: "The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth" (Ex. 34:6)
  - [→] God's glory = God's name = God's character = **love**
- **Christ is that image made visible**
  - "the brightness of His glory, and the express image of His person" (Heb. 1:3)
  - "He that hath seen Me hath seen the Father" (John 14:9)
  - [→] to see Christ is to see the character of God — the very image man was made to bear
- **Law = love** — a lawyer asked Christ the way to eternal life:
  - "What is written in the law?" (Luke 10:26) → "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart... and thy neighbour as thyself" (Luke 10:27)
  - Christ's verdict: "This do, and thou shalt live" (Luke 10:28)
  - law = love = life — they are the same thing
- **God is love** — "He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love" (1 John 4:8)
- **Knowing God = eternal life** — "And this is life eternal, that they might know Thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom Thou hast sent" (John 17:3)

**Salvation restores what sin broke.**

[→] Follow the chain: **Image = Character = Love = Law = Knowing God = Life**. Sin shattered the image. The gospel restores it. _That_ is what salvation is — not just pardon, but a **new heart**. And every page of the Old Testament is about this restoration.

[→] Let's look — starting where Christ started. Moses.

---

## Christ in Genesis

- **Genesis 1:26-27** — "Let _Us_ make man in _Our_ image"
  - "all things were made by Him; and without Him was not any thing made that was made" (John 1:3)
  - Christ present at creation — Author of life itself
  - man made in the **image of God** — bearing His character, His love, His law written in the heart
  - [→] this is what was lost in the fall — and what the entire gospel exists to restore
- **Genesis 3:15** — the protoevangelium, first gospel promise
  - "I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel"
  - seed of the woman = Christ (Gal. 3:16 — "And to thy seed, which is Christ")
  - bruised heel = the cross; bruised head = sin's final destruction
  - [→] Adam and Eve received the gospel the same day they fell
- **Genesis 4:4** — Abel's lamb
  - "By faith Abel offered unto God a more excellent sacrifice than Cain" (Heb. 11:4)
  - faith in the Lamb promised in 3:15
- **Genesis 15:6** — Abraham believed
  - "he believed in the LORD; and He counted it to him for righteousness"
  - same imputed righteousness Paul preaches (Rom. 4:3)
- **Genesis 22** — Isaac on Moriah
  - "God will provide Himself a lamb" (Gen. 22:8) — father offering his only son on a mountain
  - "the Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the heathen through faith, preached before the gospel unto Abraham" (Gal. 3:8)
  - "Your father Abraham rejoiced to see My day: and he saw it, and was glad" (John 8:56)

[→] Genesis gives the pattern: promise, sacrifice, faith, righteousness — the image restored by trusting the provided Lamb.

Supporting notes:

- **Genesis 28:12** — Jacob's ladder: "a ladder set up on the earth, and the top of it reached to heaven: and behold the angels of God ascending and descending on it." Christ claimed it: "ye shall see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of man" (John 1:51). _He_ is the connection between heaven and earth.
- **Genesis 49:10** — Shiloh prophecy: "The sceptre shall not depart from Judah... until Shiloh come; and unto Him shall the gathering of the people be." Shiloh = the peace-giver, the Messiah.
- **Believers = Abraham's children** — "Know ye therefore that they which are of faith, the same are the children of Abraham" (Gal. 3:7). Not bloodline — faith-line.
- **Gen 15:6** — first occurrence of "believed" (Hebrew "aman" = to trust, rely on) connected with righteousness. Foundational gospel text.

## Christ in Exodus

- **Exodus 3:14** — "I AM THAT I AM"
  - the self-existent One who appeared to Moses in the bush
  - Christ claimed the same name: "Before Abraham was, I am" (John 8:58)
  - [→] the God of the burning bush is the God of the Gospels
- **Exodus 33-34** — God reveals His name = His character
  - Moses asks: "shew me Thy glory" (Ex. 33:18)
  - God answers: "I will proclaim the name of the LORD" (Ex. 33:19)
  - the proclamation: "merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth" (Ex. 34:6)
  - [→] God's glory = His name = His character = **love**. This is the image man was made in — and the image the gospel restores
- **Exodus 12** — the Passover lamb
  - "a lamb without blemish" (Ex. 12:5) — Christ "a lamb without blemish and without spot" (1 Pet. 1:19)
  - blood on the doorpost = saved from judgment: "when I see the blood, I will pass over you" (Ex. 12:13)
  - "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" (1 Cor. 5:7)
  - no bone broken (Ex. 12:46) — fulfilled at the cross (John 19:36)
- **Exodus 16-17** — manna and water from the rock
  - "I am the living bread which came down from heaven" (John 6:51)
  - "they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that Rock was Christ" (1 Cor. 10:4)
  - Israel ate Christ, drank Christ, was sheltered by Christ — they lived on Him daily without knowing His name

Supporting notes:

- **Moses' faith in Christ** — "By faith Moses... refused to be called the son of Pharaoh's daughter; choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God... esteeming the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures in Egypt" (Heb. 11:24-26). Moses knew _whose_ reproach he bore — 1,400 years before the incarnation.
- **Manna details** — "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to Me shall never hunger" (John 6:35). Manna given daily, gathered morning by morning — Christ received fresh each day, not stored from yesterday's faith.
- **Rock smitten once** — Christ crucified once (Heb. 9:28). When Moses struck the rock a second time (Num. 20:11), he broke the type — and cost himself Canaan.

## Christ in Leviticus — the Sanctuary

- **Ex. 25:8** — "Let them make Me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them"
  - _dwell among them_ = **Immanuel**, God with us
  - the whole point: God closing the gap sin created
- **The sanctuary is Jacob's ladder** — heaven meeting earth
  - "This is none other but the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven" (Gen. 28:17)
  - Christ claimed it: "ye shall see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of man" (John 1:51)
- **_He_ is the sanctuary. _We_ are the temple.**
  - "know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you?" (1 Cor. 3:16)
  - [→] image restored = God dwelling in His people again

Every piece of furniture is a portrait of Christ. Walk through the sanctuary and you walk through the gospel:

### Outer Court — the cost of restoration

- **Altar of sacrifice** — "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" (John 1:29)
  - "without shedding of blood is no remission" (Heb. 9:22)
  - Lev. 17:11: "the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls"
- **Laver** — washing before ministry; "the washing of regeneration" (Titus 3:5)
  - priests cleansed before entering — we are cleansed before serving

### Holy Place — the life of restoration

- **Candlestick** — "I am the light of the world" (John 8:12)
  - seven branches, pure gold, never goes out — Christ's Spirit illuminating His people
- **Table of showbread** — "I am the bread of life" (John 6:35)
  - 12 loaves, renewed every Sabbath — Christ sustains all His people continually
- **Altar of incense** — "He ever liveth to make intercession for them" (Heb. 7:25)
  - prayers mingled with Christ's merit rise before God (Rev. 8:3-4)

### Most Holy Place — the goal of restoration

- **Ark of the covenant** — God's law _inside_, mercy seat _above_, blood _between_
  - law beneath = God's unchanging character
  - mercy seat above = grace provided through sacrifice
  - blood between = Christ's atonement satisfying the law while extending mercy
  - [→] the image restored: God's law written in the heart, covered by His mercy, sealed by His blood
- **Leviticus 16** — Day of Atonement
  - **high priest** enters with **his own blood** — **once** — **eternal redemption** (Heb. 9:11-12)
  - **scapegoat** sent into wilderness bearing sins (Lev. 16:21-22) — final disposition of sin

[→] The sanctuary is the gospel in architecture. Every shadow, Christ's body casting it (Col. 2:17). The blood cleanses the record _and_ the worshipper — so God can dwell with a restored people.

## Christ in Numbers

- **Numbers 21:8-9** — the bronze serpent
  - "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up: that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (John 3:14-15)
  - look and live — the gospel in one act
  - "He hath made Him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in Him" (2 Cor. 5:21)
  - [→] beholding the lifted Christ reverses the serpent's image in us — "we all... are changed into the same image" (2 Cor. 3:18)

Supporting notes:

- **Numbers 24:17** — Balaam's prophecy: "there shall come a Star out of Jacob, and a Sceptre shall rise out of Israel." Even a hired pagan prophet could not curse what God had blessed — compelled to prophesy Messiah.
- **Serpent on a pole** — why a serpent? "He hath made Him to be sin for us, who knew no sin" (2 Cor. 5:21). Christ bore the _image_ of the curse without being cursed Himself (Rom. 8:3).

## Christ in Deuteronomy

- **Deuteronomy 18:15** — the Prophet like Moses
  - "The LORD thy God will raise up unto thee a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like unto me; unto Him ye shall hearken"
  - [→] Moses pointed beyond himself to Christ — the Prophet who would write God's words not on stone but in the heart (Jer. 31:33)
- **Deuteronomy 21:23** — "he that is hanged is accursed of God"
  - "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree" (Gal. 3:13)
  - He bears the curse so the law can be written back into the heart, not merely over the record

"For had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed Me; **for he wrote of Me**" (John 5:46). Every book: Genesis (seed, sacrifice), Exodus (lamb, manna, rock), Leviticus (atonement), Numbers (serpent), Deuteronomy (Prophet, curse-bearer).

[→] Moses laid the foundation. Now the prophets build on it.

## Christ in the Prophets

- "To Him give all the prophets witness, that through His name whosoever believeth in Him shall receive remission of sins" (Acts 10:43)
- "the Spirit of Christ which was in them" drove every prophet (1 Peter 1:10-11)

### What He does: restores the image

- **Jeremiah 31:33** — "I will put My law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts"
  - not stone tablets — **heart tablets**. Law written within = image restored
- **Jeremiah 24:7** — "I will give them an heart to know Me"
  - knowing God = eternal life (John 17:3) — starts with a **new heart**
- **Ezekiel 36:26-27** — "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you"
  - **God does the heart surgery**
  - His Spirit within → walking in His law → image restored → character of love
- **Isaiah 53:5** — "with His stripes we are healed"
  - healed of what? The broken image. Every prophecy of suffering points to _why_ He suffered — to restore what was lost

### What He fulfills: every detail foretold

- **David** — Ps. 22:1, 16-18: **forsaken cry**, **pierced hands/feet**, **garments divided** — 1,000 years before Calvary
  - Ps. 16:10: **resurrection** — "not suffer Thine Holy One to see corruption" (Acts 2:27-31)
  - Ps. 110:4: **priest for ever** after Melchizedek — heavenly priesthood
- **Isaiah** — 7:14: **virgin birth**, Immanuel; 9:6: **Mighty God**, Prince of Peace
- **Daniel** — 9:25-26: **Messiah the Prince**, date of coming, **cut off but not for Himself**
- **Micah** — 5:2: **Bethlehem** named, **goings forth from everlasting**
- **Habakkuk** — 2:4: "The just shall live by his faith" — 600 years before Paul, same gospel
- **Zechariah** — 11:12-13: **thirty pieces of silver**, **cast to the potter**
- **Malachi** — 3:1: **messenger prepares the way**, Lord **suddenly comes to His temple**

[→] Not trivia proofs. Every detail serves the one work: Christ comes, suffers, ministers, and returns — to restore the image.

[Q] **If the Old Testament saints had the gospel, what changed at the cross?**
→ Not the gospel — the administration. Shadow became substance. Type met antitype. The faith, the grace, the Savior — unchanged.
→ "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever" (Heb. 13:8)

## The Emmaus Method

What Christ did with those two disciples is what He invites us to do with every Bible study:

1. **Start with Moses** — don't skip Genesis through Deuteronomy
2. **Move through all the prophets** — every one of them pointed to Him
3. **See Christ in all the Scriptures** — not just the "messianic" passages
4. **Let your heart burn** — "Did not our heart burn within us, while He talked with us by the way, and while He opened to us the Scriptures?" (Luke 24:32)

This is not academic exercise. When you see Christ in the Pentateuch, in the prophets, in the Psalms — your heart burns. The Bible becomes one book with one Author telling one story about one Savior doing one work: **restoring the image of God in man**.

- Image lost in Genesis 3 → image restored through the gospel
- Law on stone (Sinai) → law in the heart (Jeremiah 31)
- Character of love broken by sin → character of love rebuilt by the Spirit (Ezekiel 36)
- "Looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith" (Heb. 12:2)
  - "archegos" = originator, pioneer — He started this story in Eden
  - "teleiotes" = perfecter, completer — He will finish it at His return
  - "we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory" (2 Cor. 3:18)

## Appeal

The gospel is not a New Testament invention. It was preached to Adam at the gate. It was typed in Abel's lamb. It was believed by Abraham. It was foreshadowed in the Passover. It was ritualized in the sanctuary. It was proclaimed by every prophet. And it was fulfilled at Calvary.

And what is this gospel? Not merely forgiveness — **restoration**. A new heart. The law of love written within. The image of God rebuilt in fallen man. Knowing Him — and being changed by the knowing.

One plan. One Savior. One faith. One work: image restored, character transformed, life eternal.

_"And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, He expounded unto them in all the Scriptures the things concerning Himself."_ (Luke 24:27)

Walk the Emmaus road. See Him everywhere. Let your heart burn.
