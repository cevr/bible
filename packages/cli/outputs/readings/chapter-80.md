# Reading 80: The Gospel in the Old Testament

One plan of salvation—from Eden to eternity. Christ is not Plan B.

---

## The Universal Problem

- **Adam's sin** brought death to all
  - "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin: and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned" (Rom. 5:12)
  - not just Adam's guilt—we all participate in fallen nature
- **Condemnation** came universally through one offense
  - "by the offense of one judgment came upon all men to condemnation" (Rom. 5:18a)
  - [→] universal problem requires universal solution

[DYK🔎] "death passed upon all men" — Greek "dierchomai" = spread through, penetrate completely. Sin didn't just touch humanity; it permeated it.

---

## The Universal Remedy

- **One righteousness** answers one offense
  - "even so by the righteousness of one the free gift came upon all men unto justification of life" (Rom. 5:18b)
  - same scope: all condemned → all offered justification
- **No alternative name**
  - "Neither is there salvation in any other; for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved" (Acts 4:12)
  - not Moses, not prophets, not angels—Christ alone
- **Grace through faith** — God's method from the beginning
  - "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God" (Eph. 2:8)
  - salvation = gift received, not wage earned

[Q] **If salvation is only through Christ, what about people before the cross?**
→ Same Christ, same faith—different direction of view (forward vs. backward)
→ "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever" (Heb. 13:8)

---

## Faith: The Constant Method

- **Patriarchs** received promises by faith
  - "Therefore it is of faith, that it might be by grace; to the end the promise might be sure to all the seed" (Rom. 4:16)
  - faith ensures grace; works would make salvation uncertain
- **Prophets** taught justification by faith
  - "The just shall live by his faith" (Hab. 2:4)
  - Habakkuk 600+ years before Paul—same gospel
  - Paul quotes this 3× (Rom. 1:17; Gal. 3:11; Heb. 10:38)
- **All prophets** pointed to Christ for remission
  - "To Him give all the prophets witness, that through His name whosoever believeth in Him shall receive remission of sins" (Acts 10:43)
  - not some prophets—all; one unified testimony

[→] Abraham demonstrates this ancient faith in action

---

## Abraham: Model of Gospel Faith

- **Believed God** → counted righteous
  - "Abraham believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness" (Rom. 4:3)
  - imputed righteousness—credited to his account
- **Saw Christ's day** — and rejoiced
  - "Your father Abraham rejoiced to see My day: and he saw it, and was glad" (John 8:56)
  - not vague hope; specific anticipation of Messiah
- **Gospel preached** to Abraham
  - "And the Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the heathen through faith, preached before the gospel unto Abraham" (Gal. 3:8)
  - same "euangelion" (good news) we preach today

[ILL] Man in burning building sees rescuer coming from distance. Trusts, leaps to safety. Did the distance change the rescuer's identity? Abraham saw from afar; we see from near—same Savior.

- **Believers = Abraham's children**
  - "Know ye therefore that they which are of faith, the same are the children of Abraham" (Gal. 3:7)
  - not bloodline but faith-line defines true Israel
  - [→] connects to Rom. 9:6-8; spiritual seed

[DYK🔎] Genesis 15:6 is the first occurrence of "believed" (Hebrew "aman" = to trust, rely on) connected with righteousness—foundational gospel text.

---

## Moses: Faith in Christ Before Bethlehem

- **Refused Egypt's throne** by faith
  - "By faith Moses, when he was come to years, refused to be called the son of Pharaoh's daughter" (Heb. 11:24)
- **Chose affliction** over temporary pleasure
  - "choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God, than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season" (Heb. 11:25)
- **Esteemed Christ's reproach** — yes, Christ!
  - "esteeming the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures in Egypt" (Heb. 11:26)
  - Moses knew whose reproach he bore
  - 1,400 years before incarnation—Christ present in Old Testament faith

[Q] **How could Moses know about Christ?**
→ The Spirit of Christ taught through types, promises, direct revelation
→ "Christ" = "Anointed One" / "Messiah" — concept known from Eden promise (Gen. 3:15)

---

## Israel: Sustained by Christ

- **Baptized unto Moses** — yet drinking from Christ
  - "And were all baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea" (1 Cor. 10:2)
- **Spiritual food and drink** from one source
  - "and did all eat the same spiritual meat; and did all drink the same spiritual drink" (1 Cor. 10:3-4a)
  - manna = bread from heaven (John 6:31-35)
  - water from rock = living water (John 4:14; 7:37-38)
- **That Rock was Christ**
  - "for they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that Rock was Christ" (1 Cor. 10:4b)
  - not symbolic—actual spiritual provision from pre-incarnate Christ

[→] This explains why Moses wrote of Christ

---

## Moses' Writings: Christ-Centered

- **Believe Moses → believe Christ**
  - "For had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed Me; for he wrote of Me" (John 5:46)
  - Pentateuch is Christological
- **Reject Moses → reject Christ**
  - "But if ye believe not his writings, how shall ye believe My words?" (John 5:47)
  - Old Testament faith and New Testament faith are inseparable

[TANGENT] Christ in the Pentateuch: Gen. 3:15 (seed of woman); Gen. 22 (Isaac offering); Ex. 12 (Passover lamb); Lev. 16 (Day of Atonement); Num. 21 (bronze serpent); Deut. 18:15 (Prophet like Moses)

---

## The Spirit of Christ in the Prophets

- **Prophets inquired** about coming grace
  - "Of which salvation the prophets have inquired and searched diligently, who prophesied of the grace that should come unto you" (1 Peter 1:10)
  - they didn't fully understand what they wrote
- **Christ's Spirit** inspired them
  - "searching what, or what manner of time the Spirit of Christ which was in them did signify" (1 Peter 1:11)
  - same Holy Spirit = Spirit of Christ
  - Old Testament = Christ speaking through prophets

[DYK🔎] "Spirit of Christ which was in them" — prophets were Christ's mouthpieces. When Isaiah wrote "unto us a child is born" (Isa. 9:6), Christ's own Spirit prompted those words.

---

## Christ's Own Bible Study Method

- **Beginning at Moses** — not skipping to "relevant" parts
  - "And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, He expounded unto them in all the Scriptures the things concerning Himself" (Luke 24:27)
- **All the Scriptures** = about Him
  - Emmaus road: entire Old Testament opened as Christ's testimony
  - the disciples' hearts burned (Luke 24:32)
  - [→] our hearts should burn when we see Christ in Genesis through Malachi

[ILL] Hidden picture puzzle—shapes seem random until you see the face. Once seen, you can't unsee it. Christ in the Old Testament: once revealed, He's everywhere.

---

## Author and Finisher

- **Jesus** = source and completer
  - "Looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith" (Heb. 12:2)
  - "archegos" (author) = originator, pioneer, leader
  - "teleiotes" (finisher) = perfecter, completer
- **One plan** — from Genesis 3:15 to Revelation 22:21
  - Christ initiated salvation in Eden
  - Christ completes it at His return
  - same faith, same grace, same Savior throughout

[Q] **Why does this matter practically?**
→ The God of Abraham is your God
→ The faith that saved Moses can save you
→ The Christ who followed Israel follows you
→ One unbroken covenant of grace—you are included

---

## Sanctuary Connection

- **Outer court** (sacrifice) — pointed to Christ's death
  - every lamb = the Lamb (John 1:29)
  - patriarchs understood substitution
- **Holy place** (daily ministry) — pointed to Christ's intercession
  - lampstand, showbread, incense = Christ's ongoing work
  - Old Testament believers accessed this by faith
- **Most holy place** (atonement) — pointed to final cleansing
  - Day of Atonement = final judgment (Daniel 7-8)
  - same sanctuary Christ now ministers in heaven (Heb. 8:1-2)

[→] One sanctuary message; two covenants expressing same truth
