# 7. God's Covering Glory (Dress Reform)

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2016/05/07/7-gods-covering-glory-dress-reform/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

God's glory is His character. That glory must be visibly reflected in how believers dress — clothing is the external index of an inward character transformed by God's word. Dress reform is therefore inseparable from glorifying God, and specific standards (length, modesty, color, gender distinction) are divinely mandated, not merely cultural preferences.

## Key Claims

- Adam and Eve were not literally bare; they were "clothed with a covering of light and glory" before the Fall.
- After the Fall, God personally made "coats of skins" that enshrouded the body from shoulders to feet.
- Nakedness = absence of God's light-covering; clothing substitutes for lost divine covering.
- The dress "should reach somewhat below the top of the boot" but not trail the ground.
- For women wearing shoes (not boots), the dress must cover the ankle; the ankle exposed with only a stocking or sock is deemed immodest.
- A shorter dress is appropriate for housework or outdoor labor.
- "There is but one woman in a thousand who clothes her limbs as she should."
- Women's limbs should be as thoroughly covered as men's.
- Limbs covered only by a cotton stocking (summer) or flannel (winter) are described as immodest.
- The Greek behind "modest apparel" (1 Tim. 2:9) is interpreted as meaning _flowing garments_ — an enshrouding.
- Modesty means whatever position the body is in, nakedness remains covered.
- Imitating worldly fashion causes gradual loss of "that peculiar, holy character" that distinguishes God's people.
- Modest (non-attention-drawing) colors are required.
- "Large and fiery" figures on fabric reflect "vanity and shallow pride."
- Colors must blend; nothing should draw attention to the body.
- Pantsuits on women constitute the "American costume" condemned in Deut. 22:5 — explicitly called "an abomination to God."
- Men's clothing covers fully; the world consistently leaves some part of the woman uncovered.
- Aaron's priestly garments were "for glory and for beauty" (Ex. 28:2) — the type for believers' clothing.
- The linen breeches (Ex. 28:42) exist specifically "to cover their nakedness."
- God's own covering is light (Ps. 104:2); His "skirts filled the temple" (Isa. 6:1, margin).
- Any exposure of the female form through tight or form-fitting garments is objectionable.
