# 18. God's Way of Dress in the Sanctuary – Part 2

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/09/11/18-gods-way-of-dress-part-2/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

The priestly garments of the Hebrew sanctuary establish God's timeless standard for modest, gender-distinct, body-covering dress. This typology — holiness made visible — obligates God's remnant people to dress differently from the world, functioning as a spiritual/identity marker analogous to Israel's blue ribbon.

## Key Claims

- Nakedness is a shame requiring covering to be "beautiful in God's eyes."
- Only within marriage is nakedness not an abomination.
- Ham's curse is linked to the principle of uncovering nakedness inappropriately.
- Priestly linen was dense; breeches underneath ensured complete coverage.
- Dresses sweeping streets are immodest _and_ unhealthy (cold, wet fabric striking ankles).
- Dress should clear street filth by "an inch or two."
- The second vision-company's dresses "does not reach to the knee" — shown as too short.
- The approved third-company dress cleared street filth "a few inches under all circumstances."
- Limbs must be clothed as thoroughly as males', using lined pants gathered at the ankle.
- No sleeveless dress, no low neckline, no form-fitting or transparent material.
- Form and figure should be "thoroughly cloaked."
- The body's form should not be revealed — no see-through material, nothing form-fitting.
- Wearing fashionable dress while professing faith causes unbelievers to say: "There is no distinction between us."
- Those on the broad road are "occupied with their persons, their dress, and the pleasures."
- Corsets and whale bones compress the female form against health.
- The "American Costume" (pant suits, vest/coat/boots imitating male dress) violates Deut. 22:5.
- God "pronounces it abomination" for women to dress like men.
- Priestly attire depicted "beauty of holiness, for glory and for beauty."
- The sanctuary had "many coverings"; dress should similarly cover thoroughly.
- The dress reform functions like "the ribbon of blue to ancient Israel."
- Dress reform and health reform are explicitly linked.
- Heavy skirts on hips cause disease; hips "were not formed to bear heavy weights."
- Corsets prevent healthy lung and heart action.
