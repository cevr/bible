# Adornment for the One We Love

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/08/10/adornment-for-the-one-we-love/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

God's redemptive love — demonstrated through Israel's deliverance, the New Covenant, and Christ's death — should so transform the believer's inner spirit that external appearance (clothing, hair, adornment) naturally reflects internal meekness, modesty, and separation from worldly fashion. True adornment is the "ornament of a meek and quiet spirit" (1 Pet. 3:4), which manifests outwardly in modest, concealing dress and restrained hairstyle. Failure to dress modestly indexes a proud, unconverted heart.

## Key Claims

- Adam and Eve were originally clothed with divine light; sin exposed their nakedness, which they covered with fig leaves — insufficient covering.
- God's response was to make "coats of skins" — a typological act pointing to Christ's righteousness.
- "Any part of your body that you display is contrary to the cross of Christ."
- The body's shape and contours should not be visible through clothing; holy garments conceal form entirely.
- Exposure of legs, ankles, and limbs is declared immodest and shameful.
- Sheer stockings are condemned as originating from worldly fashion.
- Bending in loose material that reveals any part of the body is treated as nakedness.
- "The outside appearance is an index to the heart."
- Worldly fashion is equated with a proud, haughty spirit.
- Ponytails / upswept hair are compared to a "spirited," prancing horse — an index of a proud heart — versus hair worn down as an index of meekness.
- "Clothing is the most powerful tool to display the stubbornness of men and women today."
- Aaron's priestly garments → symbol of Christ's righteousness covering the believer.
- God clothed in light (Ps. 104) → Adam and Eve clothed in light before the fall → coats of skins after the fall → Aaron's holy garments → believer's modest dress. All form one typological chain.
- The daughters of Zion (Isa. 3:16) display proud hearts through their manner of walking and adorning.
