# The Fashions of Babylon

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/09/01/the-fashions-of-babylon/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

Dress is a spiritual matter rooted in obedience to God's commandments. Following worldly or Babylonian fashions leads to moral and spiritual ruin, while plain, modest dress reflects trust in God and separation from worldly corruption.

## Key Claims

- OT garments were designed to visually remind wearers of God's commandments.
- Seeking fashion leads to "going a whoring" after one's own heart/eyes.
- Women wearing masculine attire is an abomination to God.
- Mixing linen and wool in garments corrupts the typological figure of Christ's righteousness.
- Worldly fashions existed in ancient times, not just modernity.
- The "daughters of Zion" following worldly fashion caused Zion to become desolate.
- Only firm trust in God enabled holy women to dress plainly.
- Ornaments (earrings, rings, nose jewels, etc.) are associated with idolatry and spiritual compromise.
- Achan's coveting of a Babylonish garment cost him his place in Israel.
- Jacob's family's worldly dress was linked to idolatry; spiritual renewal required changing garments.
