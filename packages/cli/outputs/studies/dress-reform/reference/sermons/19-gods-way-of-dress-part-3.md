# 19. God's Way of Dress in the Sanctuary – Part 3

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/09/10/19-gods-way-of-dress-part-3/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

God's way of dress is symbolically connected to spiritual purity and character. Clothing fiber, style, and modesty all typologically mirror Christ's righteousness — pure, unmingled, without "one thread of human devising." Babylonish/mixed-fiber garments are spiritually cursed, morally corrupting, and incompatible with representing Christ.

## Key Claims

- God has explicit preferences about dress for those who serve Him (sanctuary typology).
- White linen = righteousness of saints (Rev. 19:8).
- Mixing fabric fibers violates a divine statute and defiles the symbol of Christ's pure righteousness.
- The "Babylonish garment" taken by Achan was the _accursed thing_, not the gold/silver.
- Certain fabrics create static electricity, affecting the body and morals unconsciously.
- Tight/sensual clothing (e.g., jeans) influences youthful morality.
- Isaiah 3 applies specifically to last-days church members ("daughters of Zion").
- Jacob's household becoming properly dressed caused surrounding peoples to fear them (divine protection).
- The fringe/ribbon of blue in Numbers 15 typologically prefigures dress reform principles.
- Clothing style is a "badge of admission to the palace of the King."
- Covering, style, and fiber all connect to holiness.
- High-heeled shoes are associated with sensuality ("tinkling with their feet").
