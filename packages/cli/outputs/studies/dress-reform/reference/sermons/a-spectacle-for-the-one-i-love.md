# A Spectacle For the One I Love

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2014/01/14/a-spectacle-for-the-one-i-love/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

Those who submit to the Holy Spirit's possession—following God's voice rather than Satan's—become visibly distinct from a society progressively conditioned by demonic influence. This singularity, especially in dress, modesty, and lifestyle, makes the faithful Christian a "spectacle" to the fallen world, mirroring Christ's own experience. Loving Jesus means willingly accepting that spectacle-status rather than conforming to worldly norms.

## Key Claims

- Satan works incrementally through a seductive voice, gradually normalizing immodesty—just as he possessed a spiritualist woman and directed her behavior step-by-step.
- Mixed-sex bathing and progressively revealing swimwear were early stages of Satan's conditioning of Protestant society.
- Men wearing shorts was once unthinkable; working men in forest photographs never appear in shorts.
- Showing an ankle was once considered immodest; shortening hemlines was a staged satanic progression.
- Sheer stockings on women were once regarded as horrifying within Christian society.
- Coloured hair, street-level undress, and beach attire are presented as the logical endpoint of this progression.
- African tribal body modification (neck rings, bones through noses/ears) is cited as an analog to pagan spiritual influence over bodily appearance.
- The fall of Sardis-era Protestant churches is linked to opening doors to Satan's fashion/cultural influence.
- A true Christian's outward appearance reflects inner character, just as Christ wore righteousness visibly.
- Women wearing trousers, unthinkable in 1924–25 Europe, is cited as a prophetic cultural marker ("the end of the world").
- Dress reform is non-negotiable; softening it to avoid repelling the world is itself a capitulation to the wrong voice.
- The 144,000 are described as those who will face courts/harassment in end-time society for their distinctiveness.
