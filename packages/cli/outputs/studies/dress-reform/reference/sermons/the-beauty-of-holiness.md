# The Beauty of Holiness

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/08/16/the-beauty-of-holiness/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

True beauty is spiritually discerned — only the born-again believer can perceive holiness as beautiful. Worldly beauty standards (fashion, sensuality, pride) blind the natural mind. Genuine conversion must transform dress, music, lifestyle, and speech to reflect God's standard of holiness, as typified by the priestly garments.

## Key Claims

- Beauty is entirely relative; only the spiritually reborn can perceive divine beauty.
- The Hebrew priestly garments were literal types of heavenly/character-based beauty.
- Christ had "no beauty" in natural human perception (per Isaiah 53).
- Dress is the "index" of inward character.
- Babylonian fashion influences corrupt the believer's aesthetic judgment.
- New converts must be specifically instructed on dress reform before/at baptism.
- Modesty, neatness, and purity are the three outward marks of heavenly dress.
- Hairstyle, jewelry, and fashionable apparel are contrasted with inward meek-spirit beauty.
- Failure to reform dress = remaining conformed to Babylon.
