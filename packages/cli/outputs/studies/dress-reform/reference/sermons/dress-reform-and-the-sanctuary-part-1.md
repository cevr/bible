# Dress Reform and the Sanctuary – Part 1

Source: https://sabbathsermons.com/2009/08/13/dress-reform-and-the-sanctuary-part-1/
Fetched: 2026-05-27

## Thesis

Dress reform must be understood through the sanctuary typology, not as an external rule. The body is the courtyard; physical modesty mirrors the priest's holy garments. Correct motivation (sanctuary understanding) produces correct dress. Clothing symbolizes righteousness and God's character.

## Key Claims

- The body = the courtyard of the sanctuary; the mind = the first apartment; the frontal lobe = the Most Holy Place.
- The sanctuary's white linen perimeter typifies bodily covering with righteousness.
- Priestly garments ("for glory and for beauty") are the model for Christian dress.
- Adam and Eve were not naked in the sense of exposed flesh — they wore _light_, like God.
- Sin removed the robe of light; God replaced it with _coats of skins_ (typifying full coverage).
- Exposing the thigh/leg constitutes nakedness in God's sight (Isaiah 47).
- Cutting garments to mid-length (like modern shorts) was a cause of shame (2 Samuel 10).
- Women's long hair is their God-given head covering, signifying submission within God's government.
- Angels observe and require the sign of a woman's head covering to minister effectively.
- Haughty dress (Isaiah 3) is connected to spiritual rebellion, not merely vanity.
- The 144,000 in vision wore "white mantle from their shoulders to their feet."
- Dress reveals character; "a refined taste, a cultivated mind, will be revealed in the choice of simple and appropriate attire."
