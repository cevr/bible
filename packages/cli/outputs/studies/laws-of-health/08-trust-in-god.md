# Study 8: Trust in God

**Salvation is the restoration of God's image in the mind (Study 3, _The Way Home_). Chronic stress — the physiology of prolonged fight-or-flight — erodes every other pillar of health simultaneously: sleep, immunity, digestion, cardiovascular function, cognition. The physiology of trust is the opposite: the parasympathetic state in which the body heals, the mind clears, and the Spirit's quiet voice can be heard. Trust in God is not the soft spiritual appendix to seven "real" health laws. It is the foundation that makes the other seven sustainable — and the faculty without which sanctification cannot proceed.**

## Introduction — The Eighth Pillar Holds the Other Seven

- **Why trust comes last but functions first**
  - NEWSTART ends with Trust because it contextualizes everything before it
  - Without trust, the other seven laws become either legalism (striving to earn health as merit) or performance (anxious self-improvement project)
  - With trust, the other seven become stewardship — cooperation with a God who designed the body and cares about its wellbeing
  - [→] The seven physical laws operate through a mind. The mind operates best in a state of trust. Trust is not a spiritual add-on; it is the operating system.

- **The mental health crisis and the epidemic of anxiety**
  - Rates of anxiety, depression, and chronic stress in developed countries have risen dramatically in recent decades
  - Modern life combines abundant information about danger with minimal control over most of it — a biochemical recipe for chronic low-grade stress
  - Behind much physical disease is unprocessed, unresolved stress — wearing the body down without ever being treated
  - [→] The body heals poorly when the stress response never stands down. This is not pious exaggeration; it is physiology.

## The Physiology of Stress

### The HPA Axis and Cortisol

- **Acute stress — the system working as designed**
  - Perceived threat → hypothalamus activates → pituitary releases ACTH → adrenals release cortisol and adrenaline
  - Heart rate up, blood to muscles, pupils dilate, digestion pauses, immune response mobilized
  - This system kept our ancestors alive in lion country; it is wonderfully designed for short, acute challenges
  - After the threat passes, the parasympathetic nervous system kicks in: heart rate drops, digestion resumes, repair happens

- **Chronic stress — the system failing to reset**
  - Modern life keeps the HPA axis continuously low-grade activated: traffic, email, news, social conflict, financial pressure, persistent rumination
  - There is no lion to kill and no clear escape — the system stays on, day after day
  - Chronically elevated cortisol damages nearly every organ system over time

### What Chronic Stress Actually Does

- **Cardiovascular**
  - Elevated blood pressure, damaged blood vessel linings, increased clotting
  - Chronic stress is an independent risk factor for heart attack, stroke, and hypertension

- **Immune**
  - Acute stress mobilizes immunity; chronic stress suppresses it
  - Chronic cortisol shrinks immune cells, reduces antibody production, and slows wound healing
  - This is why people get sick during exam season, during divorce, during extended caregiving — the stress suppresses the defense

- **Digestive**
  - "Rest and digest" happens only in the parasympathetic state; chronic stress means chronic digestive disruption
  - Irritable bowel syndrome, ulcers, reflux, malabsorption — stress-correlated conditions
  - The gut-brain axis works both directions: stressed brain → compromised gut; compromised gut → compromised brain

- **Brain and cognition**
  - Chronic stress and cortisol dysregulation are associated with hippocampal remodeling and volume loss, and with altered amygdala reactivity and structure
  - Translation, in practice: harder to remember, easier to panic, slower to recover emotionally
  - Depression, anxiety, and cognitive decline all correlate with chronic stress exposure (though causality runs in multiple directions)
  - [→] Here we intersect every other study. Chronic stress degrades sleep (Study 7), dysregulates appetite (Study 1), suppresses exercise motivation (Study 2), and drives toward addictive coping (Study 5). It is the force multiplier for every other health problem.

- **Metabolic**
  - Cortisol raises blood sugar (to fuel the stress response) — chronically, this drives insulin resistance and abdominal fat accumulation
  - Stress eating is biochemical: cortisol + disrupted leptin/ghrelin = cravings for calorie-dense comfort foods

[DYK🔎] Major medical organizations now treat chronic stress as clinically relevant, especially in cardiovascular and mental health risk. The American Heart Association, in a 2017 scientific statement, said meditation "may be reasonable to consider as an adjunct" to standard cardiovascular care, though not as a replacement. Interventions that reduce chronic stress — meditation, prayer, regular exercise, therapy, supportive relationships, restful sleep — are increasingly recognized in primary prevention contexts.

## The Physiology of Trust

### The Parasympathetic State

- **The "rest and digest" nervous system**
  - The parasympathetic nervous system is the body's default mode when the threat response is off
  - In this state: heart rate slows, heart rate variability rises, digestion works, immunity restores, tissue repairs
  - The body heals best here — and heals poorly when the stress response never stands down.

- **Vagal tone**
  - The vagus nerve connects the brain to most major organs — it is the main carrier of parasympathetic signals
  - High vagal tone (the ability to shift into parasympathetic dominance) is associated with better cardiovascular health, emotional regulation, and resilience
  - Practices associated with improved autonomic regulation include: slow breathing, singing, meditation and prayer, and social connection. (Cold exposure is used by some for similar ends, but it is sympathetic-stimulating acutely and not universally appropriate.)
  - [→] The same practices Scripture has commanded for millennia — prayer, singing, community — are among the interventions modern research identifies as supporting the healing state.

### Prayer, Meditation, and the Nervous System

- **Contemplative prayer and meditation**
  - Extensive research (including work by Herbert Benson at Harvard on "the relaxation response," 1975 onward) shows contemplative practices lower blood pressure, heart rate, cortisol, and inflammation
  - Effect sizes are modest but consistent across many studies
  - The mechanism: repeated focus + slow breathing + mental stillness activates parasympathetic dominance

- **Prayer specifically**
  - Prayer is the Christian contemplative tradition — with the crucial addition of a _Person_ being addressed, not just a void being emptied into
  - Research on prayer and health (Harold Koenig at Duke, decades of work) finds associations between regular prayer and lower rates of depression, anxiety, addiction, and better overall health
  - Association, not proven causation — but the association is robust across many studies, populations, and methodologies

- **The biological mechanism, roughly**
  - Prayer typically involves: slow breathing + postural stillness + mental focus + a sense of safety with the One addressed
  - These elements each engage mechanisms associated with parasympathetic regulation
  - Together, they can produce a sustained physiological state the body rarely enters in modern life — though the deeper biblical claim is theological, not mechanical: God Himself guards the heart and mind in Christ (Phil 4:7)

### Faith, Forgiveness, and Mental Health

- **The forgiveness research**
  - Krause and Ellison's 2003 late-life study on unconditional vs. conditional forgiveness:
    - People who forgave unconditionally (without requiring the offender to apologize) showed lower depression, lower anxiety, and less somatization of distress
    - People who forgave only conditionally showed the opposite pattern
  - The strongest predictor of which group someone fell into: _do they believe God has forgiven them?_
  - Perceived forgiveness by God predicted unconditional forgiveness of others — Seheult summarizes the odds ratio as approximately 2.5

- **Faith-integrated therapy**
  - RCTs and reviews suggest religion-adapted cognitive behavioral therapy is at least as effective as conventional CBT, and sometimes more effective for religious patients when the adaptation matches the patient's own faith
  - Non-religious patients do not benefit from faith-imposed therapy and should not have it imposed
  - The point: for the believer, faith is not a placebo alternative to treatment — it is an active component of healing that engages the whole system

- **Religion and mortality research**
  - Multiple large cohort studies associate regular religious attendance and practice with lower mortality, less depression, less addiction, better health behaviors
  - Mechanisms include: community support, meaning-making, moral coherence, stress buffering, behavioral norms (e.g., Adventist lifestyle cluster)
  - [→] This research is association, not randomized evidence. Causation is multi-layered. But the direction of association is consistent and remarkable.

[DYK🔎] Dr. Roger Seheult (Adventist pulmonologist, MedCram): "There is a silo of science and there is a silo of faith. But what we can't ignore is the growing body of evidence from the scientific world that's peeking over and looking at faith. People who have a good faith and trust in God... are better apt and able to deal with stress and depression and anxiety. This is something scientifically that has been shown." A critical-care physician does not say "trust in God is scientifically associated with better health" lightly.

## Trust in Scripture

- **Proverbs 3:5-6 — the core instruction**
  - "Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding. In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths"
  - Not "trust after you understand" but "trust when you don't" — the point of trust is that it operates precisely where autonomous comprehension fails
  - Hebrew _batach_ means "to trust, confide, feel secure"; paired with _sha'an_ ("lean") in the next clause, the verse pictures embodied reliance, not intellectual assent

- **Matthew 6:25-34 — the physiology of worry**
  - "Take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink.... Which of you by taking thought can add one cubit unto his stature?"
  - "Take no thought" translates Greek _me merimnate_ — the word carries anxious concern, a mind pulled apart by cares
  - Christ names anxiety as the default mode of the world and trust as the alternative
  - "Sufficient unto the day is the evil thereof" (v.34) — one day at a time, built into the instruction

- **Philippians 4:6-7 — the antidote formula**
  - "Be careful for nothing; but in every thing by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God. And the peace of God, which passeth all understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus"
  - Prayer + thanksgiving → peace that exceeds understanding → guards the heart and mind
  - This is more than metaphor. Prayer and thanksgiving engage mechanisms associated with parasympathetic regulation; the deeper claim is theological: God Himself guards the heart and mind in Christ.

- **1 Peter 5:6-7 — the cast**
  - "Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you"
  - Greek _epiripto_ — literally "throw upon" — an active transfer of the mental load
  - The Psalms repeatedly model this: naming the fear, the injustice, the grief — and explicitly handing it to God

- **Isaiah 26:3**
  - "Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind is stayed on thee: because he trusteth in thee"
  - _Perfect peace_ = Hebrew _shalom shalom_ (doubled for emphasis; sometimes translated "peace, peace")
  - The condition: the mind _stayed_ on God — held, fixed, resting

- **Psalm 46:10**
  - "Be still, and know that I am God"
  - _Be still_ = Hebrew _raphah_ — to let go, release, drop the hands
  - Stillness is the precondition of knowing; the anxious mind cannot perceive what the calm mind receives

[EB] Romans 8:28 — "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." The verse anchors trust not in circumstances but in a God whose purposes comprehend the circumstances.

[EGW] "There are many whose hearts are aching under a load of care because they seek to reach the world's standard.... The continual worry is wearing out the life forces. Our Lord desires them to lay aside this yoke of bondage. He invites them to accept His yoke; He says, 'My yoke is easy, and My burden is light.' Worry is blind and cannot discern the future; but Jesus sees the end from the beginning. In every difficulty He has His way prepared to bring relief" (MH 481.1). And elsewhere: "Grief, anxiety, discontent, remorse, guilt, distrust, all tend to break down the life forces and to invite decay and death" (MH 241.1). She wrote these in 1905. Modern psychoneuroimmunology has filled in the details.

## Worry as Intemperance of the Mind

- **A spiritual diagnosis**
  - We typically treat anxiety as a psychological problem or a medical one; Scripture also treats it as a _spiritual_ one
  - Worry is trust misdirected — reliance on the self instead of God
  - Worry is forgetfulness — of who God is, what He has done, what He has promised
  - Worry is functionally atheistic — operating as if one is alone in the universe, even when one professes otherwise

- **"Fear not"**
  - Homiletically cited as ~365 "fear not" or "be not afraid" passages; exact counts vary and the "once for every day" framing is preachable rather than precise
  - Whatever the exact count, "fear not" is one of Scripture's most repeated commands
  - God did not repeat the instruction so often because fear is rare; He repeated it because it is nearly universal
  - [→] Worry is our native soil. Trust is a cultivated response.

- **Worry and health — closing the loop**
  - Worry is one of chronic stress's most common maintainers (not its only source — trauma, poverty, caregiving, pain, illness, unsafe relationships, and work demands all produce chronic stress independent of worry)
  - Treating the body without addressing recurring fear, rumination, or the circumstances feeding them often leaves the stress loop intact
  - Anxiolytics, therapy, and meditation can calm the body; for believers, reframing what the worry is _about_ — in light of who God is — addresses a layer the body alone cannot reach

[ILL] A man afraid of the water can swim — technically — but he swims badly. Every stroke fights the water. He sinks with effort. The man who trusts the water lets it hold him up; his body relaxes into the medium and, paradoxically, floats. Trust is not passive; it is the active posture that allows the substance to do what the substance is designed to do. God is the water we are meant to float in.

## How Trust Is Built

### Trust Is Not Willpower

- **You cannot decide to trust**
  - Trust is a _response_ to trustworthiness; you cannot generate it by force of will
  - Telling someone "just trust God" is like telling someone "just stop being afraid" — the instruction cannot produce the state
  - Trust is built through relationship with a Person revealed as trustworthy

### Building Trust Practically

- **Know who you are trusting — spend time in Scripture**
  - The chain from Study 3: knowing God → beholding Christ → transformed into His image
  - Trust grows as the understanding of God's character grows
  - Regular time in Scripture — especially the Gospels and Psalms — exposes the mind to the character of the God being trusted
  - [→] You can't trust someone you don't know. Time with God is not a legalistic discipline; it is relational formation.

- **Remember what He has done — keep a record**
  - Biblical faith is historical — it trusts _because_ of what has already happened (exodus, cross, resurrection)
  - Personal history matters too: journaling answered prayers, unexpected provision, lessons learned
  - Anxiety has amnesia; faith remembers

- **Prayer as practice**
  - Daily prayer — not as ritual but as actual conversation
  - Bring the worries specifically: name them, acknowledge them, explicitly transfer them
  - "Be careful for nothing" (Phil 4:6) is _lived_ by doing the three things in the next verse: prayer, supplication, thanksgiving

- **Community and trusted relationships**
  - Trust is rebuilt through relationships with people who demonstrate trustworthiness
  - Isolation amplifies anxiety; community buffers it
  - Small groups, trusted friends, honest conversation — these are not optional extras for serious discipleship

- **Practice the parasympathetic tools**
  - Slow breathing during prayer
  - Scripture reading aloud (slow breathing built in)
  - Hymns and worship music (singing activates vagal tone)
  - Nature walks (Study 4 compounds with this)
  - Sabbath observance (Study 7 — the weekly rehearsal of rest)
  - [→] The spiritual disciplines are also the parasympathetic-activation practices. The tradition is the therapy.

### When Trust Is Hard — the Honest Part

- **Trust is developed, not downloaded**
  - Even Abraham, the "father of faith," took decades to learn to trust
  - The Psalms model the _process_ of trust more than the finished product — lament, honesty, struggle, gradual resolution
  - If trust does not come easily, you are not a defective Christian; you are a normal human being learning a countercultural response

- **Mental illness is not automatically a trust failure**
  - Clinical depression and anxiety are medical conditions, not simply spiritual weaknesses
  - Ellen White herself acknowledged multiple causes of depression and the continued love of God for the discouraged — she did not treat a heavy heart as proof of unfaithfulness
  - Medication, therapy, and spiritual practice are complementary, not competitors
  - Judging yourself or others for needing clinical help is bad theology and worse pastoring

- **The dark night**
  - Mature believers across history (Job, David, the Psalmists, Paul, John of the Cross) describe seasons of God's felt absence
  - Trust in those seasons is _not_ feeling trusting; it is continuing to address God even when the sense of His presence has withdrawn
  - The KJV reading of Job 13:15 — "Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him" — has become the classic English formulation for trust-against-experience, though the underlying Hebrew has a textual issue (ketiv/qere) and some modern translations read differently. The uncontested high-water mark is Daniel 3:17-18 ("Our God is able to deliver us... _but if not_, be it known unto thee, O king, that we will not serve thy gods") or Habakkuk 3:17-18 ("Although the fig tree shall not blossom... _yet_ I will rejoice in the LORD").

[Aside] Dr. Seheult's clinical observation: "I don't believe that any of this stuff should be placed on people without their permission." Faith-integrated medicine respects patients' worldviews. For the believer, faith is medicine; for the non-believer, imposing it is not. The science shows association; the _appropriateness_ of the intervention is always relational. This is also how God operates — knocking, inviting, never forcing (Rev 3:20).

## The Verdict — Channel or Obstruction

- **Trust in God is where the whole series converges**
  - Without it, the first seven laws become either legalism (earning health) or performance (anxious striving)
  - With it, the first seven become stewardship (cooperating with the Designer)
  - The physiology of trust activates the healing state; the physiology of chronic anxiety blocks it

- **The chain — complete**
  - Trust → parasympathetic dominance → lower cortisol, better sleep, restored immunity, functioning digestion, clearer cognition → the body cares for the mind → capacity to behold Christ (2 Cor 3:18) → the Spirit works through a less-hindered instrument → image restored (Study 3)
  - Chronic anxiety breaks the chain at the root

- **Why trust is the eighth pillar**
  - Not because it is the least important, but because it is the one that contextualizes the other seven
  - The body stewarded by an anxious soul is a body being healed with one hand and wounded with the other
  - The body stewarded by a trusting soul is a body where grace has room to work

## Appeal

- Trust is the faculty that makes salvation by grace livable on a daily basis.
- Trust cannot merit grace, produce holiness, or replace surrender. It _is_ the posture of surrender — the open hand that receives rather than the closed fist that manufactures.
- The physiology of the healing state is the physiology of prayer, Sabbath, Scripture, community, hymn-singing, and honest relationship with a God who revealed Himself as trustworthy at the cross.
- The God who designed your body also designed the soul that animates it. The two are not separate projects. He wants both restored — and the restoration of the one requires the restoration of the other.
- "Cast thy burden upon the LORD, and he shall sustain thee" (Ps 55:22).
- "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest" (Matt 11:28).
- Trust Him. Not perfectly — nobody does. But today, with today's worry, cast today's burden. Tomorrow, again. And slowly the body learns what the soul is being taught: the God who made the lungs and fed the birds and numbered the stars also numbered your hairs. He is trustworthy. Rest in Him.
