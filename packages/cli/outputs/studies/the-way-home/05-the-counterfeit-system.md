# Study 5: The Counterfeit System

**Satan could not destroy the sanctuary — so he buried it. Empire by empire, he built a counterfeit system over the top of God's truth, replacing every sanctuary reality with an earthly imitation. Four empires. Four counterfeits. One strategy: make the copy so familiar that the original looks strange.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 2:31-45
- 2 Corinthians 11:14
- Leviticus 17:11
- Ecclesiastes 9:5-6
- Exodus 20:8-11

---

## The Blueprint of Deception

- **Satan's method is never open destruction — it is replacement**
  - "I will be like the most High" (Isa 14:14) — not "I will destroy the Most High"
  - The counterfeit must resemble the original closely enough to deceive
  - Study 4 revealed what the sanctuary truly is — God's path to restore His character in humanity. Satan cannot destroy that path. So he builds a parallel one that looks the same but leads nowhere.
  - Every sanctuary truth God gave, Satan built an earthly substitute

- **Daniel's image reveals the strategy**
  - "Thou, O king, sawest, and behold a great image... This image's head was of fine gold, his breast and his arms of silver, his belly and his thighs of brass, his legs of iron" (Dan 2:31-33)
  - Four metals = four empires — but also four layers of counterfeit
  - Each empire contributed a specific corruption to the system Satan was building
  - The statue is a single image — the counterfeits form a single system

- **the mystery of iniquity — already at work**
  - "For the mystery of iniquity doth already work" (2 Thess 2:7)
  - Not a sudden invention — a slow, layered construction across centuries
  - "Who opposeth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God" (2 Thess 2:4)
  - [→] Sitting in the temple — not destroying it. Occupying the sanctuary's place. That is the counterfeit method.

- **the beast blasphemes the tabernacle**
  - "And he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God, to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle, and them that dwell in heaven" (Rev 13:6)
  - Blasphemes His name — misrepresents His character
  - Blasphemes His tabernacle — replaces His ministry
  - Blasphemes them that dwell in heaven — usurps the role of heavenly beings

> "The system of heathen worship was Satan's method of obscuring the plan of salvation." (PP 91)

[→] Each empire below follows the same spine: (1) what sanctuary truth God gave, (2) how the empire counterfeited it, (3) where that counterfeit still lives today, (4) how Christ restores the true thing.

## Babylon — The Head of Gold: Counterfeit Priesthood

- **the sanctuary truth: one High Priest mediates between God and man**
  - "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2)
  - Access to God through one divinely appointed Mediator
  - "For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus" (1 Tim 2:5)

- **Babylon's counterfeit: human priest-kings as mediators**
  - "Thou art this head of gold" (Dan 2:38)
  - Nimrod — the first empire builder and false priest-king
  - "And Cush begat Nimrod: he began to be a mighty one in the earth" (Gen 10:8)
  - "He was a mighty hunter before the LORD" (Gen 10:9) — "before" (לִפְנֵי) can mean "in the face of" — in opposition to
  - Nimrod established Babel — the first organized system of false worship (Gen 10:10)
  - [→] From Nimrod forward: a human stands where only God's appointed Priest should stand

- **the Babylonian confessional system**
  - priests became gatekeepers to divine access
  - confession of sin directed to human mediators instead of God
  - Semiramis and Tammuz — counterfeit intercessors, mother-and-son worship
  - "The children gather wood, and the fathers kindle the fire, and the women knead their dough, to make cakes to the queen of heaven" (Jer 7:18)
  - "We will certainly... burn incense unto the queen of heaven" (Jer 44:17)

[DYK🔎] The title "Pontifex Maximus" (greatest bridge-builder) originated in ancient Roman religion but traces its concept back to Babylon — the priest as bridge between gods and men. It was adopted by the Caesars, then inherited by a Christian office. The title still exists today. The bridge Babylon built was always a counterfeit of the ladder Jacob saw.

- **where it lives today**
  - human priesthood as necessary mediator
  - confessional directed to a man instead of to God
  - veneration of Mary as intercessor — "queen of heaven" in new language
  - any system that places a human gatekeeper between the sinner and Christ

- **Christ restores the true thing**
  - "Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our profession" (Heb 4:14)
  - "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need" (Heb 4:16)
  - No human intermediary needed — direct access through Christ alone
  - [→] You do not need permission from any man to approach God. The High Priest already knows your name.

## Medo-Persia — The Silver Arms: Counterfeit Atonement

- **the sanctuary truth: atonement through blood alone**
  - "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul" (Lev 17:11)
  - "And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission" (Heb 9:22)
  - The sanctuary system taught one lesson relentlessly: sin costs life, and only a substitute's blood can cover it

- **Medo-Persia's counterfeit: works-based purification without blood**
  - "And after thee shall arise another kingdom inferior to thee" (Dan 2:39)
  - Zoroastrianism — the religion of Persia — introduced cosmic dualism: two equal forces, good and evil, endlessly balanced
  - purification through fire, not blood — fire worship replaced the altar
  - righteousness achieved by weighing good deeds against evil deeds
  - no substitute, no sacrifice — human effort as the means of acceptance
  - [→] Dualism makes the controversy a stalemate between equals. The Bible makes it a settled outcome: Christ has already won.

- **where it lives today**
  - salvation by works — good deeds outweighing bad on some cosmic scale
  - moral improvement as the path to God's acceptance
  - any gospel that says "do enough good and God will accept you"
  - purification through ritual, penance, or human effort instead of through the blood of Christ

[Q] **"Isn't obedience important?"**

> Absolutely — but obedience is the fruit of salvation, not the root. Medo-Persia's error was not valuing good works — it was making them the payment instead of the response. The sanctuary never taught "do good and live." It taught "a substitute dies so that you may live and then do good."

- **Christ restores the true thing**
  - "But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God" (Heb 10:12)
  - "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified" (Heb 10:14)
  - One sacrifice. Once. Forever. No fire ritual, no scales, no earning — finished.

## Greece — The Bronze Belly: Counterfeit Immortality

- **recap: what Study 2 established about death**
  - Only God possesses immortality (1 Tim 6:16) — man's life was sustained by access to the tree, not inherent
  - The dead know nothing (Eccl 9:5); death is sleep (John 11:11-14); the soul is the whole living person, not an entity inside the body (Gen 2:7)
  - The resurrection is the only hope — without it, "they also which are fallen asleep in Christ are perished" (1 Cor 15:18)
  - The serpent's first lie — "ye shall not surely die" (Gen 3:4) — is the seed of every false doctrine about death

- **Greece made the lie respectable**
  - "And after thee shall arise... another third kingdom of brass, which shall bear rule over all the earth" (Dan 2:39)
  - Greek philosophy — Plato in particular — taught that the soul is inherently immortal
  - Death recast as liberation — the soul escaping the prison of the body
  - "Ye shall not surely die" — now it sounds like Socrates instead of a snake
  - [→] Satan told the first lie in a garden. Greece gave it an academy.

- **where it lives today**
  - the immortal soul doctrine is embedded in most of Christianity
  - prayers to dead saints, eternal conscious torment, death as "going home"
  - every one of these builds on the assumption that the dead are conscious — the assumption Scripture denies

- **Christ restores the true thing**
  - "For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first" (1 Thess 4:16)
  - The resurrection — not a disembodied departure — is the hope. Greece buried it. Christ restores it.

## Rome — The Iron Legs: Counterfeit Worship

- **the sanctuary truth: the seventh-day Sabbath as Creator's memorial**
  - "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work: but the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God" (Ex 20:8-10)
  - "For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it" (Ex 20:11)
  - The Sabbath is the seal of the Creator embedded in the heart of the law — identifying WHO is worshipped (the LORD), WHAT He did (created), and WHAT is His territory (heaven and earth)

- **Rome's counterfeit: sun worship absorbed into Christianity**
  - "And the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron: forasmuch as iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things: and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise" (Dan 2:40)
  - Mithraism — the dominant religion of Roman soldiers — worshipped the sun god on Dies Solis (Day of the Sun)
  - Constantine's Edict of 321 AD — "On the venerable Day of the Sun let the magistrates and people residing in cities rest"
  - the merger: paganism was not destroyed — it was baptized
  - Sunday sacredness entered Christianity not through Scripture but through imperial decree and gradual custom
  - [→] Iron does not reason — it crushes. Rome did not argue the Sabbath away; it legislated it away.

- **God's assessment of sun worship**
  - "And he brought me into the inner court of the LORD's house, and, behold, at the door of the temple of the LORD, between the porch and the altar, were about five and twenty men, with their backs toward the temple of the LORD, and their faces toward the east; and they worshipped the sun toward the east" (Ezek 8:16)
  - "And he said unto me, Hast thou seen this, O son of man?... they have filled the land with violence, and have returned to provoke me to anger" (Ezek 8:17)
  - Sun worship in the very court of God's temple — called abomination
  - [→] The location matters: this is not pagans in a pagan temple. These are God's people, in God's house, facing the wrong direction.

[TANGENT] The day was never neutral territory. God blessed and sanctified the seventh day at Creation — before sin, before Israel, before Sinai (Gen 2:2-3). The Sabbath is not Jewish; it is Edenic. It belongs to the human race because it was given to the human race. Satan's counterfeit targets the one commandment that begins with "Remember" — the one most likely to be forgotten.

- **where it lives today**
  - Sunday sacredness as assumed Christian practice
  - no biblical command for Sunday worship exists — the change was institutional, not scriptural
  - "In vain they do worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of men" (Matt 15:9)

- **Christ restores the true thing**
  - "For the Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day" (Matt 12:8)
  - "If ye love me, keep my commandments" (John 14:15)
  - "And he came to Nazareth, where he had been brought up: and, as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day" (Luke 4:16)
  - The Sabbath is not abolished — it is restored to its original meaning: rest in the Creator, trust in the Redeemer, memorial of the God who made you and is remaking you

## The Complete Counterfeit Sanctuary

- **four empires, one system — each layer built on the previous**
  - Babylon: replaced the Priest — who you go through
  - Medo-Persia: replaced the Sacrifice — how you are saved
  - Greece: replaced the Truth about death — what you hope for
  - Rome: replaced the Sabbath — whom you worship and when
  - stack them together and you have a complete counterfeit sanctuary: wrong mediator, wrong means of salvation, wrong understanding of death, wrong day of worship

- **the statue is one image — the system is one system**
  - "Mystery, Babylon the Great, the Mother of Harlots and Abominations of the Earth" (Rev 17:5)
  - Revelation calls the final system "Babylon" — because it carries all four layers
  - [→] Any institution that holds all four counterfeits is not a random collection of errors. It is the completed statue.

- **every counterfeit serves the accuser's charges**
  - Study 1 filed two charges: "They only obey for reward" (Charge A) and "The law cannot be kept" (Charge B)
  - Replace the Priest → people can't access God directly → Charge B reinforced (you can't know God, so you can't bear His image)
  - Replace the Sacrifice → salvation becomes transactional → Charge A reinforced (they serve God for what they get)
  - Replace the Truth about death → no real judgment, no accountability → both charges shielded from exposure
  - Replace the Sabbath → the law is changeable → Charge B confirmed (if God's law can be altered, it was never the standard)
  - [→] The counterfeit system is not random error. It is Satan's charges institutionalized as doctrine. Study 9 will make this explicit — but notice it here: each layer of the statue serves the accuser's case.

- **but the stone strikes**
  - "And in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed: and the kingdom shall not be left to other people, but it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever" (Dan 2:44)
  - The stone is "cut out without hands" — divine, not human
  - It does not reform the statue — it destroys it
  - [→] You cannot fix a counterfeit system. You can only replace it with the original.

## The Test

- **one question exposes every counterfeit**
  - "To the law and to the testimony: if they speak not according to this word, it is because there is no light in them" (Isa 8:20)
  - Applied: Does this teaching point to Christ in heaven, or to an earthly substitute?
  - Does it direct you to a human priest — or to Christ your High Priest?
  - Does it teach you to earn salvation — or to receive a finished sacrifice?
  - Does it tell you the dead are alive — or that Christ will raise them?
  - Does it honor a day man appointed — or the day God blessed?
  - [→] Four questions. Four counterfeits. One diagnostic. Every doctrine must answer to Isaiah 8:20.

## Appeal

- The counterfeits are not ancient history — they are present reality
- Babylon's priesthood still stands between sinners and Christ
- Persia's scales still weigh human works against divine grace
- Greece's lie still tells the dying they will not really die
- Rome's sun-day still displaces the Creator's Sabbath
- But the stone is coming — cut without hands, unstoppable, final
- The question is not whether the counterfeits will fall — Daniel 2:44 settles that
- The question is whether you are standing on the statue or on the Rock
- "To the law and to the testimony" — let every doctrine be tested, and let every counterfeit be abandoned
