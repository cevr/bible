# Study 2: The Bridge — Destroyed and Restored

**The witnesses fell. The bridge between heaven and earth collapsed. But before the dust settled — before Adam even finished hiding — God was already walking toward him. The same study that shows you what sin destroyed will show you what God rebuilt.**

## Prep Reading

- Genesis 2:7; 3:1-24
- Ecclesiastes 9:5-6
- 1 Corinthians 15:16-22, 51-55
- Genesis 28:10-17
- Exodus 25:8-9
- Hebrews 8:1-5; 10:19-20

---

## The Witnesses Fall

- **Satan's strategy — attack trust, not God**
  - He cannot confront God directly — so he targets God's representatives
  - "Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?" (Gen 3:1)
  - Not a frontal assault — a question. Reframes God's generous provision as restriction.
  - "Ye shall not surely die" (Gen 3:4) — direct contradiction of God's plain statement (Gen 2:17)
  - "Ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil" (Gen 3:5) — godhood without God, knowledge without obedience
  - [→] Satan's first recorded words in Scripture are a distortion of God's words — his method has never changed

[DYK🔎] The serpent's promise — "ye shall be as gods" (Gen 3:5) — mirrors Satan's own ambition: "I will be like the most High" (Isa 14:14). He recruited humanity into his own rebellion by offering them his own aspiration.

- **Senses overrode revelation**
  - "And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it was pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make one wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat" (Gen 3:6)
  - Three appeals: appetite ("good for food"), aesthetics ("pleasant to the eyes"), ambition ("desired to make one wise")
  - [→] Compare 1 John 2:16 — "the lust of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life" — same pattern, same enemy, same strategy across millennia

- **Trust transferred from God to self**
  - Eve had God's word: "thou shalt surely die" (Gen 2:17)
  - She had the serpent's word: "ye shall not surely die" (Gen 3:4)
  - She chose to judge for herself — sight over faith, sensation over revelation
  - The fall was not primarily an act of appetite — it was an act of independence

- **Fig leaves — humanity's first religion**
  - "And the eyes of them both were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together, and made themselves aprons" (Gen 3:7)
  - Immediate awareness of exposure, shame, vulnerability
  - Immediate response: self-effort to cover what only God can heal
  - [→] Fig leaves are the prototype of every human system that tries to solve a divine problem with human resources — moralism, ritual, philosophy, self-improvement
  - The covering didn't work. It never does.

## The Broken Connection

- **Fellowship replaced by fear**
  - "And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden" (Gen 3:8)
  - Before the fall: communion with God was natural, desired, life-giving
  - After the fall: the same Presence that was home became terror
  - They hid — not because God changed, but because they did

- **Driven out — the bridge severed**
  - "Therefore the LORD God sent him forth from the garden of Eden, to till the ground from whence he was taken" (Gen 3:23)
  - "So he drove out the man; and he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life" (Gen 3:24)
  - The way back was blocked — not to be cruel, but to prevent eternal existence in a sinful state
  - [→] Immortality in sin would be endless suffering, not mercy. The cherubim guarded humanity from a fate worse than death.

- **The separation defined**
  - "But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear" (Isa 59:2)
  - "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned" (Rom 5:12)
  - "For the wages of sin is death" (Rom 6:23)
  - Death is not an arbitrary punishment God invented — it is the natural consequence of severing from the Source of life
  - Cut a branch from the vine and it dies — not because the vine punishes it, but because the vine was its life

- **Death enters creation**
  - Before sin there was no death — "God made not death" (Wisdom 1:13, cf. Rom 5:12)
  - God had warned plainly: "In the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die" (Gen 2:17)
  - Satan's counter-testimony: "Ye shall not surely die" (Gen 3:4) — the first lie. And the most consequential, because it redefined death itself.
  - The moment Adam sinned, the process began — not instant annihilation, but separation from the Source of life, with death as the inevitable terminus
  - [→] Every false doctrine about death traces back to this single lie in Eden. If the serpent can redefine what dying means, he can rebuild everything downstream.

- **Satan's claim of victory**
  - The witnesses are corrupted — they chose Satan's word over God's
  - Satan now claims the loyalty of earth's representatives
  - "The prince of this world" (John 12:31) — a title earned by conquest, not creation
  - Adam was given dominion (Gen 1:28); Adam surrendered that dominion to Satan through obedience to him
  - Satan leverages this claim: "All this power will I give thee, and the glory of them: for that is delivered unto me" (Luke 4:6)
  - [→] Satan didn't steal the earth — Adam handed it over. The dominion was legally transferred by allegiance.

[TANGENT] In the book of Job, Satan appears among the "sons of God" — apparently as earth's representative (Job 1:6-7). When God asks where he's been, Satan answers: "From going to and fro in the earth, and from walking up and down in it." He speaks as one who owns the territory. This is the usurped dominion in action.

- **What was lost**
  - Direct presence of God — face to face communion
  - The image of God in humanity — marred, not erased, but devastated
  - Dominion over the earth — surrendered to the enemy
  - Access to the tree of life — immortality forfeited
  - Peace — replaced by fear, shame, blame (Gen 3:10-12)
  - "In consequence of sin, communion between heaven and earth was broken." (DA 115.2)
  - [→] The fall did not break one thing — it broke everything

[Q] **"Does God kill sinners, or does sin kill sinners?"**
→ Both truths exist in Scripture, but the foundation is this: God is life (John 14:6; Acts 17:28). Separation from life is death. Sin separates. Therefore sin kills. God's "punishment" is ultimately allowing the natural consequence to unfold — the withdrawal of sustaining grace from those who have permanently rejected it.

## What Death Really Is — The Bible's Teaching

- **Only God possesses immortality**
  - "Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto" (1 Tim 6:16)
  - Immortality is not a human birthright — it is a divine attribute
  - Man was given access to the tree of life (Gen 2:9) — immortality was sustained, not inherent
  - When access was cut off (Gen 3:24), the sustaining source was removed
  - [→] If humanity were inherently immortal, there would have been no need for a tree of life. The tree proves the point: life was a gift, not a possession.

- **The dead know nothing**
  - "For the living know that they shall die: but the dead know not any thing, neither have they any more a reward; for the memory of them is forgotten. Also their love, and their hatred, and their envy, is now perished; neither have they any more a portion for ever in any thing that is done under the sun" (Eccl 9:5-6)
  - "His breath goeth forth, he returneth to his earth; in that very day his thoughts perish" (Ps 146:4)
  - "The dead praise not the LORD, neither any that go down into silence" (Ps 115:17)
  - Not conscious in heaven. Not suffering in hell. Not hovering over the living. Silent. Asleep. Waiting.

- **Death is sleep — Jesus' own word for it**
  - "Our friend Lazarus sleepeth; but I go, that I may awake him out of sleep... Then said Jesus unto them plainly, Lazarus is dead" (John 11:11, 14)
  - Jesus equates death with sleep — unconscious, temporary, awaiting a waking
  - "And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life" (Dan 12:2)
  - "We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed" (1 Cor 15:51)
  - Paul uses the same language: "them also which sleep in Jesus will God bring with him" (1 Thess 4:14)
  - [→] The Bible never calls death a doorway to another conscious existence. It calls it sleep — every time.

[Q] **"What about 'absent from the body, present with the Lord' (2 Cor 5:8)?"**
→ Paul expresses a desire — he would _prefer_ to be with the Lord rather than in this mortal body. But he doesn't describe the mechanism or timing. In 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17, he does — and it's the resurrection, not a disembodied departure. Paul's longing is real; his theology places the fulfillment at the Second Coming, not at the moment of death.

- **What happens at death — creation in reverse**
  - "And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul" (Gen 2:7)
  - Dust + breath = living soul. The soul is not a separate entity placed inside the body — it is the whole person, alive.
  - "Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was: and the spirit shall return unto God who gave it" (Eccl 12:7)
  - The "spirit" (רוּחַ, _ruach_) that returns to God is the breath of life — the animating power, not a conscious personality
  - The same word is used for animals: "They have all one breath" (Eccl 3:19)
  - [→] A lightbulb has electricity flowing through it. Turn off the power and the light ceases — but the electricity doesn't float away as a ghost-light. The breath returns to God; the person sleeps until resurrection.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word _nephesh_ (נֶפֶשׁ), translated "soul" in Genesis 2:7, is the same word used for animals in Genesis 1:20-21, 24. It means "living creature" — not "immortal spirit." The idea of an immortal soul as a separate entity inside the body comes from Greek philosophy (Plato), not from the Hebrew Scriptures.

- **The serpent's lie — still the foundation of every counterfeit**
  - "Ye shall not surely die" (Gen 3:4) — the first lie told on earth
  - If the dead are conscious, they can be contacted — ancestor worship, necromancy, spiritualism
  - If the dead are conscious, they can intercede — prayers to dead saints
  - If the dead go immediately to reward or punishment — purgatory, immediate heaven or hell, and the entire doctrine of eternal torment
  - If the soul is inherently immortal — then God is a torturer who keeps sinners alive forever to suffer
  - [→] One lie — "ye shall not surely die" — produced an entire system of doctrines that distort God's character. We will see in Study 4 how Greece made this lie respectable.

[Q] **"But doesn't eternal fire mean eternal burning?"**
→ "Even as Sodom and Gomorrha... are set forth for an example, suffering the vengeance of eternal fire" (Jude 7). Sodom is not still burning. The fire is "eternal" in its results, not its duration. "Everlasting punishment" (Matt 25:46) is everlasting in consequence — death that never reverses — not everlasting in process. God is not an infinite torturer. He is a just judge whose final sentence is permanent death — "the second death" (Rev 20:14) — not perpetual agony.

- **The only hope — resurrection**
  - "For if the dead rise not, then is not Christ raised: and if Christ be not raised, your faith is vain; ye are yet in your sins. Then they also which are fallen asleep in Christ are perished" (1 Cor 15:16-18)
  - Paul's logic is devastating: if there is no resurrection, the dead in Christ have _perished_. If they had immortal souls already in heaven, his argument makes no sense.
  - "For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first" (1 Thess 4:16)
  - The dead are not already with Christ — they are _raised_ to be with Him at His coming
  - "For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality" (1 Cor 15:53)
  - Immortality is _put on_ at the resurrection — future tense. We do not already have it.
  - [→] The resurrection is not a formality for people already in heaven. It is the event. It is the hope. Without it, Paul says, we are of all men most miserable (1 Cor 15:19).

[ILL] If you were told a prisoner had already been released and was home safe, you would never rally to break open the prison doors. That is exactly what the immortal soul doctrine does to the resurrection — it makes Christ's greatest act unnecessary. Why would He come back to raise the dead if the dead are already alive?

## God Moves First

- **God seeks the sinner before the sinner seeks God**
  - "And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou?" (Gen 3:9)
  - Not accusation — invitation
  - Adam hid; God pursued
  - The pattern of all salvation: God initiates, man responds

[ILL] A child breaks a lamp and hides under the bed. The father walks through the house calling the child's name. The father is not searching because he doesn't know — he's calling because the child needs to come out of hiding. "Where art thou?" is not an interrogation. It is an invitation.

- **The protoevangelium — first promise of redemption**
  - "And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel" (Gen 3:15)
  - Spoken to the serpent — God announces Satan's defeat before addressing Adam's punishment
  - The seed of the woman = Christ (Gal 4:4)
  - Bruise his head = fatal, decisive victory
  - Bruise his heel = the cross — painful, but not final

- **The first sacrifice — blood before law**
  - "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them" (Gen 3:21)
  - Skins require death — an animal died
  - God performed the first sacrifice; Adam did not
  - Fig leaves (man's covering) replaced by skins (God's covering)
  - [→] The first death in creation was not punishment — it was provision

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for "atonement" (כָּפַר, _kaphar_) means "to cover." Before the tabernacle, before Sinai, before the priesthood — God was already covering sin with blood. The sanctuary system formalized what Eden inaugurated.

> "The instant man accepted the temptations of Satan... a Saviour was provided." (RH, March 12, 1901)

## Jacob's Ladder — The Bridge Restored in Vision

- **The setting — a fugitive at rock bottom**
  - Jacob fleeing from Esau, guilty of deception
  - Alone, night falling, a stone for a pillow (Gen 28:11)
  - No merit, no temple, no priest — just a sinner in the dirt

- **The vision — heaven reconnected to earth**
  - "And he dreamed, and behold a ladder set up on the earth, and the top of it reached to heaven: and behold the angels of God ascending and descending on it" (Gen 28:12)
  - The ladder bridges the gap that sin created
  - Angels ascending and descending — active, ongoing ministry between heaven and earth
  - Communication restored; the connection is not theoretical but operational

- **God stands above it — He initiates**
  - "And, behold, the LORD stood above it, and said, I am the LORD God of Abraham thy father, and the God of Isaac" (Gen 28:13)
  - God identifies Himself by covenant relationship
  - He speaks promise, not condemnation, to a man who just lied and cheated his way out of the family home

- **Jacob's response — "the house of God"**
  - "How dreadful is this place! this is none other but the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven" (Gen 28:17)
  - "House of God" — sanctuary language before any sanctuary existed
  - "Gate of heaven" — the access point, the entry, the bridge
  - [→] Jacob names the place Bethel — "house of God." The sanctuary concept is revealed in a dream before it is built in a desert.

- **Christ declares Himself the ladder**
  - "Hereafter ye shall see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of man" (John 1:51)
  - Jesus directly claims Jacob's ladder as Himself
  - He is the bridge between heaven and earth
  - Not a structure — a Person

> "The ladder represents Jesus, the appointed medium of communication." (PP 184.2)

[Q] **"If the ladder is Christ, why build a physical sanctuary at all?"**
→ Because God teaches through the concrete. Abstract truth must be made tangible — especially for a people emerging from 400 years of Egyptian idolatry. The sanctuary is the ladder made visible, touchable, daily.

## The Sanctuary — God's Bridge Made Visible

- **The purpose — "that I may dwell among them"**
  - "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them" (Ex 25:8)
  - Not "dwell in it" — "dwell among **them**"
  - The building is the means; the people are the goal
  - Sin drove God out of direct contact (Gen 3:24); the sanctuary brings Him back — veiled, mediated, but present

- **Built after a pattern — earthly copy of heavenly reality**
  - "According to all that I shew thee, after the pattern of the tabernacle, and the pattern of all the instruments thereof, even so shall ye make it" (Ex 25:9)
  - "Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount" (Heb 8:5)
  - Moses received the blueprint on Sinai — this is not human invention
  - The earthly sanctuary was never the point — it was the curriculum. The classroom is not the truth; it teaches the truth.

- **Every element is Christ**
  - The Lamb — "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" (John 1:29). Every morning and evening sacrifice (Ex 29:38-39) — a daily pointer to the cross.
  - The Bread — "I am the bread of life" (John 6:35). Twelve loaves of showbread, set fresh each Sabbath (Lev 24:5-8) — sustenance in God's presence.
  - The Light — "I am the light of the world" (John 8:12). Seven-branched lampstand, the only light in the Holy Place (Ex 25:31-37) — the Spirit's unceasing ministry.
  - The Incense — "He ever liveth to make intercession for them" (Heb 7:25). Burned morning and evening on the golden altar (Ex 30:7-8) — Christ's righteousness making our prayers acceptable.
  - The High Priest — "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2). He bore Israel's names on his breastplate over his heart (Ex 28:29) — Christ carries His people.
  - [→] There is no room in this building that is not about Jesus.

[ILL] Imagine walking through the sanctuary gate. You see the altar — that's the cross. You see the laver — that's baptism and cleansing. You step inside and see bread — that's Christ feeding you. You see light — that's Christ guiding you. You smell incense — that's Christ praying for you. You look toward the veil — behind it, a High Priest stands in your name.

[TANGENT] The veil separating the Holy from the Most Holy Place was embroidered with cherubim (Ex 26:31) — the same cherubim that guarded the way to the tree of life (Gen 3:24). The veil is the barrier sin created. When Christ died, "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:51). From the top — God tore it, not man. The barrier is removed.

## The Way Is Open Now

- **Christ entered the greater tabernacle**
  - "But Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands... neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption for us" (Heb 9:11-12)
  - Not animal blood — His own
  - Not an earthly copy — the heavenly reality
  - Not repeatedly — once, with permanent effect

- **A new and living way**
  - "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" (Heb 10:19-20)
  - "Boldness" — not timidity, not uncertainty
  - "Through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" — Christ's broken body is the open door

- **Come boldly — the throne of grace**
  - "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need" (Heb 4:16)
  - The throne is not a courtroom bench — it is a throne of **grace**
  - Mercy for past failure; grace for present need
  - "In time of need" — not after you've cleaned yourself up, but when you need it most
  - [→] The bridge is open. The High Priest is there. The invitation stands.

[Q] **"If the way is already open, why does it matter that I understand the sanctuary?"**
→ Because understanding the sanctuary is understanding Christ's current ministry — what He is doing for you right now. A gift unopened is still a gift, but it does you no good. "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" (Hosea 4:6). The sanctuary reveals not just what Christ did at the cross but what He does at the throne.

## Appeal

The bridge between heaven and earth was shattered by sin. But God did not wait for you to rebuild it — He moved first.

He called in the garden. He promised at the tree. He sacrificed at the gate. He showed Jacob the ladder and told Moses to build the pattern. Every lamb, every flame, every grain of incense whispered one name: **Christ**.

And now the type has met the antitype — Christ Himself ministers in the heavenly sanctuary. The veil is torn. The way is open. The High Priest knows your name.

Will you come boldly?
