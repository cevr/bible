# Study 16: The Time of Trouble and Deliverance

**Probation has closed. The seal is set. Christ stands up from His mediatorial throne. What follows is the darkest hour earth has ever known — and the brightest morning. The seven last plagues fall on those who chose the mark. God's people stand without a mediator — not without Christ's righteousness, but without ongoing intercession. And then He comes.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 12:1
- Revelation 15:1, 5-8
- Revelation 16:1-21
- Psalm 91
- 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17
- 1 Corinthians 15:51-55

---

## The Close of Probation — The Line Is Drawn

- **Christ stands up**
  - "And at that time shall Michael stand up, the great prince which standeth for the children of thy people" (Dan 12:1)
  - Michael = Christ (Jude 9; 1 Thess 4:16). He has been standing as Mediator in the heavenly sanctuary since the ascension. Now He stands _up_ — His mediatorial work is finished.
  - [→] This is the moment the Most Holy Place ministry ends. The Day of Atonement is complete. The cases are decided.

- **The final decree**
  - "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still" (Rev 22:11)
  - No more change of character. No more opportunity. The record is sealed.
  - [→] This is not arbitrary. God does not close probation while anyone still wants to respond. He closes it because every case has been decided — by the choices of the people themselves.

- **Standing without a mediator**
  - "Those who are living upon the earth when the intercession of Christ shall cease in the sanctuary above are to stand in the sight of a holy God without a mediator." (GC 425)
  - Not without Christ's righteousness — without ongoing intercession
  - The character work is complete. The seal is set. They stand because Christ's character has been so fully received that it has become their own.
  - [→] This is not terrifying if the work has been done. It is terrifying only if it hasn't. Study 14 showed how God conquers character territory. Studies 12-13 showed the sign and the temple. By this point, the work is finished — or it isn't.

## The Time of Jacob's Trouble

- **"A time of trouble, such as never was"**
  - "And there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation even to that same time: and at that time thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book" (Dan 12:1)
  - Notice: _delivered_. The promise is deliverance, not exemption. They go through it — they don't go around it.

- **Jacob's wrestling — the pattern told**
  - "Alas! for that day is great, so that none is like it: it is even the time of Jacob's trouble; but he shall be saved out of it" (Jer 30:7)
  - Jeremiah names it. Genesis tells the story.
  - Jacob had sinned — he had stolen the birthright by deception (Gen 27). Years later, returning home, he learned that Esau was coming with 400 men. His past was coming for him.
  - He sent his family across the brook. He was alone. And in the darkness, someone seized him.
  - "And Jacob was left alone; and there wrestled a man with him until the breaking of the day" (Gen 32:24)
  - All night he fought — not knowing who he wrestled. Terrified that Esau would destroy him. Terrified that his own sin had disqualified him from the promise.
  - "And when he saw that he prevailed not against him, he touched the hollow of his thigh; and the hollow of Jacob's thigh was out of joint" (Gen 32:25)
  - Wounded. Crippled. Unable to fight by strength. And then, in his weakness, Jacob did the only thing left: he held on.
  - "I will not let thee go, except thou bless me" (Gen 32:26)
  - The Angel asked: "What is thy name?" And he said: "Jacob" — _supplanter, deceiver_. He confessed who he was.
  - "Thy name shall be called no more Jacob, but Israel: for as a prince hast thou power with God and with men, and hast prevailed" (Gen 32:28)
  - A new name. A new identity. Not earned by strength — earned by refusing to let go.
  - "And Jacob called the name of the place Peniel: for I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved" (Gen 32:30)

- **The pattern applied**
  - God's people in the time of trouble experience the same anguish — not doubt of God's existence, but terror that their own past sin disqualifies them
  - Satan accuses them of every past failure. He presents every sin as proof that they don't belong to Christ.
  - They cannot fight by strength. They can only hold on: "I will not let thee go."
  - [→] The time of Jacob's trouble is not God testing whether He should save them — that is already decided (Rev 22:11). It is Satan's last attempt to make them _feel_ unsaved. The accuser makes his final argument. But the verdict is already in. They have been sealed. The name is written.

- **Both charges answered in the time of trouble**
  - **Charge A: "They only obey for reward"** — every external reward has been stripped. No buying. No selling. No social standing. Death decree hanging. And still they trust. "Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him" (Job 13:15). Satan said no one serves God without a bribe. The time of trouble proves him wrong — they serve God after every bribe is removed.
  - **Charge B: "The law cannot be kept"** — through the plagues, under the death decree, without a mediator, they keep the commandments of God. Not by their own strength but by the character of Christ fully reproduced in them. The law is kept under the worst conditions earth has ever seen.
  - [→] The time of trouble is not just suffering. It is the final courtroom exhibit. Study 1's charges are answered here — in real time, before the watching universe.

- **Job's trial — again**
  - Study 10 connected Job's trial to the judgment: God vindicating His people by display.
  - The time of trouble is Job 1-2 at global scale. Satan accuses. God's people endure. Heaven watches.
  - Job didn't know about the courtroom. Neither do they — not fully. Their faithfulness is authentic because it is not performed.

[Q] **"How can anyone survive without a mediator?"**

> Because the mediation has already done its work. Christ does not abandon His people — He ceases the specific work of intercession because that work is complete. A surgeon who has finished the operation does not leave the patient — he steps back because the surgery succeeded. Christ's character has been fully reproduced in them (COL 69.1). The seal is set. They do not stand on their own merit — they stand on the finished work of Christ written into their character. His righteousness is not imputed from outside at that point — it has become their own by the Spirit's transforming work. The mediator's work is complete, not absent. Christ is still their Savior. He is still present. He is preparing to come get them.

[TANGENT] The time to prepare for the time of trouble is not during it — it is now. "Daniel purposed in his heart" before the test (Dan 1:8). The three Hebrews knew their answer before the furnace (Dan 3:16-18). Preparation means: hiding God's Word in the heart so deeply that when the books are closed, the truth is already written within (Jer 31:33). Building the habit of trust in small trials so it holds in the great one. Surrendering cherished sins now, while the Mediator still ministers, rather than clinging to them until it is too late. The time of trouble does not build character — it reveals it.

## The Seven Last Plagues — Judgment Without Mixture

- **No more mercy mixed in**
  - "And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God" (Rev 15:1)
  - "And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled" (Rev 15:8)
  - No one can enter the temple — intercession has ceased. The plagues are unmixed with mercy.
  - [→] Throughout history, God's judgments have been tempered with mercy. The plagues are what happens when mercy's work is done and only justice remains.

- **The plagues summarized**

| Plague  | Target                       | Text                                                                                                        |
| ------- | ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1st** | Earth — sores                | "A noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast" (Rev 16:2)                       |
| **2nd** | Sea — blood                  | "The sea became as the blood of a dead man: and every living soul died in the sea" (Rev 16:3)               |
| **3rd** | Fresh water — blood          | "The rivers and fountains of waters became blood" (Rev 16:4)                                                |
| **4th** | Sun — scorching heat         | "Power was given unto him to scorch men with fire" (Rev 16:8)                                               |
| **5th** | Seat of the beast — darkness | "His kingdom was full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain" (Rev 16:10)                      |
| **6th** | Euphrates — dried up         | "The water thereof was dried up, that the way of the kings of the east might be prepared" (Rev 16:12)       |
| **7th** | Air — earthquake, hail       | "There was a great earthquake, such as was not since men were upon the earth... great hail" (Rev 16:18, 21) |

- **Notice: the plagues fall on those who have the mark**
  - Rev 16:2 is explicit: the first plague targets "men which had the mark of the beast"
  - The plagues are not random catastrophe. They are targeted judgment on a world that chose the counterfeit.
  - [→] God's people are in the world during the plagues — but not targeted by them. This is Egypt and the Exodus repeated: plagues fell on Egypt; Goshen was spared.

- **The Euphrates dries up — Babylon's support collapses**
  - The sixth plague: the river that sustained ancient Babylon dries up (Rev 16:12)
  - Babylon's support = the people who sustained the system. Their confidence collapses.
  - "The way of the kings of the east" is prepared — Christ comes from the east (Matt 24:27). The path is cleared for deliverance.

## Psalm 91 — The Shelter in the Storm

- **The promise to those who dwell in the secret place**
  - "He that dwelleth in the secret place of the most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty" (Ps 91:1)
  - "Surely he shall deliver thee from the snare of the fowler, and from the noisome pestilence" (Ps 91:3)
  - "A thousand shall fall at thy side, and ten thousand at thy right hand; but it shall not come nigh thee" (Ps 91:7)
  - "There shall no evil befall thee, neither shall any plague come nigh thy dwelling" (Ps 91:10)
  - "For he shall give his angels charge over thee, to keep thee in all thy ways" (Ps 91:11)

- **The condition: dwelling**
  - The promise is to those who _dwell_ — not visit. Not occasionally retreat. Dwell. Abide. Live there.
  - [→] The secret place is not a location found during the crisis. It is a relationship built before it. Those who dwell with God in the ordinary days are the ones sheltered in the extraordinary ones.

- **What the promise means — and what it doesn't**
  - Psalm 91 does not promise that God's people will experience no suffering. Jacob wrestled all night and walked away limping.
  - It promises that the plagues — which are targeted judgment on the mark-bearers — will not touch those who are sealed.
  - It promises that God is present, that angels guard, that deliverance comes.
  - [→] The shelter is not comfort — it is companionship. "I will be with him in trouble" (Ps 91:15). Not "I will remove the trouble" but "I will be in it with you."

[ILL] A child in a thunderstorm does not stop hearing the thunder when his father holds him. The storm is still there. But the fear is gone — because the grip is real. Psalm 91 is the Father's grip in the storm.

## Deliverance — He Comes

- **Every eye sees Him**
  - "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they also which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him" (Rev 1:7)
  - "For as the lightning cometh out of the east, and shineth even unto the west; so shall also the coming of the Son of man be" (Matt 24:27)
  - Not secret. Not spiritual. Not symbolic. Visible. Audible. Global.
  - [→] Every counterfeit "coming" is local, secret, or spiritual. The real one is unmistakable.

- **The shout, the trumpet, the resurrection**
  - "For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord" (1 Thess 4:16-17)
  - The dead are raised. The living are transformed. Both are gathered.
  - "In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed" (1 Cor 15:52)

- **The body glorified — the image completed**
  - "Who shall change our vile body, that it may be fashioned like unto his glorious body" (Phil 3:21)
  - "This corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality" (1 Cor 15:53)
  - Study 5 mapped the sanctuary path: justification (courtyard), sanctification (holy place), glorification (most holy place). The character was glorified at sealing. Now the body catches up.
  - [→] The image of God was broken in two dimensions: character and body. The seal restored the character. The Second Coming restores the body. The image is fully complete.

- **The response of the saved**
  - "And it shall be said in that day, Lo, this is our God; we have waited for him, and he will save us: this is the LORD; we have waited for him, we will be glad and rejoice in his salvation" (Isa 25:9)
  - "We have waited for him" — the patience of the saints (Rev 14:12). The endurance that outlasted the crisis.
  - [→] This is the moment every study has been building toward. The cosmic trial began in Study 1. The Way Home was mapped in Study 5. The crisis was endured in Studies 15-16. And now: "Lo, this is our God."

- **The response of the lost**
  - "And the kings of the earth, and the great men, and the rich men, and the chief captains, and the mighty men, and every bondman, and every free man, hid themselves in the dens and in the rocks of the mountains; and said to the mountains and rocks, Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne, and from the wrath of the Lamb" (Rev 6:15-16)
  - The same Lamb who offered mercy now brings justice. The same face they could have beheld for transformation (2 Cor 3:18) they now beg to be hidden from.
  - [→] The difference between the saved and the lost at the Second Coming is not information — both see the same Christ. The difference is what they did with the invitation while it was open.

- **"O death, where is thy sting?"**
  - "Then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, Death is swallowed up in victory. O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?" (1 Cor 15:54-55)
  - Death — the curse of Eden, the consequence of sin, the weapon of the accuser — is swallowed up.
  - The Way Home passes through death and comes out the other side.

## Appeal

- The time of trouble is coming. Daniel said so. Jeremiah said so. Jesus said so.
- But it comes with a promise: "at that time thy people shall be delivered" (Dan 12:1)
- The plagues fall on those who chose the mark. The shelter covers those who dwell in the secret place.
- You will not stand alone. Psalm 91 is not a wish — it is a covenant promise to those who abide.
- Jacob wrestled all night and received a new name. God's people will wrestle — and be delivered.
- And then He comes. Not secretly. Not symbolically. With a shout, a trumpet, and the voice of the archangel.
- The dead rise. The living are changed. The body is glorified. The image is complete.
- "Lo, this is our God; we have waited for him."
- The question is not whether He will come. The question is whether you will be waiting.
