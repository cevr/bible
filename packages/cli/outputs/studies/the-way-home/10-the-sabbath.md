# Study 10: The Sabbath — God's Seal in Time

**Study 3 laid down the chain: Image = Character = Law = Love = God. Salvation is the restoration of every link. The Sabbath is the sign of that restoration — the only truth embedded in time itself, woven into the fabric of the week at creation, before sin, before Israel, before the law was written on stone. It is the sign of creation, the memorial of redemption, the mark of sanctification, and the seal of the finished image. Satan attacked it because it carries all four.**

## Prep Reading

- Genesis 2:1-3
- Exodus 20:8-11
- Ezekiel 20:12, 20
- Isaiah 58:13-14
- Mark 2:27-28
- Hebrews 4:4-11

## Before Sin, Before Israel, Before Sinai

- **The Sabbath is older than the fall**
  - "And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made" (Gen 2:2-3)
  - Three acts: rested, blessed, sanctified. God did not merely stop working — He set the day apart.
  - No Jew existed. No commandment had been spoken. No sin had entered.
  - [→] The Sabbath was not given to a nation. It was built into creation itself. It belongs to humanity, not to Israel.

- **Made for man — not man for the Sabbath**
  - "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath" (Mark 2:27)
  - Christ says "man" — not "Jew." The Sabbath was made at the same time man was made: creation week.
  - It is a gift, not a burden. A celebration, not a restriction.
  - [→] If the Sabbath was made for man at creation, then it was made for all men, for all time. Its scope is as universal as the humanity it was designed to serve.

- **Why "Remember"?**
  - "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy" (Ex 20:8)
  - No other commandment begins with "remember." Why this one?
  - Because it was already in place. God did not institute the Sabbath at Sinai — He reminded them of it.
  - [→] "Remember" implies prior knowledge. You cannot remember what you never knew. The Sabbath existed before the tablets were carved.

[DYK🔎] The word "sanctified" in Genesis 2:3 is the Hebrew _qadash_ (קָדַשׁ) — to set apart, to make holy. The same word is used for the sanctuary (Ex 29:44) and for the people God calls His own (Lev 20:8). God sanctified a day before He sanctified a place or a people. Time was holy before space was.

## The Seal Inside the Law

- **A seal carries three elements: name, title, territory**
  - Every royal seal identifies who, what authority, and over what domain
  - Nine of the ten commandments could be issued by any ruler — "thou shalt not kill" needs no divine signature
  - Only the fourth commandment contains all three elements of a seal:

| Element       | Text                                                 | Reference |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | --------- |
| **Name**      | "the LORD thy God"                                   | Ex 20:10  |
| **Title**     | "made" (Creator)                                     | Ex 20:11  |
| **Territory** | "heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is" | Ex 20:11  |

- **Remove the fourth commandment and the law is unsigned**
  - Any god could claim the other nine. Only the Creator God can claim the Sabbath.
  - [→] This is why the first angel's message echoes the fourth commandment: "worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters" (Rev 14:7). The final call to worship uses Sabbath language because the Sabbath identifies which God you worship.

- **The Sabbath is a perpetual covenant**
  - "It is a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever: for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed" (Ex 31:17)
  - "Perpetual covenant" — same language as the rainbow (Gen 9:16), circumcision (Gen 17:13), and the Levitical priesthood (Num 25:13)
  - [→] God does not make perpetual covenants and then quietly retire them.

## Four Meanings — One Day

The Sabbath is not one thing. It is four things layered into a single institution. Each layer answers a different question in the cosmic trial.

### 1. Sign of Creation — Who Made You

- "For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day" (Ex 20:11)
- The Sabbath is a weekly memorial of origins — a reminder that you were designed, not accidental
- Every Sabbath declares: there is a Creator, and He made you in His image
- [→] This is the foundation. If there is no Creator, there is no image. If there is no image, there is nothing to restore. The Sabbath anchors the entire plan of salvation to creation.

### 2. Memorial of Redemption — Who Freed You

- "And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt, and that the LORD thy God brought thee out thence through a mighty hand and by a stretched out arm: therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the sabbath day" (Deut 5:15)
- Exodus restates the Sabbath commandment — but adds a second reason: deliverance
- The same God who created you is the God who redeems you
- [→] The Sabbath looks back to creation AND to the cross. One day, two acts of divine power: making you and remaking you.

### 3. Sign of Sanctification — Who Is Changing You

- "Moreover also I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the LORD that sanctify them" (Eze 20:12)
- "Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it is a sign between me and you throughout your generations; that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you" (Ex 31:13)
- The Sabbath is the sign that God is doing the work of transformation — not you
- Sanctification is God's work, not human effort. The Sabbath says: rest. He is doing it.

- **Connect this to the chain:**
  - Study 3: Image = Character = Law = Love = Knowing God = Life
  - Sanctification = the restoration of God's image = His character being reproduced in you
  - The Sabbath is the sign of _that_. Not merely the sign that you're observing a day — the sign that the entire chain is being rebuilt in you.
  - "I am the LORD that sanctify them" — I am the one restoring My image. The Sabbath is My signature on the work in progress.
  - [→] Satan says the law cannot be kept, transformation is impossible. The Sabbath says: "I am the LORD that sanctify them." God signs His name to the work. Every Sabbath is a declaration that the image is being restored.

### 4. The Seal — Who Has Finished the Work in You

- Study 15 will show: "the fourth commandment contains all three elements of a seal: name, title, territory" (Rev 7:1-3)
- The seal is placed on those whose characters reflect God's own — the image fully restored
- The Sabbath is the sign of sanctification in process AND the seal of sanctification complete
- [→] Same institution, two phases. While the work is underway, the Sabbath is a sign. When the work is finished, it becomes the seal. The Sabbath bookends the entire journey from shattered image to restored image.

[ILL] A potter marks every piece with his studio's seal — but only after the kiln. The mark says: this was made here, by these hands, and the fire is finished. The Sabbath is God's potter's mark. He signs the work when it is done.

## Christ and the Sabbath

- **His custom**
  - "And he came to Nazareth, where he had been brought up: and, as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day, and stood up for to read" (Luke 4:16)
  - Not obligation. Custom. This was His rhythm.

- **Lord of the Sabbath**
  - "Therefore the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath" (Mark 2:28)
  - He did not say He was Lord over a cancelled institution. Lord = authority over. You don't claim authority over something you intend to abolish.
  - [→] Christ's claim is ownership, not termination. The Sabbath is His — He made it, He kept it, He rules it.

- **He expected it to continue**
  - "But pray ye that your flight be not in the winter, neither on the sabbath day" (Matt 24:20)
  - Speaking of the destruction of Jerusalem in AD 70 — decades after the cross
  - If the Sabbath was nailed to the cross, why would Christ tell His followers to pray about it 40 years later?
  - [→] Christ treated the Sabbath as binding before the cross, during His ministry, and after the cross in prophecy.

- **His followers kept it after the cross**
  - "And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment" (Luke 23:56)
  - The day between the crucifixion and the resurrection. Christ is in the tomb. His closest followers rest — "according to the commandment."
  - They did not understand the resurrection yet. But they understood the Sabbath.

## The Apostles and the Sabbath

- **Paul's manner**
  - "And Paul, as his manner was, went in unto them, and three sabbath days reasoned with them out of the scriptures" (Acts 17:2)
  - "And he reasoned in the synagogue every sabbath, and persuaded the Jews and the Greeks" (Acts 18:4)
  - Not a Jewish holdover — Paul was the apostle to the Gentiles, and his manner was Sabbath worship

- **Gentiles and the Sabbath**
  - "And when the Jews were gone out of the synagogue, the Gentiles besought that these words might be preached to them the next sabbath" (Acts 13:42)
  - "And the next sabbath day came almost the whole city together to hear the word of God" (Acts 13:44)
  - If Paul had taught Sunday worship, why would the Gentiles wait until the next Sabbath?

- **At Philippi — no synagogue, still Sabbath**
  - "And on the sabbath we went out of the city by a river side, where prayer was wont to be made" (Acts 16:13)
  - No synagogue in Philippi. Paul still observed the Sabbath — outdoors, by a river, with Gentile women.
  - [→] The Sabbath was not tied to the synagogue system. Paul kept it wherever he was, with whoever would listen — Jew or Gentile.

[Q] **"What about Colossians 2:16 — 'Let no man judge you in respect of sabbath days'?"**

> Paul is addressing ceremonial sabbaths — annual feast-day sabbaths tied to the sanctuary calendar (Lev 23:24, 32, 39). The context says these are "a shadow of things to come" (Col 2:17). The weekly Sabbath is not a shadow — it predates the sanctuary system by millennia. It was established at creation, before there was anything to foreshadow. Colossians 2 dismantles the ceremonial calendar, not the fourth commandment.

## Why Satan Attacked This Commandment

- **The Sabbath threatens both of Satan's charges**
  - Charge B: "The law cannot be kept" → The Sabbath is the sign that God sanctifies (Eze 20:12). It says transformation is real. The law _can_ be kept — because God does the keeping.
  - Charge A: "They only obey for reward" → The Sabbath is rest, not earning. It is the one commandment that requires you to _stop striving_. You cannot earn the Sabbath. You can only receive it.
  - [→] The Sabbath answers both charges simultaneously. No wonder it was the target.

- **Daniel 7:25 — the predicted attack**
  - "And he shall speak great words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws" (Dan 7:25)
  - "Times and laws" — the only commandment that involves both time and law is the fourth
  - The counterfeit system did not merely neglect the Sabbath — it replaced it with a different day and claimed divine authority for the change
  - [→] Studies 5-7 traced the counterfeit system's rise and its attack on God's truth. The Sabbath was the primary target because it is the seal of God's authority. Replace the seal and you replace the King.

- **The mark of the beast is the counterfeit of the seal of God**
  - Seal of God = Sabbath, the sign of the Creator's authority (Ex 20:8-11; Rev 7:1-3)
  - Mark of the beast = enforced counterfeit worship, the sign of a usurped authority (Rev 13:16-17)
  - The final crisis is not about a day. It is about authority — whose seal you bear, whose image you reflect.
  - [→] The Sabbath question is the allegiance question. It always was.

## The Sabbath on the New Earth

- **From creation to new creation — unbroken**
  - "For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me, saith the LORD, so shall your seed and your name remain. And it shall come to pass, that from one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the LORD" (Isa 66:22-23)
  - The Sabbath was kept in Eden before sin. It will be kept on the new earth after sin is destroyed.
  - If it existed before the fall and survives after the restoration, on what basis was it abolished in between?

- **Hebrews 4 — a rest that "remaineth"**
  - "There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God" (Heb 4:9)
  - The Greek is _sabbatismos_ — a Sabbath-keeping. The only time this word appears in the New Testament.
  - Not a metaphor for spiritual rest. A Sabbath-rest that "remaineth" — present tense, still in force.
  - "For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his" (Heb 4:10)
  - [→] Ceasing from your own works, as God ceased from His. The Sabbath is the weekly practice of the gospel: stop striving. Rest in what God has done. Trust His finished work.

[TANGENT] The Sabbath is the only commandment that is simultaneously a memorial (creation), a sign (sanctification), a seal (completed character), and a prophecy (new earth worship). No other institution in Scripture spans the entire arc from Genesis to Revelation. It is the thread that runs through the whole story.

## The Sabbath and the Image Restored

- **The chain from Study 3 runs through the Sabbath**
  - Image = Glory = Name = Character = Love = Law = Knowing God = Life
  - Sin shattered every link. The gospel repairs every link. And the Sabbath is the sign that the repair is underway.

- **Walk the chain:**
  - **Image**: God created man in His image (Gen 1:26-27). The Sabbath memorializes that creation — it points back to the week when the image was first stamped.
  - **Character = Law**: The law is a transcript of God's character (Study 3). The Sabbath sits at the center of that law as its seal. Remove the Sabbath and the law loses its signature; the character standard loses its Author.
  - **Love**: "God is love" (1 John 4:8). The Sabbath is a gift of love — time set apart not for production but for relationship. "Call the sabbath a delight" (Isa 58:13). It is the weekly practice of loving God, not earning from Him.
  - **Knowing God**: "That ye may know that I am the LORD your God" (Eze 20:20). The Sabbath exists so that you _know_ God — not know about Him, but know Him. And knowing God is eternal life (John 17:3).
  - **Sanctification = Image Restored**: "I am the LORD that sanctify them" (Eze 20:12). The Sabbath is the sign that God is restoring His own character in you — the image being rebuilt link by link.
  - [→] The Sabbath is not a standalone doctrine bolted onto the chain. It runs _through_ every link. It is the sign of the whole thing.

- **Beholding and resting**
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - Study 3 said: you become what you behold. The Sabbath is the day set apart for beholding. Cease from your own works. Gaze at the Creator. Let the Spirit do the transforming.
  - [→] The Sabbath is the weekly rhythm of the mechanism of salvation: stop striving, start beholding, be changed.

- **Rest as trust**
  - "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest" (Matt 11:28)
  - "For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his" (Heb 4:10)
  - The Sabbath enacts this invitation weekly. You stop. You trust. You rest in what God has done.
  - Satan says you must earn your standing. The Sabbath says: cease from your own works.
  - Satan says the image cannot be restored. The Sabbath says: I am the LORD that sanctify them.
  - [→] Sabbath-keeping is not legalism. It is the opposite of legalism — it is a weekly declaration that salvation is God's work, not yours. The image is restored by beholding, not by striving. The Sabbath is the day designed for exactly that.

## Appeal

- The Sabbath was there before sin entered — woven into the first week of creation
- It was there when God freed Israel from Egypt — a memorial of redemption
- It was there when Ezekiel wrote — a sign of sanctification
- It was there when Christ walked the earth — His custom, His claim, His authority
- It was there when Paul preached to Gentiles — his manner, in every city, every Sabbath
- It will be there on the new earth — from one Sabbath to another, all flesh worshiping the Creator

- Satan attacked this commandment because it carries the seal of God's authority — name, title, territory
- The counterfeit system replaced the day. Prophecy said it would (Dan 7:25). History confirms it did.
- The first angel calls the world back to Creator worship (Rev 14:7) — Sabbath language, Sabbath truth

- The Sabbath is not a burden. It is the gift of weekly rest in a God who creates, redeems, sanctifies, and seals.
- "If thou turn away thy foot from the sabbath, from doing thy pleasure on my holy day; and call the sabbath a delight, the holy of the LORD, honourable... then shalt thou delight thyself in the LORD" (Isa 58:13-14)

- Call it what God calls it: a delight. And keep it as He designed it: a sign that He is the LORD who sanctifies you.
