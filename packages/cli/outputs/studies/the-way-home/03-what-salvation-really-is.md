# Study 3: What Salvation Really Is

**Most people think salvation is forgiveness. It is not. Forgiveness is the beginning — like clearing the rubble before rebuilding the house. Salvation is the restoration of God's image in man. Until you understand what was lost, you cannot understand what Christ came to restore.**

## Prep Reading

- Genesis 1:26-27; 5:1-3
- Exodus 33:18–34:7
- John 14:7-11
- Hebrews 1:1-3
- Luke 10:25-28
- 1 John 4:7-8
- John 17:1-3
- 2 Corinthians 3:18

---

## Created in the Image

- **The original design**
  - "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness" (Gen 1:26)
  - "So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them" (Gen 1:27)
  - This is the starting point of everything. Humanity was designed to be a reflection of God.

- **The image marred**
  - "And Adam lived an hundred and thirty years, and begat a son in his own likeness, after his image; and called his name Seth" (Gen 5:3)
  - Genesis 1:27 — created in _God's_ image. Genesis 5:3 — bearing _Adam's_ image.
  - The portrait was not erased — humanity still bears traces (Gen 9:6; James 3:9) — but what passed to Adam's children was fallen, distorted
  - [→] The entire plan of salvation exists to reverse what happened between Genesis 1:27 and Genesis 5:3

- **Two images — earthy and heavenly**
  - "As we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly" (1 Cor 15:49)
  - Paul names the problem and the promise in one verse
  - We have borne Adam's image — fallen, dying, self-centered
  - We _shall_ bear Christ's image — restored, living, love-centered
  - [→] But what _is_ this image? What does it look like? How do you define it?

---

## Show Me Thy Glory — God Defines Himself

- **Moses' request**
  - "I beseech thee, shew me thy glory" (Ex 33:18)
  - Moses wants to _see_ what God looks like — he expects a vision, fire, overwhelming splendor

- **God's answer — not sight but speech**
  - "I will make all my goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the LORD before thee" (Ex 33:19)
  - Moses asked to see _glory_. God said: I will proclaim my _name_.
  - [→] Glory = name. God's glory is not a visual display — it is a character declaration.

- **The proclamation — God's self-portrait**
  - "The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear the guilty" (Ex 34:6-7)
  - This is God defining God. No prophet's interpretation. No theologian's system.
  - Merciful, gracious, longsuffering, abundant in goodness and truth
  - [→] This is what the image of God looks like. This is what was stamped on humanity at creation. This is what sin destroyed.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for "glory" (כָּבוֹד, _kavod_) literally means "weight" or "heaviness." When Moses asked to see God's glory, he was asking to experience the full weight of who God is. God's answer was to speak His character. The weight of God is not His brightness — it is His goodness.

- **Man is the image AND glory of God**
  - "For a man indeed ought not to cover his head, forasmuch as he is the image and glory of God" (1 Cor 11:7)
  - Image and glory are linked — not two things but one
  - We were designed to carry the weight of God's character

## Christ — The Image Made Visible

Philip asked the same question Moses did. He got a different answer — and a better one.

- **"Shew us the Father"**
  - "Philip saith unto him, Lord, shew us the Father, and it sufficeth us" (John 14:8)
  - Same request as Moses: show me what God looks like

- **"Have I been so long time with you?"**
  - "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father; and how sayest thou then, Shew us the Father?" (John 14:9)
  - Christ says: you've already seen. You've been _looking_ at the image of God for three years.

- **The express image**
  - "Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person" (Heb 1:3)
  - "brightness of his glory" — same glory Moses asked to see
  - "express image" (Greek _charakter_) — an exact imprint, like a seal pressed into wax
  - Christ is the Exodus 34:6-7 attributes walking among humanity

- **The image of the invisible God**
  - "Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature" (Col 1:15)
  - "the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God" (2 Cor 4:4)
  - "God, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, hath shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ" (2 Cor 4:6)
  - [→] Glory → knowledge → face → Christ. The same Exodus 33 chain in New Testament language.

- **The Word made flesh**
  - "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth" (John 1:14)
  - "we beheld his glory" — what Moses asked for, the disciples received
  - "full of grace and truth" — the Ex 34:6 character in person

- **The form of God**
  - "Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: but made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men" (Phil 2:6-7)
  - He who _had_ the image emptied Himself and came down — to restore it in us
  - "Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus" (Phil 2:5)
  - [→] The mind of Christ = the character of God = the image we were created to bear

[ILL] A child who has never met his father finds a portrait in the attic — faded, cracked, barely recognizable. That portrait is Genesis 1:27 after the fall. Then the father himself walks through the door. That is the incarnation. You don't need the portrait anymore — you have the Person. But the portrait tells you what the family was always meant to look like.

## The Law IS the Image

Here is where the chain locks tight.

- **What is eternal life?** — a lawyer asks Jesus
  - "Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?" (Luke 10:25)
  - Christ's response: "What is written in the law? how readest thou?" (Luke 10:26)
  - The lawyer answers: "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy strength, and with all thy mind; and thy neighbour as thyself" (Luke 10:27)
  - Christ's verdict: "Thou hast answered right: this do, and thou shalt live" (Luke 10:28)
  - [→] Eternal life = do the law = love

- **God IS love**
  - "He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love" (1 John 4:8)
  - The law is summarized by love. God _is_ love. Therefore: **the law is a transcript of God's character.**
  - This is not a metaphor. It is a logical identity: Law = Love = God's Character = God's Image

- **Knowing God = keeping commandments = having the image**
  - "And hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his commandments" (1 John 2:3)
  - "He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a liar, and the truth is not in him" (1 John 2:4)
  - Knowing God is not intellectual — it is relational, experiential, character-forming
  - You cannot know love and remain loveless

- **Knowing God = eternal life**
  - "And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent" (John 17:3)
  - Life ≠ not dying. Life = knowing God. Knowing God = having His character. Having His character = bearing His image.

- **Destroyed for lack of knowledge**
  - "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" (Hosea 4:6)
  - The inverse of John 17:3. Not knowing God = death. Not having His image = not having life.

[→] Follow the chain: **Image = Glory = Name = Character = Love = Law = Knowing God = Life.** Every link is the same reality from a different angle. Sin broke every link at once. The gospel repairs every link at once.

[Q] **"If keeping the law = eternal life, isn't that salvation by works?"**
→ Christ told the lawyer "this do, and thou shalt live" — not mockingly, but truthfully. The law _is_ the standard of life because it _is_ God's character. The problem is not the standard — it is that fallen humanity cannot meet it in their own strength. That is why the gospel exists: not to lower the standard, but to restore the ability to meet it. "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts" (Jer 31:33). God does not change the test. He gives you a new heart to pass it.

## What Salvation Actually Is

- **Not just pardon — restoration**
  - Forgiveness deals with the _record_. Salvation deals with the _person_.
  - A doctor who cancels your medical debt but doesn't cure your disease has not saved you. You're financially free but still dying.
  - "He shall save his people FROM their sins" (Matt 1:21) — not IN their sins, but FROM them

- **A new heart**
  - "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes" (Eze 36:26-27)
  - **God does the heart surgery.** His Spirit within → walking in His law → image restored → character of love

- **The law written within**
  - "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my people" (Jer 31:33)
  - Not stone tablets — **heart tablets**
  - The content of the law hasn't changed. The _location_ has. Stone to flesh. Outside to inside. Obligation to identity.

- **A new creation**
  - "Put on the new man, which is renewed in knowledge after the image of him that created him" (Col 3:10)
  - "Put on the new man, which after God is created in righteousness and true holiness" (Eph 4:24)
  - The new man is created _after the image of the Creator_ — Genesis 1:26 language. Salvation is re-creation.

- **God's predestined purpose**
  - "For whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brethren" (Rom 8:29)
  - God's plan from before the foundation of the world: a family conformed to Christ's image
  - Not just forgiven sinners — but restored image-bearers

- **Create in me a clean heart**
  - "Create in me a clean heart, O God; and renew a right spirit within me" (Ps 51:10)
  - David knew. The same Hebrew word for "create" (בָּרָא, _bara_) used in Genesis 1:1. Salvation is as much a creative act as the original creation.

[DYK🔎] _Bara_ (בָּרָא) appears in only three verses of Genesis 1 — creating the heavens and earth (v.1), creating animal life (v.21), and creating man in God's image (v.27). David uses the same word in Psalm 51:10. The new heart is not a renovation — it is a divine creation. Only God can do it.

## How It Happens — Beholding

- **Changed by beholding**
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - This is the mechanism of restoration
  - Beholding → changed → same image → glory to glory
  - Not self-effort. Not willpower. Not behavior modification. _Beholding._
  - The Spirit does the transforming. We do the beholding.

- **The glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ**
  - "God, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, hath shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ" (2 Cor 4:6)
  - What do we behold? The glory of God. Where? In the face of Christ.
  - [→] This is Exodus 33 fulfilled. Moses asked to see the glory. We see it — in Christ.

- **Satan's counter-strategy — blindness**
  - "In whom the god of this world hath blinded the minds of them which believe not, lest the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine unto them" (2 Cor 4:4)
  - Satan's strategy is not to argue against the gospel — it is to prevent you from _seeing_ Christ
  - If you cannot behold, you cannot be changed. If you cannot be changed, the image is never restored.
  - [→] Every distraction, every false picture of God, every counterfeit gospel exists for one purpose: to keep you from beholding Christ as He truly is

[Q] **"Does 'beholding' mean just reading the Bible?"**
→ It includes that, but it's more. To behold is to contemplate, to gaze, to absorb. It is the sustained attention of the heart — in Scripture, in prayer, in the sanctuary, in service — toward the person of Christ. "Consider him" (Heb 12:3). You become what you gaze at. This is why worship matters — what you worship, you become. Worship the beast, bear his image (Rev 14:9). Behold Christ, bear His image (2 Cor 3:18).

## The Chain — Laid Out

Let it land:

1. **Image** — God created man in His image (Gen 1:26-27)
2. **Image marred** — Adam's children bear Adam's image (Gen 5:3)
3. **Image = glory = name = character** — God's glory is His character (Ex 33-34)
4. **Christ = the image** — express image of God's person (Heb 1:3; Col 1:15; John 14:9)
5. **Character = love** — God is love (1 John 4:8)
6. **Law = love = life** — do this and live (Luke 10:25-28)
7. **Knowing God = life** — this is eternal life (John 17:3)
8. **Knowing = keeping commandments = having the image** (1 John 2:3-4)
9. **Beholding Christ = restored to the image** — glory to glory (2 Cor 3:18)
10. **New heart = law written within** — image restored from inside out (Jer 31:33; Eze 36:26-27)
11. **Predestined to bear His image** — conformed to the image of His Son (Rom 8:29)
12. **Promise** — "we shall also bear the image of the heavenly" (1 Cor 15:49)

Sin broke every link. The gospel repairs every link. Not by lowering the standard — but by restoring the person.

---

## Appeal

Salvation is not just God letting you off the hook. It is God remaking you from the inside out — writing His law in your heart, restoring His image in your character, giving you a new heart that loves what He loves.

The standard has never changed. It cannot. The standard is God Himself — His character, His love, His law. What changed is what He offers you: not a pass on the test, but a new heart to meet it. Not a lowered bar, but a lifted person.

Behold Him. In every page of Scripture, in every station of the sanctuary, in every moment of prayer — look at Christ. And as you look, the Spirit does what you cannot do for yourself: He changes you into the same image, from glory to glory.

The image was shattered. It will be restored. That is what salvation is.
