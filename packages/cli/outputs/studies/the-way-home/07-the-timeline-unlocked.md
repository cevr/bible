# Study 7: The Timeline Unlocked

**God does not ask for blind faith. He gave Daniel the longest time prophecy in Scripture — 2300 years — and then fulfilled every date with surgical precision: Christ's baptism, His death, the gospel to the Gentiles, and the opening of heaven's final judgment. The timeline is the proof.**

## Daniel 8: The Sanctuary Under Attack

- **the little horn targets heaven itself**
  - "And it waxed great, even to the host of heaven; and it cast down some of the host and of the stars to the ground, and stamped upon them" (Dan 8:10)
  - "Yea, he magnified himself even to the prince of the host, and by him the daily sacrifice was taken away, and the place of his sanctuary was cast down" (Dan 8:11)
  - Study 6 identified WHO this power is — Daniel 7's little horn, the counterfeit religious system
  - Now Daniel 8 reveals WHAT it attacks: the sanctuary and its ministry

- **the "daily" — Christ's continual intercession**
  - "daily" = Hebrew tamid = continual, perpetual
  - not a reference to the Jewish daily sacrifice — a reference to Christ's continual ministry in heaven
  - "Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them" (Heb 7:25)
  - the tamid is what Christ does without ceasing — mediates, intercedes, applies His blood
  - [->] the "daily" was not removed from heaven — Christ never stopped ministering. It was obscured from the view of God's people by a counterfeit priesthood.

- **truth cast to the ground**
  - "And an host was given him against the daily sacrifice by reason of transgression, and it cast down the truth to the ground; and it practised, and prospered" (Dan 8:12)
  - truth = the sanctuary truth, Christ's heavenly ministry
  - "practised, and prospered" — the counterfeit system succeeded. For centuries, the knowledge of Christ's heavenly priesthood was buried.
  - [->] When you replace Christ's intercession with a human priest, Christ's sacrifice with a repeated mass, Christ's mediation with confession to a man — you have taken away the tamid. Not from heaven, but from earth's view.

The word tamid appears 103 times in the Old Testament. It describes the continual burnt offering (Ex 29:42), the continual bread (Num 4:7), the continual light (Ex 27:20). Every use points to something ongoing, unbroken, perpetual — exactly what Christ's heavenly ministry is.

## The 2300 Days: God Answers

- **the question and the answer**
  - "Then I heard one saint speaking, and another saint said unto that certain saint which spake, How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot?" (Dan 8:13)
  - "And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed" (Dan 8:14)
  - How long will the counterfeit system trample truth? 2300 prophetic days.

- **prophetic days = literal years**
  - "I have appointed thee each day for a year" (Eze 4:6)
  - "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, each day for a year" (Num 14:34)
  - 2300 prophetic days = 2300 literal years
  - the longest time prophecy in the Bible

- **Daniel did not understand**
  - "And I Daniel fainted, and was sick certain days; afterward I rose up, and did the king's business; and I was astonished at the vision, but none understood it" (Dan 8:27)
  - Gabriel had been sent to explain: "Understand, O son of man: for at the time of the end shall be the vision" (Dan 8:17)
  - but Daniel fainted before the 2300 days were explained
  - the number hung in the air — no starting point, no interpretation
  - [->] God does not leave questions unanswered. Gabriel will return.

## Daniel 9: Gabriel Returns to Finish the Job

- **Daniel prays — Gabriel answers**
  - Daniel studies Jeremiah's prophecy of 70 years of captivity (Dan 9:2)
  - he prays one of Scripture's greatest prayers of confession and intercession (Dan 9:3-19)
  - "Yea, whiles I was speaking in prayer, even the man Gabriel, whom I had seen in the vision at the beginning, being caused to fly swiftly, touched me about the time of the evening oblation" (Dan 9:21)
  - "at the beginning" — the vision of chapter 8. Same angel, same assignment, unfinished business.

- **the command: understand the vision**
  - "And he informed me, and talked with me, and said, O Daniel, I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding" (Dan 9:22)
  - "At the beginning of thy supplications the commandment came forth, and I am come to shew thee; for thou art greatly beloved: therefore understand the matter, and consider the vision" (Dan 9:23)
  - "the vision" — which vision? The 2300-day vision from chapter 8 that was never explained
  - Gabriel did not come to deliver a new prophecy. He came to finish the old one.

[Q] **"How do we know Daniel 9 explains Daniel 8?"**

> Because Gabriel says so. "Consider the vision" — and the only unexplained vision Daniel had was the 2300 days of chapter 8. Gabriel's opening statement is a direct link. These are not two separate prophecies; they are one prophecy, delivered in two installments.

## The 70 Weeks Cut Off

- **the decree**
  - "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, and to make an end of sins, and to make reconciliation for iniquity, and to bring in everlasting righteousness, and to seal up the vision and prophecy, and to anoint the most Holy" (Dan 9:24)
  - six purposes — every one fulfilled in Christ

- **"determined" = cut off**
  - Hebrew chathak = to cut off, to amputate
  - 70 weeks are CUT OFF — from what? From the only time period under discussion: the 2300 days
  - the 70 weeks are the first portion of the 2300 years
  - [->] this is the key that unlocks the entire prophecy. The 70 weeks are not separate — they are carved out of the 2300. Same starting point.

- **the math**
  - 70 weeks = 70 x 7 = 490 prophetic days = 490 literal years
  - these 490 years are the Jewish portion — "determined upon thy people"
  - [->] if we can find where the 490 starts, we find where the 2300 starts. Same anchor.

---

## The Starting Point: 457 BC

- **the commandment to restore Jerusalem**
  - "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem..." (Dan 9:25)
  - three decrees were issued regarding Jerusalem's restoration:
    - Cyrus, 536 BC (Ezra 1) — rebuilding the temple
    - Darius, 519 BC (Ezra 6) — temple completion
    - Artaxerxes, 457 BC (Ezra 7:12-26) — full civil and judicial authority restored
  - only the decree of Artaxerxes in 457 BC restored Jerusalem as a self-governing entity — not just a building project but a nation
  - [->] 457 BC is the anchor. Every calculation hangs on this date. And every calculation checks out.

[DYK] Artaxerxes' decree in Ezra 7 didn't just authorize rebuilding walls. It restored judicial authority — the power to appoint magistrates, enforce law, and govern. "Restore and build" is political, not merely architectural. This is why the 457 BC decree, not the earlier ones, fits the prophecy.

## Messiah Arrives On Time

- **the calculation**
  - "From the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks" (Dan 9:25)
  - 7 weeks + 62 weeks = 69 weeks
  - 69 x 7 = 483 prophetic days = 483 literal years
  - 457 BC + 483 years = 27 AD (accounting for no year zero)

- **what happened in 27 AD?**
  - "Now when all the people were baptized, it came to pass, that Jesus also being baptized, and praying, the heaven was opened, and the Holy Ghost descended in a bodily shape like a dove upon him, and a voice came from heaven, which said, Thou art my beloved Son; in thee I am well pleased" (Luke 3:21-22)
  - Jesus was baptized and anointed — Messiah means "Anointed One"
  - He began His public ministry in the exact year Daniel predicted, 500 years earlier

- **Jesus knew the timeline**
  - "Now after that John was put in prison, Jesus came into Galilee, preaching the gospel of the kingdom of God, and saying, The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand: repent ye, and believe the gospel" (Mark 1:15)
  - "The time is fulfilled" — what time? Daniel's prophetic time.
  - Jesus was not speaking vaguely. He was pointing to a specific prophecy with a specific fulfillment date — and He was standing in it.
  - [->] Christ did not arrive approximately. He arrived precisely. To the year.

[ILL] Imagine predicting the exact year a specific person would appear — 500 years before their birth. No human could do this. No human did. God dictated the date to Daniel, then walked onto the stage of history and met it Himself. This is not coincidence. This is omniscience making an appointment.

## The Final Week: Messiah Cut Off

- **the middle of the week**
  - "And he shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and in the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease" (Dan 9:27)
  - the 70th week begins at Christ's baptism: 27 AD
  - "in the midst of the week" = 3.5 years into the final week
  - 27 AD + 3.5 years = Spring 31 AD

- **what happened in Spring 31 AD?**
  - "And after threescore and two weeks shall Messiah be cut off, but not for himself" (Dan 9:26)
  - Christ crucified — "cut off, but not for himself" — He died for others
  - "he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease" — the moment Christ died, the entire sacrificial system became obsolete
  - "And, behold, the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:51)
  - God Himself tore the veil — the type had met the antitype
  - [->] the sacrificial system did not gradually fade. It was terminated by heaven at the exact prophetic moment.

- **the end of the 70 weeks: 34 AD**
  - 27 AD + 7 years (one prophetic week) = 34 AD
  - "And they stoned Stephen" (Acts 7:59) — the first Christian martyr
  - the Jewish leadership formally rejected the gospel by killing its witness
  - the apostles turned to the Gentiles: "It was necessary that the word of God should first have been spoken to you: but seeing ye put it from you... lo, we turn to the Gentiles" (Acts 13:46)
  - 490 years of special probation for the Jewish nation — complete
  - [->] 34 AD closes the 70 weeks. Every date within it verified by history: 27 AD, 31 AD, 34 AD.

[TANGENT] The stoning of Stephen in Acts 7 is the mirror image of the anointing of Christ in Luke 3. The 70 weeks opened with the Messiah confirmed; they closed with the Messiah rejected. Both events are markers — both dated to the year by Daniel's prophecy. One opened the door; the other closed the era.

## Calculating to 1844

- **the remaining years**
  - total prophecy: 2300 years
  - first portion (70 weeks): 490 years, ending 34 AD
  - remaining: 2300 - 490 = 1810 years
  - 34 AD + 1810 = 1844 AD

- **"then shall the sanctuary be cleansed" (Dan 8:14)**
  - the sanctuary to be cleansed is not the earthly temple — it was destroyed in 70 AD
  - it is the heavenly sanctuary where Christ ministers (Heb 8:1-2)
  - 1844 marks the beginning of a new phase of Christ's heavenly ministry
  - [->] what that phase means is the subject of Study 8

- **the precision demands attention**
  - 457 BC + 483 years = 27 AD — Christ baptized. Confirmed.
  - 27 AD + 3.5 years = 31 AD — Christ crucified. Confirmed.
  - 457 BC + 490 years = 34 AD — gospel to Gentiles. Confirmed.
  - 457 BC + 2300 years = 1844 AD — sanctuary cleansed. The only date without a visible earthly event.
  - three out of four dates verified by recorded history
  - [->] if the prophecy was right about 27 AD, right about 31 AD, and right about 34 AD — on what basis would you reject 1844?

[Q] **"Why should I trust a date when I can't see what happened?"**

> Because you can see what happened at every other date in the prophecy. The 2300-day prophecy predicted Christ's baptism 500 years early. It predicted His death to the half-year. It predicted the shift to the Gentiles. Every verifiable date was verified. The one remaining date — 1844 — stands on the credibility of the three that came before it.
