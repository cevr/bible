# Study 5: Thy Way, O God

**Study 3 defined what salvation is: the restoration of God's image. Study 4 showed _who_ — why only Christ could do it, why He became a man, how His death satisfies justice and mercy simultaneously. Now the question is: _how_ does God do it? He built a path. Station by station, sacrifice by sacrifice. That path is the sanctuary. That path is Christ.**

## Prep Reading

- Psalm 77:13
- Exodus 25:8-9
- Hebrews 8:1-5; 9:1-12
- Matthew 5:17-18
- Isaiah 33:14

---

## The Problem — Why You Can't Just Come Back

- **God's presence is fire to sin**
  - "The sinners in Zion are afraid; fearfulness hath surprised the hypocrites. Who among us shall dwell with the everlasting burnings?" (Isa 33:14)
  - This is not a threat — it is a description. God's holiness is inherently consuming to sin. Not because He is angry, but because He is pure.
  - "For our God is a consuming fire" (Heb 12:29)
  - "Who shall ascend into the hill of the LORD? or who shall stand in his holy place? He that hath clean hands, and a pure heart" (Ps 24:3-4)

- **Sin created an existential gap — not an emotional one**
  - "But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you" (Isa 59:2)
  - Study 2 established that sin separates. This study asks: _why?_
  - Not because God is offended and walks away. Because if He draws near, the sinner cannot survive the encounter.
  - The cherubim at Eden's gate (Gen 3:24) were not a punishment — they were a protection. The way to the tree of life passed through the presence of God, and the presence of God is lethal to sin.
  - [→] The problem is not that God _won't_ come near you. It is that if He does, you won't survive it. The sanctuary is how He solves that.

[Q] **"Does God want to destroy sinners?"**
→ No. "Have I any pleasure at all that the wicked should die? saith the Lord GOD: and not that he should return from his ways, and live?" (Eze 18:23). God does not desire death. But He cannot un-be Himself. His holiness is not a weapon He wields — it is who He is. The sanctuary is His answer: how to bring sinners into His presence without destroying them.

## Recap — What Studies 3-4 Established

- **Image = Glory = Name = Character = Love = Law = Knowing God = Life** (Study 3)
- Only the Author of the image can restore it. Christ took our nature, overcame as a man, and His death satisfies both justice and mercy (Study 4).
- Beholding Christ is the mechanism: "changed into the same image from glory to glory" (2 Cor 3:18)
- [→] We know _what_ salvation is and _who_ accomplishes it. Now the question: _how_ does God do the restoring? What is the path?

## Why the Standard Cannot Be Lowered

- **The law is who God is** (Study 3) — so abolishing it would mean abolishing Himself. God cannot un-love. He cannot un-be just.
  - "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil" (Matt 5:17)
  - "Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law" (Matt 5:18)
  - [→] Salvation is not God changing the rules. It is God giving everything He has to redeem you _within_ the rules. The standard stands. The question is: how does a sinner meet it?

[Q] **"If no one can keep the law, why not just get rid of it?"**
→ For the same reason you don't smash the mirror because you don't like what it shows you. The law is diagnostic — "by the law is the knowledge of sin" (Rom 3:20). Destroying the diagnostic tool doesn't cure the disease. And the law is more than diagnostic — it is the portrait of who you were created to be. You don't destroy the blueprint; you rebuild to match it.

- **The charge this answers**
  - Study 1 identified Satan's accusation: "The law cannot be kept" (Charge B). Study 4 showed Christ answered it in human flesh. But now we see the _process_ by which that answer is worked out in us.
  - If the law is God's character, then "the law cannot be kept" is the same as "God's image cannot be restored in humanity." Satan is not merely arguing against obedience — he is arguing that what God lost in Eden, He can never recover.
  - The sanctuary is God's answer to that charge — not a courtroom defense, but a working demonstration. Station by station, it transforms the sinner back into the image of God. The charge says it can't be done. The sanctuary does it.

## The Sanctuary — God's Path of Restoration

- **"Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary"**
  - "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary: who is so great a God as our God?" (Ps 77:13)
  - "Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" (John 14:6)
  - The way is in the sanctuary. The way is Christ. These are not two separate ideas — they are one revelation from two angles.
  - The sanctuary is not a building you visit — it is a journey you walk. And every station transforms you.

- **The purpose — "that I may dwell among them"**
  - "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them" (Ex 25:8)
  - Not "dwell in it" — "dwell among _them_"
  - The building is the means; the people are the goal
  - God's presence burns sin (Isa 33:14). The sanctuary is the process by which He makes it safe for sinners to be in that presence — not by dimming His glory, but by transforming the sinner.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for sanctuary is מִקְדָּשׁ (_miqdash_), from the root קדשׁ (_qadash_) — "to be holy, to set apart." The sanctuary is literally "the holy place" — but its purpose is to make _you_ holy. The holiness flows outward from the Shekinah through every station until it reaches the sinner at the gate.

---

## Three Stages — One Restoration

The sanctuary maps salvation as a journey in three stages. Paul gives the endpoints in one breath:

- "whom He did predestinate, them He also **called**: and whom He called, them He also **justified**: and whom He justified, them He also **glorified**" (Rom 8:30)
- Paul names calling, justification, and glorification. The sanctuary fills in the path between them — the daily walk of sanctification that conforms you to the image of His Son (Rom 8:29).

Each stage has a location in the sanctuary, a work of Christ, and a dimension of the image restored:

| Stage              | Sanctuary       | Work                      | Image Restored In         |
| ------------------ | --------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Justification**  | Courtyard       | Faith, forgiveness, power | Standing before God       |
| **Sanctification** | Holy Place      | The daily walk            | Heart and character       |
| **Glorification**  | Most Holy Place | Sealing and completion    | Body — fashioned like His |

- "whom He did foreknow, He also did predestinate to be **conformed to the image of His Son**" (Rom 8:29)
- [→] That is the goal of all three stages. The sanctuary is the map. Let's walk it.

## The Courtyard — Justification (Image Restored in Standing)

- **The gate — Christ is the entrance**
  - "I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved" (John 10:9)
  - The journey begins before the sacrifice. You must enter. Christ is the only way in.

- **The altar of burnt offering — faith and forgiveness**
  - The sinner brings the lamb. He lays his hand on its head (Lev 1:4) — confession, identification, transference. He kills it himself (Lev 1:5).
  - "He shall confess that he hath sinned" (Lev 5:5) — the hand on the head is not silent; it is spoken
  - "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" (1 John 1:9)
  - This is not remote. You feel the warmth, the weight, the life leaving. Your sin did this.
  - "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" (John 1:29)
  - "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him" (2 Cor 5:21)
  - "Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" (Rom 3:24)
  - But even faith is not ours to manufacture — He draws it out of us:
    - "And I, if I be lifted up from the earth, will draw all men unto me" (John 12:32)
    - "No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him" (John 6:44)
    - "God hath dealt to every man the measure of faith" (Rom 12:3)
    - Jesus is "the author and finisher of our faith" (Heb 12:2) — He starts it, He completes it
  - [→] The altar is where faith meets forgiveness. He draws. He gives faith. Faith lays hands on the Lamb. The record is cleared. But forgiveness alone is not salvation — the laver follows.

- **The laver — power**
  - "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost" (Titus 3:5)
  - "Buried with him by baptism into death: that like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life" (Rom 6:4)
  - "Our old man is crucified with him, that the body of sin might be destroyed, that henceforth we should not serve sin" (Rom 6:6)
  - "Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost" (Acts 2:38)
  - [→] The altar forgives. The laver empowers. The old man dies at the altar; the new man rises at the laver. Both in the courtyard — both at justification.

- **Christ demonstrates the pattern: altar (faith + forgiveness) → laver (power)**
  - **The paralytic** (Mark 2:1-12)
    - _Altar_: "When Jesus saw _their faith_" (Mark 2:5) — He drew them; they came; He saw it. "Son, thy sins be forgiven thee" — faith and forgiveness at the altar.
    - _Laver_: "Arise, and take up thy bed, and walk" (Mark 2:11) — power. The man came carried; he left carrying.
  - **The woman in adultery** (John 8:1-11)
    - _Altar_: "She said, No man, Lord" (John 8:11) — faith (she calls Him Lord). "Neither do I condemn thee" — forgiveness.
    - _Laver_: "Go, and sin no more" — power. Christ does not command what He does not enable. "Sin no more" is not a demand shouted at a cripple — it is a gift given with the legs to walk on.

- **But justification is not the end — it is the courtyard**
  - "If, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life" (Rom 5:10)
  - Justified by His _death_, saved by His _life_. The courtyard grants new birth; the Holy Place grows what was born.

- **The courtyard feasts — Passover, Unleavened Bread, Firstfruits**
  - **Passover** — "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" (1 Cor 5:7) — the altar in feast form
  - **Unleavened Bread** — leaven represents sin (1 Cor 5:8). The old leaven is purged. New life, unleavened, begins.
  - **Firstfruits** — "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept" (1 Cor 15:20). The resurrection is the laver in feast form — raised to walk in newness of life (Rom 6:4).
  - [→] Death (Passover), cleansing (Unleavened Bread), resurrection power (Firstfruits). The feast calendar maps the courtyard exactly.

## The Holy Place — Sanctification (Image Restored in the Heart)

- **"Go unto thine house" — the bridge from courtyard to holy place**
  - Back to the paralytic. Christ didn't just heal him and leave him on the floor. He said: "Arise, and take up thy bed, and **go thy way into thine house**" (Mark 2:11)
  - The man has power. He can stand. He can walk. But does he _have_ to go home?
  - No. He could walk anywhere. The power is real, the legs work, the paralysis is gone. He is free.
  - But Christ bids him: _go home_.
  - [→] That is sanctification. You have the power. You have the new legs. Now walk — not wherever you please, but _home_. The Holy Place is the journey home. It is freely chosen, empowered by the courtyard, directed by Christ.

- **The daily walk — not a moment but a journey**
  - The courtyard was an event. The Holy Place is a process. The priest entered daily — morning and evening (Ex 29:38-39). Sanctification is sustained communion, not a single crisis.
  - "This is the will of God, even your sanctification" (1 Thess 4:3)
  - "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God" (Gal 2:20)
  - [→] Justification changed your standing. Sanctification changes your _character_. The image of God is rebuilt here — day by day, station by station.

- **The table of showbread — the Word of God**
  - "And thou shalt set upon the table shewbread before me alway" (Ex 25:30)
  - Twelve loaves, set fresh every Sabbath (Lev 24:5-8) — constant provision, never stale
  - "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" (Deut 8:3)
  - "I am the living bread which came down from heaven" (John 6:51)
  - "Thy words were found, and I did eat them; and thy word was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart" (Jer 15:16)
  - Character is formed by what you consume. The showbread is the daily intake of God's Word — not information but sustenance. You become what you eat.

- **The lampstand — the Spirit's light through you**
  - "And thou shalt make a candlestick of pure gold" (Ex 25:31)
  - "Command the children of Israel, that they bring unto thee pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamps to burn continually" (Lev 24:2)
  - Oil in Scripture is the Holy Spirit (1 Sam 16:13 — anointing; Zech 4:6 — "not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit")
  - The Spirit was _received_ at the laver (justification). The lampstand is the Spirit _working through_ you — illumination becoming witness
  - "Ye are the light of the world. A city that is set on an hill cannot be hid" (Matt 5:14)
  - "Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven" (Matt 5:16)
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - [→] Beholding transforms. The sanctuary teaches you _what_ to behold. Christ shows you _who_ to behold. The Spirit does the transforming. The image of God is restored by sustained looking, not by self-effort.

[DYK🔎] The seven-branched lampstand was the only source of light in the Holy Place — there were no windows. Without the Spirit's illumination, the priest would serve in total darkness. Without the Holy Spirit, the Christian walks blind — even if the bread is on the table and the incense is burning. The Spirit makes everything else visible.

- **The altar of incense — prayer**
  - "And Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning: when he dresseth the lamps, he shall burn incense upon it. And when Aaron lighteth the lamps at even, he shall burn incense upon it, a perpetual incense before the LORD" (Ex 30:7-8)
  - "Let my prayer be set forth before thee as incense; and the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice" (Ps 141:2)
  - "And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne" (Rev 8:3)
  - The incense altar stood directly before the veil — closest to the Most Holy Place. Prayer is the nearest approach to God's presence in the daily service.
  - "Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered" (Rom 8:26)
  - [→] The Word feeds. The Spirit illuminates. Prayer communicates. These three sustain the daily walk — bread for strength, light for direction, incense for communion. Remove any one and sanctification stalls.

- **Pentecost — the Holy Place feast**
  - "And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind" (Acts 2:1-2)
  - Pentecost fell fifty days after Firstfruits — fifty days after the resurrection. The Spirit was poured out in power, not for the first time, but in fullness.
  - The disciples had already been converted (courtyard). Pentecost was _commission_ — the Spirit empowering them for witness, service, and daily transformation.
  - [→] Pentecost is the Holy Place inaugurated. The same Spirit that raised Christ from the dead now indwells His people for the daily work of sanctification and witness.

## The Most Holy Place — Final Atonement (Image Sealed; Body Glorified at Advent)

- **Through the veil — the transition**
  - "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" (Heb 10:19-20)
  - The veil separated the Holy from the Most Holy — embroidered with cherubim (Ex 26:31), the same cherubim that guarded Eden's gate (Gen 3:24). The veil is the barrier sin created.
  - Christ's flesh is the veil. His death opened the way. "The veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:51) — God tore it, not man.
  - [→] The sanctuary walk has been leading here. The incense altar stood directly before this veil — prayer brings you to the threshold. Now enter.

- **The ark of the covenant — the standard within the mercy**
  - "And thou shalt put the mercy seat above upon the ark; and in the ark thou shalt put the testimony that I shall give thee" (Ex 25:21)
  - Inside the ark: the two tables of stone — "the testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God" (Ex 31:18)
  - The law is inside the ark. The mercy seat is above the law. The Shekinah — God's presence — dwells above the mercy seat, between the cherubim.
  - [→] The spatial arrangement is the theology: the law defines the standard. Mercy covers it. God's presence rests where justice and mercy meet.

[DYK🔎] The law was kept _inside_ the ark — beneath the mercy seat, within the Most Holy Place, under the Shekinah glory. Not tacked onto the door as a list of restrictions — placed in the innermost chamber as the defining reality of God's presence. Where God dwells, His character defines the space.

- **The law written on the heart — character glorification**
  - "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts" (Jer 31:33)
  - At Sinai: law on stone — external, imposed. In the new covenant: law on heart — internal, desired, lived.
  - The content hasn't changed. The _location_ has. Stone to flesh. Outside to inside. Obligation to identity.
  - "Create in me a clean heart, O God; and renew a right spirit within me" (Ps 51:10)
  - [→] When the law of God becomes the desire of your heart, the image of God is restored in character. You keep the commandments not because you must — but because they are who you have become.

- **Fashioned like His glorious body — body glorification**
  - Character first, then body. The sanctuary maps the first. The Second Coming delivers the second.
  - "Who shall change our vile body, that it may be **fashioned like unto his glorious body**, according to the working whereby he is able even to subdue all things unto himself" (Phil 3:21)
  - "Beloved, now are we the sons of God, and it doth not yet appear what we shall be: but we know that, when he shall appear, **we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is**" (1 John 3:2)
  - "As we have borne the **image of the earthy**, we shall also bear the **image of the heavenly**" (1 Cor 15:49)
  - "For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality" (1 Cor 15:53)
  - [→] The image of Adam (earthy, fallen, dying) exchanged for the image of Christ (heavenly, restored, immortal). This is the full restoration — character AND body. Genesis 1:27 recovered completely.
  - "Every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure" (1 John 3:3) — the hope of glorification drives sanctification. The end fuels the journey.

- **The Most Holy Place feasts — Trumpets and Day of Atonement**
  - **Feast of Trumpets** (Lev 23:24) — the wake-up call before judgment. A summons to self-examination, preparation, solemnity. The pre-judgment alarm.
  - **Day of Atonement** — once a year the high priest entered the Most Holy Place (Lev 16:15-16) — the only day, the only access
    - He brought blood and incense — atonement for the accumulated sin-record of the year
    - "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD" (Lev 16:30)
    - The Day of Atonement was a day of judgment — who was genuinely in covenant, who merely appeared to be
    - It is the completion of the sanctuary process: the sin confessed at the altar, the character shaped in the Holy Place, is now _sealed_ in the Most Holy Place
    - The people waited outside while the high priest was within (Lev 16:17). When he emerged alive — atonement accepted, judgment passed — they knew they were clean.
    - [→] The Day of Atonement will receive its full treatment later in this series. For now, see the pattern: the sinner who entered at the gate covered in the lamb's blood now stands, by faith, in the presence of God — cleansed, transformed, sealed.

- **This is happening now**
  - "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed" (Dan 8:14) — since 1844, Christ our High Priest has been officiating the antitypical Day of Atonement in the heavenly sanctuary
  - "Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us" (Heb 9:24)
  - He is able "to present you faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding joy" (Jude 24)
  - But He will not force the work. If we wish for Him to present us faultless before the throne, we must allow Him to cleanse our hearts. The Day of Atonement is not just _about_ us — it is _for_ us. And it requires our consent.
  - "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need" (Heb 4:16)
  - [→] The throne is not a courtroom bench — it is a throne of **grace**. The High Priest is there. The atonement is in progress. The question is: will you let Him finish?

[Q] **"Why character before body?"**
→ Because God will not immortalize what has not been transformed. The seal of God in the forehead (Rev 7:3) is settled character — not arbitrary election. Only when the image is restored in character does God say: now I can safely restore the body. "We shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is" — seeing Him completes the work beholding Him began (2 Cor 3:18).

## The Sanctuary Is Christ — And That Is Why It Transforms

- **Every station transforms you because every station is Him**
  - Study 2 showed that each article of the sanctuary identifies with Christ — the altar is His sacrifice, the bread is His Word, the light is His Spirit, the incense is His intercession. That was identification. This is implication:
  - The sanctuary transforms because you cannot walk through Christ and remain unchanged. You are not navigating a system — you are being conformed to a Person.
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - Beholding _Him_ at every station is what restores the image. The furniture is not the point — the Person behind it is.

- **Christ walked the sanctuary path Himself**
  - **Courtyard**: He died — the Lamb on the altar. "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" (1 Cor 5:7)
  - **Holy Place**: He rose and ministered through the Spirit — "by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption for us" (Heb 9:12)
  - **Most Holy Place**: He ascended to the Father's very presence — "sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high" (Heb 1:3)
  - He is not asking you to walk a path He has not walked first.

- **The High Priest walks the path with you**
  - "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2)
  - "For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin" (Heb 4:15)
  - He is not a distant administrator. He has walked this path Himself — tempted, tested, faithful. He ministers from experience, not theory. He does not point the way from heaven; He walks it with you from the altar to the Shekinah.

- **The way and the sanctuary are the same Person**
  - "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary" (Ps 77:13)
  - "I am the way" (John 14:6)
  - The sanctuary is not a system you navigate — it is a Person you follow
  - From the gate to the Shekinah, from justification to glorification, from the shattered image to the restored one — every step is with Him, through Him, in Him

> "Christ is the connecting link in the chain of humanity uniting earth with heaven and the finite with the infinite." (ST, June 26, 1893)

## Appeal

The image of God in you was shattered at the fall. But the portrait was never thrown away — it was placed at the center of the sanctuary, written in stone, covered by mercy, illuminated by glory.

God did not lower the standard. He could not. The standard is Himself. Instead, He built a way — through blood, through water, through bread and light and incense and law and mercy — to bring you from what you are to what you were meant to be.

The way is in the sanctuary. The way is Christ. And He is walking it with you.

Will you follow?

**Central Verse:** "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary: who is so great a God as our God?" (Psalm 77:13)

**[→ Study 6]** The sanctuary path is built on two laws working together — the moral law that defines the image being restored in you, and the ceremonial law that diagrammed Christ's mission until He fulfilled it. Confuse them and the Sabbath collapses, the seal disappears, and the whole way home is lost. Distinguish them and every step that follows holds.
