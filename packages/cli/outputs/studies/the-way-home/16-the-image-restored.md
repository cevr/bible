# Study 16: The Image Restored — Seal, 144,000, Millennium, New Earth

**The trial that opened in Eden reaches its verdict. God's seal marks those whose characters reflect His own. The 144,000 answer both of Satan's charges — not by human achievement, but by Christ's character fully reproduced. The image of God is restored. The Way Home is complete.**

## The Seal of God — The Judge's Mark of Approval

- **sealing before destruction — a judicial act**
  - "Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads" (Rev 7:1-3)
  - four angels hold back final destruction until sealing is complete
  - the seal is not arbitrary protection — it is the Judge's mark of approval on examined evidence
  - [→] God does not seal people to protect them from judgment. He seals them because they have passed judgment.

- **what the seal signifies**
  - a seal carries three elements: name, title, territory
  - the fourth commandment contains all three: "the LORD" (name), "made" (title — Creator), "heaven and earth, the sea" (territory) (Ex 20:11)
  - seal = ownership + character endorsement
  - God does not stamp His name on unfinished work

- **the mark of Ezekiel 9 — the character test**
  - "Set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations that be done in the midst thereof" (Eze 9:4)
  - not marked for doctrinal precision alone — marked for moral sensitivity
  - those who grieve over sin, not merely avoid it
  - [→] the seal is not a reward for perfect behavior. It is a recognition of a heart fully aligned with God's own grief over evil.

- **the Sabbath — the very sign Satan attacked**
  - "Moreover also I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the LORD that sanctify them" (Eze 20:12)
  - the Sabbath is the sign of sanctification — God completing His work in the believer
  - the same commandment Satan's counterfeit system targeted (Dan 7:25) is the one God uses as His seal
  - [→] this is not coincidence. Satan attacked the sign because the sign points to what he denies: that God can actually transform people.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for "sign" in Ezekiel 20:12 is אוֹת (_ot_) — the same word used for the mark of Cain (Gen 4:15), the rainbow covenant (Gen 9:12), and circumcision (Gen 17:11). Every _ot_ in Scripture marks a covenant relationship. The Sabbath is God's final covenant marker — the sign that He who began the work has finished it.

## The 144,000 — Both Charges Answered

- **five characteristics — the finished product**
  - "And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads" (Rev 14:1)
  - Father's name in foreheads — His character reproduced in their minds (v.1)
  - Redeemed from the earth, firstfruits unto God (v.3-4)
  - Follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth (v.4)
  - No guile found in their mouths (v.5)
  - Without fault before the throne of God (v.5)

- **charge #1 answered: "They only obey for reward"**
  - Satan's original accusation: "Doth Job fear God for nought?" (Job 1:9)
  - the 144,000 follow the Lamb through suffering, loss, social death, and the threat of physical death
  - they pass through the time of Jacob's trouble without a Mediator (GC 425)
  - they are willing to be slain rather than compromise
  - "Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him" (Job 13:15) — Job's answer becomes theirs
  - [→] Satan said no one serves God without a bribe. The 144,000 serve Him after every bribe is removed.

- **charge #2 answered: "Your law cannot be kept"**
  - "without fault before the throne of God" (Rev 14:5)
  - not self-generated sinlessness — Christ's righteousness fully received and fully lived
  - the law is kept — not by human willpower, but by divine character reproduced in human flesh
  - [→] Satan said the law was impossible. The 144,000 are the impossibility made visible.

- **Rev 14:12 — the verdict read aloud**
  - "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus" (Rev 14:12)
  - "patience" (ὑπομονή, _hupomone_) — endurance under trial. The trial was long. They outlasted it.
  - "keep the commandments of God" — charge #2 refuted. It can be done.
  - "the faith of Jesus" — not merely faith IN Jesus, but faith LIKE Jesus. The same trust He expressed: "Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit" (Luke 23:46)
  - charge #1 refuted. Their faith is the same faith Jesus had — loyalty without visible reward.
  - [→] one verse. Both charges answered. The entire cosmic trial resolved in a single sentence. The faith of Jesus is what separates religion from relationship. Religion believes God exists. The faith of Jesus trusts Him when He is silent.

[Q] **"Does 'without fault' mean absolute sinlessness?"**

> It means what the sanctuary has always meant: sins confessed, transferred, and removed by the blood of the Lamb — and a character so fully surrendered to Christ that known, cherished sin has been abandoned. Not perfection of performance. Perfection of surrender. The mirror does not generate light. It reflects what shines upon it.

## Christ's Perfecting Work — The Image Rebuilt

- **one sacrifice, ongoing transformation**
  - "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified" (Heb 10:14)
  - "perfected" — legal standing, settled once
  - "are sanctified" — present tense, continuous. The work is ongoing.
  - justification is the courtroom badge. Sanctification is the character evidence.

- **glory to glory — the progressive restoration**
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - as we saw in Study 3: changed by beholding, not by self-effort

- **Genesis 1 meets Revelation 14**
  - "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness" (Gen 1:26)
  - "having his Father's name written in their foreheads" (Rev 14:1)
  - image of God at creation → image of God at the end
  - what was lost in Eden is restored in the 144,000
  - the entire Bible is the story of that restoration
  - [→] the Father's name in their foreheads IS the image of God restored. Full circle.

[ILL] A mirror does not generate light. It does not strain to be bright. It simply faces the source — and reflects what shines upon it. Turn it away, and it goes dark. Not because the light failed, but because the mirror moved. The 144,000 are mirrors that stopped turning away.

- **the EGW statement that governs the timeline**

> "Christ is waiting with longing desire for the manifestation of Himself in His church. When the character of Christ shall be perfectly reproduced in His people, then He will come to claim them as His own." (COL 69.1)

- Christ is waiting — not for more time, but for character reproduction
- "perfectly reproduced" — not improved, not approximated — reproduced
- "then He will come" — the Second Coming is tied to character readiness, not calendar date
- [→] 1844 opened the judgment. The judgment waits for witnesses whose lives close the case. The delay is not God's reluctance. It is ours.

## The Verdict — Christ Returns

- **the harvest follows immediately**
  - "And I looked, and behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown, and in his hand a sharp sickle" (Rev 14:14)
  - "And another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud, Thrust in thy sickle, and reap: for the time is come for thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is ripe" (Rev 14:15)
  - [→] Notice the sequence in Revelation 14: the 144,000 described (v.1-5), the three angels proclaim (v.6-12), the verdict stated (v.12), the harvest comes (v.14-16). Character reproduction → proclamation → verdict → return. That is the order.

- **the bride made ready**
  - "Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready" (Rev 19:7)
  - "And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints" (Rev 19:8)
  - the bride is not dressed by accident — she "made herself ready"
  - the fine linen is granted — it is Christ's righteousness, but she chose to wear it
  - [→] grace provides the garment. Faith puts it on. Both are required.

## The Millennium — A Thousand Years of Review

- **Satan bound — the accuser silenced**
  - "And I saw an angel come down from heaven, having the key of the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand. And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years" (Rev 20:1-2)
  - "And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled" (Rev 20:3)
  - The earth lies desolate. The wicked are dead. Satan is confined to a ruined world with no one to deceive.
  - [→] The scapegoat of Leviticus 16 is now fulfilled. Sin is traced back to its author. He wanders the waste bearing the guilt he originated.

- **The saints review the records**
  - "And I saw thrones, and they sat upon them, and judgment was given unto them" (Rev 20:4)
  - "And they lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years" (Rev 20:4)
  - For a thousand years the redeemed examine the records of judgment — not to overrule God, but to confirm His justice
  - Every question answered. Every case reviewed. Every decision vindicated.
  - [→] Study 9 said the judgment vindicates God's people before the universe. The millennium vindicates God before His people. He opens every book. He hides nothing. At the end of a thousand years, every saint will say: God was just.

[ILL] A jury retires to deliberate. The judge opens every file — every piece of evidence, every transcript, every sealed record. Nothing is withheld. The jury takes as long as it needs. That is the millennium: God handing the redeemed a thousand years to examine every decision He ever made — and finding not one error.

- **The first resurrection and the second death**
  - "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death hath no power" (Rev 20:6)
  - "But the rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" (Rev 20:5)
  - Two resurrections: the righteous at Christ's coming, the wicked after the millennium
  - The second death is final — eternal destruction, not eternal torment

## The Third Coming — Final Judgment

- **The Holy City descends**
  - "And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband" (Rev 21:2)
  - "And his feet shall stand in that day upon the mount of Olives, which is before Jerusalem on the east, and the mount of Olives shall cleave in the midst thereof" (Zech 14:4)
  - Christ returns to earth with His saints and the Holy City. The earth is made ready.

- **Satan loosed — the last deception**
  - "And when the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be loosed out of his prison, and shall go out to deceive the nations which are in the four quarters of the earth... to gather them together to battle" (Rev 20:7-8)
  - The wicked are raised — the second resurrection. Satan has an army again.
  - "And they went up on the breadth of the earth, and compassed the camp of the saints about, and the beloved city" (Rev 20:9)
  - [→] Even after a thousand years of bearing guilt on a ruined earth, Satan's character is unchanged. He cannot repent. He marshals the wicked for one final assault against God. This proves what the great controversy has always been about: sin is not a mistake to be fixed but a rebellion that must end.

- **Every knee bows**
  - "That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of things in heaven, and things in earth, and things under the earth; and that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father" (Phil 2:10-11)
  - Before the fire falls, every being — saved and lost, angel and demon — acknowledges that God is just
  - The wicked do not repent. They acknowledge. Satan himself bows.
  - [→] This is the final vindication. Not forced worship — forced honesty. Every intelligence in the universe, having seen the evidence, confesses: God was right.

- **The great white throne**
  - "And I saw a great white throne, and him that sat on it... and the dead were judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works" (Rev 20:11-12)
  - "And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire" (Rev 20:15)
  - "And death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death" (Rev 20:14)

- **Sin destroyed — not perpetuated**
  - "For, behold, the day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud, yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble: and the day that cometh shall burn them up, saith the LORD of hosts, that it shall leave them neither root nor branch" (Mal 4:1)
  - "And ye shall tread down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet" (Mal 4:3)
  - Not eternal torment — eternal destruction. Ashes, not agony. Root and branch consumed.
  - "He will make an utter end: affliction shall not rise up the second time" (Nah 1:9)
  - [→] God does not perpetuate suffering. He ends it. Sin, sinners, death itself — consumed. Never to rise again.

[Q] **"Does 'for ever and ever' in Revelation 20:10 mean eternal torment?"**

> The same phrase describes the destruction of Edom (Isa 34:10) — "the smoke thereof shall go up for ever." Edom is not still burning. "For ever" means "until complete" — as long as there is something to consume. When the fuel is spent, the fire ends. Malachi says ashes. Nahum says it will not rise again. The second death is final, not perpetual.

## The New Earth — Eden Restored

- **"Behold, I make all things new"**
  - "And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away" (Rev 21:1)
  - "And he that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things new" (Rev 21:5)
  - Not a repair. A re-creation. The same God who said "Let there be light" says "Behold, I make all things new."

- **God dwells with man — the tabernacle fulfilled**
  - "And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God" (Rev 21:3)
  - Every sanctuary in Scripture pointed to this: the garden, the tabernacle, the temple, Christ incarnate, the believer's body. All were temporary dwellings. This is the permanent one.
  - [→] The tabernacle in the wilderness was called _mishkan_ — "dwelling place." God's entire purpose in the sanctuary system was to get here: Himself with His people, no veil between.

- **No more curse**
  - "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away" (Rev 21:4)
  - "And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him" (Rev 22:3)
  - The curse of Genesis 3 — death, sorrow, thorns, sweat — is undone.

- **The tree of life — access restored**
  - "In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations" (Rev 22:2)
  - The tree that was guarded by a flaming sword (Gen 3:24) is now freely accessible.
  - [→] The Way Home ends at the tree. The sword is removed. The way is open.

- **They shall see His face**
  - "And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads" (Rev 22:4)
  - His name = His character = His image. The very thing Study 3 defined: Image = Glory = Name = Character.
  - Moses asked to see His glory (Ex 33:18). Now they see His face.
  - [→] The name in their foreheads IS the image of God restored. Full circle. Genesis 1:26 answered by Revelation 22:4.

## The Circle Closes — The Whole Arc

- **image → broken image → restored image**
  - Gen 1:26-27 — created in God's image
  - Gen 3:6-7 — image shattered by sin
  - Rev 14:1 / Rev 22:4 — image restored, name in foreheads
  - the entire biblical narrative fits inside this arc

- **the sanctuary mapped the whole journey**
  - outer court: sacrifice — the cost of restoration (the cross)
  - holy place: intercession — the process of restoration (sanctification)
  - most holy place: judgment — the completion of restoration (the seal)
  - the sanctuary was never just a building. It was a map home.
  - [→] the Way Home was always through the sanctuary. Every piece pointed to this: God dwelling with man again, His image fully restored.

- **the new earth is the new Eden — but better**
  - "For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind" (Isa 65:17)
  - "The wolf and the lamb shall feed together, and the lion shall eat straw like the bullock" (Isa 65:25)
  - "Nevertheless we, according to his promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness" (2 Pet 3:13)
  - Eden had innocence. The new earth has tested, proven, vindicated righteousness. The image restored is stronger than the image created — because it has been tested and survived.
