# Study 3: The Bridge Restored — Sanctuary and Christ

**Sin severed the heaven-earth connection, but God moved first. Before Adam could even flee, God was seeking. Before the curse was finished, a promise was spoken. The sanctuary is God's bridge back to humanity — and every beam, every curtain, every flame is Christ.**

## Prep Reading

- Genesis 3:9-21
- Genesis 28:10-17
- John 1:51
- Exodus 25:8-9
- Hebrews 8:1-5

---

## The First Gospel — God Moves First

- **God seeks the sinner before the sinner seeks God**
  - "And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou?" (Gen 3:9)
  - Not accusation — invitation
  - Adam hid; God pursued
  - The pattern of all salvation: God initiates, man responds

- **The protoevangelium — first promise of redemption**
  - "And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel" (Gen 3:15)
  - Spoken to the serpent — God announces Satan's defeat before addressing Adam's punishment
  - The seed of the woman = Christ (Gal 4:4)
  - Bruise his head = fatal, decisive victory
  - Bruise his heel = the cross — painful, but not final

- **The first sacrifice — blood before law**
  - "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them" (Gen 3:21)
  - Skins require death — an animal died
  - God performed the first sacrifice; Adam did not
  - Fig leaves (man's covering) replaced by skins (God's covering)
  - [→] The first death in creation was not punishment — it was provision

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for "atonement" (כָּפַר, _kaphar_) means "to cover." Before the tabernacle, before Sinai, before the priesthood — God was already covering sin with blood. The sanctuary system formalized what Eden inaugurated.

> "The instant man accepted the temptations of Satan... a Saviour was provided." (RH, March 12, 1901)

[→] God's remedy was immediate. But how would He communicate the fullness of this plan? Through a vision — a ladder.

---

## Jacob's Ladder — The Bridge Restored in Vision

- **The setting — a fugitive at rock bottom**
  - Jacob fleeing from Esau, guilty of deception
  - Alone, night falling, a stone for a pillow (Gen 28:11)
  - No merit, no temple, no priest — just a sinner in the dirt

- **The vision — heaven reconnected to earth**
  - "And he dreamed, and behold a ladder set up on the earth, and the top of it reached to heaven: and behold the angels of God ascending and descending on it" (Gen 28:12)
  - The ladder bridges the gap that sin created
  - Angels ascending and descending — active, ongoing ministry between heaven and earth
  - Communication restored; the connection is not theoretical but operational

- **God stands above it — He initiates**
  - "And, behold, the LORD stood above it, and said, I am the LORD God of Abraham thy father, and the God of Isaac" (Gen 28:13)
  - God identifies Himself by covenant relationship
  - He speaks promise, not condemnation, to a man who just lied and cheated his way out of the family home

- **Jacob's response — "the house of God"**
  - "How dreadful is this place! this is none other but the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven" (Gen 28:17)
  - "House of God" — sanctuary language before any sanctuary existed
  - "Gate of heaven" — the access point, the entry, the bridge
  - [→] Jacob names the place Bethel — "house of God." The sanctuary concept is revealed in a dream before it is built in a desert.

- **Christ declares Himself the ladder**
  - "Hereafter ye shall see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of man" (John 1:51)
  - Jesus directly claims Jacob's ladder as Himself
  - He is the bridge between heaven and earth
  - Not a structure — a Person

> "The ladder represents Jesus, the appointed medium of communication." (PP 184.2)

[Q] **"If the ladder is Christ, why build a physical sanctuary at all?"**
→ Because God teaches through the concrete. Abstract truth must be made tangible — especially for a people emerging from 400 years of Egyptian idolatry. The sanctuary is the ladder made visible, touchable, daily.

## The Sanctuary Pattern — God's Dwelling Place

- **The purpose — "that I may dwell among them"**
  - "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them" (Ex 25:8)
  - Not "dwell in it" — "dwell among **them**"
  - The building is the means; the people are the goal
  - Sin drove God out of direct contact (Gen 3:24); the sanctuary brings Him back — veiled, mediated, but present

- **Built after a pattern — earthly copy of heavenly reality**
  - "According to all that I shew thee, after the pattern of the tabernacle, and the pattern of all the instruments thereof, even so shall ye make it" (Ex 25:9)
  - Moses received the blueprint on Sinai — this is not human invention
  - Every measurement, every material, every arrangement has meaning

- **The heavenly original confirmed**
  - "Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount" (Heb 8:5)
  - "Shadow" = the earthly sanctuary
  - "Heavenly things" = the reality Christ ministers in
  - [→] The earthly sanctuary was never the point — it was the curriculum. The classroom is not the truth; it teaches the truth.

## The Sanctuary Typifies Christ — Every Element Points to Him

- **The Lamb — Christ's sacrifice**
  - "The next day John seeth Jesus coming unto him, and saith, Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" (John 1:29)
  - Every morning and evening sacrifice (Ex 29:38-39) — a daily pointer to the cross
  - The lamb was without blemish (Ex 12:5) — Christ "without spot" (1 Pet 1:19)
  - The sinner laid hands on the lamb's head, confessing sin (Lev 4:4) — transfer of guilt to the substitute

- **The Bread — Christ's sustenance**
  - "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger" (John 6:35)
  - Twelve loaves of showbread, set fresh each Sabbath (Lev 24:5-8)
  - Called "bread of the presence" — sustenance in God's presence
  - The Word of God as daily nourishment: "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" (Matt 4:4)

- **The Light — Christ's guidance**
  - "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life" (John 8:12)
  - Seven-branched lampstand (Ex 25:31-37) — the only light source in the Holy Place
  - Oil kept burning continually (Ex 27:20) — the Spirit's unceasing ministry
  - Without this light, the priest would serve in total darkness

- **The Incense — Christ's intercession**
  - "Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them" (Heb 7:25)
  - Incense burned morning and evening on the golden altar (Ex 30:7-8)
  - Its fragrance mingled with the prayers of the people: "Let my prayer be set forth before thee as incense" (Ps 141:2)
  - Christ's righteousness — the "sweet savour" — makes our prayers acceptable

- **The High Priest — Christ Himself**
  - "Now of the things which we have spoken this is the sum: We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2)
  - The high priest bore Israel's names on his breastplate over his heart (Ex 28:29) — Christ carries His people
  - The high priest alone entered the Most Holy Place (Lev 16:17) — Christ alone mediates
  - [→] Every piece of furniture, every act of ministry, every ingredient of incense — all Christ, all the time

The earthly sanctuary existed so that fallen humans could understand Christ's heavenly work through visible, tangible symbols. Without it, the plan of salvation remained abstract.

[ILL] Imagine walking through the sanctuary gate. You see the altar — that's the cross. You see the laver — that's baptism and cleansing. You step inside and see bread — that's Christ feeding you. You see light — that's Christ guiding you. You smell incense — that's Christ praying for you. You look toward the veil — behind it, a High Priest stands in your name. There is no room in this building that is not about Jesus.

[TANGENT] The veil separating the Holy from the Most Holy Place was embroidered with cherubim (Ex 26:31) — the same cherubim that guarded the way to the tree of life (Gen 3:24). The veil is the barrier sin created. When Christ died, "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:51). From the top — God tore it, not man. The barrier is removed.

## The Way Is Open NOW

- **Christ entered the greater tabernacle**
  - "But Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands, that is to say, not of this building; neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption for us" (Heb 9:11-12)
  - Not animal blood — His own
  - Not an earthly copy — the heavenly reality
  - Not repeatedly — once, with permanent effect

- **Boldness to enter — a new and living way**
  - "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" (Heb 10:19-20)
  - "Boldness" — not timidity, not uncertainty
  - "New and living" — not dead ritual but a risen Christ
  - "Through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" — Christ's broken body is the open door

- **Come boldly — the throne of grace**
  - "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need" (Heb 4:16)
  - The throne is not a courtroom bench — it is a throne of **grace**
  - Mercy for past failure; grace for present need
  - "In time of need" — not after you've cleaned yourself up, but when you need it most
  - [→] The bridge is open. The High Priest is there. The invitation stands.

[Q] **"If the way is already open, why does it matter that I understand the sanctuary?"**
→ Because understanding the sanctuary is understanding Christ's current ministry — what He is doing for you right now. A gift unopened is still a gift, but it does you no good. "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" (Hosea 4:6). The sanctuary reveals not just what Christ did at the cross but what He does at the throne.

## Appeal

- The bridge between heaven and earth was shattered by sin
- God did not wait for you to rebuild it — He moved first
- He called in the garden, promised at the tree, sacrificed at the gate
- He showed Jacob the ladder and told Moses to build the pattern
- Every lamb, every flame, every grain of incense whispered one name: **Christ**
- And now the type has met the antitype — Christ Himself ministers in the heavenly sanctuary
- The veil is torn. The way is open. The High Priest knows your name.
- Will you come boldly?
