# Study 6: Prophecy Names the System

**God did not leave His people guessing. Through Daniel, He gave prophecies so specific that the power consolidating every counterfeit is identified by name, by character, and by calendar — and every detail has been fulfilled.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 7 (whole chapter)
- Revelation 13:1-10
- Ezekiel 4:6

## Daniel 7 — The Same Empires, Unmasked

- **four beasts from the sea — same sequence, new lens**
  - "Daniel spake and said, I saw in my vision by night, and, behold, the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea. And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another" (Dan 7:2-3)
  - Daniel 2 showed political succession through metals — how empires look from the outside
  - Daniel 7 reveals beastly character — how empires look from heaven's perspective
  - [→] Man sees kingdoms as gold and silver. God sees them as predators.

- **Lion — Babylon**
  - "The first was like a lion, and had eagle's wings" (Dan 7:4)
  - Winged lions guarded Babylon's gates — the symbol is unmistakable
  - Wings plucked, made to stand as a man — Nebuchadnezzar's humiliation and restoration (Dan 4)

- **Bear — Medo-Persia**
  - "And behold another beast, a second, like to a bear, and it raised up itself on one side" (Dan 7:5)
  - Raised on one side — Persia dominated the alliance
  - "Three ribs in the mouth of it" — Babylon, Lydia, Egypt conquered

- **Leopard — Greece**
  - "After this I beheld, and lo another, like a leopard, which had upon the back of it four wings of a fowl; the beast had also four heads" (Dan 7:6)
  - Four wings — speed of Alexander's conquest (conquered the known world by age 30)
  - Four heads — the four generals who divided the empire after his death

- **The terrible beast — Rome**
  - "After this I saw in the night visions, and behold a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth" (Dan 7:7)
  - No animal in nature could represent it — beyond classification
  - Iron teeth — connecting directly to the iron legs of Daniel 2
  - "It devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it" (Dan 7:7)

[DYK🔎] Daniel 7 is the only chapter in the Bible that uses four different animals for four successive kingdoms AND gives the interpretation within the same chapter (Dan 7:17-23). God was not being cryptic — He was building a case.

## Ten Horns and One Little Horn

- **ten horns — the breakup of Rome**
  - "And it had... ten horns" (Dan 7:7)
  - The ten divisions of Western Rome — the barbarian kingdoms that replaced it
  - Same as the ten toes of Daniel 2's image — iron and clay, partly strong and partly broken

- **the little horn rises among the ten**
  - "I considered the horns, and, behold, there came up among them another little horn, before whom there were three of the first horns plucked up by the roots" (Dan 7:8)
  - Rises AMONG the ten — geographically within Western Europe
  - Three horns uprooted — the Heruli (493), Vandals (534), and Ostrogoths (538) — three Arian kingdoms that opposed papal authority were eliminated one by one
  - [→] Not a political power replacing another political power. Something different.

- **"eyes like the eyes of a man, and a mouth speaking great things" (Dan 7:8)**
  - Eyes of a man — human intelligence, human leadership at the head
  - Mouth speaking great things — claims of authority that exceed any earthly government
  - [→] A power with a human leader making superhuman claims. Religious and political fused.

[Q] **"Why does Daniel call it 'little'?"**

> Because it didn't start with military conquest. It rose small and grew — not by the sword initially, but by religious influence that accumulated political authority. The littleness is its origin, not its outcome.

## Three Identifying Marks — Daniel 7:25

**This is the core of the study. Three marks. One verse. No ambiguity.**

- **"And he shall speak great words against the most High"** (Dan 7:25a)

- **Mark 1: Blasphemy — claiming divine prerogatives**
  - "Great words against the most High" — not merely criticizing God, but assuming His role
  - What constitutes blasphemy? Scripture defines it:
  - "Why doth this man thus speak blasphemies? who can forgive sins but God only?" (Mark 2:7)
  - Claiming authority to forgive sins = blasphemy (unless you are God)
  - "The Jews answered him, saying, For a good work we stone thee not; but for blasphemy; and because that thou, being a man, makest thyself God" (John 10:33)
  - A man claiming to stand in God's place = blasphemy (unless you are God)
  - [→] Any human institution that claims the power to forgive sins and to speak with divine authority on earth fulfills this mark. Scripture is clear on what blasphemy means — it leaves no room for reinterpretation.

- **"And shall wear out the saints of the most High"** (Dan 7:25b)

- **Mark 2: Persecution — systematic, prolonged, institutional**
  - "Wear out" — not a single event, but sustained grinding pressure
  - Not mobs acting spontaneously — institutional, authorized, official policy
  - The Inquisition. The persecution of Waldenses, Albigenses, Huguenots. Millions martyred.
  - "And I saw the woman drunken with the blood of the saints, and with the blood of the martyrs of Jesus" (Rev 17:6)
  - [→] Any power that both claims divine authority AND systematically persecutes dissenters has fulfilled two marks. These narrow the field dramatically.

- **"And think to change times and laws"** (Dan 7:25c)

- **Mark 3: Tampering with God's law**
  - "Think to change" — not actually change (God's law is immutable), but claim the authority to change
  - "Times and laws" — not civil law. The law of the Most High. Sacred time.
  - Which commandment involves both time and law? The fourth: "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy" (Exod 20:8)
  - The Sabbath — God's sign of authority, His seal in the law (Exod 31:13, 17)
  - Shifted from the seventh day (Saturday) to the first day (Sunday) — not by Scripture, but by claimed ecclesiastical authority
  - [→] The power itself admits this change rests on its own authority, not on any biblical command. The mark is not hidden — it is boasted in.

[ILL] If someone broke into a courthouse, removed a judge's ruling, and replaced it with their own — that would not just be vandalism. It would be a direct claim of authority over the judge. Changing God's law is not a minor doctrinal adjustment. It is a claim to sit above the Lawgiver.

## Four Counterfeits Consolidated

- **Study 5 traced four counterfeits through four empires. The little horn gathered them all.**

- **From Babylon: human priesthood mediating access to God**
  - The sanctuary teaches one Mediator: "For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus" (1 Tim 2:5)
  - The counterfeit: a human priest class standing between the sinner and God
  - Confessional, priestly absolution, required human intermediaries

- **From Persia: works-based merit system**
  - The sanctuary teaches righteousness by faith: "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God" (Eph 2:8)
  - The counterfeit: penance, indulgences, accumulated merit — salvation as transaction

- **From Greece: natural immortality of the soul**
  - The sanctuary teaches that death is sleep until resurrection: "For the living know that they shall die: but the dead know not any thing" (Eccl 9:5)
  - The counterfeit: immortal soul doctrine — borrowed from Platonic philosophy, not Scripture
  - This doctrine opens the door to purgatory, prayers for the dead, saint veneration, and spiritualism

- **From Rome: Sunday sacredness replacing the Sabbath**
  - The sanctuary is anchored in the Sabbath — creation's memorial, sealed in the law
  - The counterfeit: Sunday observance, established by imperial and ecclesiastical decree, not by "Thus saith the LORD"

- **Daniel saw this merger in the feet of the image**
  - "And whereas thou sawest the feet and toes, part of potters' clay, and part of iron, the kingdom shall be divided" (Dan 2:41)
  - Iron (Rome) mixed with clay (religion) — church and state fused
  - "They shall mingle themselves with the seed of men: but they shall not cleave one to another" (Dan 2:43)
  - [→] Every counterfeit that passed through pagan empires found its permanent home in one system. This is not coincidence — it is consolidation.

[TANGENT] The individual doctrines (human mediators, works salvation, immortal soul, Sunday) may seem like separate theological disagreements. But seen through the sanctuary lens, they form a unified system — each one blocking a different facet of the sanctuary truth. Together, they obscure the entire Way Home.

## The 1260 Years — Prophetic Time Fulfilled

- **"A time and times and the dividing of time" (Dan 7:25)**
  - Time (1) + times (2) + dividing of time (½) = 3½ prophetic years
  - 3½ years × 360 days/year = 1260 days
  - Same period appears seven times in Scripture: Dan 7:25, Dan 12:7, Rev 11:2, Rev 11:3, Rev 12:6, Rev 12:14, Rev 13:5

- **Day-year principle — Scripture interprets itself**
  - "I have appointed thee each day for a year" (Ezek 4:6)
  - "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, each day for a year" (Num 14:34)
  - 1260 prophetic days = 1260 literal years

- **538 AD to 1798 AD — exact fulfillment**
  - 538 AD: Justinian's decree establishing papal supremacy fully operative; last Arian opposition removed
  - 1798 AD: Napoleon's General Berthier enters Rome, takes the pope captive — the deadly wound
  - 1260 years to the year

- **The deadly wound — and its healing**
  - "And I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast" (Rev 13:3)
  - The wound was dealt in 1798. The healing is ongoing.
  - [→] If the wound is healing, this prophecy is not merely historical. You are living in it.

> "Paganism, while appearing to be vanquished, became the conqueror." (GC 49.2)

[Q] **"Are we attacking people who belong to this system?"**

> Never. God has faithful children in every church and every institution. Prophecy identifies the SYSTEM, not the individuals within it. "And other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring" (John 10:16). The call of Revelation 18:4 is "Come out of her, MY PEOPLE" — God calls them His people even while they are still inside. Identifying the system is not condemnation — it is the necessary first step to calling people out of it.
