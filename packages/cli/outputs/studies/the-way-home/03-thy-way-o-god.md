# Study 3: Thy Way, O God

**Sin didn't just break a rule — it shattered a portrait. The image of God in humanity was His character, and His character is His law, and His law is love. God cannot lower the standard without ceasing to be Himself. So He didn't change the rules. He built a path — station by station, sacrifice by sacrifice — to restore what was lost. That path is the sanctuary. That path is Christ.**

## Prep Reading

- Exodus 33:18–34:7
- Genesis 1:26-27; 5:1-3
- Luke 10:25-28
- 1 John 4:7-8
- Psalm 77:13
- Hebrews 9:1-12

## The Problem — Why You Can't Just Come Back

- **God's presence is fire to sin**
  - "The sinners in Zion are afraid; fearfulness hath surprised the hypocrites. Who among us shall dwell with the everlasting burnings?" (Isa 33:14)
  - This is not a threat — it is a description. God's holiness is inherently consuming to sin. Not because He is angry, but because He is pure.
  - "For our God is a consuming fire" (Heb 12:29)
  - "Who shall ascend into the hill of the LORD? or who shall stand in his holy place? He that hath clean hands, and a pure heart" (Ps 24:3-4)

- **Sin created an existential gap — not an emotional one**
  - "But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you" (Isa 59:2)
  - Study 2 established that sin separates. This study asks: _why?_
  - Not because God is offended and walks away. Because if He draws near, the sinner cannot survive the encounter.
  - The cherubim at Eden's gate (Gen 3:24) were not a punishment — they were a protection. The way to the tree of life passed through the presence of God, and the presence of God is lethal to sin.
  - [→] The problem is not that God _won't_ come near you. It is that if He does, you won't survive it. The sanctuary is how He solves that.

[Q] **"Does God want to destroy sinners?"**
→ No. "Have I any pleasure at all that the wicked should die? saith the Lord GOD: and not that he should return from his ways, and live?" (Eze 18:23). God does not desire death. But He cannot un-be Himself. His holiness is not a weapon He wields — it is who He is. The sanctuary is His answer: how to bring sinners into His presence without destroying them.

## What Was Lost — The Image of God

- **Created in the image**
  - "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness" (Gen 1:26)
  - "So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them" (Gen 1:27)
  - This is the original design — humanity as a reflection of God

- **The image marred**
  - "And Adam lived an hundred and thirty years, and begat a son in his own likeness, after his image" (Gen 5:3)
  - Genesis 1:27 — created in _God's_ image. Genesis 5:3 — bearing _Adam's_ image.
  - The shift is devastating. The portrait was not erased — humanity still bears traces of the divine likeness (Gen 9:6; James 3:9) — but it was so deeply marred that what passed to Adam's children was a fallen, distorted reflection rather than a clear one.
  - [→] The entire plan of salvation is about restoring what was damaged between Genesis 1:27 and Genesis 5:3. Everything in between — and everything after — is the story of that restoration.

- **What is the image?**
  - Not physical form — "God is a Spirit" (John 4:24). The image was never about body shape.
  - The image is _character_ — the moral and relational likeness of God reproduced in humanity
  - But what _is_ God's character? How do you define it?

## Show Me Thy Glory — God Defines Himself

- **Moses' request**
  - "And he said, I beseech thee, shew me thy glory" (Ex 33:18)
  - Moses asks to _see_ glory. He expects a vision — light, fire, overwhelming splendor.

- **God's answer — not sight but speech**
  - "And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live" (Ex 33:20)
  - The unmediated fullness of God's character would destroy a sinful being — not because God strikes, but because infinite holiness and unrepented sin cannot coexist
  - "And the LORD said... I will make all my goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the LORD before thee" (Ex 33:19)
  - Moses asked to _see_ glory. God said: I will _proclaim my name_. Glory is not a visual display — it is a character declaration.

- **The proclamation — God's self-portrait**
  - "And the LORD descended in the cloud, and stood with him there, and proclaimed the name of the LORD. And the LORD passed by before him, and proclaimed, The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear the guilty" (Ex 34:5-7)
  - This is God defining God. No prophet's interpretation. No theologian's system. God Himself tells you what He is like.
  - Merciful — compassion for the suffering
  - Gracious — generosity to the undeserving
  - Longsuffering — patience that outlasts rebellion
  - Abundant in goodness and truth — overflowing integrity
  - Keeping mercy for thousands — covenant faithfulness across generations
  - Forgiving iniquity, transgression, and sin — every category of failure covered
  - Will by no means clear the guilty — justice is real; mercy does not erase accountability
  - [→] This is the character that was imaged in humanity at creation. This is what was lost at the fall. This is what the sanctuary restores.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for "glory" (כָּבוֹד, _kavod_) literally means "weight" or "heaviness." When Moses asked to see God's glory, he was asking to experience the _full weight_ of who God is. God's answer was to speak His character. The weight of God is not His brightness — it is His goodness.

- **The glory made visible — Christ**
  - "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father" (John 14:9)
  - "Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature" (Col 1:15)
  - "Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person" (Heb 1:3)
  - "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth" (John 1:14)
  - Christ is the Exodus 34:6-7 attributes walking among humanity
  - Every healing — mercy. Every parable — truth. Every confrontation with Pharisees — justice. Every meal with sinners — grace. Every prayer in Gethsemane — longsuffering.
  - [→] If you want to know what God looks like, don't look up. Look at Jesus.

[ILL] A child who has never met his father finds a portrait in the attic — faded, cracked, barely recognizable. That portrait is Genesis 1:27 after the fall. Then the father himself walks through the door. That is the incarnation. You don't need the portrait anymore — you have the Person. But the portrait tells you what the family was always meant to look like.

## The Standard That Cannot Change

- **The law is God's character in commandment form**
  - "Master, which is the great commandment in the law? Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the first and great commandment. And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets" (Matt 22:36-40)
  - The law hangs on love. Every commandment is an expression of love — toward God (first four) and toward others (last six).

- **God is love**
  - "He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love" (1 John 4:8)
  - The law is summarized by love. God _is_ love. Therefore: the law is a transcript of God's character.
  - This is not a metaphor. It is a logical identity. If A = B and B = C, then A = C.
  - Walk the commandments and you walk through God's character:
  - "Thou shalt not bear false witness" (Ex 20:16) — because God is truth (John 14:6). He cannot lie (Titus 1:2). The commandment is not an arbitrary rule — it is His nature expressed as instruction.
  - "Thou shalt not kill" (Ex 20:13) — because God is the Author and Giver of life (Acts 17:25). To destroy life is to act contrary to His very essence.
  - "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy" (Ex 20:8) — because God is Creator and Restorer (Ex 20:11; Eze 20:12). The Sabbath declares who He is and what He does. It is His signature in time.
  - [→] Every commandment, without exception, is a character trait of God translated into human conduct. To keep the law is to reflect the Lawgiver.

- **The law is the standard of life**
  - "And, behold, a certain lawyer stood up, and tempted him, saying, Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life? He said unto him, What is written in the law? how readest thou?" (Luke 10:25-26)
  - Jesus points the lawyer to the law — not as a burden but as the definition of life
  - "And he answering said, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy strength, and with all thy mind; and thy neighbour as thyself. And he said unto him, Thou hast answered right: this do, and thou shalt live" (Luke 10:27-28)
  - [→] If the law = God's character, and the law = the standard of life, then the standard of life is having God's character. The test of heaven has never changed.

- **Why the standard cannot be lowered**
  - If the law were arbitrary rules, God could simply change them. Edit the list. Lower the bar.
  - But if the law is a transcript of who God _is_, then abolishing the law would mean abolishing Himself. God cannot un-love. He cannot un-be just. He cannot un-be merciful.
  - "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil" (Matt 5:17)
  - "Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law" (Matt 5:18)
  - [→] Salvation is not God changing the rules. It is God giving everything He has to redeem you _within_ the rules. The standard stands. The question is: how does a sinner meet it?

[Q] **"If no one can keep the law, why not just get rid of it?"**
→ For the same reason you don't smash the mirror because you don't like what it shows you. The law is diagnostic — "by the law is the knowledge of sin" (Rom 3:20). Destroying the diagnostic tool doesn't cure the disease. And the law is more than diagnostic — it is the portrait of who you were created to be. You don't destroy the blueprint; you rebuild to match it.

[TANGENT] The law was kept inside the ark of the covenant — beneath the mercy seat, within the Most Holy Place, under the Shekinah glory. Think about the spatial theology: the law is at the _center_ of the sanctuary, at the _heart_ of God's dwelling. Not tacked onto the door as a list of restrictions — placed in the innermost chamber as the defining reality of God's presence. Where God dwells, His character defines the space.

- **The charge this answers**
  - Study 1 identified Satan's accusation: "The law cannot be kept" (Charge B). But now we see what that charge really means.
  - If the law is God's character, then "the law cannot be kept" is the same as "God's image cannot be restored in humanity." Satan is not merely arguing against obedience — he is arguing that what God lost in Eden, He can never recover.
  - The sanctuary is God's answer to that charge — not a courtroom defense, but a working demonstration. Station by station, it transforms the sinner back into the image of God. The charge says it can't be done. The sanctuary does it.

## The Sanctuary — God's Path of Restoration

- **"Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary"**
  - "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary: who is so great a God as our God?" (Ps 77:13)
  - "Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" (John 14:6)
  - The way is in the sanctuary. The way is Christ. These are not two separate ideas — they are one revelation from two angles.
  - The sanctuary is not a building you visit — it is a journey you walk. And every station transforms you.

- **The purpose — "that I may dwell among them"**
  - "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them" (Ex 25:8)
  - Not "dwell in it" — "dwell among _them_"
  - The building is the means; the people are the goal
  - God's presence burns sin (Isa 33:14). The sanctuary is the process by which He makes it safe for sinners to be in that presence — not by dimming His glory, but by transforming the sinner.

[DYK🔎] The Hebrew word for sanctuary is מִקְדָּשׁ (_miqdash_), from the root קדשׁ (_qadash_) — "to be holy, to set apart." The sanctuary is literally "the holy place" — but its purpose is to make _you_ holy. The holiness flows outward from the Shekinah through every station until it reaches the sinner at the gate.

## The Courtyard — Justification

- **The altar of burnt offering — death of the old life**
  - "Now this is that which thou shalt offer upon the altar; two lambs of the first year day by day continually" (Ex 29:38)
  - The sinner brings the lamb. He lays his hand on its head (Lev 1:4) — identification, transference. He kills it himself (Lev 1:5).
  - This is not remote. You feel the warmth, the weight, the life leaving. Your sin did this.
  - "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" (John 1:29)
  - "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him" (2 Cor 5:21)
  - [→] The altar is not God punishing an innocent animal. It is God showing you what sin costs — and then paying it Himself.

- **The laver — washing and power**
  - "And thou shalt set the laver between the tabernacle of the congregation and the altar, and shalt put water therein" (Ex 40:30)
  - The priests washed at the laver before entering the Holy Place — cleansing was required for service
  - "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost" (Titus 3:5)
  - "Buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with him through the faith of the operation of God, who hath raised him from the dead" (Col 2:12)
  - "Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost" (Acts 2:38)

- **Justification is forgiveness AND power — simultaneously**
  - The altar deals with guilt. The laver deals with defilement and grants new life. Both happen at the courtyard — both happen at once.
  - Mark 2 — the paralytic: "Son, thy sins be forgiven thee" AND "Arise, and take up thy bed, and walk" (Mark 2:5, 11). Forgiveness and power in the same breath.
  - John 8 — the woman in adultery: "Neither do I condemn thee" AND "go, and sin no more" (John 8:11). Pardon and enablement in the same sentence.
  - Christ does not command what He does not enable. "Go and sin no more" is not a demand shouted at a cripple — it is a gift given with the legs to walk on.
  - [→] Justification is not just God's declaration over you. It is God's _initial_ transformation — forgiven and empowered, pardoned and raised. The courtyard grants new birth; the Holy Place grows what was born. Regeneration begins here. Sanctification continues inside.

[ILL] A man drowning in the ocean is rescued by a boat. He is pulled from the water — that is forgiveness, the removal of condemnation. But the rescue crew doesn't throw him back in and say "now swim." They set him on the deck — solid ground, new footing. The laver is the deck. You don't just get pulled out of death. You get placed into life.

- **The Passover and Unleavened Bread — the courtyard feasts**
  - "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" (1 Cor 5:7) — the altar in feast form
  - The Passover lamb died on the 14th of Nisan — Christ died on Passover (John 19:14)
  - Unleavened Bread followed immediately — leaven represents sin (1 Cor 5:8). The old leaven is purged. New life, unleavened, begins.
  - [→] The feast calendar confirms the courtyard pattern: death of the substitute, then immediate removal of the old life. Justification is decisive, not gradual.

## The Holy Place — Sanctification

- **The daily walk — not a moment but a journey**
  - The courtyard was an event. The Holy Place is a process. The priest entered daily — morning and evening (Ex 29:38-39). Sanctification is sustained communion, not a single crisis.
  - "This is the will of God, even your sanctification" (1 Thess 4:3)
  - The justified sinner is now free. Free to go anywhere. Free to walk home — or to wander. Sanctification is the command to walk home, not the force that drags you there.

- **The table of showbread — the Word of God**
  - "And thou shalt set upon the table shewbread before me alway" (Ex 25:30)
  - Twelve loaves, set fresh every Sabbath (Lev 24:5-8) — constant provision, never stale
  - "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" (Deut 8:3)
  - "I am the living bread which came down from heaven" (John 6:51)
  - "Thy words were found, and I did eat them; and thy word was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart" (Jer 15:16)
  - Character is formed by what you consume. The showbread is the daily intake of God's Word — not information but sustenance. You become what you eat.

- **The lampstand — the Spirit's light through you**
  - "And thou shalt make a candlestick of pure gold" (Ex 25:31)
  - "Command the children of Israel, that they bring unto thee pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamps to burn continually" (Lev 24:2)
  - Oil in Scripture is the Holy Spirit (1 Sam 16:13 — anointing; Zech 4:6 — "not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit")
  - The Spirit was _received_ at the laver (justification). The lampstand is the Spirit _working through_ you — illumination becoming witness
  - "Ye are the light of the world. A city that is set on an hill cannot be hid" (Matt 5:14)
  - "Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven" (Matt 5:16)
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - [→] Beholding transforms. The sanctuary teaches you _what_ to behold. Christ shows you _who_ to behold. The Spirit does the transforming. The image of God is restored by sustained looking, not by self-effort.

[DYK🔎] The seven-branched lampstand was the only source of light in the Holy Place — there were no windows. Without the Spirit's illumination, the priest would serve in total darkness. Without the Holy Spirit, the Christian walks blind — even if the bread is on the table and the incense is burning. The Spirit makes everything else visible.

- **The altar of incense — prayer**
  - "And Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning: when he dresseth the lamps, he shall burn incense upon it. And when Aaron lighteth the lamps at even, he shall burn incense upon it, a perpetual incense before the LORD" (Ex 30:7-8)
  - "Let my prayer be set forth before thee as incense; and the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice" (Ps 141:2)
  - "And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne" (Rev 8:3)
  - The incense altar stood directly before the veil — closest to the Most Holy Place. Prayer is the nearest approach to God's presence in the daily service.
  - "Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered" (Rom 8:26)
  - [→] The Word feeds. The Spirit illuminates. Prayer communicates. These three sustain the daily walk — bread for strength, light for direction, incense for communion. Remove any one and sanctification stalls.

- **Pentecost — the Holy Place feast**
  - "And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind" (Acts 2:1-2)
  - Pentecost fell fifty days after Firstfruits — fifty days after the resurrection. The Spirit was poured out in power, not for the first time, but in fullness.
  - The disciples had already been converted (courtyard). Pentecost was _commission_ — the Spirit empowering them for witness, service, and daily transformation.
  - [→] Pentecost is the Holy Place inaugurated. The same Spirit that raised Christ from the dead now indwells His people for the daily work of sanctification and witness.

## The Most Holy Place — Glorification

- **The ark of the covenant — the standard within the mercy**
  - "And thou shalt put the mercy seat above upon the ark; and in the ark thou shalt put the testimony that I shall give thee" (Ex 25:21)
  - Inside the ark: the two tables of stone — "the testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God" (Ex 31:18)
  - The covenant _is_ the Ten Commandments (Deut 4:13). The law is inside the ark. The mercy seat is above the law. The Shekinah — God's presence — dwells above the mercy seat, between the cherubim.
  - [→] The spatial arrangement is the theology: the law defines the standard. Mercy covers it. God's presence rests where justice and mercy meet. This is not contradiction — it is completion.

- **The law written on the heart — the image restored**
  - "But this shall be the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel; After those days, saith the LORD, I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my people" (Jer 31:33)
  - At Sinai, the law was written on stone — external, imposed, unyielding. In the new covenant, the same law is written on the heart — internal, desired, lived.
  - The content hasn't changed. The _location_ has. Stone to flesh. Outside to inside. Obligation to identity.
  - "Create in me a clean heart, O God; and renew a right spirit within me" (Ps 51:10)
  - [→] When the law of God becomes the desire of your heart, the image of God is restored. You no longer keep the commandments because you must — you keep them because they are who you have become. This is glorification of character.

- **The Day of Atonement — the Most Holy Place feast**
  - Once a year the high priest entered the Most Holy Place (Lev 16:15-16) — the only day, the only access
  - He brought blood and incense — atonement for the accumulated sin-record of the year
  - "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD" (Lev 16:30)
  - The Day of Atonement was a day of judgment — who was genuinely in covenant, who merely appeared to be
  - It is the completion of the sanctuary process: the sin that was confessed at the altar, the character that was shaped in the Holy Place, is now _sealed_ in the Most Holy Place
  - [→] The Day of Atonement will receive its full treatment later in this series. For now, see the pattern: the sinner who entered at the gate covered in the lamb's blood now stands, by faith, in the presence of God — cleansed, transformed, sealed.

[Q] **"Is glorification about character or about receiving an immortal body?"**
→ Both — but in sequence. The glorification of character happens _before_ Christ returns: the law written on the heart, the image fully restored, the seal of God in the forehead (Rev 7:3). The glorification of the body happens _at_ His return: "this mortal must put on immortality" (1 Cor 15:53). The sanctuary maps the first. The Second Coming delivers the second. Character first, then body — because God will not immortalize what has not been transformed.

## The Sanctuary Is Christ — And That Is Why It Transforms

- **Every station transforms you because every station is Him**
  - Study 2 showed that each article of the sanctuary identifies with Christ — the altar is His sacrifice, the bread is His Word, the light is His Spirit, the incense is His intercession. That was identification. This is implication:
  - The sanctuary transforms because you cannot walk through Christ and remain unchanged. You are not navigating a system — you are being conformed to a Person.
  - "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory" (2 Cor 3:18)
  - Beholding _Him_ at every station is what restores the image. The furniture is not the point — the Person behind it is.

- **The High Priest walks the path with you**
  - "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2)
  - "For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin" (Heb 4:15)
  - He is not a distant administrator. He has walked this path Himself — tempted, tested, faithful. He ministers from experience, not theory. He does not point the way from heaven; He walks it with you from the altar to the Shekinah.

- **The way and the sanctuary are the same Person**
  - "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary" (Ps 77:13)
  - "I am the way" (John 14:6)
  - The sanctuary is not a system you navigate — it is a Person you follow
  - From the gate to the Shekinah, from justification to glorification, from the shattered image to the restored one — every step is with Him, through Him, in Him

> "Christ is the connecting link in the chain of humanity uniting earth with heaven and the finite with the infinite." (ST, June 26, 1893)

## Appeal

The image of God in you was shattered at the fall. But the portrait was never thrown away — it was placed at the center of the sanctuary, written in stone, covered by mercy, illuminated by glory.

God did not lower the standard. He could not. The standard is Himself. Instead, He built a way — through blood, through water, through bread and light and incense and law and mercy — to bring you from what you are to what you were meant to be.

The way is in the sanctuary. The way is Christ. And He is walking it with you.

Will you follow?
