# Study 9: Conquering Canaan — How Transformation Works

**Coming out of Babylon clears the ground. But a swept house left empty invites worse tenants. God did not call you out of the counterfeit just to leave you hollow — He called you into Canaan, where every giant sin is conquered, every fortified habit dismantled, and His character fills the space the counterfeits once occupied. The question is not "Does God have power to transform?" but "Will you show up to the war room?"**

## Prep Reading

- Numbers 13:26 - 14:9
- Deuteronomy 7:17-23
- Joshua 5:13-15
- Galatians 5:22-25
- Psalm 1

## Why We Try to Help God

- **the fig leaf instinct** — inherited from Eden
  - Adam's first act after sin: cover himself (Gen 3:7)
  - self-effort predates even the first human conversation about sin
  - we are born into a race that tries to fix what only God can heal

- **cooperation vs. contribution** — the critical distinction
  - God asks for **cooperation** (surrender, participation on His terms)
  - we offer **contribution** (our ideas, our methods, our timing)
  - "And Sarai said unto Abram... I pray thee, go in unto my maid" (Gen 16:2)
    - God promised a son — Sarah "helped"
    - result: Ishmael, not Isaac
    - [→] helping God always produces an Ishmael

- **old covenant error defined**
  - "All that the LORD hath spoken we will do" (Ex 19:8)
  - promise made in their own strength, not God's
  - Sinai broken within 40 days

[Q] **"But doesn't God want us to do something?"**

> Yes — but what? "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling. For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure" (Phil 2:12-13). God works **in** us; we work **out** what He works in. The order matters.

[→] If we've always been prone to "help" God, where did this promise actually begin?

## The Promise of Canaan: Where It All Began

- **God's covenant with Abraham**
  - "Get thee out of thy country... unto a land that I will shew thee" (Gen 12:1)
  - Abraham left everything on a promise — no map, no contract, just God's word
  - "And I will give unto thee, and to thy seed after thee, the land wherein thou art a stranger, all the land of Canaan, for an everlasting possession" (Gen 17:8)

- **the promise was always bigger than real estate**
  - Abraham "looked for a city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God" (Heb 11:10)
  - "These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having seen them afar off" (Heb 11:13)
  - the land pointed forward — to a restored character, a restored earth, an eternal inheritance
  - [→] Canaan was a type; the antitype is something far greater

- **400 years of waiting**
  - "Know of a surety that thy seed shall be a stranger in a land that is not theirs... four hundred years" (Gen 15:13)
  - God told Abraham the timeline — generations would pass before possession
  - Egypt = bondage to sin; Exodus = deliverance by God's power; Canaan = the character He promised to give
  - the whole arc: **promise → waiting → deliverance → conquest**
  - [→] Study 8 was the Exodus — coming out of Babylon. This study is the conquest — what happens after you leave.

- **the promise renewed at the border**
  - Moses reminds Israel: "The LORD thy God bringeth thee into a good land" (Deut 8:7)
  - "Not for thy righteousness... for thou art a stiffnecked people" (Deut 9:6)
  - the land was never earned — it was **given**. The conquest was never about Israel's strength — it was about God's faithfulness to His promise to Abraham.

[→] So what does Canaan represent for us today?

## Canaan as Character: The Central Typology

- **Canaan = your mind, your character**
  - the promised land was not just geography — it was inheritance
  - what God promised Abraham physically, He promises us spiritually
  - "For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence stedfast unto the end" (Heb 3:14)
  - as Israel must possess Canaan for rest, we must possess Christlike character for heaven
  - "There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God" (Heb 4:9)

- **God promised conquest**
  - "The LORD thy God, he will go over before thee, and he will destroy these nations from before thee, and thou shalt possess them" (Deut 31:3)
  - the promise was clear: **God would conquer**
  - Israel's role: march, trust, obey

### The Spy Report: Faith's Test (Numbers 13-14)

- **the reconnaissance mission**
  - twelve spies sent to survey the land (Num 13:1-2)
  - all twelve saw the same land
  - all twelve saw the same giants

- **two reports, one reality**
  - **ten spies (unbelief):**
    - "We be not able to go up against the people; for they are stronger than we" (Num 13:31)
    - "And there we saw the giants... and we were in our own sight as grasshoppers" (Num 13:33)
    - magnified the problem, minimized God
  - **two spies (faith):**
    - "Let us go up at once, and possess it; for we are well able to overcome it" (Num 13:30)
    - "If the LORD delight in us, then he will bring us into this land" (Num 14:8)
    - same giants — different focus

[ILL] Two men look through prison bars. One sees mud, the other sees stars. The giants were real. The walls were high. But God was bigger than all of it.

> What you focus on determines what you believe is possible.

- **the giants in your character are real**
  - anger — real
  - lust — real
  - pride — real
  - fear — real
  - but God is bigger than every one of them

[→] The majority report is often the unbelief report. Faithfulness may be unpopular. But if Canaan is character, why doesn't God change us instantly?

## The "Little by Little" Principle

**Controlling claim: God removes enemies gradually because vacant ground is dangerous. Conquered territory must be occupied by His Word — or something worse moves in.**

- **God's method revealed**
  - "And the LORD thy God will put out those nations before thee **little by little**: thou mayest not consume them at once, lest the beasts of the field increase upon thee" (Deut 7:22)
  - sanctification is **progressive**, not instantaneous
  - each victory builds faith for the next battle
  - "The path of the just is as the shining light, that shineth more and more unto the perfect day" (Prov 4:18)

[DYK🔎] "Little by little" (מְעַט מְעַט, _me'at me'at_) appears only here in all of Scripture. God's progressive sanctification method is unique — and intentional.

- **the parable of the empty house** (Matt 12:43-45)
  - "When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man, he walketh through dry places, seeking rest, and findeth none" (Matt 12:43)
  - he returns and finds the house "empty, swept, and garnished" (Matt 12:44)
  - seven worse spirits move in — "the last state of that man is worse than the first" (Matt 12:45)
  - the house was cleaned — but never **filled**
  - [→] This is the danger of leaving Babylon without entering Canaan. You came out — but what moved in? A cleaned-out life without Christ filling it is a vacancy sign for the enemy.

- **what fills the house? The Word — meditated, not merely heard**
  - the unclean spirit wanders "dry places, seeking rest" — contrast with the blessed man of Psalm 1: "planted by the rivers of water" (Ps 1:3)
  - the difference? The Psalm 1 man **meditates** on God's law "day and night" (Ps 1:2)
  - "meditate" (הָגָה, _hagah_) = to murmur, to ruminate, to chew over — like an animal chewing cud
  - listening to sermons is not enough. Hearing podcasts is not enough. Reading casually is not enough.
  - the Word must be taken in, broken down, digested, absorbed into the life
  - "Thy words were found, and I did eat them; and thy word was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart" (Jer 15:16)
  - [→] The tree doesn't go looking for water — it is planted beside it. But it must put down roots. God provides the river; you must drink.
  - "Whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst; but the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life" (John 4:14)
  - Christ implants a **fountain** — an internal, living source. Not external information, but indwelling transformation.
  - result: "bringeth forth his fruit in his season; his leaf also shall not wither" (Ps 1:3) — fruit comes from position, not strain

[→] If the war is gradual and the ground must be occupied, how do we actually fight?

## The Warfare Pattern: Joshua's Model

### The Commander Appears (Joshua 5:13-15)

- **Joshua's encounter**
  - "There stood a man over against him with his sword drawn" (Josh 5:13)
  - Joshua asked: "Art thou for us, or for our adversaries?"
  - answer: "Nay; but as captain of the host of the LORD am I now come" (Josh 5:14)

- **not "for us" — above us**
  - Christ doesn't join our agenda — we join His
  - "And Joshua fell on his face to the earth, and did worship, and said unto him, What saith my Lord unto his servant?" (Josh 5:14)
  - the right response: surrender, then ask for orders

[→] Joshua withdrew from the army **before** meeting the Commander. The war room comes before the battlefield.

### Prayer as Military Intelligence

- **daily convening with the Commander**
  - "Evening, and morning, and at noon, will I pray" (Ps 55:17)
  - "My voice shalt thou hear in the morning, O LORD; in the morning will I direct my prayer unto thee, and will look up" (Ps 5:3)
  - not ritual — reconnaissance

- **what to pray for:**
  - **reveal my blind spots** — "Search me, O God, and know my heart: try me, and know my thoughts: and see if there be any wicked way in me" (Ps 139:23-24)
  - **show me my weak points** — "Who can understand his errors? cleanse thou me from secret faults" (Ps 19:12)
  - **give me today's orders** — "Cause me to know the way wherein I should walk" (Ps 143:8)

### Mapping the Terrain: Self-Examination

- **know your specific sins**
  - not vague "I'm a sinner"
  - specific: "I struggle with impatience with my children at 5pm"
  - specific: "I am prideful when corrected"
  - specific: "I indulge in lustful thoughts when alone"
  - generals don't fight wars with vague objectives

- **identify patterns and triggers**
  - when do you fall? (time of day, fatigue level, emotional state)
  - where do you fall? (location, environment, company)
  - what precedes the fall? (what sequence leads to sin?)
  - "Let us search and try our ways, and turn again to the LORD" (Lam 3:40)

### The Whole Armor of God (Ephesians 6:10-17)

- **Paul uses the same warfare language**
  - "Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil" (Eph 6:11)
  - "For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers" (Eph 6:12)
  - the enemy is spiritual — the equipment must be divine

- **the armor is Christ's character worn as yours**
  - belt of **truth** — honesty before God and self
  - breastplate of **righteousness** — Christ's righteousness imputed and imparted
  - feet shod with the **gospel of peace** — readiness to walk and share
  - shield of **faith** — quenches every fiery dart of doubt
  - helmet of **salvation** — assurance guards the mind
  - sword of the Spirit = **the Word of God** — the only offensive weapon
  - [→] every piece is something God provides. You put it on — He forged it.

[Q] **"Why is the Word the only offensive weapon?"**

> Because truth is what sets free (John 8:32). Christ defeated Satan in the wilderness with "It is written" — three times, nothing else (Matt 4:4, 7, 10). The sword you hold in Canaan is the same sword.

[→] We know the battle and the equipment. But what are we fighting toward?

## The Standard: Christ's Character

- **the target is not abstract**
  - "He that saith he abideth in him ought himself also so to walk, even as he walked" (1 John 2:6)
  - not "try to be good" — **study how Jesus actually lived**

### Making It Concrete

- **how did Christ speak?**
  - "Never man spake like this man" (John 7:46)
  - "Gracious words which proceeded out of his mouth" (Luke 4:22)
  - no harshness, no sarcasm, no cutting remarks
  - "A soft answer turneth away wrath" (Prov 15:1)

- **how did Christ respond to criticism?**
  - "When he was reviled, reviled not again" (1 Pet 2:23)
  - "When he suffered, he threatened not"
  - "But committed himself to him that judgeth righteously"

- **how did Christ treat sinners?**
  - "Neither do I condemn thee: go, and sin no more" (John 8:11)
  - firm on sin — gentle with the sinner
  - truth and grace together

- **what did Christ consume?**
  - "I have meat to eat that ye know not of" (John 4:32)
  - His food: doing the Father's will
  - what do you feed your mind? Eyes? Ears?

- **how did Christ use His time?**
  - "I must work the works of him that sent me, while it is day" (John 9:4)
  - no idle hours, no wasted moments
  - yet not frantic — purposeful rest, deliberate withdrawal

### The Fruit Standard (Galatians 5:22-23)

- **love** — do I act for others' good even when inconvenient?
- **joy** — is my inner state dependent on circumstances or on Christ?
- **peace** — am I anxious, or do I trust God with outcomes?
- **longsuffering** — how do I respond when wronged repeatedly?
- **gentleness** — is my manner harsh or kind — especially under pressure?
- **goodness** — do I actively seek to bless others?
- **faith** — do I trust God's promises when giants appear?
- **meekness** — can I receive correction without defensiveness?
- **temperance** — do I control my appetites, or do they control me?

[→] We know the standard. But how is that character actually formed in us?

## The Means: Knowledge + Faith

### Key 1: Knowledge

- **"My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge"** (Hosea 4:6)
  - not lack of sincerity
  - not lack of effort
  - **lack of knowledge**

- **knowledge of God's character**
  - "And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent" (John 17:3)
  - without knowing who God really is, we cannot trust Him
  - without knowing His promises, we cannot claim them

- **knowledge = the Word = Christ Himself**
  - "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God" (John 1:1)
  - the Word is not just information — it is a Person
  - "If ye abide in me, and my words abide in you, ye shall ask what ye will, and it shall be done unto you" (John 15:7)
  - "I am the vine, ye are the branches... without me ye can do nothing" (John 15:5)
  - [→] Abiding in the Word = abiding in Christ = bearing fruit. The chain is unbreakable.

- **knowledge of your own condition**
  - "The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?" (Jer 17:9)
  - self-knowledge comes through Scripture as mirror (James 1:23-25), prayer for revelation (Ps 139:23-24), and Holy Spirit conviction (John 16:8)

- **knowledge of the enemy's tactics**
  - "Lest Satan should get an advantage of us: for we are not ignorant of his devices" (2 Cor 2:11)
  - know your particular vulnerabilities and how temptation works in your life

### Key 2: Faith

- **"Without faith it is impossible to please him"** (Heb 11:6)
  - not merely intellectual assent
  - faith = trust that acts on God's promises
  - "Faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God" (Rom 10:17)

- **faith looks at the Commander, not the giants**
  - Caleb: "We are well able to overcome" — why? Not self-confidence, but God-confidence
  - "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me" (Phil 4:13)

- **faith claims the promise before seeing the fulfillment**
  - "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen" (Heb 11:1)
  - Abraham "against hope believed in hope" (Rom 4:18)

[→] We know the standard and the means. Now the honest question: is this actually happening in your life?

## The Proof Test: Are You in the Fight?

- **this is not condemnation — it is an invitation to honesty**
  - God doesn't ask us to examine ourselves to crush us
  - He asks because He wants to heal what we're willing to see
  - "I counsel thee to buy of me gold tried in the fire... and anoint thine eyes with eyesalve, that thou mayest see" (Rev 3:18)

- **the command**
  - "Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the faith; prove your own selves" (2 Cor 13:5)
  - **prove** = test, put to trial — not gentle reflection, but honest audit
  - "Know ye not your own selves, how that Jesus Christ is in you, except ye be reprobates?" (2 Cor 13:5b)

- **the logic**
  - God promised transformation → "A new heart also will I give you" (Eze 36:26)
  - God cannot fail → "Hath he said, and shall he not do it?" (Num 23:19)
  - if transformation is not happening, the invitation is not guilt — it is to look deeper
  - where is the blockage? Is the house being filled, or only swept?
  - look at the fruit: is love growing? Is patience deepening? Is temperance strengthening?
  - "Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone" (James 2:17)
  - "By their fruits ye shall know them" (Matt 7:20)

- **the Laodicean danger**
  - "I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing" (Rev 3:17)
  - they thought they were spiritually wealthy — but were "wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked"
  - the greatest threat is not the giant you can see — it's the self-deception you can't
  - but notice: Christ stands at the door and **knocks** (Rev 3:20). He doesn't break in. He waits for you to open.
  - [→] Are you still sewing fig leaves — or are you in Canaan with sword drawn?

## The Synthesis: Surrendered Warfare

- **not passive, not self-effort**
  - passivity says: "God will do it; I'll wait"
  - self-effort says: "I must do it; God will help"
  - faith says: "God will do it through me as I cooperate"

- **the sword is in your hand — the victory is His**
  - "Fight the good fight of faith" (1 Tim 6:12) — you must fight
  - "Thanks be to God, which giveth us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ" (1 Cor 15:57) — He gives victory
  - you cannot win without Him; He will not fight without you

[TANGENT] The daily burnt offering at the sanctuary — morning and evening — pictures daily consecration. The fire never went out (Lev 6:12-13). Continuous surrender, continuous sacrifice.

---

## Appeal

- Daily surrender is the most active thing you will ever do
- It costs everything — it demands attention, intention, discipline
- It is not drifting — it is deliberate
- The Commander is faithful: "God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation also make a way to escape" (1 Cor 10:13)
- He has never lost a battle. He has never broken a promise.
- Every giant conquered in your character is evidence entered in heaven's courtroom.
- What is your Canaan? What are your giants? Name them. Map them. Bring them to the Commander.
- The question is not "Can God conquer?" — the question is: will you show up to the war room tomorrow morning?
