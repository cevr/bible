# Study 11: The Temple of the Holy Spirit

**Salvation is the restoration of God's image — and that image is restored in the mind. But the mind does not exist in a vacuum. It lives in a body. A clouded body produces a clouded mind. A clouded mind cannot behold Christ. And if you cannot behold, you cannot be changed (2 Cor 3:18). The health of the body is not peripheral to the gospel. It is part of the restoration.**

## Prep Reading

- 1 Corinthians 6:19-20
- 1 Corinthians 9:25-27
- Romans 12:1-2
- Genesis 1:29; 9:3-4
- Daniel 1:8-20
- Luke 21:34

---

## Where Restoration Happens

- **A new heart — which is a new mind**
  - "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you" (Eze 36:26)
  - "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts" (Jer 31:33)
  - Study 3 established: salvation = restoration of God's image. That image is restored in the mind — the seat of character, decision, love.

- **Transformed by the renewing of your mind**
  - "Be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God" (Rom 12:2)
  - The word "transformed" is _metamorphoo_ — the same word used for Christ's transfiguration (Matt 17:2)
  - Transformation happens in the mind. The mind is where character lives. The mind is where beholding happens (2 Cor 3:18).
  - [→] If the mind is the seat of restoration, then anything that affects the mind affects the restoration. This is why the body matters.

- **The body-mind link**
  - The mind does not float free of the body. It depends on it.
  - A fevered brain thinks poorly. A drugged mind cannot discern. A malnourished body produces a sluggish mind.
  - If the mind cannot think clearly, it cannot behold clearly. If it cannot behold, it cannot be changed.
  - [→] This is not about health tips. This is about whether the instrument of transformation — the mind — is functional enough to do its work. Satan knows this. It is why appetite was his first weapon.

## Appetite — Satan's First Weapon

- **The first temptation in Eden was appetite**
  - "And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it was pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make one wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat" (Gen 3:6)
  - The first sin was not murder, theft, or idolatry. It was eating what God said not to eat.
  - "Good for food" — appetite. "Pleasant to the eyes" — desire. "Make one wise" — pride.
  - [→] Satan did not begin with doctrine. He began with the body. He knew: control the appetite and you control the person.

- **Christ's first temptation was appetite**
  - "And when he had fasted forty days and forty nights, he was afterward an hungred. And when the tempter came to him, he said, If thou be the Son of God, command that these stones be made bread" (Matt 4:2-3)
  - Christ met the same test Eve failed — appetite under pressure
  - He conquered where she fell. Not by willpower but by the word: "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" (Matt 4:4)
  - [→] The battle for the image begins at the body. Both Eden and the wilderness prove it. Satan's opening move — every time — is appetite.

- **Paul knew the danger**
  - "But I keep under my body, and bring it into subjection: lest that by any means, when I have preached to others, I myself should be a castaway" (1 Cor 9:27)
  - "Every man that striveth for the mastery is temperate in all things. Now they do it to obtain a corruptible crown; but we an incorruptible" (1 Cor 9:25)
  - Paul is not describing health advice. He is describing survival. He disciplines his body because the body can disqualify the preacher.
  - [→] If the apostle Paul considered his own body a threat to his salvation, on what basis would we consider ours irrelevant?

[Q] **"Isn't this legalism? Shouldn't grace cover what I eat?"**

> Grace does not make the body irrelevant — it makes it sacred. "Ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's" (1 Cor 6:20). The body is not yours to abuse. It was purchased. And the purchase was not cheap. This is not legalism — it is stewardship of something that belongs to Someone else.

## The Body Is a Temple

- **Not a metaphor — a declaration**
  - "Know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you?" (1 Cor 3:16)
  - "If any man defile the temple of God, him shall God destroy; for the temple of God is holy, which temple ye are" (1 Cor 3:17)
  - The sanctuary in the Old Testament was where God dwelt among His people. Now: your body is where God dwells.
  - Study 4 mapped the sanctuary as the path of restoration. The body is the final sanctuary — the one God inhabits.

- **Bought with a price**
  - "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's" (1 Cor 6:19-20)
  - Two claims: you are not your own + glorify God in your body
  - The body is not neutral territory. It is purchased property. And the Owner has standards.

- **A living sacrifice**
  - "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service" (Rom 12:1)
  - "Your reasonable service" — not extreme devotion. _Reasonable._ Caring for the temple is the minimum, not the maximum.
  - And then the next verse: "be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind" (Rom 12:2)
  - [→] Paul connects them deliberately. Present your body (v.1) → mind is renewed (v.2) → transformation happens. Body first, then mind, then character. The order is not accidental.

- **Whether you eat or drink**
  - "Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God" (1 Cor 10:31)
  - Glory = character (Ex 33-34, Study 3). To eat and drink to the glory of God is to eat and drink in a way that supports the restoration of His character in you.
  - [→] This verse makes eating and drinking a matter of God's glory — the same glory that is His character, the same character that is His image, the same image that salvation restores. The chain runs through the dinner table.

## God's Original Diet — The Creation Design

- **Eden: plant-based by design**
  - "And God said, Behold, I have given you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree, in the which is the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for meat" (Gen 1:29)
  - God designed the human body. He also designed its fuel. The two match.
  - Fruits, grains, nuts, seeds — this was the original diet. No death required. No blood shed.
  - [→] Before sin, the diet was plant-based. This is the Creator's design specification — not a cultural preference but an engineering decision.

- **After sin: herbs added, labor required**
  - "Cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life. Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field" (Gen 3:17-18)
  - The ground is cursed. Getting food now involves labor and thorns. Vegetables (_herb of the field_) are added to the diet.
  - The diet expanded — but still no meat. From creation to the flood, humanity's diet was plant-based.

- **After the flood: meat permitted — with limits**
  - "Every moving thing that liveth shall be meat for you; even as the green herb have I given you all things. But flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat" (Gen 9:3-4)
  - The flood destroyed all vegetation. Meat was permitted as an emergency concession — not an upgrade.
  - But even in the permission: no blood. God set limits immediately.
  - [→] Meat was allowed after a global catastrophe destroyed the food supply. It was survival, not design. The original blueprint was never revised — only supplemented under duress.

- **Clean and unclean — before the law**
  - "Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two" (Gen 7:2)
  - Noah knew the difference between clean and unclean animals — long before Leviticus was written
  - This distinction predates Israel, predates the law, predates the sanctuary
  - [→] Like the Sabbath, the clean/unclean distinction was built into creation knowledge, not invented at Sinai.

[DYK🔎] After the flood, human lifespan plummeted. Before the flood: Adam 930, Methuselah 969, Noah 950. After the flood: Shem 600, Eber 464, Peleg 239, Abraham 175, Moses 120. Multiple factors contributed — the post-flood environment, the loss of the antediluvian canopy, and the change of diet all play a role. But the correlation is striking: the introduction of meat into the diet accompanies the sharpest decline in human longevity in the biblical record.

## Daniel — The Test Case

- **Babylon's table vs. God's design**
  - "But Daniel purposed in his heart that he would not defile himself with the portion of the king's meat, nor with the wine which he drank" (Dan 1:8)
  - Daniel was a captive. He had no power. He had every reason to comply.
  - But he "purposed in his heart" — a mind-level decision about a body-level practice
  - [→] The first act of faithfulness in the book of Daniel is a dietary choice. Not a prayer. Not a theological debate. A decision about food.

- **The test**
  - "Prove thy servants, I beseech thee, ten days; and let them give us pulse to eat, and water to drink" (Dan 1:12)
  - Pulse = plant-based food. Water instead of wine.
  - "At the end of ten days their countenances appeared fairer and fatter in flesh than all the children which did eat the portion of the king's meat" (Dan 1:15)

- **The result — mind, not just body**
  - "As for these four children, God gave them knowledge and skill in all learning and wisdom: and Daniel had understanding in all visions and dreams" (Dan 1:17)
  - "He found them ten times better than all the magicians and astrologers that were in all his realm" (Dan 1:20)
  - The diet affected their minds. Clearer bodies → clearer minds → greater wisdom → capacity for divine revelation.
  - Daniel received the very visions that unlock the prophecies of Studies 6-8. A clouded mind could not have received them.
  - [→] Daniel's dietary faithfulness was not a health experiment. It was part of the preparation God used to give him prophetic clarity. We cannot say diet alone produced the visions — God gives revelation as He wills — but Scripture connects the clear mind to the capacity for divine wisdom. The body-mind-Spirit chain is real.

[DYK🔎] "Ten times better" — the Hebrew is _eser yadot_, literally "ten hands." Ten — the number of God's commandments. Whether the echo is intentional or not, the resonance is worth noting: Daniel kept his body clear, his mind was clear, and the result was measured in the number of the law itself. The chain from Study 3 — clear body → clear mind → the law written on the character → the image of God reflected — runs right through Daniel's plate.

[ILL] A radio receives a signal — but only if the antenna is clean and the circuits are functional. Static in the hardware means static in the reception. Daniel kept his receiver clear. God sent the signal. The visions of Daniel 2, 7, 8, and 9 came through a mind that was not clouded by Babylon's diet.

## The Days of Noah — A Last-Day Warning

- **Christ ties His return to Noah's time**
  - "But as the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be. For as in the days that were before the flood they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark" (Matt 24:37-38)
  - "Eating and drinking" — not merely existing, but indulging without discernment
  - Christ names this as a sign of the last days: consumption without thought, appetite without restraint

- **Two camps in Noah's time**
  - Noah knew clean from unclean (Gen 7:2). His family lived by creation principles.
  - The world around them? "Eating and drinking" — the phrase implies excess, indulgence, heedlessness.
  - [→] What do you imagine God's people were eating and drinking vs. the wicked? The distinction was there from the beginning. It will be there at the end.

- **Take heed — surfeiting dulls the heart**
  - "And take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged with surfeiting, and drunkenness, and cares of this life, and so that day come upon you unawares" (Luke 21:34)
  - "Surfeiting" = excess in eating. Christ directly links overeating and drunkenness to being caught unaware at His return.
  - The body dulls the heart. The heart misses the signs. The day comes unawares.
  - [→] This is not a health lecture. It is an eschatological warning. What you consume determines whether you are awake or asleep when Christ returns.

- **Whose god is their belly**
  - "Whose end is destruction, whose God is their belly, and whose glory is in their shame, who mind earthly things" (Phil 3:19)
  - Appetite as god — the belly ruling the mind, the body ruling the spirit
  - The inversion of God's order: Spirit → mind → body becomes belly → mind → everything else
  - [→] When appetite becomes master, the image cannot be restored. The instrument of transformation is enslaved to the very thing it was meant to govern.

## Temperance — The Forgotten Virtue

- **Peter's chain includes it**
  - "And beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge; and to knowledge temperance; and to temperance patience; and to patience godliness" (2 Pet 1:5-6)
  - Temperance sits between knowledge and patience. You cannot endure patiently if you cannot govern yourself.
  - The Greek is _egkrateia_ — self-mastery, self-control. Power over the appetites and passions.

- **Wine and strong drink**
  - "Wine is a mocker, strong drink is raging: and whosoever is deceived thereby is not wise" (Prov 20:1)
  - "Be not among winebibbers; among riotous eaters of flesh: for the drunkard and the glutton shall come to poverty" (Prov 23:20-21)
  - "Woe unto them that rise up early in the morning, that they may follow strong drink" (Isa 5:11)
  - Scripture does not treat alcohol as neutral. It treats it as a deceiver — something that impairs the very faculty God is trying to restore.
  - [→] If the mind is the seat of transformation, and alcohol clouds the mind, then alcohol works directly against the plan of salvation. Not because God is arbitrary — but because the mind cannot behold what it cannot clearly see.

- **The principle: anything that clouds the mind hinders the restoration**
  - Drugs, alcohol, overeating, stimulants — not a list of arbitrary rules but a single principle
  - Whatever impairs the mind impairs the beholding. Whatever impairs the beholding impairs the transformation. Whatever impairs the transformation delays the restoration.
  - [→] Temperance is not a side issue. It is the guardrail on the road of sanctification. Remove it and the vehicle leaves the path.

## The Chain Runs Through the Body

- **Study 3's chain, completed:**
  - Image = Character = Law = Love = Knowing God = Life
  - The image is restored in the mind (Eze 36:26; Rom 12:2)
  - The mind depends on the body (1 Cor 9:27; Dan 1:17)
  - The body is the temple of the Spirit (1 Cor 6:19)
  - Defile the temple → cloud the mind → block the beholding → stop the restoration
  - [→] The health message is not an appendix to the gospel. It is part of the mechanism of the gospel. The chain runs from the dinner table to the throne of God.

- **The two charges run through the body**
  - Charge B: "The law cannot be kept." Satan knows: cloud the mind and the law _can't_ be kept — not because the law is flawed, but because the instrument of obedience is impaired. Every substance that dulls the mind is Charge B's ally.
  - Charge A: "They only obey for reward." Appetite is the original bribe — Eve saw that the tree was "good for food" (Gen 3:6). When appetite rules, self-interest rules, and Charge A is confirmed.
  - [→] Satan attacked the body first in Eden and first in the wilderness. He knows: control the body and you control the mind. Control the mind and you prevent the restoration. The health message is not a side issue — it is the front line of the cosmic trial.

- **From Eden to Eden**
  - Eden: plant-based diet, no death, no disease, image of God intact (Gen 1:29)
  - New Earth: tree of life, leaves for healing, no more pain, image of God restored (Rev 22:1-2; Rev 21:4)
  - Between the two: God progressively restoring what was lost — including the body
  - [→] The original diet and the final restoration bookend the story. God's health design is not a cultural relic. It is a preview of Eden restored.

## Appeal

- Salvation is the restoration of God's image — and that image is restored in the mind
- The mind lives in a body. What you do with that body either aids the restoration or hinders it.
- God designed the body and designed its fuel. The two match. The further we drift from the design, the cloudier the mind, the harder the beholding, the slower the transformation.
- Satan's first weapon was appetite — in Eden and in the wilderness. He knows the body-mind link.
- Daniel proved it: a clear body produces a clear mind produces prophetic clarity.
- Christ warned it: in the days of Noah, eating and drinking without thought was the mark of those who were destroyed.
- Paul practiced it: he kept his body under subjection lest he himself become a castaway.

- Your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit. It is purchased property. Treat it as such.
- "Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God" (1 Cor 10:31)
- Glory = character = image. Eating to the glory of God means eating in a way that supports the restoration of His image in you.
- The chain runs through the body. Don't break it at the table.
