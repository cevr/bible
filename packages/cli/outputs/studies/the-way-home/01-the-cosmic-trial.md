# Study 1: The Cosmic Trial — Why You Exist

**You were not an accident of divine boredom. You were created as living evidence in a cosmic courtroom — witnesses designed to answer charges that Satan has brought against the character of God.**

## Prep Reading

- Ezekiel 28:12-17
- Isaiah 14:12-14
- Job 1:6-12
- Revelation 12:7-10
- Ephesians 3:10
- Romans 3:4

---

## A Controversy Before Creation

- **Lucifer's position — the highest created being**
  - "Thou sealest up the sum, full of wisdom, and perfect in beauty" (Eze 28:12)
  - "Thou art the anointed cherub that covereth; and I have set thee so: thou wast upon the holy mountain of God" (Eze 28:14)
  - Not a low-ranking rebel — the chief minister of heaven
  - "Thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee" (Eze 28:15)
  - [→] Iniquity was not placed in him. It was _found_ in him. It originated there.

- **The five "I wills" — ambition dressed as grievance**
  - "How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning!" (Isa 14:12)
  - "I will ascend into heaven" (Isa 14:13)
  - "I will exalt my throne above the stars of God" (Isa 14:13)
  - "I will sit also upon the mount of the congregation, in the sides of the north" (Isa 14:13)
  - "I will ascend above the heights of the clouds" (Isa 14:14)
  - "I will be like the most High" (Isa 14:14)
  - Five declarations — every one aimed at displacing God, not serving Him
  - [→] Notice: he did not say "I will destroy God." He said "I will be _like_ God." The counterfeit always mimics, never openly opposes.

- **The origin of evil — self-exaltation**
  - "Thine heart was lifted up because of thy beauty, thou hast corrupted thy wisdom by reason of thy brightness" (Eze 28:17)
  - The very gifts God gave him became the ground of his rebellion
  - Beauty produced vanity; wisdom produced cunning
  - [→] Sin's first form is not murder or theft — it is a heart that turns inward

[DYK🔎] Lucifer's name means "light-bearer" (הֵילֵל, _helel_, from the root "to shine"). He was designed to reflect God's glory. Instead he absorbed it. Sin, at its root, is taking what was meant to pass through you and hoarding it for yourself.

## War in Heaven

- **Not merely rebellion — war**
  - "And there was war in heaven: Michael and his angels fought against the dragon; and the dragon fought and his angels" (Rev 12:7)
  - "And prevailed not; neither was their place found any more in heaven" (Rev 12:8)
  - "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him" (Rev 12:9)

- **A third of the angels sided with Lucifer**
  - "And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth" (Rev 12:4)
  - These were not ignorant beings — they were holy angels who had seen God's glory firsthand
  - [→] If beings who stood in God's presence could be deceived, what does that say about the subtlety of Satan's arguments?

[Q] **"Why didn't God just destroy Satan immediately?"**
→ Because the charges Satan brought were about God's _character_. Destroying the accuser would not answer the accusation — it would seem to confirm it. "Let God be true, but every man a liar... that thou mightest be justified in thy sayings, and mightest overcome when thou art judged" (Rom 3:4). God chose to be _vindicated_, not merely victorious. Force can silence an objection; only truth can answer it.

## Satan's Four Charges Against God

**These are not merely historical grievances — they are the operating assumptions behind every temptation you face.**

### Charge 1: God Is Selfish and Tyrannical

- Satan's claim: God hoards power and demands worship for His own ego
- "I will be like the most High" (Isa 14:14) — as if God's position were stolen, not inherent
- Repeated in Eden: "For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods" (Gen 3:5)
- The implication: God is keeping something from you — He rules for His own benefit, not yours
- [→] Every temptation whispers this: "God is withholding good from you"

### Charge 2: God's Law Is Arbitrary and Impossible to Keep

- "Yea, hath God said...?" (Gen 3:1) — the first recorded words of the serpent
- Not a denial of God's existence — a questioning of His fairness
- The claim: God sets up rules no one can follow, then punishes failure
- "The carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be" (Rom 8:7)
- [→] Satan uses fallen humanity's inability to keep the law as proof that the law itself is the problem

### Charge 3: God Buys Loyalty Through Bribery

- "Doth Job fear God for nought?" (Job 1:9)
- "Hast not thou made an hedge about him, and about his house, and about all that he hath on every side? thou hast blessed the work of his hands, and his substance is increased in the land" (Job 1:10)
- "But put forth thine hand now, and touch all that he hath, and he will curse thee to thy face" (Job 1:11)
- Escalation: "Skin for skin, yea, all that a man hath will he give for his life. But put forth thine hand now, and touch his bone and his flesh, and he will curse thee to thy face" (Job 2:4-5)
- The charge: no one serves God out of love — only out of self-interest. Remove the benefits and the worship stops.
- [→] This is why suffering is allowed in the life of the faithful — not as punishment, but as evidence that love for God is not transactional

[ILL] A wealthy father wonders: do my children love me, or my money? He could never know — unless the money were removed. Job's trial was not cruelty. It was a courtroom exhibit.

### Charge 4: God Is Unjust and Vengeful

- "Yet saith the house of Israel, The way of the Lord is not equal" (Eze 18:25)
- Satan accuses God of being disproportionate — harsh in judgment, selective in mercy
- Yet God Himself declares: "I knew that thou art a gracious God, and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repentest thee of the evil" (Jonah 4:2)
- Even Jonah complained that God was _too_ merciful — the charges cut both ways
- [→] Satan tells sinners that God is too harsh; he tells the self-righteous that God is too lenient. The same accuser, opposite lies, one goal: distrust.

## Why God Cannot Simply Win by Force

- **God is on trial — and He chose to submit to the verdict of the evidence**
  - "That thou mightest be justified in thy sayings, and mightest overcome when thou art judged" (Rom 3:4)
  - God is not afraid of being judged — He invites it
  - His character can withstand any scrutiny

- **Force proves power, not righteousness**
  - God could have annihilated Satan in an instant
  - But an annihilated accuser is a silenced witness, not a refuted one
  - The watching universe needed to see the charges answered, not merely crushed
  - [→] This is why the controversy takes time. Truth must be demonstrated, not just declared.

[TANGENT] This principle governs how God deals with you, too. He does not coerce obedience. He does not override your will. He demonstrates His character and waits for your response. Love that is forced is not love — it is tyranny dressed in affection.

## A New Order of Witnesses Required

- **Angels could not fully answer the charges**
  - "For verily he took not on him the nature of angels" (Heb 2:16)
  - Angels had never experienced weakness, mortality, or the pull of a fallen nature
  - Their testimony, while loyal, was incomplete

- **The universe is watching**
  - "For we are made a spectacle unto the world, and to angels, and to men" (1 Cor 4:9)
  - The word "spectacle" is θέατρον (_theatron_) — a theater. You are on stage.
  - [→] This is not a metaphor. The cosmic trial has an audience.

- **A new kind of witness was needed**
  - Beings who could experience what Satan claimed — weakness, temptation, suffering — and still choose God
  - Beings through whom God's character could be demonstrated under the very conditions Satan said made obedience impossible
  - [→] That is why you exist.

## The Central Verse: Why the Church Exists

- **"To the intent that now unto the principalities and powers in heavenly places might be known by the church the manifold wisdom of God"** (Eph 3:10)
  - "To the intent" — this is purpose, not accident
  - "Principalities and powers in heavenly places" — the audience is cosmic, not merely human
  - "Might be known" — God is revealing something through you
  - "By the church" — through redeemed sinners, not through raw power
  - "The manifold wisdom of God" — the full spectrum of God's character on display

- **You were created for God's glory — and glory means character**
  - "Even every one that is called by my name: for I have created him for my glory, I have formed him; yea, I have made him" (Isa 43:7)
  - Glory (כָּבוֹד, _kavod_) = weight, substance, character
  - You were made to display who God really is — to a universe that is watching the trial unfold

- **The verdict will come through the saints**
  - "And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death" (Rev 12:11)
  - Blood of the Lamb = **legal standing** — Christ's sacrifice qualifies sinners to testify at all
  - Word of their testimony = **evidence** — not just words but the substance of transformed lives
  - Loved not their lives unto death = **credibility** — a witness who will die for their testimony cannot be bought
  - [→] This is the final answer to Job 1:9. Satan says loyalty has a price. The saints prove it doesn't.
  - "The accuser of our brethren is cast down, which accused them before our God day and night" (Rev 12:10)
  - Cast down — not by force, but by evidence. The saints _are_ the evidence.

## The Two Charges That Drive the Series

- Four charges were filed. But going forward, watch how they consolidate into **two operational accusations** that Satan presses throughout history:
  - **Charge A: "They only obey for reward"** — God's people are self-interested, not genuinely loyal (Job 1:9). Combines charges 1 and 3.
  - **Charge B: "The law cannot be kept"** — obedience is impossible, so God is unjust to require it (Gen 3:1, Rom 8:7). Combines charges 2 and 4.
- Every counterfeit, every persecution, every false doctrine in this series will trace back to one or both of these charges
- The final verdict (Rev 14:12) answers both: "keep the commandments of God" (charge B refuted) + "the faith of Jesus" (charge A refuted)
- [→] Remember these two charges. You will see them everywhere.

---

## Appeal

- You are not here by accident
- You were created as a witness in a cosmic trial — your character, your choices, your loyalty under pressure answer charges against the God of the universe
- Two accusations drive everything: "they only obey for reward" and "the law cannot be kept"
- Every day you live either confirms or refutes them
