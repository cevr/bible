## Marker registry rules

- `[KEY🔑]` is **sparing** — only when a passage _defines_ a symbol or interpretive method (year-day, recapitulation, beast=kingdom, Lamb=slain Christ, the seal=Sabbath relationship). Once defined, future chapters reference rather than re-define.
- `[⟲]` is the **thread breadcrumb** — appears at every link in a registered chain. Every Sanctuary thread chapter gets the same `[⟲]` block format.
- `[CTRF]` is reserved for **counterfeit/original pairs** that Scripture itself sets up (image of God / image of beast; seal / mark; Lamb / lamb-with-dragon-voice; Bride / Babylon-the-harlot).
- `[IMG]` tracks the **image-formation arc** — the Bible's metanarrative of the human being made in God's image, defaced, and being restored.
- `[DYK🔎]` is **historical/linguistic asides** — dates, etymology, manuscript notes; aim 1-3 per study.
- `[Q]` is **reflection** — questions worth pausing on; aim 1-2 per study.
- `[→]` is **teaching/application connectors** — abundant; usually 5-15 per study.

# DANIEL

## Daniel 1 — The Diet Test and the Two Cities

- **Theme**: a captive people in Babylon, a hidden test about loyalty to God's authority over the body and the mind. The whole great controversy in miniature: _whose image is being formed in you?_ Dan 1 is the prologue of the entire book.
- **Threads**: Image formation (the diet test as discipleship of the body), Worship axis (loyalty when Babylon owns your address), Great Controversy (two cities), the Word (Daniel "purposed in his heart")
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 1:8 ("Daniel purposed in his heart") sets the **purposed heart** as the controlling principle for every later test (Dan 3, 6); `[KEY🔑]` — the **two-cities chiasm** (Jerusalem at vs.1, Babylon at vs.2; Babylon/king at vs.21) frames the entire book.
- **Counterfeits / images**: Babylon's _training_ (vs.4-5) vs. God's _wisdom_ (vs.17, 20) — first counterfeit pair; the king's meat & wine vs. the simple diet of Eden — first image of God / image of Babylon contrast

## Daniel 2 — The Image and the Stone

- **Theme**: God reveals to a pagan king the spine of all future history — four metals, ten toes, a stone cut without hands. The skeleton of every prophecy in Daniel and Revelation.
- **Threads**: Great Controversy (kingdoms of men vs. the kingdom that fills the earth), Counterfeits (Nebuchadnezzar's image vs. Dan 3's golden image vs. Rev 13's image of the beast), Image formation (the _image_ word reused at Dan 3 and Rev 13:14-15), Time/Kingdoms
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 2:28 ("there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets") establishes the **revelatory frame** for the whole book; `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 2:31-45 sets **the four-empires sequence** (Babylon → Medo-Persia → Greece → Rome → divided Rome → stone kingdom) as the canon's master timeline.
- **Counterfeits / images**: the king's image (Dan 2) anticipates the king's golden image (Dan 3); the stone "cut without hands" (Dan 2:34) vs. the work of human hands (Dan 5's idols, Rev 13's image)

## Daniel 3 — The Fiery Furnace and the Image of Gold

- **Theme**: the worship test on the plain of Dura. A 60×6-cubit golden image, six instruments, three Hebrews who refuse, a fourth man "like the Son of God" in the fire. Dan 3 is **the Rev 13 prototype** — image-worship enforced on pain of death; God's people preserved through fire.
- **Threads**: Worship axis (the central thread for the chapter), Counterfeits (Dan 3's image vs. Christ in the fire), Image formation, Remnant/Seal (the three-stand-as-one as a shadow of Rev 14:1), Great Controversy
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 3:17-18 ("our God is able... but if not") sets the **"but if not" stance** — faithfulness whether or not deliverance comes; `[KEY🔑]` — the **666 vs. 777 pattern** (Dan 3's six 60s and six instruments and six gathering officials vs. the trinitarian sevens of Rev 14-15)
- **Counterfeits / images**: golden image vs. true image of God; six-fold pattern vs. seven-fold; Nebuchadnezzar's three accusers vs. the three Hebrews who stand

## Daniel 4 — The Tree, the Madness, the Pride

- **Theme**: God brings the proudest man on earth to eat grass for seven years until he confesses "those that walk in pride he is able to abase" (Dan 4:37). The chapter is Nebuchadnezzar's testimony — written by him, in the first person — to the universe.
- **Threads**: Great Controversy (whose kingdom rules?), Image formation (a man becomes a beast and is restored to a man — the Rev 13 reverse-arc), Worship axis (compulsory acknowledgment of "the most High"), Time-prophecy (the "seven times" parallel to Lev 26 / Dan 7's "time, times, and dividing of time")
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 4:17 / 25 / 32 ("the most High ruleth in the kingdom of men") — repeated three times — establishes **God's sovereignty over earthly kingdoms** as the explicit thesis; `[KEY🔑]` — the **seven-times** language as a possible structural prototype for Dan 7's "time, times, and dividing of time"
- **Counterfeits / images**: man as beast (Nebuchadnezzar) vs. beast as man (the Son of Man, Dan 7); the tree as kingdom (cf. Eze 31)
- **References**:

## Daniel 5 — Belshazzar's Feast and the Handwriting

- **Theme**: 539 BC. The night Babylon fell. A drunken sacrilege with the temple vessels, a hand writing on the wall, and a kingdom changing hands before sunrise. _Numbered, weighed, divided._ The judgment scene played out in advance.
- **Threads**: Judgment courtroom (Dan 5 is a _foretaste_ of Dan 7's investigative judgment — same vocabulary: weighed, written, divided); Counterfeits (temple vessels → secular feast → divine handwriting); Great Controversy (Babylon's last night)
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — **MENE, MENE, TEKEL, UPHARSIN** (Dan 5:25-28) sets the **judgment vocabulary** (numbered/weighed/divided) carried into Dan 7 and Rev 20:12; `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 5:23 ("the God in whose hand thy breath is, and whose are all thy ways") — the unrecognized God of Dan 4 catches up to the unrepentant grandson
- **Counterfeits / images**: temple vessels in pagan service vs. Rev 4-5 vessels in priestly service; "wisdom of Babylon" vs. Daniel's wisdom from God

## Daniel 6 — The Lions' Den

- **Theme**: a law signed _to trap a man for praying_. Dan 6 is **the Rev 13 prototype on the religious-liberty side** — a state law making the right kind of worship illegal, a faithful remnant who refuses, divine deliverance, vindication. Dan 3 was image-worship-enforced; Dan 6 is prayer-prohibited.
- **Threads**: Worship axis (private worship made illegal), Remnant/Seal (the man whose worship windows opened toward Jerusalem), Counterfeits (a 30-day decree of human worship vs. lifelong worship of God), Great Controversy
- **Key candidates**: `[KEY🔑]` — Dan 6:10 ("**he kneeled upon his knees three times a day, and prayed, and gave thanks before his God, as he did aforetime**") — establishes the **steady habit** as the unbreakable line; settled worship was already practice before the test
- **Counterfeits / images**: a 30-day law vs. a continuing covenant; lions of the den vs. the Lion of Judah (Rev 5:5); Darius the unwilling executioner vs. Pilate
- **References**:
  - KJV: Daniel 6; Ps 55:17 (three times a day); Acts 5:29 (obey God rather than men)
