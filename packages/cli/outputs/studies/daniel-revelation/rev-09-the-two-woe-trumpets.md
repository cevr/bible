# Revelation 9 — The Two Woe Trumpets (Saracen Locusts and Ottoman Cavalry)

**The first four trumpets brought down Western Rome. The eagle's triple woe (Rev 8:13) warned that the next three would be qualitatively different — reaching the Christian East. The fifth trumpet sounds, and a star falls from heaven with the key of the bottomless pit. Smoke rises like the smoke of a great furnace; the sun and air are darkened. Out of the smoke come locusts with crowns like gold, faces of men, hair like women, teeth like lions, breastplates of iron, and scorpion tails. They torment for five months. Historicist pioneers (Mede, Newton, Keith, Litch, Smith) read the imagery as covering the Saracen/Arab expansion and the Ottoman consolidation of the same Near-Eastern territory; the five months (year-day = 150 years) frames the Ottoman rise from AD 1299 to AD 1449. The sixth trumpet looses four angels at the Euphrates with an army "two hundred thousand thousand," prepared "for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year" — 391 years and 15 days by the year-day principle. Josiah Litch published the calculation in 1838, naming August 11, 1840 as the endpoint two years in advance. On that date the Convention of London placed the Ottoman Empire under European arbitration. The Millerite movement read it as a public verification of the prophetic method that would carry into 1844. Then Rev 9:20-21 closes the chapter on the harder note: judgments fell, and the men did not repent.**

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 9 Sits

Rev 8 closed the first four trumpets and lifted the eagle's triple woe over the next three (Rev 8:13). **Rev 9 sounds the fifth and sixth — the first two woes.** The third woe is the seventh trumpet, which Rev 11:14-19 will sound. Rev 9 is the central chapter of the trumpet-cycle.

- **The first four trumpets brought down Rome's western half** (AD 410-476). The fifth and sixth deal with what rose in her place across the Christian East.
- **The fifth trumpet's five months and the sixth trumpet's 391 years are sequential, not overlapping.** The historicist tradition reads the fifth's 150-year period (1299-1449) as ending where the sixth's 391-year-and-15-day period (1449-1840) begins. The two trumpets are two phases of one Eastern story.
- **This is where historicism made its sharpest public apologetic test.** Josiah Litch's published 1838 prediction that the sixth trumpet would end on **August 11, 1840** — and the geopolitical event that answered it on that day — is the historicist method's clearest falsifiable claim. The pioneers carried that confidence into the 2300-day calculation and 1844.

[KEY🔑] **The trumpets are loud, but Rev 9:20-21 is the sharpest verse in the chapter.** Two-hundred-million-strong armies, walls broken by gunpowder, empires placed under arbitration — and "yet repented not of the works of their hands... neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts." **Judgment falls and hearts stay the same.** The deepest question Rev 9 leaves is not "did Litch get the date right?" but **"what does it take to soften a heart that has watched empires fall and still chosen idols?"** The trumpets are God's louder voice. The still small voice (1 Kgs 19:12) is the one that actually changes us.

## Rev 9:1 — The Star with the Key

> "And the fifth angel sounded, and I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit." (Rev 9:1)

- "**A star fall from heaven**" — Greek _astera ek tou ouranou peptōkota_, perfect participle: _having fallen_. The star is already down when John sees it.
- "**Unto the earth**" — Greek _eis tēn gēn_. The star landed.
- "**To him was given**" — Greek _edothē autō_. **Permitted authority, not seized.** Same construction Revelation uses repeatedly to mark powers that operate under God's permission (the four horsemen at Rev 6 — "power was given").
- "**The key of the bottomless pit**" — Greek _hē kleis tou phreatos tēs abyssou_. The pit (_abyssos_) is the place of restraint for hostile powers (Lk 8:31; Rev 20:1-3). A key opens; whatever the star is, it is permitted to _release_ what was previously bound.
- **Views differ on the star's identity.** Some historicists (Smith) read it as Muhammad himself; others read it as a more general spiritual/political permission given to the rising power; others as Satan personally (matching Rev 12:9's "cast to earth," and Rev 9:11 names the locust-king "the angel of the bottomless pit"). The text does not name the star. **What matters is the action**: a previously sealed region is unsealed; a previously restrained power is permitted to act.

## Rev 9:2 — The Smoke from the Pit

> "And he opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke of the pit." (Rev 9:2)

- "**A smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace**" — Greek _hōs kapnos kaminou megalēs_. Furnace-scale smoke. The image is of a sealed underground space being opened and emitting choking, sun-blocking volume.
- "**The sun and the air were darkened**" — the fourth trumpet's darkening (Rev 8:12) was the political luminaries of the West going out; this fifth-trumpet darkening reaches the _spiritual_ atmosphere — the Christian East's sky obscured by a doctrinal system rising from below.
- **Historicist reading**: the rapid spread of a doctrinal-political system from the Arabian peninsula across the formerly Christian Near East. The "smoke" rises from a previously off-the-map region; the "darkening" of sun and air = the obscuring of the pure gospel across the lands that had received it for six centuries. The Christian East — Asia Minor, Syria, Palestine, Egypt, North Africa — fell to Islamic political and religious dominance over roughly a century (AD 632-732), and the trumpet's vocabulary registers the change.

## Rev 9:3-4 — The Locusts and Their Restraint

> "And there came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth: and unto them was given power, as the scorpions of the earth have power. And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads." (Rev 9:3-4)

- "**Locusts upon the earth**" — Greek _akrides_. Joel's locust-army imagery (Joel 1:4-7; 2:1-11) at the trumpet's scale. **In Scripture, locusts = a vast, irresistible invading host.**
- "**Power, as the scorpions of the earth have power**" — Greek _exousia_, authority. **Scorpion power, not lion power** — venom, sting, paralysis, lingering torment, not outright kill.
- "**Should not hurt the grass... neither any green thing, neither any tree**" — **the inverse of an ordinary locust swarm.** Locusts strip vegetation; these are commanded NOT to. Ordinary natural-world targets are spared.
- **Historicist note**: traditional Islamic rules of war — attributed to Caliph Abu Bakr's instructions to his commander in AD 632 — explicitly forbade the destruction of fruit-bearing trees and inhabited places during military campaigns ("Do not cut down a fruit-bearing tree. Do not destroy an inhabited place"). The pattern broadly matches Rev 9:4's vegetation-restraint language. Alexander Keith's _Signs of the Times, as Denoted by the Fulfilment of Historical Predictions_ (1832) collected the Arab historians' record of these rules and connected them to Rev 9:4. The match is striking on any reading.
- "**Only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads**" — **Rev 7's seal cross-references back here.** The fifth trumpet's torment falls on the unsealed; those marked in the forehead with the seal of the living God (Rev 7:3) are passed over. The sealing of Rev 7 and the limitation of Rev 9 are two angles on the same divine protection.

[⟲] **Remnant/seal thread:** Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:2-3 → **Rev 9:4 (seal-of-God exemption)** → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. Rev 9:4 is link three — the first place after the sealing where the seal's protection is operationally invoked.

## Rev 9:5-6 — Five Months of Torment

> "And to them it was given that they should not kill them, but that they should be tormented five months: and their torment was as the torment of a scorpion, when he striketh a man. And in those days shall men seek death, and shall not find it; and shall desire to die, and death shall flee from them." (Rev 9:5-6)

- "**Should not kill them, but... tormented**" — Greek _basanisthōsin_, tortured. **Subjugation, not extermination.** The Ottoman _dhimmi_ system — Christians and Jews permitted to live as second-class subjects under Islamic political rule, paying the _jizya_ tax, with restrictions on worship, building, and civic life — fits the trumpet's vocabulary: tormented, not killed.
- "**Five months**" — Greek _mēnas pente_. **Repeated at v. 5 and v. 10**, marking emphasis on a single 150-day period.
- **The year-day principle** (Num 14:34: "each day for a year"; Eze 4:6: "I have appointed thee each day for a year") — five months × 30 days = **150 days = 150 literal years**.
- **Historicist calculation** (Litch, _Prophetic Expositions_ 1838; Smith, _Daniel and the Revelation_ 1867): from the traditional anchor of Osman I's first major campaign against Byzantine territory (dated by Edward Gibbon, and followed by Litch, to **July 27, 1299**) to **AD 1449**, when the last Greek emperor Constantine XI took the throne only with Ottoman permission — **150 years marking the Ottoman rise to effective dominance over the Byzantine East.**
- **Honesty note on the start-date**: the July 27, 1299 date comes from Gibbon's _Decline and Fall_ chronology and was carried into Adventist tradition by Litch; modern Ottoman historians more often cite the Battle of Bapheus (July 27, 1302) as the first major datable Ottoman victory over Byzantine forces. The 1449 endpoint is well-documented. **The 150-year period frames the Ottoman rise accurately in broad terms; the precise start-date is the historicist tradition's anchor, not a neutral historical consensus.**
- "**Men shall seek death, and shall not find it**" — the torment is unbearable yet not fatal. The dhimmi condition: civic and religious life under continuous restriction without the release of either restoration or martyrdom.

## Rev 9:7-9 — The Locusts Described

> "And the shapes of the locusts were like unto horses prepared unto battle; and on their heads were as it were crowns like gold, and their faces were as the faces of men. And they had hair as the hair of women, and their teeth were as the teeth of lions. And they had breastplates, as it were breastplates of iron; and the sound of their wings was as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle." (Rev 9:7-9)

- "**Like unto horses prepared unto battle**" — Joel 2:4 ("the appearance of them is as the appearance of horses; and as horsemen, so shall they run") at the trumpet's scale. **Cavalry imagery dominates.**
- "**Crowns like gold**" — Greek _stephanoi homoioi chrysō_. _Stephanoi_ (victor's wreaths) are flexible enough to cover ornamental headgear; the historicist tradition reads this as the gold-trimmed turbans of Saracen and Ottoman commanders.
- "**Faces as the faces of men... hair as the hair of women**" — the Arabic warrior tradition of full beards and long hair worn by cavalrymen.
- "**Teeth as the teeth of lions**" — Joel 1:6 again ("his teeth are the teeth of a lion"). Aggressive, devouring power.
- "**Breastplates of iron**" — heavily armored cavalry. The Saracen and Ottoman armies were notable for their armored-cavalry tradition.
- "**The sound of their wings was as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle**" — the famous noise of Arab and Turkish cavalry charges.

## Rev 9:10 — Scorpion Tails

> "And they had tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails: and their power was to hurt men five months." (Rev 9:10)

- "**Tails like unto scorpions, and there were stings in their tails**" — **the cavalry archer's signature tactic**: shooting backwards from a retreating horse. The "Parthian shot," carried into Arab and Turkish military practice; the trumpet's "stinging tails" matches the cavalry archer's effective rear fire.
- "**Their power was to hurt men five months**" — second naming of the 150-year period (cf. v. 5). **Repetition for emphasis on a single, defined period.**

## Rev 9:11 — Their King: Abaddon / Apollyon

> "And they had a king over them, which is the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath his name Apollyon." (Rev 9:11)

- "**A king over them, which is the angel of the bottomless pit**" — Greek _ton angelon tēs abyssou_. **Note the reversal of an earlier proverbial saying**: Prov 30:27 says "the locusts have no king." Rev 9 says these locusts _do_. The reversal marks them as no ordinary swarm.
- "**In the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon**" — Hebrew _Abaddōn_, "destruction" (Job 26:6; Ps 88:11; Prov 15:11). A name the OT uses for the place of the dead.
- "**In the Greek tongue hath his name Apollyon**" — Greek _Apollyōn_, "Destroyer."
- **Both names mean Destroyer.** Whatever level of identification one accepts (Satan personally, a representative angel of darkness, or the spiritual force animating the political movement), **the king's name confirms the trumpet's character: this is destruction, not preservation.**

## Rev 9:12 — One Woe Past

> "One woe is past; and, behold, there come two woes more hereafter." (Rev 9:12)

- The fifth trumpet ends. **The eagle's three woes (Rev 8:13) are now structurally placed**: woe 1 = trumpet 5; woe 2 = trumpet 6; woe 3 = trumpet 7 (Rev 11:14-19).
- "**One woe is past**" registers the completion of the 150-year period. The reader (and the trumpet's announcing structure) knows the next phase is coming.

[CTRF] **The locusts are not the saved, the locusts are the locusts.** Rev 9:4 explicitly distinguishes those who have the seal of God in their foreheads from those who do not. The locust-torment falls on the unsealed. The seal of Rev 7 is the same operational protection invoked here. **There is a counterfeit on the horizon** (Rev 13:16-17's mark in hand or forehead), and the Rev 9:4 distinction is the early signal of how the seal/mark axis will run.

## Rev 9:13-14 — The Sixth Trumpet: Loose the Four at the Euphrates

> "And the sixth angel sounded, and I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God, Saying to the sixth angel which had the trumpet, Loose the four angels which are bound in the great river Euphrates." (Rev 9:13-14)

- "**A voice from the four horns of the golden altar**" — the same altar of incense Rev 8:3-5 showed receiving the saints' prayers. **The voice that releases the next judgment comes from the same altar where the saints' prayers were received.** Rev 9:13's loosing is, again, prayer-released judgment.
- "**Loose the four angels which are bound in the great river Euphrates**" — Greek _tessaras angelous tous dedemenous epi tō potamō tō megalō Euphratē_. Four restraining angels, previously _bound_, now to be _loosed_.
- "**The great river Euphrates**" — Babylon's river (Jer 51:63-64), the eastern boundary of the promised land (Gen 15:18). **In Revelation's geography, the Euphrates marks the region of God's people's enemies.** The Ottoman empire's heartland straddled the historic Euphrates region — Anatolia, Mesopotamia, the Levant.
- **Historicist reading**: the four angels "bound" at the Euphrates = the Ottoman power, restrained for a defined period (the fifth trumpet's 150 years), then loosed (the sixth trumpet's 391 years and 15 days). Constantinople fell to the Ottomans in **AD 1453** under Mehmet II, ending the Eastern Roman Empire — an event that occurred _inside_ the sixth trumpet's period, not as its starting trigger.

## Rev 9:15 — The Hour, Day, Month, and Year

> "And the four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men." (Rev 9:15)

- "**Prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year**" — Greek _eis tēn hōran kai hēmeran kai mēna kai eniauton_. **A composite prophetic time-marker.**
- **Year-day calculation** (each unit at the prophetic-year scale of 360 days):
  - 1 year = 360 days = **360 years**
  - 1 month = 30 days = **30 years**
  - 1 day = **1 year**
  - 1 hour = 1/24 of a day = 1/24 of a year ≈ **15 days**
  - **Total: 391 years and 15 days**

[DYK🔎] **The 360-day prophetic year is biblically grounded.** Gen 7:11 + 8:3-4 give 150 days for 5 months, yielding 30-day months. Dan 12:7 and Rev 12:14 give the "time, and times, and half a time" (3.5 prophetic years) formula; Rev 12:6 names the same wilderness period as 1260 days — the two readings together fix the prophetic year at 360 days. The year-day principle itself comes from Num 14:34 ("each day for a year, even forty days, ye shall bear your iniquities") and Eze 4:6 ("each day for a year"). **The method is not historicist invention; it is biblical instruction read at the scale of long prophetic periods** (Dan 8:14's 2300 days, Dan 9:24-27's 70 weeks demonstrating the principle in fulfilled history at Christ's first coming).

- "**For to slay the third part of men**" — partial scope (the trumpet-cycle's "third part" signature continues).

## Rev 9:16 — Two Hundred Thousand Thousand

> "And the number of the army of the horsemen were two hundred thousand thousand: and I heard the number of them." (Rev 9:16)

- "**Two hundred thousand thousand**" — Greek _dismyriades myriadōn_, "twice ten thousand times ten thousand" = **two hundred million.**
- The number is **vast and symbolic** rather than a literal headcount. Compare Rev 5:11's heavenly host: "ten thousand times ten thousand, and thousands of thousands" — innumerable. **Cavalry beyond counting is the picture.**
- "**I heard the number of them**" — John heard the number announced. Same auditory device as the 144,000 of Rev 7:4 (heard) before the great multitude (seen, uncountable).

## Rev 9:17-19 — The Cavalry Described

> "And thus I saw the horses in the vision, and them that sat on them, having breastplates of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone: and the heads of the horses were as the heads of lions; and out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone. By these three was the third part of men killed, by the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone, which issued out of their mouths. For their power is in their mouth, and in their tails: for their tails were like unto serpents, and had heads, and with them they do hurt." (Rev 9:17-19)

- "**Breastplates of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone**" — three colors: red (fire), blue (jacinth, a sapphire-like stone), yellow (brimstone/sulfur). **Red-blue-yellow = a recognizable Ottoman uniform palette.**
- "**The heads of the horses were as the heads of lions**" — devouring, predator imagery.
- "**Out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone**" — **the three signatures of gunpowder artillery**: flame (fire), smoke, and the sulfurous smell of black powder (brimstone). Revelation describes a weapon that **did not exist when John wrote.**
- "**By these three was the third part of men killed**" — partial scope continued; the killing is by the artillery-discharge cluster.
- "**Their power is in their mouth, and in their tails**" — power in two directions: the mouth's forward fire (the cannon's muzzle blast) and the tails' lingering hurt (the recoil/aftermath/territorial consequence of the conquest).

[DYK🔎] **"Fire and smoke and brimstone" — the Ottoman artillery at Constantinople, AD 1453.** For more than a thousand years after Constantine founded his city, Goths, Huns, Avars, Persians, Bulgarians, Saracens, Russians, and the Ottomans themselves had besieged Constantinople — and the city's walls held. Then Sultan Mehmet II asked the Hungarian cannon-founder Orban (who had defected from the Byzantine service for higher Ottoman pay) if he could cast a gun large enough to breach the walls. A foundry was established at Adrianople, and a massive bombard (the "Basilica," firing stone shot of c. 600 lbs) was forged. **May 29, 1453**, the cannon broke the walls, the Ottomans poured in, the last Byzantine emperor Constantine XI fell in the breach, and the Eastern Roman Empire ended. The trumpet's vivid imagery of fire, smoke, and brimstone issuing from horse-mouths matches the line of artillery firing the breach — a piece of pictorial prophecy that fits the technology by which the city actually fell. In Smith's frame, the sixth trumpet's 391-year-and-15-day period had already begun in 1449 (when the 150 years of trumpet 5 ended); **the 1453 fall of Constantinople is one of the early major fulfillment-scenes _inside_ the sixth trumpet's period**, not its starting trigger.

## Josiah Litch and August 11, 1840

The sixth trumpet is where the historicist case is strongest, **because it gives a date, and the date was published in advance.**

- **Josiah Litch's calculation** (_The Probability of the Second Coming of Christ About A.D. 1843_, 1838; revised in later Millerite publications):
  - Litch anchored the combined trumpet chronology at **July 27, 1299** (following Gibbon) — Osman I's first major campaign against Byzantine territory
  - The five prophetic months (150 years) of trumpet 5 reach **July 27, 1449**; the sixth trumpet's 391 years and 15 days then reach **August 11, 1840**
  - Litch published this prediction in 1838, **two years in advance**, naming the exact date. His book and articles circulated publicly through Millerite networks
- **What happened on August 11, 1840:**
  - On July 15, 1840, Britain, Austria, Prussia, Russia, and the Ottoman Empire signed the Convention of London to settle the Egyptian crisis (Mehmet Ali's revolt against the Sultan)
  - The European powers issued an ultimatum to Mehmet Ali that — according to contemporary Millerite reports — was placed in his hands "about August 11, 1840"
  - Litch and the Millerites read this diplomatic moment — **when the Ottoman Empire's internal sovereignty became dependent on European arbitration** — as the public fulfillment of the sixth trumpet's "loosing"
  - Contemporary secular reporting documented the diplomatic event (_Blackwood's Edinburgh Magazine_, 1840; _The Times_ of London); the _prophetic_ interpretation was Litch's, and the Millerite movement amplified it
- **What this meant for the Advent movement:**
  - Litch was not an Adventist when he made the prediction — he was a Methodist minister reading historicist commentaries
  - When the date hit, the wider historicist world took notice. **William Miller's audiences expanded dramatically.** Thousands who had been skeptical of the year-day principle took it seriously after seeing it publicly verified
  - The effect on the Advent movement was structural: **if the year-day principle worked for the 391-year period of the sixth trumpet, it would work for the 2300-day period of Dan 8:14 ending in 1844.** The pioneers did not invent the method; Litch had demonstrated it
  - Le Roy Froom documented this sequence in _Prophetic Faith of Our Fathers_ (vol. 4)
- **Honesty notes:**
  - The July 27, 1299 starting date comes from Gibbon's _Decline and Fall_ chronology and was carried by Litch into Adventist tradition; modern Ottoman historians more often date the first major Byzantine defeat to the Battle of Bapheus (July 27, 1302). The 1449 endpoint (Constantine XI's accession with Ottoman permission) is well-documented
  - The August 11, 1840 diplomatic event is documented in secular sources; **Litch's prediction of it is published and dated before the event**
  - **What this case is**: a public apologetic test for Millerite historicism — a predicted date, published two years in advance, where a major geopolitical event answering the prophetic description occurred on the named day. Millerite audiences grew sharply afterward
  - **What this case is not**: a neutral verification by secular historians of the historicist method. Historians documented the diplomatic event; interpreting that event as the sixth trumpet's fulfillment was Litch's prophetic reading

[→] The sixth trumpet was **the moment historicist prediction met public date-fixing.** Litch named a date two years in advance; the Ottoman Empire's sovereignty was publicly placed under European arbitration on that day; Millerite confidence in the prophetic method grew sharply. **Whatever weight one gives the reading, the sixth trumpet is the place where the historicist tradition made its clearest falsifiable claim.** The same interpretive framework — the year-day principle, the Jewish prophetic year, careful anchoring of start-dates — is the framework that produces 1844 in Dan 8:14.

## Rev 9:20-21 — The Hardness That Followed

> "And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk: Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts." (Rev 9:20-21)

- "**The rest of the men which were not killed... yet repented not**" — the survivors. Western Rome fell, the Christian East fell, the Ottoman Empire was placed under arbitration — and the survivors did not turn.
- "**The works of their hands... idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood**" — Daniel 5:23 echoes the same idol-polemic material list at Belshazzar's last night, with iron added and a different order (gold, silver, brass, iron, wood, stone). **The materials that make a god out of a thing.** The trumpets are loud enough to rattle empires, and the men kept the idols.
- "**Which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk**" — Ps 115:5-7 / Ps 135:15-17 / Isa 44:9-20 / Jer 10:5's standing critique of idols. **The idol-makers become like what they make: blind, deaf, lame.**
- "**Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts**" — **anticipates the final exclusion lists of Rev 21:8 and Rev 22:15 through overlapping sins** (murderers, sorcerers, fornicators/whoremongers, idolaters). The sixth trumpet announces that judgment is coming; the men's response announces that hearts have not turned.

[Q] **"Isn't it uncomfortable to read a prophecy as being about Islam?"**
→ It is, and it should be read with care. Four honest qualifications: (1) Revelation's fifth trumpet describes a _judgment_ — not on Muslims personally, but on the **formerly Christian territories** that had received the gospel and then fallen into the "darkness" of doctrinal compromise (the black horse, Pergamos, the fourth trumpet's darkened sun). The Near East had been Christian for centuries before Islam arose. **The judgment is on the prior compromise**, not on the people who carried its consequences. (2) The historicist reading distinguishes the political rise of a power from the spiritual condition of individuals within it. (3) The text says the locusts were _restrained_ from total killing — the judgment is bounded. (4) Every prophetic mapping, honestly done, can be read with respect for the peoples involved. **The point is not polemic. It is to read what Revelation actually says about the history it addresses.**

## After the Verses — What Rev 9 Hands to the Rest of the Book

- **The trumpets close with men unrepentant.** Rev 9:20-21 is the chapter's deepest note. Empires can be struck and survivors keep their idols. The trumpets have done what they were sent to do; the next move is the kingdom's announcement, but only after Rev 10-11's interlude.
- **The interlude before the seventh trumpet.** Rev 10 (the mighty angel with the little book) and Rev 11 (the two witnesses, the temple measured, the seventh trumpet) intervene before the kingdom is announced. Rev 11:14-19 will sound the third woe / seventh trumpet at the chapter's close.
- **The year-day method is now publicly tested.** The 391 years and 15 days of the sixth trumpet, with Litch's published 1838 prediction landing on August 11, 1840, gave the historicist tradition its sharpest apologetic anchor. The same method's application to Dan 8:14 produces 1844.
- **The seal/mark axis sharpens.** Rev 9:4's "those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads" sets up Rev 13:16-17's coercive counterfeit. The fifth trumpet's torment-passes-the-sealed is the operational early signal of how the late-history seal/mark contrast will run.
- **The five-fold idolatry of v. 20 indicts every era.** Gold/silver/brass/stone/wood was Belshazzar's final-night list (Dan 5:23) and is the survivors' list at the trumpet's close. **Idols outlive empires unless something deeper moves the heart.**

[→] The trumpets are loud. They tear down empires. They publish dates. They give the prophetic method its public test. **But Rev 9:20-21 closes the chapter on a quieter, sharper note: judgments fell, and hearts stayed the same.** The deepest question this chapter leaves is not "did Litch get the date right?" — it is **"what does it take to soften a heart that has watched empires fall and still chosen idols?"** The trumpets are God's louder voice. The still small voice (1 Kgs 19:12) is the one that actually changes us. Where the trumpets in your own life have already sounded — losses, warnings, providences that woke you up for a season — has the heart turned, or has it only counted the noise?

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 9 = the first two woes (trumpets 5 and 6); the third woe (trumpet 7) lands at Rev 11:14-19 after the Rev 10-11 interlude
- The fifth trumpet's 150 years and the sixth trumpet's 391 years and 15 days are sequential, framing the Saracen/Ottoman story (1299 → 1449 → 1840)
- The fifth trumpet's "five months" is repeated at v. 5 and v. 10 — emphasis on a single 150-day / 150-year period
- Year-day method: Num 14:34 and Eze 4:6 give the principle; Gen 7:11 + 8:3-4 and Dan 12:7 / Rev 12:14 fix the prophetic year at 360 days
- The "hour, day, month, and year" of Rev 9:15 = 360 + 30 + 1 + 15-days = **391 years and 15 days** by year-day reckoning
- The historicist anchor: July 27, 1299 (Gibbon-followed) + 5 prophetic months (150 years) reaches July 27, 1449; + 391 years and 15 days = **August 11, 1840** (Convention of London ultimatum to Mehmet Ali)
- **Josiah Litch published the calculation in 1838, naming the date two years in advance**; on August 11, 1840 the Ottoman Empire's internal sovereignty was placed under European arbitration; Millerite movement read it as public verification of the year-day method that would carry into 1844
- Honesty note: 1299 anchor is Gibbon's date carried by Litch (modern Ottoman historians prefer 1302's Battle of Bapheus); the 1449 and 1840 endpoints are well-documented; what this case _is_ = a published falsifiable claim that landed; what it _is not_ = a neutral secular verification of the historicist method
- Rev 9:4's "those which have not the seal of God in their foreheads" cross-anchors Rev 7's sealing; the seal protects through the trumpet-torment
- Fall of Constantinople AD 1453 (Sultan Mehmet II's Orban-cast bombard breaching the Theodosian walls) = a major fulfillment-scene _inside_ the sixth trumpet's period, fitting the "fire, smoke, brimstone" cluster that names gunpowder artillery a millennium and a half before its invention
- Rev 9:20-21 closes on the harder note: judgments fell, men did not repent; idolatry list (gold/silver/brass/stone/wood) cross-anchors Belshazzar's last night (Dan 5:23); the survivor's sin-list (murders/sorceries/fornication/thefts) anticipates Rev 21:8 and Rev 22:15
- The trumpets are God's louder voice; the still small voice (1 Kgs 19:12) is what actually changes a heart

## Next: Revelation 10 — The Mighty Angel and the Little Book

Between the sixth and seventh trumpets, John sees a mighty angel descending from heaven clothed with a cloud, with a rainbow on his head, his face like the sun, his feet as pillars of fire. He has "a little book open" in his hand. He sets one foot on the sea and one on the land, and cries with a great voice. Seven thunders speak; John is told not to write what they said. The angel swears by Him that lives forever that "there should be time no longer" — the time-prophecy era is over. John is told to take the little book and eat it: it is "sweet as honey" in the mouth, "bitter" in the belly. The Advent movement's 1844 hope and disappointment is in that sentence. Rev 10 is where Daniel's sealed book (Dan 12:9) finally opens.
