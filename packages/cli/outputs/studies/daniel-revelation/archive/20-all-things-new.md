# Study 20: All Things New — Revelation 21-22

**Sin is ended. Death is ended. The lake of fire has consumed every enemy. The old earth has been purified by the same fire. And now the book that began in heaven — "the revelation of Jesus Christ" — ends on earth. A new heaven. A new earth. The holy city descending out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. A voice from the throne: "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them." No more tears. No more death. No more sorrow, crying, or pain. "Behold, I make all things new." Then John is shown the city up close — gates of pearl, foundations of precious stones, walls of jasper, streets of gold. No temple in the city; no sun; the glory of God is its light, and the Lamb is the lamp. A pure river of water of life flows from God's throne, and the tree of life — last seen in Eden — stands on either side of it, yielding its fruit every month, its leaves for the healing of the nations. "There shall be no more curse." Revelation ends where Genesis began: God and man face to face, the tree of life accessible, and the long story of sin completely undone.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 21 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 22 (entire chapter)
- Genesis 2:8-17 and 3:22-24 (the original garden and the guarding of the tree)
- Isaiah 65:17-25 (the new heavens and new earth promise)

## "Behold, I Make All Things New"

The transition from Rev 20's final judgment to Rev 21's new heaven and earth is the seam.

- **The new creation** (Rev 21:1)
  - "I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away; and there was no more sea"
  - _No more sea_ — the sea in Revelation represented the populated, chaotic world that gave rise to the beast (Rev 13:1); now the threatening waters are gone. In Hebrew cosmology, the unruly sea symbolizes the forces of chaos (Ps 89:9; Isa 57:20). In the new creation, chaos is finished
  - The _first_ heaven and earth have "passed away" — 2 Pet 3:10-13's fire has done its work; the universe is remade

- **The holy city descends** (Rev 21:2-3)
  - "I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband"
  - "I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be their people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God"
  - _Tabernacle_ — the word takes us back to Ex 25:8 ("let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them"), to the whole sanctuary typology traced in Study 4
  - God's original intention for His relationship with humanity — to _dwell_ with them — has been the theme of the whole Bible. Now, in the new creation, it is finally and permanently realized

- **"He will dwell with them, and they shall be his people"** (v. 3)
  - Echo of Lev 26:11-12, Eze 37:27 — God's covenant formula across both Testaments: "I will dwell among them... and I will be their God, and they shall be my people"
  - The formula's full realization has always been the ultimate goal. In the new earth, the formula becomes the permanent reality

- **The ending of tears** (Rev 21:4)
  - "God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away"
  - Echo of Isa 25:8 — "he will swallow up death in victory; and the Lord GOD will wipe away tears from off all faces"
  - _God_ personally wipes tears. Not an angel, not a ceremony. God's own hand, on each face

- **"Behold, I make all things new"** (v. 5)
  - "He that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things new. And he said unto me, Write: for these words are true and faithful"
  - The one on the throne speaks directly to John — only the third direct statement from the throne in Rev (cf. Rev 1:8; 16:17). The weight of the declaration matches its content
  - _All things new_ — not _new things made_, but _old things restored and remade_. The second creation is not a replacement; it is a re-creation — sin's damage undone, the original beauty intensified

- **"It is done"** (v. 6)
  - "And he said unto me, It is done. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end"
  - The second "It is done" in Revelation (cf. Rev 16:17 at the seventh plague). What was pronounced then in judgment is now pronounced in completion. The same voice, the same finality, the same truth: God's work is complete

- [→] Rev 21:1-7 is the whole Bible's climax. The Genesis-to-Revelation arc bends back to its beginning: God dwelling with humanity, the curse removed, the tree of life accessible. Everything Scripture has been moving toward is here.

## The Two Destinies (Rev 21:7-8)

- **The overcomer's inheritance** (Rev 21:7)
  - "He that overcometh shall inherit all things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my son"
  - _Overcomer_ — the word that has run through every letter to the seven churches (Rev 2:7, 11, 17, 26; 3:5, 12, 21)
  - _All things_ — inheritance is universal, not partial. "All things are yours" (1 Cor 3:21-23)
  - _He shall be my son_ — intimate relationship language; the Spirit of adoption (Rom 8:15-17)

- **The excluded** (Rev 21:8)
  - "The fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable, and murderers, and whoremongers, and sorcerers, and idolaters, and all liars, shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death"
  - Eight categories of the excluded — a sobering list
  - _The fearful_ listed first — not heroic villains but those too afraid to take their stand with Christ. Fear kept more from the kingdom than active rebellion did
  - _The lake which burneth_ = the _second death_ — not eternal conscious torment but final destruction (Study 19)
  - The list's structure recurs at Rev 22:15, framing the chapter

- [→] The two destinies are the whole book's argument: the overcomer inherits all things; the refuser receives the second death. Between them, no third option. The entire book has been working toward this clean, unmistakable binary.

## The Holy City — Up Close

Rev 21:9-27 describes the city in detail.

- **The setup** (Rev 21:9-10)
  - "Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife"
  - "He carried me away in the spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God"
  - The bride and the city are _one_ — the redeemed and their dwelling, inseparable. God does not separate His people from their home

- **The walls and gates** (Rev 21:12-14)
  - "A wall great and high, and had twelve gates, and at the gates twelve angels, and names written thereon, which are the names of the twelve tribes of the children of Israel"
  - "On the east three gates; on the north three gates; on the south three gates; and on the west three gates"
  - "The wall of the city had twelve foundations, and in them the names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb"
  - OT and NT are both represented: twelve tribes on the gates, twelve apostles on the foundations. The city is built on the _whole_ witness of Scripture, not one testament's portion
  - _Twelve at each direction_ — access from every approach. No preferred entry; the city is open from all sides

- **The measurement** (Rev 21:15-17)
  - "The city lieth foursquare"
  - "The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal" — 12,000 furlongs each (about 1,400 miles / 2,200 km per side)
  - A cube. Perfect symmetry — the same shape as the Most Holy Place of Solomon's temple (1 Kings 6:20 — 20 × 20 × 20 cubits; Heb 9:7 supplies the Most Holy Place category). The whole city is a Most Holy Place, where God dwells with His people
  - The 12,000-furlongs-per-side and 144-cubit walls both echo the 12 × 12 × 1000 = 144,000 of the sealed

- **The materials** (Rev 21:18-21)
  - "The building of the wall of it was of jasper: and the city was pure gold, like unto clear glass"
  - "The foundations of the wall of the city were garnished with all manner of precious stones" — jasper, sapphire, chalcedony, emerald, sardonyx, sardius, chrysolite, beryl, topaz, chrysoprase, jacinth, amethyst
  - The list substantially overlaps with the twelve stones of the high priest's breastplate (Ex 28:17-20 — jasper, sapphire, emerald, sardius, topaz, beryl, amethyst all appear in both lists, with other stones varying). The city's foundations echo the breastplate — the people God's heart has carried are now built into the city's foundations
  - "The twelve gates were twelve pearls; every several gate was of one pearl" — each gate a single immense pearl. (As an illustration, pearls are formed by an oyster's gradual response to an intruding grain; suffering transformed into beauty is a natural homiletic echo, though Revelation itself does not define the pearl symbolism)
  - "The street of the city was pure gold, as it were transparent glass" — gold so pure it is clear

- **No temple** (Rev 21:22)
  - "I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it"
  - The sanctuary's whole purpose was to bring God and humanity together. Now God is with His people directly; no mediating structure is needed
  - `[⟲]` **Sanctuary thread — eschatological coda.** The thread's registered final link remains Rev 15:5-8 (the Day of Atonement ministry closing). Rev 21:22 is the _fulfillment_ the whole sanctuary system pointed toward: the sanctuary's goal — God dwelling with His people — achieved directly, without any mediating structure

- **No sun, no night** (Rev 21:23-25)
  - "The city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof"
  - "The nations of them which are saved shall walk in the light of it: and the kings of the earth do bring their glory and honour into it"
  - "The gates of it shall not be shut at all by day: for there shall be no night there"
  - Gates open permanently. Light continuous. Nations entering. The city is not a gated fortress; it is the open center of a new creation

- **No unclean thing** (Rev 21:27)
  - "There shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life"
  - The book of life (Dan 12:1; Phil 4:3; Rev 3:5; Rev 13:8; 17:8; 20:12, 15) reaches its final use: the register of those who enter

## The River and the Tree

Rev 22 opens with a scene from Eden, restored.

- **The river** (Rev 22:1)
  - "He shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb"
  - The river flows from the throne — the source of the universe's life is God Himself. Echo of Eze 47:1-12 (water flowing from the temple), Ps 46:4 ("a river, the streams whereof shall make glad the city of God"), John 7:37-39 (Christ's promised living water)

- **The tree of life** (Rev 22:2)
  - "In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations"
  - The tree last seen in Gen 3:22-24 — from which Adam and Eve were barred after sin
  - Now, in the new creation, the tree is not hidden. It stands on both sides of the river, accessible. The long exile from the tree of life ends
  - _Twelve manner of fruits, yielded her fruit every month_ — abundance; a fruit for every month of the year; twelve = completeness
  - _The leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations_ — sin's damage to the nations (physical, emotional, relational, spiritual) is reversed. The healing is not just spiritual; it is comprehensive

- **"There shall be no more curse"** (Rev 22:3)
  - The curse of Gen 3 — on the ground, on the woman's pain, on the man's labor, on death itself — is _removed_
  - Gen 3 reversed. The long arc of sin's consequences ends here

- **"They shall see his face"** (Rev 22:4)
  - "The throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him: And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads"
  - _See his face_ — what even Moses could not do (Ex 33:20 — "there shall no man see me, and live"). In the new creation, the impossibility is resolved. The redeemed see God's face and live
  - _His name in their foreheads_ — the sealing that began at Rev 7:3 and continued through Rev 14:1 now completes. The character is fully formed; God's name is fully written
  - `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread — final link.** Dan 12:1's deliverance of "every one that shall be found written in the book" → Rev 7:1-4's sealing of the 144,000 → Rev 12:17's remnant who keep the commandments and have the testimony of Jesus → Rev 14:1, 12's 144,000 with the Father's name in their foreheads, the saints who have the patience of Jesus → Rev 22:4. The whole chain has been one trajectory: the formation of a people who carry God's name. Now the carrying is permanent.
  - [IMG] _His name in their foreheads_ is the end of the image-restoration arc that has run from Gen 1:26-27 ("let us make man in our image, after our likeness"), through Gen 5:3's tragic shift to Adam begetting a son "in his own likeness, after his image," through 2 Cor 3:18's slow rewriting ("we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory"), through every counterfeit image-formation pressure the prophecy has exposed (Babylon's diet-name-education program in Dan 1, the gold image on Dura, the beast's image, the mark on hand and forehead). The whole cosmic trial has been about whose image gets formed in the human heart. Rev 22:4 is the answer: the Father's name — His character — fully written. The image lost in Eden is now permanently restored, not by human striving but by sustained beholding (2 Cor 3:18's _grammar_ — _are changed_, passive). The sealing was God's signature being written; the new creation is the signature finally complete and imperishable.

- **"And they shall reign for ever and ever"** (Rev 22:5)
  - The end of the long story of evil. No more night, no lamp or sun, the Lord God lightens them, and they reign with Him forever

## The Final Invitations

Rev 22:6-21 closes the book with repeated calls.

- **"I come quickly" — three times** (Rev 22:7, 12, 20)
  - Rev 22:7 — "Behold, I come quickly"; Rev 22:12 — "And, behold, I come quickly"; Rev 22:20 — "Surely I come quickly"
  - _Quickly_ in Greek (_tachy_) = swiftly, with dispatch. Not "soon in calendar time" but "when He comes, it will be suddenly, without delay"
  - The three occurrences frame the book's last statement, building to the final "Surely I come quickly"

- **"Behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be"** (v. 12)
  - Not works-salvation but works-reward. Each saved person's life has meaning, is noticed, is _rewarded_ — not with more salvation (they already have salvation completely) but with the specific fruit of their faithfulness

- **"I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last"** (v. 13)
  - Christ's own testimony about Himself — bracketing the whole of history

- **"Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life"** (v. 14)
  - Rev 22:14 echoes the remnant's characterization throughout (Rev 12:17; 14:12)
  - _Right_ (Greek _exousia_ — authority, permission) _to the tree of life_ — the same tree Gen 3 barred; the same tree they may now access
  - Obedience does not earn the tree; it _demonstrates_ the character that inhabits the new creation

- **"Come"** (Rev 22:17)
  - "The Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely"
  - Three explicit "Comes" in one verse, expanding into a universal offer:
    - The Spirit and the bride say _Come_ (to the hearer)
    - The hearer says _Come_ (to another)
    - The thirsty may _come_ themselves
    - And the final phrase: _whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely_ — the invitation is unrestricted
  - The gospel offer is open until the book closes

- **"Do not add or take away"** (Rev 22:18-19)
  - A warning against altering the prophecy. The book is complete; it is not to be edited

- **"Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus"** (Rev 22:20)
  - Christ's last spoken words in the Bible: "Surely I come quickly"
  - John's response — the last recorded human response in Scripture: "Even so, come, Lord Jesus"
  - The Bible ends with a prayer

- **"The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen"** (Rev 22:21)
  - A benediction. Grace — the word that defines the whole story from Eden onward — is the last word
  - The Bible begins with a garden and ends with _grace_

## Revelation Ends Where Genesis Began

Consider the correspondence between the Bible's first and last pages.

| Genesis 1-3                                                       | Revelation 21-22                                |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth" (Gen 1:1) | "I saw a new heaven and a new earth" (Rev 21:1) |
| Garden with tree of life (Gen 2:9)                                | City with tree of life (Rev 22:2)               |
| River from Eden (Gen 2:10)                                        | River from the throne (Rev 22:1)                |
| God walks with Adam in the cool of the day (Gen 3:8)              | God's tabernacle with men (Rev 21:3)            |
| Curse pronounced (Gen 3:14-19)                                    | "No more curse" (Rev 22:3)                      |
| Access to the tree barred (Gen 3:24)                              | Access restored (Rev 22:2, 14)                  |
| Pain, sorrow, death enter (Gen 3:16-19)                           | No more tears, death, sorrow, pain (Rev 21:4)   |
| Man driven out (Gen 3:23-24)                                      | They shall see His face (Rev 22:4)              |
| Serpent's head will be bruised (Gen 3:15)                         | Dragon destroyed (Rev 20:10)                    |

- [→][IMG] Revelation is not a new story. It is the completion of the story Genesis began. The whole Bible is one book, with one protagonist (God), one problem (sin), one solution (the Lamb), and one ending — and the ending is not just _all things new_ in the abstract. It is _all things new_ in _you_. The image that fell in Eden is restored in the city; the name lost when Adam chose another lord is rewritten when the redeemed see the Lamb's face. The whole question the book has been pressing — whose image, whose name, whose mark — comes home in Rev 22:4: His name, in your forehead, forever. The cosmic trial closes when the human heart finally and fully bears the likeness it was made for.

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 21:1-5 = the new heaven and new earth; God dwells with humanity; all tears, death, sorrow, and pain pass away; "Behold, I make all things new"
- The holy city is the bride — redeemed and their dwelling together; walls great and high with twelve gates (tribes) and twelve foundations (apostles) — OT + NT together
- The city is a cube (Rev 21:16) — the shape of the Most Holy Place; the whole city is where God dwells
- No temple in the city (Rev 21:22) — the sanctuary thread's eschatological fulfillment; the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb _are_ the temple, the goal toward which every earthly sanctuary pointed
- No sun (Rev 21:23) — God's glory is the light; "no night there"; gates never shut
- Only those in the Lamb's book of life enter (Rev 21:27)
- The river of life flows from God's throne (Rev 22:1); the tree of life is accessible on both sides (Rev 22:2) — the Gen 3:24 exile ended
- Tree's leaves = healing of the nations (Rev 22:2)
- "No more curse" (Rev 22:3) — Gen 3 reversed
- "They shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads" (Rev 22:4) — the remnant/seal thread's final link, and the completion of the image-restoration arc that began in Gen 1:26-27
- "Behold, I come quickly" — Christ's last spoken words in Scripture; "Even so, come, Lord Jesus" — humanity's last recorded response
- The Bible begins with a garden (Eden) and ends with a city (New Jerusalem) in a restored earth, God walking with His people, the tree of life accessible, and grace the last word
