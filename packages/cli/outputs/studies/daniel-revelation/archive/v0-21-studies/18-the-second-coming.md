# Study 18: The Second Coming — Revelation 19

**Heaven responds to Babylon's fall with four Alleluias — the only Alleluias in the entire New Testament. The marriage supper of the Lamb is announced. The bride has made herself ready. Then heaven opens. John sees a white horse and a Rider whose name is _Faithful and True_. His eyes are flames of fire; His head carries many crowns; His vesture is dipped in blood; His name is _The Word of God_. The armies of heaven follow on white horses. Out of His mouth goes a sharp sword. On His thigh is written: KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS. The beast and false prophet are taken and cast into the lake of fire. The armies of the earth, gathered at Armageddon, are slain by the word of the Rider's mouth. Rev 19 is the arrival the whole book has been building toward — the Second Coming of Christ.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 19 (entire chapter)
- Matthew 24:27-31 (the Second Coming in Jesus's own words)
- 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18 (Paul's Second Coming description)
- 2 Thessalonians 2:8 (destroyed by the brightness of His coming)

## The Four Alleluias

Rev 19 opens with worship.

- **"Alleluia" — four times in the opening six verses** (Rev 19:1, 3, 4, 6)
  - _Alleluia_ is the Greek transliteration of the Hebrew _Hallelujah_ — "Praise the LORD"
  - The word is common in the Psalms but appears in the NT _only_ in Rev 19:1-6. Four times in six verses, after silence for the rest of the NT, and then never again
  - The occasion: Babylon has just fallen (Rev 18). The martyrs' "How long?" (Rev 6:10) has been answered. The plagues have completed God's justice. Heaven erupts in Hallelujah

- **What heaven praises**
  - _Salvation and glory and honour and power, unto the Lord our God_ (v. 1)
  - _True and righteous are his judgments: for he hath judged the great whore... and hath avenged the blood of his servants_ (v. 2)
  - _Her smoke rose up for ever and ever_ (v. 3) — Babylon's judgment is permanent
  - _The Lord God omnipotent reigneth_ (v. 6)

- [→] Rev 19's worship is not detached from the preceding judgment. It is _because of_ the judgment that heaven praises. God's justice, long delayed, has finally vindicated the saints and cleansed the universe of the system that killed them. The Alleluias are the praise of delivered witnesses.

## The Marriage Supper of the Lamb

Between the Alleluias and the Second Coming, a wedding is announced.

- **The announcement** (Rev 19:7-9)
  - "Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready"
  - "To her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints"
  - "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb"

- **Who is the bride?**
  - The bride = the church, Christ's covenant people (Eph 5:25-32; 2 Cor 11:2)
  - Rev 21:9-10 confirms: "Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife. And he carried me away in the spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem"
  - The bride and the New Jerusalem are one — the redeemed and their dwelling place, together

- **"Arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints"** (v. 8)
  - The wedding garment is _given_ ("to her was _granted_"), not earned
  - _The righteousness of saints_ — Christ's righteousness, imputed by faith and imparted by the Spirit over a lifetime of discipleship
  - This contrasts directly with Babylon's purple-and-scarlet finery (Rev 17:4): Babylon wears stolen imperial color; the bride wears _given_ linen whiteness

- **The marriage supper's timing**
  - Rev 19:7 announces the marriage _is come_ just before the Rider appears; Rev 19:9 pronounces the blessing on those called to the _supper_ itself. Historicist/SDA interpreters typically distinguish several movements within this covenant-wedding motif: the pre-Advent investigative judgment preparing the bride; Christ receiving the kingdom; the Second Coming, when the groom comes for the bride; and the full celebratory supper when the redeemed are joined to Christ in the New Jerusalem (Rev 21-22)
  - Precise timing of each element is debated within the tradition. The clear point is that Rev 19's announcement climaxes the sequence: the groom is about to arrive

- [→] The cross betrothed us. The investigative judgment (1844) prepared the guest list. The Second Coming (Rev 19:11-16) is the groom coming for the bride. The marriage supper is the final celebration of the covenant's complete fulfillment.

[DYK🔎] One reading of the marriage-supper imagery, going back at least to Adam Clarke's nineteenth-century commentary on Matt 22:2 (and picked up by Uriah Smith), is that some ancient royal _inaugurations_ — the public investiture of a king — could be described in the language of marriage, with the inaugural banquet called a marriage supper (compare 1 Kings 1:5-40, where Solomon's enthronement included a feast). The reading is not a watertight ANE-archaeology claim — it is a traditional way of explaining why several of Jesus's parables blend kingdom-coming with marriage-and-supper imagery (Matt 22:1-14, the king's son's marriage; Matt 25:1-13, the ten virgins waiting for the bridegroom; Luke 14:16-24, the great supper). On this reading, Rev 19:7-9's marriage announcement carries a kingdom-public-acknowledgment register at the same time it carries a covenant register: the bride is the church (Eph 5:25-32), and the same scene also marks the public, universally-witnessed acknowledgment of the kingship inscribed on His thigh (Rev 19:16, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS). The Adventist tradition is careful here: Christ _received_ the kingdom in heaven at the close of the investigative judgment (Dan 7:13-14, taken up at Study 3 and threaded through the Judgment chain) — not at the Second Coming. Rev 19 is therefore not the moment Christ becomes King; it is the moment the kingship He has long held is publicly _manifested_ on earth (Phil 2:10-11), with the bride He has long betrothed now united to Him. This also explains the immediate sequence in Rev 19: the marriage is announced (vv. 7-9), then heaven opens and the King rides forth (vv. 11-16) — the title-inscription on His vesture and thigh (Rev 19:13, 16) is the public assertion of an authority already established.

## John Falls at the Angel's Feet

A small but important incident interrupts the flow.

- **Rev 19:10** — "I fell at his feet to worship him. And he said unto me, See thou do it not: I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus: worship God: for the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy"

- **Three things this verse establishes**
  - _Angels refuse worship._ Only God is worshiped (cf. Col 2:18; Rev 22:8-9). The contrast with the beast who demands worship (Rev 13:4, 8, 12, 15) is total
  - _Prophets and those with the testimony of Jesus are fellow-servants with the angels._ The same ministry shared across heaven and earth
  - _The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy._ The remnant's characteristic (Rev 12:17) is explicitly defined here: they carry the prophetic gift

- `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread definitional callback:** the registered chain is Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. Rev 19:10 is the thread's _definitional_ callback — the verse that explicitly defines "the testimony of Jesus" (first named in Rev 12:17) as "the spirit of prophecy"

## Heaven Opens — The Rider on the White Horse

Rev 19:11-16 describes the arrival.

- **The opening** (Rev 19:11)
  - "I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war"
  - _Heaven opened_ — not a door (Rev 4:1), but the full heavens; the veil between realms torn back
  - _White horse_ — the same imagery as Rev 6:2 (the first seal's apostolic-victory horse). The first seal's rider foreshadowed; this is the fulfillment: Christ, riding in His own conquering victory

- **"Faithful and True"** (v. 11)
  - The same title Christ claimed in Rev 3:14 (the letter to Laodicea: "These things saith the Amen, the faithful and true witness")
  - The Rider is the _witness_ of Rev 1-3 now arriving as the _judge-warrior_ of Rev 19
  - "In righteousness he doth judge and make war" — His war is righteous because it executes a judgment already rendered (Dan 7:9-14, the investigative judgment concluded)

- **The description** (Rev 19:12-13)
  - "His eyes were as a flame of fire" — the same eyes that "searched the reins and hearts" in Rev 2:23; judgment eyes
  - "On his head were many crowns [_diadēmata_ — royal diadems, not _stephanoi_ / victor wreaths]" — universal kingship; contrast the dragon's seven crowned heads and the beast's ten crowned horns (Rev 12:3; 13:1). Christ's crowns are _many_, not limited to a specific domain
  - "He had a name written, that no man knew, but he himself" — a hidden name; ultimate mystery reserved for the Son
  - "He was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood" — _dipped in blood_ before the battle. The blood may point to His own sacrificial victory (the cross is His identification — He conquers as the crucified Lamb), yet the strongest textual echo is Isa 63:1-3, where the warrior's garments are red from treading the winepress of judgment — and Rev 19:15 immediately mentions the winepress. Both readings fit: the conqueror is the crucified Judge, arriving at once as the Lamb who died and as the Judge who tramples His enemies
  - "His name is called The Word of God" (_ho Logos tou Theou_) — the same title John used at the Gospel's opening (John 1:1, 14)

- **The armies of heaven** (v. 14)
  - "The armies which were in heaven followed him upon white horses, clothed in fine linen, white and clean"
  - The saints ride with Christ? Some interpret these as the raised righteous who return with Christ in full triumph (Rev 17:14 — "they that are with him are called, and chosen, and faithful"); the stronger Second Coming precedent points to angels (Matt 25:31 — "the Son of man shall come in his glory, and all the holy angels with him"; 2 Thess 1:7 — "revealed from heaven with his mighty angels"; Jude 14 — "the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints" where _saints_ [_hagioi_] can mean holy angels)
  - In either reading, Christ does not come alone; heaven's full complement accompanies Him

- **The sword and the rod of iron** (v. 15)
  - "Out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations"
  - The sword is the _word_. Christ conquers not by physical violence but by the power of His spoken word — the same word that spoke creation into existence
  - "He shall rule them with a rod of iron" — fulfillment of Psa 2:9's messianic prophecy
  - "He treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God" — the vintage of Rev 14:19-20 now fully executed

- **The inscription** (v. 16)
  - "He hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS"
  - The title Dan 2:37-47 assigned to Christ's kingdom now publicly inscribed. Every earthly king sees his own title eclipsed

- `[⟲]` **Judgment thread execution callback:** the registered chain is Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12. Rev 19:11-16 is not a separate numbered link; it is the _public execution_ of the judgment the court already decided. The books (Rev 20:11-12) remain the thread's final link

## The Supper of the Great God

A shocking counterpoint to the marriage supper of the Lamb.

- **The angel's announcement** (Rev 19:17-18)
  - "I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the fowls that fly in the midst of heaven, Come and gather yourselves together unto the supper of the great God"
  - "That ye may eat the flesh of kings, and the flesh of captains, and the flesh of mighty men, and the flesh of horses, and of them that sit on them, and the flesh of all men, both free and bond, both small and great"

- **Two suppers, two destinations**
  - _Marriage supper of the Lamb_ (Rev 19:9) — for the bride; the guests are blessed
  - _Supper of the great God_ (Rev 19:17) — for the vultures; the corpses are the guests' meal
  - Every person is at one supper or the other. No third option

- **Echo of Ezekiel 39**
  - Eze 39:17-20 described a parallel scene: after the defeat of Gog, God calls the fowls of heaven to feast on the slain
  - Rev 19 universalizes the same pattern: what Ezekiel prophesied against a single enemy now covers the full coalition of the dragon-beast-false-prophet alliance

## The Beast and False Prophet — the Lake of Fire

- **The capture** (Rev 19:19-20)
  - "I saw the beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together to make war against him that sat on the horse, and against his army"
  - "The beast was taken, and with him the false prophet that wrought miracles before him... These both were cast alive into a lake of fire burning with brimstone"

- **The first lake-of-fire judgment**
  - The beast and false prophet are the first into the lake of fire. They arrive _alive_ — the text is emphatic ("cast _alive_")
  - Their followers are slain by the sword of the Rider's mouth (v. 21)
  - This is _not_ yet the final judgment of the millennium's end (Rev 20:11-15). The beast and false prophet are cast in first; Satan will follow at the millennium's end (Rev 20:10)

- **Where Satan is at this point**
  - Satan is not cast into the fire yet. He survives the Second Coming and enters a thousand-year imprisonment on the earth (Rev 20:1-3)
  - The millennium (Study 19) is the period between Rev 19's arrival and Rev 20's final judgment — the period during which Satan has "no nations to deceive" because the saved are in heaven and the wicked are dead

---

## What the Second Coming Does to the Wicked

- **Slain by the word of His mouth** (Rev 19:21)
  - "The remnant were slain with the sword of him that sat upon the horse, which sword proceeded out of his mouth"
  - The wicked are _killed_, not tortured indefinitely. Death is the outcome of Rev 19:21
  - Paul confirms in 2 Thess 2:8: "Then shall that Wicked be revealed, whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth, and shall destroy with the brightness of his coming"

- **No surviving wicked on earth during the millennium**
  - Rev 19 kills the wicked; Rev 20 shows the dead remaining dead until the second resurrection ("the rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" — Rev 20:5)
  - Jer 25:33 — "The slain of the LORD shall be at that day from one end of the earth even unto the other end of the earth: they shall not be lamented, neither gathered, nor buried; they shall be dung upon the ground"
  - Jer 4:23-26 — the earth "without form, and void" after God's judgment, echoing Gen 1:2: the passage's immediate referent is Judah's judgment, but the language is prophetically echoed at the global scale for the millennium's desolate earth

- [→] The Second Coming is not a secret event. It is universal, physical, visible, and catastrophic for those who oppose the Lamb. Every eye sees Him; every enemy falls; the faithful are gathered; the earth is emptied. Rev 20 will show what the next thousand years look like.

## The Second Coming in Revelation's Pattern

- **Foreshadowed throughout the book**
  - Rev 1:7 — "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they also which pierced him"
  - Rev 3:11 — "Behold, I come quickly"
  - Rev 6:14-17 — the sixth seal's final terror
  - Rev 14:14-16 — the Son of Man on the cloud with the sickle
  - Rev 16:15 — "Behold, I come as a thief" — inserted into the plague sequence
  - Rev 19:11-21 — the full arrival

- **Every symbol has led here**
  - The stone that struck the image (Dan 2:45) lands
  - The Son of Man who came to the Ancient of Days (Dan 7:13-14) now returns with His kingdom
  - The white horse of the first seal (Rev 6:2) rides out for the final victory
  - The Lamb of Rev 5:6 appears as the Lion of the tribe of Judah (Rev 5:5, 19:11-16)
  - The High Priest of Rev 1:13-16 has finished His Most Holy Place ministry
  - The bride has made herself ready
  - The dragon's war against the remnant reaches its final page

- [→] Rev 19:11 opens heaven and shows the Second Coming the whole book has been preparing for. Every prophetic thread — the 2300 days, the 1260 years, the Dan 2 image, the Son of Man's judgment, the sanctuary's cleansing, the three angels' messages, the mark and the seal, Babylon's fall, the plagues — all flow into this moment. The threads converge on a face. The book has not been working you toward an event but toward the One the event reveals — the rider whose name no one knows but Himself, whose vesture is dipped in blood, whose eyes are flame, whose word is a sword, whose arrival is the answer every prior chapter held in tension. The ache of every prophecy is to see _Him_. Live in such a way that the day He appears, your heart is ready to be _glad_, not surprised.

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 19 opens with the NT's _only_ Alleluias — four, in response to Babylon's fall and the universe's vindication
- The marriage of the Lamb is announced; the bride = the church, clothed in the _given_ righteousness of saints (v. 8)
- John's falling at the angel's feet and being told "worship God... the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy" (Rev 19:10) defines the remnant's prophetic gift
- Heaven opens (v. 11); the Rider is called Faithful and True, Word of God, KING OF KINGS AND LORD OF LORDS
- His vesture is dipped in blood _before_ the battle — His own blood, from the cross; the Rider comes wearing the mark of His sacrifice
- The armies of heaven follow Him; out of His mouth proceeds the sword of His word
- The beast and false prophet are cast _alive_ into the lake of fire (v. 20); their followers are slain by the sword of the Rider's mouth (v. 21)
- Satan is _not_ yet in the lake of fire; he enters a thousand-year imprisonment at Rev 20:1-3
- The wicked are killed, not tortured indefinitely (2 Thess 2:8; Jer 25:33)
- The Second Coming is the arrival the whole book has been building toward — every prophetic thread converges here
- The registered Judgment chain (Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12) reaches its public-execution moment in Rev 19:11-16; the thread's formal final link remains Rev 20:11-12's great-white-throne books

## Next: Study 19 — The Millennium and the Final Judgment (Revelation 20)

The wicked are slain. The righteous are caught up. The earth is emptied. Satan is bound "that he should deceive the nations no more" — with no nations left to deceive. For a thousand years the saints reign with Christ in heaven and "the judgment was given unto them"; they see every decision God has made. At the millennium's end, Satan is loosed, the wicked are raised, and the final judgment at the great white throne concludes the long history of sin. Fire comes down; death and hell are cast into the lake of fire; every record of evil is blotted out forever. Study 19 is the end of sin's history.
