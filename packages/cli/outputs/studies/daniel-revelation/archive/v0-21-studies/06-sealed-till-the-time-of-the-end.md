# Study 6: Sealed Till the Time of the End — Daniel 12

**Thirteen verses. Michael stands up. A time of trouble unlike any in history. A resurrection — partial, strangely split. Two numbers given nowhere else in Scripture: 1290 and 1335 days. And a book, sealed "to the time of the end," that will be opened again in Revelation 10. Daniel is old. This is his final vision. He does not understand the time. He hears the sworn answer, then asks his own question. And the man clothed in linen gives the chapter's answers in three movements, each time more definite. Then He tells the aged prophet: "Go thy way, Daniel... thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days." Daniel's book closes here. Revelation will pick up the thread.**

- Daniel 12 (entire chapter)
- Matthew 27:52-53
- Revelation 10 (preview of where the sealed book reopens)
- Revelation 12:7-9

## Before the Verses — Reading Dan 12 as a Chiasm

Dan 12 is short — thirteen verses — and tightly built. Following Waterhouse's structural reading, the chapter mirrors itself around Daniel's incomprehension at v.8 — the doubled end-question that the chapter centers on:

```
A     vss. 1-2     Michael stands up; time of trouble; resurrection
B     vs.  3       The wise shine; turn many to righteousness
  a   vs.  4       Shut up the words, seal the book
  b   vss. 5-7a    Two others; the Man in Linen above the river
  c   vs.  7b      "a time, times, and an half"
  d   vs.  8       "I heard, and I understood not... what shall be the end?"
  c'  vs.  9       "the words are shut up and sealed"
  b'  vs.  10      The wise, the wicked, purified and tried
B'    vss. 11-12   1290 days; 1335 days. "Blessed is he that waiteth"
A'    vs.  13      "Go thy way... rest... stand in thy lot at the end of the days"
```

- A and A' both center on **Michael / Daniel _standing_** — Michael stands up to deliver (vs.1); Daniel will stand in his lot at the end (vs.13). The chapter opens with one Person standing up to act and closes with another rising to receive his portion. The structural rhyme is intentional.
- B and b' both name the wise — vs.3's _maskilim_ "shine as the brightness of the firmament," vs.10 the same wise "purified, and made white, and tried." The chapter twice insists wisdom is the trait that survives.
- d (vs.8) is the apex — Daniel's honest "I understood not." The whole chapter centers on a question.
- `[KEY🔑]` **Reading habit, not just structure.** When a prophetic chapter is built as a chiasm, the apex is the interpretive key. Dan 12's center is not a date — it is a confession of incomprehension. The form is telling us how to read what follows: the numbers are real, but the prophet's posture is humility, and the chapter ends with rest, not resolution.

[DYK🔎] Dan 12's chiasm overlaps with the larger Dan 10-12 macro-chiasm (Study 5). The macro-chiasm splits Dan 12 across its own boundaries (Waterhouse puts 11:40b-12:2 in `B'` and 12:3-13 in `A'`), so the chapter-level loop is _embedded within_ rather than _nested cleanly inside_ the larger unit. Two chiastic structures crossing each other in the same passage. The vision's prophetic geometry is doing what the prophecy says: knowledge increases the more carefully you read.

Dan 11 ended with the willful king falling between the seas at the glorious holy mountain, with none to help him. Dan 12 picks up immediately — no new date, no new setting. The same vision continuing.

## Dan 12:1 — Michael stands up; time of trouble; the book

> "And at that time shall Michael stand up, the great prince which standeth for the children of thy people: and there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation even to that same time: and at that time thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book." (Dan 12:1)

- "**At that time**" ties Dan 12 directly to Dan 11:45 — the fall of the northern power is the signal for Michael's standing up.
- "**Michael**" — we met Him in Dan 10 as the One who came to help the angelic messenger; now He acts personally. The pre-incarnate Christ (Study 5).
- "**Stand up**" (Heb. _amad_) signals a change of posture from ministry to action. Michael's standing up triggers the time of trouble and the deliverance — it is not more intercession but the move _beyond_ intercession to executive deliverance. Christ's priestly/intercessory ministry is grounded in Heb 7:25, 8:1-2, 9:24; Heb 10:12's "sat down" marks the enthronement of the completed sacrifice.
- [→] Read against Lev 16 (the high priest emerging from the Most Holy Place when atonement is complete) and Rev 22:11 ("he that is unjust, let him be unjust still... he that is righteous, let him be righteous still" — probation fixed), Michael's standing up in Dan 12:1 marks the close of the mediatorial phase. Rev 19:11-16 pictures what follows: Christ rides forth. The sanctuary is empty. The judgment's verdicts are executed.
- [→] Across Daniel, the Day of Atonement framework Study 4 named (Dan 7:9-14 court convened; Dan 8:14 sanctuary cleansed; Dan 9:24 anointing language) reaches its closing scene here. Dan 12:1 is the priest emerging from the Most Holy Place when atonement is complete.
- "**A time of trouble, such as never was**" — the phrase is unmatched in Scripture. Jesus echoes it directly: "For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be" (Matt 24:21). Matt 24 has both a near-term reference (AD 70) and a far-term reference (the end); on the historicist reading, Jesus' unmatched-tribulation language reaches beyond AD 70 to the final crisis. Dan 12:1's "such as never was" belongs with the far horizon.
- This is the period Rev 16 describes as the seven last plagues, and Jer 30:7 calls "the time of Jacob's trouble."
- [→] **The order matters.** Probation closes _before_ the trouble begins. The sealed saints (Rev 7:1-3) go through the trouble, but they are already sealed when it starts.
- "**Thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book.**" Book = the book of life (Ex 32:32-33; Mal 3:16-17; Rev 20:12-15). Deliverance is by name, not by category — _each one_ found in the book.
- `[⟲]` **Michael thread:** Dan 10:13 → Dan 10:21 → **Dan 12:1 (this link)** → Jude 9 → Rev 12:7. The Warrior-Prince's third textual appearance — the decisive one, where He _stands up_ to close His mediatorial phase.
- `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread (link 1):** **Dan 12:1 (this link)** → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. The named-and-sealed remnant thread begins here.

[DYK🔎] The name "Michael" itself is a question: Hebrew _mi-ka-el_ = "Who is like God?" Daniel's archangel-Prince bears the question as His name. Watch for it in Rev 13:4, where the dragon-empowered beast is worshipped with the parodic question "Who is like unto the beast?" Same question, twisted target. The full counterfeit-original treatment lands in Study 13.

## Dan 12:2 — Many of them that sleep shall awake

> "And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt." (Dan 12:2)

- **One of the clearest OT statements of bodily resurrection — and one of the strangest.**
- "**Many** of them that sleep" — _not all_. This is key. The general resurrection of all the dead comes later (Rev 20:5-6, 12-13). Dan 12:2's "many" is partial.
- _Both_ righteous and wicked in the same resurrection: "some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt." Two destinies named in one verse.
- **Historicist reading: a special resurrection at Christ's return.** Rev 1:7 — "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and _they also which pierced him_." Those who pierced Christ are long dead by the Second Coming. For them to see Him return requires a special resurrection _before_ the general resurrection. Dan 12:2 accounts for this: some righteous are raised (saints who preached through persecution); some wicked are raised (those complicit in Christ's death).
- **Matt 27:52-53** gives a precedent: at the crucifixion, "many bodies of the saints which slept arose, and came out of the graves after his resurrection, and went into the holy city, and appeared unto many." A _partial_ resurrection of saints on a specific prophetic occasion.
- The text itself names the two destinies but does not itemize which individuals fall in which group — that is interpretive extrapolation from Rev 1:7, not a statement Dan 12:2 makes alone.
- An alternative reading takes Dan 12:2's "many" as a Hebrew idiom for "the multitude" / the full resurrection. That reading is grammatically possible but does not account for Rev 1:7's requirement of an earlier resurrection of Christ's piercers.
- Either way, Dan 12:2 is a clear OT witness to: bodily resurrection of both righteous and wicked (cf. John 5:28-29; Acts 24:15), two eternal destinies, and God's power to raise the dead — stated centuries before Easter, against later Sadducee skepticism (Matt 22:29-32).

## Dan 12:3 — They that be wise shall shine

> "And they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever." (Dan 12:3)

- "**The wise**" — _maskilim_, "those who give understanding" (the same word from Dan 11:33). Inward wisdom + outward fruit: those who understand AND who turn many to righteousness. The same people doing both things.
- "**Shine as the brightness of the firmament**" — Gen 1's firmament holds the lights God set to rule day and night; Dan 12:3's wise are lit with the same kind of glory.
- Rev 14 will pick up this company: "a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand" (Rev 14:1) — the sealed ones who understood and turned many.
- [→][IMG] Tracking the [IMG] thread: the law in the ark is the image-in-words (Study 4); Christ at the mercy seat is the image-in-flesh; Dan 12:3's _maskilim_ are the **image-in-character** — Christ's likeness formed in the saint, now shining outward. The Day of Atonement settles the verdict (Study 4); Dan 12:3 is what the settled image looks like.

## Dan 12:4 — Shut up the words; seal the book

> "But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased." (Dan 12:4)

- "**Seal**" (Heb. _chatham_) = to close, ratify, authenticate — like a sealed document whose contents are kept from general reading until authorized.
- "**Even to the time of the end**" — not the end itself, but the era leading up to it. Daniel's book is sealed for a duration, then unsealed.
- "**Many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased.**" Historical readings have ranged: increased travel and education in the modern era (19th century forward); or, more contextually, prophetic students "running to and fro" through the Scriptures in search of understanding once the seal lifts.
- The latter fits the immediate context: the book itself is what has been sealed; the ones running to and fro are those searching it out.
- [→] The prophecy that closes Daniel's book is its own commentary on what happens when the book reopens: a generation will seek its meaning, and knowledge will increase. The early 19th-century Advent awakening (Miller, Wolff, Lacunza, Irving — Study 3) is exactly this. Once the seal breaks, the running to and fro begins.

## Dan 12:5-6 — Two others; the Man in Linen above the river

> "Then I Daniel looked, and, behold, there stood other two, the one on this side of the bank of the river, and the other on that side of the bank of the river. And one said to the man clothed in linen, which was upon the waters of the river, How long shall it be to the end of these wonders?" (Dan 12:5-6)

- Daniel sees two others — one on each side of the river. The man clothed in linen (the same figure from Dan 10:5-6 — the Day-of-Atonement High Priest, **Christ**) stands "upon the waters of the river."
- "**Upon the waters**" — Christ stands above the chaos of time itself. The river that flows runs under His feet. He owns the timeline.
- The "two others" frame the scene; one of them asks the question. Compare Ex 25:18-22 — the two cherubim flanking the mercy seat, with God meeting between them.
- "**How long shall it be to the end of these wonders?**" — the saints' perennial cry. Compare Hab 1:2; Rev 6:10. Heaven takes the question seriously enough to answer under oath.

## Dan 12:7 — The double-handed oath; time, times, and a half

> "And I heard the man clothed in linen, which was upon the waters of the river, when he held up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven, and sware by him that liveth for ever that it shall be for a time, times, and an half; and when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people, all these things shall be finished." (Dan 12:7)

- "**Held up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven**" — a double-handed oath. Compare Deut 32:40, where God Himself swears with a single lifted hand. **Lifting both is the heightened form** — the most solemn oath form in Scripture.
- "**Sware by him that liveth for ever**" — by God's own eternal life. The strongest possible guarantee.
- "**Time, times, and an half**" = three-and-a-half times = 1260 days = **1260 years** (year-day, Study 3). The same period Dan 7:25 already named.
- "**When he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people**" — the 1260 years is defined as the period during which the papal power "scatters" (breaks, disperses) the authority of God's people.
- Historically: AD 538 (Justinian's AD 533 decree recognizing Roman primacy became practically enforceable after the Ostrogothic siege of Rome was lifted) → AD 1798 (French general Berthier captures Pope Pius VI and removes him from Rome). Dan 7's "wear out the saints" meets Dan 12's "scatter the power of the holy people" at this exact span.
- [→] The two-handed, sworn-by-the-Eternal oath is God personally ratifying the 1260-year period. The same number Dan 7 mentioned in passing is here set under the strongest possible guarantee. Not a number historicists invented. **A number God Himself sealed with an oath.**
- `[⟲]` **1260-year thread (oath link):** Dan 7:25 → **Dan 12:7 (this link, oath confirmation)** → Rev 11:2 → Rev 11:3 → Rev 12:6 → Rev 12:14 → Rev 13:5. Seven witnesses to the same period; Dan 12:7 is the formal oath-confirmation.

[DYK🔎] Dan 12:5-7 belongs among Scripture's most solemn oath scenes — the double-handed lift, "by him that liveth for ever," named alongside Deut 32:40 and the patriarchal oaths (Gen 22:16; Heb 6:13). Same question Habakkuk asks (Hab 1:2 — "O LORD, how long shall I cry?"), same question the souls under the altar ask (Rev 6:10 — "How long, O Lord?"). Across the canon, "how long?" is the saint's prayer. Dan 12:5-7 is heaven taking the question seriously enough to swear under oath in answer to it.

## Dan 12:8 — I heard, but I understood not

> "And I heard, but I understood not: then said I, O my Lord, what shall be the end of these things?" (Dan 12:8)

- **The chapter's apex. The chiasm centers here.**
- Daniel is honest. In his mid-eighties. A lifetime of visions behind him. And he still does not grasp the timing.
- "**O my Lord, what shall be the end of these things?**" — not merely _when_ (he has already heard the sworn 1260 answer, and the 1290/1335 answer will follow in vss.11-12), but _what_ — what does the end look like?
- The doubled-question pattern of Dan 12 is now visible: vs.6 asked "how long?"; vs.8 asks "what shall be the end?" Two questions, two prophetic-number answers (vs.7 = 1260, vss.11-12 = 1290 + 1335). Daniel still rests without comprehension (vs.13).
- [→] **The pattern says something theologically important: prophetic numbers are honestly given; full grasp of them is not the condition of faithful waiting.** Daniel is told to _go thy way_ — not because the answer is withheld but because resting in the partial answer is itself the test.

## Dan 12:9 — The words are shut up and sealed till the time of the end

> "And he said, Go thy way, Daniel: for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end." (Dan 12:9)

- "**Go thy way, Daniel.**" Daniel will not live to see the unsealing. He will rest. He will wait. The book goes to other readers — generations later — who will open it when the time comes.
- "**Closed up and sealed till the time of the end.**" Same verb (_chatham_) as vs.4. The instruction at the front of the chiasm is sworn at the back: seal it, and it stays sealed until the appointed era.
- [→] The prophet who served God across seventy years in Babylon does not get the answer. The grace here is not in the information; it is in the trust. Daniel dies without fully understanding, and God tells him that is fine. The book will open when it should.

## Dan 12:10 — Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried

> "Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand." (Dan 12:10)

- _Yitbarru, yitlabbenu, yitsarefu_ — "purified, made white, refined." Sanctuary-purity language carried into the saint.
- Rev 7:14's great-multitude have "washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb." Dan 8:14's _nitsdaq_ ("be cleansed/vindicated") names what happens to the holy place; Dan 12:10's three verbs name what happens to the people. **Different roots, same sanctuary-purity field — the cleansing of the sanctuary and the cleansing of the saint are one process.**
- "**The wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand.**" Understanding prophetically is a moral condition, not just an intellectual one. The wicked are not simply ignorant — they are unable to understand, because they refuse to obey (cf. John 7:17 — "If any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine").
- [→][IMG] The same text stands before two readers. One reads and is purified. The other reads and confirms his own rebellion. **The difference is not the text.** The [IMG] thread continues: the law in the ark, Christ at the mercy seat, the saints whose image is being purified by the same Day-of-Atonement work.

## Dan 12:11 — From the time the daily is taken away: 1290 days

> "And from the time that the daily sacrifice shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days." (Dan 12:11)

- **1290 prophetic days = 1290 years** (year-day).
- **Starting point**: when the daily is taken away and the abomination is set up — tied to Dan 8:11-13 and Dan 11:31 (see Study 5). Historicists place this start at **AD 508**: Clovis' Catholic military consolidation in the West, defeating the Arian Visigoths at Vouillé (AD 507) and being recognized by Emperor Anastasius soon after — a step in the rise of the papal-political fusion.
- **508 + 1290 = AD 1798**, the same terminus as the 1260.
- The 30-year difference (1290 vs. 1260) = two different starting points for related events. The 1260 starts at AD 538 (papal supremacy formal); the 1290 starts at AD 508 (the transition phase that prepared it).

## Dan 12:12 — Blessed is he that waiteth: 1335 days

> "Blessed is he that waiteth, and cometh to the thousand three hundred and five and thirty days." (Dan 12:12)

- **1335 - 1290 = 45-year extension.** 45 years from AD 1798 = **AD 1843**.
- Historicist reading: the Advent awakening (which preached the end in 1843/1844) is the "blessed" movement Dan 12:12 describes — those who waited through the 1335 and reached the era of the sealed book's opening.
- The "blessing" is for those who reach this moment, whether they understand every detail or not. The Millerites' 1843-1844 calculations were imperfect (they miscalculated by one year initially), but their _waiting on the prophecy_ put them in the blessed position Dan 12:12 named.
- **Caveat:** these secondary dates (AD 508, AD 1798, AD 1843) are held less tightly than the 70-weeks anchor dates (Study 3). They represent the best historicist synthesis, not decrees of Scripture. We hold what the text requires — a period of papal dominance ending around the turn of the 19th century, followed by an Advent awakening — with confidence; the precise year-anchors are held with the confidence they have earned.

[DYK🔎] The three numbers 1260, 1290, 1335 all converge on the same window — late 18th to mid-19th century. The 1260 (Dan 7:25; Rev 12, 13) ends at 1798 with Napoleon's general Berthier deposing Pius VI. The 1290 (Dan 12:11) ends at the same year, 1798, starting 30 years earlier at AD 508. The 1335 (Dan 12:12) ends at 1843, pointing to the Advent awakening that preceded 1844's heavenly Day of Atonement. **Three independent prophetic numbers converging on the same historical moment is not an accident of interpretation. It is the prophecy insisting on its own identification of the era of its unsealing.**

## Dan 12:13 — Go thy way; rest; stand in thy lot

> "But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days." (Dan 12:13)

- **Three movements: _go_, _rest_, _stand_.** Life, death, resurrection.
- "**Stand in thy lot**" = receive your portion in the inheritance, at the final distribution of the kingdom. _Goral_ ("lot") is the same word used for tribal land allotments in Joshua — Daniel will receive his territory in the kingdom.
- The chiasm closes. Vs.1 opened with **Michael standing up** to act; vs.13 closes with **Daniel standing in his lot** to receive. The Person who stands up to deliver is the One who guarantees the prophet's standing.
- [→] Daniel's closing promise is not that he will understand every prophetic detail. **It is that he will _be there_ when the kingdom is given.** The same promise covers every faithful reader who closes the book without every question answered.

## After the Verses — The Inverse Commands

- **Dan 12:4** — "Shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end"
- **Rev 22:10** — "Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand"

Two books, two commands, two eras.

- Daniel's book is sealed until the time of the end.
- Revelation is _commanded open_, from the moment it is given, because the time is at hand.
- The sealing of one matches the opening of the other. When the seal lifts on Daniel (at "the time of the end"), Revelation is already open and waiting.
- [→] The unsealing of Daniel happens precisely when Revelation's "little book open in the angel's hand" (Rev 10:2) is presented to the church — i.e., the Advent awakening leading to 1844. From that moment forward, Daniel and Revelation are meant to be read together, as one book, in the final generation.

**The privilege is also the test.** The book opened in your generation is opened _to_ your generation. Daniel longed to understand and was told to wait (Dan 12:9, 13). You were born after the seal lifted. The question Daniel could not answer — _what shall be the end?_ — has been answered in the open book on your shelf. The remaining question is no longer "Can it be understood?" It is "Will the readers of this book live as people who have actually read it?"

- The Dan 12 chiasm overlaps with the larger Dan 10-12 macro-chiasm (Study 5) — embedded within, not cleanly nested inside

- Michael's "standing up" = the close of Christ's mediatorial/intercessory priestly work (cf. Heb 10:12's "sat down" implies standing is the opposite move); the closing scene of the Day of Atonement framework Study 4 named (high priest emerging from the Most Holy Place)
- The name _mi-ka-el_ ("Who is like God?") is the seed for Rev 13:4's parodic "Who is like unto the beast?" — full counterfeit-original treatment in Study 13
- The "time of trouble, such as never was" (Dan 12:1) is echoed by Jesus in Matt 24:21; the unmatched-tribulation wording reaches past AD 70 to the final crisis
- Dan 12:2's partial resurrection "some... some" fits with Rev 1:7's requirement that Christ's piercers see Him return — a special resurrection at the Second Coming, before the general one of Rev 20
- The wise "shine as the brightness of the firmament" (vs.3) and are "purified, made white, and tried" (vs.10) — the sanctuary-purity vocabulary that names the holy place's cleansing (Dan 8:14) names the saint's cleansing here, the same process. Image-formation thread (Study 4 → Dan 12:3, 10)
- Understanding prophecy is a moral condition (Dan 12:10); the same text purifies one reader and confirms another's rebellion
- The doubled "how long?" / "what shall be the end?" question pattern shows that prophetic numbers are honestly given but full grasp is not the condition of faithful waiting
- The 1260-year period is here sworn under God's most solemn oath (Dan 12:7) — the oath-confirmation within the seven-witness `[⟲]` 1260-year chain
- The 1290 (Dan 12:11) starts at AD 508, ends 1798; the 1335 (Dan 12:12) ends at AD 1843 (Advent awakening); held as historicist synthesis with the confidence they earn
- Daniel dies without fully understanding (Dan 12:8-9, 13); the book is sealed to his era but opens at the time of the end
- Dan 12:4's sealing + Rev 22:10's "seal not" = inverse commands across two eras; the final generation gets both books unsealed together

## Next: Study 7 — The Revelation of Jesus Christ (Rev 1-3)

Daniel's book closes sealed. Revelation opens with Christ standing in the middle of seven golden candlesticks, dressed in language that echoes the Man in Linen from Dan 10. The description matches feature for feature. Same Person, new phase. And John is told something Daniel was not told: _do not seal this._ The time is now. Seven churches receive seven letters, each addressing a specific era in church history. Every promise is to "him that overcometh." Study 7 opens Revelation's first recapitulation cycle.

[→][IMG] Notice what closes Daniel: not a chart, not a date, but a Person. The Man in Linen — Christ — stands above the river, hands lifted, swearing by the Eternal that the persecution _will_ end and the people _will_ be delivered. The image-formation thread reaches its first horizon here: vs.3's _maskilim_ shine "as the brightness of the firmament" — the saint who has been formed under the High Priest's Day-of-Atonement work now bears the light He bears. Daniel does not see all the answers; he sees the Face that has them. Then he is told to rest and to stand in his lot. **The image is settled when the eye that loves it closes in trust.** Revelation will pick the picture up in chapter 1 — the same Christ, now standing among the candlesticks, with the same eye looking back.
