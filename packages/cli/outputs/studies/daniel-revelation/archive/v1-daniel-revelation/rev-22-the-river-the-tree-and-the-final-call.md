# Revelation 22 — The River, the Tree, and the Final Call

**Rev 21 ended with the new-creation city descending and the throne-occupant declaring "It is done." Rev 22 opens inside the city: a pure river of water of life proceeds out of the throne of God and of the Lamb; on either side of the river the tree of life bears twelve manner of fruits, yielding fruit every month, with leaves for the healing of the nations. "There shall be no more curse." The servants of God see His face, and His name is in their foreheads. They reign forever and ever. The angel testifies the sayings are faithful and true. Christ Himself speaks three times: "Behold, I come quickly... Behold, I come quickly... Surely I come quickly." A solemn warning closes the canon: do not add to or take away from this book. The Spirit and the bride say "Come"; whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely. The book ends — and the Bible ends — with John's reply: "Even so, come, Lord Jesus. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen." Rev 22 is the chapter where Eden is restored, the curse is reversed, the face of God is seen, and the canon closes with the bride's cry for the Bridegroom.**

- Revelation 22 (entire chapter)

- Zechariah 14:8, 11 (living waters going out from Jerusalem; "no more utter destruction")
- Psalm 1:3 (the righteous as a tree planted by rivers of water, fruit in season)
- Psalm 36:9 (with thee is the fountain of life)
- Matthew 5:8 ("Blessed are the pure in heart: for they shall see God")
- Hebrews 12:14 (without holiness no man shall see the Lord)

- Matthew 16:27 (the Son of man shall come and reward every man according to his works)
- Deuteronomy 4:2; 12:32 (don't add to or take from God's word — the canon-protection precedent)

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 22 Sits

Rev 22 is the closing chapter of Revelation and the closing chapter of the entire Bible. **Rev 21 announced the new creation and showed the bride-city; Rev 22 takes us inside the city and then closes the canon with Christ's final words.** The chapter has three movements: the Eden-restored interior (vv. 1-5), the threefold "I come quickly" with its angelic and Christic certifications (vv. 6-16), and the final invitation, warning, and benediction (vv. 17-21).

- **Eden lost in Gen 3 is Eden restored in Rev 22.** The river of Gen 2:10 is here in v. 1; the tree of life of Gen 2:9 is here in v. 2; the cherub-guarded exile of Gen 3:22-24 is reversed in v. 14 ("they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city"). **The Bible's first three chapters and last three chapters frame the whole story** — creation, fall, exile / new creation, no curse, return.
- **The image-of-God-restored arc terminates at v. 4.** Gen 1:26-27 had man made in God's image; sin marred it; 2 Cor 3:18 said believers are being changed into His image from glory to glory; Rev 22:4 says "they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads." **The face-to-face dwelling and the name-on-the-forehead together are the climax of the salvation arc.**
- **The sixth Revelation beatitude appears in v. 7, and the seventh and final appears in v. 14.** Revelation has seven beatitudes — Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14. **Rev 22:7 ("blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book") continues the closing blessing pattern**, with v. 14 ("Blessed are they that do his commandments") completing it.
- **The three "I come quickly" sayings are the book's closing chime.** Rev 22:7, 22:12, 22:20 — Christ Himself speaking. **The book that opened with "Behold, he cometh with clouds" (Rev 1:7) closes with "Surely I come quickly... Even so, come, Lord Jesus" (Rev 22:20).** The whole prophecy is bracketed by the second-advent expectation.
- **The canon-protection warning of vv. 18-19 is anchored in Deut 4:2 and 12:32.** Moses gave the same warning at the start of canonical Scripture; John gives it at the end. **The Bible's bookends both forbid tampering with God's word.**
- **The Spirit-and-bride invitation of v. 17** is the church's final mission statement. The Holy Spirit and the bride (the church/redeemed) say "Come" — and the call is open: "whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely." **The gospel invitation runs right up to the second coming.**

[KEY🔑] **Rev 22 is the chapter where Eden is restored, the curse is reversed, the face of God is seen, and the bride cries for the Bridegroom.** Gen 3:22-24 ended with the cherubim and a flaming sword guarding the way to the tree of life; Rev 22:14 reopens the way ("they may have right to the tree of life"). Gen 3:17's curse on the ground is undone in Rev 22:3 ("there shall be no more curse"). Gen 3:8's broken face-to-face fellowship is restored in Rev 22:4 ("they shall see his face"). **What was lost is given back — and given back permanently.** The Bible begins in a garden with a river and a tree and humans walking with God; the Bible ends in a city with a river and a tree and humans walking with God. **Between those two scenes is the whole drama of the great controversy, and Rev 22 is its resolution.** The closing words — "Even so, come, Lord Jesus" — are the bride's response to the whole prophecy: not a passive waiting but an active longing that the call of Rev 22:17 ("Come") would soon be answered by His coming in v. 20.

## Rev 22:1-2 — The River and the Tree

> "And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb. In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations." (Rev 22:1-2)

- "**A pure river of water of life, clear as crystal**" — Greek _potamon hydatos zōēs lampron hōs krystallon_. **_Hydatos zōēs_** = "of water of life," genitive of quality — water that **is** life or **gives** life. **The river restores what Eden had** — "and a river went out of Eden to water the garden" (Gen 2:10). **Compare Eze 47:1** ("waters issued out from under the threshold of the house eastward"); **Zech 14:8** ("living waters shall go out from Jerusalem"); **Ps 36:9** ("with thee is the fountain of life"); **Jn 4:14** ("the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life"); **Jn 7:38-39** (the rivers of living water from the believer's belly = the Spirit). **The OT river-prophecies all converge here.**
- "**Proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb**" — **the throne is the source.** **The throne of judgment in Rev 20 has become the throne of life-giving in Rev 22.** **The river proceeds from both** — God and the Lamb together, one source, the same united throne already announced in Rev 21:22-23.
- "**In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river**" — Greek _en mesō tēs plateias autēs_. **The tree is in the city's main avenue, with the river running down the middle, and the tree on both banks.** **The architectural picture is precise** — the tree spans the river. **Compare Eze 47:7, 12** ("at the bank of the river were very many trees on the one side and on the other... shall grow all trees for meat, whose leaf shall not fade, neither shall the fruit thereof be consumed: it shall bring forth new fruit according to his months... and the leaf thereof for medicine"). **Ezekiel's vision is unfolded here with the same details.**
- "**The tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month**" — Greek _xylon zōēs poioun karpous dōdeka, kata mēna hekaston apodidoun ton karpon autou_. **_Xylon_** = wood/tree (the same word used of the cross — Acts 5:30, 10:39, 13:29; Gal 3:13; 1 Pet 2:24 — **the cross-tree gave the redeemed access to the life-tree**). **Twelve manner of fruits** — twelve crops, one each month. **Compare Gen 2:9** ("the tree of life also in the midst of the garden") and **Gen 3:22-24** (Adam barred from the tree). **The cherub-guarded exile is reversed; the tree is now accessible.**
- "**The leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations**" — Greek _ta phylla tou xylou eis therapeian tōn ethnōn_. **_Therapeian_** = healing/cure (English "therapy" derives from this); in the new-earth context this is restorative and sustaining, not evidence of ongoing sickness. **_Tōn ethnōn_** = of the nations. **The redeemed nations of Rev 21:24 receive ongoing life from the tree.** **Compare Eze 47:12** ("the leaf thereof for medicine"). **The leaves are not for sick people becoming well; the leaves are for already-healed people staying well — sustained life, not pharmaceutical cure.**

[⟲] **The river-and-tree of Rev 22:1-2 closes a recapitulation arc that runs from Eden through the prophets to the new creation.** **Gen 2:9-10** — original tree and original river. **Eze 47:1-12** — eschatological river from the temple with healing trees on its banks, fruit every month, leaves for medicine. **Zech 14:8** — living waters from Jerusalem. **Ps 1:3** — the righteous one as a tree planted by rivers, fruit in his season. **Jn 7:38-39** — the river from the believer's belly identified with the Spirit. **Rev 22:1-2** — the eternal-state convergence. **All the river-and-tree texts of Scripture meet here.** The Eden picture, the Ezekiel temple-vision, the Zechariah Jerusalem-prophecy, the psalmic righteous-tree image, and the gospel Spirit-promise are all one trajectory aimed at this verse.

## Rev 22:3-5 — No More Curse; They Shall See His Face

> "And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him: And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads. And there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light: and they shall reign for ever and ever." (Rev 22:3-5)

- "**There shall be no more curse**" — Greek _kai pan katathema ouk estai eti_. **_Katathema_** = curse, devoted-thing-under-judgment (related to _anathema_). **The Genesis curse on the serpent and the ground, and the sorrow attending childbirth and labor — gone.** **Compare Zech 14:11** ("there shall be no more utter destruction; but Jerusalem shall be safely inhabited"). **The curse-reversal is complete.**
- "**The throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it**" — **the city contains the throne.** **One throne, two occupants.** The unified throne-language of Rev 22:1, 22:3 is unmistakable — God and the Lamb share the throne. **Compare Rev 3:21** ("To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with me in my throne, even as I also overcame, and am set down with my Father in his throne") and **Rev 5:6** (the Lamb in the midst of the throne). **The Lamb's enthronement, secured by the cross and the resurrection, reaches its eternal expression here.**
- "**His servants shall serve him**" — Greek _hoi douloi autou latreusousin autō_. **_Latreuō_** = render priestly/sacred service, worship-service (the same root as the Septuagint's word for tabernacle ministry). **The redeemed are priest-servants in the eternal sanctuary-city.** **Compare Rev 1:6** ("hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father"); **Rev 5:10** ("hath made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth"); **1 Pet 2:9** ("a royal priesthood, an holy nation"). **The priest-people identity, given at Sinai and renewed in Christ, is the eternal-state vocation.**
- "**They shall see his face**" — Greek _kai opsontai to prosōpon autou_. **The face-to-face reality.** **Compare Ex 33:20** ("Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live") — **the prohibition under fallen conditions is reversed in glorified conditions.** **Compare Mt 5:8** ("Blessed are the pure in heart: for they shall see God"); **1 Cor 13:12** ("now we see through a glass, darkly; but then face to face"); **1 Jn 3:2** ("when he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is"); **Heb 12:14** ("holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord"). **The beatific vision — promised throughout the NT — is realized here.**
- "**His name shall be in their foreheads**" — Greek _kai to onoma autou epi tōn metōpōn autōn_. **The seal-of-God thread reaches its terminus.** **Rev 7:3** sealed the servants of God in their foreheads. **Rev 14:1** showed the 144,000 with the Father's name written in their foreheads. **Rev 22:4** confirms the seal as the eternal mark.\*\* **Counter to the mark of the beast in the forehead (Rev 13:16, 14:9) — two foreheads, two destinies.** **The name on the forehead is character-restoration: God's name = God's character, written into the redeemed.**
- "**There shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light**" — **direct repetition of Rev 21:23, 25.** The double declaration confirms permanence. **Compare Isa 60:19-20.**
- "**They shall reign for ever and ever**" — Greek _basileusousin eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_. **The promise of Rev 5:10 ("we shall reign on the earth") and Rev 20:6 ("they shall reign with him a thousand years") and Rev 22:5 ("they shall reign for ever and ever") forms a three-stage reigning-arc** — the millennial reign in heaven, the post-millennial reign on the new earth, and the eternal reign that continues without end. **The redeemed share Christ's throne forever** (Rev 3:21).

[CTRF] **Two foreheads. Two reigns. Two destinies — and the great controversy ends here.** The mark of the beast in the forehead (Rev 13:16, 14:9) is character-conformity to the rebellion; the Father's name in the forehead (Rev 14:1, 22:4) is character-conformity to God. **Rev 13's beast-system stamped allegiance into worshipers; Rev 22:4's redeemed bear God's name as the eternal counter-stamp.** The beast's followers had no rest day or night (Rev 14:11); the redeemed reign forever (Rev 22:5). The harlot reigned over the kings of the earth (Rev 17:18) for a season; the bride reigns with the Lamb forever (Rev 22:5). **Every great-controversy contrast — beast vs. Lamb, harlot vs. bride, Babylon vs. New Jerusalem, mark in the forehead vs. name in the forehead — has its final terminus in Rev 22.** The forehead-distinction that ran through the seven last plagues, that determined who was sealed and who was marked, that decided the harvest of Rev 14, is the same distinction that determines who sees God's face in Rev 22:4.

## Rev 22:6-7 — Faithful and True; Behold, I Come Quickly

> "And he said unto me, These sayings are faithful and true: and the Lord God of the holy prophets sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done. Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book." (Rev 22:6-7)

- "**These sayings are faithful and true**" — Greek _houtoi hoi logoi pistoi kai alēthinoi_. **Verbatim repetition of Rev 21:5's certification.** **The whole vision — from Rev 1:1 through Rev 22:5 — bears the divine attestation.** **Compare Rev 19:9, 19:11** (Christ Himself called Faithful and True). **The book's authority is its Author's character.**
- "**The Lord God of the holy prophets**" — TR/KJV: "the Lord God of the holy prophets"; critical text: _kyrios ho theos tōn pneumatōn tōn prophētōn_ ("the Lord, the God of the spirits of the prophets"). **The same God who spoke through Isaiah, Daniel, Ezekiel, Zechariah is the source of John's vision.** **Continuity of inspiration** — Revelation is in the prophetic tradition, not a novel revelation.
- "**Sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done**" — Greek _hosa dei genesthai en tachei_. **Verbatim repetition of Rev 1:1.** **The book's prologue is echoed in its epilogue** — the bracketing announces the same prophetic mission that opened in Rev 1.
- "**The things which must shortly be done**" — _en tachei_ = "with quickness/speed." **Not necessarily "soon in time" but "quickly when it begins."** **The historicist reading takes _en tachei_ alongside the year-day-unfolding centuries (1260, 2300, 1844)** — the prophetic clock begins immediately and runs without delay through history; the events are not at a remote future but are being executed now. **John lived in AD 95; the seals, trumpets, and beast-period began their unfolding at once and have been running ever since.**
- "**Behold, I come quickly**" — Greek _idou erchomai tachy_. **The first of three Christic "I come quickly" sayings** (vv. 7, 12, 20). **_Tachy_** = swiftly/suddenly when it happens. **Compare Mt 24:42-44** ("watch... for in such an hour as ye think not the Son of man cometh"); **Lk 12:40** ("be ye therefore ready also: for the Son of man cometh at an hour when ye think not"); **2 Pet 3:10** ("the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night"). **The suddenness, not the immediacy, is the point.**
- "**Blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book**" — Greek _makarios ho tērōn tous logous tēs prophēteias tou bibliou toutou_. **The sixth of seven Revelation beatitudes** (Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14). **Rev 1:3's opening beatitude returns as Rev 22:7's near-closing beatitude** — the book that opened with a blessing on the reader/keeper closes with the same.

[DYK🔎] **Revelation contains seven beatitudes — a seventh-fold blessing structure that mirrors the book's seven-fold architecture (seven churches, seals, trumpets, plagues, etc.).** **(1) Rev 1:3** — blessed is he that readeth and keepeth the prophecy. **(2) Rev 14:13** — blessed are the dead which die in the Lord. **(3) Rev 16:15** — blessed is he that watcheth and keepeth his garments. **(4) Rev 19:9** — blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb. **(5) Rev 20:6** — blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection. **(6) Rev 22:7** — blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book. **(7) Rev 22:14** — blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life. **The sevenfold structure tracks the Christian's whole journey** — from reading the prophecy (1) to entering the city through the gates (7). The 14:13 beatitude on the faithful dead and the 20:6 beatitude on the first resurrection bracket the millennium; the 19:9 marriage-supper invitation and the 22:14 city-entrance complete the wedding-banquet-then-residence sequence. **The seventh beatitude (22:14) names commandment-keeping — the same identifying mark as the Rev 14:12 saints — as the qualification for tree-access and city-entry.**

## Rev 22:8-9 — John Falls Down; the Angel Refuses Worship

> "And I John saw these things, and heard them. And when I had heard and seen, I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel which shewed me these things. Then saith he unto me, See thou do it not: for I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren the prophets, and of them which keep the sayings of this book: worship God." (Rev 22:8-9)

- "**I John saw these things, and heard them**" — **John's personal authorship attestation, paralleling Rev 1:1, 1:4, 1:9, 21:2.** **The book is signed.** John presents himself as eyewitness to the vision.
- "**I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel**" — **the second time John makes this mistake** (cf. Rev 19:10, where the same reaction was rebuked at the marriage-supper announcement). **John's repetition shows how overwhelming the visions were** — even an apostle, even after a previous correction, fell before the messenger.
- "**See thou do it not: for I am thy fellowservant... worship God**" — Greek _hora mē; syndoulos sou eimi... tō theō proskynēson_. **The angel's correction repeats the worship-refusal of Rev 19:10, but changes the fellowship-list:** Rev 19:10 names those who have the testimony of Jesus; Rev 22:9 names the prophets and those who keep the sayings of the book. **The angel identifies with prophets and with prophecy-keepers.** **Worship is restricted to God alone** — not to angels, not even to glorified beings, not to Mary, not to saints. **The first commandment (Ex 20:3-5) is the heaven-ratified eternal standard.**
- **The doubled refusal in Revelation matters polemically.** Two times John tries to worship an angel; two times the angel refuses. **The mediation-creep that historically attached to angels, departed saints, Mary, and the sacramental clergy is rejected by heaven itself.** **The Reformation's _soli Deo gloria_ has its scriptural ground here.**

## Rev 22:10-11 — Seal Not the Sayings; Each Is Fixed at His Coming

> "And he saith unto me, Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand. He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still." (Rev 22:10-11)

- "**Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand**" — Greek _mē sphragisēs tous logous tēs prophēteias tou bibliou toutou; ho kairos gar engys estin_. **Direct contrast with Daniel's instruction.** **Compare Dan 12:4** ("But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end") and **Dan 12:9** ("the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end"). **Daniel's prophecy was sealed; Revelation's prophecy is not sealed.** **The Daniel-Revelation pairing is built into both books' framing** — what Daniel could not yet say, Revelation now publishes openly. **The sealed-Daniel/unsealed-Revelation polarity is the structural argument that the books belong together** and that the time of Revelation's unfolding is the "time of the end" Daniel anticipated.
- "**The time is at hand**" — Greek _ho kairos engys estin_. **The eschatological clock is running.** Same phrase as Rev 1:3. **Bracketing the book** — opens and closes with the at-hand-ness of the prophetic events.
- "**He that is unjust, let him be unjust still... he that is holy, let him be holy still**" — Greek _ho adikōn adikēsatō eti... ho hagios hagiasthētō eti_. **The four-fold pronouncement at the close of probation.** **Once probation closes — at the conclusion of the heavenly Day-of-Atonement (Dan 8:14, 1844, the pre-Advent investigative judgment) — character is fixed.** **Compare Eze 3:27** ("He that heareth, let him hear; and he that forbeareth, let him forbear") and **Dan 12:10** ("the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand"). **The line drawn in Rev 15:8 (no man able to enter the temple till the seven plagues are fulfilled) and Rev 22:11 (each one settled in his state) is the same line — the close-of-probation line.**
- **The historicist reading aligns Rev 22:11 with the close of probation that follows the heavenly Day of Atonement.** **The investigative judgment that began in 1844 examines the cases of all who have professed Christ; when it concludes, character is permanent.** **There is no probation past the close of probation** — Rev 22:11's "let him be unjust still" is judicial, not casual.

[→] **Daniel sealed; Revelation unsealed; the time of the end is the unsealing window.** **Dan 12:4** — "shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end." **Dan 12:9** — "go thy way, Daniel: for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end." **Rev 22:10** — "Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand." **The two prophetic books are bookends with a deliberate symmetry: what was closed in Daniel's day is opened in John's day, and Daniel's "time of the end" is the window in which Revelation is read with understanding.** **The Adventist-historicist movement that arose in the 1840s read the Daniel-Revelation pairing this way** — the unsealing of Daniel (esp. the 2300-day prophecy of Dan 8:14) belongs to the "time of the end" that began in 1798 with the deadly wound of the papal beast (Rev 13:3) and that opens into the heavenly Day of Atonement of 1844. **Rev 22:10 confirms the unsealing posture — the prophecy is to be published, studied, understood, and obeyed, not hidden.**

## Rev 22:12-13 — My Reward Is With Me; Alpha and Omega

> "And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last." (Rev 22:12-13)

- "**Behold, I come quickly**" — **the second of three Christic announcements** (cf. v. 7, v. 20). **Each has its own emphasis** — v. 7 attaches the keep-the-prophecy beatitude; v. 12 attaches the reward-by-works principle; v. 20 attaches the bride's responsive cry.
- "**My reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be**" — Greek _ho misthos mou met' emou, apodounai hekastō hōs to ergon estin autou_. **Compare Isa 40:10** ("his reward is with him, and his work before him") and **Isa 62:11** ("his reward is with him, and his work before him"). **The OT promise of the LORD's coming with His reward is now Christ's own self-claim.** **Compare Mt 16:27** ("the Son of man shall come in the glory of his Father with his angels; and then he shall reward every man according to his works"); **Rom 2:6** ("Who will render to every man according to his deeds"); **2 Cor 5:10** ("we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, according to that he hath done"). **Judgment-by-works is consistent NT teaching** — works do not earn salvation, but works reveal whose servant a person is, and the Judge rewards accordingly.
- "**I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last**" — Greek _egō to Alpha kai to Ō, hē archē kai to telos, ho prōtos kai ho eschatos_. **A triple self-designation.** **(1)** Alpha-Omega — the Greek alphabet's bookends (covers everything in between). **(2)** First-Last — temporal eternity (covers all history). **(3)** Beginning-End — origin and goal (covers all causation). **Compare Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12** (Yahweh's "I am the first, and I am the last" — the divine self-designation). **Rev 1:8, 1:11, 21:6, 22:13** — four Alpha-Omega declarations in the KJV/TR; **Rev 1:17 and 2:8** add two First-Last declarations. **Rev 22:13 is the climactic last instance — Christ as Yahweh's full equal speaks the divine name as His own.**

## Rev 22:14-15 — Blessed Are They That Do His Commandments

> "Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city. For without are dogs, and sorcerers, and whoremongers, and murderers, and idolaters, and whosoever loveth and maketh a lie." (Rev 22:14-15)

- "**Blessed are they that do his commandments**" — Greek _Makarioi hoi poiountes tas entolas autou_. **TR/KJV reading; some critical editions read "those who wash their robes" (_hoi plynontes tas stolas autōn_).** **The KJV "do his commandments" is consistent with Rev 12:17 ("the remnant... which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ") and Rev 14:12 ("here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus") — the same identifying mark of the saints.** **The seventh and final Revelation beatitude.**
- "**That they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city**" — Greek _hina estai hē exousia autōn epi to xylon tēs zōēs, kai tois pylōsin eiselthōsin eis tēn polin_. **_Exousia_** = right, authority, license. **The cherub-guarded barrier of Gen 3:24 is reversed.** **The redeemed have legal-judicial access to the tree** — not by works as merit, but as the grace-given inheritance (Rev 21:7) to those whose character has been conformed to the Lamb's.
- "**For without are dogs**" — Greek _exō hoi kynes_. **"Dogs"** in OT idiom is shorthand for the morally unclean and the male temple prostitutes (Deut 23:18, "thou shalt not bring the price of a dog into the house of the LORD thy God"); also Phil 3:2 ("beware of dogs, beware of evil workers"). **The shameless and ritually-unclean are excluded.**
- "**Sorcerers**" — Greek _hoi pharmakoi_ (cf. Rev 9:21, 18:23, 21:8). **Same drug/occult lemma as before.** **The Babylon-of-Rev-18 deception that cast its sorcery over all nations cannot enter the city.**
- "**Whoremongers, murderers, idolaters, whosoever loveth and maketh a lie**" — Greek _hoi pornoi, hoi phoneis, hoi eidōlolatrai, kai pas philōn kai poiōn pseudos_. **Six categories** here, paralleling and condensing the eight of Rev 21:8. **"Loveth and maketh a lie"** — the deceiver's identity. Satan's character is called "a liar, and the father of it" (Jn 8:44); Babylon "deceived the nations" (Rev 18:23); the false prophet performed lying signs (Rev 13:13-14, 19:20). **Lie-loving and lie-making — both inner disposition and outward practice — are excluded.**

## Rev 22:16 — I Jesus; Root and Offspring of David; Bright Morning Star

> "I Jesus have sent mine angel to testify unto you these things in the churches. I am the root and the offspring of David, and the bright and morning star." (Rev 22:16)

- "**I Jesus**" — Greek _Egō Iēsous_. **The first time in the entire book that Christ uses His own personal name.** Throughout Revelation He has been "the Son of man," "the Lamb," "Faithful and True," "the Word of God," "Alpha and Omega" — but in v. 16 He says **"I, Jesus"** by His Bethlehem-Calvary name. **The emphasis is personal and direct.**
- "**Have sent mine angel to testify unto you these things in the churches**" — **the chain-of-revelation closes back to Rev 1:1's opening** ("The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified it by his angel unto his servant John"). **Same chain — God to Christ to angel to John to the churches.** **"In the churches" places the seven-church audience of Rev 1-3 squarely back in view as the receiving body of the whole prophecy.**
- "**The root and the offspring of David**" — Greek _hē rhiza kai to genos Dauid_. **Compare Isa 11:1, 10** ("a Branch... out of his roots"; "in that day there shall be a root of Jesse... to it shall the Gentiles seek"); **Jer 23:5, 33:15** (the righteous Branch); **Rom 1:3** ("made of the seed of David"); **Rev 5:5** ("the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David"). **Jesus is both David's source and David's descendant — pre-existent root, incarnate offspring.** **The Davidic-Messianic credentials are claimed in full.**
- "**The bright and morning star**" — Greek _ho astēr ho lampros ho prōinos_. **Compare Num 24:17** ("there shall come a Star out of Jacob"); **2 Pet 1:19** ("until the day dawn, and the day star arise in your hearts"); **Rev 2:28** ("I will give him the morning star" — the same gift to the overcomer). **The morning star is the herald of the new day** — Christ as the dawning of the everlasting day.

## Rev 22:17 — The Spirit and the Bride Say, Come

> "And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely." (Rev 22:17)

- "**The Spirit and the bride say, Come**" — Greek _kai to pneuma kai hē nymphē legousin Erchou_. **The Holy Spirit and the church/bride together issue the call.** **The bride is the same bride of Rev 19:7-8 and Rev 21:2, 9** — the redeemed people. **The Spirit's witness to Christ (Jn 15:26, Jn 16:13-14) and the church's gospel-mission converge in one call.**
- "**Let him that heareth say, Come**" — **the gospel call multiplies.** Each hearer becomes a caller. **The mission of the church is extended through every believer who hears.** **Compare Mt 28:18-20** (the Great Commission); **Acts 1:8** ("ye shall be witnesses unto me... unto the uttermost part of the earth"). **Until the Bridegroom comes, the bride's voice keeps calling.**
- "**Let him that is athirst come**" — Greek _ho dipsōn erchesthō_. **Compare Isa 55:1** ("Ho, every one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters"); **Jn 7:37** ("If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink"); **Rev 21:6** ("I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life freely"). **The thirst-and-water motif — running from Isaiah through John to Revelation — issues its final invitation here.**
- "**Whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely**" — Greek _ho thelōn labetō hydōr zōēs dōrean_. **_Dōrean_** = freely, as a gift, without cost (the same word as Rom 3:24, "justified freely by his grace"). **The gospel offer is universal in its reach** ("whosoever will") **and free in its terms** (no purchase price). **The water of life is given, never sold.** **This is the final gospel invitation in the Bible** — the canon's last open call.
- **The "Come" of v. 17 and the "I come" of v. 20 are reciprocal.** **The bride says "Come" to seekers; the bride says "Come, Lord Jesus" to the Bridegroom.** **Mission and longing are one breath** — the church calls the world to Christ until Christ returns for the church.

[→] **Rev 22:17 is the canon's final gospel invitation, and it is open to whosoever will.** **The verse contains three explicit "come" calls and one final "take" invitation:** (1) the Spirit and the bride say "Come" to the seeker; (2) the hearer is to say "Come" — passing the call along; (3) the thirsty one is invited to "come"; (4) whosoever will is to "take the water of life freely." **The progression is: Spirit-and-bride-call → hearer-becomes-caller → thirst-recognized → free reception.** **The gospel structure of Revelation's whole closing — the Lamb's wedding, the city descending, the river flowing, the tree healing, the gates open day and night — is summarized in this one verse's invitation.** **No one is excluded by predestinarian fiat; whosoever will, may come.** **This invitation runs right up to the moment of Christ's return** — the bride's mission does not pause until the Bridegroom appears. **Every believer reading Rev 22:17 is conscripted as a "Come"-sayer.**

## Rev 22:18-19 — Add Not, Take Not

> "For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues that are written in this book: And if any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life, and out of the holy city, and from the things which are written in this book." (Rev 22:18-19)

- "**I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book**" — Greek _martyrō egō panti tō akouonti tous logous tēs prophēteias tou bibliou toutou_. **The testifier here is Christ Himself** (cf. v. 16, 20 — He has been speaking continuously). **The warning is from the Author of the prophecy.**
- "**If any man shall add... God shall add unto him the plagues**" — the seven last plagues of Rev 15-16. **Tampering with Revelation's prophecy invites participation in its judgments.** **The principle is severe.**
- "**If any man shall take away... God shall take away his part out of the book of life, and out of the holy city**" — the inverse threat. **Subtraction from the prophecy means subtraction from the book of life.** **Excised from the eternal register; excised from the eternal city.**
- "**Compare Deut 4:2** ("Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish ought from it") and **Deut 12:32** ("What thing soever I command you, observe to do it: thou shalt not add thereto, nor diminish from it"). **The Mosaic canon-protection formula bookends the biblical canon.** **The whole Bible is bracketed by the same prohibition** — Moses at the front, John at the back.
- **The warning applies in particular to the prophecy of Revelation, but the principle is broader.** **The historicist/Protestant reading takes vv. 18-19 alongside Deut 4:2 as the sufficiency-and-finality-of-Scripture principle** — no church council, no later "ongoing magisterium," no Mary-co-redemption development, no continuing revelation that overrides the apostolic deposit, no allegorical dissolving of literal prophetic content. **The book is closed; the canon is fixed; the gospel is sufficient.**

## Rev 22:20 — Surely I Come Quickly; Even So, Come, Lord Jesus

> "He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus." (Rev 22:20)

- "**He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly**" — Greek _Legei ho martyrōn tauta, Nai, erchomai tachy_. **The third and final "I come quickly" of the chapter** (vv. 7, 12, 20). **_Nai_** = "yes," "surely," "indeed" — a strong affirmation. **Christ's last recorded words in the Bible** before the closing benediction — and they are a promise of His own return.
- "**Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus**" — Greek _Amēn. Erchou kyrie Iēsou_. **The Hebrew _Amēn_ ("so be it / truly") is the bride's seal on the promise.** **_Erchou_** = imperative, "come." **Compare _Maranatha_ in 1 Cor 16:22** ("our Lord, come") — the early-church liturgical cry. **The Bible's last petition is the bride's cry for the Bridegroom.** **The whole prophecy converges on this longing.**
- **The structure of v. 20 is reciprocal call-and-response.** Christ says "I come"; the bride says "Come." **The three explicit "comes" of v. 17** (the gospel call to the seeker) **and the two "comes" of v. 20** (Christ's promise and the bride's response) form a five-fold "come"-pattern at the canon's close.
- **For the historicist reader, "Even so, come, Lord Jesus" is the appropriate response to every prophetic chart, every 1844 explanation, every sanctuary lesson, every great-controversy outline.** **The prophecies do not exist to satisfy curiosity but to prepare a people who long for Christ's return.** **The bride's cry is the right closing prayer of every study.**

## Rev 22:21 — The Grace of Our Lord Jesus Christ Be With You All

> "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen." (Rev 22:21)

- **The Bible's last verse is a benediction of grace.** **Greek _Hē charis tou kyriou Iēsou Christou meta pantōn hymōn. Amēn._** **Pauline-style benediction** (cf. 2 Cor 13:14, 1 Thess 5:28, 2 Thess 3:18, Phil 4:23) — **the formula is the apostolic farewell.**
- "**The grace**" — Greek _hē charis_. **The whole Bible's storyline distills to grace** — the unmerited favor of God that gave the Son, that provides the cleansing, that builds the city, that restores the tree. **Rev 22:17's "freely" (_dōrean_) and Rev 22:21's "grace" (_charis_) are the same gospel.**
- "**With you all**" — Greek _meta pantōn hymōn_. **Universal in scope** — every reader, every hearer, every believer. **The book that opened with "Grace be unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come" (Rev 1:4) closes with "the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all."** **Grace bookends the Apocalypse.**
- "**Amen**" — **the last word of the Bible.** **Hebrew root _aman_ — to be firm, faithful, true.** **The whole canon ends with the Hebrew/Aramaic word for "it is so, it is firm, it is true."** **Sixty-six books, forty authors, sixteen centuries of writing, one Author, one storyline, one closing word: Amen.**

## Key Takeaways

- **Rev 22 closes the Bible by restoring Eden in the eternal-state city.** The river of Gen 2:10, the tree of life of Gen 2:9, the face-to-face dwelling of Gen 3:8 — all return, this time forever, with the curse reversed (v. 3) and the cherub-guarded barrier removed (v. 14).
- **The image-of-God arc terminates at Rev 22:4.** "They shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads." The Mt 5:8 beatific vision and the 2 Cor 3:18 image-restoration reach permanent realization.
- **The seal-of-God in the forehead is the eternal mark of the redeemed.** Rev 7:3 → 14:1 → 22:4 — the same forehead-stamp, now visible forever, in deliberate counter to the mark-in-the-forehead of the beast (Rev 13:16, 14:9).
- **Three "I come quickly" sayings (vv. 7, 12, 20) close the book with Christ's own promise.** Each carries a different attached emphasis — keep-the-prophecy, reward-by-works, bride's responsive cry.
- **Revelation contains seven beatitudes** (1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14) — a sevenfold blessing-structure tracking the Christian's whole journey from reading the prophecy (1) to entering the city through the gates (7).
- **Daniel sealed; Revelation unsealed.** Dan 12:4, 9 vs. Rev 22:10. The two prophetic books are a sealed-then-opened pair, and the "time of the end" is the unsealing window.
- **Rev 22:11 is the close-of-probation declaration.** Once the heavenly Day-of-Atonement investigative judgment finishes, character is fixed: "he that is unjust, let him be unjust still... he that is holy, let him be holy still."
- **The Spirit-and-bride invitation (v. 17) is the canon's final gospel call, open to "whosoever will" with the water of life given "freely" (_dōrean_, gift-language).** The bride's mission runs until the Bridegroom returns.
- **The canon-protection warning (vv. 18-19) bookends the Bible with Deut 4:2's "ye shall not add unto the word."** No add, no subtract, no later magisterial development that overrides the apostolic deposit, no allegorical dissolving of literal prophecy.
- **The Bible's last petition is "Even so, come, Lord Jesus" (v. 20).** The bride's longing for the Bridegroom is the right closing prayer of every prophetic study.
- **The Bible's last verse is a benediction of grace (v. 21).** Grace bookends Revelation (Rev 1:4 → 22:21) and bookends the whole NT. **Sixty-six books, one closing word: Amen.**
