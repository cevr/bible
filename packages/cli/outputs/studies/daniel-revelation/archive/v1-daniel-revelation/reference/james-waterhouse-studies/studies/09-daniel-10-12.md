## Key Texts

### Daniel 11:31 — The Anti-Sanctuary of Strength

- "Arms of men shall arise from him and they shall pollute **the sanctuary of strength** and shall take away the daily and they shall place the abomination that makes desolate."
- This parallels Daniel 8:11-12 — the little horn's attack on the sanctuary and the daily.

### Daniel 11:36-38 — The Two-Headed King of the North

- **vs. 36** — "The king shall do according to his will; and he shall exalt himself, and magnify himself above every god, and shall speak marvelous things against the God of gods."
- **vs. 37** — "Neither shall he regard the God of his fathers, nor the desire of women, nor regard any god: for he shall magnify himself above all."
- **vs. 38** — "But in his estate shall he honour the god of forces, and a god whom his fathers knew not shall he honour with gold and silver."

### North vs. South = God vs. Man

The four directions and four creatures:

```
        North
        eagle

(sacrifice)       (king)

        South
        man

```

- **North vs. South** = God vs. man = true religion vs. paganism/atheism.
- Paganism and atheism are opposite sides of the same coin: paganism = worship of dead men; atheism = worship of living men and human achievement.
- Climate activism as a form of nature worship = modern expression.

### "Speak marvelous things against the God of gods" (vs. 36)

- = **Daniel 7:25** — "He shall speak great words against the Most High... and think to change times and laws."
- A reference to the Sabbath (one of two institutions from Creation) and the 10 Commandments.

### "The desire of women" (vs. 37)

- The desire God gave to men: to be the foundation for marriage and family.
- "Not regard the desire of women" = disregard for marriage.
- Marriage is the first of two institutions from Creation (marriage and Sabbath).
- The two-headed king of the north attacks **both** Creation institutions: Sabbath (vs. 36, "times and laws") and marriage (vs. 37).

### "Honour the god of forces" (vs. 38)

- **vss. 36, 38 contrast:** "speak marvelous things against the God of gods" vs. "honour the god of forces."
- The Sabbath institution of the Creator vs. the Sun-day of man, 666, implemented through force by a false human trinity (three-fold union of human powers).
- The marriage institution of the Creator (the image of God) vs. the image of man or idols.

#### Key Texts

- **vs. 1** — "At that time Michael the great prince... shall stand up, and there shall be a time of trouble."
- **vs. 2** — Resurrection: "many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake."
- **vs. 3** — "They that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament."
- **vs. 4** — "Shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end."
- **vss. 6-7** — "How long?" → "a time, times, and an half" (3.5 prophetic years = 1260 days/years).
- **vss. 11-12** — 1290 days; 1335 days. "Blessed is he that waiteth."
- **vs. 13** — "Go thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days."

## Novel Findings

1. **The two-headed king of the north** — anti-north (against God/Creator) AND pro-south (exalting man). Atheistic AND humanistic at the same time.
2. **Both Creation institutions attacked** — Sabbath (vs. 36) and marriage (vs. 37). The two institutions from Eden.
3. **The North/South compass** maps to the God/man axis of the four living creatures, giving theological meaning to the king of the north/south conflict.
4. **The Roman section** (vss. 16-22) contains Christ's birth (vs. 20, Augustus) and crucifixion (vs. 22b) as structural markers within the prophecy.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Sabbath:** One of two Creation institutions attacked by the end-time power.
- **Marriage:** The other Creation institution under attack — connected to the image of God.
- **Church and State:** "The god of forces" — worship enforced by state power.
- **Second Coming:** Daniel 12:1-3 — Michael stands up, time of trouble, resurrection.
- **1260/1290/1335 days:** Prophetic time periods marking phases of the end-time conflict.

## Teaching Flow

1. Show the macro chiasm: Man in Linen frames the entire section.
2. Walk through the historical sequence: Persia → Greece → Rome → anti-typical phases.
3. Focus on vss. 31, 36-38: the sanctuary attacked, the two-headed king.
4. North vs. South: draw the compass diagram with the four creatures.
5. The two Creation institutions: Sabbath and marriage — both under attack.
6. Daniel 12: the end — time of trouble, resurrection, the wise shall shine.
7. Close with vs. 13: "thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days."
