## Key Texts

### The Man in Linen = The High Priest

- **Daniel 10:5-6** — "a certain man clothed in linen, whose loins were girded with fine gold of Uphaz: his body also was like the beryl, and his face as the appearance of lightning, and his eyes as lamps of fire, and his arms and his feet like in colour to polished brass, and the voice of his words like the voice of a multitude."
- **Daniel 12:6-7** — "the man clothed in linen, which was upon the waters of the river" — raises both hands, swears by Him who lives forever.
- **Leviticus 16:4** — "He shall put on the holy linen coat... these are holy garments" — the High Priest's garments for the Day of Atonement.

### The Man Between Two Others = God Between Cherubim

- **Daniel 12:5-7** — Two others stand on opposite banks of the river; the Man in Linen is between them above the water.
- **Leviticus 16:2,13-14** — God appears between the two cherubim on the mercy seat. The High Priest enters the Most Holy Place where God dwells between the cherubim.
- **Exodus 25:22** — "I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat, from between the two cherubims."

The parallels are too many to be coincidental, yet the differences are deliberate. **Daniel 10's Man in Linen is the High Priest entering the Day of Atonement; Revelation 1's Christ is the High Priest during it.**

### Daniel 8 Structure: He-Goat-Killed Ram and Mount Zion

- **Daniel 8:7** — The he-goat "cast him [the ram] down to the ground, and stamped upon him."
- **Daniel 11:45** — "He shall plant the tabernacles of his palace between the seas in the glorious holy mountain" (Mount Zion).
- These are paired in the chiastic structure of Daniel 8-11:
  - A (vss. 8:3-7) — He-goat-killed ram / the Great Controversy
  - ... (center)
  - A' (vs. 11:44-45) — Mount Zion

### The Crucified Christ in the Judgment

- Christ as the Lamb slain appears in the Judgment. The crucified Christ stands in our place if we are His servants.
- **Revelation 5:6** — "a Lamb as it had been slain" — standing in the midst of the throne and the four beasts and the elders.
- **Revelation 14:1** — "a Lamb stood on the mount Sion" with the 144,000.
- This is why the Judgment is not a source of fear for the Christian — the crucified Christ appears in our place.

## The "Transgression of Desolation" Links Daniel 8 and 9

- **Daniel 8:13** — "How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation?"
- **Daniel 9:24** — "to finish the transgression"
- **Daniel 9:26b-27b** — "desolations... desolate... desolate"
- The "transgression of desolation" of Daniel 8:13 spans both the 70 weeks (Daniel 9's "transgression" and "desolation") AND the 2300 days.
- Therefore: 70 weeks is a subset of the 2300 days. Both begin from the same decree.

## Novel Findings

1. **The entire second half of Daniel is Day of Atonement** — not just chapter 8's "sanctuary cleansed," but the animals (ram/goat = Leviticus 16 sacrifices), the Man in Linen (= High Priest's Day of Atonement garments), and the Man between two figures (= God between cherubim).
2. **Daniel 10 vs. Revelation 1** — same vision of the High Priest, but Daniel 10 shows Him entering; Revelation 1 shows Him ministering.
3. **The crucified Christ appears in the Judgment** — the ram "stamped upon" in Daniel 8 connects to the Lamb "as it had been slain" in Revelation 5.
4. **The "transgression of desolation"** is the structural link between Daniel 8's 2300 days and Daniel 9's 70 weeks.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Investigative Judgment:** The entire framework of Daniel 8-12 IS the investigative judgment / Day of Atonement.
- **Sanctuary:** The heavenly sanctuary, where Christ serves as High Priest, is the setting.
- **2300 Days:** Begins with the same decree as the 70 weeks; the sanctuary cleansing = the beginning of the investigative judgment in 1844.
- **Christ's High Priesthood:** The Man in Linen is our High Priest in the Day of Atonement.
- **Second Coming:** The Day of Atonement concludes with the scapegoat sent into the wilderness (Satan bound) and the people freed.

## Teaching Flow

1. Show the macro structure of Daniel 8-12: two prophecies (chs. 8 and 11) framed by two visions of the Man in Linen (chs. 10 and 12).
2. What is the Man in Linen wearing? Linen = the Day of Atonement garments (Lev 16:4).
3. What are the animals? Ram and goat = the Day of Atonement sacrifices (Lev 16:5).
4. What is the Man between two others? God between the cherubim on the mercy seat (Ex 25:22).
5. Compare Daniel 10 and Revelation 1 side by side. Same vision — different phase.
6. The "transgression of desolation" links Daniel 8 and 9 — the 70 weeks is cut from the 2300 days.
7. The crucified Christ in the judgment: the ram stamped upon = the Lamb slain = our Advocate.
8. Close: we are living in the Day of Atonement. Christ is our High Priest. The judgment is not to be feared.
