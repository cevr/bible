## Theme

"A Tale of Two Cities: Jerusalem and Babylon; AND the Four"

## Chiastic Structure

```
A     vss. 1-2      Babylon takes Jerusalem
B     vss. 3-4      The king's selection criteria
C     vs. 5         The king's delicacies and wine
D     vs. 6         Daniel, Hananiah, Mishael, Azariah (Hebrew names)
D'    vs. 7         Babylonian names given
C'    vss. 8-10     Daniel refuses the king's delicacies and wine
B'    vss. 11-20    The test and its result
  1   vss. 11-16      The 10-day test
  2   vss. 18-20      The king's examination
A'    vs. 21         Daniel continues until Cyrus (Babylon falls)
```

## Key Texts

### The Two Cities (vss. 1-2, 21)

- **Daniel 1:1-2** — Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon comes to Jerusalem, besieges it. The Lord gives Jehoiakim into his hand. Vessels of God's house carried to Shinar.
- **Daniel 1:21** — "Daniel continued until the first year of Cyrus the king."
- **Revelation 16:12** — The sixth angel dries up the Euphrates (site of Babylon) so "the way of the kings of the east" (anti-typical Cyrus/Christ) might be prepared.

### The Principle

All of prophecy is about these two cities. Judah disobeyed God → sent into typical Babylon. The church disobeys God → sent into anti-typical Babylon. As typical Cyrus destroyed typical Babylon and returned Judah to Jerusalem, anti-typical Cyrus (Jesus Christ) will destroy anti-typical Babylon and return His people to the New Jerusalem.

### The Four (vss. 6, 7, 11, 18-19)

- **Daniel, Hananiah, Mishael, Azariah** — listed in Hebrew alphabetical order.
- Four is deeply symbolic: the four camps of Israel (Numbers 2:1-31), the four directions, the four living creatures (lion, bull, man, eagle).

### The Number 10 and the 10 Commandments (vss. 12, 14-15, 20)

- "Ten" occurs **10 times** in Daniel 1. Count them.
- Daniel's name = compound of "dan" (judge) + "El" (God) = "God is my judge."
- How are we judged? By God's law, the 10 Commandments.
- "Ten hands" (v. 20) — Daniel and friends found to be "ten hands" above all others.
- **Deuteronomy 6:6-8** — The commandments shall be "a sign upon thine hand" and "frontlets between thine eyes."
- **Revelation 13:16** — The mark of the beast received "in their right hand, or in their foreheads."
- Conclusion: "10 hands" = a mark of God's 10 Commandments on our hands (actions).

### The Test: Diet and Defilement (vss. 8, 12, 16)

- **Daniel 1:8** — "Daniel purposed in his heart that he would not defile himself with the portion of the king's delicacies nor with the wine which he drank."
- Vegetables (plant-based diet) = as close to Eden's diet as possible.
- Diet is implicitly part of the 10 Commandments: love God with all heart/mind (summary of first four); do not kill, including slow self-destruction (sixth commandment).
- **Revelation 17:2** — "the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication."
- **Revelation 18:3** — "all nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication... the merchants of the earth are waxed rich through the abundance of her delicacies."
- The "king's delicacies" = the delicacies of Babylon = anything that defiles and turns minds from Christ (worldly diet, entertainment, etc.).

### The Search for Wise Men (vss. 17-20)

- **Daniel 12:3-4** — "They that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament... many shall run to and fro... and knowledge shall be increased."
- The structure of ch. 1 tells the story: the world is in search of "wise men." They will be found in God's church that has been taken captive into Babylon — unwilling to be defiled.
- God's people must be willing to be put to the test by God's 10 Commandments. If faithful, God gives wisdom to stand up for Him. It will be visible in our hand, "ten hands" (v. 20) — our actions.

## Novel Findings

1. **The 10 count** — "ten" appearing exactly 10 times in the chapter, linking to the 10 Commandments as the standard of judgment.
2. **The two cities framework** — all of prophecy reduced to Jerusalem vs. Babylon, with Cyrus as a type of Christ the deliverer.
3. **The four = the 144,000** — Daniel and his three friends represent the final movement of God's people: the last great preaching and teaching movement.
4. **Hebrew alphabetical order** of the names — deliberate, symbolic arrangement.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Sabbath:** The test of Daniel 1 is a test by God's law (the 10 Commandments), of which the Sabbath is the seal.
- **Health reform:** Diet as an implicit component of faithfulness to the 10 Commandments.
- **Babylon:** The church is currently in captivity; God's people must refuse to be defiled by Babylon's "delicacies."
- **144,000:** Daniel and friends as a type of the 144,000 — the last-day faithful who refuse compromise.
- **Second Coming:** Cyrus as a type of Christ's return to end Babylon.

## Teaching Flow

1. Open with vss. 1-2: the fall of Jerusalem to Babylon — set up the two-city framework.
2. Introduce the four: Daniel and friends, Hebrew names, alphabetical order → significance of four.
3. The test: king's delicacies vs. vegetables — the principle of not defiling oneself with Babylon.
4. The number 10: count occurrences → 10 Commandments → the standard of judgment.
5. The result: "ten hands" above all → faithfulness to God's law produces visible fruit.
6. Close with v. 21: Daniel continues until Cyrus → Cyrus destroys Babylon → Christ (anti-typical Cyrus) will destroy anti-typical Babylon and take us home.
