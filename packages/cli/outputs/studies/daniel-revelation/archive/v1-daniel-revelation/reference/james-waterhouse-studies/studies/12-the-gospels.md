## Matthew: The Lion (King)

### Matthew 1-5: The Exodus from Egypt, the Sanctuary, and the Judgment

**Why is Rachel mentioned by Matthew?**

- **Matthew 2:17-18** — "Then was fulfilled that which was spoken by Jeremy the prophet, saying, In Rama was there a voice heard, lamentation, and weeping, and great mourning, **Rachel weeping for her children**, and would not be comforted, because they are not."
- Rachel → Joseph (her son, the dreamer) → Egypt. The Rachel-Joseph-Egypt chain is Matthew's key typological thread.

**Why is Joseph the Dreamer in the Gospel of Matthew?**

- **Matthew 1:20** — "the angel of the Lord appeared unto him in **a dream**, saying, **Joseph**..."
- **Matthew 2:13** — "the angel of the Lord appeareth to **Joseph** in **a dream**: Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and flee into **Egypt**."
- **Matthew 2:15** — "Out of **Egypt** have I called my son" (quoting **Hosea 11:1**).
- Matthew connects Joseph, husband of Mary, to the patriarch Joseph (Rachel's son), a dreamer who went into Egypt.
- **Genesis 30:22-24** — "And Rachel... bare a son; and called his name **Joseph**."
- **Genesis 37:5-9** — "Joseph dreamed a dream... he dreamed yet another dream."
- **Genesis 37:28** — "they... sold Joseph to the Ishmeelites... and **they brought Joseph into Egypt**."
- **Genesis 45:13; 46:6-7** — Jacob's family comes into Egypt because of Joseph.
- Like the patriarch Joseph, Jesus' family goes into Egypt and later comes out.

**The Exodus-Sanctuary-Jesus Parallel:**
| The Exodus | The Sanctuary | Jesus |
|---|---|---|
| In Egypt: the Passover | Altar of Sacrifice | In Egypt |
| "Baptism" in the Red Sea | Bronze Laver | Baptism in the Jordan |
| In the Wilderness: the Manna Test | The Holy Place: Table of Shew Bread | In the Wilderness: the Stone to Bread Test |
| Mount Sinai* | The Most Holy Place: The Ark* | Mount of the Sermon\* |

- **John 5:16-18** — "The Jews persecute Jesus, and **sought to slay him, because he had done these things on the sabbath day**."

### Bread from Heaven = The Sabbath Test

- **John 6:31-33** — "He gave them bread from heaven... my Father giveth you the true bread from heaven."
- **Exodus 16:4-5,19-29** — "I will rain bread from heaven... **that I may prove them, whether they will walk in my law, or no**."
- The manna tested Israel's willingness to keep the Sabbath (gather six days, rest the seventh). Jesus is the true manna — the true Sabbath test.

## Novel Findings

1. **The Sermon on the Mount = Sermon from the Judgment** — it parallels the Most Holy Place and the Ark of the Covenant, which the High Priest accesses only on the Day of Atonement.
2. **Matthew's Joseph-Exodus-Sanctuary typology** — Matthew systematically connects Jesus to Joseph the dreamer, then to the Exodus, then to the sanctuary, progressing toward the Most Holy Place.
3. **John's Gospel is structurally centered on the Sabbath conflict** — the chiastic structure of John 2-7 reveals the Sabbath as the central issue leading to Christ's death.
4. **Manna = Sabbath test** — Jesus as the "bread from heaven" is the Sabbath test personified.
5. **Luke 17's chiastic structure** maps to historical end-time events in sequence.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Sabbath:** The Sabbath is the central conflict in John's Gospel and the trigger for Christ's death.
- **Investigative Judgment:** The Sermon on the Mount points to the Day of Atonement. The cleansing of the temple (John 2) = the Judgment.
- **Health Reform / Manna:** The manna test is a Sabbath test — obedience in diet and rest.
- **Second Coming:** Luke 17's chiastic structure outlines end-time events.

## Teaching Flow

1. Show the four Gospels as four creatures: Lion, Calf, Man, Eagle. Evidence from each Gospel.
2. Matthew: the Joseph connection, the Exodus parallel, the Sanctuary progression → Sermon on the Mount = Sermon from the Judgment.
3. John: the Sabbath conflict. Walk through John 2-7 chiasm. The Jews killed Jesus over the Sabbath.
4. Bread from Heaven = manna = the Sabbath test.
5. Luke 17: the coming of the Kingdom — structured prophecy of end-time events.
6. Close: every Gospel, from its own angle, points to the Sabbath, the Judgment, and Christ's kingship.
