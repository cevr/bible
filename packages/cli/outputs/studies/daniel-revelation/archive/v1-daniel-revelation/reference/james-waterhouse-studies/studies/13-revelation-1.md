## Theme

"The Centrality of the Second Coming" — Revelation 1 establishes the judgment, the Second Coming, and Christ as High Priest.

```

    2   vss. 4-6       The Judgment: the 3 (God, Spirit, Christ)
```

## Revelation 1:1-3 — John, the Revealed Christ, and the Shortness of Time

### Structure of vss. 1-2

```
1   vs. 1a     The Revelation of Jesus Christ
2   vs. 1b     which God gave to him
3   vs. 1c     to show to his servants
4   vs. 1d     things which must come to pass shortly.
3'  vs. 1e     And he signified it by sending it by his angel to his servant John
2'  vs. 2a     who testified of the word of God
1'  vs. 2b     and the testimony of Jesus Christ of all things that he saw.

A'  vs. 3      Blessed is the one that readeth and the ones that hear... for the time is at hand.
```

### Key points:

- **vs. 1** — "The Revelation of Jesus Christ" — prophecy's ultimate goal is salvational: to give us a clearer vision of Christ so we can be transformed into His likeness. This is the only book in the entire Bible that explicitly says there is a blessing for reading and keeping its contents. Why? Because it is the "Revelation of Jesus Christ."
- **vs. 1** — "which God gave to him... to show to his servants" — prophecy is written in symbols; must be deciphered through Scripture itself.
- **2 Peter 1:20-21** — "no prophecy of the scripture is of any private interpretation... holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost."
- **1 Corinthians 12:4-10,28-30** — Prophecy is one of the gifts of the Spirit given to the church.
- **Ephesians 4:7-11** — "And he gave some, apostles; and some, **prophets**; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers."
- **vs. 1 — John** symbolizes the 144,000. Just as Daniel is symbolic of God's last-day messengers, so is John.
- **Revelation 1:1-2** — John "bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ." To be a "John" requires both the Word of God AND the Spirit of Prophecy.
- **Revelation 19:10** — "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy."
- **Joel 2:28-29** — "I will pour out my spirit upon all flesh; and your sons and your daughters shall prophesy."
- **Luke 11:13** — "how much more shall your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to them that ask him?"
- Tradition says John was boiled in oil, in order to try to kill him. He ended up on Patmos because he could not be killed.
- **vss. 1,3 — "come to pass shortly" and "the time is at hand":** urgency. Time is short. The world is lost in deception — they need to see God, love in action, Christ in us. We need directions from Omniscience, thus the need of an unrestricted outpouring of the Holy Spirit.

## Revelation 1:7 — The Second Coming

- "Behold he cometh with the clouds and every eye shall see him and they also which pierced him and all the kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen!"
- **"They also which pierced him"** — a reference to **Psalm 22:16** — "For dogs have compassed me: the assembly of the wicked have inclosed me: **they pierced my hands and my feet**." The dogs are symbolic of the Romans.
- **The sequence in Revelation 1: Judgment (vss. 4-6) THEN Second Coming (vs. 7).** The investigative judgment precedes the Second Coming.

## Revelation 1:8-9 — John, Jesus Christ, and Persecution

### Structure

```
A   vs. 8a     I am the Alpha and the Omega, the beginning and the ending,
B   vs. 8b     (even) saith the Lord God,
C   vs. 8c     who is and who was and who is to come, the Almighty.
D   vs. 9a     I John, your brother and companion in the tribulation
C'  vs. 9b     and (in the) kingdom and patience in and of Jesus Christ
B'  vs. 9c     was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God
A'  vs. 9d     and (for) the testimony of Jesus Christ.
```

- The structure establishes Christ's divinity through three titles:
  - **"Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending"** = "the First and the Last" — the Old Testament/Hebrew title (Isaiah 41:4; 44:6; 48:12).
  - **"who is and who was and who is to come"** = Yahweh, the I AM — him whose nature is one who is ever existent, expressed in Greek as the one who fills past, present, and future (Jehovah, however you wish to write and pronounce it).
  - **"the Almighty"** = El Shaddai, God Almighty (Genesis 17:1; 28:3; 35:11; 43:14; 48:3; 49:25; Isaiah 43:10).
- **vs. 8 footnote:** "saith" (λέγει, _legei_) vs. "word" (λόγον, _logon_) — two different Greek words. Christ is the Word (_Logos_), but He also _speaks_ (_legei_). The structure pairs "saith the Lord God" (B) with "for the word of God" (B'), distinguishing the person who speaks from the message spoken.
- **vs. 9 — "patience in and of Jesus Christ":** patience that is **in** Jesus Christ and patience that is like the patience **of** Jesus Christ — a dual meaning.
- **vs. 9 — "companion in tribulation":** to be a John means conflict, torture, perhaps even attempted murder (actual death, if you only think you are a John, but are not one).

- **Acts 13:33** — "God hath fulfilled... in that he hath raised up Jesus again; as it is also written in the second psalm, Thou art my Son, **this day have I begotten thee**."
- **Hebrews 1:5-6** — "For unto which of the angels said he at any time, **Thou art my Son, this day have I begotten thee**? And again, I will be to him a Father, and he shall be to me a Son? And again, when he bringeth in the **firstbegotten** into the world, he saith, And let all the angels of God worship him."
- This is the only statement in Scripture, by God, about **WHEN** He begot Christ. It occurred at the resurrection. This should take precedence above all other opinions concerning Christ's begotten nature.

## Revelation 1:1,3 and 22:7 — The Blessing for Reading

- **Revelation 1:1,3** — "The Revelation of Jesus Christ... Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time is at hand."
- **Revelation 22:7** — "Blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book."
- This is the only book in the entire Bible that explicitly promises a blessing for reading and keeping its contents.
- **1 Corinthians 14:32** — "The spirits of the prophets are subject to the prophets" (i.e., the Scriptures or word of God). John is said to testify of both the word of God and the testimony of Jesus Christ because the words he prophesied were in agreement with God's word, the Scriptures.

## We Live in the Prophetic Sign Era

### Acts 2:16-20 — The Prophecy

- **Acts 2:16-20** — "But this is that which was spoken by the prophet Joel; And it shall come to pass in the last days, saith God, I will pour out of my Spirit upon all flesh: and your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, and your young men shall see visions, and your old men shall dream dreams: And on my servants and on my handmaidens I will pour out in those days of my Spirit; and they shall prophesy: And I will shew wonders in heaven above, and signs in the earth beneath; blood, and fire, and vapour of smoke: **The sun shall be turned into darkness, and the moon into blood**, before that great and notable day of the Lord come."

### Revelation 6:12-13 — The Fulfillment

- **Revelation 6:12-13** — "And I beheld when he had opened the sixth seal, and, lo, there was a great earthquake; and **the sun became black** as sackcloth of hair, and **the moon became as blood**; And the stars of heaven fell unto the earth, even as a fig tree casteth her untimely figs, when she is shaken of a mighty wind."

### The Three Signs Fulfilled

1. **"A great earthquake"** — the Great Lisbon Earthquake of November 1, 1755.
2. **"The sun became black"** — the Dark Day of New England, May 19, 1780.
3. **"The stars of heaven fell"** — the meteor shower of November 12-13, 1833.

## Novel Findings

1. **Revelation 1:4-6 IS the judgment scene of Daniel 7** — demonstrated through detailed structural parallel.
2. **Judgment THEN Second Coming** — the structural sequence in Revelation 1 (vss. 4-6 then vs. 7) proves the investigative judgment precedes the Second Coming.
3. **Daniel 10 → Revelation 1** — same High Priest vision, different phase. Daniel 10 = entering; Revelation 1 = ministering.
4. **Psalm 2:7 "begotten" = the resurrection** — Acts 13:33 is the definitive NT interpretation. The only biblical statement about WHEN God "begot" Christ.
5. **Psalm 2 is the backbone of Revelation** — quoted repeatedly throughout the book.
6. **Christ's divinity established by the structure** of vss. 8-9: Alpha/Omega + I AM + Almighty = the full divine title.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Investigative Judgment:** Revelation 1:4-6 = Daniel 7:9-14. The judgment is in session.
- **Second Coming:** Follows the judgment (vs. 7). "Every eye shall see him."
- **Christ's High Priesthood:** vss. 13-16 describe Christ in high-priestly garments, ministering among the seven candlesticks.
- **State of the Dead:** "The first begotten of the dead" = Christ rose from literal death. "Begotten" = the resurrection (Acts 13:33).
- **Spirit of Prophecy:** John as a prophet, testifying of the Word of God and the testimony of Jesus (the spirit of prophecy, Rev 19:10).
- **Sabbath:** "The Lord's day" (vs. 10) = the Sabbath.

## Teaching Flow

1. Revelation 1:1-3 — the purpose of prophecy: to reveal Christ. Time is short. John = the 144,000.
2. vss. 4-6 — the three persons of the Godhead. Show the parallel with Daniel 7:9-14 and Revelation 4-5. This is the judgment scene.
3. vs. 7 — the Second Coming. AFTER the judgment. The sequence matters.
4. vss. 8-9 — Christ's divinity; John's persecution. To be a "John" costs everything.
5. vss. 10-20 — Christ as High Priest. Compare with Daniel 10:5-6. Same vision, different phase.
6. Psalm 2: the backbone of Revelation. "This day have I begotten thee" = the resurrection (Acts 13:33).
7. The prophetic signs: 1755, 1780, 1833.
8. Close: the judgment is in session. Christ is our High Priest. The Second Coming is next.
