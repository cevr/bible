# Revelation 15 — The Seven Last Plagues Prepared

**Rev 14 ended with two harvests; the wheat reaped, the vintage cast into the great winepress of the wrath of God. Rev 15 opens the next scene with "another sign in heaven, great and marvellous" — seven angels with the seven last plagues, "for in them is filled up the wrath of God." A sea of glass mingled with fire stands before the throne, and on it stand the victors over the beast, his image, his mark, and the number of his name, with the harps of God in their hands, singing the song of Moses the servant of God and the song of the Lamb. The temple of the tabernacle of testimony in heaven is opened. Seven angels come out of the temple, clothed in pure and white linen, with golden girdles. One of the four living creatures hands them seven golden vials full of the wrath of God. The temple is filled with smoke from the glory of God and from His power; and no man is able to enter the temple till the seven plagues are fulfilled. Rev 15 is the briefest chapter in Revelation — eight verses — but the most loaded. The investigative judgment Rev 14:7 announced has finished its work; the plagues Rev 16 will pour out are now being prepared; and the silent moment between is the close of probation.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 15 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 14:14-20 (the harvest just completed)
- Revelation 16 (the plagues that follow)
- Exodus 7-12 (the plagues of Egypt — the pattern)
- Exodus 15:1-19 (the song of Moses at the Red Sea)
- Leviticus 16:17 (the Day of Atonement's empty tabernacle while the high priest mediates alone)
- Numbers 1:50-53 (the tabernacle of testimony — naming the law-of-God-at-the-center)
- 1 Kings 8:10-11 (Solomon's temple filled with the cloud of glory; priests cannot stand to minister)

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 15 Sits

Rev 14:14-20 closed with the two harvests. **Rev 15 immediately opens the next sign — not another harvest, but the loading of the seven last plagues.** The chapter is preparatory: the saints sing first, the temple opens, the angels are equipped, and then the door closes. **Rev 16 will then pour the seven plagues; Rev 17-18 will detail Babylon's destruction; Rev 19 will bring the white-horse Rider.** Rev 15 is the silent overture to the loudest movements of the book.

- **The chapter is about transition, not action.** The plagues do not fall in Rev 15; they are loaded. The temple is opened, the angels emerge, the vials are handed off, the door closes. **The scene shows the heavenly side of probation's close.**
- **Three "signs in heaven" frame the great-controversy section.** Rev 12:1 — "a great sign in heaven, a woman clothed with the sun" (the church). Rev 12:3 — "another sign in heaven, behold a great red dragon" (Satan). Rev 15:1 — "another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues" (judgment). **Three signs: the church, the dragon, the judgment.** The first two have been at war through Rev 12-14; the third is heaven's resolution.
- **Rev 15 is structured around the Day of Atonement closing scene.** Lev 16:17 is the verbal mirror: "There shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out." The smoke that fills the temple, the no-one-able-to-enter — this is the Levitical pattern projected onto the heavenly sanctuary at the end of Christ's atoning ministry.
- **The Exodus structure is also deliberate.** Israel's deliverance went plagues → Red Sea → song of Moses (Ex 7-15). Rev 15-16 inverts the order: song first (Rev 15:2-4), then plagues (Rev 16). **The reason: the deliverance has already happened by the seal, before the plagues come.** The sealed sing before the plagues fall; the plagues are not their judgment but their deliverance.

[KEY🔑] **Rev 15 is the chapter where probation closes.** The three angels of Rev 14 finish their work. The investigative judgment finishes its decisions. The High Priest hands off the censer; the seven angels take the seven vials; the temple fills with smoke; "no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled" (v. 8). **From this moment, every case is fixed.** "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still" (Rev 22:11). The remnant stands sealed; the unsealed stand marked; the door has closed; the plagues are about to fall. **Rev 15 is not the chapter of wrath — it is the chapter of finality.**

## Rev 15:1 — Another Sign in Heaven

> "And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God." (Rev 15:1)

- "**Another sign in heaven**" — Greek _allo sēmeion en tō ouranō_, **another sign**. **Three "signs in heaven" in Revelation:** Rev 12:1 (the woman clothed with the sun), Rev 12:3 (the great red dragon), Rev 15:1 (the seven angels with the last plagues). **The three signs frame the great-controversy section.** Church → adversary → judgment.
- "**Great and marvellous**" — Greek _mega kai thaumaston_. The same _thaumaston_ root that described the world wondering after the beast in Rev 13:3 (_ethaumasen holē hē gē_). **The world wondered after the beast; John wonders at the beast's judgment.** The marvel runs both directions.
- "**Seven angels having the seven last plagues**" — Greek _angelous hepta echontas plēgas hepta tas eschatas_. **_Eschatas_ — the last.** The same root as _eschatology_. **These are the FINAL plagues** — not a recurring chastisement, not a periodic judgment, but the closing-out plagues of the great controversy.
- "**For in them is filled up the wrath of God**" — Greek _hoti en autais etelesthē ho thymos tou theou_. **_Etelesthē_ — was completed, was brought to its end.** Same verb root as Christ's _tetelestai_ at the cross (Jn 19:30). **At the cross the work of redemption was finished; in the seven plagues the work of judgment is finished.**
- "**The wrath of God**" — Greek _ho thymos tou theou_. **_Thymos_** = wrath/fierceness, the intense outpouring of divine anger; Rev 14:10 pairs it with _orgē_ ("indignation"), and Rev 19:15 pairs the terms as "fierceness and wrath." **This is not arbitrary temper, but God's completed wrath now ready to be poured out by deliberate decree.**

[⟲] **The wrath-of-God thread comes to its head in Rev 15:1.** Rev 6:16-17 (the sixth seal — "the great day of his wrath is come; and who shall be able to stand?"). Rev 11:18 (the seventh trumpet — "thy wrath is come, and the time of the dead, that they should be judged"). Rev 14:10 (the third angel — "the wine of the wrath of God"). **Rev 15:1 — the wrath is _completed_ in the seven plagues.** Rev 16 will pour it out; Rev 19 will see Christ tread the winepress in person. The thread that runs from the sixth seal's question ("who shall be able to stand?") to the seventh plague's "It is done" closes here at v. 1's "in them is filled up."

## Rev 15:2 — The Sea of Glass and the Victors

> "And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God." (Rev 15:2)

- "**A sea of glass mingled with fire**" — Greek _hōs thalassan hyalinēn memigmenēn pyri_. **The same crystal floor of Rev 4:6** ("before the throne there was a sea of glass like unto crystal"). **Now mingled with fire** — the glass that was previously calm is now charged with judgment. **The crystal sea has become the standing-ground of those who passed through fire.**
- "**Them that had gotten the victory over the beast**" — Greek _tous nikōntas ek tou thēriou_. **_Tous nikōntas_** — the overcomers. **Same word as the seven-churches "to him that overcometh" formula (Rev 2:7, 11, 17, 26; 3:5, 12, 21).** The overcomers who were promised in Rev 2-3 are here standing on the sea of glass. **The promise was for the closing crisis; the overcomers were forged in the closing crisis.**
- **Four objects of the victory.** Over the beast. Over his image. Over his mark. Over the number of his name. **Four victories — exactly the four targets Rev 13 set up against them.** Every counterfeit Rev 13 produced is here defeated. **The closing-conflict's overcomers are the same 144,000 of Rev 14:1.**
- "**Having the harps of God**" — Greek _kitharas tou theou_. **The harps that were in the elders' hands at Rev 5:8 and in the harpers' hands at Rev 14:2 are now in the victors' hands.** The instrument of heavenly worship is given to those who came through. **The redeemed do not stand silent on the crystal sea; they sing.**

[CTRF] **Rev 15:2's four victories answer Rev 13's four claims point-by-point.** Rev 13:4 — they worshipped **the beast**. Rev 13:14 — they made **his image**. Rev 13:16 — they received **his mark**. Rev 13:18 — counted **the number of his name**. **Rev 15:2** — those who got the victory over **the beast**, over **his image**, over **his mark**, over **the number of his name**. **Four counterfeits. Four victories.** The same four; the inverted column. Where the unsealed bowed to four targets, the sealed overcame four targets.

## Rev 15:3-4 — The Song of Moses and of the Lamb

> "And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints. Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for thou only art holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest." (Rev 15:3-4)

- "**The song of Moses the servant of God**" — Greek _tēn ōdēn Mōuseōs tou doulou tou theou_. **Direct allusion to Ex 15:1-19** — Israel's song at the Red Sea after Pharaoh's army was drowned. _Ex 15:11_ — "Who is like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods? who is like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?" **The lyrics of Rev 15:3-4 are saturated with Ex 15's grammar.**
- "**And the song of the Lamb**" — Greek _kai tēn ōdēn tou arniou_. **One song with two titles.** Moses' song celebrated deliverance through the Red Sea waters; the Lamb's song celebrates deliverance through His blood. **Both deliverances; both covenants; one redemptive arc.** The OT and NT in one song.
- "**Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty**" — Greek _megala kai thaumasta ta erga sou Kyrie ho theos ho pantokratōr_. **_Pantokratōr_** = the Almighty, the One who holds all in His grip. The full divine title.
- "**Just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints**" — Greek _dikaiai kai alēthinai hai hodoi sou ho basileus tōn ethnōn_ in many manuscripts ("King of nations"); _ho basileus tōn aiōnōn_ in others ("King of ages"); the KJV/TR reads "King of saints." **Whichever manuscript reading, the saints sing about the JUSTICE of God's WAYS just before the plagues fall.** They are not embarrassed by the wrath; they vindicate it.
- "**Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name?**" — direct echo of the first angel's call (Rev 14:7 — "Fear God, and give glory to him"). **The saints are now living what the first angel called for.** What was a summons in Rev 14 is a confession in Rev 15.
- "**For thou only art holy**" — Greek _hoti monos hosios_. The closing-conflict's resolution — God ALONE is holy. The beast's claim to be like God (Rev 13:4 — "Who is like unto the beast?") is repudiated. The dragon's claim to be like the Most High (Isa 14:14) is exposed. **Only one Holy.**
- "**For all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest**" — Greek _hoti ta dikaiōmata sou ephanerōthēsan_. **The judgments — _ta dikaiōmata_, righteous decrees — are now openly displayed.** The investigative judgment that was hidden in heaven is now publicly known. The closing crisis vindicates God before the watching universe.

[DYK🔎] **The Exodus structure of Rev 15-16 is more deliberate than it looks at first reading.** In Egypt, the plagues fell first and the song followed: Ex 7-12 (plagues) → Ex 14 (Red Sea crossing) → Ex 15 (song of Moses). **In Revelation, the order inverts: the song is sung first (Rev 15:2-4), then the plagues fall (Rev 16).** The reason is that the deliverance has already happened by the time Rev 15 opens — not by the plagues, but by the seal. **The sealed remnant is delivered before the plagues come, in the same way Israel's firstborn were delivered before the death-plague struck Egypt:** not by a defensive miracle in the moment, but by a mark identifying whose house belonged to God (Ex 12:13 — "when I see the blood, I will pass over you"; Eze 9:4-6 — the mark on the foreheads in Jerusalem's coming destruction). **Rev 7:3's seal "in their foreheads" is the antitypical Passover mark.** The plagues then become not _their_ judgment but _their_ deliverance — exactly as Egypt's plagues were Israel's deliverance. The song is sung first because the singers already know how it ends.

## Rev 15:5 — The Temple of the Tabernacle of Testimony Opened

> "And after that I looked, and, behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened:" (Rev 15:5)

- "**The temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened**" — Greek _ēnoigē ho naos tēs skēnēs tou martyriou en tō ouranō_. **_Naos_** = the inner sanctuary (not _hieron_, the broader temple complex). **_Skēnē tou martyriou_** = the tabernacle of testimony — Numbers 1:50, 53; 9:15; 17:7; 18:2 use this exact phrase for the sanctuary in the wilderness, named after the testimony (the two tables of stone) which it housed.
- **The same heavenly sanctuary opened in Rev 11:19** ("the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament"). **Rev 11:19 opened the Most Holy Place at the seventh trumpet's announcement; Rev 15:5 opens it again at the close of probation.** The same scene; the closing phase.
- **Why "of the testimony"?** Because the testimony — the two tables of God's law — is what every great-controversy chapter of Revelation has been driving toward. The dragon's war is against the keepers of the commandments (Rev 12:17). The beast's war is against the law (the fourth commandment specifically — bonus study on Mark and Seal). **The plagues come from the temple of the testimony because they are the law's vindication.**
- **The order matters.** Rev 11:19 opened the Most Holy Place at the announcement of the kingdom; Rev 14:6-7 announced the judgment-hour publicly; Rev 14:14-20 reaped the harvest; **Rev 15:5 opens the temple AGAIN, this time for the angels carrying the seven plagues to come out.** The opening of v. 5 is the issuing of the verdict.

[⟲] **The sanctuary thread reaches its seventh and final canonical link in Rev 15:5-8.** Lev 16 (Day of Atonement pattern) → Dan 8:11-14 (little horn against the daily; cleansing at end of 2300 days) → Heb 8-9 (heavenly sanctuary as antitype) → Rev 4-5 (Lamb at the throne; sealed book) → Rev 11:19 (heavenly Most Holy opened, ark seen, Sinai-storm) → Rev 14:6-7 (judgment-hour publicly proclaimed) → **Rev 15:5-8 (temple opened, plagues issued, smoke fills, no man can enter — Day of Atonement ministry closes).** Seven canonical links from Leviticus to Revelation; the last is the close of the heavenly Day of Atonement at v. 8.

## Rev 15:6 — The Seven Angels in Linen and Gold

> "And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles." (Rev 15:6)

- "**Came out of the temple**" — they emerge from the same naos that just opened. **They are heaven's official messengers, dispatched on the official decree.**
- "**Clothed in pure and white linen**" — Greek _endedymenoi linon katharon lampron_. Some manuscripts read _lithon_ ("stone") instead of _linon_ ("linen"); the KJV/TR follows the linen reading. **Either way, the angels are clothed in the priestly garb of the sanctuary** — Lev 16:4's high-priestly linen on the Day of Atonement, Eze 9:2-3's man with the writer's inkhorn clothed in linen, Dan 10:5's man "clothed in linen, whose loins were girded with fine gold."
- "**Their breasts girded with golden girdles**" — Greek _periezōsmenoi peri ta stēthē zōnas chrysas_. **The same girdle Rev 1:13 placed on Christ Himself** ("girt about the paps with a golden girdle"). **The angels wear the High Priest's girdle.** They are dispatched as His instruments; they carry His authority.
- **The judgment is priestly, not vindictive.** The angels who pour out the plagues do so wearing the priest's linen and the High Priest's golden girdle. **God's judgments are part of His priestly work, not in opposition to it.** The same Christ who atoned now executes; the same sanctuary that received the blood now releases the plagues.

## Rev 15:7 — The Vials Handed Off

> "And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever." (Rev 15:7)

- "**One of the four beasts**" — Greek _hen ek tōn tessarōn zōōn_. **One of the four living creatures** of Rev 4:6-8 (KJV's "beasts" = _zōa_, "living creatures" — not _thēria_, the predatory beasts of Rev 13). **One of the four cherubim around the throne.**
- **The four living creatures have been worship leaders throughout** — Rev 4:8 ("Holy, holy, holy"), Rev 5:8-14 (worship of the Lamb), Rev 6:1-7 (calling the four horsemen), Rev 7:11. **Now one of them dispatches the wrath.** The same throne-cherubim who lead heaven's worship hand the vials to the angels.
- "**Seven golden vials full of the wrath of God**" — Greek _hepta phialas chrysas gemousas tou thymou tou theou_. **_Phialē_** = a shallow bowl, the priestly censer-bowl used in temple service (Ex 27:3; 1 Kgs 7:50). **The same kind of bowl Rev 5:8 placed in the elders' hands "full of odours, which are the prayers of saints."** **The bowl that held prayers in Rev 5:8 holds wrath in Rev 15:7.** The prayers cried out "How long?" (Rev 6:10); the wrath is the answer.
- **The vials are the answer to the fifth-seal cry.** Rev 6:9-10 — "I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God... they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?" Rev 6:11 — they were given white robes and told to rest "yet for a little season." **Rev 15:7's bowls of wrath are heaven's specific answer to that cry.** The blood of saints will be answered with blood (Rev 16:6).
- "**Who liveth for ever and ever**" — Greek _tou zōntos eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_. **The eternal God's wrath is not a passing temper.** It is the considered, eternal, unchanging response of holy love to settled rebellion.

## Rev 15:8 — The Temple Filled, the Door Closed

> "And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled." (Rev 15:8)

- "**The temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power**" — Greek _egemisthē ho naos kapnou ek tēs doxēs tou theou kai ek tēs dynameōs autou_. **The same smoke that filled Solomon's temple at its dedication** (1 Kgs 8:10-11 — "the cloud filled the house of the LORD, so that the priests could not stand to minister because of the cloud: for the glory of the LORD had filled the house of the LORD"). **The same smoke that filled Isaiah's vision of God's throne** (Isa 6:4 — "the house was filled with smoke"). **The same cloud Moses could not enter at the tabernacle's first dedication** (Ex 40:34-35 — "Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle"). **God's own glory is now what blocks entry.**
- "**No man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled**" — Greek _oudeis edynato eiselthein eis ton naon, achri telesthōsin hai hepta plēgai tōn hepta angelōn_. **The Day of Atonement closing pattern — Lev 16:17:** "And there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out." **On the ancient Day of Atonement, while the high priest ministered alone in the Most Holy Place, no one else could enter — neither priest nor people.** Rev 15:8 extends that pattern to the close of the heavenly Day of Atonement: when the High Priest's mediation is complete, the temple closes, and no further intercession is possible until the plagues finish.
- **This is the close of probation.** The High Priest has finished His mediatorial work. The investigative judgment has decided every case. The Spirit's specifically-convicting ministry on earth is withdrawn. The door is closed. **No further additions to the book of life. No further removals. No further intercession.** Rev 22:11's declaration applies from this moment: "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still."
- **The plagues then fall on a closed-probation world.** The mark-receivers receive the plagues; the sealed stand through them protected (the Passover pattern — the destroyer passes over the houses with the blood/seal).

[CTRF] **Rev 15:8 is the silent counterfeit of the cross.** At Calvary, the Father's face was hidden from the Son ("My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" — Mt 27:46) so that mediation could open for sinners. **At Rev 15:8, the Father's glory hides the Son's mediatorial chamber so that mediation closes.** The same throne; the same chamber; the inversion. The sinner who trusted Christ during probation walks through; the sinner who would have begged for entry now finds the door shut. **The most loving moment in heaven's history (Calvary) and the most solemn moment in heaven's history (Rev 15:8) are linked at the throne.** Both are about whether the door is open. Probation closes from the inside.

[Q] **"What does probation's close mean for those still alive?"**
→ Probation's close is not announced on earth. There is no trumpet, no headline, no sky-tear. **Those still living do not hear the door close — they live through it without recognizing it.** The first hours after probation's close look normal. They are not. The Spirit's specifically-convicting ministry is withdrawn; consciences that struggled with the unrepentant cease to struggle; sinners no longer feel the pull to repent. **The sealed do not lose salvation; their salvation was sealed before the door closed.** The mark-receivers do not get a final chance; their character was fixed before the door closed. **Probation's close is the ratification of choices already made over a lifetime, not an arbitrary cutoff for sincere seekers.** What it does mean for the living: any sealing that was going to happen has happened; any decision-making that was going to happen has happened; what remains is the pressure-test of the seven plagues, through which the sealed stand and under which the unsealed fall. **The reader's question is not "when will probation close?" but "is my decision actually made?"** The bonus study on the Final Appeal develops the full case.

## After the Verses — What Rev 15 Hands to the Rest of the Book

- **Rev 15 hands Rev 16 the loaded vials.** The seven plagues are not arbitrary wrath; they are the contents of the seven golden vials that have just been handed off in Rev 15:7. **Rev 16 will pour what Rev 15 has loaded.**
- **Rev 15 hands Rev 17-18 the verdict on Babylon.** The wrath that Rev 15 declares "filled up" will fall specifically on Babylon — Rev 16:19 ("great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath"); Rev 17-18 will detail her fall. **The wrath of Rev 15:1 IS the wrath of Rev 17-18's destroyer.**
- **Rev 15 hands Rev 19 the white-horse Rider.** Rev 19:13 — "His vesture dipped in blood... he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God." **The wrath that Rev 15:7 handed to the angels is what Rev 19 sees Christ executing in person.** The same wrath; different agents.
- **Rev 15 hands Rev 22:11 the silent moment.** When the temple closes and the door is shut, Rev 22:11's declaration applies: "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still." **The character-fixing moment is Rev 15:8.**
- **Rev 15 closes the sanctuary thread.** Lev 16's Day of Atonement pattern is now complete in the heavenly sanctuary. Christ's mediatorial work — begun at His ascension, intensified at the close of the 2300 days in 1844, public-faced through the three angels — is finished. **The next chapter Christ wears the garments of the King-Conqueror, not the garments of the Priest-Mediator.** Rev 19's white-horse Rider is the same Christ as Rev 1's High Priest, but the vestment has changed.
- **Rev 15 vindicates God's character.** The saints sing on the sea of glass BEFORE the plagues fall: "Just and true are thy ways" (v. 3). **The judgment is not what the saints have to forgive God for; it is what they sing about.** They are not embarrassed by the wrath; they understand it. The saints' worship of God's justice is the central feature of the chapter.

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 15 is the briefest chapter in Revelation (8 verses) and the most pivotal — it is the chapter where probation closes
- The "another sign in heaven" of Rev 15:1 is the third of three signs that frame Rev 12-15 (the woman, the dragon, the seven plagues)
- The wrath is "filled up" (v. 1) — _etelesthē_, the same root as Christ's _tetelestai_ at the cross; redemption was finished at Calvary, judgment is finished here
- The sea of glass mingled with fire (v. 2) is Rev 4:6's crystal floor now charged with judgment — the standing-ground of those who came through the closing crisis
- The four victories (v. 2) — over the beast, his image, his mark, the number of his name — answer Rev 13's four counterfeits point by point
- The song of Moses + the song of the Lamb (vv. 3-4) celebrates BOTH covenants of deliverance — Red Sea waters and Calvary blood — as one redemptive arc
- The Exodus structure inverts: song first (Rev 15:2-4) THEN plagues (Rev 16), because the seal is the antitypical Passover mark — deliverance happened before the plagues fell
- The temple of the tabernacle of testimony (v. 5) — _naos tēs skēnēs tou martyriou_ — names the heavenly sanctuary by its central content, the testimony / law of God, the same ark visible in Rev 11:19
- The seven angels emerge in priestly linen and the High Priest's golden girdle (v. 6) — the judgment is priestly, not vindictive; God's judgments are part of His priestly work
- The vials (v. 7) are the same _phialē_ bowls that held the saints' prayers in Rev 5:8 — the prayers cried "How long?", the bowls now hold the answer
- The temple filled with smoke (v. 8) is the Day-of-Atonement closing pattern of Lev 16:17 — when the High Priest ministers alone, no one else can enter; the close of probation
- The sanctuary thread reaches its seventh and final canonical link in Rev 15:5-8 (Lev 16 → Dan 8:11-14 → Heb 8-9 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 15:5-8)
- Rev 22:11's declaration ("he that is unjust, let him be unjust still") applies from Rev 15:8 forward — characters are ratified, the door is shut, the plagues fall

## Next: Revelation 16 — The Seven Last Plagues Poured Out

The vials are loaded. The temple has closed. Now the plagues fall. Rev 16 takes the seven golden vials Rev 15 just handed off and pours them: a noisome and grievous sore on those with the mark; the sea becomes blood; the rivers and fountains become blood and the angel of the waters cries "thou art righteous, O Lord"; the sun scorches men with fire; darkness fills the seat of the beast; the Euphrates dries up to prepare the way of the kings of the east; three unclean spirits like frogs go out of the dragon, the beast, and the false prophet to gather the kings of the earth to the battle of that great day of God Almighty at Armageddon; the seventh angel pours into the air and a great voice from the throne says "It is done." Rev 16 is what Rev 15 prepared.
