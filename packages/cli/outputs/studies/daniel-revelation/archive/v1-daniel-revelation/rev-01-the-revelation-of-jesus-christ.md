# Revelation 1 — The Revelation of Jesus Christ

**Daniel's book closed sealed. John opens his and sees Christ standing among seven golden candlesticks — dressed in language that echoes the Man in linen of Dan 10. Same Person. New phase. The first words of the book are "the revelation _of Jesus Christ_" — not of future events, but of a Person. Twenty verses. Seven blessings introduced. The Second Coming verse at structural center. The ascended High Priest standing in the middle of His churches, holding their leaders in His right hand. Miss this chapter and you read the rest of Revelation without the key.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 1 (entire chapter)
- Daniel 10:5-6 (the OT Christophany Rev 1 echoes)
- Daniel 7:9-14 (the courtroom Rev 1 condenses into greeting form)
- Zechariah 12:10 (the pierced One)
- Psalm 2 (the Christological backbone Revelation keeps quoting)

## Before the Verses — Reading Rev 1 as a Chiasm

Rev 1 mirrors itself around v. 7 — the Second Coming verse — at the apex.

```
A   1   vv. 1-3        Christ and John; "the time is at hand"
    2   vv. 4-6        Greetings from the Three (Father, Spirit, Son)
B       v.  7          The Second Coming
A'  1'  vv. 8-9        Christ and John; persecution, "I John... in tribulation"
    2'  vv. 10-20      Christ as Great High Priest among the candlesticks
```

- A and A' both pair Christ with John in the prophetic-witness mode. A opens with the time-pressure ("the time is at hand"); A' opens with the cost ("I John, your brother and companion in tribulation").
- 2 and 2' both unfold the Judgment / High Priest scene — vv. 4-6 names the persons of the Godhead in throne-greeting form (Dan 7:9-14 supplies the courtroom frame); vv. 10-20 names the High-Priestly Christ in His priestly garments (the same Person Daniel met in Dan 10).
- B (v. 7) sits at the apex. The whole chapter is built around the Second Coming verse.

[KEY🔑] **Read the apex as the thesis.** Rev 1's chiasm puts v. 7 at the center because the chapter — and the book that opens with it — is announcing what the rest will unfold. When the form of a chapter is asking "what is this about?", the apex is the answer.

## Rev 1:1 — The Revelation of Jesus Christ

> "The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified [it] by his angel unto his servant John:" (Rev 1:1)

- "**The Revelation**" — Greek _apokalypsis_ = unveiling, disclosure. The opposite of hidden. The book's name announces it is a book that opens, not closes.
- "**Of Jesus Christ**" — grammatically ambiguous on purpose: the revelation _belongs to_ Him (He is the owner/giver) _and_ it _unveils_ Him (He is the subject). Both readings are intended.
- Not primarily the revelation of Antichrist. Not primarily the revelation of the end times. **The revelation of Jesus Christ.** If your reading of Revelation obscures Christ rather than reveals Him, you are reading it sideways.
- The chain of communication: God → Jesus → angel → John → servants. Five parties, four handoffs; Christ is the mediating giver. The book is a courier delivery from heaven.
- "**Things which must shortly come to pass**" — Greek _en tachei_ carries speed/soonness. In the historicist reading, the phrase signals that once the prophetic events begin to unfold they move with speed; it does not require the whole book to exhaust itself in the first century.

## Rev 1:2 — The threefold testimony

> "Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw." (Rev 1:2)

- Three things John witnesses: **the word of God**, **the testimony of Jesus Christ**, **all things that he saw**. The word/testimony pairing recurs at Rev 1:9, 6:9, 20:4; Rev 12:17 and Rev 19:10 develop the testimony/remnant strand. **It is the fingerprint of the faithful remnant.**

`[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread.** Dan 12:1 → **Rev 1:2 (this link, "word of God + testimony of Jesus" pattern introduced)** → Rev 1:9 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 19:10 → Rev 22:4. The phrase that names the persecuted faithful is set at the front of the book.

## Rev 1:3 — The seven blessings begin

> "Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time [is] at hand." (Rev 1:3)

- The first of seven beatitudes in Revelation (1:3; 14:13; 16:15; 19:9; 20:6; 22:7; 22:14).
- **Three actions, one blessing**: _read, hear, keep_. Not a puzzle to solve. A prophecy to obey.
- "**Blessed is he that readeth**" — singular reader; "**they that hear**" — plural hearers. Public reading in worship is in view. Revelation was meant to be read aloud to congregations.
- "**The time is at hand**" — _kairos eggys_ = the appointed time is near. This is the front-of-book counterpart to Rev 22:10's back-of-book "the time is at hand." The whole book is bracketed by urgency.
- [→] A book God blesses its readers for reading is not a book God meant to be locked. The lock was on Daniel (Dan 12:9). The key is Revelation. Now is the moment for both.

## Rev 1:4 — Greetings from the throne

> "John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace [be] unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;" (Rev 1:4)

- "**Seven churches in Asia**" — real first-century congregations in Roman Asia (western Asia Minor, modern Turkey). Named in v. 11.
- "**Him which is, and which was, and which is to come**" — a threefold name of God: present, past, future — eternity condensed into a grammatical tense. Echoes _ehyeh asher ehyeh_ ("I AM that I AM," Ex 3:14).
- "**The seven Spirits which are before his throne**" — the Holy Spirit in His sevenfold completeness (Isa 11:2 names the seven attributes of the Spirit on the messianic Branch). Not seven different spirits; the one Spirit in His full operation.

## Rev 1:5 — Three titles of Christ

> "And from Jesus Christ, [who is] the faithful witness, [and] the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood," (Rev 1:5)

- Three titles in order: **faithful witness** (His earthly ministry), **first begotten of the dead** (His resurrection), **prince of the kings of the earth** (His enthronement). Past, present, future — earthly, resurrected, reigning.
- "**Faithful witness**" (Greek _martys pistos_) — the Christian word _martyr_ is born from this title. Christ's name for the church to wear (cf. Rev 2:13's Antipas).
- "**The first begotten of the dead**" draws on Ps 89:27's royal-Davidic "firstborn... higher than the kings of the earth," while Acts 13:33 reads Ps 2:7's begetting language through the resurrection.
- Psalm 2 is the backbone Revelation keeps coming back to — the trial/crucifixion conspiracy (Ps 2:1-2 in Acts 4:24-28), enthronement (Ps 2:6), resurrection-as-begetting (Ps 2:7 + Acts 13:33), and the Second Coming "rod of iron" (Ps 2:9 quoted in Rev 2:27, 12:5, 19:15).
- "**Loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood**" — past-tense, finished work.

## Rev 1:6 — Made us kings and priests

> "And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him [be] glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen." (Rev 1:6)

- "**Kings and priests**" — quotes Ex 19:6's covenant promise to Israel ("a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation") and applies it to the church. The promise Israel forfeited at Sinai is fulfilled in the saints redeemed by Christ's blood.
- This title returns at Rev 5:10 (the elders' song) and Rev 20:6 (the resurrection promise).
- [→] Rev 1:4-6 names the same judgment scene Daniel saw in Dan 7. Rev 4-5 will give the same scene a fuller frame.

| Dan 7:9-14                                                       | Rev 1:4-6 (with Rev 4-5 as fuller frame)                                         |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "The Ancient of Days did sit" on a throne (v. 9)                 | "him which is, and which was, and which is to come" on a throne (1:4)            |
| "A fiery stream issued and came forth" (v. 10)                   | "seven Spirits before the throne" (1:4); "seven lamps of fire burning" (Rev 4:5) |
| "Thousand thousands ministered unto him" (v. 10)                 | echoed at Rev 5:11                                                               |
| "One like the Son of man came" (v. 13)                           | "Jesus Christ" (1:5)                                                             |
| "There was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom" (v. 14) | "to him be glory and dominion for ever and ever" (1:6)                           |

- Rev 1 introduces the order the rest of Revelation will unfold: vv. 4-6 names the throne scene; v. 7 announces the Second Coming. **Judgment first, then return.**

[DYK🔎] John quotes no OT text by name but weaves over 500 OT allusions into Revelation, more than any other NT book. If you do not know Daniel, Zechariah, Ezekiel, Isaiah, and the Psalms, you cannot read Revelation. If you do, half the book is already translated.

## Rev 1:7 — Behold, He cometh with clouds

> "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they [also] which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen." (Rev 1:7)

- **The chapter's apex. The book's thesis verse.**
- The verse stitches two OT texts: "one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven" (Dan 7:13) and "they shall look upon me whom they have pierced" (Zech 12:10).
- **Three movements in one verse:**
  1. "**He cometh with clouds**" — the Second Coming. Note the contrast with Dan 7:13's coming _to_ the Ancient of Days; this is the coming _to earth_.
  2. "**Every eye shall see him**" — universal visibility. No secret rapture, no esoteric signal.
  3. "**They also which pierced him**" — requires a special resurrection of Christ's enemies. Dan 12:2's partial resurrection is what makes this clause possible.
- "**All kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him**" — Zech 12:10's mourning, transposed to global scale.
- [→] Rev 1:7 is the book's thesis in one verse. Everything that follows — churches, seals, trumpets, beasts, plagues, marriage supper, millennium — moves toward this arrival. **Memorize the verse. It is the compass.**

## Rev 1:8 — I am Alpha and Omega

> "‹I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending,› saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty." (Rev 1:8)

- Rev 1:8 places the **Alpha-and-Omega** claim in the speaker's mouth and identifies Him with the eternal "**which is, and which was, and which is to come**" / **Almighty** language. Rev 1:11 and 1:17 then attach the Alpha-Omega / first-last claim directly to Christ in the vision.
- "**Alpha and Omega**" — the Greek-letter form of Isaiah's YHWH "first/last" formula (Isa 41:4; 44:6; 48:12). Most fully Isa 44:6: "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God." YHWH's claim about Himself.
- "**The Almighty**" — Greek _pantokratōr_ = "the all-ruling"; corresponds to OT _El Shaddai_ (Gen 17:1; Ex 6:3) and the wider "Lord God Almighty" language Revelation repeats (Rev 4:8; 11:17; 15:3; 16:7, 14; 19:6, 15; 21:22).
- Rev 1:8 is not a poetic flourish — it is a Christological assertion that the speaker is YHWH. **The book opens by saying who is speaking.**

## Rev 1:9 — I John, your brother in tribulation

> "I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ." (Rev 1:9)

- "**Your brother, and companion in tribulation**" — John writes from inside the suffering, not above it. The early church's persecution under Domitian (~AD 95) is the immediate setting.
- "**Patmos**" — a small rocky island in the Aegean, used by Rome for political exile.
- "**For the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ**" — exiled because of his witness. The same fingerprint phrase from v. 2.

`[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread (link).** Rev 1:2 → **Rev 1:9 (this link, John living the testimony he names)** → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:12 → Rev 19:10 → Rev 22:4.

## Rev 1:10 — In the Spirit on the Lord's Day

> "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet," (Rev 1:10)

- "**In the Spirit**" — prophetic vision state. John is taken outside ordinary perception (cf. Eze 3:12; Acts 10:10).
- "**The Lord's day**" — historicist reading: the seventh-day Sabbath, the day Christ called His own ("the Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day," Matt 12:8; cf. Mk 2:28; Lk 6:5). The phrase _kyriakē hēmera_ does not name Sunday in the NT; the text identifies its referent through Christ's own claim of lordship over a specific day.
- "**A great voice, as of a trumpet**" — the same trumpet-voice that gave the law at Sinai (Ex 19:16, 19; Heb 12:19). The Lawgiver speaks again.

## Rev 1:11 — Write to the seven churches

> "Saying, ‹I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send [it] unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea.›" (Rev 1:11)

- Seven cities named in the order a Roman messenger would travel through western Asia Minor: Ephesus → Smyrna → Pergamos → Thyatira → Sardis → Philadelphia → Laodicea. Real geography.
- Seven cities, seven letters, seven church eras. **The postal logic and the prophetic chronology coincide.** The letters operate at three levels simultaneously: real first-century correspondence, real postal sequence, and real prophetic arrangement.
- The seven-church mapping (Ephesus = apostolic ~AD 31-100 → Smyrna = persecution ~AD 100-313 → Pergamos = accommodation ~AD 313-538 → Thyatira = medieval/papal ~AD 538-1517 → Sardis = Reformation ~AD 1517-1755 → Philadelphia = Advent awakening ~AD 1755-1844 → Laodicea = final church ~AD 1844-present) will be unfolded in Rev 2-3.

## Rev 1:12-13 — Seven candlesticks; the Son of Man

> "And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks [one] like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle." (Rev 1:12-13)

- **Seven golden candlesticks** = the seven churches (v. 20 defines this). The candlestick is the menorah of the sanctuary's holy place (Ex 25:31-40) — the church is heaven's lampstand on earth.
- **"In the midst"** — Christ stands _among_ His churches, not outside them. Heb 4:14-16's High Priest is operative here.
- **"Clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle"** — the priestly long robe and high-priestly girdle (cf. Ex 28:4-8). The girdle is at the chest, not the waist — the high priest's posture, not the laborer's.
- [→] Christ is dressed for ministry, standing in the middle of His congregations, conducting His high-priestly work. **This is the era of the seven churches: the high-priestly era.**

## Rev 1:14-16 — Seven features of His glory

> "His head and [his] hairs [were] white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes [were] as a flame of fire; And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters. And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance [was] as the sun shineth in his strength." (Rev 1:14-16)

The description substantially tracks Dan 10:5-6 across the main visible features, with additions and omissions.

| Dan 10:5-6                                               | Rev 1:13-16                                                                      |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "A certain man clothed in linen"                         | "Clothed with a garment down to the foot"                                        |
| "His loins were girded with fine gold of Uphaz"          | "Girt about the paps with a golden girdle"                                       |
| —                                                        | "Head and hairs white like wool, as white as snow" (cf. Dan 7:9 Ancient of Days) |
| "His face as the appearance of lightning"                | "His countenance as the sun shineth in his strength"                             |
| "His eyes as lamps of fire"                              | "His eyes were as a flame of fire"                                               |
| "His arms and his feet like in colour to polished brass" | "His feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace"                  |
| "The voice of his words like the voice of a multitude"   | "His voice as the sound of many waters"                                          |

- The parallel is dense — point after point matches. **Same Person, fuller disclosure.** The Man Daniel saw in linen is the Christ John sees among the candlesticks. Dan 10 showed Him entering the priestly work. Rev 1 shows Him conducting it.
- **What the imagery means:**
  - **High-priestly robe and golden girdle** = high-priestly ministry (Ex 28:4-8)
  - **White hair** = Ancient-of-Days imagery (Dan 7:9) now carried by the Son — equality with the Father
  - **Burning feet, fiery eyes** = judgment (Mal 3:2-3; Eze 1:27-28)
  - **Voice of many waters** = the voice of God (Eze 43:2)
  - **Seven stars in His right hand** = the angels (messengers) of the seven churches (v. 20)
  - **Twoedged sword from His mouth** = the Word of God (Heb 4:12; Eph 6:17)
  - **Countenance like the sun** = unmediated glory (cf. Christ's transfiguration, Matt 17:2)
- [→] Christ is pictured in the midst of His churches as **High Priest, Judge, and Speaker of the Word**. That is what He is doing during every era of church history the seven letters will describe.

## Rev 1:17-18 — Fear not; I am the first and the last

> "And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, ‹Fear not; I am the first and the last:› [I am] ‹he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.›" (Rev 1:17-18)

- John's reaction matches Daniel's at Dan 10:8-9 — falling as dead. **Same Person, same effect** on the prophet who beholds Him.
- "**Fear not**" — Christ's first word. The same word He spoke through every gospel scene (Matt 14:27; Lk 12:32; Jn 14:1).
- "**I am the first and the last**" — the YHWH first/last claim from Isaiah (Isa 41:4; 44:6; 48:12), repeated.
- "**Was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore**" — the resurrection certified. The High Priest among the candlesticks bears the marks of the cross.
- "**Have the keys of hell and of death**" — _hadēs_ and _thanatos_, the realm and the power. Christ holds both. Rev 6:8 will personify them; Rev 20:13-14 will dispose of them.

## Rev 1:19 — The book's outline

> "‹Write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter;›" (Rev 1:19)

- **Three tenses, one outline:**
  - "**Things which thou hast seen**" = the Christ-vision of Rev 1
  - "**Things which are**" = the seven churches in their present condition (Rev 2-3)
  - "**Things which shall be hereafter**" = Rev 4 onward (note Rev 4:1's identical phrase: "Come up hither, and I will shew thee things which must be hereafter")
- **The outline is given by Christ Himself.** Revelation is structured by the Author.

## Rev 1:20 — The mystery of the stars and candlesticks

> "‹The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches: and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.›" (Rev 1:20)

- Christ Himself decodes the symbols. **This is the interpretive key for the whole book**: Revelation is a symbolic prophecy, and where it gives the decoder, the decoder is binding.
- "**Angels of the seven churches**" — Greek _angelos_ = messenger; here, the human messengers/leaders/representatives of each congregation. The letters are addressed to them as the responsible party.
- The stars are held in His right hand; the candlesticks are surrounded by His presence — **the leaders of His churches are held personally by Christ, and the churches themselves are surrounded by Him.**

[DYK🔎] "The mystery" (_to mystērion_) is a key word in NT prophecy. Paul uses it for the gospel itself (Eph 3:3-6; Col 1:26-27); John uses it here for Revelation's symbolic decoding. A "mystery" in Scripture is not something that stays hidden — it is something that _was_ hidden and is now being disclosed. **Revelation _is_ the disclosure.**

## After the Verses — Daniel 10 / Revelation 1: Same Person, Two Phases

Christ is the bookend Person across both prophetic books. Dan 10 met Him at the Tigris, entering the priestly ministry of the final vision. Rev 1 meets Him among the candlesticks, conducting that priestly ministry through every age of the church. The features match because the Person is the same. The differences map the change of phase:

- **Dan 10:** Christ at the river, just before opening the prophecy that culminates at "the glorious holy mountain" (Dan 11:45). The OT setting, the OT phase.
- **Rev 1:** Christ in the sanctuary's holy place, in the middle of the seven golden candlesticks, with seven stars in His right hand. The NT setting, the priestly phase. From this position He will dictate the seven letters that map His own work through every church era.

Same Christ. Same garments. Same eyes of fire. Same voice. The Old Testament's most extended Christophany (Dan 10:5-6) and the New Testament's opening Christophany (Rev 1:13-16) are joined to teach a single point: **the Christ Daniel saw is the Christ ministering now.**

## Key Takeaways

- The book is "the revelation _of Jesus Christ_" — a Person disclosed, not future events alone. If your reading obscures Christ, you are reading sideways.
- Rev 1 is a chiasm with the Second Coming verse (v. 7) at the apex. The book's thesis is at the chapter's center.
- The book is _commanded open_ from the start (Rev 1:3, "the time is at hand"; Rev 22:10, "seal not"). The inverse of Daniel's "seal the book" (Dan 12:4, 9). Sealed Daniel + open Revelation = the unsealing era.
- The "word of God + testimony of Jesus" pairing is the fingerprint of the faithful remnant (Rev 1:2, 9; 12:17; 14:12; 19:10).
- Rev 1:7 stitches Dan 7:13 ("clouds") and Zech 12:10 ("they shall look upon me whom they have pierced") into one verse. The Second Coming is universally visible and includes a special resurrection of Christ's enemies (cf. Dan 12:2).
- The Alpha-Omega / first-last / Almighty language (Rev 1:8, 11, 17) identifies the Speaker as YHWH.
- "The Lord's day" (Rev 1:10) is the seventh-day Sabbath — the day Christ explicitly called His own (Matt 12:8).
- The seven candlesticks = seven churches; the seven stars = their messengers (v. 20). Christ Himself decodes the symbols — the interpretive key for the whole book.
- Rev 1:13-16 substantially tracks Dan 10:5-6: same Person, fuller disclosure. The Man Daniel saw in linen is the Christ John sees among the candlesticks. Dan 10 = entering the priestly work; Rev 1 = conducting it.
- Christ stands among His churches as **High Priest, Judge, and Speaker of the Word**. That is what He is doing during every era of church history Rev 2-3 will describe.
- Rev 1:19 gives the book's three-tense outline straight from Christ: things seen (Rev 1), things which are (Rev 2-3), things to come (Rev 4 onward).

## Next: Revelation 2 — The First Four Letters (Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamos, Thyatira)

The first four letters cover the first four church eras — apostolic, persecution, accommodation, medieval/papal — from Pentecost through the Reformation's eve. Each letter follows a seven-part template: address, identification, commendation, rebuke, counsel, hearing-call, promise. Each ends with "to him that overcometh" + an Eden-restoring promise. Christ walks among the candlesticks the whole time, holding the leaders in His right hand, and judging from the inside.
