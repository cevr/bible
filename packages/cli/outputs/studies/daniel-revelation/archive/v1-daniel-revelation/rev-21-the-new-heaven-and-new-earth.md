# Revelation 21 — The New Heaven, the New Earth, and the Holy City

**Rev 20 ended with death and hades cast into the lake of fire and the universe finally cleansed of every enemy. Rev 21 opens with the new creation: "I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away; and there was no more sea." John sees the holy city, New Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. A great voice from the throne announces: "the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people." God Himself wipes away all tears; no more death, no more sorrow, no more crying, no more pain — "for the former things are passed away." The throne-occupant speaks: "Behold, I make all things new... It is done. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end." The water of life is offered freely to the thirsty. The overcomer inherits all things; the unrepentant have their part in the second death. Then one of the seven vial-angels carries John to a great high mountain to show him "the bride, the Lamb's wife" — the city itself. The walls are jasper, with twelve gates of pearl (twelve tribes named on them) and twelve foundations (twelve apostles named on them). The city is a cube — 12,000 furlongs each side — built of pure gold like clear glass, foundations garnished with twelve precious stones. There is no temple in the city, "for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it." No sun, no moon — the glory of God is the light, and the Lamb is the lamp. The gates never close; no night ever falls. Nothing that defileth enters; only those written in the Lamb's book of life. Rev 21 is the chapter where God's original Eden-intent — to dwell with His people — is finally and permanently realized.**

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 21 Sits

Rev 21 is the climax of the book and of the whole Bible. **Rev 20 cleared the universe; Rev 21 fills it again with the dwelling-of-God-with-man.** The chapter has three movements: the new creation announcement and the throne-voice (vv. 1-8), the angel's carry-away to see the bride-city (vv. 9-21), and the city's interior life (vv. 22-27).

- **The Genesis-Revelation arc closes here.** Gen 1 had a creation; Rev 21 has a re-creation. Gen 2 had a river and a tree of life; Rev 22 will restore them. Gen 3 had the curse and the cherub-guarded exile; Rev 22:3 will say "no more curse." **The whole Bible is one story, and Rev 21 is its hinge into the eternal scene.**
- **The bride and the city are one.** Rev 21:2 sees the holy city "as a bride adorned for her husband"; Rev 21:9-10 has the angel say, "I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife" and then shows John the city. **The redeemed and their dwelling are inseparable** — the city is the people, and the people are the city. The marriage of Rev 19 reaches its dwelling-place here.
- **The "tabernacle of God" with men** (v. 3) is the goal of every previous sanctuary. The wilderness tabernacle (Ex 25:8 — "that I may dwell among them"), Solomon's temple (1 Ki 8:27), the second temple, Christ-as-tabernacle in Jn 1:14 (_eskēnōsen en hēmin_ — "tabernacled among us"), the heavenly sanctuary's ministry through Hebrews — all anticipated this. **Rev 21:3 is the sanctuary thread's terminus.**
- **The cube-city of v. 16 is the Most Holy Place expanded.** Solomon's Most Holy Place was a 20-cubit cube (1 Ki 6:20); the New Jerusalem is a 12,000-stadia cube. **The whole city is a Most Holy Place** — the entire dwelling-area is where God dwells unmediated.
- **The "no temple" of v. 22 is the antitypical fulfillment, not the loss, of the sanctuary system.** Every previous sanctuary was a structure to bring God and humanity into the same space. **In Rev 21 the structure is no longer needed because the goal — God-with-people — is achieved directly.** The Lord God Almighty and the Lamb _are_ the temple; the city's whole interior is sanctuary-space.
- **Only those in the Lamb's book of life enter** (v. 27). Rev 20:15's criterion is repeated. **The book of life — last seen at the great white throne — is the city's entrance-register.**
- **The chapter's "It is done" (v. 6) echoes Rev 16:17's "It is done."** Rev 16:17's _gegonen_ closed the seventh-vial wrath; Rev 21:6's _gegonan_ (variant: _gegona_, with first-person "I have become / I have come to pass") declares the new creation finished. **Two "done" announcements — one for judgment, one for restoration.** Both spoken from the throne.

[KEY🔑] **Rev 21 is the chapter where the dwelling-of-God-with-man, the goal of every sanctuary from Eden onward, becomes permanent reality.** Gen 3:8's "they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day" recorded the original arrangement: God dwelt with man face-to-face. Sin broke that. Ex 25:8's tabernacle, 1 Ki 6's temple, Eze 37:26-28's promised eternal sanctuary, Jn 1:14's incarnation ("the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us"), Heb 8-9's heavenly-sanctuary ministry, Rev 4-5's throne-room scenes, Rev 11:19's opened temple, Rev 15:5-8's Day-of-Atonement closing — every sanctuary movement aimed at restoring what Eden had. **Rev 21:3 — "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God" — is the announcement that the goal is now achieved.** No more mediation needed; no more atonement needed; no more separation. **God walks with His people again, this time forever.** The whole sanctuary thread, the whole great-controversy arc, the whole biblical narrative bend back to this verse.

## Rev 21:1 — A New Heaven and a New Earth

> "And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away; and there was no more sea." (Rev 21:1)

- "**A new heaven and a new earth**" — Greek _ouranon kainon kai gēn kainēn_. **_Kainos_** = new in kind/quality, not just chronologically next (contrast _neos_ = new in time but possibly same kind). **The new creation is qualitatively different** — the same kind of "new" as Christ's new covenant (_kainē diathēkē_, Heb 8:8) and the new commandment (_entolēn kainēn_, Jn 13:34). **Compare Isa 65:17** ("Behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered") and **Isa 66:22** ("the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me"); **2 Pet 3:13** ("we, according to his promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness"). **The OT promise that became Christian hope is now the post-judgment reality.**
- "**The first heaven and the first earth were passed away**" — Greek _ho gar prōtos ouranos kai hē prōtē gē apēlthan_. **_Apēlthan_** — passed away, departed. **Compare Rev 20:11** ("from whose face the earth and the heaven fled away; and there was found no place for them"); **2 Pet 3:10** ("the heavens shall pass away with a great noise, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat"); **Mt 24:35** (Christ — "Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away"). **The fire of 2 Pet 3 has done its work.** The great-white-throne fire of Rev 20:9 simultaneously consumed the wicked and cleansed the planet for re-creation.
- "**No more sea**" — Greek _kai hē thalassa ouk estin eti_. **The sea in Revelation has been the restless source of evil** (Rev 13:1 — the sea-beast rises out of the sea; Rev 17:15 — the waters are peoples and nations) and the OT-cosmological symbol of chaos (Ps 89:9; Isa 57:20 — "the wicked are like the troubled sea"). **In the new creation, chaos is finished.** The threatening waters that gave rise to beast-empires and stored sin's restless multitudes are gone. **Compare Dan 7:2-3** (the four winds striving on the great sea; the four beasts coming up from the sea) — the sea-source of empire is dried up.

## Rev 21:2 — The Holy City, New Jerusalem, Descending

> "And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband." (Rev 21:2)

- "**The holy city, new Jerusalem**" — Greek _tēn polin tēn hagian Ierousalēm kainēn_. **_Kainēn_** — qualitatively new, the same _kainos_ root as the new heaven and earth. **Compare Heb 12:22** ("ye are come unto mount Sion, and unto the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem"); **Gal 4:26** ("Jerusalem which is above is free, which is the mother of us all"); **Heb 11:10, 16** (Abraham looked for "a city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God"). **The patriarchs' hoped-for city descends here.**
- "**Coming down from God out of heaven**" — Greek _katabainousan ek tou ouranou apo tou theou_. **The city descends; the saints do not ascend permanently.** The New Jerusalem comes to earth; it is the dwelling-place on the new earth. **The redeemed live with God on the renewed planet** — not in a disembodied heavenly state forever but on a new earth, with the city, with God dwelling there.
- "**Prepared as a bride adorned for her husband**" — Greek _hētoimasmenēn hōs nymphēn kekosmēmenēn tō andri autēs_. **_Hētoimasmenēn_** — perfect passive participle of _hetoimazō_ (to prepare) — already prepared, fully ready. **Compare Rev 19:7** ("his wife hath made herself ready" — _hē gynē autou hētoimasen heautēn_). **The bride who made herself ready in Rev 19 is the city descending in Rev 21.** **The bride and the city are one.** Compare also **Jn 14:2-3** (Christ — "I go to prepare a place for you... I will come again, and receive you unto myself"). **The "place prepared" of Christ's promise is the bride-city of Rev 21:2.**

## Rev 21:3 — The Tabernacle of God with Men

> "And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God." (Rev 21:3)

- "**A great voice out of heaven**" — Greek _phōnēs megalēs ek tou thronou_ (some MSS _ek tou ouranou_). **The throne-voice or heaven-voice** — the same authoritative announcement-channel as Rev 16:17, Rev 19:5. **The most consequential announcement of the chapter comes from the highest source.**
- "**Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men**" — Greek _Idou hē skēnē tou theou meta tōn anthrōpōn_. **_Skēnē_** = tabernacle, tent, dwelling-place. **Same root as Jn 1:14** (_eskēnōsen en hēmin_ — "the Word was made flesh, and tabernacled among us"). **Compare Ex 25:8** ("let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell [_shakan_] among them"); **Lev 26:11-12** ("I will set my tabernacle among you... I will walk among you, and will be your God, and ye shall be my people"); **Eze 37:26-28** ("I will set my sanctuary in the midst of them for evermore. My tabernacle also shall be with them"). **The dwelling-with-man motif that began in Eden and ran through every sanctuary is now realized.**
- "**He will dwell with them**" — Greek _kai skēnōsei met' autōn_. **The verb form of _skēnē_** — to tabernacle, to dwell-tent-wise. **Permanent dwelling, not temporary visitation.**
- "**They shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God**" — Greek _kai autoi laoi autou esontai, kai autos ho theos met' autōn estai, autōn theos_. **The covenant formula** in its OT form: "I will be your God, and ye shall be my people" (Lev 26:12; Jer 31:33; Eze 36:28; etc.). **The phrase appears across both Testaments as the covenant's heart.** Now it is realized in its permanent form. **_Autos ho theos_** — God Himself, emphatic; not a representative, not a mediating presence — God in person.

[⟲] **The sanctuary thread reaches its terminus here.** Eden-original (Gen 3:8 — God walks with Adam; the original dwelling). Ex 25:8 (the wilderness tabernacle — "that I may dwell among them"). Lev 16 (the Day of Atonement, the closing scene of the year's sanctuary work). 1 Ki 8:10-11 (the glory fills Solomon's temple). Eze 37:26-28 (the promised eternal sanctuary). Dan 8:11-14 (the heavenly sanctuary cleansed, 2300-day prophecy fulfilled AD 1844). Heb 8-9 (the true tabernacle in heaven, Christ's high-priestly ministry). Jn 1:14 (the Word made flesh tabernacled among us — sanctuary in person). Rev 4-5 (the throne-room with the Lamb in the midst). Rev 11:19 (the temple opened, the ark seen). Rev 15:5-8 (the temple of the tabernacle of testimony opened, Day-of-Atonement closing). **Rev 21:3 — "the tabernacle of God is with men" — closes the thread.** The whole sanctuary system was a 4000-year-long arrow pointing at this verse. **The Eden-arrangement is finally restored; God dwells with His people, face to face, forever.**

## Rev 21:4 — No More Death, Sorrow, Crying, or Pain

> "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away." (Rev 21:4)

- "**God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes**" — Greek _exaleipsei pan dakryon ek tōn ophthalmōn autōn_. **_Exaleipsei_** — wipe out, wipe away, blot out (same verb as Rev 3:5 — Christ "will not blot out his name out of the book of life"; Acts 3:19 — "that your sins may be blotted out"). **God personally** wipes the tear. **Compare Isa 25:8** ("he will swallow up death in victory; and the Lord GOD will wipe away tears from off all faces"); **Rev 7:17** ("the Lamb... shall feed them, and shall lead them unto living fountains of waters: and God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes"). **Rev 7:17 promised it for the great-tribulation company; Rev 21:4 universalizes the promise to all the redeemed.**
- "**No more death**" — Greek _ho thanatos ouk estai eti_. **The direct fulfillment of Rev 20:14** — Death itself was cast into the lake of fire; therefore in Rev 21:4 there is no more death. **Compare 1 Cor 15:26, 54-55** ("the last enemy that shall be destroyed is death... Death is swallowed up in victory"); **Hos 13:14** ("O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction"). **The "no more death" of Rev 21:4 is grounded in the lake-of-fire's destruction of death itself in Rev 20:14.**
- "**Neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain**" — Greek _oute penthos oute kraugē oute ponos_. **Three negations after "no more death":** sorrow (mourning over loss), crying (the cry that issues from sorrow), pain (physical and emotional suffering). **All four — death, sorrow, crying, pain — are gone.**
- "**The former things are passed away**" — Greek _hoti ta prōta apēlthan_. **Same _apēlthan_ root as v. 1's "first heaven and earth passed away."** **The Edenic-curse universe has gone to its end.**

## Rev 21:5 — Behold, I Make All Things New

> "And he that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things new. And he said unto me, Write: for these words are true and faithful." (Rev 21:5)

- "**He that sat upon the throne**" — Greek _ho kathēmenos epi tō thronō_. **The throne-occupant speaks directly.** This is a direct throne-occupant statement; compare the prior throne-voice announcements in Rev 16:17 and Rev 19:5. **The weight of the speaker matches the weight of the announcement.**
- "**Behold, I make all things new**" — Greek _Idou kaina poiō panta_. **_Kaina poiō_** — "I am making new" (present tense, ongoing). **Not _new things I make_** but _all things I make new._ **The new creation is restoration plus intensification, not replacement.** The same created order, redeemed and remade. **Compare Isa 43:19** ("Behold, I will do a new thing; now it shall spring forth"); **2 Cor 5:17** ("if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature [_kainē ktisis_]: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new"). **The personal new-creation of 2 Cor 5:17 issues in the cosmic new-creation of Rev 21:5.**
- "**Write: for these words are true and faithful**" — Greek _Grapson, hoti houtoi hoi logoi pistoi kai alēthinoi eisin_. **Another direct command to John to write** in Revelation (Rev 1:11, 1:19, 14:13, 19:9, 21:5; plus the seven church-letter write commands in Rev 2-3; cf. Rev 10:4's command not to write). **"True and faithful"** — same pair as Rev 19:11 (the Rider "called Faithful and True"); Rev 22:6 ("these sayings are faithful and true"). **The throne-voice's certification anchors the new-creation announcement.**

## Rev 21:6 — It Is Done; Alpha and Omega; the Water of Life

> "And he said unto me, It is done. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end. I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life freely." (Rev 21:6)

- "**It is done**" — Greek _gegonan_ (3rd plural perfect of _ginomai_; some MSS _gegona_, "I am become") — "they have come to pass" / "it has come to pass." **Compare Rev 16:17** (_gegonen_ — "It is done" — same _ginomai_ perfect, spoken from the throne at the seventh vial). **Two "done" announcements:** Rev 16:17 closes the wrath; Rev 21:6 closes the restoration. **Compare also Jn 19:30** (_tetelestai_ — "It is finished" — perfect of _teleō_, the Calvary cry). **Three perfect-tense divine announcements:** _tetelestai_ (the cross-work finished), _gegonen_ (the wrath finished), _gegonan_ (the restoration finished). **Each closes a phase of the great controversy.**
- "**I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end**" — Greek _Egō to Alpha kai to Ō, hē archē kai to telos_. **Compare Rev 1:8** ("I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty"); **Rev 22:13** ("I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last"). **In the KJV/TR text, four Alpha-Omega declarations bracket the book — Rev 1:8, Rev 1:11, Rev 21:6, and Rev 22:13.** **The Person who began creation is the Person who closes it as restored.**
- "**I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life freely**" — Greek _egō tō dipsōnti dōsō ek tēs pēgēs tou hydatos tēs zōēs dōrean_. **_Dōrean_** — freely, gratis, without cost. **Compare Isa 55:1** ("Ho, every one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters... come, buy wine and milk without money and without price"); **Jn 4:14** (Christ to the woman at the well — "whosoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst"); **Jn 7:37-39** ("If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink"); **Rev 22:17** ("And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely"). **The eschatological fulfillment of the gospel's "come and drink" invitation.**

## Rev 21:7 — He That Overcometh Inherits All Things

> "He that overcometh shall inherit all things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my son." (Rev 21:7)

- "**He that overcometh**" — Greek _ho nikōn_. **The same _ho nikōn_ that closed every letter to the seven churches** (Rev 2:7, 11, 17, 26; 3:5, 12, 21). **The promises to the overcomers find their summary realization here.** Each church-letter promised a specific reward (tree of life, no second death, hidden manna, white stone, white raiment, name not blotted out, pillar in the temple, throne to sit on); Rev 21:7 sums them as "all things."
- "**Shall inherit all things**" — Greek _klēronomēsei tauta_ ("shall inherit these things" — referring to all the new-creation realities of vv. 1-6). **Compare Mt 5:5** ("the meek shall inherit the earth"); **Mt 25:34** ("Come, ye blessed of my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world"); **Rom 8:17** ("heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ"); **1 Cor 3:21-23** ("all things are yours... and ye are Christ's; and Christ is God's"). **The overcomer's inheritance is universal, not partial.**
- "**I will be his God, and he shall be my son**" — Greek _kai esomai autō theos kai autos estai moi huios_. **The covenant-formula of v. 3 personalized.** **Compare 2 Sam 7:14** (the Davidic-covenant promise — "I will be his father, and he shall be my son"); **Rom 8:14-17** (the Spirit of adoption — "ye have received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, Abba, Father"); **Heb 1:5** (the Davidic promise applied to Christ). **The Davidic father-son covenant, fulfilled in Christ, now extends to every overcomer.**

## Rev 21:8 — The Eight Categories of the Excluded

> "But the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable, and murderers, and whoremongers, and sorcerers, and idolaters, and all liars, shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death." (Rev 21:8)

- "**The fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable, and murderers, and whoremongers, and sorcerers, and idolaters, and all liars**" — Greek _tois de deilois kai apistois kai ebdelygmenois kai phoneusin kai pornois kai pharmakois kai eidōlolatrais kai pasi tois pseudesin_. **Eight categories.** Listed first: **_deilois_** = fearful, cowardly, faint-hearted. **The first listed is not a moral monster but a coward** — those who feared man more than God, who shrank from confessing Christ, who would not take their stand. **Fear keeps more from the kingdom than active rebellion does.** Compare Rev 22:15's parallel list (dogs, sorcerers, whoremongers, murderers, idolaters, lovers and makers of a lie). **_Pharmakois_** — sorcerers, drug-using-sorcerers (the same root as English "pharmacy"); not pharmacists but those practicing occult/spiritualistic drug-mediated sorcery (cf. Rev 9:21, 18:23 — Babylon's _pharmakeia_).
- "**Shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone**" — Greek _hē meris autōn en tē limnē tē kaiomenē pyri kai theiō_. **Same lake of fire** as Rev 19:20, Rev 20:10, Rev 20:14-15. **The brimstone (sulfur)** echoes Sodom's destruction (Gen 19:24; Lk 17:29; 2 Pet 2:6 — "an ensample unto those that after should live ungodly"). **The Sodom-precedent frames the lake's nature as consuming, not preserving.**
- "**Which is the second death**" — Greek _ho estin ho thanatos ho deuteros_. **Same gloss as Rev 20:14** ("the lake of fire... is the second death"). **A "death" is a cessation, not a continuation.** Conditional immortality applies. **The eight categories perish in the second death** — final extinction, not eternal conscious torment. **Compare Mal 4:1-3** (the wicked become ashes "neither root nor branch"; "ye shall tread down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet").

## Rev 21:9 — The Vial-Angel Shows the Bride

> "And there came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last plagues, and talked with me, saying, Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife." (Rev 21:9)

- "**One of the seven angels which had the seven vials**" — Greek _heis ek tōn hepta angelōn tōn echontōn tas hepta phialas_. **The same vial-angels who poured out the seven last plagues** (Rev 15:7, Rev 16:1-21). **Compare Rev 17:1** ("there came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, and talked with me, saying, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore"). **The same kind of angel that showed John the harlot now shows him the bride.** Same opening formula ("Come hither; I will shew thee") frames both. **Rev 17 and Rev 21 are deliberate frame-pairs:** the harlot exposed in Rev 17, the bride displayed in Rev 21. **Two cities; two women; two destinies.**
- "**The bride, the Lamb's wife**" — Greek _tēn nymphēn tēn gynaika tou arniou_. **_Nymphēn_** (bride) and _gynaika_ (wife) — both terms in one phrase. **The marriage announced in Rev 19:7 is consummated in Rev 21:9.** The bride who made herself ready, who was granted fine linen, who was called to the marriage supper — is now "the Lamb's wife." **The Lamb-bride / dragon-harlot polarity** runs through Rev 12-22 as the structuring contrast.

## Rev 21:10 — Carried Away to See the Holy Jerusalem

> "And he carried me away in the spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God," (Rev 21:10)

- "**Carried me away in the spirit**" — Greek _apēnegken me en pneumati_. **Compare Rev 17:3** ("so he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness" — to see the harlot); **Eze 40:1-2** ("the hand of the LORD was upon me, and brought me thither... and set me upon a very high mountain, by which was as the frame of a city"). **John's vision parallels Ezekiel's temple-vision** of Eze 40-48 — both prophets carried to a high mountain to see a measured city/temple.
- "**A great and high mountain**" — Greek _epi oros mega kai hypsēlon_. **Compare Eze 40:2** (Ezekiel set on "a very high mountain" to see the city); **Isa 2:2-3** ("the mountain of the LORD's house shall be established in the top of the mountains... all nations shall flow unto it"). **The mountain-of-the-Lord's-house motif fulfilled.**
- "**That great city, the holy Jerusalem, descending out of heaven from God**" — Greek _tēn polin tēn hagian Ierousalēm katabainousan ek tou ouranou apo tou theou_. **Same descent-language as v. 2.** **Compare Heb 11:10** (Abraham "looked for a city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God") and **Heb 13:14** ("for here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come"). **The patriarchs' hoped-for city is shown.**

## Rev 21:11 — Having the Glory of God; Like a Jasper Stone

> "Having the glory of God: and her light was like unto a stone most precious, even like a jasper stone, clear as crystal;" (Rev 21:11)

- "**Having the glory of God**" — Greek _echousan tēn doxan tou theou_. **_Doxa tou theou_** = the glory of God (the _shekinah_ presence). **Compare Eze 43:2** ("the glory of the God of Israel came from the way of the east... and the earth shined with his glory"); **Hab 2:14** ("the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD"); **Jn 1:14** ("we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father"). **The city's defining illumination is God's own glory.**
- "**Her light was like unto a stone most precious, even like a jasper stone, clear as crystal**" — Greek _ho phōstēr autēs homoios lithō timiōtatō, hōs lithō iaspidi krystallizonti_. **_Phōstēr_** = luminary, light-source. **_Iaspis_** — jasper (in modern terminology this stone may correspond to a clear gemstone, perhaps diamond-like, given "clear as crystal" — though the exact gem identification is debated; ancient _iaspis_ was sometimes a translucent stone). **Compare Rev 4:3** (the throne-occupant — "he that sat was to look upon like a jasper and a sardine stone"). **The city's light is the same gem-quality as the throne's appearance** — visual continuity between throne and city.

## Rev 21:12-14 — Twelve Gates, Twelve Foundations

> "And had a wall great and high, and had twelve gates, and at the gates twelve angels, and names written thereon, which are the names of the twelve tribes of the children of Israel: On the east three gates; on the north three gates; on the south three gates; and on the west three gates. And the wall of the city had twelve foundations, and in them the names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb." (Rev 21:12-14)

- "**A wall great and high**" — Greek _teichos mega kai hypsēlon_. **Walls signify protection, definition, security** — but in this city they will not exclude (gates always open, v. 25); they define the holy space.
- "**Twelve gates, and at the gates twelve angels**" — Greek _pylōnas dōdeka, kai epi tois pylōsin angelous dōdeka_. **Twelve angel-gatekeepers.** **Compare 1 Chr 9:17-27** (the temple gatekeepers). **The angels who served the redeemed (Heb 1:14) are still serving them** at the gates of the city.
- "**The names of the twelve tribes of the children of Israel**" — Greek _onomata tōn dōdeka phylōn huiōn Israēl_. **The OT covenant people named on the gates.** **Compare Eze 48:30-35** (Ezekiel's eschatological city with twelve gates named for the tribes — three on each side, exactly as Rev 21:13). **John's pattern is Ezekiel's pattern.**
- "**Three gates on each side**" — Greek _apo anatolēs pylōnes treis... apo borra... apo notou... apo dysmōn_. **Same twelve-gates-on-four-sides as Eze 48:31-34** and **Num 2** (the twelve tribes camped three-per-side around the wilderness tabernacle). **The wilderness camp's geometry is the New Jerusalem's geometry.** The covenant people, originally arranged three-per-side around the tabernacle, are now arranged three-per-side at the city's gates.
- "**Twelve foundations, and in them the names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb**" — Greek _themelious dōdeka, kai ep' autōn dōdeka onomata tōn dōdeka apostolōn tou arniou_. **The NT covenant's apostolic-witness foundation.** **Compare Eph 2:20** ("ye are... built upon the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Jesus Christ himself being the chief corner stone"); **Mt 19:28** (Christ to the twelve — "ye also shall sit upon twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel"). **The city is built on the whole Bible** — twelve tribes (OT) on the gates, twelve apostles (NT) on the foundations. **No covenant-people displacement; one continuous people of God.**

[CTRF] **The bride-city of Rev 21 vs. the harlot-city of Rev 17 — the chapter's defining contrast.** Rev 17:1 and Rev 21:9 begin with the same vial-angel formula ("Come hither, I will shew thee..."). Rev 17:3 and Rev 21:10 use the same "carried me away in the spirit" language. Rev 17 places the harlot in the wilderness (_eis erēmon_); Rev 21 places the bride on a great high mountain. The harlot wears purple, scarlet, and gold (Rev 17:4); the bride is built of jasper, gold, and twelve precious stones. The harlot is named "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT" (Rev 17:5); the bride is named "the holy city, new Jerusalem" (Rev 21:2). The harlot rides a beast (Rev 17:3, 7); the bride descends from God out of heaven (Rev 21:2, 10). The harlot is drunken with the blood of the saints (Rev 17:6); the bride's gates bear the names of the twelve tribes who suffered for her (Rev 21:12). The harlot sits on many waters (Rev 17:1, 15); the bride has the river of life flowing through her (Rev 22:1). The harlot's destiny is the lake of fire (Rev 17:16, 18:8, 19:3); the bride's destiny is to have God dwelling with her forever (Rev 21:3, 22:3-5). **Two women, two cities, two destinies, deliberately frame-paired.** The vial-angel shows John both — the same angel, the same opening formula, the same kind of vision — so the contrast is unmistakable. **Rev 17 and Rev 21 are the book's structural bookends on the question "which city, which woman, which destiny."**

## Rev 21:15-17 — The Measurement of the City

> "And he that talked with me had a golden reed to measure the city, and the gates thereof, and the wall thereof. And the city lieth foursquare, and the length is as large as the breadth: and he measured the city with the reed, twelve thousand furlongs. The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal. And he measured the wall thereof, an hundred and forty and four cubits, according to the measure of a man, that is, of the angel." (Rev 21:15-17)

- "**A golden reed to measure**" — Greek _kalamon chrysoun, hina metrēsē tēn polin_. **Compare Eze 40:3, 5** (Ezekiel's measuring-angel with "a measuring reed"); **Rev 11:1** (John told to "rise, and measure the temple of God, and the altar"); **Zech 2:1-2** ("a man with a measuring line in his hand... To measure Jerusalem"). **The measuring-the-holy-place motif.** Measuring signifies marking-out for preservation/possession.
- "**The city lieth foursquare**" — Greek _hē polis tetragōnos keitai_. **Foursquare** — perfectly square in plan. **The Most Holy Place was a cube** (1 Ki 6:20 — 20 cubits each direction); the city's cube-shape extends the Most Holy Place to architectural scale.
- "**Twelve thousand furlongs**" — Greek _stadiōn dōdeka chiliadōn_. **_Stadion_** = furlong (about 600 feet / 185 meters). **12,000 furlongs ≈ 1,400 miles / 2,200 km** per side. **A volume of approximately 2.7 billion cubic miles.** The number 12,000 echoes 12 × 1000 — the same factor that produces the 144,000 of Rev 7:4 (12 × 12 × 1000). **Twelve as covenant-completeness amplified by 1000.**
- "**The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal**" — Greek _to mēkos kai to platos kai to hypsos autēs isa estin_. **A cube.** **Compare 1 Ki 6:20** (Solomon's Most Holy Place — "twenty cubits in length, and twenty cubits in breadth, and twenty cubits in the height thereof"). **The whole city is the Most Holy Place.** Not a city _with_ a Most Holy Place inside, but a city that _is_ a Most Holy Place — every cubic foot is sanctuary-space, every street is the divine-presence corridor.
- "**The wall thereof, an hundred and forty and four cubits**" — Greek _hekaton tesserakonta tessarōn pēchōn_. **144 cubits ≈ 216 feet / 66 meters** thick (or in some readings, tall). **144 = 12 × 12** — the same factor in 144,000 (Rev 7:4, 14:1). **The wall's measurement and the sealed-company's measurement use the same numerology.** **The wall is the redeemed people in stone-symbol form.**
- "**According to the measure of a man, that is, of the angel**" — Greek _metron anthrōpou, ho estin angelou_. **A note on the unit:** the cubit used is the human cubit, the same unit the angel is using. **The measurement is intelligible to humans, not in some special angelic unit.**

## Rev 21:18-21 — Materials: Jasper, Gold, Precious Stones, Pearls

> "And the building of the wall of it was of jasper: and the city was pure gold, like unto clear glass. And the foundations of the wall of the city were garnished with all manner of precious stones. The first foundation was jasper; the second, sapphire; the third, a chalcedony; the fourth, an emerald; The fifth, sardonyx; the sixth, sardius; the seventh, chrysolite; the eighth, beryl; the ninth, a topaz; the tenth, a chrysoprasus; the eleventh, a jacinth; the twelfth, an amethyst. And the twelve gates were twelve pearls; every several gate was of one pearl: and the street of the city was pure gold, as it were transparent glass." (Rev 21:18-21)

- "**The wall of it was of jasper**" — Greek _hē endōmēsis tou teichous autēs iaspis_. **The same jasper that lit the city in v. 11** — the wall participates in the city's gem-illumination.
- "**The city was pure gold, like unto clear glass**" — Greek _hē polis chrysion katharon homoion hyalō katharō_. **Gold so pure it is transparent.** Earthly gold is opaque; the city's gold is clarified to glass-like transparency. **A material that combines gold's preciousness with glass's clarity.**
- "**The foundations garnished with all manner of precious stones**" — twelve foundations, each a different stone:
  1. **Jasper** (_iaspis_) — gem-stone, possibly chalcedony or diamond-like quartz
  2. **Sapphire** (_sappheiros_) — blue gem (cf. Ex 24:10 — under God's feet "as it were a paved work of a sapphire stone")
  3. **Chalcedony** (_chalkēdōn_) — translucent gemstone, often blue or grey
  4. **Emerald** (_smaragdos_) — green gem (cf. Rev 4:3 — the rainbow round the throne "in sight like unto an emerald")
  5. **Sardonyx** (_sardonyx_) — banded reddish-white gem
  6. **Sardius** (_sardios_) — red gem, carnelian (cf. Rev 4:3 — throne-occupant like jasper and sardine)
  7. **Chrysolite** (_chrysolithos_) — yellow-green gem
  8. **Beryl** (_bēryllos_) — pale green or blue-green gem
  9. **Topaz** (_topazion_) — yellow gem
  10. **Chrysoprase** (_chrysoprasos_) — apple-green gem
  11. **Jacinth** (_hyakinthos_) — blue/purple gem
  12. **Amethyst** (_amethystos_) — purple gem
      **The list overlaps substantially with the high priest's breastplate** of Ex 28:17-20 (which had jasper, sapphire, emerald, sardius, topaz, amethyst, beryl among its twelve, with other stones differing). **The breastplate carried the names of the twelve tribes over the high priest's heart;** the city's foundations now display the same precious-stone palette built into the eternal architecture. **The people God's heart carried through history are now built into the city's foundations.**
- "**The twelve gates were twelve pearls; every several gate was of one pearl**" — Greek _hoi dōdeka pylōnes dōdeka margaritai, ana heis hekastos tōn pylōnōn ēn ex henos margaritou_. **Each gate a single immense pearl.** **No OT precedent for pearl-gates** — pearls are a NT-era trade-good (cf. Mt 13:45-46 — the parable of the pearl of great price; Mt 7:6 — "neither cast ye your pearls before swine"). **The "pearl of great price" of Mt 13:46 — for which a man sells all that he has — now forms the city's gates.**
- "**The street of the city was pure gold, as it were transparent glass**" — same gold-as-glass material as the city itself. **The walking-surface is the same precious material as the building-material.** Earthly cities have streets of dirt and stone; this city's streets are of clarified gold.

## Rev 21:22 — No Temple in the City

> "And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it." (Rev 21:22)

- "**I saw no temple therein**" — Greek _kai naon ouk eidon en autē_. **_Naos_** = temple, particularly the inner sanctuary (the holy and most-holy compartments), distinct from _hieron_ (the whole temple complex). **The most surprising statement in the chapter for any first-century Jewish reader.** Every previous covenant-age had a temple; the new earth has none.
- "**The Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it**" — Greek _ho gar kyrios ho theos ho pantokratōr naos autēs estin, kai to arnion_. **The temple-purpose is fulfilled.** Every previous sanctuary was a structure to bring God and humanity into the same space. **In Rev 21 the goal — God-with-people — is achieved without the structure.** The Lord God Almighty and the Lamb _are_ the sanctuary; the people dwell directly in their presence. **Compare Jn 2:19-21** (Christ — "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up... he spake of the temple of his body"); **Jn 4:21-24** (Christ to the woman at the well — "the hour cometh, when ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem, worship the Father... the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth"). **Rev 21:22 is the eschatological fulfillment of Christ's "neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem" — worship is now directly God-ward, structure-free.**

[→] **Rev 21:22 closes the sanctuary-thread eschatologically.** Eden — God dwells with man, no structure needed (Gen 3:8). Wilderness tabernacle — God dwells in a tent (Ex 25:8). Solomon's temple — God dwells in stone (1 Ki 6). Christ — God dwells in flesh (Jn 1:14). Heavenly sanctuary — Christ ministers as high priest (Heb 8-9). Day of Atonement closure — the heavenly Most Holy Place's work finished (Rev 11:19; 15:5-8). New Jerusalem — God dwells with His people, no structure needed (Rev 21:22). **The thread bends back to its beginning.** Eden had no temple because God walked with Adam directly; Rev 21 has no temple because God walks with the redeemed directly. **The structures were transitional — necessary while sin separated; unnecessary once sin is removed.** Compare also **Eze 47:1-12** (river from the temple) — the river still flows in Rev 22:1, but from "the throne of God and of the Lamb" rather than from a temple-structure. **The temple-function continues; the temple-building is gone.**

## Rev 21:23-26 — No Sun, No Night; Nations Walking in the Light

> "And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof. And the nations of them which are saved shall walk in the light of it: and the kings of the earth do bring their glory and honour into it. And the gates of it shall not be shut at all by day: for there shall be no night there. And they shall bring the glory and honour of the nations into it." (Rev 21:23-26)

- "**No need of the sun, neither of the moon**" — Greek _ou chreian echei tou hēliou oude tēs selēnēs_. **Compare Isa 60:19-20** ("The sun shall be no more thy light by day; neither for brightness shall the moon give light unto thee: but the LORD shall be unto thee an everlasting light, and thy God thy glory. Thy sun shall no more go down; neither shall thy moon withdraw itself: for the LORD shall be thine everlasting light"). **Direct fulfillment of Isaiah.**
- "**The glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof**" — Greek _hē gar doxa tou theou ephōtisen autēn, kai ho lychnos autēs to arnion_. **_Lychnos_** = lamp; **_lychnia_** is the lampstand. **The Lamb is the lamp through which God's glory shines.** **Compare Jn 1:4-9, 8:12** (Christ as "the light of the world"); **2 Cor 4:6** ("God, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, hath shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ"); **Jn 14:9** (Christ — "he that hath seen me hath seen the Father"). **God's glory is mediated through the Lamb — the same revelatory pattern as in the incarnation, now permanent and unmediated by veil or distance.**
- "**The nations of them which are saved shall walk in the light of it**" — Greek _peripatēsousin ta ethnē dia tou phōtos autēs_. **_Ta ethnē_** = the nations / Gentiles. **The redeemed nations (those whose names are in the book of life from every tribe, kindred, tongue, people — Rev 7:9; Rev 14:6) walk in the city's light.** **Compare Isa 60:3** ("the Gentiles shall come to thy light, and kings to the brightness of thy rising"). **Direct Isa 60 fulfillment.**
- "**The kings of the earth do bring their glory and honour into it**" — Greek _hoi basileis tēs gēs pherousin tēn doxan autōn eis autēn_. **Compare Isa 60:11** ("therefore thy gates shall be open continually... that men may bring unto thee the forces of the Gentiles, and that their kings may be brought"). **The redeemed kings — those who came out of every nation, including political leaders who turned to Christ — bring their honor into the city.** Whatever genuine glory the nations had is preserved and incorporated; what was true and beautiful is not lost.
- "**The gates of it shall not be shut at all by day: for there shall be no night there**" — Greek _hoi pylōnes autēs ou mē kleisthōsin hēmeras, nyx gar ouk estai ekei_. **No closing-time, no night-shift, no danger.** **Gates of an ancient city closed at night for protection; this city has no need.** **Compare Isa 60:11** ("Thy gates shall be open continually; they shall not be shut day nor night"). **Direct Isa 60 fulfillment again.** **The "no night" is permanent** — Rev 22:5 will repeat it ("there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun").

## Rev 21:27 — Nothing Defileth Enters; Only the Book of Life

> "And there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life." (Rev 21:27)

- "**There shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth**" — Greek _ou mē eiselthē eis autēn pan koinon_. **_Koinon_** = common, profane, defiling. **The Levitical clean/unclean distinction** (Lev 11; Acts 10:14) reaches its eschatological resolution. **No defilement enters the city.**
- "**Neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie**" — Greek _kai ho poiōn bdelygma kai pseudos_. **_Bdelygma_** = abomination (the same word LXX uses for OT idolatry; also Rev 17:4-5 — the harlot's golden cup "full of abominations"). **_Pseudos_** = a lie, falsehood. **The two great categories of corruption — idolatry and falsehood — are barred.**
- "**But they which are written in the Lamb's book of life**" — Greek _ei mē hoi gegrammenoi en tō bibliō tēs zōēs tou arniou_. **Same book-of-life criterion as Rev 20:15** ("whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire"). **The book of life is the city's entrance-register.** **Compare Rev 13:8** (the worshippers of the beast — "whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb"); **Rev 17:8** (those who wonder after the beast — "whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world"); **Rev 22:19** (any who add or take from this prophecy — "God shall take away his part out of the book of life"). **The same register that decides the lake-of-fire decides the city.** **The Lamb's book** — the Lamb whose blood paid the entrance-fee, whose names were registered through faith in His finished work.

## After the Verses — What Rev 21 Settles

- **The Bible's first three chapters and last two chapters frame one story.** Gen 1-3 records creation, the original dwelling-with-God in Eden, the entrance of sin, the curse, and the cherub-guarded exile from the tree of life. Rev 21-22 records re-creation, the dwelling-with-God in the New Jerusalem, the abolition of sin, the removal of the curse, and the restored access to the tree of life. **What Genesis lost, Revelation restores; what Eden held, the New Jerusalem holds permanently.** The Bible is one book.
- **The bride and the city are one.** Rev 19 announced the marriage; Rev 21:2 sees the bride descending; Rev 21:9-10 has the angel say "I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife" and then shows the city. **The redeemed and their dwelling are inseparable** — the people are the city, and the city is the people. The architecture is the architecture of redeemed humanity.
- **The "tabernacle of God with men" (v. 3) closes the sanctuary thread.** From Eden's face-to-face dwelling through every transitional sanctuary (wilderness tabernacle, Solomon's temple, Christ's incarnation, the heavenly sanctuary, the Day-of-Atonement closure) the entire arc has been moving toward Rev 21:3. **God dwells with His people again, this time forever, this time without separation.**
- **The cube-city (v. 16) makes the entire dwelling a Most Holy Place.** Solomon's Most Holy Place was a 20-cubit cube reserved for the high priest one day a year. The New Jerusalem is a 12,000-stadia cube where the redeemed live continually. **Every cubic inch is sanctuary-space; every street is throne-corridor.** The exclusivity of the OT Most Holy Place is replaced by the universality of the Most Holy City.
- **The "no temple" (v. 22) is the sanctuary system's fulfillment, not its loss.** Every previous sanctuary was a structure designed to bring God and humanity into the same space. **In Rev 21 the structure is unnecessary because the goal is achieved.** The Lord God Almighty and the Lamb _are_ the temple; the people dwell directly with God. **The temple-purpose continues forever; the temple-architecture is no longer needed.**
- **The bride-city of Rev 21 vs. the harlot-city of Rev 17 — the chapter's structural contrast.** Same vial-angel; same opening formula; same "carried me away in the spirit." Two women, two cities, two destinies, deliberately frame-paired. The harlot rides a beast; the bride descends from God. The harlot is drunken with saints' blood; the bride bears the tribes' names on her gates. The harlot's destiny is the lake of fire; the bride's destiny is God's eternal dwelling. **Rev 17 and Rev 21 are the book's structural bookends on which-city-which-destiny.**
- **The covenant continuity — twelve tribes on the gates, twelve apostles on the foundations.** No replacement-theology displacement of either covenant-people; one continuous people of God built on both Testaments. **The OT covenant people are at the entrance; the NT covenant witness is in the foundation; the redeemed of all ages walk the streets together.**
- **The breastplate-foundations.** The twelve precious stones overlap substantially with the high priest's breastplate (Ex 28:17-20) — jasper, sapphire, emerald, sardius, topaz, amethyst, beryl among others appearing in both lists. **The names the high priest carried over his heart are now built into the city's foundations.** What the priesthood mediated provisionally is now structurally permanent.
- **The pearl-gates.** Each gate a single immense pearl (v. 21). **Compare Mt 13:45-46** — the pearl of great price for which a man sells all. **The gospel's central transaction-image — selling all to gain the one pearl — is now the city's gateway.** Each entrance reminds the redeemed of the pearl-of-great-price exchange.
- **The book-of-life criterion is repeated** (v. 27). Same standard as the great white throne (Rev 20:15). **The book is the register of accepted-grace; only those whose names are there enter.** No other entrance-criterion exists.
- **The eight excluded categories of v. 8 begin with "the fearful."** Not heroic villains — cowards. **Fear of man kept more from the kingdom than active rebellion did.** The first excluded category is the silent who would not take their stand. The closing-crisis test (Sabbath/mark) presses precisely this fear-or-faith line.
- **Conditional immortality re-anchored at v. 8.** The lake of fire is "the second death" — same gloss as Rev 20:14. **Final extinction, not eternal conscious torment.** The brimstone of v. 8 echoes Sodom's destruction (Gen 19:24; 2 Pet 2:6 — "an ensample"). The fire consumes; "for ever and ever" names the verdict's permanence.
- **The "It is done" of v. 6 closes the restoration arc.** Rev 16:17's _gegonen_ closed the wrath; Rev 21:6's _gegonan_ closes the new-creation announcement. **Two divine "done" announcements, one for judgment, one for restoration. Both spoken from the throne.** The cross-cry _tetelestai_ (Jn 19:30) initiated the redemption-work; Rev 16:17's _gegonen_ closed wrath; Rev 21:6's _gegonan_ closes restoration. **Three divine perfect-tense announcements bracket the great-controversy resolution.**

## Key Takeaways

- "A new heaven and a new earth" (v. 1) — _kainos_ = qualitatively new; the first heaven and earth pass away (2 Pet 3:10's fire), and "no more sea" = no more chaos-source
- The holy city, New Jerusalem, descends "as a bride adorned for her husband" (v. 2) — the bride and the city are one; the descent is to earth, not the saints' permanent ascent
- "The tabernacle of God is with men" (v. 3) — the sanctuary thread's terminus; Eden's face-to-face dwelling restored permanently
- God personally wipes every tear (v. 4) — Isa 25:8's promise universally fulfilled; "no more death, sorrow, crying, or pain" because Rev 20:14 destroyed death itself
- "Behold, I make all things new" (v. 5) — _kaina poiō panta_; the third recorded throne-statement (with Rev 1:8, 16:17); restoration plus intensification, not replacement
- "It is done" (v. 6) — _gegonan_ closes the restoration; companion to Rev 16:17's _gegonen_ closing the wrath; both spoken from the throne
- Alpha and Omega (v. 6) — one of four Alpha-Omega declarations in the KJV/TR text bracketing the book (Rev 1:8, 1:11, 21:6, 22:13)
- The water of life given freely (v. 6) — eschatological fulfillment of Isa 55:1, Jn 4:14, Jn 7:37-39
- The overcomer inherits all things (v. 7) — _ho nikōn_ from every church-letter sums his rewards; "I will be his God, and he shall be my son" — Davidic-covenant adoption
- Eight excluded categories (v. 8) begin with "the fearful" — coward-fear keeps more out than active rebellion; the lake = the second death; conditional immortality
- Vial-angel from Rev 17 frames the bride-vision (v. 9) — same opening formula, deliberate structural contrast: harlot-city in Rev 17, bride-city in Rev 21
- Twelve gates (twelve tribes — OT) and twelve foundations (twelve apostles — NT) — one continuous covenant-people; no replacement theology
- The city is a 12,000-furlong cube (v. 16) — the Most Holy Place expanded to architectural scale; the whole city is sanctuary-space
- The wall is 144 cubits (v. 17) — same 12 × 12 numerology as the 144,000 sealed
- Twelve precious stones (vv. 19-20) overlap substantially with the high priest's breastplate (Ex 28:17-20) — the names God's heart carried are now built into the city's foundations
- Twelve pearl-gates (v. 21) — each a single pearl; pearl-of-great-price (Mt 13:45-46) at every entrance
- "I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it" (v. 22) — sanctuary-purpose fulfilled; structure no longer needed
- "No need of sun, neither of moon" (v. 23) — direct Isa 60:19-20 fulfillment; the glory of God lights the city; the Lamb is the lamp
- Gates never shut, no night (vv. 25-26) — Isa 60:11 fulfilled; the redeemed nations walk in the light; kings bring their glory and honor in
- Only those in the Lamb's book of life enter (v. 27) — same criterion as Rev 20:15

## Next

Rev 22 opens within the city — a pure river of water of life flowing out of the throne of God and of the Lamb, clear as crystal. On either side of the river stands the tree of life, bearing twelve manner of fruits and yielding her fruit every month, the leaves for the healing of the nations. "There shall be no more curse." The throne of God and of the Lamb is in the city; His servants serve Him; they see His face; His name is in their foreheads. There is no night; God lights them; they reign forever. Christ speaks: "Behold, I come quickly." The book closes with the Spirit and the bride saying "Come"; with the warning not to add or take from the prophecy; with Christ's last spoken words in Scripture — "Surely I come quickly" — and humanity's last recorded reply — "Even so, come, Lord Jesus." The Bible ends in a garden-city, with grace.
