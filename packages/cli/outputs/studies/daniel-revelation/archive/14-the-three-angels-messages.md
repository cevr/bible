# Study 14: The Three Angels' Messages — Revelation 14

**The beasts have been identified. The dragon has gone to war against the remnant. Now heaven responds. John sees the 144,000 standing with the Lamb on Mount Zion, singing a song no one else can learn. Then three angels fly through the midst of heaven, each carrying a message to "every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people." The first: the everlasting gospel, the hour of judgment is come. The second: "Babylon is fallen." The third: the most solemn warning in Scripture — against receiving the mark of the beast. The chapter closes with the harvest: Christ on a cloud with a sickle, two harvests, wheat gathered and grape-clusters pressed in the great winepress of God's wrath. Rev 14 is God's final message to the earth. It is also Adventism's mission statement in its clearest form — the reason this denomination exists.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 14 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 13:14-17 (the mark, for the contrast)
- Exodus 20:8-11 (the fourth commandment)

---

## The 144,000 on Mount Zion

Rev 14 opens with an answer to Rev 13's mark.

- **The scene** (Rev 14:1-5)
  - "I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads"
  - "I heard a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of a great thunder: and I heard the voice of harpers harping with their harps"
  - "They sung as it were a new song before the throne... and no man could learn that song but the hundred and forty and four thousand"
  - "These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are virgins" — spiritual fidelity, not ritual celibacy; the same image Paul uses for the church in 2 Cor 11:2
  - "These are they which follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth"
  - "These were redeemed from among men, being the firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb"
  - "In their mouth was found no guile: for they are without fault before the throne of God"

- **Father's name in their foreheads** (Rev 14:1)
  - The answer to Rev 13:16-17's mark. Where the beast places its number, God places _His own name_
  - Compare Rev 22:4 — "His name shall be in their foreheads" — the sealing that began in Rev 7:3 has now fully written God's name on His people
  - `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread:** Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. Rev 14:1 is link four

- **"Mount Sion"** — not literal earthly Jerusalem but the heavenly Zion of Heb 12:22 ("ye are come unto mount Sion... the heavenly Jerusalem"). The 144,000 stand with the Lamb in the place of ultimate victory — the scene is proleptic, showing where their sealing leads

[DYK🔎] Rev 14:1's scene is the structural answer to Rev 13's number. Rev 13:18 closed with 666 — the number of a man (Study 13). Rev 14:1 opens with 144,000 — sealed (Rev 7:3), undefiled (Rev 14:4), without fault before God's throne (Rev 14:5), the Father's name in their foreheads. Where Rev 13 ended in counterfeit worship under threat, Rev 14 begins in true worship that no threat could shake. The contrast is not subtle. The chapter break is the punchline.

- [→] Rev 14:1-5 is positioned as the answer to Rev 13's threat before the three angels even start speaking. The warning that follows is not issued in a vacuum; it is issued with the victory already visible.

## The First Angel — The Everlasting Gospel and the Hour of Judgment

Rev 14:6-7 is the everlasting gospel, freshly proclaimed for the final crisis.

- **The text** (Rev 14:6-7)
  - "I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people"
  - "Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters"

- **Four elements in this first message:**
  1. **The everlasting gospel**
     - The gospel that saves in Rev 14 is the same gospel Abraham believed (Gal 3:8), the same gospel Paul preached (Gal 1:8-9) — grace by faith in Christ alone. Not a new message
     - "Everlasting" — this is not a new edition of the gospel. It is the old gospel restated to a world that has forgotten it

  2. **"To every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people"**
     - The worldwide scope. Rev 10:11 had commissioned John — "thou must prophesy again before many peoples, and nations, and tongues, and kings" (Study 11). Rev 14:6's first angel carries the same global formula forward. What John was told to do, the angel does

  3. **"Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come"**
     - _The hour of his judgment is come_ — present-tense. The judgment has _begun_. This is Dan 7:9-14's courtroom (Study 2) — the investigative judgment that opened at the end of the 2300 days in 1844 (Study 3)
     - `[⟲]` **Judgment thread:** Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12. Rev 14:6-7 is link four, explicitly announcing the judgment now in session
     - The first angel's message is Revelation's explicit proclamation that the investigative judgment has begun and that humanity must respond

  4. **"Worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters"**
     - The language deliberately echoes the fourth commandment: "For in six days the LORD _made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is_" (Ex 20:11). Four of Rev 14:7's creation terms (made, heaven, earth, sea) match the commandment's; Rev replaces "all that in them is" with "the fountains of waters" — an allusion, not a word-for-word quotation
     - The first angel's call to worship the Creator aligns, in the historicist/SDA reading, with the Sabbath (Ex 20:8-11) — the commandment that _identifies_ God as Creator by grounding worship in His creation
     - This is why the Advent movement recovered the seventh-day Sabbath: the first angel's message carries the Sabbath commandment's creation-signature into the final hour
     - The issue in the final crisis is not doctrinal preference; it is the question Rev 14 puts to everyone on earth: _Who made you_? — and therefore, _Whose day will you keep_?

- [→] The first angel's message is the complete final-generation gospel in one sentence: the everlasting gospel (salvation by faith in Christ alone), the judgment now in session, and the summons to worship the Creator by keeping His day. Adventism's mission is to carry that specific message to the world.

## The Second Angel — Babylon Fallen

The first angel announces the gospel. The second angel exposes the counterfeit.

- **The text** (Rev 14:8)
  - "There followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication"

- **Babylon in Revelation**
  - _Babylon_ in the Bible: the tower of rebellion (Gen 11), the city that destroyed Solomon's temple (2 Kings 24-25), the place of Israel's captivity (Jer 29), the symbol of every system that stands against God's city (Psa 137)
  - _Babylon_ in Revelation: introduced here (Rev 14:8); developed in Rev 17's great harlot and Rev 18's detailed fall; final in Rev 19's marriage-supper announcement of her destruction
  - Rev 17:5 and 18:2 will detail Babylon's full identification. Rev 14:8 gives the summary verdict — _she has fallen_
  - `[⟲]` **Babylon thread:** Dan 1-2 (literal Babylon) → Dan 4-5 (Nebuchadnezzar humbled; Belshazzar's fall) → Rev 14:8 (fall announced — this link) → Rev 16:19 (cup of wrath) → Rev 17-18 (spiritual Babylon fully exposed and destroyed) → Rev 19:1-3 (heaven's verdict). Rev 14:8 is the thread's announcement link — what the later links will detail and execute in full

- **The counterpart structure**
  - Rev 12's _woman clothed with the sun_ = God's faithful people
  - Rev 17's _great harlot_ = the counterfeit religious system (fallen Babylon)
  - Two women, two cities, two systems. Rev 14's second angel names the fallen one
  - Historicist reading: Babylon in Rev represents the full apostate system — the papal system (the "first beast" of Rev 13), united with apostate Protestantism (the "image of the beast" the second beast will fabricate — Rev 13:14-15), united with spiritism and false worship generally
  - Rev 18's call — "Come out of her, my people" (18:4) — shows that God has many faithful children still _inside_ the Babylonian system at the moment of its fall; the call is to come out before the final judgment falls on the system

- **"Is fallen, is fallen"** — the double declaration
  - A double announcement in biblical rhetoric = certainty and completeness (Gen 41:32; Jer 22:29)
  - Some historicist interpreters read the doubling as indicating two stages of Babylon's fall:
    - _First fall_: the moral/spiritual fall — the mixed system loses its spiritual integrity long before it is politically judged. Historically, this corresponds to the progressive rejection of the clear call to Reformation gains (post-Reformation decline into formalism, then into the modern "ecumenical" mix of truth and error)
    - _Second fall_: the final public judgment, described in Rev 18

- **"Made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication"**
  - _Wine_ in Revelation = a teaching/doctrine that intoxicates those who drink it
  - _Fornication_ = illicit union between a religious system and political power (the woman of Rev 17 rides the political beast)
  - _Wrath of her fornication_ = the accumulating judgment due because of this illicit relationship — the same judgment her wine has spread to the nations

- [→] The second angel names what the first angel is rescuing people from. The call to worship the Creator (first angel) is accompanied by the warning: the system that has counterfeited true worship is under judgment. Come out of her.

## The Third Angel — The Mark of the Beast Warning

The third angel's message is the most solemn end-time warning in Scripture — the line beyond which no compromise is possible.

- **The text** (Rev 14:9-11)
  - "The third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand"
  - "The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation"
  - "And he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb"
  - "And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name"

- **The warning's structure**
  - _Three conditions triggering the warning_: worship the beast, worship his image, receive his mark
  - _Three descriptors of the consequence_: drink of God's wrath, be tormented in the presence of the Lamb and angels, the smoke of the torment rises "for ever and ever"
  - The triple structure mirrors the three angels: this is the angel _most_ intensely developed

- **"Torment for ever and ever"**
  - Jude 7 describes Sodom and Gomorrha as "an example, suffering the vengeance of eternal fire" — the cities are _not_ still burning. The fire's _result_ is eternal (the destruction is permanent), but the fire itself was not everlasting. That same permanence-of-outcome logic applies to Rev 14:11's "for ever and ever" (_eis aiōnas aiōnōn_, unto ages of ages)
  - The smoke "ascendeth up for ever and ever" (Rev 14:11) parallels Isa 34:10's description of Edom's judgment — "the smoke thereof shall go up for ever" — though Edom was not still smoking when Isaiah's readers looked
  - Consistent with Mal 4:1-3 ("the wicked... shall be ashes under the soles of your feet") and Rev 20:9 ("fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them"), the _eternal_ is the consequence's permanence, not its experienced duration
  - Way Home Study 14 already developed the full case against eternal conscious torment; Rev 14:11 fits that broader biblical frame
  - What is NOT being said: that the wicked burn forever in conscious agony. What IS being said: the judgment is permanent, final, and public. The smoke "ascends" as visible witness to the judgment's reality

- **"Here is the patience of the saints"** (Rev 14:12)
  - "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus"
  - _Patience_ (Greek _hupomonē_) = endurance, bearing up under pressure; the saints' mark is perseverance in the final crisis
  - _Keep the commandments of God_ — the same language Rev 12:17 used of the remnant
  - _Faith of Jesus_ — can mean "faith _in_ Jesus" or "the faith _Jesus had_" (Gal 2:16's _pistis Iēsou Christou_ has the same ambiguity); in context, Christ's own covenant-keeping faithfulness and the saints' faith directed toward Him are both present
  - [→] This verse answers both cosmic-trial charges (Way Home Study 1): the charge that "the law cannot be kept" is answered in the saints' patience and commandment-keeping; the charge that "they only obey for reward" is answered in the saints' faith/loyalty — staying faithful under the beast's pressure when reward is not the motivation. Grace saves and the saved love God enough to keep His commandments under fire. One reality, two faces.
  - [IMG] Rev 14:12 is the verse where the image-restoration thread of this series reaches its functional completion. The story began in Gen 1:26-27 with God's image stamped on man, was lost in Gen 5:3 (Adam begetting in his own likeness), was rewritten in words at Sinai (Ex 20), was rewritten in flesh at Bethlehem (Col 1:15, Heb 1:3), and is now rewritten on the heart by the Spirit (2 Cor 3:18, Rom 8:29). Rev 14:12 names the finished product: "they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus" — the law-as-image-in-words and the Lamb-as-image-in-flesh, both alive in one people. The image fully formed is not a feeling, a profession, or a doctrinal score. It is a life that endures the beast's pressure without breaking faith. Rev 22:4's "his name in their foreheads" is what Rev 14:12 has already begun to write.

- [→] Rev 14:12 is one of the most concentrated verses in Scripture about the end-time church. It is the verse every generation of Adventists has memorized — not as a proof-text, but as a self-description. The Sabbath-keeping, Jesus-trusting, commandment-obeying, gospel-believing remnant.

## The Three Angels as One Message

- **Not sequential, but cumulative**
  - The first angel does not finish his work before the second starts; all three are still flying as Rev 14 closes
  - The messages are layered: the gospel (first angel) is the ground; the Babylon warning (second angel) is the call to leave the counterfeit; the mark-of-the-beast warning (third angel) is the final line beyond which no compromise is possible
  - Together, they are one integrated message to the last generation

- **The three angels' global trajectory**
  - Rev 14 says the angels are preaching to "every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people." This is prophecy of what _will_ happen, not only what _has_ happened
  - The Advent movement's global mission — begun under Miller, expanded by the Adventist pioneers, accelerated in the 20th century — is understood by the SDA as the historical instrument carrying this message. Today, the Seventh-day Adventist Church operates in more than 200 countries; the first angel's global scope is being progressively fulfilled

- [→] Rev 14 is not describing three angels taking turns. It is describing three messages sounding together, rising in intensity, carrying the final gospel to every people on earth. If you are reading this and these messages are new to you — the gospel, the judgment now in session, the call to worship the Creator on His appointed day, the warning against the beast's mark — you are hearing what Rev 14 said you would hear.

[DYK🔎] The three angels' messages have a precise Old Testament prototype: Daniel 3. Nebuchadnezzar erects a state-mandated image (Dan 3:1, dimensions 60 × 6 = three sixes) and orchestrates universal worship under penalty of death (Dan 3:4-6) — exactly what the second beast does in Rev 13:14-15. The three friends refuse, and the structural parallel runs four-deep: Hananiah → first angel; Mishael → second angel; Azariah → third angel; the fourth figure in the furnace ("the form of the fourth is like the Son of God," Dan 3:25) → Rev 18:1's fourth angel ("Come out of her, my people"), the final call before the city falls. The mapping is structural (three faithful + one Christ-figure on the OT side; three angels + one fourth-angel call on the NT side), not based on the friends' name meanings. The Dan 3 scene is the Rev 14 scene compressed into a single fiery hour. The friends did not break ranks; they walked unburned because the One who matched their faithfulness was in the fire with them.

## The Harvest — Rev 14:14-20

The chapter closes with the result of the three angels' work: the harvest of the earth.

- **The Son of Man on the cloud** (Rev 14:14-16)
  - "Behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown [_stephanos_], and in his hand a sharp sickle"
  - "Another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud, Thrust in thy sickle, and reap: for the time is come for thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is ripe"
  - "He that sat on the cloud thrust in his sickle on the earth; and the earth was reaped"
  - Historicist reading: this is the Second Coming harvest of the righteous (Matt 13:39 — "the harvest is the end of the world... the reapers are the angels"; Matt 24:30 — "they shall see the Son of man coming in the clouds")

- **The vintage of wrath** (Rev 14:17-20)
  - "Another angel came out of the temple which is in heaven, he also having a sharp sickle"
  - Another angel "cried with a loud cry to him that had the sharp sickle, saying, Thrust in thy sharp sickle, and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth; for her grapes are fully ripe"
  - "The angel thrust in his sickle into the earth, and gathered the vine of the earth, and cast it into the great winepress of the wrath of God"
  - "The winepress was trodden without the city, and blood came out of the winepress, even unto the horse bridles, by the space of a thousand and six hundred furlongs"
  - The vintage harvest is the _second_ harvest — the destruction of the wicked after the righteous are gathered
  - "Without the city" = outside the holy city, corresponding to where judgment falls after the New Jerusalem descends (Rev 20:9)
  - "A thousand and six hundred furlongs" (~184 miles or ~297 km) is most likely symbolic: 1600 = 4 × 4 × 100, and "four" in Revelation often carries worldwide scope (four winds, four corners of the earth, four living creatures); the distance points to complete worldwide judgment rather than a literal geographic measurement

- [→] Rev 14 moves from message to harvest. The three angels proclaim; the harvest follows. Those who respond to the first angel's gospel are gathered in the first harvest. Those who refuse and receive the beast's mark fall in the second. Rev 15-20 develops what the second harvest will look like in detail.

- [→][IMG] Before turning to the harvest mechanics: notice how Rev 14 is constructed. It opens with 144,000 _already standing_ on Mt. Zion (v. 1) — the image of God fully formed in a people. It closes with two harvests — the difference between the two is whether the image of God or the image of the beast got formed first. Everything between the two scenes is the message that does the forming. The first angel calls; the second exposes the counterfeit; the third pulls the alarm. The work between v. 1 and v. 14 is character formation under fire. The Lamb is not asking you to memorize three angels. He is asking you to let what each angel says actually rewrite the image you walk around with. Whose name is being written in your forehead?

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 14:1-5's 144,000 on Mount Zion answers Rev 13:16-17's mark: God's name on the forehead vs. the beast's mark
- The three angels' messages are Rev's final message to humanity before Christ's return; they are _cumulative_, not sequential
- **First angel** (Rev 14:6-7): the everlasting gospel + "the hour of his judgment is come" + worship the Creator (Ex 20:11 language = the fourth commandment). This is the investigative judgment now in session (Dan 7:9-14, since 1844) + the Sabbath test
- The registered Judgment chain (Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12) reaches its fourth link at Rev 14:6-7
- **Second angel** (Rev 14:8): "Babylon is fallen, is fallen" — the counterfeit system has fallen morally; Rev 17-18 will detail
- **Third angel** (Rev 14:9-11): the warning against the mark of the beast — the most solemn warning in Scripture
- "Torment for ever and ever" in Rev 14:11 = the permanence of the judgment's result (Jude 7's precedent), not eternal conscious burning
- Rev 14:12 = the summary description of the remnant: _patience_ (endurance), _keeping commandments of God_, _faith of Jesus_
- The harvest (Rev 14:14-20) concludes the chapter with the two harvests: righteous gathered to Christ, wicked trodden in the winepress of God's wrath
- Rev 14 is Adventism's mission statement in Rev's own words
