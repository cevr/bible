# Study 17: Probation's Close and the Seven Last Plagues — Revelation 15-16

**There is a silent moment in Revelation that most readers pass over without noticing. Rev 15:8 — "The temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled." The heavenly sanctuary is closed. The High Priest's intercession is complete. Every case has been decided. Every name has been fixed in either the book of life or outside it. "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still... he that is righteous, let him be righteous still" (Rev 22:11). This is the close of probation — the most solemn moment in all of redemptive history. And it happens just before Rev 16's seven plagues fall. The plagues are not arbitrary wrath; they are God's answer to the martyrs' cry "How long?" (Rev 6:10), and they are the first phase of the deliverance promised to the sealed remnant. Study 17 walks the close of probation, the seven last plagues, and the battle of that great day of God Almighty.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 15 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 16 (entire chapter)
- Leviticus 16:17 (the Day of Atonement's empty sanctuary)
- Exodus 7-12 (the plagues of Egypt — the pattern)

## The Close of Probation

Rev 15 opens with what the text calls "another sign in heaven, great and marvellous" — the seven angels with the seven last plagues.

- **The sea of glass and the victors** (Rev 15:2-4)
  - "A sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God"
  - "They sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints"
  - The 144,000 of Rev 14:1-5 are seen again — the sealed remnant, having stood through the final crisis, now standing on the sea of glass (the heavenly crystal floor of Rev 4:6)
  - The song of Moses (Ex 15:1-19) was Israel's song at the Red Sea, after deliverance. The saints sing it _before_ the plagues, because they have already been delivered from the beast's power

[DYK🔎] The Exodus structure of Rev 15-16 is more deliberate than it looks at first reading. In Egypt, the plagues fell first and the song followed: Ex 7-12 (plagues) → Ex 14 (Red Sea crossing) → Ex 15 (the song of Moses). In Revelation, the order inverts: the song is sung first (Rev 15:2-4), then the plagues fall (Rev 16). The reason is that the deliverance has _already_ happened by the time Rev 15 opens — not by the plagues, but by the seal. The sealed remnant is delivered before the plagues come, in the same way Israel's firstborn were delivered before the death-plague struck Egypt: not by a defensive miracle in the moment, but by a mark identifying whose house belonged to God (Ex 12:13 — "when I see the blood, I will pass over you"; cf. Eze 9:4-6 — the mark on the foreheads in Jerusalem's coming destruction). Rev 7:3's seal "in their foreheads" is the antitypical Passover mark. The plagues then become not _their_ judgment but _their_ deliverance — exactly as Egypt's plagues were Israel's deliverance. The song is sung first because the singers already know how it ends.

- **The temple opened and closed** (Rev 15:5-8)
  - "After that I looked, and, behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened"
  - "The seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles"
  - "One of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God"
  - "The temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled"

- **The Day of Atonement parallel**
  - Lev 16:17 — "There shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out" — on the ancient Day of Atonement, while the high priest ministered alone in the Most Holy Place, no one else could enter; the people stood outside in solemn self-examination
  - Rev 15:8 extends this image: when the full Day of Atonement work is complete, the High Priest has finished His mediation, and "no man was able to enter" the sanctuary. The smoke of God's glory fills the temple; His work is done
  - `[⟲]` **Sanctuary thread:** Lev 16 → Dan 8:11-14 → Heb 8-9 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 15:5-8. Rev 15:5-8 is the thread's seventh and final link — the Day of Atonement ministry closes

- **"He that is unjust, let him be unjust still"** (Rev 22:11)
  - Rev 22:11 is the declaration that corresponds to the closing of the temple
  - "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still"
  - Each person's character is now fixed. No further changes. Every case has been decided in the investigative judgment (Study 3, Dan 7:9-14) — the wicked keep their chosen wickedness, the righteous keep their received righteousness
  - This is _not_ God's rejection of sinners. It is God's acknowledgment that their own lifelong choices have now hardened into finality

- **What probation's close does NOT mean**
  - _Not_: the loss of salvation for those already saved. The sealed remnant's salvation is secure; the Spirit's sealing is what makes them able to stand without a Mediator during the plagues
  - _Not_: Christ's love ends for the lost. Christ still loves them; but their choices have become immutable
  - _Not_: the church disappears. The sealed remnant continues on earth as God's living witness — not in a conversion-call sense after the door is shut, but as the vindication of His name before the watching universe, until the Second Coming
  - _Not_: the warning is pointless now. Before probation closes, delay is dangerous but mercy remains; probation's close is not an arbitrary interruption of sincere seeking but God's final ratification of character already chosen over a lifetime

- **What it DOES mean**
  - Final cases are decided. The investigative judgment's results are now registered; the books are closed
  - No further additions to the book of life. No further defections from it
  - The Spirit is withdrawn in His specifically-convicting function; conscience no longer struggles with the unrepentant
  - The plagues now fall on those who have received the beast's mark. The sealed stand through them protected

- [→] Probation's close is not announced on earth. Those still living do not hear a trumpet. They hear silence — the silence of an earth from which the Spirit's convicting work has been withdrawn. The final crisis's first hours look normal. They are not.

## The Plagues and the Exodus Pattern

The seven last plagues are not random judgments. They selectively and deliberately echo the plagues of Egypt (Ex 7-12). God delivers His people again, through the same pattern.

| Egyptian plague (Ex 7-12)     | Rev 16 last plague                                       |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Water to blood (Ex 7:20)      | Sea and rivers to blood (16:3-4)                         |
| Frogs (Ex 8:6)                | Unclean spirits like frogs (16:13)                       |
| Lice / dust (Ex 8:17)         | —                                                        |
| Flies (Ex 8:24)               | —                                                        |
| Pestilence on cattle (Ex 9:3) | —                                                        |
| Boils (Ex 9:9)                | "A noisome and grievous sore" (16:2)                     |
| Hail (Ex 9:23)                | Great hail (16:21)                                       |
| Locusts (Ex 10:13)            | —                                                        |
| Darkness (Ex 10:22)           | Darkness on the beast's throne (16:10)                   |
| Death of firstborn (Ex 12:29) | Final decisive judgment; oppressor's collapse (16:17-21) |

- **The Exodus echo is deliberate**
  - Rev 15:3 — the saints sing "the song of Moses... and the song of the Lamb." Moses' song celebrated deliverance through water; the Lamb's song celebrates deliverance through His blood
  - Pharaoh's hardening-and-plague cycle is being replayed on a global scale. The beast power has hardened; the plagues come; and the remnant is delivered
  - The plagues have no redemptive purpose for those who receive them; they are simply the outworking of rejected mercy. "They repented not" is repeated three times in Rev 16 (vv. 9, 11, 21)

## The Seven Plagues in Detail

- **First plague — sores on those with the mark** (Rev 16:2)
  - "A noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast, and upon them which worshipped his image"
  - The plague falls _only_ on those who received the mark. The sealed are exempt
  - Like Egypt's boils (Ex 9:11) — physical suffering specifically targeting those who sided with the opposing power
  - The mark-receivers have chosen their allegiance; the plague declares their alignment publicly

- **Second plague — sea becomes blood** (Rev 16:3)
  - "The sea became as the blood of a dead man: and every living soul died in the sea"
  - Read through Rev 18's merchants-on-the-sea imagery (Rev 18:17-19), the plague implies global commercial collapse — the trade routes Babylon depended on are killed

- **Third plague — rivers and fountains to blood** (Rev 16:4-7)
  - "The rivers and fountains of waters... they became blood"
  - The angel of the waters responds: "Thou art righteous, O Lord... because thou hast judged thus. For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy"
  - The altar echoes: "True and righteous are thy judgments"
  - The plague's justice: those who shed the blood of saints are given blood. The punishment fits the crime
  - [→] The third plague contains a heavenly defense of the plagues' justice — precisely because human hearts will otherwise call them unjust. God's people can sing "just and true are thy ways" even while the world curses

- **Fourth plague — scorching sun** (Rev 16:8-9)
  - "Power was given unto him to scorch men with fire. And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory"
  - The "blazing sun" contrasts with Rev 7:16 for the sealed — "the sun shall not light on them, nor any heat"
  - The unrepentant _blaspheme_ rather than repent. The plagues do not soften them; they harden them

- **Fifth plague — darkness on the beast's throne** (Rev 16:10-11)
  - "The fifth angel poured out his vial upon the seat [throne] of the beast; and his kingdom was full of darkness"
  - "They gnawed their tongues for pain, and blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores"
  - The beast's political and religious authority is darkened — the center of its power fails
  - Echo of Egypt's ninth plague (Ex 10:21-23): "thick darkness in all the land of Egypt three days... but all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings"

- **Sixth plague — the Euphrates dried up** (Rev 16:12-16)
  - "The sixth angel poured out his vial upon the great river Euphrates; and the water thereof was dried up, that the way of the kings of the east might be prepared"
  - OT background: Daniel 5 records Babylon's fall; Isaiah 44:27-45:1 and Jeremiah 50:38; 51:36 supply the prophetic dry-river / opened-gates imagery, naming Cyrus (Isa 45:1); classical historians (Herodotus, _Histories_ 1.191; Xenophon, _Cyropaedia_ 7.5) preserve the river-diversion tradition. Rev 16:12 picks up this combined imagery
  - Symbolic reading: the Euphrates represented Babylon's supporters (Rev 17:15 — "the waters... are peoples"). The drying up = Babylon's popular support withdrawing; the "kings of the east" = the Christ-led forces of deliverance (cf. Cyrus from the east in Isa 46:11; the Sun of Righteousness of Mal 4:2 as resonance; Rev 7:2's sealing angel "from the east"; Christ's return from the east in Matt 24:27)
  - Verse 15 is inserted: "Behold, I come as a thief. Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments"
  - Verse 16: "He gathered them together into a place called in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon"

- **Seventh plague — "It is done"** (Rev 16:17-21)
  - "The seventh angel poured out his vial into the air; and there came a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done"
  - Theological echo (not a lexical identity): Jesus cried "It is finished" at the cross (John 19:30, _tetelestai_) when the atoning sacrifice was completed; the seventh plague declares "It is done" (_gegonen_) when the judgment-execution is completed. Two Greek verbs, two endings, the same finality: the work God set out to do is complete
  - "There were voices, and thunders, and lightnings; and there was a great earthquake, such as was not since men were upon the earth"
  - "The great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell: and great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath"
  - "Every island fled away, and the mountains were not found"
  - "There fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, every stone about the weight of a talent" (a talent = ~60-100 pounds / 27-45 kg — crushing weight)
  - Echo of Rev 6:14-17 (the sixth seal's final collapse)
  - The seventh plague consummates Babylon's destruction announced in Rev 14:8, detailed in Rev 17-18. The beast system is physically leveled

## Armageddon

Rev 16:14-16 names the final battle.

- **"Spirits of devils, working miracles"** (Rev 16:13-14)
  - "Three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet"
  - The three unclean spirits = a trinitarian false power matching the dragon (Satan), beast (papal Rome), and false prophet (the earth beast's speaking-like-a-dragon phase, Rev 13:11-17)
  - _Working miracles_ — the final deception combines political power, religious authority, and supernatural counterfeit

[CTRF] **Pair 11 — dragon + beast + false prophet (Rev 16:13) vs. Father + Son + Spirit.** The eleventh and final entry in the parody catalog: the Trinity itself has a counterfeit, and Rev 16:13 names it openly. The dragon (Rev 12:9 — "that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan") parodies the Father, the source of the false-trinity's authority. The sea-beast, which "had a name of blasphemy" and "received his power... and his seat" from the dragon (Rev 13:1-2), parodies the Son who receives all authority from the Father (John 5:22-23). The lamblike-but-dragon false prophet (Rev 13:11) parodies the Spirit by _function_ — directing worship toward the beast (Rev 13:12), working signs and wonders that animate the counterfeit system (Rev 13:13-14), and bearing witness to a false Lamb just as the true Spirit bears witness to the true Lamb (John 15:26; cf. 1 John 5:6-7). The three are united in purpose at Rev 16:13-14, paralleling the Father, Son, and Spirit who are united in salvation. Three unclean spirits go forth to gather the kings of the earth, paralleling (by parody) the way the Spirit gathers the world to Christ in the gospel age. The deception is total because the parody is total. The whole earlier catalog (counterfeit image, counterfeit sanctuary, counterfeit mark, counterfeit Lamb, counterfeit bride) was building to this: a counterfeit Godhead leading a counterfeit church into a counterfeit worship-test against the only true God.

- **"They go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the whole world, to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty"** (Rev 16:14)
  - Armageddon is not primarily a Middle-Eastern regional conflict
  - It is the _cosmic_ battle between the dragon-beast-false-prophet coalition and the Lamb with His sealed people
  - "The battle of _that great day of God Almighty_" = the final confrontation, fought on the moral-spiritual plane before it becomes manifest physically

- **"Har-Megiddo"**
  - Literal = "mountain of Megiddo." Megiddo in northern Israel was the site of decisive OT battles (Judg 5:19 — Barak; 2 Kings 9:27; 2 Kings 23:29 — Josiah's death)
  - Symbolic: Megiddo evokes decisive covenant-history conflict and national catastrophe. Rev 16 places the final cosmic battle under that banner
  - No literal Middle-Eastern battlefield is being identified. The symbol names a final decisive conflict, not a GPS coordinate

- **"Behold, I come as a thief"** (Rev 16:15)
  - Verse 15 inserted into the Armageddon sequence: "Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments"
  - Christ's return is imminent within the final plagues — the Second Coming is the resolution of Armageddon

## The Plagues as Answer to "How Long?"

- The fifth seal martyrs (Study 9) cried: "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?" (Rev 6:10)
- They were told: "Rest yet for a little season, until their fellowservants also and their brethren, that should be killed as they were, should be fulfilled" (Rev 6:11)
- The seven last plagues are the answer
  - The third plague's angel says: "Thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy" (Rev 16:6)
  - The altar answers: "True and righteous are thy judgments" (Rev 16:7)
  - Babylon is destroyed; the saints' blood is avenged; God's justice is publicly vindicated

- [→] Justice delayed is not justice denied. The fifth seal's martyrs waited; the sixth seal began; the seventh seal brought the trumpets; the interlude opened Daniel's book; the three angels flew; the close of probation came; the plagues fell. From the martyr's cry to the plagues' fall is a single connected answer, stretched across the whole book — and the answer means something for hearts living through delay now. Whatever cry of yours feels long-buried under the altar, God has not forgotten the fifth seal. He is moving on the timetable Revelation discloses, not the one impatience would set. The faith that waits in the long silence between the cry and the answer is the same faith that will stand in the plagues; the saints of Rev 14:12 are people who learned to trust God's calendar before they had to.

## Key Takeaways

- The close of probation (Rev 15:8, Rev 22:11) = the end of Christ's mediatorial ministry; all cases decided in the investigative judgment are now final
- The Day of Atonement pattern (Lev 16:17): when the high priest is in the sanctuary alone, no one else enters. The temple filled with smoke (Rev 15:8) signals the High Priest's work is complete
- What probation's close does NOT mean: loss of salvation for the saved, end of Christ's love, cessation of the church's witness on earth
- What it DOES mean: characters are fixed ("he that is unjust, let him be unjust still"), the Spirit withdraws His convicting ministry, the plagues begin
- The seven last plagues parallel the Egyptian plagues — God's Exodus-pattern deliverance of His sealed people
- Plague 1: sores on mark-receivers only. Plague 2: sea to blood. Plague 3: rivers to blood + God's justice vindicated. Plague 4: scorching sun. Plague 5: darkness on the beast's throne. Plague 6: Euphrates dried up — Babylon's supporters withdraw. Plague 7: "It is done" — the cosmic earthquake and hail that level Babylon
- Armageddon (Rev 16:14-16) is the cosmic battle — dragon, beast, false prophet vs. the Lamb; not a Middle-Eastern regional conflict
- "Behold, I come as a thief" (Rev 16:15) is inserted into the plague sequence — Christ's return resolves Armageddon
- The plagues are God's answer to the fifth seal martyrs' "How long?" — justice delayed, finally delivered
- The registered Sanctuary chain (Lev 16 → Dan 8:11-14 → Heb 8-9 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 15:5-8) reaches its seventh and final link in Rev 15:5-8 — the Day of Atonement ministry closes; the temple fills with smoke; no man can enter

## Next: Study 18 — The Second Coming (Revelation 19)

Armageddon gathers the world's powers against the Lamb. Rev 19 shows what happens when the Lamb arrives. Heaven opens. A white horse appears with a Rider called "Faithful and True" — "in righteousness he doth judge and make war." His eyes are flames of fire, His vesture is dipped in blood, and "the armies which were in heaven followed him." A sharp sword proceeds from His mouth. On His thigh is written KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS. The beast and false prophet are cast into the lake of fire; the armies gathered against Him are slain by the brightness of His coming. Study 18 is the arrival the whole book has been building toward.
