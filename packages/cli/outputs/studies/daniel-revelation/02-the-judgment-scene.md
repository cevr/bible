# Study 2: The Judgment Scene — Daniel 7

**Fifty years after the statue, Daniel sees the same history again — but now as beasts, not metals. And in the middle of the beast vision, a courtroom opens in heaven. The Ancient of Days takes His seat. Books are opened. The Son of Man comes — not to earth — but _to_ the Ancient of Days. This is not the Second Coming. It is the scene Revelation will revisit four more times in a five-stop chain, the scene the whole Bible is waiting on, and the single most important vision in Daniel.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 7 (entire chapter)
- Daniel 2:31-45 (review)
- Revelation 1:7
- Revelation 4-5 (skim)
- Revelation 13:5-7

## Same Story, Second Pass

Daniel 7 is dated "in the first year of Belshazzar king of Babylon" (Dan 7:1) — about 553 BC, roughly fifty years after the statue dream of Dan 2. Daniel is now an old man. The empire he has served since his teens is within a generation of falling.

And God gives him the same prophecy a second time.

- **Dan 2 from Nebuchadnezzar's view** (a pagan king sees a statue):
  - Gold, silver, bronze, iron, iron + clay
  - _Political_ sequence — the outside of history
- **Dan 7 from Daniel's view** (a prophet sees beasts):
  - Lion, bear, leopard, terrible beast, ten horns, little horn
  - _Religious-political_ sequence — the inside of history, with judgment added

- `[KEY🔑]` **The recapitulation principle**
  - Daniel does not tell his story once. Dan 2 covers four kingdoms. Dan 7 covers _the same four kingdoms_ plus the courtroom. Dan 8-9 will cover _the same sequence_ plus the sanctuary and the 2300-day timeline. Dan 11 will cover it again in maximum detail, walking from Persia to the end. Four passes. Same history. Different emphasis each time.
  - This is not an inference — it is the structure of the book itself. Same empires, same endpoint, retold with more resolution
  - Revelation will do the same thing on its own scale: churches (Rev 1-3), seals (Rev 6-7), trumpets (Rev 8-11), great controversy (Rev 12-14), final-phase endgame (Rev 15-22) — multiple passes covering the ground from John's era to the end
  - [→] Recapitulation means: when you see a symbol that feels familiar, it probably is. You are not reading a new storyline; you are reading the same story from a different angle. Each pass adds what the last one left out.

[DYK🔎] Dan 2-7 is written in Aramaic (the language of the Babylonian court), while Dan 1 and Dan 8-12 are in Hebrew. And within those six Aramaic chapters, the order is deliberately _not_ chronological — Dan 7 (dated to Belshazzar's first year) sits _after_ Dan 5-6 (dated later), not with Dan 4. The arrangement makes a chiasm: Dan 2 (prophecy) // Dan 3 (persecution) // Dan 4-5 (Babylonian kings humbled and judged) // Dan 6 (persecution) // Dan 7 (prophecy). Prophecy frames persecution frames the humbling and fall of Babylon's kings — the whole engine of the book in one literary shape.

## Four Beasts from the Sea

- **The setting**
  - "The four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea" (Dan 7:2)
  - "And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another" (Dan 7:3)
  - Wind in Scripture often pictures strife and conflict (Jer 25:32-33; 49:36). "The great sea" — in Rev 17:15 the Bible itself will define waters as "peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." Four empires rise out of political turmoil among the nations.

- **The four beasts and their kingdoms**

  | Beast                                           | Identifiers in the text                                                                                         | Kingdom     | Daniel 2 equivalent |
  | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------- |
  | Lion with eagle's wings (Dan 7:4)               | Lifted up, made to stand as a man, given a man's heart                                                          | Babylon     | Gold                |
  | Bear raised on one side, three ribs (Dan 7:5)   | "Arise, devour much flesh" — the one-sided bear matches the Medo-Persia coalition where Persia outweighed Media | Medo-Persia | Silver              |
  | Leopard, four wings, four heads (Dan 7:6)       | Dominion given; four heads = the four-way split after Alexander (Cassander, Lysimachus, Seleucus, Ptolemy)      | Greece      | Bronze              |
  | Dreadful beast, iron teeth, ten horns (Dan 7:7) | Devoured, broke in pieces, stamped residue; diverse from all before it                                          | Rome        | Iron                |
  - Same sequence as Dan 2. Same four powers. Same historical verification.
  - Dan 7:17 and 7:23 spell it out: "these great beasts, which are four, _are four kings_" / "the fourth beast shall be the _fourth kingdom_ upon earth." The `[KEY🔑]` we established in Study 1 is confirmed here.

- **Babylon's lion wings and human heart** (Dan 7:4)
  - The description is a compressed biography of Nebuchadnezzar: pride (lion + wings), humiliation (wings plucked), restoration (stood as a man, a man's heart given to it) — exactly Dan 4's story
  - [→] Daniel's readers would have lived through this. The symbol is not abstract.

[DYK🔎] The leopard's _four heads_ match Alexander the Great's empire being divided among four of his generals after his death in 323 BC: Cassander (Macedonia), Lysimachus (Thrace), Seleucus (Syria and east), and Ptolemy (Egypt). We will meet these four again in Dan 8 as the "four notable horns" that replace the great horn of the goat (Dan 8:8, 21-22), and again in Dan 11 as "the king of the north" and "the king of the south." The Bible keeps naming the same four Hellenistic kingdoms from different angles.

## The Fourth Beast — Rome and What Grows Out of It

This is where Dan 7 leaves Dan 2 behind and adds detail.

- **The beast itself** (Dan 7:7)
  - "Dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth" — iron teeth tie back to Dan 2's iron legs
  - "It devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it" — Rome's method of conquest
  - "It was diverse from all the beasts that were before it" — Rome was not a mere successor; it was a different _kind_ of empire: legal, bureaucratic, continent-spanning

- **The ten horns** (Dan 7:7, 24)
  - "And it had ten horns" / "the ten horns out of this kingdom are ten kings that shall arise"
  - Ten horns = the successor kingdoms born from Rome's collapse. Historicist teachers traditionally list: Alamanni, Franks, Burgundians, Suevi, Vandals, Visigoths, Ostrogoths, Heruli, Lombards, Anglo-Saxons. The exact list varies by source, and some of these (Heruli, Vandals, Ostrogoths — see below) are short-lived. The pattern is what matters: Rome fragmented into multiple successor kingdoms that formed the post-Roman map from which modern Europe developed.
  - Dan 2 saw this as the iron-and-clay feet. Dan 7 sees it as ten horns. Same reality, different angle.

- **The little horn** (Dan 7:8, 20-25) — seven identifying marks
  1. Rises _among_ the ten, _after_ the ten (Dan 7:8, 24) — so it arises from within post-Rome Europe, not before
  2. Plucks up three of the first horns by the roots (Dan 7:8, 24) — three kingdoms removed to make room for it
  3. Has "eyes like the eyes of man" and "a mouth speaking great things" (Dan 7:8, 20) — a seeing, speaking power, not merely a political one
  4. Is "diverse from" the other horns (Dan 7:24) — a different _kind_ of power than the surrounding nations
  5. Speaks "great words against the most High" (Dan 7:25) — blasphemous claims
  6. Wears out "the saints of the most High" (Dan 7:25) — long-term persecution, not a single flare-up
  7. Thinks to "change times and laws" (Dan 7:25) — attempts to alter the very law of God

- **The time period** (Dan 7:25)
  - "They shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time"
  - _A time_ + _times_ (dual in Aramaic, so two) + _half a time_ = three and a half "times"
  - In the time prophecies of apocalyptic, a "time" = a year (this will be confirmed and calculated in Study 3). So three and a half years = 1260 days, and by the year-day principle = 1260 years.
  - Dan 7:25 is the first appearance of this period. We will meet it again five times across Rev — Rev 11:2, Rev 11:3, Rev 12:6, Rev 12:14, and Rev 13:5
  - `[⟲]` 1260 years — six witnesses total: Dan 7:25, Rev 11:2, Rev 11:3, Rev 12:6, Rev 12:14, Rev 13:5. Dan 7:25 is link one.

[Q] **"Seven identifying marks feels excessive. Why so many?"**
→ Because the little horn is the most significant anti-God power in Daniel, and Scripture is careful not to let it be misidentified. If the text gave only one or two marks, candidates could be multiplied (as futurist systems do, naming every tyrant in every century). With seven marks _all converging on the same power_, the fit becomes unmistakable. Many Reformation and post-Reformation historicists — Wycliffe, Tyndale, Luther, Knox, Isaac Newton — identified the same power: the papal system that arose from within fallen Rome, saw three Arian kingdoms (Heruli, Vandals, Ostrogoths) removed from its path in the late fifth and sixth centuries, spoke as the "Vicar of Christ," persecuted dissenters for more than a thousand years, and claimed authority to change the Sabbath commandment. The marks fit one system. They do not fit a future individual.

## "Think to Change Times and Laws"

One mark of the little horn deserves its own treatment. It goes to the heart of the whole conflict.

- **The text**
  - "And he shall speak great words against the most High, and shall wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws" (Dan 7:25)
  - Two targets: sacred _time_ and divine _law_. In the Decalogue — the only body of law God Himself wrote (Ex 31:18) — the Sabbath is exactly where sacred time and divine law converge

- **Which time?**
  - God's law has one time-element: the Sabbath (Ex 20:8-11)
  - The fourth commandment is the one commandment that is _about_ time — the seventh day, the weekly rhythm
  - Any claim to alter God's weekly worship cycle qualifies as "thinking to change times"

- **Which laws?**
  - The Ten Commandments — the one moral code God wrote with His own finger (Ex 31:18; Deut 9:10)
  - "Think to change" does not mean _succeeds_. The phrase suggests an attempt, a claim of authority. God's law is not actually altered; the power only claims the authority to alter it

- [→] We will revisit the "changing of times" in Study 4 (Sanctuary) and Study 14 (Three Angels). For now, notice: the little horn's signature move is not merely political conquest or military persecution. It is _legislative_ — it reaches for God's commandment and claims the right to rewrite it.

## The Courtroom Opens

Halfway through the beast vision, the scene changes. Daniel is still seeing the fourth beast "because of the voice of the great words which the horn spake" (Dan 7:11) — and suddenly the camera pans up. Heaven opens.

- **The scene** (Dan 7:9-10)
  - "I beheld till the thrones were cast down [set up], and the Ancient of days did sit"
  - "His garment was white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool: his throne was like the fiery flame, and his wheels as burning fire"
  - "A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him"
  - "Thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him"
  - "The judgment was set, and the books were opened"

- **What this is**
  - A courtroom. Specifically, a _capital_ courtroom — the Judge, the angelic witnesses, the records, the accused in view (the little horn's mouth is still speaking)
  - The Ancient of Days = God the Father (the description parallels Eze 1:26-28 and Rev 1:14)
  - Books opened = records. The judgment proceeds by evidence, not by decree.
  - [→] Dan 7's judgment is an _investigative_ procedure. Records are examined _before_ a verdict is pronounced (Dan 7:11-14, 22, 26). The verdict removes the little horn's dominion and gives the kingdom to the saints. That is the shape.

- **`[Judgment thread]`**
  - This scene is the single most cross-referenced chain in Dan + Rev. We will name the thread every time it surfaces, so the cumulative picture builds:
  - Dan 7:9-14 (court convened) → Rev 4-5 (Lamb qualified) → Rev 11:15-19 (temple opened) → Rev 14:6-7 ("hour of his judgment is come") → Rev 20:11-12 (great white throne, books opened)
  - Mark it with `[⟲]` when it appears. Five stops across the chain.

[DYK🔎] In Rev 4-5, John sees the same throne-room pattern from a closer angle: the throne (Rev 4:2 || Dan 7:9), the fiery lamps (Rev 4:5 || Dan 7:10's fiery stream), the myriads of angels ("ten thousand times ten thousand" in both Dan 7:10 and Rev 5:11), and — now — the Lamb who takes the sealed scroll. Note the differences too: Rev 5 has one specific book/scroll, while Dan 7:10 has "books" opened at the judgment. The throne-sitter in Rev 4 corresponds to Daniel's Ancient of Days; the Lamb enters the scene in Rev 5. Same throne room, different moment in the process.

## The Son of Man Comes — But Not to Earth

This is where many readings of Daniel go sideways. Miss the direction of Dan 7:13 and you will misread the whole investigative judgment.

- **The text**
  - "I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days, and they brought him near before him" (Dan 7:13)
  - "And there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom... his dominion is an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away" (Dan 7:14)

- **Read it carefully — where is He going?**
  - "Came _to_ the Ancient of days"
  - The Son of Man comes _to the Father_. He does not come _to earth_. He comes _into_ the judgment, not _out_ of it.
  - "They brought him near before him" — courtroom language, not descent-language

- **Contrast with the Second Coming** (Rev 1:7)
  - "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and _every eye shall see him_, and they also which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him" (Rev 1:7)
  - At the Second Coming, Christ comes _to earth_, _every eye_ sees Him, _earth's inhabitants_ respond
  - In Dan 7:13, Christ comes _to heaven's courtroom_, the _Ancient of Days_ receives Him, _angels_ attend
  - Different destination. Different audience. Different event.

- **Why the distinction matters**
  - Dan 7:13 is not the Second Coming. It is Christ's _accession to the judgment_ — the moment the High Priest enters the Most Holy Place of the heavenly sanctuary to begin the final phase of His priestly work
  - The Second Coming follows the judgment, not the other way around. Verdict first, then sentence executed
  - Rev 22:12 confirms: "my reward is with me" — the judgment's verdicts are already rendered when Christ returns to earth. Nobody pleads at the Second Coming. The pleading belongs to the session in Dan 7
  - [→] If you conflate Dan 7:13 with the Second Coming, you collapse the investigative judgment into nothing. The sanctuary service, the books, the "hour of his judgment" in Rev 14:7 — all of it requires this distinction to stand.

[Q] **"Where else in the Bible does 'Son of Man' coming with clouds NOT mean the Second Coming?"**
→ Acts 1:9-11 confirms Christ _ascended_ in clouds and will return the same way. The upward trip is to the Father (the Ascension — Matthew 26:64 has Christ quoting Dan 7:13 to the high priest just before the cross; cf. Heb 9:24 "Christ is... entered into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us"). The downward trip is the Second Coming. Dan 7:13 is the upward trip at a later phase — when the accession leads to the judgment. Every cloud-coming is not the same cloud-coming. Look where He is going.

## Whose Kingdom Is It?

The vision closes by settling ownership.

- **The little horn loses** (Dan 7:11, 26)
  - "I beheld even till the beast was slain, and his body destroyed, and given to the burning flame"
  - "But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to destroy it unto the end"

- **The Son of Man receives** (Dan 7:14)
  - "There was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him: his dominion is an everlasting dominion"

- **The saints receive** (Dan 7:18, 22, 27) — three times, in case we miss it
  - "But the saints of the most High shall take the kingdom, and possess the kingdom for ever, even for ever and ever" (v. 18)
  - "And the time came that the saints possessed the kingdom" (v. 22)
  - "And the kingdom and dominion, and the greatness of the kingdom under the whole heaven, shall be given to the people of the saints of the most High" (v. 27)

- [→] The stone from Dan 2 becomes a mountain that fills the earth. Dan 7:27 tells us who inhabits that mountain. _The saints._ The ones who refused Babylon's table in Dan 1, stood through Babylon's furnace in Dan 3, and survived the little horn's 1260 years — they get the kingdom. Not the political empires. Not the religious horn. The faithful.

## The Shape of Revelation in Advance

Everything the rest of Revelation will do, Dan 7 has already set up.

- **Rev 13's beast** = a composite of Dan 7's beasts, concentrated in the papal-Roman phase
  - Rev 13:2 — "the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion"
  - That is Dan 7:4, 5, 6 composited — lion + bear + leopard features gathered into one beast in Rev 13, rising on the ground of the fourth beast
  - Rev 13:5 — "there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies" (extending Dan 7:8's "mouth speaking great things")
  - Rev 13:5 — "power was given unto him to continue forty and two months" = 1260 days. `[⟲]` 1260 years chain: Dan 7:25 → Rev 13:5
  - Rev 13:7 — "make war with the saints, and to overcome them" = Dan 7:21, 25
  - [→] If you know Dan 7, you already know most of Rev 13.

- **Rev 14's judgment announcement** = Dan 7's courtroom going public
  - Rev 14:7 — "Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come"
  - The judgment scene Daniel saw from a distance, Rev 14 announces to every nation
  - `[⟲]` Judgment thread: Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 14:6-7

- **Rev 20's great white throne** = Dan 7's books, revisited
  - Rev 20:12 — "and the books were opened"
  - `[⟲]` Judgment thread's final link

## Key Takeaways

- `[KEY🔑]` **Recapitulation:** Daniel retells the same history multiple times at increasing resolution. Dan 7 is Dan 2 from a different angle, with the courtroom added. This is the interpretive key for the whole prophetic corpus.
- Four beasts = four kingdoms (Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome) — confirming and expanding Dan 2
- The fourth beast's ten horns = the nations born from Rome's collapse
- The little horn has seven identifying marks — no single future individual fits all seven; the papal system historically does
- "Think to change times and laws" (Dan 7:25) targets God's Sabbath and His commandments specifically
- The 1260-year period (Dan 7:25) is the first appearance of a chain that runs through Rev 11, 12, and 13 — `[⟲]` 1260 thread, six witnesses total
- `[KEY🔑]` **Dan 7:13 is NOT the Second Coming.** The Son of Man comes _to the Ancient of Days_ — up, into the judgment — not down, to earth. This is the structural key to the investigative judgment.
- The judgment begins _before_ the kingdom is given; the verdict precedes the execution
- The kingdom ultimately belongs to the saints, not to the beast or the horn (Dan 7:18, 22, 27)
