# Revelation 20 — The Millennium and the Great White Throne

**Rev 19 ended with the beast and the false prophet cast alive into the lake of fire and the rest of their armies slain by the sword from the Rider's mouth. Rev 20 opens with the third member of the unholy trinity. An angel descends from heaven with the key of the bottomless pit and a great chain. He lays hold on the dragon — "that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan" — binds him a thousand years, casts him into the abyss, shuts him up, and sets a seal upon him "that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled." Then John sees thrones, and judgment given to those who sit on them — the souls of the beheaded for the witness of Jesus, and they reign with Christ a thousand years. The rest of the dead live not again until the thousand years are finished. Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection: on him the second death has no power. When the thousand years expire, Satan is loosed, gathers Gog and Magog from the four quarters of the earth, and surrounds the camp of the saints and the beloved city. Fire comes down from God out of heaven and devours them. The devil is cast into the lake of fire and brimstone where the beast and false prophet are. Then a great white throne; the dead small and great stand before God; the books are opened; another book is opened, the book of life; the dead are judged according to their works. Death and hell are cast into the lake of fire — "this is the second death." Whosoever was not found written in the book of life is cast into the lake of fire. Rev 20 is the chapter where sin's history reaches its forensic close.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 20 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 19:19-21 (the chapter Rev 20 continues — beast and false prophet judged; Satan now next)
- Daniel 7:9-10 (the Ancient of Days, thrones set, books opened — pattern for Rev 20:11-12)
- Jeremiah 4:23-26 ("without form, and void" — the desolated earth, Satan's millennial prison)
- Isaiah 24:1-3, 21-22 (the LORD makes the earth empty; the high ones gathered as prisoners in the pit)
- Ezekiel 38-39 (Gog and Magog — the original coalition language Rev 20:8 invokes)
- Malachi 4:1-3 (the wicked as ashes — the lake of fire's actual outcome)
- 1 Corinthians 15:20-28, 51-57 (the resurrection order; death the last enemy)
- 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18 (the saints caught up at the Second Coming)

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 20 Sits

Rev 20 is the bridge-chapter between the Second Coming (Rev 19:11-21) and the new earth (Rev 21-22). **Rev 19 finished the beast and the false prophet; Rev 20 finishes the dragon and the entire unsaved race.** The chapter has four scenes: the binding of Satan and the millennium begun (vv. 1-3); the saints reigning with Christ (vv. 4-6); the loosing of Satan and the final assault (vv. 7-10); the great white throne judgment (vv. 11-15).

- **The chapter unfolds the third member of the unholy trinity's destruction.** Rev 16:13 listed dragon, beast, and false prophet as the three frog-spirit-emitters. Rev 19:20 cast beast and false prophet into the lake. **Rev 20:10 completes the trio:** "the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet are." The unholy trinity is now fully destroyed — but in two waves a thousand years apart.
- **The "thousand years" appears six times in the chapter** (vv. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7). It is the structural backbone of the millennium. Historicist tradition reads these as a literal thousand years, bracketed by the first resurrection at the Second Coming and the second resurrection at the millennium's end.
- **First resurrection / second resurrection** (vv. 5-6) — the two-stage resurrection scheme that 1 Cor 15:23 ("every man in his own order") and Jn 5:28-29 (resurrection of life / resurrection of damnation) frame. The first is the righteous, raised at the Second Coming (1 Th 4:16); the second is the wicked, raised after the thousand years for the great white throne judgment.
- **Satan's binding is a binding by circumstance, not by literal chain.** Rev 19:21 has just emptied the earth of the wicked living; 1 Th 4:16-17 has caught up the righteous to meet the Lord in the air. **No one remains to deceive.** Satan's "chain" (v. 1) is the chain of conditions that strands him on a desolated planet for a thousand years with no one to work on. The picture is the antitype of the OT scapegoat carried into the wilderness (Lev 16:21-22) — Satan, the antitypical _Azazel_, bears the responsibility for sin he originated and is exiled to the desolate place.
- **The earth as Satan's prison.** Jer 4:23-26 ("I beheld the earth, and, lo, it was without form, and void") deliberately echoes Gen 1:2 ("without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep"). The Greek _abyssos_ in Rev 20:1, 3 corresponds to LXX _abyssos_ at Gen 1:2 ("the deep" — Hebrew _tehom_). **The earth at the Second Coming is reduced to pre-creation chaos, and Satan — the original disturber of creation order — is locked back inside that chaos for a thousand years.**
- **The great white throne is the final phase of the judgment.** The judgment chain: Dan 7:9-10 (the Ancient of Days, thrones set, books opened — opens the heavenly judgment in 1844) → Rev 14:7 ("the hour of his judgment is come" — the first angel's announcement) → Rev 11:18 (the seventh trumpet's "time of the dead, that they should be judged") → the pre-Advent investigative judgment (Dan 8:14's 2300-day cleansing of the sanctuary, beginning AD 1844) → the Second Coming executive verdict (Rev 19) → the millennial review by the saints (Rev 20:4) → the great white throne final judgment of the wicked (Rev 20:11-15). **Rev 20:11-15 is the closing scene of a judgment-arc that began in Daniel.**
- **"For ever and ever" in Rev 20:10 is biblical idiom for permanence-of-result.** The lake of fire is itself named "the second death" two verses later (v. 14); Mal 4:1-3 says the wicked become ashes "neither root nor branch"; Isa 34:10 has Edom's smoke ascending "for ever" though Edom no longer smokes; 2 Pet 2:6 makes Sodom's destruction "an ensample" of how the ungodly perish. **The biblical doctrine: the result is forever; the burning is not.** The Rev 19:20 brimstone-lake of Sodom-precedent (Gen 19:24) carries through Rev 20.

[KEY🔑] **Rev 20 is the chapter where the great controversy reaches its forensic close.** The cross of John 12:31-32 ("now is the judgment of this world: now shall the prince of this world be cast out") set the verdict in principle. The 1844 investigative judgment (Dan 7:10's books opened, Dan 8:14's sanctuary cleansed) ratified it for the saved. The Second Coming (Rev 19:11-21) executed it on the living wicked. **Rev 20 finishes it on the dead wicked, on Satan, and on death itself.** The thousand years are not a golden age or a Jewish-restoration kingdom on this earth; they are God's transparent judicial review — a millennium during which the saints, with Christ, see every record (v. 4) and every question about God's justice is answered before the second death falls. **At the end of Rev 20, three things are gone forever: the wicked dead, the devil who deceived them, and death-and-hades themselves.** The universe is not just cleansed; it is made trial-proof. **No charge against God's character will ever rise again, because every charge has been heard and answered.** Rev 20 is the chapter that makes Rev 21's "no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying" possible.

## Rev 20:1 — The Angel with the Key and the Chain

> "And I saw an angel come down from heaven, having the key of the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand." (Rev 20:1)

- "**An angel come down from heaven**" — Greek _angelon katabainonta ek tou ouranou_. **Same descent-formula as Rev 10:1** (the mighty angel) and **Rev 18:1** (the Rev 18 angel who lights the earth with his glory). **A heaven-to-earth movement.** The chapter opens with a heavenly agent acting on earth, not with an earthly figure rising.
- "**The key of the bottomless pit**" — Greek _tēn kleida tēs abyssou_. **Compare Rev 9:1** (the fifth-trumpet star with "the key of the bottomless pit"); **Rev 1:18** (Christ — "I have the keys of hell and of death"). **Authority over the abyss belongs to heaven.** The same key that opened the pit at Rev 9 now closes it at Rev 20. **Heaven holds the keys.**
- "**A great chain**" — Greek _halysin megalēn_. **The chain is symbolic.** Satan is a spirit (Eph 6:12); no metal chain binds spirits. **The chain = the chain of circumstances** that Rev 19:21 and 1 Th 4:16-17 between them produce: every wicked person dead, every righteous person caught up, the earth depopulated. **Compare Isa 24:21-22** ("the high ones... shall be gathered together, as prisoners are gathered in the pit, and shall be shut up in the prison, and after many days shall they be visited") — the OT precedent for a binding-then-final-visitation pattern. **The chain that holds Satan is the empty earth itself.**
- "**The bottomless pit**" — Greek _hē abyssos_. **In LXX _abyssos_ translates Hebrew _tehom_** ("the deep") at Gen 1:2 — "the deep" over which the Spirit of God moved before God said, "Let there be light." **The earth at the Second Coming has been reduced to the pre-creation chaos** (Jer 4:23-26's "without form, and void" deliberately echoes Gen 1:2). **The disordered realm Satan corrupted is now his prison.**

## Rev 20:2 — The Dragon Bound

> "And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years," (Rev 20:2)

- "**Laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan**" — Greek _ekratēsen ton drakonta, ton ophin ton archaion, hos estin Diabolos kai ho Satanas_. **Four titles in one breath:** dragon, ancient serpent, Devil, Satan. **Identical four-fold identification as Rev 12:9** ("the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world"). **Rev 20:2 deliberately recapitulates Rev 12:9** — the same enemy, the same titles, now finally bound. **The arc from Eden (Gen 3:1's serpent) through the cross (Jn 12:31's prince of this world cast out) through Rev 12 (the dragon cast down to earth) closes here.**
- "**Bound him a thousand years**" — Greek _edēsen auton chilia etē_. **Aorist _edēsen_** — bound. **The first of six occurrences of "thousand years"** in the chapter (vv. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7). **The literal thousand-year duration** is the chapter's structural backbone — historicist tradition reads it as a literal millennium between the Second Coming and the final judgment, not as a metaphor for the church age (amillennialism) or a pre-Second-Coming earthly kingdom (premillennial dispensationalism). **The thousand years are bracketed by two resurrections** (vv. 5-6) and end with the loosing of Satan and the final assault (v. 7).

## Rev 20:3 — Cast Into the Pit, Sealed, Loosed Little Season

> "And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled: and after that he must be loosed a little season." (Rev 20:3)

- "**Cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him**" — Greek _ebalen auton eis tēn abysson kai ekleisen kai esphragisen epanō autou_. **Three verbs of containment:** cast, shut, sealed. **The seal upon Satan is the heavenly equivalent** of Mt 27:66's seal on the tomb of Christ — except this seal contains, not entombs. **The deceiver is sealed into the abyss for a thousand years.**
- "**That he should deceive the nations no more**" — Greek _hina mē planēsē eti ta ethnē_. **_Planaō_** = lead astray, deceive. **The defining verb of Satan's life-work** (Rev 12:9 — "which deceiveth the whole world"; Rev 13:14 — "and deceiveth them that dwell on the earth"; Rev 19:20 — the false prophet "deceived them that had received the mark"; Rev 20:8 — Satan, after loosing, "shall go out to deceive the nations"; Rev 20:10 — "the devil that deceived them"). **Satan's binding stops his deception** because the nations are not present to deceive — they are dead.
- "**Till the thousand years should be fulfilled**" — Greek _achri telesthē ta chilia etē_. **_Telesthē_** — passive aorist subjunctive of _teleō_ ("to complete, finish"). **Same root as Rev 15:1's _etelesthē_** (the seven last plagues; "in them is filled up the wrath of God") and Rev 17:17's _telesthōsin_ (the words of God fulfilled). **A definite end is appointed.**
- "**After that he must be loosed a little season**" — Greek _meta tauta dei lythēnai auton mikron chronon_. **_Dei_** ("it must be") — divine necessity. **The loosing is not Satan's escape but God's permission** for the final demonstration. **A "little season"** (compare Rev 6:11 — the fifth-seal martyrs told to rest "a little season" — _chronon mikron_, same phrase) — short duration. **The release is purposive, not accidental.**

[⟲] **The deceiver-thread reaches its end here.** Gen 3:1 (the serpent more subtle than any beast — the first deception). Mt 24:24 (false christs and false prophets shall deceive — _planēsai_ — if possible the elect). 2 Th 2:9-10 (the man of sin coming "with all power and signs and lying wonders, And with all deceivableness of unrighteousness"). Rev 12:9 (the dragon "which deceiveth the whole world"). Rev 13:14 (the earth-beast "deceiveth them that dwell on the earth"). Rev 19:20 (the false prophet "deceived them that had received the mark of the beast"). **Rev 20:3 — Satan is sealed up "that he should deceive the nations no more."** Rev 20:8 (he is loosed and "shall go out to deceive the nations" one final time). **Rev 20:10 (the deceiver-of-them is cast into the lake of fire — the deceiver-thread closes forever.)** The serpent's tongue is finally silenced.

## Rev 20:4 — Thrones, Judgment Given, the Beheaded Reign

> "And I saw thrones, and they sat upon them, and judgment was given unto them: and I saw the souls of them that were beheaded for the witness of Jesus, and for the word of God, and which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image, neither had received his mark upon their foreheads, or in their hands; and they lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years." (Rev 20:4)

- "**Thrones, and they sat upon them**" — Greek _thronous, kai ekathisan ep' autous_. **Compare Dan 7:9** ("I beheld till the thrones were cast down [or "set"], and the Ancient of days did sit"); **Mt 19:28** (Christ to the twelve — "ye also shall sit upon twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel"); **Lk 22:30** (same promise). **The promised thrones are now occupied.** Daniel's vision and Christ's promise meet at Rev 20:4.
- "**Judgment was given unto them**" — Greek _krima edothē autois_. **_Krima_** — judgment, sentence, judicial decision. **Compare 1 Cor 6:2-3** (Paul — "Do ye not know that the saints shall judge the world?... Know ye not that we shall judge angels?"); **Dan 7:22** ("judgment was given to the saints of the most High; and the time came that the saints possessed the kingdom"). **The saints are not on trial during the millennium; they are judges.** The millennial review's purpose: every saint emerges with every question about God's justice fully answered, so that when the second death falls the universe says with one voice "true and righteous are thy judgments."
- "**The souls of them that were beheaded for the witness of Jesus**" — Greek _tas psychas tōn pepelekismenōn dia tēn martyrian Iēsou_. **_Pelekizō_** — to behead with an axe. **Compare Rev 6:9** (the fifth-seal "souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held"). **The beheaded company is the same fifth-seal martyr-company,** now reigning. **"The witness of Jesus" / "the word of God"** — same two-fold identification as Rev 1:9 and Rev 6:9; Rev 12:17 and Rev 19:10 carry the testimony-of-Jesus side of the motif.
- "**Which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image, neither had received his mark**" — Greek _kai hoitines ou prosekynēsan to thērion oude tēn eikona autou_. **Direct cross-anchor to Rev 13:15-17 and Rev 14:9-11** — the very people the third-angel's message warned and the fifth-seal cried for. **The closing-crisis loyalists who refused the mark.** The beheaded-martyrs and the closing-crisis non-worshippers are presented together as one company. **The historicist closing-crisis test of Rev 13-14 issues in the millennial reign of Rev 20:4.**
- "**They lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years**" — Greek _ezēsan kai ebasileusan meta tou Christou chilia etē_. **_Ezēsan_** — lived (came to life). **The first resurrection** is enacted in this verb. **Compare Rev 5:10** (the song of the redeemed — "thou hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth") — though the millennial reign begins in heaven (vv. 4 places thrones in the heavenly throne-room visible to John since Rev 4), the reign culminates on the new earth in Rev 21-22.

## Rev 20:5 — The First Resurrection

> "But the rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished. This is the first resurrection." (Rev 20:5)

- "**The rest of the dead**" — Greek _hoi loipoi tōn nekrōn_. **Same _hoi loipoi_ phrase as Rev 19:21** (the rest of the wicked army slain by the sword from His mouth). **The "rest of the dead" are the wicked dead** — those who were not raised at the first resurrection. **The two resurrections are explicitly distinguished here:** the righteous live now; the rest of the dead live not _until_ the thousand years are finished.
- "**Lived not again until the thousand years were finished**" — Greek _ouk ezēsan achri telesthē ta chilia etē_. **_Achri telesthē_** — "until [the thousand years] should be finished/completed." **A definite endpoint** to the wicked's gravelock. **The dead wicked are conscious of nothing during the millennium** (consistent with Eccl 9:5 — "the dead know not any thing"; Ps 146:4 — "in that very day his thoughts perish"; Jn 11:11 — Lazarus "sleepeth"). **The "soul-sleep" doctrine flows directly from this verse:** the wicked dead remain dead a full thousand years.
- "**This is the first resurrection**" — Greek _hautē hē anastasis hē prōtē_. **The first / earlier resurrection** is identified as the previous verse's "lived and reigned with Christ" — i.e., the resurrection of the righteous at the Second Coming (1 Th 4:16 — "the dead in Christ shall rise first"). **The first resurrection ≠ Christ's resurrection alone; it is the corporate resurrection of the saints at the Second Coming.** Compare **Jn 5:28-29** (Christ — "all that are in the graves shall hear his voice, And shall come forth; they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation") — the two-fold resurrection scheme that Rev 20 finally times explicitly: the resurrection-of-life now, the resurrection-of-damnation a thousand years later.

## Rev 20:6 — Blessed and Holy; Second Death Hath No Power

> "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death hath no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years." (Rev 20:6)

- "**Blessed and holy**" — Greek _makarios kai hagios_. **The fifth of Revelation's seven beatitudes** (Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14). **Two adjectives, not one:** blessed (the divine favor) and holy (the sanctified state). **Both apply to the first-resurrection company.**
- "**On such the second death hath no power**" — Greek _epi toutōn ho deuteros thanatos ouk echei exousian_. **_Deuteros thanatos_** — "second death." **The chapter's defining phrase** (also v. 14 — "this is the second death"). **The first death = the universal mortality** every human inherits from Adam (Gen 3:19; Rom 5:12). **The second death = the lake-of-fire execution** that befalls those whose names are not in the book of life (v. 15). **Compare Rev 2:11** (Christ to Smyrna — "He that overcometh shall not be hurt of the second death") and **Rev 21:8** (the unbelieving and abominable "shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death"). **First-resurrection participants are forever immune** to this second death. **The first death they have already passed through and from which they have been raised; the second death cannot touch them.**
- "**Priests of God and of Christ**" — Greek _hiereis tou theou kai tou Christou_. **Compare Rev 1:6** ("hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father"); **Rev 5:10** ("hast made us unto our God kings and priests"); **1 Pet 2:9** ("a royal priesthood"). **The redeemed are priests in the reign-and-judgment role.** They participate in the millennial review (v. 4's "judgment was given") in a priestly-mediatorial mode — surveying records, understanding cases, vindicating God's character.
- "**Shall reign with him a thousand years**" — Greek _kai basileusousin met' autou ta chilia etē_. **The reign-promise** of Rev 5:10 ("we shall reign on the earth") begins in this thousand-year window (the throne-rooms of v. 4 are heavenly) and continues forever in Rev 22:5 ("they shall reign for ever and ever") on the new earth.

[CTRF] **The first resurrection vs. the second resurrection — counterfeits of life and death.** The first resurrection (vv. 4-6) is the bodily resurrection of the righteous at the Second Coming — they live and reign with Christ a thousand years; the second death has no power over them. The second resurrection (v. 5b, vv. 11-13) is the bodily resurrection of the wicked at the millennium's end — they live just long enough to face the great white throne, the fire, and the second death. **Two resurrections; two destinies; a thousand-year gap.** The historicist tradition rejects the modern dispensational pattern (a "rapture" before tribulation, then a thousand-year earthly Jewish kingdom centered on a rebuilt Jerusalem temple, then a final apostasy, then the new heavens and earth). Rev 20 has only one rapture-event — the Second Coming of Rev 19:11-21 — and the millennium that follows is in heaven for the saints and in desolation for Satan. **The millennium is not Christ reigning over unsaved nations on this earth; it is Christ and His saints reigning above while the earth lies in chaos as Satan's prison.** The dispensational counterfeit gives Israel a second-chance national kingdom on this present earth; the biblical pattern gives the redeemed a heavenly reign followed by the new earth of Rev 21. **Rev 20:5 — "the rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" — flatly excludes any mortal population on earth during the millennium. The earth is empty.**

## Rev 20:7-8 — Satan Loosed; Gog and Magog Gathered

> "And when the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be loosed out of his prison, And shall go out to deceive the nations which are in the four quarters of the earth, Gog and Magog, to gather them together to battle: the number of whom is as the sand of the sea." (Rev 20:7-8)

- "**When the thousand years are expired**" — Greek _hotan telesthē ta chilia etē_. **Same _telesthē_ as v. 3** — the appointed end. **The millennium ends; the final scene begins.**
- "**Satan shall be loosed out of his prison**" — Greek _lythēsetai ho Satanas ek tēs phylakēs autou_. **_Phylakē_** — prison, ward. **Satan's binding ends not because his power has been restored but because the wicked have been raised** (the second resurrection of v. 5) — and thus his "audience for deception" exists again.
- "**Go out to deceive the nations which are in the four quarters of the earth**" — Greek _exeleusetai planēsai ta ethnē ta en tais tessarsi gōniais tēs gēs_. **_Planēsai_** — same _planaō_ root that v. 3 said the binding stopped. **The deceiver returns to deceiving** — but now to a population that has just been raised in unrepentant condition (the second resurrection raises the wicked unchanged from how they died).
- "**Gog and Magog**" — Greek _ton Gōg kai Magōg_. **Direct invocation of Eze 38-39** (the Magog-coalition Yahweh judges on Israel's mountains; the supper of birds Yahweh prepares — Rev 19:17-18 already drew on Eze 39's birds-supper). **John takes the Eze 38-39 vocabulary as the cipher for the final unsaved coalition** — every wicked person, raised, gathered against the holy city. **Note:** Eze 38-39 is _not_ a still-future earthly battle in Israel during a future millennium (the dispensational reading). It is the OT pattern that Rev 19:17-21 and Rev 20:7-9 fulfill — Eze 39's birds-supper at Rev 19:17-18, Eze 38-39's Gog-Magog coalition at Rev 20:8.
- "**To gather them together to battle**" — Greek _synagagein autous eis ton polemon_. **Same _synagagein_ root as Rev 16:14, 16's Armageddon gathering.** **There is one final gathering after the millennium, just as there was one final gathering before it** — Armageddon (the wicked living gathered to fight Christ at His coming, judged by sword from His mouth) and Gog-Magog (the wicked raised gathered to assault the holy city, judged by fire from heaven). **Both are Satan's last attempts; both fail catastrophically.**
- "**The number of whom is as the sand of the sea**" — Greek _hōn ho arithmos autōn hōs hē ammos tēs thalassēs_. **Compare Gen 22:17** (Abraham's seed "as the sand which is upon the sea shore"); **Hos 1:10** (Israel's number as the sand of the sea). **A vast multitude.** The promise once made for the redeemed is now applied negatively to the unredeemed dead.

## Rev 20:9 — The Camp of the Saints; Fire from God

> "And they went up on the breadth of the earth, and compassed the camp of the saints about, and the beloved city: and fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them." (Rev 20:9)

- "**Went up on the breadth of the earth**" — Greek _anebēsan epi to platos tēs gēs_. **_Platos_** — breadth, width. **The wicked-army covers the earth's full breadth** — the largest assembly of unsaved humanity in history, every wicked person ever raised, gathered. **Earth is full again, briefly, with all the wicked dead now standing on it.**
- "**Compassed the camp of the saints about**" — Greek _ekyklōsan tēn parembolēn tōn hagiōn_. **_Parembolē_** — encampment, military camp. **The OT-wilderness "camp" vocabulary** (Num 2 — the tribes camped around the tabernacle). **The redeemed are pictured as the encamped people of God, with the holy city in their midst.**
- "**And the beloved city**" — Greek _kai tēn polin tēn ēgapēmenēn_. **_Tēn polin tēn ēgapēmenēn_** — the beloved city. **Same beloved-city vocabulary as Ps 78:68** ("mount Zion which he loved"); **Ps 87:2** ("the LORD loveth the gates of Zion"). **Compare Rev 21:2** (the New Jerusalem descending out of heaven from God) — chronologically, the New Jerusalem has descended at the start of this final scene (or in connection with the second resurrection); the saints are inside it; the wicked surround it. **The OT-Jerusalem typology is fulfilled in the antitypical New Jerusalem** at this final assault.
- "**Fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them**" — Greek _katebē pyr ek tou ouranou apo tou theou kai katephagen autous_. **_Katephagen_** — devoured, consumed entirely. **Compare 2 Ki 1:10, 12** (Elijah — "fire came down from heaven, and consumed [the captains and their fifties]"); **Lev 10:1-2** (Nadab and Abihu — "there went out fire from the LORD, and devoured them"); **Num 16:35** (the 250 censer-bearers with Korah — "there came out a fire from the LORD, and consumed the two hundred and fifty"). **The OT pattern of consuming-fire** is the pattern Rev 20:9's fire fulfills. **It does not preserve; it devours.**

## Rev 20:10 — The Devil Cast Into the Lake of Fire

> "And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet are, and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever." (Rev 20:10)

- "**The devil that deceived them**" — Greek _ho diabolos ho planōn autous_. **Same _planōn_ as Rev 12:9** ("which deceiveth the whole world"). **Satan's defining work-title is the title of his judgment.**
- "**Cast into the lake of fire and brimstone**" — Greek _eblēthē eis tēn limnēn tou pyros kai theiou_. **The third member of the unholy trinity meets the same lake** the beast and false prophet entered at Rev 19:20. **The trinity is now complete in destruction.**
- "**Where the beast and the false prophet are**" — Greek _hopou kai to thērion kai ho pseudoprophētēs_. **The beast and false prophet have been there a thousand years already** (since Rev 19:20). **Whether they exist as conscious sufferers for that span or were destroyed and the lake awaits the dragon — the language allows both readings, and the "for ever and ever" phrasing has to be read against Rev 20:14 ("this is the second death"), Mal 4:1-3, and the consuming-fire OT pattern.** The historicist Adventist reading: the lake of fire is the final consuming-fire that executes the second death; "for ever and ever" names the permanence of the result, not the eternality of the burning.
- "**Tormented day and night for ever and ever**" — Greek _basanisthēsontai hēmeras kai nyktos eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_. **_Basanizō_** = torment, torture. **_Eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_** — "to the ages of the ages." **The Greek formula _eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_** appears 14 times in Revelation (1:6, 18; 4:9, 10; 5:13, 14; 7:12; 10:6; 11:15; 14:11; 15:7; 19:3; 20:10; 22:5). **The phrase names lasting/permanent reality** — applied to God's life and reign (positively) and to the verdict on God's enemies (judicially). **Rev 20:14's gloss — "the lake of fire... is the second death" — defines the lake's nature.** A "death" is a cessation of life, not infinite conscious continuation. **Compare Isa 34:8-10** (the day of the LORD's vengeance against Edom — "the smoke thereof shall go up for ever") — Edom is no longer smoking; the "for ever" names the verdict's permanence. **Compare Jude 7** (Sodom and Gomorrha "suffering the vengeance of eternal fire") — Sodom is not still burning; the fire's eternity is its result, not its duration. **The lake-of-fire torment "for ever and ever" describes the irreversible-permanent quality of the verdict on Satan and his system.**

[DYK🔎] **The "for ever and ever" of Rev 20:10 is biblical idiom for permanence-of-result, not infinite-conscious-duration.** Three converging lines settle this. **First**, Rev 20:14 itself glosses the lake of fire as "the second death" — and a death is a cessation of life, not its endless extension in conscious agony. The second death undoes Adamic mortality not by extending it but by terminating it. **Second**, the OT pattern Rev 20:9's "fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them" deliberately invokes — Lev 10:1-2 (Nadab and Abihu devoured), Num 16:35 (the 250 with Korah devoured), 2 Ki 1:10, 12 (Elijah's captains devoured), Mal 4:1-3 ("the day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud, yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble: and the day that cometh shall burn them up... it shall leave them neither root nor branch... ye shall tread down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet") — every OT "fire from God" instance is _consuming_, not preserving. **Third**, Jude 7 calls the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrha an example of "eternal fire," yet the cities are not still burning (2 Pet 2:6 — "turning the cities of Sodom and Gomorrha into ashes condemned them with an overthrow, making them an ensample"); Isa 34:10 says Edom's smoke "shall go up for ever" yet Edom no longer smokes; Jonah 2:6 calls the depths "for ever" though Jonah was there three days; Ex 21:6 has the bondservant serve "for ever" though that means his lifetime. **"For ever" / "for ever and ever" in biblical Hebrew (_le-olam_) and Greek (_eis tous aiōnas_) regularly names enduring/age-long reality bounded by context, not strict mathematical infinity.** The lake of fire's "for ever and ever" therefore names the verdict's irreversible permanence. **Satan, the beast, the false prophet, and the wicked dead enter the lake; they do not return; the universe is permanently free of them.** The fire consumes; the smoke as memorial of the verdict (Isa 34:10's pattern) testifies that the verdict was just and final. **Conditional immortality** — the doctrine that immortality is a gift conferred only on the redeemed at the resurrection (1 Cor 15:53-54 — "this mortal must put on immortality"; 1 Tim 6:16 — God "only hath immortality"; 2 Tim 1:10 — Christ "hath brought life and immortality to light through the gospel") — fits the second-death language: the wicked, never granted immortality, perish utterly in the lake. **Eternal conscious torment** is a post-Augustine doctrine that requires reading Rev 20:10's idiom flat-literal while ignoring Rev 20:14's gloss, the OT consuming-fire pattern, and the conditional-immortality texts. **The lake of fire is the second death — final, permanent, just, devouring.**

## Rev 20:11 — The Great White Throne

> "And I saw a great white throne, and him that sat on it, from whose face the earth and the heaven fled away; and there was found no place for them." (Rev 20:11)

- "**A great white throne**" — Greek _thronon megan leukon_. **_Megan_** — great. **_Leukon_** — white. **Same throne-vocabulary as Dan 7:9** ("his throne was like the fiery flame"); **Rev 4:2-5** (the throne in heaven — but Rev 20:11's throne is positioned for the final judgment, not the ongoing intercession). **"Great" = supreme jurisdiction; "white" = unimpeachable purity.** No higher court exists; no charge of unfairness can be sustained.
- "**Him that sat on it**" — Greek _ton kathēmenon ep' autou_. **The Father in Dan 7:9-14** (the Ancient of Days), the Son in Jn 5:22 ("the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son"). **The throne-occupant identity is explicitly Christ in NT-final-judgment passages** (Mt 25:31; Acts 17:31; 2 Cor 5:10) while remaining the Father's authority delegated to the Son. **One throne, divine occupancy.**
- "**From whose face the earth and the heaven fled away**" — Greek _hou apo tou prosōpou ephygen hē gē kai ho ouranos_. **_Ephygen_** — fled. **Compare Heb 12:26-27** ("Yet once more I shake not the earth only, but also heaven... the removing of those things that are shaken... that those things which cannot be shaken may remain"); **2 Pet 3:10** ("the heavens shall pass away with a great noise, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat, the earth also and the works that are therein shall be burned up"). **At the final judgment scene, the present cosmos is consumed.** The throne is so holy that the present-creation-order cannot stand before it.
- "**There was found no place for them**" — Greek _kai topos ouch heurethē autois_. **The old earth and old heaven have no continuing existence** in the new order. **Compare Rev 21:1** ("I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away"). **Rev 20:11 describes the dissolution; Rev 21:1 describes the replacement.** The two halves of the same transition.

## Rev 20:12 — The Books Opened

> "And I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God; and the books were opened: and another book was opened, which is the book of life: and the dead were judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works." (Rev 20:12)

- "**The dead, small and great, stand before God**" — Greek _tous nekrous, tous megalous kai tous mikrous, hestōtas enōpion tou theou_ (KJV/TR; some editions read _enōpion tou thronou_). **_Mikrous kai megalous_** — same "small and great" vocabulary as Rev 11:18, 13:16, 19:5, 19:18. **Universal classification.** No earthly status defends; no obscurity hides. **All the wicked dead stand together.**
- "**The books were opened**" — Greek _kai biblia ēnoichthēsan_. **Direct echo of Dan 7:10** ("the judgment was set, and the books were opened" — _biblia ēneōchthēsan_ in Theodotion's Greek). **The books that opened in heaven before the Ancient of Days at the start of the heavenly judgment-arc (1844) are the books that produce the final earthly verdict here.** Same image, same purpose, terminal stage.
- "**Another book was opened, which is the book of life**" — Greek _kai allo biblion ēnoichthē, ho estin tēs zōēs_. **The book of life** appears throughout Scripture: Ex 32:32-33 (Moses' intercession — "blot me, I pray thee, out of thy book"); Ps 69:28; Dan 12:1; Phil 4:3; Rev 3:5 ("I will not blot out his name out of the book of life"); Rev 13:8; Rev 17:8; Rev 20:15; Rev 21:27; Rev 22:19. **The register of the saved.** **The wicked dead at the great white throne are checked against the book of life and not found** (v. 15).
- "**Judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works**" — Greek _ekrithēsan hoi nekroi ek tōn gegrammenōn en tois bibliois kata ta erga autōn_. **_Kata ta erga autōn_** — "according to their works." **Compare Mt 16:27** (Christ — "shall reward every man according to his works"); **Rom 2:6** ("Who will render to every man according to his deeds"); **Rev 22:12** ("my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be"). **Works are the evidence of character; the books are the universe's judicial transcript.** The wicked dead are judged out of the records of their own lives.

[→] **The judgment-of-the-books thread completes here.** Dan 7:9-10 (the Ancient of Days, thrones, books opened — the heavenly court convenes for the 2300-day investigative judgment that began AD 1844). Dan 12:1 (Michael stands up; deliverance for those "found written in the book"). Mal 3:16 ("a book of remembrance was written before him for them that feared the LORD"). Rev 14:7 (the first angel — "the hour of his judgment is come" — investigative-judgment proclamation begins). Rev 11:18 (the seventh trumpet — "the time of the dead, that they should be judged"). Rev 20:12 — **the same books, the final session, the wicked dead judged out of their own records.** From Daniel's vision through 1844's pre-Advent investigative judgment of the saved through the great white throne's post-millennial judgment of the wicked, **the same court is in continuous session.** Rev 20:12 is the closing verdict.

## Rev 20:13 — Sea, Death, and Hell Give Up Their Dead

> "And the sea gave up the dead which were in it; and death and hell delivered up the dead which were in them: and they were judged every man according to their works." (Rev 20:13)

- "**The sea gave up the dead which were in it**" — Greek _edōken hē thalassa tous nekrous tous en autē_. **_Edōken_** — gave up, surrendered. **Every body the sea has swallowed — sailors lost, ships sunken, drowned multitudes — is surrendered.** No grave hides forever.
- "**Death and hell delivered up the dead which were in them**" — Greek _kai ho thanatos kai ho hadēs edōkan tous nekrous tous en autois_. **_Hadēs_** = the realm of the dead, the grave (Hebrew _sheol_). **Not the medieval Latin _infernus_ ("hell")** of conscious torment but the OT-Hebrew _sheol_ — the state of the dead, the unconscious-grave waiting-room. **Death and Hades are personified** as gatekeepers who now release everyone they hold. **Compare Rev 1:18** (Christ — "I have the keys of hell [_hadēs_] and of death") — the keys are now used to open the second-resurrection door.
- "**Judged every man according to their works**" — Greek _ekrithēsan hekastos kata ta erga autōn_. **Repetition of v. 12's standard.** Each individual is judged by his own record. **No collective verdict; no group salvation; no unjust generalization.** Each life is its own case.

## Rev 20:14 — Death and Hell Cast Into the Lake; the Second Death

> "And death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death." (Rev 20:14)

- "**Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire**" — Greek _ho thanatos kai ho hadēs eblēthēsan eis tēn limnēn tou pyros_. **The personified gatekeepers of v. 13 are themselves destroyed.** **Compare 1 Cor 15:26** ("the last enemy that shall be destroyed is death"); **1 Cor 15:54-55** ("Death is swallowed up in victory. O death, where is thy sting? O grave [_hadēs_], where is thy victory?"); **Hos 13:14** ("I will ransom them from the power of the grave; I will redeem them from death: O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction"). **Death itself dies in the lake of fire.** The instrument of Adamic mortality is consumed. **The grave itself is annihilated.** Rev 21:4's "no more death" is grounded in this verse.
- "**This is the second death**" — Greek _houtos ho thanatos ho deuteros estin, hē limnē tou pyros_. **The lake of fire = the second death.** Direct gloss; the chapter's interpretive key. **A "death" is a cessation, not an extension.** The second death undoes life — completely, permanently, finally. **Conditional immortality** — the doctrine that the wicked perish utterly rather than suffer infinite conscious torment — is anchored in this gloss. The lake of fire is _called_ the second death because that is what it _is_ — final extinction of the unsaved.

## Rev 20:15 — Not Found in the Book of Life

> "And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire." (Rev 20:15)

- "**Not found written in the book of life**" — Greek _ouch heurethē en tē biblō tēs zōēs gegrammenos_. **The single criterion** for the great white throne. **Not "those who did terrible deeds";** not "those who refused the gospel categorically"; not "those of certain denominations." **Whosoever's name was not in the book.** The names recorded in the book of life are those who, by faith in Christ, accepted God's offered righteousness; the names not recorded are those who refused. **The book is the record of accepted-grace.** Compare **Lk 10:20** (Christ to the seventy — "rejoice, because your names are written in heaven"); **Phil 4:3** (Paul on Clement and the rest — "whose names are in the book of life"); **Rev 3:5** (Christ to Sardis — "I will not blot out his name out of the book of life").
- "**Cast into the lake of fire**" — Greek _eblēthē eis tēn limnēn tou pyros_. **The same lake** that received the beast (Rev 19:20), the false prophet (Rev 19:20), the dragon (Rev 20:10), and now Death and Hades (Rev 20:14). **Every enemy of God's order — institutional (beast, false prophet), cosmic (Satan), existential (death, grave), and personal (the unsaved dead) — is consumed in the same lake.** The cleansing is total. **The second death falls.**

## After the Verses — What Rev 20 Settles

- **The dragon's binding fulfills the OT scapegoat-pattern.** Lev 16:21-22 — the high priest laid both hands on the live goat, confessed all the iniquities of Israel over it, sent it away by the hand of a fit man into the wilderness "unto a land not inhabited." **The antitypical Day of Atonement, completed in 1844-and-onward, sees Satan as the antitypical _Azazel_.** Rev 20:1-3 — Satan is laid hold on, the responsibility for the sin he originated is laid on him, he is sent into the desolated wilderness-earth for a thousand years. **Rev 20 is the antitype of the scapegoat-rite.**
- **The millennium is in heaven for the saints, not on earth.** Christ promised "I go to prepare a place for you... that where I am, there ye may be also" (Jn 14:2-3); Paul promised "we... shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord" (1 Th 4:17); John saw "thrones, and they sat upon them" (Rev 20:4) in the heavenly throne-room he has been seeing since Rev 4. **The dispensational picture of an earthly Jewish kingdom during the thousand years contradicts every NT text on where the saints are during this period.**
- **The earth is depopulated and desolate during the millennium.** Rev 19:21 has slain every wicked living person; 1 Th 4:16-17 has caught up every righteous person. The earth lies in the chaos-state of Jer 4:23-26's "without form, and void" (Gen 1:2's pre-creation chaos). **Satan, with his angels, walks this dead planet alone for a thousand years** — the chain that binds him is the empty earth itself.
- **The judgment "given unto" the saints (v. 4) is the millennial review.** 1 Cor 6:2-3 — "the saints shall judge the world... we shall judge angels." **During the thousand years the redeemed, with Christ, see every record.** Every "but what about my friend, my relative, this missing face?" is answered. Every charge against God's character is investigated and either upheld or refuted from the records. **By the end, every saint emerges with every question answered.**
- **The first resurrection vs. the second resurrection.** First — the righteous, at the Second Coming (1 Th 4:16; Rev 20:5b-6). Second — the wicked, at the millennium's end (Rev 20:5, 12-13). **The two resurrections are separated by exactly one thousand years.** Jn 5:28-29 collapses them in one phrase ("they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of damnation"); Rev 20 times them.
- **Gog and Magog of v. 8 is not a future Mideast battle.** The dispensational reading places Eze 38-39's Gog-Magog war in a future Israel during a millennial-kingdom period. **Rev 20:7-9 places it after the millennium, with all wicked dead raised, surrounding the New Jerusalem — not Old Jerusalem.** John's use of Eze 38-39 as the cipher for the post-millennial coalition is the inspired interpretation.
- **Phil 2:10-11 is fulfilled at this assault.** "Every knee should bow... every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father." Even the wicked, beholding the holy city and the records, acknowledge God's justice — not in repentance but in recognition. **The final vindication of God's character is universal and unanimous before the fire falls.** The great-controversy charge of unfair-tyrant-God is forever silenced.
- **The fire from God of v. 9 is the consuming fire of the OT pattern.** Lev 10:1-2 (Nadab and Abihu); Num 16:35 (Korah's 250); 2 Ki 1:10, 12 (Elijah's captains); Mal 4:1-3 (the wicked become ashes). **The fire devours; it does not preserve.** "Day and night for ever and ever" (v. 10) is biblical idiom for permanence-of-result, not infinite duration of conscious torment.
- **Death and hell themselves are destroyed (v. 14).** The lake of fire is the second death — not just a punishment-place but the executioner of mortality itself. **Adamic death, the grave, and the wicked dead all enter the lake together.** Rev 21:4's "no more death" is the direct consequence.
- **The book of life is the final criterion.** Rev 20:15's whosoever-not-found gloss settles the universe's sifting on one ground: name written or not. **The book is the record of accepted-grace.** Those who, in this life, accepted Christ's righteousness have their names there; those who refused have their names absent. **No other criterion determines the final destiny — works are the evidence; the book is the verdict.**
- **The unholy trinity is fully destroyed.** Beast and false prophet at Rev 19:20; dragon at Rev 20:10. **Three at the lake; three a thousand years apart.** The deceivers and the deceived perish in the same fire.
- **Rev 20 makes Rev 21 possible.** Without Rev 20's complete cleansing — Satan, the wicked dead, death itself, the grave itself — Rev 21:4's "no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain" would be a hope, not a reality. **The new heavens and new earth of Rev 21 require the great white throne of Rev 20.** Cleansing precedes new creation.
