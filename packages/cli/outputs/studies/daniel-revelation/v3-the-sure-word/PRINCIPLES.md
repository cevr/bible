# PRINCIPLES.md — The named-principle catalog

> Silent-reference document for [`SERIES-SPEC.md`](./SERIES-SPEC.md). Lists the interpretive principles _The Sure Word_ counters, without naming the author whose framework popularized them in modern Adventism. Each study may reference a principle inline by number — `[CONTEST P3]` — and let this catalog supply the substance to any reader who looks it up.

**What this document does NOT do.** It does not name the author. It does not engage personalities. It exposes _interpretive principles_ — the kind of move, not the man — and lets Scripture and Miller's 14 Rules adjudicate. The drifted reader recognizes their framework here without being attacked; the fresh reader gets a map of the traps the historicist reading avoids.

**Polemic direction.** The contested framework's signature move is **over-spiritualization of prophecies the pioneers (Miller, Litch, Snow, Bates, James White, Andrews, Loughborough, Smith) read as historically and geographically literal where the text and history warrant**. Pioneers were not naive literalists — they used the year-day principle (figurative time) and read Babylon as a symbol. The contest is not "literal vs. spiritual" in the abstract. It is: **where Scripture + Miller's Rules + fulfilled history have anchored a literal-historical reading (literal Turkey, literal Palestine geography, literal Revolutionary France in Dan 11:36-39, literal 1798/1840/1844 anchors), the contested framework dissolves the anchor into pure abstraction (papacy-only, principle-only, "spiritual" Babylon supporters), forfeiting the prophetic vindication the pioneer chain rested on.**

**Format for each principle:**

- **Claim:** what the principle asserts (the spiritualizing move)
- **What pioneers / DAR held:** the historicist reading being dissolved
- **Texts the move misreads:** the passages reframed
- **Rules broken:** which of Miller's 14 Rules the move violates, with one-line explanation
- **Historicist anchor:** the one-line corrective + primary source

## P1 — Spiritualizing the literal King of the North (Dan 11:40-45)

**Claim.** The "king of the north" in Dan 11:40-45 is the papacy in disguise — a purely symbolic religio-political actor with no literal geographic referent. There is no literal Turkey, no Ottoman empire, no Eastern Question; "north" and "south" name spiritual relationships, not real territories.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** The king of the north from v. 40 onward is **literal Turkey** (Ottoman empire, occupying the Seleucid northern territory of Alexander's divided empire). The king of the south is **literal Egypt**. The 1798 collision (Napoleon in Egypt; Sultan's declaration of war on France, Sept 2, 1798) marks "the time of the end" and is the first vindication of the literal reading. DAR 1432-1455 walks the Napoleonic campaign, the siege of Acre (1799), the British-Turkish recapture of Egypt (1801), and forward to the Crimean War (1853-56) as fulfilled prophecy. v. 45 (Turkey's end "between the seas in the glorious holy mountain") was held as still-future by the pioneers.

**Texts the move misreads.** Dan 11:40-45 in toto; the prophecy's own structural marker "at the time of the end" (v. 40) which signals a new historical theatre, not a continuation of the papal actor of vv. 21-35.

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule XI** (literal-first when it makes good sense and does no violence) — the literal-Turkey reading makes complete sense of every geographic name in vv. 40-45 (Egypt, Libya, Ethiopia, Edom, Moab, Ammon, glorious land, between the seas, glorious holy mountain) AND fits 1798-onward history exactly. Spiritualizing requires inventing non-textual referents for each name.
- **Rule XIII** (history vindicates prophecy by exactness) — DAR 1432-1474 documents the 1798 Egypt-Turkey-France triangle, the Acre siege, the 1801 fall of Cairo and Alexandria, the 1840 Aug. 11 ultimatum to Turkey (Litch's prediction vindicated to the day), and the Crimean War. The historicist reading has receipts. The spiritualized reading has none — it predicts nothing and vindicates nothing.
- **Rule XII** (trace the figure through the Bible) — "king of the north / king of the south" in Dan 11 is established from v. 5 onward as the literal geographic Seleucid and Ptolemaic divisions of Alexander's empire. The same actors continue at v. 40 — same geography, same naming convention. Switching to pure symbolism at v. 40 abandons the chapter's own consistent figure-tracing.

**Historicist anchor.** The literal-Turkey reading is the pioneer pillar: Miller, Litch (1838 prediction of Aug 11, 1840 vindicated), Smith (DAR), James White, Loughborough. Smith: "Egypt is still, by common agreement, the king of the south, while the territory which at first constituted the king of the north, has been for the past four hundred years wholly included within the dominions of the sultan of Turkey" (DAR 1433). To dissolve this is to dissolve the entire Eastern Question chain of vindications.

## P2 — Spiritualizing Dan 11:36-39 (collapsing Revolutionary France into the papacy)

**Claim.** Dan 11:36-39 ("the king shall do according to his will… neither shall he regard the God of his fathers, nor the desire of women… honor the god of forces") describes the SAME papal actor of vv. 21-35 — there is no transition. The atheistic specifics are read as general spiritual rebellion or as the papacy's "final form."

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Vv. 36-39 are a discrete historical fulfillment in **Revolutionary France (1793-1799)**, distinct from the papacy of vv. 21-35 and distinct from the king of the north of vv. 40-45. The French Revolution is the textually-required actor because only it fulfills the THREE specific markers DAR identifies: (1) open atheistic self-exaltation (v. 36 — the National Convention's 1793 decree declaring no God exists); (2) abolition of the marriage covenant (v. 37, "nor the desire of women" — the 1794 fornication-by-law decree); (3) worship of a "strange god whom his fathers knew not" + dividing the land for gain (vv. 38-39 — the Goddess of Reason 1793-1799 + confiscation of clerical and noble estates valued at £700,000,000). DAR 1381-1431 walks this in exhaustive historical detail.

**Texts the move misreads.** Dan 11:36-39 in full; especially v. 37 ("the God of his fathers" — Catholic France's ancestral religion which the Revolution explicitly repudiated); v. 38 ("the god of forces" — the Reason/Supreme Being cult instituted "as a political necessity" to hold the armies, per DAR 1406); v. 39 ("divide the land for gain" — the Convention's confiscation of two-thirds of the landed property of France).

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule I** (every word bears) — vv. 36-39 contain irreducibly atheistic specifics ("nor regard ANY god"; "honor the god of forces with gold, silver, precious stones"; "divide the land for gain"). The papacy is not atheistic and has never divided land for gain in a way that fits the verse. Forcing the papacy into this slot leaves multiple words unattached to anything.
- **Rule XIII** (exact historical fulfillment) — DAR documents the 1793 atheistic decree, the 1794 fornication law, the Goddess of Reason installation, the land confiscation, and the 1799 Napoleonic re-Christianization with primary-source citations (Scott's Napoleon, Alison, Lockhart, Thiers). The French Revolution fits every clause. No other actor in history does.
- **Rule IV** (form a theory without contradiction) — keeping the papacy as the actor across vv. 21-45 produces internal contradictions: the same actor cannot be simultaneously the patron of Marian devotion (papacy, "desire of women" in the historic reading is the Virgin) AND the abolisher of marriage (v. 37). The pioneer division (papacy → France → Turkey) resolves the text without contradiction; the single-actor reading does not.

**Historicist anchor.** DAR 1381: "Three peculiar features must appear in the power which fulfils this prophecy: (1) It must assume the character here delineated near the commencement of the time of the end… (2) it must be a wilful power; (3) it must be an atheistical power… A revolution exactly answering to this description did take place in France at the time indicated in the prophecy." This is also the source of P1's literal-Turkey reading — the same chapter's same primary source.

## P3 — Spiritualizing the literal Palestine battleground (Dan 11:41-45)

**Claim.** "The glorious land" (v. 41), "Edom, Moab, Ammon" (v. 41), "Egypt… Libyans and Ethiopians" (vv. 42-43), "tabernacles of his palace between the seas in the glorious holy mountain" (v. 45) — all dissolve into symbols of "God's people" / "papal opposition" / "spiritual territory." No literal Middle Eastern geography is in view.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Every geographic name is literal. The "glorious land" = literal Palestine (DAR 1457: "the 'glorious land,' Palestine, with all its provinces, here called 'countries,' fell back again under the oppressive rule of the Turk"). Edom, Moab, Ammon = the Bedouin Arabs who escaped Turkish subjugation (DAR 1457, citing Adam Clarke). Egypt = literal Egypt under Ottoman tribute (DAR 1468 quotes the actual Egypt-Turkey treaty: "six hundred thousand measures of corn, and four hundred thousand of barley"). Libyans, Ethiopians = literal Cushim / unconquered Arabs tributary to Turkey (DAR 1469). The "tabernacles of his palace between the seas" = literal Jerusalem (Mediterranean and Dead Sea), where Turkey was expected to make a final stand at her end — still-future at the time DAR was written.

**Texts the move misreads.** Dan 11:41-45; combined with Rev 16:12 (kings of the east); Joel 3:2, 12 (valley of Jehoshaphat) when these are read forward into Dan 11.

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule XI** (literal-first when it makes good sense) — every name in vv. 41-45 is a real Middle Eastern location with documentable Turkish-era political relations (Egypt's tribute treaty, the Bedouin's unconquered status). The literal reading makes good sense AND does no violence. Spiritualizing is unnecessary.
- **Rule XIII** (fulfillment exactness) — DAR shows the French campaign across Judea (1799, vv. 40-41), the British-Turkish recapture of Egypt (1801, v. 42), the Egypt-Turkey tribute (v. 43), and the Crimean War (1853, v. 44) as exact fulfillments. The spiritualized reading has zero historical anchors for these specifics.
- **Rule II** (all Scripture is necessary) — Dan 11's geography ties back to Gen 10 (Mizraim = Egypt, Cush = Ethiopia), the conquest narratives (Edom, Moab, Ammon), and the Maccabean-era northern/southern kingdoms. The whole-Bible context confirms literal geographic referents. Spiritualizing severs Dan 11 from its OT geographic vocabulary.

**Historicist anchor.** DAR 1457, 1468-1469, 1474. Smith ends the chapter: "We have now traced the prophecy of the 11th of Daniel down, step by step, and have thus far found events to fulfill all its predictions. It has all been wrought out into history except this last verse" (DAR 1475). The literal Palestine reading is what makes v. 45 a still-future hope; spiritualizing collapses the future expectation along with the past vindications.

## P4 — Spiritualizing the literal Euphrates and the Eastern Question (Rev 16:12)

**Claim.** "The great river Euphrates" of Rev 16:12 = the peoples / supporters of spiritual Babylon (papacy). "Drying up" = their withdrawal of support. "Kings of the east" = Christ and His angelic host, or pure spiritual deliverers. No literal Turkey, no literal Ottoman empire, no Eastern Question.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** A more nuanced position than either pure literal-river OR pure symbol-of-peoples. Smith's DAR 3376-3387 argues the **Euphrates symbolizes the Turkish empire specifically** — both because the historical Euphrates territory IS Ottoman territory, AND because Babylon's literal river was the channel that watered the literal city, so the figure in Revelation names "the power occupying the literal-Euphrates-watered territory." The drying = Turkey's progressive political collapse ("the Sick Man of the East"). The result, per Smith: "Turkey will be no more, and the way will be all open for the nations to make their last grand rally to the Holy Land" (DAR chapter heading 14.15: "Decay of Turkey — The Eastern Question — Spirits of Devils — The Battle of Armageddon"). The "kings of the east" then converge on the literal Palestine theatre where Turkey's end (Dan 11:45) sets the stage for Armageddon.

**Texts the move misreads.** Rev 16:12 stripped from its Daniel-11 backing; Rev 17:15 ("waters… are peoples") imported as the SOLE referent for "Euphrates" in 16:12, ignoring the specificity of "great river Euphrates" vs. generic "waters."

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule II** (all Scripture is necessary) — Rev 16:12 cannot be read without Dan 11:40-45 (the Turkey-Palestine theatre) and the OT Babylon-Euphrates typology (Jer 50-51; Isa 8:7). The spiritualized reading reads Rev 16:12 as if Daniel did not exist.
- **Rule VI** (the same thing revealed again in different figures) — the Eastern Question + Armageddon is the SAME end-game prophesied in Dan 11:45, Rev 16:12-16, and Joel 3. Three figures, one event. Collapsing Rev 16:12 into pure abstraction breaks the recapitulation.
- **Rule XIII** (history vindicates) — the Ottoman decline from 1840 onward is documented history. The historicist reading was making accurate predictions while the spiritualization was making none. The proof is the receipt.

**Historicist anchor.** DAR 3376-3387; chapter heading 14.15. Note: this principle is more nuanced than P1 — Smith allowed the "waters = peoples" reading of Rev 17:15 to apply where text-signaled, but anchored "Euphrates" in 16:12 to the Ottoman territory specifically because of Dan 11 backing. The contested move flattens this nuance into one-size-fits-all symbolism.

## P5 — Single-referent Babylon (papacy only)

**Claim.** Babylon in Revelation refers _exclusively_ to apostate Catholicism / papal Rome. Protestantism, fallen Christianity broadly, and the spiritualistic-revival end-time religious confederacy are excluded.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Babylon has layers, scripturally signaled. The mother (papal Rome) AND her daughters (Rev 17:5, plural — the apostate Protestant churches that retained Sunday, immortality of the soul, and other Romish doctrines) AND the spiritualistic confederacy that joins them at the end (Rev 16:13-14). The Second Angel's message (Rev 14:8 / 18:2-4) — "Babylon is fallen, is fallen" — was understood by the Millerites as applying first to apostate Protestantism's 1844 rejection of the Advent message (Fitch's 1843 sermon "Come Out of Her, My People"). Later the historicist reading sees Catholicism + Protestantism + spiritualism as the threefold confederacy.

**Texts misread.** Rev 14:8; Rev 17:1-5 (esp. "mother of harlots" — daughters presupposed); Rev 18:1-4; Rev 16:13-14 (threefold union).

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule I** (every word bears) — "the mother of harlots" presupposes daughter-harlots. Reducing Babylon to one referent erases the daughters.
- **Rule IX** (figures sometimes have two or more significations — but always Bible-fixed) — Babylon explicitly does: harlot, daughters, city. Three layers in Scripture itself.
- **Rule VI** (recapitulation across figures) — Babylon's biblical genealogy runs from Gen 11 (tower) → OT Babel → Daniel's literal Babylon → spiritual Babylon in Revelation. The figure has layers; collapsing it flattens the revelation.

**Historicist anchor.** Fitch's 1843 "Come Out of Her, My People" sermon (LCFMC, LJL); the Second Angel's message as historically understood from 1843 onward; later codified in pioneer writing as "fallen Christianity" — papal + apostate Protestant + spiritualistic.

## P6 — Year-day softening or rejection

**Claim.** The year-day principle is overstated, applies only in narrow cases, or yields too many "specific" fulfillments to be safe. Prophetic time periods (1260, 1290, 1335, 2300, 391y15d) are reframed as symbolic-of-duration, not literally measurable years.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Year-day is Scripture's stated key (Num 14:34; Ezek 4:6) and the foundation of the entire pioneer movement. Every fulfilled time prophecy is its receipt: 70 weeks → AD 27/31/34 (Dan 9, fulfilled in Christ); 1260 days → 538-1798 (Rev 12); 391 years 15 days → Aug 11, 1840 (Rev 9:15, Litch's 1838 prediction vindicated); 2300 days → 1844 (Dan 8:14, Miller). The Aug 11, 1840 vindication is the single most powerful historicist apologetic — Litch published the date in 1838, and Ottoman sovereignty was surrendered to the European powers on exactly that day.

**Texts misread.** Num 14:34, Ezek 4:6 minimized as ad-hoc; Rev 9:15 (391 years 15 days) treated as symbolic-of-duration; Dan 8:14 (2300 days) reframed; Dan 7:25, 12:7, Rev 12:6, 14, Rev 13:5 (1260 days) softened.

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule VII** (figures have Bible-fixed meanings) — Miller cites Ezek 4:6 explicitly under Rule VII. Year-day IS one of the fixed figures.
- **Rule XIII** (history vindicates) — softening year-day forfeits the historical vindication of 1798, 1844, 1840 (Aug 11). The 1840 case is unique: a published prediction, vindicated to the day, by a known mechanism (year-day applied to Rev 9:15).
- **Rule XIV** (faith — believing the plain text even when costly) — abandoning year-day is the interpretive cost of avoiding the conclusions year-day produces.

**Historicist anchor.** Litch, PREX1 / PREX2 (Prophetic Expositions, 1842) — the Aug 11, 1840 prediction and vindication. Miller MWV1 / MWV2 for the 2300-day calculation. Andrews S23D / SOTB for the 2300 days. Loughborough GSAM for the movement's whole story.

## P7 — Postponement of fulfilled prophecy

**Claim.** Prophetic events Adventism has historically dated to past fulfillment (1798 wounding of the beast, 1844 cleansing of the sanctuary, 1840 vindication of year-day, the 1789 French Revolution as Dan 11:36-39) are reopened, reframed as future, or made dual (past + still-future).

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Where Scripture and history have fixed a date, the date stands. 1798 = wounding of the beast (Rev 13:3; Berthier's capture of Pius VI) and end of the 1260. 1840 (Aug 11) = year-day vindicated by the surrender of Ottoman sovereignty. 1844 = 2300 days end, the heavenly investigative judgment opens. 1789-1799 = Dan 11:36-39 fulfilled in the French Revolution. These are pillars, not provisional readings.

**Texts misread.** Rev 13:3 read as still-future; Dan 8:14 reframed; Dan 11:36-39 reopened (see [[P2]]); Rev 9 trumpets future-applied.

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule XIII** (every word literally fulfilled = true history) — once a prophecy IS literally fulfilled, the interpreter has no warrant to "look for another event." Postponement violates the rule's positive side.
- **Rule III** (what God reveals He intends His children to see) — postponement implies the pioneers were wrong about what God did reveal to them. The pillars become provisional, awaiting modern revision.
- **Rule V** (Scripture its own expositor) — postponement typically requires importing a modern news-reading lens over Scripture's historical markers.

**Historicist anchor.** The full pioneer corpus (Miller, Litch, Smith, James White, Andrews, Loughborough) consistently anchors 1798, 1840, 1844 as fulfilled. EGW's reaffirmation across decades (the Great Controversy, Early Writings, GC88) cements these as the "platform" — pillars not to be moved.

## P8 — Selective literalization (geography literal, Babylon symbolic — applied INCONSISTENTLY)

**Claim.** When the prophecy's literal-reading suits the framework's conclusions, it is literal; when it doesn't, it is figurative. The hermeneutic shifts mid-verse without textual warrant.

**Note on direction.** This principle is inversion-symmetric — it cuts against BOTH ends of the spiritualization spectrum. The contested framework typically literalizes WHERE the framework needs a literal (e.g., "Israel" in some end-time texts when convenient), while spiritualizing WHERE the framework needs a symbol (Dan 11:40-45 Turkey, Rev 16:12 Euphrates). The pioneer answer is: apply the same hermeneutic consistently, governed by Rule XI (literal first when it makes good sense and does no violence) and Rules V-VII (Scripture defines its own figures).

**Texts misread.** Rev 16:12 (literal-vs-symbolic Euphrates depending on framework need); Rev 11:8 ("Sodom and Egypt" — literal or figurative within a single verse, picked to suit); Dan 11:41 ("the glorious land" — sometimes literal-Israel, sometimes pure symbol, depending on whether the framework wants a Mideast theatre or wants to avoid one).

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule I** (every word bears) — selective literalization treats half a verse as figurative and the other half as literal without a textual warrant.
- **Rule IV** (form a theory without contradiction) — the same hermeneutic produces opposite results in the same verse.
- **Rule XI** (literal-first when it makes good sense and does no violence) — Rule XI gives the procedure for the choice; the framework's choices ignore it and decide by conclusion-protection instead.

**Historicist anchor.** Miller's Rules themselves — especially Rules I, IV, V, VII, XI — supply the consistent procedure. Pioneer practice: literal where the text and history warrant (Turkey, Palestine, Egypt in Dan 11:40-45); symbolic where Scripture itself signals it (year-day, beasts, Babylon-as-system). The procedure is uniform; the conclusions follow the procedure.

## P9 — The two witnesses literalized

**Claim.** The two witnesses of Rev 11 are literal future prophets (often: Moses and Elijah returning, or two specific end-time individuals). The "great city" where they lie dead is literal Jerusalem. The 1260 days are literal future days.

**What pioneers / DAR held.** The two witnesses = the OT and NT Scriptures, prophesying in sackcloth through the 1260 years of papal suppression (538-1798), "killed" in the "great city" of revolutionary atheistic France during the Reign of Terror (1793-1795), "resurrected" at the end of the period (1798 onward, with the founding of Bible societies). DAR develops this exhaustively. The same French Revolution that fulfills Dan 11:36-39 (see [[P2]]) fulfills Rev 11:7-13.

**Texts misread.** Rev 11:3-12 in full; the 1260 days; the "great city … where also our Lord was crucified."

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule VII** (figures have Bible-fixed meanings) — the two witnesses are explicitly identified inside the text: "These are the two olive trees, and the two candlesticks standing before the God of the earth" (Rev 11:4). Zech 4 supplies the figure.
- **Rule VII** (year-day) — 1260 days in apocalyptic prophecy = 1260 years (Rev 12:6, 14; 13:5; Dan 7:25; 12:7). Literalizing forfeits consistency with five parallel uses.
- **Rule IX** (figures sometimes have multiple significations — but Bible-fixed) — "great city" in Rev 11:8 carries its own signal: "spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt." The text says "spiritually." Literal-Jerusalem ignores the textual marker.

**Historicist anchor.** DAR on Rev 11; the French Revolution as the historical anchor. Same actor as Dan 11:36-39.

## P10 — The "remnant" identified by Israel-relation rather than commandments + testimony

**Claim.** The end-time remnant is identified or shaped by its relation to literal Israel, Jewish-Christian observance, or Mideast eschatology — rather than by Rev 12:17's twin marks (commandments of God + testimony of Jesus).

**What pioneers / DAR held.** Rev 12:17 names the remnant's marks plainly: "which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ." Two marks. Both spiritual. Rev 19:10 identifies "the testimony of Jesus" as "the spirit of prophecy." The two marks together identify the SDA movement (Sabbath-keeping + prophetic gift) and exclude all other claimants. No third (Israel-related) mark exists in the text.

**Texts misread.** Rev 12:17 reframed around literal-Israel context; Rom 11 imported as defining end-time remnant geography; Zech 12-14 imported as the lens for Rev 12:17.

**Rules broken.**

- **Rule I** (every word bears) — Rev 12:17 names two marks. No third. No literal-Israel mark.
- **Rule V** (Scripture is its own expositor) — Rev 19:10 self-defines "testimony of Jesus." Both marks are spiritual; neither geographic.
- **Rule XIV** (faith — accepting the text's plain identification) — the literal-Israel-remnant overlay obscures the SDA self-identification grounded in Rev 12:17.

**Historicist anchor.** Pioneer self-identification from the 1840s-1860s as the Sabbath-keeping Adventist remnant marked by Rev 12:17 + Rev 14:12. Bates (BP1-3), James White (WLF, TTAM), Andrews (TMR).

## How studies cite this catalog

In a study body, a contested point may carry a `[CONTEST P3]` marker (referring to principle P3 above). The catalog supplies what the marker compresses. Studies are not required to cite by number — `[CONTEST]` alone may suffice — but when a study walks territory where the spiritualization-trap is concentrated and the corrective is dense, the principle reference saves boilerplate.

**Studies must never:**

- Name the author whose framework popularized these principles
- Quote the framework's literature
- Address the reader as having adopted the framework
- Polemicize against persons

**Studies must:**

- Consult DAR + pioneer corpus FIRST, before drafting (see SERIES-SPEC.md process gate)
- Let Scripture and Miller's Rules carry every corrective
- Treat the reader as a fellow Bible student
- Walk the text positively first — show the pioneer reading standing on its own — then surface the contest only where the text demands it
- End every study with appeal grounded in Scripture, not in opposition

## What changed from the first draft

The first draft had the polarity inverted. It treated the pioneers' literal readings (literal Turkey, literal Palestine, literal Revolutionary France) as the contested move and the spiritualization as the historicist default. The truth is the opposite: **the pioneers held literal where Scripture + history + Rule XI warranted; the contested framework spiritualizes those very anchors**. P1, P2, P3, P4, P7 in this version reflect the correct direction. P5, P6, P8, P9, P10 carry over largely unchanged. The earlier drafts' P10 (Dan 9:27 dispensational covenant) and P12 (millennium reopened) were removed as not directly attributable to the contested framework's signature moves — they belong to a different (dispensational) framework that overlaps only at the edges.
