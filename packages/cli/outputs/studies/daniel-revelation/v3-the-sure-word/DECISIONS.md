# DECISIONS.md — v3 (The Sure Word)

Q+A log from the grilling session that produced [`SERIES-SPEC.md`](./SERIES-SPEC.md). 17 decisions, in the order asked. Each entry: question, options offered, resolution, brief rationale.

---

## D1 — Which Were-position(s) should the series target as primary polemic?

**Options:** (A) literal-Israel pivot; (B) single-referent Babylon; (C) year-day rejection; (D) selective geography literalization.

**Decision:** **All four.** Maximal scope. The series targets the entire framework, not a single tenet.

**Implication:** A full counter-exposition, not a 6-session corrective. Drives the ~30-35 study count.

---

## D2 — How should the series be shaped around the polemic?

**Options:** (A) re-exposition with corrections woven in; (B) pure polemic head-to-head; (C) comparison series side-by-side.

**Decision:** **Use the framework's principles silently; do studies inline with `studies.md` format.** Don't name the author. Most readers don't know him; naming would credit and amplify. Refute the principles, not the person.

**Implication:** Forced reframe of the format choice (away from v2's devotional prose to studies.md's whiteboard outline). Drives every downstream format decision.

---

## D3 — Build the spiritualization-principle catalog upfront, or extract as we go?

**Options:** (A) catalog upfront; (B) extract from chapter-walks; (C) skip catalog.

**Decision:** **Build upfront.** `PRINCIPLES.md` is the silent reference behind every study.

**Rationale:** Without the catalog, each study reinvents its corrective framing. The catalog also proves we've understood the target before answering it.

---

## D4 — Spine ordering?

**Options:** (A) canonical, weighted; (B) thematic by principle; (C) pure canonical, even weight.

**Decision:** **(A) Canonical, weighted to contested chapters.**

**Rationale:** Canonical is what readers expect when they pick up "Daniel and Revelation." Weighting puts the polemic intensity where the spiritualizations live (Dan 7, 8, 9, 11; Rev 10-13, 16-18) and compresses uncontested chapters (Dan 1, 3, 5, 6).

---

## D5 — Pilot study choice?

**Options:** (A) Dan 11 (King of the North); (B) Rev 16:12 Armageddon; (C) Rev 11 two witnesses; (D) Dan 2 image.

**Decision:** **(A) Daniel 11.** Densest concentration of the literal-Israel pivot. Stress-tests every Miller Rule.

---

## D6 — Pilot scope?

**Options:** (A) Dan 11:40-45 only; (B) full Dan 11 single study; (C) full Dan 11 split into 3.

**Decision:** **(C) Full Dan 11 split into 3 studies** (vv. 1-22 / 23-39 / 40-45).

**Rationale:** Tests the sub-chaptering pattern as well as the format. The rest of the spine will need sub-studies on heavy chapters; the pilot proves the pattern works.

---

## D7 — Miller's Rules: how should they appear?

**Options:** (A) inline `[RULE N]` at point of contest; (B) standing sidebar; (C) closing "Rules check" section.

**Decision:** **(A) Inline `[RULE N]` at the point of contest.**

**Rationale:** Matches `studies.md`'s "no forced connections" rule. Surgical, non-repetitive. Rules do load-bearing work in 1-2 lines exactly where the contest lives.

---

## D8 — DAR weight in study bodies?

**Options:** (A) silent backing + sparing quotes; (B) constant inline reference; (C) Scripture-only body + Sources section.

**Decision:** **(A) Silent backing + sparing in-body quotes at adjudication points.**

**Rationale:** Per `studies.md`, Scripture stays load-bearing. DAR frames the historicist case but appears in the body only when Smith's adjudication is uniquely sharp. ~1-3 DAR quotes per study.

---

## D9 — Series name + framing?

**Options:** (A) "One Fulfillment, Not Many"; (B) "The Plain Reading"; (C) **"The Sure Word"**; (D) "The Year-Day Chain".

**Decision:** **(C) The Sure Word.** Anchored on 2 Pet 1:19.

**Rationale:** Warmer framing. Less polemical signaling. Frames the series as a return-to-foundations, not a corrective. Stronger pastoral move than the more thesis-naming options.

---

## D10 — Series directory name?

**Options:** (A) `v3-the-sure-word/`; (B) `the-sure-word/`; (C) `v3-historicist-corrective/`.

**Decision:** **(A) `v3-the-sure-word/`.**

**Rationale:** Preserves v1/v2/v3 evolution in the directory listing. Matches v2's `archive/v1-…` naming pattern. Short slug.

---

## D11 — Tone toward the drifted reader?

**Options:** (A) peer-teaching, never names the drift; (B) diagnostic, names spiritualization moves; (C) confessional first-person.

**Decision:** **(A) Peer-teaching, never names the drift.**

**Rationale:** Matches the "don't name the author" call. Reader is treated as a fellow Bible student. Texts and Rules do all correction. Reader feels accompanied, not corrected.

---

## D12 — Master-thesis study placement?

**Options:** (A) Study 01 — opens the series; (B) threaded into every chapter without standalone; (C) Study 01 + recapped at Daniel→Revelation pivot.

**Decision:** **(A) Study 01 = the master thesis ("literal = one; spiritual = infinite").**

**Rationale:** Gives the reader the interpretive lens before any text. Chapter-walks then demonstrate the thesis. Later studies can cite back (`[→ TSW01]`).

---

## D13 — Sub-chaptering pattern across spine?

**Options:** (A) split on natural break + contest density; (B) strict one-chapter-per-study; (C) pair short + split longest.

**Decision:** **(A) Split on natural break + contest density.**

**Rationale:** Dan 11 splits because its natural breaks ARE its contest boundaries. Rev 13 same. Short uncontested chapters stay single. Net: ~30-35 studies.

---

## D14 — Frontmatter beyond `studies.md` defaults?

**Options:** (A) studies.md defaults only; (B) add series-context fields (study_num, contested_principles, anchor_chapter).

**Decision:** **(A) studies.md defaults only** (`created_at`, `topic`, `apple_note_id` after export).

**Rationale:** Keep frontmatter minimal per `studies.md`. Series-context lives in the filename + body, not in metadata.

---

## D15 — SOP (EGW) authority pattern?

**Options:** (A) same as DAR (silent backing + sparing quotes); (B) heavier than DAR.

**Decision:** **(A) Same as DAR.**

**Rationale:** `studies.md` already mandates "confirming witness, not independent authority." Consistency with D8.

---

## D16 — Per-study length target?

**Options:** (A) `studies.md` default (no cap, glanceable); (B) cap at ~1500 words; (C) inherit v2's 1500-2500 budget.

**Decision:** **(A) studies.md default.** No cap. Optimize for the glance test. Typical lands 600-1500 words.

**Rationale:** A cap would force splits that the natural pattern doesn't need. The glance test is the real constraint; length follows from it.

---

## D17 — Export to Apple Notes?

**Options:** (A) yes, inherit v2; (B) markdown only.

**Decision:** **(A) Yes, inherit v2.** `bible export -f <file> --folder studies`. `apple_note_id` appended on success.

---

## D18 — Add `[CONTEST]` marker for spiritualization-trap points?

**Options:** (A) add `[CONTEST]`; (B) just `[RULE N]`, no new marker; (C) add `[CONTEST]` AND `[PIONEER]`.

**Decision:** **(A) Add `[CONTEST]`.**

**Rationale:** Marks the trap-points even when no Rule citation immediately follows. Lets the drifted reader feel recognition; lets a fresh reader skip past. Pairs with `[RULE N]` but doesn't require one.

---

## D19 — Process gate: consult DAR + pioneers before drafting any study?

**Options:** (A) make it a hard process gate in SERIES-SPEC.md; (B) leave as implicit "good practice"; (C) gate only the contested chapters.

**Decision:** **(A) Hard process gate.** No study begins until DAR commentary + pioneer FTS + EGW search have been run for the passage.

**Rationale:** The first draft of `PRINCIPLES.md` inverted the polarity of P1 / P5 / P6 / P7 (treated the pioneers' literal-Turkey + literal-Palestine readings as the contested move when they are the _defended_ historicist reading). The root cause was building the catalog from memory of the contested framework rather than from primary sources. That class of error is prevented only by sourcing before drafting. **The pioneer reading is the answer; assemble it first, then walk the text.** Catalogued in SERIES-SPEC.md Method §7 with a per-study source checklist.

---

## D20 — Daniel 11 pilot split (revised)

**Options considered:**

- (A) Original 3-way per D6 (vv.1-22 / 23-39 / 40-45)
- (B) 4-way with France standalone (vv.1-22 / 23-35 / 36-39 / 40-45)
- (C) Alternate 3-way (vv.1-35 / 36-39 / 40-45)

**Decision:** **(A) 3-way: vv.1-22 / 23-39 / 40-45.** Confirms D6 after polarity correction.

**Rationale.** DAR 1381-1431 still reads vv.36-39 as a discrete pioneer actor (Revolutionary France 1793-1799), but pairing it with vv.23-35 (papacy + 1260) in a single study is the cleaner pedagogical move because **both verses share the same contest** — the framework collapses vv.23-39 into "papacy throughout." Putting both pioneer actors (papacy + France) in one study lets the corrective land in one place: "the chapter shows two actors here, not one." A standalone France study (option B) would split the contest across two studies and force the reader to mentally re-engage the same correction twice. Option C (fusing setup with the papacy) wastes the natural seam at v.22 where literal Persia/Greece/pagan-Rome geography is locked in before the contested papal interpretation arrives. Net spine remains ~34 studies.

**Implication for TSW15.** TSW15 carries the highest contest density of any study in the series. It must show: (1) vv.23-35 = papacy + 1260 (1798 terminus); (2) v.35's "even to the time of the end" as the seam that opens to (3) vv.36-39 = Revolutionary France 1793-1799 with DAR's three markers (atheism / abolition of marriage / Goddess of Reason + land confiscation). One study, two actors, [CONTEST P2] anchored.
