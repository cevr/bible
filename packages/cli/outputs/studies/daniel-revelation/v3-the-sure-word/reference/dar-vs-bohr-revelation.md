# DAR vs Bohr — Revelation (Contested Chapters)

A verse-by-verse comparison of Uriah Smith's _Daniel and the Revelation_ (1897 ed., abbreviated **DAR**) against Stephen Bohr's published Revelation commentary from Secrets Unsealed / Anchor School of Theology (abbreviated **Bohr** below), restricted to the chapters of Revelation where the two streams diverge most sharply.

> **Sources used.** Bohr's Revelation position is now reconstructed from his dedicated volumes — primarily _Revelation's Seven Trumpets: A Contextual Approach_ (covers Rev 8-11) and _From the Close of Probation to the New Earth: A Study of Revelation Chapters 15 to 22_, with cross-references from _Studies on Daniel 11_ (SecretsUnsealed, 2022). PDFs are in `reference/bohr/`. The earlier draft of this document was reconstructed from scattered Revelation cross-references in Bohr's Daniel 11 volume and **significantly understated his divergence from DAR**, especially on Rev 9. This version corrects that.
>
> **Companion file.** See `dar-vs-bohr-daniel-11.md` for the Daniel-side comparison, including the foundational divergence (literal-vs-spiritual reading of Dan 11:40-45) that drives Bohr's Revelation handling.

---

## Why these six chapters

Both streams agree on a substantial spine of Revelation — the seven churches, the throne scenes, the New Jerusalem, the sea-beast = papacy identification. The friction is concentrated where the **identity of literal vs. symbolic players** in the prophetic chain is at stake:

| Chapter       | The contested question                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rev 9**     | Are the trumpets the **Saracens + Turks** (DAR — literal Islamic powers, 1299-1840, with Litch's 1840 prediction as the load-bearing vindication), or the **French Revolution + post-1844 demonic deception** (Bohr — Islam methodologically excluded from the prophetic chain, the trumpets read as Satan's end-time assault)? |
| **Rev 11**    | Is vv.7-13 **atheist France 1793-1798** as completed prophecy (DAR), or France 1789-1797 **as a type that recurs spiritually** in an end-time secular-power assault (Bohr)?                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Rev 13**    | What is the deadly wound's healing, and what triggers the image of the beast? Both agree on the basic frame (papacy + USA) but differ sharply on **chronology** and on whether **Spiritualism** is the chapter's central deceiving agency.                                                                                      |
| **Rev 16:12** | Is the drying-up of Euphrates the dissolution of the **literal Ottoman Empire** (DAR), or the symbolic drying-up of **Babylon's peoples-and-multitudes** per Rev 17:15 (Bohr)?                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Rev 17**    | Are the seven heads **seven forms of Roman government** (DAR — kingly, consular, decemvirate, dictatorial, triumvirate, imperial, Exarchate of Ravenna), or **seven successive world empires** allied with apostate religion (Bohr — Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome, civil powers of Europe, USA, global confederation)?    |
| **Rev 18**    | Is "Babylon" a new fallen body (apostate Protestantism — DAR), or **the same harlot of ch. 17 in her final globalized capitalist form** (Bohr — "Babylonian Capitalism," the Amazons / Facebooks / Googles)?                                                                                                                    |

---

## Foundational divergence (carried over from Daniel)

Bohr's Revelation hermeneutic inherits the Were-stream "automatic transition from literal to spiritual": fulfilled literal symbols get re-cast onto end-time spiritual players. Bohr articulates the principle directly in his trumpets volume:

> "An important prophetic principle is that after the cross, prophecy uses the same terminology as the Old Testament but removes the geographical limitations." (_Seven Trumpets_, p. 317)

DAR works the opposite direction: where the prophecy names a geography (Euphrates, Megiddo, the East), DAR keeps the geography literal unless an inspired writer explicitly says otherwise. The split runs through every contested verse below.

A second methodological driver in Bohr is his **exclusion of Islam from the prophetic chain**. He frames this as a consistency argument: Islam appears nowhere in Daniel 2, 7, 8, 9, 11-12, in the churches, the seals, Rev 12, Rev 13, or Rev 17 — so it should not be inserted into the trumpets either (_Seven Trumpets_, p. 6).

**Net for Rev 9 — total disagreement.** This is the chapter where the streams diverge most completely. They do not merely interpret the same data differently; **they identify different historical events as the chapter's referent.** DAR's framework: Saracens (1299-1449) + Ottoman Turks (1449-1840), with Litch's 1840 prediction as historicism's most public vindication. Bohr's framework: Enlightenment (1637-1789) + French Revolution (1789-1797) + post-1844 demonic deception, with Litch's prediction explicitly disowned as uninspired. **There is no overlap.** Bohr's positive identification of the trumpets:

> "The fifth trumpet (Revelation 9:1-12), Revelation 11:7-10 and Daniel 11:40 are all describing the same historical event: The French Revolution." (_Seven Trumpets_, p. 170)
>
> "The sixth trumpet carries us from 1844 all the way to the close of probation." (_Seven Trumpets_, p. ~before Lesson 8)

Bohr quotes Joseph Seiss at length to demolish the historicist Saracen reading, marshalling Seiss's argument that (i) Mohammed cannot be both fallen star and king of locusts (they are distinct in the text), (ii) the locusts were forbidden to kill but the Saracens killed in vast numbers, (iii) the locusts torment only those _without_ God's seal but the Saracens attacked Christians especially, (iv) "five months" = 150 years cannot stretch to cover Saracen activity exceeding 400 years, and (v) Abaddon/Apollyon was not Mohammed's name (_Seven Trumpets_, pp. 171-173). The Litch-1840 piece — DAR's most cited public proof of historicism — Bohr treats as a sincere but uninspired interpretation that God permitted to encourage the Millerite movement but that has no place in mature exegesis.

## Areas of substantive agreement (worth noting)

Despite the contested chapters, the two streams agree on a substantial spine:

1. **Papacy = sea-beast / little horn / man of sin / harlot.** Both unambiguously identify them. Bohr quotes James and Ellen White from 1847 (_A Word to the Little Flock_) for the equation.
2. **538-1798 = the 1260 years.** Both. No dispute.
3. **The deadly wound of 1798.** Same date, same agent (Berthier capturing Pius VI), same significance.
4. **The two witnesses = OT + NT.** Identical reading of Rev 11.
5. **France 1789-1798 as the historical fulfillment of Rev 11:7-10.** Identical (DAR uses 1793-1798 for the Bible-suppression decree window; Bohr broadens to 1789-1797 for the Revolution's full arc).
6. **USA = the two-horned lamb-beast of Rev 13:11.** Identical.
7. **The three unclean spirits = three great deceiving systems (dragon/beast/false prophet).** Identical structure (with the small wrinkle that Bohr labels the dragon "secular powers" where DAR labels it "Paganism").
8. **The image of the beast is future and involves civil enforcement of religious dogma.** Identical.
9. **The plagues are future and post-probationary.** Identical.
10. **The harlot is the Roman Catholic papacy** (Rev 17:1-6). Identical.
11. **The seven heads' three-phase chronology** (was / is not / shall be) maps to the papacy's pre-1798 / 1798-now / end-time-resurrected stages. Identical structural reading, even though they differ on which seven powers occupy the heads.
12. **The harlot is destroyed by the ten horns at the end (Rev 17:16-17).** Identical, though Bohr's ten horns are global and DAR's are western European.
13. **Loud Cry of Rev 18:1-2 = a second-angel's-message echo before the close of probation.** Identical.
14. **"Come out of her" (18:4) = the final separation of God's people from Babylon.** Identical.

The Were-stream's distinctive moves are concentrated in:

- **Rev 9** (Islam excluded from the prophetic chain; trumpets re-mapped onto French Revolution + post-1844 demonic deception)

## Observations

1. **The Rev 9 disagreement is total, not partial.** The earlier draft of this comparison described Bohr as "largely silent on the trumpets" — that was an artifact of working only from Bohr's Daniel 11 volume, where the trumpets surface only in fragments. With his dedicated _Seven Trumpets_ volume in hand, the picture changes: Bohr has a **fully developed alternative framework** for Rev 9 that disagrees with DAR at every single verse. Different fallen star (Satan vs. Saracen leader), different abyss (demon realm vs. Arabian desert), different locusts (demons vs. Arab cavalry), different five-months (1637-1787 vs. 1299-1449), different sixth trumpet event (post-1844 demonic deception vs. Ottoman empire 1449-1840), different "hour-day-month-year" (Oct 22 1844 vs. Aug 11 1840), and an explicit disowning of Litch. Where the earlier draft inferred Bohr's _silence_, his dedicated volume shows **active opposition**.

2. **Bohr's rejection of Litch is the most operationally significant departure from pioneer Adventism in this entire comparison.** Litch's 1838 prediction of the Ottoman fall on August 11, 1840 was the public proof that legitimized the Millerite movement to outside observers. _Great Controversy_ (p. 334-335) treats it as a divinely-given confirmation. DAR builds its entire trumpet exposition around it. Bohr, despite his Adventist commitments, concludes: "Josiah Litch's interpretation was not worked out under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit" (_Seven Trumpets_, p. 370). For a study series that aims to recover the prophetic spine of the 1844 message, this is a load-bearing point of friction.

3. **The Rev 17 seven-heads divergence is the most exegetically interesting.** DAR reads the seven heads as **internal to Rome** (Rome's seven successive forms of government). Bohr reads them as **Rome's place in the larger prophetic chain** (Babylon → Medo-Persia → Greece → Rome → modern Europe → USA → global confederation). Both readings claim grounding in the rule that "kings = kingdoms," and both can produce a coherent narrative. But Bohr's reading aligns Rev 17 with Dan 2 and Dan 7 in a way that DAR's does not, while DAR's reading aligns Rev 17 with the granular Roman political history that John's first-century readers would have known.

4. **Rev 16:12 functions as the Were-stream's most consequential single move.** Once Euphrates becomes symbolic peoples-multitudes (per Rev 17:15) and "kings of the East" becomes Christ and His angels, the entire literal-Ottoman / Eastern-Question framework that animated 19th-century Adventist preaching dissolves. DAR resists this move on the grounds that the symbolic-Euphrates reading is _available only because_ the angel later defines it in Rev 17:15 — but that definition applies to Rev 17, not retroactively to every Euphrates reference in Revelation. The disagreement is hermeneutical at the deepest level: when does an angel's later definition govern earlier symbols?

5. **Bohr's "Babylonian Capitalism" reading of Rev 18 is novel relative to DAR.** DAR sees Rev 18 as primarily a religious indictment with economic side-effects. Bohr inverts the emphasis: the economic system _is_ the Babylonian apparatus, with apostate religion as its theological scaffolding. The application to "the Amazons, the Facebooks, and the Googles" is recent and pastoral, and not something DAR addresses (couldn't have — 1897). This is one place where Bohr's reading adds material that DAR's framework does not directly contest; the two are compatible at the level of the harlot's identity even where they differ in centering economics vs. religion.

6. **Both streams cite the same EGW corpus, but with different center-of-gravity.** Bohr cites _Great Controversy_ p. 269 (France/Sodom), pp. 591, 603 (Loud Cry), and weaves _Faith I Live By_ p. 325. DAR cites _Spiritual Gifts_ / _Early Writings_ p. 137 (Rev 18 → US religious oppression) and the early Whites' _A Word to the Little Flock_ (which Bohr also cites for the 666/papacy equation). The dispute is not over Ellen White's authority but over which texts get foregrounded — and over whether her explicit endorsement of Litch's prediction (_Great Controversy_ p. 335) is normative for the interpretation of Rev 9:15.

7. **The methodological commitment matters more than any single verse.** Once Bohr's two foundational moves are granted — (a) Islam is methodologically excluded from the prophetic chain, and (b) after the cross, OT geographic terminology becomes spiritual-global — every literal symbol in the contested chapters re-types onto end-time spiritual players on cue. Once both are denied (as DAR denies them), literal symbols stay literal, the Eastern Question / Ottoman / Islamic frame is recovered automatically, and Litch's 1840 prediction stands as historicism's most public public vindication.
