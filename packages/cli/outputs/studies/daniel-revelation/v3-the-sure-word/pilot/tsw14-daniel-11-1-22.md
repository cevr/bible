# Bible Study: TSW14 — Daniel 11:1-22 (Persia → Greece → pagan Rome → the Cross)

**The chapter opens with plain history. Empires named, kings counted, dates verifiable. Before any verse becomes contested, Daniel 11 establishes its own vocabulary: literal Persia, literal Greece, literal Egypt, literal Syria, literal Rome, ending at the literal cross. Whatever the chapter means at v. 40, it must mean it in the same voice it used from v. 2.**

### Structure

```
vv. 1-2    Persia — three kings and a fourth (Cambyses, Smerdis, Darius, Xerxes)
vv. 3-4    Greece — Alexander and the fourfold division
vv. 5-13   The Diadochi wars — Ptolemies (south) vs. Seleucids (north)
v. 14      Rome enters: "the robbers of thy people shall exalt themselves"
vv. 15-22  Rome consumes Syria, Egypt, Judea — ending at "the prince of the covenant"
```

The chapter opens uncontested. Every name resolves to a person; every "year" resolves to a date. The interpretive habit that walks vv. 1-22 is the same habit that must walk vv. 23-45.

---

## The angel sets the scene — plain prose, not symbols

- **Daniel's last vision** — given B.C. 534, third year of Cyrus, six years after Dan 7, four years after Dan 8 and the 70 weeks of Dan 9
  - "_In the third year of Cyrus king of Persia a thing was revealed unto Daniel_" (Dan 10:1)
  - "_Now I am come to make thee understand what shall befall thy people in the latter days_" (Dan 10:14)
  - The vision answers Daniel's three weeks of fasting (Dan 10:2-3)
  - [→] The chapter that follows is the answer
- **Plain language, not symbols** — Daniel 2, 7, 8 used images, beasts, horns; Daniel 11 names actors directly
  - No statue. No four-headed leopard. No little horn. Just kings, armies, daughters, marriages, taxes, deaths
  - [RULE XI] Literal-first when it makes good sense and does no violence — Daniel 11 is literal-first by design. The chapter never asks to be decoded; it asks to be read

[DYK🔎] This is the last and longest single prophecy in the OT. From v. 2 to the end of Dan 12, the angel speaks without interruption — one continuous oracle covering Persia through the resurrection.

---

## Persia — three kings and a fourth (vv. 1-2)

- **"_Behold, there shall stand up yet three kings in Persia; and the fourth shall be far richer than they all_"** (Dan 11:2)
  - Cyrus is on the throne when the vision is given (Dan 10:1)
  - Three after Cyrus: Cambyses → Smerdis (an impostor) → Darius Hystaspes
  - The fourth: **Xerxes**, Esther's Ahasuerus — "_far richer than they all_"
  - [→] His wealth is the only specific the prophecy gives. The wealth funds the war
- **"_He shall stir up all against the realm of Grecia_"** (Dan 11:2)
  - Xerxes' campaign of B.C. 480 — Herodotus records the army at over five million men
  - The largest mobilization the ancient world had seen; defeated at Salamis and Plataea
  - After Xerxes, nine more Persian kings rule — but the prophecy passes them over silently
  - [→] The angel is selecting only the kings whose actions move the prophetic story forward

[Q] **Why skip nine kings?**
→ Because the next great mover is not in Persia anymore. The angel is tracking the arc from Persia's collision with Greece to Greece's rise. Xerxes' failed invasion lit the fuse that, a century and a half later, would bring Alexander east in revenge.

---

## Greece — Alexander and the fourfold division (vv. 3-4)

- **"_A mighty king shall stand up, that shall rule with great dominion, and do according to his will_"** (Dan 11:3)
  - Alexander conquered the known world in roughly six years
  - Died at Babylon B.C. 323, age 33
  - "_The greater portion of the then known habitable world_" (Prideaux Vol. I, p. 378)
  - The same actor as the leopard of Dan 7:6, the he-goat of Dan 8:5-8 — three figures, one man
- **"_His kingdom shall be broken, and shall be divided toward the four winds of heaven_"** (Dan 11:4)
  - Not to his posterity — both his sons murdered, his line extinguished
  - Divided to four of his generals: Cassander (Greece/Macedon, west); Lysimachus (Thrace/Asia Minor, north); Seleucus (Syria, east); Ptolemy (Egypt, south)
  - The fourfold division is the same prophecy as Dan 8:8 ("_the great horn was broken; and for it came up four notable ones toward the four winds of heaven_")
  - [RULE VI] The same thing is revealed again and again in different figures — Dan 2, 7, 8, 11 all walk the same Greece, four ways

[STR] **The Palestine-centered compass.** "_Toward the four winds of heaven_" — north, south, east, west — is reckoned from Palestine, the land of the prophet. This will matter at v. 5 when the angel names only two of the four: **king of the south** = Egypt (Ptolemy); **king of the north** = Syria (Seleucus, who absorbed Macedon and Thrace). The geographic vocabulary is set by Daniel's standpoint, not by a modern world map.

---

## The two kingdoms emerge — Ptolemies vs. Seleucids (vv. 5-9)

- **King of the south — Egypt** (Ptolemy I Soter, then Philadelphus, then Euergetes)
  - Annexed Cyprus, Phoenicia, Caria, Cyrene, "and many islands and cities to Egypt"
  - "_The king of the south shall be strong_" (Dan 11:5)
- **King of the north — Syria** (Seleucus I Nicator, then Antiochus I, II, III)
  - Inherited the largest portion of Alexander's empire after Lysimachus' fall
  - "_One of his princes... shall be strong above him, and have dominion_" (Dan 11:5)
- **The diplomatic marriage that fails** — Dan 11:6
  - Berenice (Egyptian princess) given to Antiochus II Theos under treaty
  - Antiochus' former wife Laodice poisons him, murders Berenice and her son
  - "_She shall be given up, and they that brought her, and he that begat her_" — every clause fulfilled in Laodice's revenge
- **Berenice's brother avenges her** — Dan 11:7-9
  - Ptolemy III Euergetes invades Syria, takes Seleucia, brings back captives and gold
  - "_Carry captives into Egypt their gods, with their princes_" — historian Jerome records 40,000 talents of silver, 2,500 idols
  - [→] Marriages, murders, military campaigns — every word matches an event the historians name

[DYK🔎] The Berenice-Laodice story (v. 6) is recorded by Polybius, Appian, and Jerome — three independent classical sources. Daniel wrote it over 300 years before it happened, in such specific terms that "_he shall not stand, nor his arm_" describes a particular poison-murder rather than a battlefield defeat.

---

## The wars sharpen — Antiochus the Great vs. Ptolemy Philopater (vv. 10-13)

- **The sons of Seleucus II stir up war** — Seleucus III (poisoned by his generals after 3 years) and **Antiochus III "the Great"**
  - Antiochus III recovers most of Syrian territory, then turns south against Egypt
- **Ptolemy IV Philopater meets him** — battle of Raphia, B.C. 217
  - "_The king of the south shall be moved with choler_" (Dan 11:11) — the luxurious and vicious Ptolemy is roused
  - Antiochus' army of 70,000+ "_given into his hand_" (v. 11)
  - Ptolemy's victory at Raphia could have ended the Seleucid kingdom — but he made peace and returned to feasting
  - "_His heart shall be lifted up... but he shall not be strengthened by it_" (v. 12)
- **Antiochus returns after 14 years** — Dan 11:13
  - Ptolemy IV dead from debauchery; his son Ptolemy V Epiphanes is a child of 4 or 5
  - Antiochus, having pacified the east, "_set forth a multitude greater than the former_"
  - The pieces are now in place for Rome to enter the story

[Q] **Why so many marriages and murders?**
→ Because the prophecy is being plain about the moral character of the actors it tracks. These are not symbolic figures whose vices can be theologized away. They are real men whose ambition, lust, and treachery moved the political map — the same map the angel will use when he reaches the cross at v. 22 and the papacy at v. 23.

---

## Rome enters — "the robbers of thy people" (v. 14)

- **"_In those times there shall many stand up against the king of the south_"** (Dan 11:14)
  - Multiple powers turn against the child-king Ptolemy V: Antiochus III from the north, Philip V of Macedon from the west, even rebellions inside Egypt
  - And: "_Also the robbers of thy people shall exalt themselves to establish the vision_"
- **"_The robbers of thy people_" — Rome makes its entrance** — B.C. 200
  - Rome interferes in defense of the infant Egyptian king (Rollin, Ancient History Bk 18 ch 50)
  - First Roman intervention in the Syria-Egypt theatre
  - The Hebrew literally reads "_sons of the violent of thy people_" — Daniel's own people (Jews) had Roman alliance from B.C. 161 (1 Maccabees 8)
  - [→] The angel marks Rome's entrance the moment Rome touches the prophecy's geography
- "_To establish the vision_"
  - The Romans are "_more prominently than any other people the subject of Daniel's prophecy_" — the fourth kingdom of Dan 2:40, the dreadful beast of Dan 7:7, the king of fierce countenance of Dan 8:23
  - The same actor, fourth time named — each figure adds detail

[RULE VI] Recapitulation — Rome is the iron of Dan 2, the dreadful beast of Dan 7, the king of fierce countenance of Dan 8, and now the robbers of v. 14. Four figures, one power. The Bible identifies its own actors by repeating them.

---

## Rome consumes Syria, Egypt, Judea (vv. 15-20)

- **Syria falls** — vv. 15-16
  - Scopas (Egyptian general) defeated by Antiochus III at Panium, besieged at Sidon, surrenders
  - But Rome turns on Antiochus next: Pompey deprives Antiochus XIII Asiaticus of his kingdom, B.C. 65 — Syria becomes a Roman province
  - "_He shall stand in the glorious land, which by his hand shall be consumed_" (v. 16) — Pompey enters Jerusalem B.C. 63
- **Judea conquered** — vv. 16-17
  - Aristobulus vs. Hyrcanus dispute brings Pompey to Jerusalem
  - 12,000 slain in the temple-mount assault; walls demolished; Jerusalem becomes tributary
  - "_He shall set his face to enter with the strength of his whole kingdom_" (v. 17) — Rome consolidates the eastern Mediterranean
- **Egypt falls — Julius Caesar and Cleopatra** — vv. 17b-19
  - "_He shall give him the daughter of women, corrupting her_" (v. 17)
  - Caesar adjudicates between Ptolemy XIII and Cleopatra VII; Cleopatra's smuggled audience (Plutarch); battle of the Nile, B.C. 47
  - "_She shall not stand on his side, neither be for him_" — Cleopatra later sides with Antony against Octavian
  - Caesar returns to Rome, "_the fort of his own land_" (v. 19); assassinated B.C. 44 in the senate, "_pierced with twenty-three wounds_"
  - "_He shall stumble and fall, and not be found_" — sudden, not in battle
- **Augustus — "_a raiser of taxes in the glory of the kingdom_"** (v. 20)
  - "_There went out a decree from Caesar Augustus, that all the world should be taxed_" (Luke 2:1)
  - The Pax Romana — "_in the glory of the kingdom_"
  - Dies peacefully in bed, A.D. 14 at Nola
  - "_Within few days he shall be destroyed, neither in anger, nor in battle_" — 18 years from the taxing to his death, "_few days_" to the prophet's distant gaze

[DYK🔎] The single most-named verse in this section is Augustus' tax decree — and it is the verse that put Mary and Joseph on the road to Bethlehem. Dan 11:20 and Luke 2:1 are the same event.

---

## Tiberius — the vile person, and the prince of the covenant (vv. 21-22)

- **"_In his estate shall stand up a vile person_"** (Dan 11:21)
  - Tiberius Caesar — Augustus' stepson, not first choice for the throne
  - Augustus had said: "_Your son is too vile to wear the purple of Rome_" — but eventually consented
  - Tacitus' record: tyranny, hypocrisy, debauchery, retirement to Capreae A.D. 26
  - "_They shall not give him the honor of the kingdom_" — never loved, never respected
- **"_He shall come in peaceably, and obtain the kingdom by flatteries_"** — succession without opposition, by the "servile senate"
- **"_Yea, also the prince of the covenant_"** (Dan 11:22)
  - "_The Messiah the Prince_" who was to "_confirm the covenant_" one week with His people (Dan 9:25-27)
  - Luke 3:1 — John the Baptist begins his ministry "_in the fifteenth year of the reign of Tiberius Caesar_"
  - The 15th year of Tiberius (reckoned from his joint reign with Augustus, A.D. 12) = A.D. 26-27
  - Christ baptized autumn A.D. 27 (end of the 69 weeks of Dan 9:25)
  - Crucified spring A.D. 31 (midpoint of the 70th week — Dan 9:27)
  - "_He shall be broken_" — the prince of the covenant cut off

[CONTEST P10] Some readings break Dan 9:24-27 from Dan 11:22 and reassign "the prince of the covenant" to a future Antichrist's seven-year treaty. The text refuses the move: vv. 21-22 are Tiberius, and "the prince of the covenant" is the Messiah whose death dated in Tiberius' reign. Daniel 9 and Daniel 11 are one prophetic system, fulfilled at one cross.

[RULE XIII] History vindicates by exactness. From v. 2 (Xerxes' invasion of Greece, B.C. 480) to v. 22 (Christ's death under Tiberius, A.D. 31), every clause has a date, every actor a name, every event a historian to record it. **511 years of prophecy fulfilled to the calendar.** The chapter does not ask the reader to trust its later verses on faith — it earns them on receipts.

## What this chapter has established (before any contest arrives)

- **The vocabulary is literal-geographic.** "King of the north" = the power on the Seleucid territory (Syria, later absorbed into Rome). "King of the south" = the power on the Ptolemaic territory (Egypt). The angel said so at v. 5; the events of vv. 6-13 proved it; nothing in the text reassigns the meaning later
- **The actors are real persons.** Cambyses, Xerxes, Alexander, Berenice, Laodice, Ptolemy Euergetes, Antiochus III, Scopas, Pompey, Julius Caesar, Cleopatra, Augustus, Tiberius, **Jesus Christ**. Every name resolves
- **The Rule that walks the chapter is Rule XI.** Literal-first when it makes good sense and does no violence. From v. 2 to v. 22 it makes good sense at every step, and never does violence to any other Scripture
- **The interpretive habit must continue.** Whatever the chapter means at vv. 23-45, it must use the same vocabulary, the same actors-are-real, and the same literal-first procedure. To pivot to pure symbolism mid-chapter would be to abandon the chapter's own method

[CONTEST] Where vv. 1-22 are uncontested, vv. 23-45 are the storm. But the storm cannot rewrite the harbor. The vocabulary established here is the vocabulary the next two studies will defend.

---

## Appeal

- The God who named Xerxes 200 years before he was born, and Tiberius 500 years before he ruled, named **the cross** in the same sentence
- "_Yea, also the prince of the covenant_" — the central event of the chapter is the central event of history
- The same precision that wrote v. 22 wrote v. 45
- Trust the text. The next two studies walk into harder ground. This study is the foundation: **the chapter speaks plainly, the actors are real, and the literal-historical reading earns its right to be trusted by what it has already proved**
- _"We have also a more sure word of prophecy; whereunto ye do well that ye take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place"_ (2 Pet 1:19)
