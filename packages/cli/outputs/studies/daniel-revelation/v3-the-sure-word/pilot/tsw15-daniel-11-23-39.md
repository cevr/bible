# Bible Study: TSW15 — Daniel 11:23-39 (Pagan Rome → Papal Rome → Revolutionary France)

**The chapter does not pause at v. 22. The same literal voice that walked Persia, Greece, and pagan Rome walks straight into the rise of papal Rome (vv. 23-35), and then — at v. 36 — pivots to a _new_ actor: the atheistic, marriage-abolishing republic of France 1793-1799. Reading vv. 23-39 as "the papacy throughout" collapses two distinct powers into one and silences the chapter's most precise vindication. There are two actors here, not one.**

### Structure

```
vv. 23-30   Pagan Rome consolidates — Actium B.C. 31 → Jerusalem A.D. 70 → Constantinople A.D. 330 → Vandal wars
v. 31       508 / 533 / 538 — paganism taken away, the daily ends, the papacy "set up"
vv. 32-34   1260 years of papal supremacy; the Reformation as "little help"
v. 35       "even to the time of the end" — 1798, the papal wound
vv. 36-39   A NEW actor: atheist Revolutionary France 1793-1799
```

The seam is v. 35 → v. 36. The 1260 closes; the time of the end opens; the angel introduces a king who will not regard any god — and the papacy is not that king.

## The league with the Jews — Rome rises with "a small people" (vv. 23-24)

- **"_After the league made with him he shall work deceitfully: for he shall come up, and shall become strong with a small people_"** (Dan 11:23)
  - The league: B.C. 161, between Judas Maccabeus and the Roman senate (1 Maccabees 8)
  - Rome at this point is "_a small people_" — one city-state on the Tiber, a small republic among giants
  - From this league forward, Rome's power grows by stages until it absorbs Syria (B.C. 65), Judea (B.C. 63), and Egypt (B.C. 30)
- **"_He shall enter peaceably even upon the fattest places of the province... he shall scatter among them the prey, and spoil, and riches_"** (Dan 11:24)
  - Rome's policy: confederacies first, conquest second; territory distributed to allies, plunder distributed to soldiers
  - "_His fathers have not done, nor his fathers' fathers_" — the Roman method was new to the world
  - [RULE XIII] Every word fits the historians' record of the Roman republic's expansion

## Actium — pagan Rome consummated (vv. 25-27)

- **"_He shall stir up his power and his courage against the king of the south with a great army_"** (Dan 11:25)
  - Octavian (Caesar Augustus) against Antony and Cleopatra
  - Battle of Actium, September 2, B.C. 31
- **"_The king of the south shall be stirred up to battle with a very great and mighty army... but they shall forecast devices against him_"**
  - Antony's force on paper: ~500 ships, 100,000 foot, 12,000 horse
  - Treachery: Cleopatra flees mid-battle with 60 ships; Antony abandons command to follow her; the rest surrender
  - "_They that feed of the portion of his meat shall destroy him_" — Antony was destroyed by his own commanders' defection
- **"_Both these kings' hearts shall be to do mischief, and they shall speak lies at one table_"** (Dan 11:27)
  - The first triumvirate (Caesar, Pompey, Crassus); later Octavian, Antony, Lepidus — alliances of convenience and betrayal
  - "_The end shall be at the time appointed_" — Augustus emerges sole emperor, B.C. 30
  - The same Augustus whose decree at v. 20 sent Mary and Joseph to Bethlehem

## Jerusalem and Constantinople (vv. 28-29)

- **"_He shall return into his land with great riches; and his heart shall be against the holy covenant_"** (Dan 11:28)
  - Vespasian and Titus return to Rome after the Jewish War, A.D. 70
  - Jerusalem destroyed; 1,100,000 dead by Josephus' count; 97,000 enslaved
  - "_His heart shall be against the holy covenant_" — Rome turns against the Christian church as well; ten persecutions, A.D. 64-313
- **"_At the time appointed he shall return, and come toward the south_"** (Dan 11:29)
  - The seat of empire moves east — Constantine founds Constantinople, A.D. 330
  - "_But it shall not be as the former, or as the latter_" — the new Rome cannot match the imperial unity of the old
  - The empire begins to split; the western capital weakens; the bishop of Rome rises into the vacuum

## The Vandals — "ships of Chittim" (v. 30)

- **"_The ships of Chittim shall come against him: therefore he shall be grieved, and return_"** (Dan 11:30)
  - "Chittim" = the western Mediterranean coastlands (Cyprus, Carthage, North Africa — Gen 10:4, Num 24:24)
  - The Vandals under Genseric sack Rome from Carthage, A.D. 455; the western empire collapses, A.D. 476
  - "_He shall have indignation against the holy covenant... and have intelligence with them that forsake the holy covenant_"
  - As pagan Rome falls, the bishop of Rome makes intelligence with apostatizing Christians — Sunday legislation, Mariolatry, the merger of pagan rite with Christian form
  - [→] The text marks the transition from pagan to papal Rome **at this verse**, not at v. 36

## 508 — 533 — 538: the papacy "set up" (v. 31)

- **"_Arms shall stand on his part, and they shall pollute the sanctuary of strength, and shall take away the daily, and they shall place the abomination that maketh desolate_"** (Dan 11:31)
- **The daily (Heb. _hattamid_) = continual paganism** — the Roman state religion
  - Pioneer reading (Miller MWV1 1320.366; Litch; Smith): "the daily" = the daily/continual worship of paganism that Rome enforced on the empire
  - "_Taken away_" — paganism legally and politically dismantled
- **508 — Clovis is baptized into Catholic Christianity, the last Arian/pagan resistance in Gaul collapses**
  - Anastasius (Eastern emperor, last pagan-tolerant) dies 518; Symmachus (Catholic Pope) consolidates Rome
  - The political/legal framework of paganism is "_taken away_"
- **533 — Justinian's decree** to Pope John II: the bishop of Rome is named "_head of all the holy churches_" and "_corrector of heretics_"
  - The decree exists on paper but cannot be enforced while the Arian Ostrogoths hold Italy
- **538 — Belisarius drives the Ostrogoths from Rome** (siege ends March, A.D. 538)
  - The last of the three Arian horns (Heruli 493, Vandals 534, Ostrogoths 538 — Dan 7:8, 24) is plucked up
  - With the Arian check removed, the Justinian decree takes legal force on the ground
  - The 1260 years of papal supremacy **begin**
- **"_The abomination that maketh desolate_" — papal Rome occupies the seat that pagan Rome vacated**
  - Christ Himself names this transition the abomination (Matt 24:15)
  - The same vocabulary at Dan 8:13, Dan 12:11, Rev 13:1-10

[RULE XIII] Three dates locked: **508** (paganism removed), **533** (decree), **538** (decree enforced). The 1260 closes at **1798** — Berthier captures Pope Pius VI, Feb 10, 1798. Subtract: 1798 - 538 = 1260. The math is not optional.

## The 1260 — flatteries, persecution, and "a little help" (vv. 32-34)

- **"_Such as do wickedly against the covenant shall he corrupt by flatteries_"** (Dan 11:32)
  - Papal Rome corrupts not primarily by sword but by reward — honors, offices, indulgences, dispensations
  - "_The people that do know their God shall be strong, and do exploits_" — the witnesses through the dark centuries; Waldenses, Albigenses, Wycliffe, Hus, the early Reformers
- **"_They that understand among the people shall instruct many: yet they shall fall by the sword, and by flame, by captivity, and by spoil, many days_"** (Dan 11:33)
  - "_Many days_" = the 1260 years (Dan 7:25; Rev 12:6, 14; 13:5)
  - Sword (Inquisition), flame (Smithfield, Constance, Florence), captivity (papal prisons), spoil (confiscation of Waldensian and Lollard property)
  - Conservative Protestant historians have estimated 50-100 million dead across the 1260 years; Smith cites the higher figure
- **"_When they shall fall, they shall be holpen with a little help_"** (Dan 11:34)
  - The Reformation — Luther (1517), Zwingli, Calvin, the English Reformers
  - "_A little help_" — significant, but partial; the daughters of Babylon brought their mother's errors with them (immortality of soul, infant baptism, Sunday observance, union of church and state)
  - "_Many shall cleave to them with flatteries_" — political Protestantism, state churches, alliances of convenience

## "Even to the time of the end" — the 1798 seam (v. 35)

- **"_Some of them of understanding shall fall, to try them, and to purge, and to make them white, even to the time of the end: because it is yet for a time appointed_"** (Dan 11:35)
  - The persecution of God's people continues — but only "_to the time of the end_"
  - The "_time of the end_" is the _terminus_ of the 1260, not its midpoint
  - **1798** — Berthier enters Rome, takes Pope Pius VI prisoner, abolishes papal sovereignty (the same wound of Rev 13:3, "_one of his heads as it were wounded to death_")
  - The verse closes a section. The 1260 ends. The "_time of the end_" begins
  - [→] What the angel describes after v. 35 happens _in the time of the end_, not before

## [CONTEST P2] — The seam at v. 35 → v. 36

- The framework that spiritualizes Daniel 11 reads vv. 36-39 as _more papacy_ — same actor, continuing description
- The text refuses this collapse on three grounds:

**1. "_The king shall do according to his will_" (v. 36) introduces a power, not continues one**

- The Hebrew construction marks a shift, not a continuation
- Mede, Wintle, Boothroyd, and other Hebraists translate: "_A certain king shall do according to his will_" (DAR 1378)
- The angel has used this construction before — at v. 3 (Alexander), v. 21 (Tiberius) — every time to introduce a new actor

**2. The atheistic specifications cannot fit the papacy**

- v. 37: "_Neither shall he regard the God of his fathers, nor the desire of women, nor regard any god_"
- The papacy has _always professed_ the God of its fathers; it has _always_ regarded a god (its own version); it has _always_ held marriage as a sacrament (even when corrupting clerical celibacy into Mariolatry)
- "_This has never been true of the papacy_" — DAR 1378
- [RULE I] Every word must have its proper bearing. "Regards no god" cannot mean "regards God wrongly"; the words mean what they say

**3. The time-marker forbids the collapse**

- v. 35 ends "_even to the time of the end_" — the 1260 closes at 1798
- vv. 36-39 describe events _in the time of the end_
- The papacy's 1260 is _over_ by the time v. 36 opens
- [RULE IV] To get the truth, no Scripture must be left to contradict another. To make the papacy continue past 1798 in this passage flatly contradicts Dan 7:25, Rev 13:3, Rev 13:5

## Revolutionary France — the atheist republic 1793-1799 (vv. 36-39)

- **"_The king shall do according to his will; and he shall exalt himself, and magnify himself above every god, and shall speak marvelous things against the God of gods_"** (Dan 11:36)
  - August 26, 1792 — the French National Convention makes "_an open profession of atheism_"
  - 1793 — the Bible publicly burned; the Deity "_denied as the voice of the nation_" (Scott, _Napoleon_ Vol. I, p. 172-173)
  - "_Shall prosper till the indignation be accomplished_" — the Reign of Terror runs its full course; only Napoleon (1799) terminates it

- **"_Neither shall he regard the God of his fathers, nor the desire of women, nor regard any god_"** (Dan 11:37)
  - **God of his fathers** — France was the eldest daughter of the Roman Catholic Church; Christianity was the religion of her fathers; she renounced it
  - **The desire of wives** (Heb. _chemdat nashim_; Bishop Newton's preferred rendering) — the marriage covenant
    - 1792 — marriage redefined "_a mere civil contract of a transitory character, which any two persons might engage in and cast loose at pleasure_" (Scott, _Napoleon_ Vol. I, p. 173)
    - The actress Sophie Arnoult called it "_the sacrament of adultery_"
  - **Regard any god** — Hebert and Chaumette "_declared that God did not exist_" (Alison Vol. I, p. 150)
    - "_The Supreme King, the God of the Jews and the Christians, is but a phantom. Jesus Christ is an impostor_" (record of the Convention's atheist clubs)
  - Every clause of the verse documented in the historians; no other regime in history fits

[STR] **Why this matters.** The framework reads "_the desire of women_" as the celibacy of the priesthood — the papacy "_not desiring women_." But the Hebrew is "_desire of wives_" — the _institution_ of marriage, not the celibate's avoidance of it. Pioneer (Newton, Smith) reading: France abolished marriage by statute in 1792. There is a regime that did so. There is no need to allegorize.

- **"_But in his estate shall he honor the God of forces: and a god whom his fathers knew not shall he honor with gold, and silver, and with precious stones, and pleasant things_"** (Dan 11:38)
  - After the atheist phase produced anarchy, the Convention introduced a substitute worship
  - **November 1793** — the **Goddess of Reason** installed in Notre Dame, personified by a dancing girl of the opera (Madame Millard), elevated on the high altar, "_received the adoration of all present_" (Scott)
  - "_A god whom his fathers knew not_" — Reason as a deity had no Catholic, no Christian, no Jewish, no even classical-pagan precedent in France
  - 1794 — Robespierre instituted the worship of the **Supreme Being** as a more powerful sanction to "_hold the people_" (Thiers, _French Revolution_)
  - **God of forces** (Heb. _eloah ma'uzzim_) — "the god of fortifications/military strength"; the renewed vows were vows "_for the prosperity of the armies of the Republic_" (Chaumette's address)

- **"_Thus shall he do in the most strong holds with a strange god, whom he shall acknowledge and increase with glory: and he shall cause them to rule over many, and shall divide the land for gain_"** (Dan 11:39)
  - The Goddess of Reason and the cult of the Supreme Being were enforced "_in the strongholds_" — Notre Dame and the great cathedrals seized as state temples
  - **"_Divide the land for gain_"** — perhaps the single most distinctive identifier
    - Pre-Revolution French law held landed estates indivisible
    - The Convention confiscated the estates of nobility, clergy, and emigrants — "_two thirds of the landed property of the kingdom_" — worth above **£700,000,000 sterling** (Alison Vol. IV, p. 151)
    - The land was sold at auction "_in small parcels_" for state revenue
  - [RULE XIII] No other regime in any century divided land for gain at this scale. The verse fingerprints France.

## [CONTEST P2] — Why the framework needs to collapse these verses

- If vv. 36-39 are Revolutionary France, then:
  - The 1260 closed at 1798 _exactly when the prophecy says_ ("_even to the time of the end_")
  - The "_time of the end_" began in 1798
  - The pioneer time-prophecy framework (1260, 1290, 1335, 2300) is fully time-anchored
  - The 1844 message — built on the 2300 day calculation reaching its terminus at the end of the 1260 — is mathematically secured
- If vv. 36-39 are spiritualized into "papacy continuing through the church age," then:
  - The 1260 has no fixed terminus
  - The time of the end has no fixed beginning
  - The 1844 date floats free of Scripture
  - The Three Angels' Messages lose their anchor in Daniel 11

[→] This is why the seam at v. 35-36 carries so much weight. The framework cannot allow the chapter to vindicate the historic dates, because if it does, 1798 → 1844 → present stands on Scripture. So the chapter must be made silent about France. The text resists.

## What this study has established

- **Pagan Rome → Papal Rome at v. 30**, not at v. 36. The "_holy covenant_" turn signals the transition
- **The papacy is in view vv. 23-35** — 1260 years bookended **538-1798**, with the "_little help_" of the Reformation in the middle
- **A new actor enters at v. 36** — atheist Revolutionary France 1793-1799 — and the angel describes her by features (atheism, abolition of marriage, Goddess of Reason, land confiscation) that fit no other regime in history
- **The seam at v. 35 anchors 1798** as the end of the 1260 and the start of the time of the end
- **The chapter's literal-historical method has not changed.** vv. 23-39 are walked the same way vv. 1-22 were walked: real powers, named dates, verifiable events

[RULE VI] Recapitulation again — Dan 7:25 (1260 years against the saints), Rev 12:6 (1260 days in the wilderness), Rev 13:5 (forty-two months of papal supremacy), Dan 11:33 (many days of persecution). Four figures of one 1260, all ending at the same 1798.
