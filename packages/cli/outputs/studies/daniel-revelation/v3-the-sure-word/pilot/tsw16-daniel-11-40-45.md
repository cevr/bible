# Bible Study: TSW16 — Daniel 11:40-45 (The Eastern Question)

**The chapter does not change voice at v. 40. The "king of the north" and "king of the south" who fought across vv. 5-13 are the same geographic powers who appear again here — Egypt and the power on Seleucid territory. By 1798, the power on that territory was the Ottoman sultan in Constantinople. The angel returns to the same literal map at the same literal places. The pioneers and Millerites read this chapter geographically; the events of 1798-1840-1856-1878 vindicated them; and v. 45 — still unfulfilled — names a specific final move: the Ottoman ruler's last stand at Jerusalem.**

### Structure

```
v. 40       At the time of the end (1798) — Egypt pushes, Turkey whirlwinds — France caught between
v. 41       The glorious land (Palestine) — Edom, Moab, Ammon escape
v. 42       Egypt brought under tribute, doesn't escape
v. 43       Treasures of Egypt; Libyans and Ethiopians at the sultan's steps
v. 44       Tidings out of the east and north trouble him — UNFOLDING; final count knowable only in retrospect
v. 45       Last stand "between the seas in the glorious holy mountain" — STILL FUTURE
```

This is the chapter's payoff section. Verses 2-43 are fulfilled in dateable history — Persia, Greece, Diadochi, pagan Rome, the cross, papal Rome, 1260, 1798, atheist France, the 1798-1801 triangular war, the Ottoman recovery of Palestine and Egypt. Verses 44 and 45 are not yet closed. The chain reaches the present and stops short of the end; how many "tidings" make up v. 44 will be visible only when v. 45 arrives.

---

## "At the time of the end" — the same 1798 (v. 40)

- **"_And at the time of the end shall the king of the south push at him: and the king of the north shall come against him like a whirlwind_"** (Dan 11:40)
- **"The time of the end"** = the same period opened at v. 35
  - 1798 — Berthier captures Pope Pius VI, Feb 10
  - The 1260 closes; the time of the end begins
  - v. 40 anchors at the _opening_ of that period — a fresh triangular war
- **The "him" caught between the two kings** = the actor of vv. 36-39 — France
  - The grammar reaches back: France (v. 36) is the power against whom Egypt pushes and Turkey whirlwinds
  - This is the only reading that joins vv. 36-45 into a continuous narrative

[RULE XIII] The triangular war must actually have happened in 1798 if the prophecy is true. **Did it?**

- **1798 — France invades Egypt.** Napoleon sails from Toulon May 19, 1798 with 500 ships, 40,000 soldiers, 10,000 sailors. Alexandria taken July 5. Battle of the Pyramids July 23. Cairo entered July 24. _Egypt pushed; France advanced_ (DAR 1443).
- **1798 — Turkey declares war on France, September 2.** The Ottoman sultan, threatened by French occupation of his Egyptian semi-dependency, joined by Russia and Britain (whose ships destroyed the French fleet at the Nile Aug 1). _The king of the north came like a whirlwind_ (DAR 1444).
- **1799 — Napoleon's Syrian campaign.** Marches Feb 27, 1799 from Cairo into Syria with 18,000 men. Takes El-Arish, Jaffa, Naplous, Jafet. Besieges St. Jean d'Acre March 18. Sir Sidney Smith reinforces the Turkish garrison with English ships; a Turkish fleet with Russian and English support — **"many ships"**. After 60 days, Napoleon raises the siege May 21, 1799 — _his first retreat_ (DAR 1446-1447).
- **Three powers meet in one year** — France, Egypt, Turkey — exactly as v. 40 specifies, at the exact time-marker the chapter gave (1798)

[RULE I] Every word bears: "_with chariots, and with horsemen, and with many ships_" — the Ottoman whirlwind was sea-and-land, with British and Russian fleets alongside. The wording is not generic; it fits the actual coalition.

## "He shall overflow and pass over" — the verb shifts (v. 40b)

- **The subject of "_he shall overflow and pass over_"** = the king of the north (Turkey), not France
- Pioneer/DAR reasoning (DAR 1452):
  - The king of the north has just been described as triumphant in the whirlwind contest
  - The natural antecedent of the "_he_" who then _overflows and passes over_ is the victor of that contest
  - "_One familiar with the Hebrew assures us that the construction of this passage is such as to make it necessary to refer the overflowing and passing over to the king of the north_"
- The remainder of the chapter (vv. 41-45) tracks the Ottoman sultan's history, not Napoleon's
- [CONTEST P1] The framework spiritualizes "king of the north" — making it papal/spiritual rather than the literal power on Seleucid territory. The text refuses: vv. 5-13 made "king of the north" mean the literal power on Syrian territory; vv. 40-45 retains that vocabulary. The literal sultanate of Turkey was on that territory from the 15th century until 1923.

## "The glorious land" — literal Palestine (v. 41)

- **"_He shall enter also into the glorious land, and many countries shall be overthrown: but these shall escape out of his hand, even Edom, and Moab, and the chief of the children of Ammon_"** (Dan 11:41)
- **"The glorious land" = Palestine** — the same vocabulary as v. 16 (where pagan Rome under Pompey "_stood in the glorious land_" in B.C. 63)
- The chapter has used this phrase before, and it meant literal Palestine
  - [RULE V] Scripture is its own expositor. The internal precedent of v. 16 fixes the meaning at v. 41
- **The retreat from St. Jean d'Acre returned Palestine to Turkish rule**
  - The French abandoned all the conquests they had made in Judea
  - Palestine "_fell back again under the oppressive rule of the Turk_" (DAR 1457)
  - The Ottoman sultan reasserted dominion over the glorious land
- **Edom, Moab, Ammon escape** — the territories south and east of the Dead Sea and Jordan
  - Out of the line of march between Syria and Egypt
  - Adam Clarke (1825): "_These and other Arabians, they [the Turks] have never been able to subdue. They still occupy the deserts, and receive a yearly pension of forty thousand crowns of gold from the Ottoman emperors_"
  - [→] A spiritualized reading has nothing to _do_ with Edom, Moab, Ammon. A literal reading puts those tribes exactly where Genesis put them — south and east of the Jordan — and the Ottoman tribute records vindicate the prophecy

[STR] **Why literal geography matters here.** Spiritualization cannot tell you why Edom, Moab, and Ammon are named — and named together — and named as the ones who _escape_. Literal geography tells you immediately: these three tribes share a _line_ on the map, the line of the Ottoman caravan route to Mecca, which the sultan paid them not to attack. The prophecy fingerprints a transaction history that survives in Ottoman tax records.

## Egypt brought under tribute (vv. 42-43)

- **"_He shall stretch forth his hand also upon the countries: and the land of Egypt shall not escape_"** (Dan 11:42)
- **1801 — French expelled from Egypt by Anglo-Turkish coalition**
  - March 13 — English fleet lands at Aboukir
  - June 27 — Cairo capitulates
  - September 2 — Alexandria capitulates
  - Egypt returns to Ottoman suzerainty — _did not escape_
- **The Egyptians actually preferred French rule** (R. R. Madden, _Travels in Egypt 1824-1827_)
  - "_The French were much regretted by the Egyptians, and extolled as benefactors_"
  - Egypt _desired_ to escape Ottoman rule, _could not_ — exactly the language of the prophecy
- **"_He shall have power over the treasures of gold and of silver, and over all the precious things of Egypt_"** (Dan 11:43)
  - Articles of agreement between the sultan and the pasha of Egypt stipulated annual tribute: gold, silver, "_six hundred thousand measures of corn, and four hundred thousand of barley_"
- **"_The Libyans and the Ethiopians shall be at his steps_"**
  - Clarke: "_the Cushim, the unconquered Arabs, who have sought the friendship of the Turks, and many of whom are tributary to them_"
  - Both adjacent African powers — Libya and Cush/Sudan — under nominal Ottoman authority through the 19th century

[RULE XIII] Every verb in v. 42-43 has its corresponding ledger entry — campaign dates, capitulation dates, tribute amounts, tribal dependencies. The chapter never asks the reader to take its word on faith.

## "Tidings out of the east and out of the north" — still unfolding (v. 44)

- **"_But tidings out of the east and out of the north shall trouble him: therefore he shall go forth with great fury to destroy, and utterly to make away many_"** (Dan 11:44)
- **The word "tidings"** is plural and open-ended. It does not name _a_ war; it names a _kind_ of disturbance — reports out of the east and north that provoke the power in view to a fury-fueled response
- The prophecy does not tell us:
  - How many such tidings there will be
  - Over what span of years they will arrive
  - Which one will be the final trigger that drives him to plant his palace at the holy mountain (v. 45)
- **Adam Clarke (1825)** wrote candidly: "_This part of the prophecy is allowed to be yet unfulfilled... If the Turkish power be understood, as in the preceding verses, it may mean that the Persians on the east, and the Russians on the north, will at some time greatly embarrass the Ottoman government._"
- **Candidates the historical record offers so far** — each a "tiding" that troubled the power on Seleucid territory:
  - **Crimean War (1853-1856)** — Russia (north) the aggressor; Persia (east) a contemporary irritant. DAR (1875+) treated this as the fulfillment in Smith's own day. Smith took it as a striking match to Clarke's 1825 forecast (DAR 1473)
  - **Russo-Turkish War (1877-1878)** — Russia again from the north; Balkan revolts; Treaty of Berlin partitions Ottoman territory
  - **Balkan Wars + WWI (1912-1918)** — Russia (north) and Britain/Arab front (east) dismember the empire
  - **Ottoman collapse (1923)** — the sultanate ends
- **The honest reading:** v. 44 is the _kind_ of trouble, not a single event. Smith identified the Crimean War as the leading candidate of his day; later generations saw further "tidings" arrive. **Which tiding finally drives the move of v. 45 will be visible only after v. 45 happens.** The chapter does not require us to lock the verse to one war in advance

[RULE XIII] History vindicates — but it vindicates _as it unfolds_, not by demanding we close every clause before the next event arrives. Clarke (1825) named the right two compass-points; every subsequent "trouble" out of east and north has come from exactly those directions. The pattern is confirmed; the final count is still being written.

[STR] **Why this restraint matters.** Pinning v. 44 to the Crimean War alone — and treating it as fully closed — leaves the reader exposed when the next "tiding" arrives. The text uses the plural for a reason. The pioneer commitment is to the _direction_ (east and north) and the _power troubled_ (the sultan on Seleucid territory), not to a hard count of wars. We are still in v. 44's range, watching tidings accumulate toward the move of v. 45.

## The unfulfilled verse — "between the seas in the glorious holy mountain" (v. 45)

- **"_And he shall plant the tabernacles of his palace between the seas in the glorious holy mountain; yet he shall come to his end, and none shall help him_"** (Dan 11:45)
- **"Tabernacles of his palace"** — Hebrew suggests _movable_ dwellings; a temporary headquarters, not a permanent capital
- **"Between the seas in the glorious holy mountain"** — geographic specifications:
  - "Glorious holy mountain" = the mount on which Jerusalem stands (same vocabulary as Dan 9:16, Joel 2:1, 3:17, Zech 8:3 — always Jerusalem)
  - "Between the seas" = between the Mediterranean (west) and the Dead Sea (east) — which is _exactly_ where Jerusalem lies, geographically
  - The phrase is a topographic fingerprint of Jerusalem
- **"He shall come to his end, and none shall help him"**
  - This power has previously _received help_ — the language presupposes prior assistance
  - Turkey's actual history: England + Russia helped her against France 1798-1801; England, Russia, Austria, Prussia helped her against Egypt 1838-1840; England, France, Sardinia helped her against Russia 1853-1856; the great powers held her up through the Russo-Turkish War 1877-1878
  - "_Since the fall of the Ottoman supremacy in 1840, the empire has existed only through the sufferance of the great powers of Europe_" (DAR 1479)
  - When their help is withdrawn, "_he must come to the ground_"
- **The verse is still future** at the time of pioneer writing (Smith's DAR 1875+ edition); still future at the time of WWI; still future today
  - The Ottoman empire dissolved in 1923; the sultanate ended; but no Ottoman ruler ever planted his final headquarters at Jerusalem
  - The geographic specification has not yet matched a fulfilling event
  - The chapter ends with this verse waiting — and v. 44's tidings still accumulating toward whatever final move triggers it

[CONTEST P3] The framework cannot account for "_he shall plant the tabernacles of his palace between the seas in the glorious holy mountain_." The phrase is too specific to spiritualize. _Whose_ palace? _Which_ seas? _What_ mountain? Spiritualization erases the geographic detail; the literal reading preserves it and tells you exactly where the last act will play out.

## [CONTEST P1, P3, P7] — Why the framework must collapse this passage

- If vv. 40-45 are read literally:
  - The 1798 anchor of v. 35 is confirmed (since v. 40 opens "_at the time of the end_" with the actual 1798 events)
  - The whole historicist time-prophecy framework is geographically anchored
  - The 1844 message stands on the same Daniel 11 chain it always stood on
  - Verse 44 is still unfolding (tidings out of east and north continue); v. 45 remains unfulfilled, waiting on a specific geographic event near Jerusalem
- If vv. 40-45 are spiritualized:
  - "King of the north" loses its 2,500-year-stable geographic meaning
  - The historical vindication evaporates
  - "Between the seas in the glorious holy mountain" must mean something other than Jerusalem
  - The chapter's literal-historical method (vv. 1-39) is abandoned at the most-documented section
- [RULE IV] To get the truth, no Scripture must be left to contradict another. Spiritualizing "king of the north" at v. 40 contradicts the chapter's own usage at vv. 5-13 — same chapter, same phrase, opposite method. The contradiction is internal.

## What this study has established

- **The "king of the north" of vv. 40-45 is the literal power on Seleucid territory — by 1798, the Ottoman sultan in Constantinople**
- **The triangular war of 1798** (France, Egypt, Turkey) is on the historical record exactly as v. 40 specified, at the exact time-marker (1798) the chapter gave
- **Palestine, Edom, Moab, Ammon, Egypt, Libya, Ethiopia** — the geographic vocabulary is literal throughout, fingerprinted by Ottoman taxation, caravan-tribute, and military campaigns
- **Adam Clarke's 1825 forecast** of Persia + Russia troubling Turkey has been vindicated by every "tiding" since — Crimean War 1853, Russo-Turkish 1877-78, WWI dismemberment, Ottoman collapse 1923 — every disturbance arriving from the directions Clarke named
- **Verses 44-45 are not yet closed.** v. 44 is still receiving tidings (plural, open-ended); v. 45 awaits its specific geographic event. The chapter's chain reaches the present and stops
- **The pioneer-Millerite reading is the only one** that:
  1. Preserves "king of the north" with its chapter-stable meaning
  2. Fits every clause of vv. 40-43 to documented events
  3. Reads v. 44 as a pattern still unfolding, not a single closed war
  4. Identifies a still-future, geographically-specific terminus at v. 45

[RULE VI] Recapitulation closes the chapter. Dan 12:1 — "_at that time shall Michael stand up_" — picks up the thread from Dan 11:45's "_he shall come to his end_." The end of the king of the north and the standing up of Michael are the same juncture, two figures of one event.

## Appeal

- The God who named **Cyrus** (Isa 44:28) before he was born, who named **Tiberius** in his reign-year (Luke 3:1 → Dan 11:22), who named **538 → 1798** to the year, and who told Adam Clarke's generation which compass-points the trouble would come from — has named one final move still to come
- That move is geographically specific. It happens at Jerusalem. It involves a power whose previous help is finally withdrawn. It ends with no one to help
- The chain holds. The chapter walks from Persia (B.C. 534) toward Jerusalem (still future) in one continuous voice, never changing method, never spiritualizing a literal name, never re-reading a literal verse as a symbol
- We live inside v. 44. Tidings out of east and north have come, are coming, will come again — and the final count is knowable only after v. 45 arrives. The angel has told us _where_ the last act happens. The Bible student's task is not to redraw the map, and not to lock the verse-count, but to watch
