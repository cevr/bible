# Foundations of Our Faith — Grilling Decisions Log

Captures every decision made during the grill-with-docs interview before the spec was drafted. The point: if context evaporates, this doc reconstructs exactly what was decided and why.

**Date of grilling session:** 2026-05-22
**Method:** `grill-with-docs` skill
**Outcome:** spec for a new series replacing the existing 34-chapter Daniel & Revelation series

---

## D1 — Relationship to existing series

**Question:** How does this new pioneer/Millerite-foundations study relate to the existing 34 chapter studies + 3 bonus studies already in `daniel-revelation/`?

**Decision:** **Replace entirely.** Archive existing 37 files; rebuild the directory around the new spine.

**Implication:** The existing `archive/` subdirectory (which holds the v0 21-study series) becomes one layer deeper. The current 37 chapter+bonus files move into a new archive layer. Anyone with bookmarks to the old paths gets pointed at the archive.

---

## D2 — Series shape

**Question:** Of the four possible shapes (chronological pioneer narrative; doctrinal pillars; method-then-proof-then-story; two-track interleaved), which fits?

**Decision:** **Chronological pioneer narrative**, but with the **complete prophetic picture woven in** — Eastern Question (Ottoman / Rev 9), Revelation symbolism, sanctuary mechanics, etc. The story is the spine; the teaching is dense.

**Implication:** Sessions follow the historical sequence God led the pioneers through. The prophetic content surfaces as the pioneers themselves opened each text.

---

## D3 — Scope window

**Question:** What's the time window? Options ranged from "narrow 1840-1844" to "include pre-Miller historicist heritage" to "extend through 1888."

**Decision:** **1816-1888.** Start at Miller's conversion; end at the Minneapolis General Conference / righteousness by faith / Jones & Waggoner. The foundation isn't complete until the gospel is centered in the message.

**Brief pre-Miller context** is retained (S01-S02) as connection-to-the-inherited-chain prologue, not as full coverage of pre-Reformation historicism.

---

## D4 — Miller's 14 Rules placement

**Question:** Where do Miller's 14 Rules fit?

**Decision:** **One foundational session that walks all 14 rules** (S04), with the **literal-vs-spiritual spine**: 2 Peter 1:19 — prophecy exists to place Jesus in the heart and establish faith; literal interpretation enforces ONE fulfillment and ONE meaning; spiritual interpretation breeds infinite reinterpretations.

**Implication:** Every later session applies the rules without re-teaching them. Specific rules get cited by number where they directly produce a result.

---

## D5 — Session granularity

**Question:** How long is each session, and how many sessions?

**Decision:** **~30-40 short sessions (15-25 min each)**, one per historical event or doctrinal landmark. Daily-study / devotional format.

**Settled count:** 36 sessions across 6 parts (see D9), plus a bonus track of ~8-10 sessions for the Revelation chapters that don't naturally land in the 1816-1888 historical narrative.

---

## D6 — Session anatomy

**Question:** What's the shape of one ~15-25 min session?

**Decision:** **Story + Scripture + Application** — three movements:

1. **The moment** — 2-4 paragraphs of historical narrative that puts the reader in the scene (Miller at his desk; Litch publishing in 1838; Snow at Exeter; Edson in the cornfield).
2. **The Scripture opened** — the prophetic text the moment unlocks, walked verse-by-verse or thread-by-thread, with Smith DAR + EGW pointing to the right passages.
3. **The application** — 1-2 short paragraphs landing the truth on the reader's heart (inherits the `[IMG]` thread principle from v1).

All existing markers retained: `[KEY🔑]`, `[⟲]`, `[CTRF]`, `[IMG]`, `[DYK🔎]`, `[Q]`, `[→]`. One new marker added (see D11).

**Question:** Which EGW corpus matters most as primary reference?

## D8 — Secondary / historical sources

**Question:** Beyond EGW + Smith DAR, which historical sources?

**Decision:** **Pioneer primary writings only** — Bates, Andrews, Loughborough, James White autobiog., early Review articles, Snow's True Midnight Cry, Crosier's Day-Star article. **No Froom (PFF / MoD).** **No modern historians (Knight, Damsteegt, Schwarz, Maxwell).**

**Verified intentional:** confirmed Froom exclusion in a follow-up question. Voices in the room only. Froom is a 1940s scholar reconstructing the chain; he's not a pioneer voice.

**Implication for receipts:** the receipts come from primary documents the pioneers wrote, not from secondary documentation chains. Vicarius Filii Dei, Litch's 1840 prediction, etc. get cited from Smith DAR / pioneer Review articles / EGW directly — not from PFF.

---

## D9 — Quote rule

**Question:** The v1 series had a strict "no EGW quotes in the body — only Scripture" rule. Does it hold?

**Decision:** **Quote freely.** Historical narrative without primary voices is anemic. When the session walks Miller's 1816 conversion, his own words belong in the text. When the session covers EGW's first vision, her own description from Early Writings belongs in the text.

**Rules:**

- Pioneer/EGW quotes appear in clearly marked blockquotes with source citation
- Scripture remains the doctrinal authority; quotes are the historical record
- The teaching point is always anchored in Scripture, never derived from the quote alone

---

## D10 — Doctrinal completeness

**Question:** How much of Daniel + Revelation gets covered?

**Decision:** **Full coverage.** Church history (Rev 2-3 seven churches) is essential because the difference between Philadelphia and Laodicea is critical to the foundation message.

**Important nuance the user added:** sessions do NOT follow canonical book order (not Rev 2 then Rev 3 then Rev 4). Sessions follow the **prophetic chronological order** that Revelation itself lays out via recapitulation. Same period gets covered multiple times from different angles, as the text itself does.

---

## D11 — Spine model (historical vs prophetic)

**Question:** Is the spine the historical 1816-1888 narrative, with prophetic chapters mapped to the historical moment they describe?

**Decision:** **Yes — historical spine; prophetic chapters mapped to the historical moment they describe or the pioneer who opened them.**

**Critical historical-doctrinal correction the user made:** **Laodicea begins post-1888.** The 1888 rejection of righteousness by faith is what tipped the church from Philadelphia into Laodicea. The Millerite/SDA pioneer era 1844-1888 is still the Philadelphia "open door" era. S33 ("Laodicea's coming threat") in Part 6 is the warning Christ gave **before** the rejection, not the diagnosis after.

---

## D12 — Unifying frame

**Question:** What's the unifying theological frame of the new series? The one sentence the series exists to make.

**Decision:** **"God led a people step by step into present truth."**

The pioneer/EGW frame. God did not give the whole message at once. He led the pioneers in His own order — first the kingdoms (Dan 2), then the judgment (Dan 7), then the time (Dan 8-9), then the disappointment (Rev 10), then the sanctuary (cornfield), then the Sabbath (Bates), then the three angels assembled, then the gift of prophecy confirmed, then righteousness by faith (1888).

**Implication:** Each truth received was earned through Bible study, prayer, and faithfulness. Present truth is a **chain**, not a list. Drop one link and the chain breaks. The reader walks the chain as the pioneers walked it.
