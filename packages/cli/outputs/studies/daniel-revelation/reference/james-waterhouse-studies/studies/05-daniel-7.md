### The He-Goat-Killed Ram and Mount Zion (A/A')

- **Daniel 8:7** — "he cast him down to the ground, and **stamped upon him**; and there was none that could deliver the ram out of his hand."
- **Daniel 11:45** — "he shall plant the tabernacles of his palace between the seas in **the glorious holy mountain**; yet he shall come to his end, and none shall help him."
- Christ, as the Lamb slain, appears in the Judgment at Mount Zion. The crucified Christ appears in our place, if we are his slaves, in the Judgment.
- **Revelation 5:6** — "in the midst of the throne... stood a **Lamb as it had been slain**."
- **Revelation 14:1** — "a **Lamb stood on the mount Sion**, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand."

### State vs. Church: Eyes and Mouth

- **Isaiah 64:8** — "But now, O Lord, thou art our father; we are the **clay**, and thou our **potter**; and we all are the work of thy hand." Clay = God's people.
- **1 Samuel 9:9** — "a **Prophet** was beforetime called a **Seer**." Eyes/mouth on the little horn = a prophet/seer figure.
- **Exodus 7:1** — "The Lord said unto Moses, See, I have made thee **a god** to Pharaoh: and **Aaron** thy brother **shall be thy prophet**."
- **Exodus 4:16** — "And **he [Aaron] shall be thy spokesman** unto the people: and he shall be, even **he shall be to thee instead of a mouth**, and **thou shalt be to him instead of God**."
- Metal/state vs. non-metal/church. Beast/state vs. human/church. Force vs. love motivated!
- **Daniel 2:33 — "feet of iron mixed with clay":** state and church united, specifically, **the Roman state and the church united, eventually becoming the Roman church!** The Vatican State is still the only church that is both a church and a state — clay mixed with iron.

### The Little Horn (vss. 8, 20-25)

- Comes up among the 10 horns
- Uproots three of the 10 horns
- Has "eyes like the eyes of a man" — a seer, a prophet figure
- Has "a mouth speaking great things" — speaks against God
- **vs. 25** — "He shall speak great words against the Most High, and shall wear out the saints of the Most High, and think to change times and laws."
- "Think to change times and laws" = attempt to change the Sabbath (time) and the 10 Commandments (laws).

### The Judgment Scene (vss. 9-10, 13-14)

- **vs. 9** — "The Ancient of Days did sit" on a throne. "The thrones were cast down" (set up).
- **vs. 10** — "A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him: thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him: the judgment was set, and the books were opened."
- **vs. 13** — "One like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of Days."
- **vs. 14** — "There was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom... his dominion is an everlasting dominion."

### Important: In Daniel 7, the Son of Man Goes TO the Father

- vs. 13 — the Son of man comes **to** the Ancient of Days (the Father). This is NOT the Second Coming to earth. This is Christ entering the judgment in heaven.
- The Second Coming (Christ coming TO EARTH) follows the judgment.
- **Revelation 1:7** — "He cometh with clouds" to earth = Second Coming.
- **Daniel 7:13** — He comes "with clouds" to the Ancient of Days = entering the judgment.

### The Sequence

1. Four beasts (four kingdoms) rise and fall (vss. 1-8)
2. The little horn speaks against God and persecutes saints (vss. 8, 20-25)
3. The judgment is set; books are opened (vss. 9-10)
4. The Son of Man comes to the Ancient of Days (vs. 13)
5. The Son of Man receives the kingdom (vs. 14)
6. The saints receive the kingdom (vss. 18, 22, 27)

## Novel Findings

1. **Daniel 7:13 is NOT the Second Coming** — it is Christ entering the judgment before the Father. The distinction between "coming to the Ancient of Days" (Dan 7) and "coming to earth" (Rev 1:7) is critical for understanding the investigative judgment.
2. **Eyes and mouth on the little horn** = a religious/prophetic figure attached to the state beast. The papacy functions as the "prophet/seer" for the state system (cf. 1 Samuel 9:9).
3. **The judgment scene** of Daniel 7:9-10 is the same scene as Revelation 4-5 and Revelation 1:4-6 — Waterhouse demonstrates this through detailed structural parallel.
4. **"Think to change times and laws"** — the little horn's attack targets the Sabbath specifically ("times") and God's moral law ("laws").

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Investigative Judgment:** The judgment scene (vss. 9-10) with books opened occurs BEFORE the kingdom is given (v. 14). The judgment is investigative — it examines records.
- **Sabbath:** "Think to change times and laws" = the papal claim to have changed the Sabbath from Saturday to Sunday.
- **Second Coming:** Follows the judgment. The Son of Man receives the kingdom THEN comes to earth.
- **Christ's High Priesthood:** Christ appears before the Father in the heavenly court.
- **Great Controversy:** The little horn's war against the saints (v. 21) is the central conflict of history.

## Teaching Flow

1. Review Daniel 2's statue. Now God shows the same prophecy from a different angle — beasts.
2. Identify the four beasts = four kingdoms (same as Daniel 2).
3. Focus on the fourth beast: iron teeth, 10 horns, then the little horn.
4. The little horn: eyes, mouth, speaks against God, wears out saints, changes times and laws.
5. The judgment scene: Ancient of Days, fiery stream, books opened. This is a courtroom.
6. Key distinction: the Son of Man comes TO the Ancient of Days (not to earth). This is the investigative judgment.
7. After the judgment: the kingdom is given to the saints (v. 27).
8. Close: we are living between the "changing of times and laws" and the judgment/Second Coming.
