# Study 10a: Exodus, Numbers, and 1 Kings — Rebellious Israel and Jeroboam's Calves

## Sources

- `Daniel and Revelation-structure and text with notes-20240831.pdf` pp. 42-44 (Exodus; Numbers 13-14,16 and 1 Kings 11-12)

## Theme

"Rebellious Israel in the Wilderness and Jeroboam and the Northern Kingdom of Israel" — Two parallel rebellions from the south, both involving golden calves, separated by centuries but structurally identical.

## The Two Rebellions: Exodus Israel and Solomon's Kingdom

| Israel of the Exodus                            | Kingdom of Solomon                   |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| 12 Spies                                        | 12 Tribes                            |
| 40 days searching out Canaan                    | 40 year reign of Solomon             |
| 10 spies vs. 2 (Joshua and Caleb)               | 10 tribes vs. 2 (Judah and Benjamin) |
| Rebellion from south (camps of Reuben & Kohath) | Rebellion from south (Egypt)         |

## Key Texts

### The Golden Calf at Sinai (Exodus 32:1-6)

- **Exodus 19:18** — "Mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because the Lord descended upon it in fire."
- **Exodus 20:1-17** — The complete 10 Commandments spoken by God.
- **Exodus 32:1-6** — "The people gathered themselves together unto Aaron, and said unto him, Up, make us gods... And Aaron said, Break off the **golden** earrings... And he received them at their hand, and fashioned it with a graving tool, after he had made it a molten **calf**; and they said, **These be thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.**"
- Note: the golden calf was made from gold — the same metal as Nebuchadnezzar's image. False worship always involves golden idols.

### The 12 Spies (Numbers 13-14)

- **Numbers 13:2** — "Send thou men, that they may search the land of Canaan... of every tribe of their fathers shall ye send a man."
- **Numbers 13:25,32-33** — "They returned from searching of the land after **forty days**. And they brought up an evil report... The land... eateth up the inhabitants thereof; and all the people that we saw in it are men of a great stature."
- **Numbers 14:6-10** — "**Joshua** the son of Nun, **and Caleb** the son of Jephunneh... spake unto all the company of the children of Israel, saying, The land which we passed through to search it, is an exceeding good land... Only rebel not ye against the Lord... But all the congregation bade stone them with stones."
- Two faithful vs. ten faithless. The same ratio recurs in the kingdom split.

### Korah's Rebellion (Numbers 16:1-3)

- **Numbers 16:1** — "Now Korah, **the son of Kohath**, the son of Levi, and Dathan and Abiram, **sons of Reuben**, took men."
- **Numbers 2:10** — "On the **south side** shall be the standard of the camp of **Reuben** according to their armies."
- **Numbers 3:29** — "The families of the sons of **Kohath** shall pitch on the side of the tabernacle **southward**."
- All the rebels are from the **south** of the camp. Rebellion comes from the south — the direction of man (cf. Study 09: South = man).

### Jeroboam's Golden Calves (1 Kings 11-12)

- **1 Kings 11:29-31** — "The prophet Ahijah the Shilonite found him in the way; and he had clad himself with a new garment; and they two were alone in the field. And Ahijah caught the new garment that was on him, and rent it in **twelve pieces**: And he said to Jeroboam, Take thee **ten pieces**: for thus saith the Lord... I will rend the kingdom out of the hand of Solomon, and will give **ten tribes** to thee."
- **1 Kings 11:40** — "Solomon sought therefore to kill Jeroboam. And Jeroboam arose, and fled **into Egypt**."
- **1 Kings 11:42** — "Solomon reigned in Jerusalem over all Israel **forty years**."
- **1 Kings 12:2,16,20-21** — Jeroboam returns from Egypt; the kingdom splits: "there was none that followed the house of David, but **the tribe of Judah** only... **the house of Judah, with the tribe of Benjamin**."
- **1 Kings 12:28-29** — "Whereupon the king took counsel, and made two **calves** of gold, and said unto them, It is too much for you to go up to Jerusalem: **behold thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.** And he set the one in **Beth-el**, and the other put he in **Dan**."

### The Exact Same Phrase

- **Exodus 32:4** — "**These be thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.**"
- **1 Kings 12:28** — "**Behold thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.**"
- Jeroboam deliberately repeated the golden calf apostasy of Exodus — not by accident, but as a calculated religious-political strategy.

## The Pattern: Rebellion from the South

Both rebellions originate from the south:

- **Exodus:** Korah (son of Kohath, who camps south) and Dathan/Abiram (sons of Reuben, who camps south).
- **1 Kings:** Jeroboam flees to and returns from **Egypt** (south of Israel).
- In the compass/creature framework (Study 09): south = man. Rebellion is man-centered — exalting human authority over God's.

## Novel Findings

1. **The Exodus-Kingdom structural parallel** — 12 spies / 12 tribes, 40 days / 40 years, 10 vs. 2 / 10 vs. 2, rebellion from south / rebellion from south.
2. **The golden calf repeated verbatim** — Jeroboam uses the exact same phrase as Aaron. The apostasy is deliberate and historical, not accidental.
3. **All rebellion comes from the south of the camp** — Korah (Kohath/south) and Dathan/Abiram (Reuben/south) map to the south direction, which = man in the four-creature framework.
4. **The 10/2 ratio recurs** — 10 faithless spies vs. 2 faithful (Joshua and Caleb) = 10 rebel tribes vs. 2 faithful (Judah and Benjamin). The remnant is always the minority.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Sabbath / Worship:** The golden calf replaces the Creator-God with a man-made idol — the fundamental worship test.
- **Church and State:** Jeroboam used religion (golden calves) as a political tool to consolidate state power — the church+state template.
- **Great Controversy:** Truth (2) vs. error (10). The faithful are always outnumbered.
- **144,000:** The remnant pattern: Joshua+Caleb (2 of 12), Judah+Benjamin (2 of 12), Daniel and three friends (4).
- **Babylon:** Egypt functions as a Babylon-type — the place of bondage, the source of rebellion.

## Teaching Flow

1. Exodus: Mount Sinai, the 10 Commandments, then immediately the golden calf. How fast the fall.
2. Numbers: 12 spies, 40 days, evil report. Only Joshua and Caleb stand faithful. 10 vs. 2.
3. Korah's rebellion: all rebels from the south of the camp. South = man.
4. 1 Kings: Solomon's 40-year reign, the kingdom torn — 10 tribes vs. 2. Same ratio.
5. Jeroboam flees to Egypt (south), returns, and sets up golden calves — the exact same words as Exodus 32.
6. The pattern: rebellion comes from the south. It's man-centered. It always involves false worship (golden images).
7. Close: the 10/2 ratio recurs throughout Scripture. The faithful remnant is always small. Which side will you stand on?
