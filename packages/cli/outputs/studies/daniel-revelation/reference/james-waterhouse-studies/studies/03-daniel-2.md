# Study 03: Daniel 2 — The Four Metal Statue

## Sources

- `Daniel and Revelation-structure and text with notes-20240831.pdf` pp. 7-12 (Daniel 2 structure, text, and notes)

## Theme

"The Four Metal Statue" — God reveals the succession of world empires and His eternal kingdom.

## Chiastic Structure

```
A       vss. 10-11     Chaldeans: no man can show the king's matter; only the gods
B       vs. 17         Four men: Daniel, Hananiah, Mishael, Azariah
C       vss. 20-21     God removes kings/kingdoms and sets up kings
D       vs. 26         Daniel's Babylonian name used: Belteshazzar
  B'    vss. 27-28     Daniel: the wise men cannot show it; God of heaven can
  J     vss. 32-35     The statue described (Babylon → Jerusalem transition)
    I   vss. 37-38     First kingdom: Gold/Babylon
    II  vs. 39a        Second kingdom
    II' vs. 39b        Third kingdom
    III vss. 40-43     Fourth kingdom (united → divided)
  J'    vs. 44         God's eternal kingdom: the stone
B''     vss. 32-35     Four metals: gold, silver, bronze, iron
C'      vs. 37-45      A sequence of earthly kingdoms followed by God's eternal kingdom
D'      vs. 49         Daniel's three companions' Babylonian names: Shadrach, Meshach, Abed-nego
```

## Key Texts

### King = Kingdom (vss. 37-39)

- **Daniel 2:37-39** — "Thou, O king, art a king of kings: for the God of heaven hath given thee a kingdom, power, and strength, and glory. And wheresoever the children of men dwell, the beasts of the field and the fowls of the heaven hath he given into thine hand, and hath made thee ruler over them all. **Thou art this head of gold.** And **after thee shall arise another kingdom** inferior to thee, and another third kingdom of brass, which shall bear rule over all the earth."
- This passage establishes the key principle: **king = kingdom**. "Thou [the king] art this head of gold" and "after thee shall arise another kingdom." See also Daniel 7:17,23.

### The Statue (vss. 32-33)

- **Head of fine gold** — Babylon (Neo-Babylonian Empire)
- **Chest and arms of silver** — Medo-Persia (Achaemenid Empire)
- **Belly and thighs of bronze** — Greece (Alexander and Hellenistic successors)
- **Legs of iron** — Rome (Roman Republic and Empire)
- **Feet of iron and clay** — Rome divided (European kingdoms, medieval to present)

### The Stone (vss. 34-35, 44-45)

- "A stone was cut out without hands" — strikes the feet of iron and clay
- Reduces all metals to powder — "the wind carried them away"
- "The stone that smote the image became a great mountain and filled the whole earth"
- **vs. 44** — "the God of heaven shall set up a kingdom which shall never be destroyed"

### Iron Mixed with Clay = Church United with State

- **Iron** = state/metal power
- **Clay** = God's people — **Isaiah 64:8**: "we are the clay, and thou our potter"
- **vs. 43** — "they shall mingle themselves with the seed of men; but they shall not cleave one to another, even as iron does not mix with clay"
- The Roman church, as the Vatican State, is the only church that is both a church and a state — literally clay mixed with iron.
- **State vs. church**: force vs. love motivated (Exodus 7:1; 4:16)

### Daniel and Friends vs. the Statue

- **vss. 17, 32-33** — Four men (Daniel, Hananiah, Mishael, Azariah) vs. four metals (gold, silver, bronze, iron).
- Daniel and his companions, as one, are in conflict with the one statue of four metals.
- In the fiery furnace (ch. 3), in the lion's den (ch. 6), in the dietary test (ch. 1).
- **In Nebuchadnezzar's vision, the four companions are the stone cut from the mountain without hands that strikes the statue and reduces it to powder.**

## Novel Findings

1. **The statue's name is Babylon** — the gold head names the whole. The entire statue represents Babylon in different phases, evolving but maintaining the same anti-God system.
2. **Four men vs. four metals** — the structure deliberately pairs Daniel and his three companions against the four metals. The faithful four ARE the stone that destroys the statue.
3. **Clay = God's people** (Isaiah 64:8), not mere weakness. Iron mixed with clay is a specific prophecy of church-state union.
4. **Eyes and a mouth on the beast** (Dan 7 parallel) = a prophet/seer for the state. The little horn has a religious spokesperson — a false prophet. (1 Samuel 9:9: "a Prophet was beforetime called a Seer")

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Church and State:** Iron mixed with clay = the papacy as a church-state entity. Template for the mark of the beast.
- **Second Coming:** The stone cut without hands = Christ's kingdom destroying all earthly kingdoms.
- **Great Controversy:** The succession of empires shows Satan working through earthly powers; God's kingdom is the inevitable conclusion.

## Teaching Flow

1. Set the scene: Nebuchadnezzar's dream, the failure of the wise men, Daniel's prayer.
2. Describe the statue top-to-bottom: gold, silver, bronze, iron, iron+clay.
3. Identify each kingdom historically.
4. Focus on the iron+clay: what does it mean? → church united with state (Isaiah 64:8 for clay).
5. The stone: cut without hands, destroys all, becomes a mountain. God's kingdom.
6. The parallel: four men vs. four metals. The faithful are the stone.
7. Close with the assurance: "the dream is certain, and the interpretation thereof sure" (v. 45).
