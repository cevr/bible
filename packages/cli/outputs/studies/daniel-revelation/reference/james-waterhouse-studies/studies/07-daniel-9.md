# Study 07: Daniel 9 — The 70 Weeks: The Sabbath Test

## Theme

"The 70 Weeks" — Daniel's prayer and Gabriel's answer: the Messiah, the covenant, and the Sabbath.

### Structure of vss. 24-27 (again)

```
vs. 24a       vs. 24b        vs. 25        vs. 26a    vs. 26b
```

The outer layer pairs **sin** (transgression, sin, iniquity — vs. 24a) with **desolation** (desolations, desolate, desolate — vss. 26b, 27b). Jewish sin leads to Jewish desolation.

## Key Texts

### 70 Weeks = A Sabbath Test

- **2 Chronicles 36:21** — "until the land had enjoyed her sabbaths: for as long as she lay desolate she kept sabbath, to fulfil threescore and ten years."
- Judah failed the 7th-day Sabbath for **490 years**. God sent them to Babylon for **70 years** (490 ÷ 7) so the land could rest.
- At restoration, God gives another **490-year test**: 70 weeks (70 x 7 = 490 prophetic years).
- **Will the people pass this Sabbath test the second time?**

### 777 vs. 666

```
"seventy weeks" (70x7)     "weeks seven weeks" (777)     "weeks" (7)
    (vs. 24)                     (vs. 25)                  (vs. 26)
                                                        "week" "week" (77)
                                                            (vs. 27)
```

- 70x7 is symbolically the Sabbath, counterpart of Daniel 3's 60x6 (666).
- "Weeks seven weeks" = literally 777.
- **Revelation 13's 666** is the counterfeit of **777** (Creation's 7th-day Sabbath).

**Only the Messiah** can bring everlasting righteousness, seal the vision and prophecy, and anoint the Holy of Holies. His death accomplished all six purposes.

### "The Prince" Is Always the Messiah (vss. 25-26)

- **vs. 25** — נָגִיד (nagid, prince #5057) + מָשִׁיחַ (mashiyach, Messiah #4899) = "Messiah the Prince"
- **vs. 26a** — מָשִׁיחַ (Messiah) alone = "Messiah shall be cut off"
- **vs. 26b** — נָגִיד (prince) alone = "the people of the Prince"
- Two of three clearly refer to Christ. For consistency, the third must also.
- **"The people of the Messiah the Prince"** who destroy Jerusalem = the Jews themselves. They brought destruction through sin and rebellion, using Rome as the instrument.
- **Daniel 11:22** — "the prince of the covenant" = the same figure.

### "Cut Off" = "Covenant" (vss. 26-27)

- **"Cut off"** = כָּרַת (#3772)
- **"Covenant"** = בְּרִית (#1285)
- These refer to the same thing: the Old Testament ritual of "cutting a covenant."
- **Genesis 15:9-18** — Abraham's covenant: animals cut in two, a burning lamp passes between the pieces. "In the same day the Lord made (cut) a covenant with Abram."
- **Jeremiah 34:18** — "the men that have transgressed my covenant, which they cut... when they cut the calf in twain, and passed between the parts thereof."
- Christ's death IS the cutting of the new covenant — His body divided like the sacrificial animals.

### "In the Midst of the Week" = Cut in Two (vs. 27)

- "Midst" = חֲצִי (#2677), from חָצָה (#2673), meaning "to cut or split in two; to halve."
- Christ was cut off in the **middle** of the 70th week (temporal meaning).
- Christ was **cut** (crucified) to establish the covenant (covenantal meaning).
- **Matthew 27:50-51** — "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom."
- **Hebrews 10:19-20** — "through the veil, that is to say, his flesh."
- The veil = Christ's flesh. The tearing of the veil = the cutting of Christ = the cutting of the new covenant = the opening of access to God's presence.
- The "flaming sword" of Eden (Genesis 3:24) was replaced by the veil in the temple. Both kept sinners from God's presence. Christ's death split both open.

### "Wing of Abominations" = Rome (vs. 27)

- "Wing" = an eagle's wing. The eagle = Rome.
- **Deuteronomy 28:49** — "The Lord shall bring a nation against thee from far, from the end of the earth, as swift as **the eagle** flieth; a nation whose tongue thou shalt not understand."

### The Abomination of Desolation Pattern

**The general pattern:**

| 1. Setup Abomination: Church unites with State | 2. Church persecutes saints through State | 3. Desolation: State destroys Church |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |

**Applied to the Jewish nation:**

| 1. Jewish Nation unites with Rome, its leaders with Pilate | 2. Jewish Nation crucifies Christ through Rome | 3. Rome destroys Jewish Nation, its city and temple |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |

While pagan Romans on the outskirts of the Holy City is an abomination, a greater abomination is the uniting of God's covenant people with those pagan Romans to kill an innocent man — namely, God's own Son, Jesus Christ.

### Jesus on the 70 Weeks (Probationary Time)

- **Matthew 18:21-22** — "Then came Peter to him, and said, Lord, **how oft shall my brother sin against me, and I forgive him?** till seven times? Jesus saith unto him, I say not unto thee, Until seven times: but, Until **seventy times seven**."
- The Jewish nation had 70 sevens of years (490) in which forgiveness was available. After that, there was no more forgiveness for them as a nation — only retribution and desolation.
- **Matthew 23:37-38** — "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, thou that killest the prophets, and stonest them which are sent unto thee, how often would I have gathered thy children together... and ye would not! Behold, **your house is left unto you desolate.**"

### The Jewish Nation vs. Jesus (vss. 26-27)

Recall Daniel 8's four horns and its little horn. Recall that the Jewish nation is a bull:

```
                the Little Horn,
            the Roman Republic and Empire
              (coming out of Macedonia)
                      (eagle)

toward the glorious                toward the east,
  (land), the Hasmonean             the Seleucid Empire
  Kingdom (Jewish Nation)            (lion)
       (bull)

                toward the south,
              Ptolemaic Egypt
                   (man)
```

Jesus is the Lamb of God while the Jewish Nation is a bull, a necessity. Our sins made the sacrifice of Christ, the Lamb, a necessity. The Jewish Nation need not have been sacrificed but their rejection of Christ made it inevitable — "the wages of sin is death." Either we accept Christ, the Lamb's sacrifice in our place or we become the sacrifice.

### 70 Weeks and the 2300 Days Connection

- **Daniel 8:13** — "the transgression of desolation" → "2300 days"
- **Daniel 9:24** — "transgression" → "70 weeks"
- **Daniel 9:26b-27b** — "desolation" / "desolate"
- The "transgression of desolation" of Daniel 8:13 encompasses both the 70 weeks AND the 2300 days.
- If the 70 weeks is 490 years, and it is part of the 2300 days, then the 2300 days must be 2300 years.

### "Cut Off" vs. the Sacrifice and the Oblation (vss. 26-27)

Christ's life was sacrificed to make the new covenant. The Bible views His death both as a sacrificial offering and as the cutting of a covenant:

- **Isaiah 53:1-12** — The full Suffering Servant passage: "**he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities**... **he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter**... **he was cut off out of the land of the living: for the transgression of my people was he stricken**... **thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin**... **he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors**."
- **Acts 8:32-35** — The Ethiopian eunuch reads Isaiah 53: "**He was led as a sheep to the slaughter**... Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."
- **John 1:29** — "Behold **the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world**."
- **1 Peter 1:19** — "the precious blood of Christ, **as of a lamb without blemish and without spot**."
- **1 Corinthians 5:7** — "Christ our passover is **sacrificed for us**."
- **Hebrews 9:26** — "he appeared to **put away sin by the sacrifice of himself**."
- **Hebrews 10:12** — "after he had **offered one sacrifice for sins for ever**, sat down on the right hand of God."
- **Ephesians 5:2** — "Christ also hath loved us, and hath **given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God**."
- **John 19:30** — "When Jesus therefore had received the vinegar, he said, **It is finished**: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost." The sacrificial system was finished — the true sacrifice had come.
- **Genesis 3:21** — "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the Lord God make coats of (lamb?) skins, and clothed them." The first sacrifice, pointing to Christ.
- **Revelation 13:8** — "the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world."

### Jesus on the 70 Weeks and the Sabbath

1. **Sabbath keeping custom** — Luke 4:16: "as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day."
2. **Easy yoke** = Sabbath — Matthew 11:28-30: "Come unto me... I will give you rest. Take my yoke..." A yoke constrains actions (like a commandment). This yoke is about labor and rest. Being yoked to Christ = being yoked to a Sabbath keeper!
3. **Lord of the Sabbath** — Matthew 12:1-8: "the Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day."
4. **Sabbath healings** — Matthew 12:9-14; John 5:15-18; John 7:1,19-23. The Jews killed Jesus because He healed on Sabbath.
5. **Bread from Heaven** = Sabbath test — John 6:31-33; Exodus 16:4-5,19-29. The manna tested Israel's willingness to keep the Sabbath. Jesus is the true bread from heaven.

## Novel Findings

1. **70 weeks = Sabbath test** — directly paralleling the 490 years of Sabbath violation that caused the Babylonian captivity.
2. **777 encoded** in "weeks seven weeks" (vs. 25) — the Sabbath number as counterpart to 666.
3. **The chiastic structure proves the Prince is always the Messiah** — three occurrences of nagid/mashiyach, all referring to Christ.
4. **"Cut off" = covenant-cutting** — Christ's death is the covenantal act of "cutting," His body divided like Abraham's sacrificial animals.
5. **"Midst" = to halve/split** — double meaning: temporal (middle of the week) and covenantal (cut in two).
6. **The veil = the flaming sword of Eden** — Christ's death reopened access to God's presence.
7. **"Wing of abominations" = the Roman eagle** — Deuteronomy 28:49 prophesied it.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **70 Weeks:** The 490-year probationary period for the Jewish nation, beginning with the decree to restore Jerusalem.
- **2300 Days:** The 70 weeks is "cut off" from the 2300 days. Same starting point. 2300 - 490 = 1810 more years → 1844.
- **Sabbath:** The entire 70 weeks is a Sabbath test. 777 vs. 666. Christ kept the Sabbath as His custom. The Sabbath is central.
- **Sanctuary/Atonement:** Verse 24's six purposes all relate to the sanctuary/atonement work.
- **Crucifixion:** "Cut off, but not for himself" = vicarious atonement. "Cause the sacrifice and oblation to cease" = Christ's death ended the sacrificial system.

## Teaching Flow

1. Context: Daniel reads Jeremiah, understands the 70 years, prays (vss. 1-21).
2. Gabriel arrives: "70 weeks are cut off for your people" (vs. 24).
3. Why 70 weeks? → 490 years of Sabbath violation → 70 years captivity → now 490 years of Sabbath retest.
4. The six purposes of the 70 weeks (vs. 24): three about sin, three about the Messiah's solution.
5. The timeline: from the decree to restore Jerusalem → Messiah the Prince → Messiah cut off → covenant confirmed → sacrifice ceases → desolation.
6. "Cut off" = covenant. Christ's death = the new covenant cut. The veil torn = access restored.
7. The connection to 2300 days: the "transgression of desolation" spans both prophecies.
8. Close: the Sabbath is at the heart of the 70 weeks, from beginning to end.
