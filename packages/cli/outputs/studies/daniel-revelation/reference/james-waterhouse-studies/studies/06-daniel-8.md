# Study 06: Daniel 8 — The Ram, the He-Goat, and the 2300 Days

## Sources

- `Daniel and Revelation-structure and text with notes-20240831.pdf` pp. 17-25 (Daniel 8 structure, text, and notes)

## Theme

"The Ram and the He-Goat" — the great controversy between Christ and Satan, framed in Day of Atonement imagery.

## Chiastic Structure

```

B       vss. 3-8       The Ram and the He-Goat
  I       vss. 3-4       The ram pushes westward, northward, southward
  II      vss. 5-7       The he-goat from the west destroys the ram
C(B')   vss. 9-14      The Little Horn
  a       vss. 10-11     Waxes great to the host, takes away the daily, casts down sanctuary
  b       vss. 12-13     Transgression of desolation; "How long?"
  a'      vs. 14         2300 evening morning: the sanctuary shall be cleansed

```

## Key Texts

### The Ram = Medo-Persia = Cyrus = A Type of Christ

- **vs. 20** — "The ram... is the kings of Media and Persia."
- **vs. 4** — The ram pushes westward, northward, southward. No beast could stand before him.
- The greatest king of Medo-Persia = **Cyrus the Great** — the only Gentile king styled "the Great."
- **Isaiah 44:28-45:1** — God calls Cyrus "my shepherd" and "his anointed" (mashiyach = Messiah!).
- Ram == Medo-Persia == Cyrus == God's shepherd & anointed == **a type of Jesus Christ**

### The He-Goat = Greece = Satan's Kingdom

- **vs. 21** — "The rough goat is the king of Grecia."
- **Leviticus 17:7** — "They shall no more offer their sacrifices unto devils (#8163; literally, 'goats')."
- **Matthew 25:31-46** — Jesus separates sheep (right hand) from goats (left hand). Sheep = saved; goats = lost.
- He-goat == Greece == **Satan's instrument**

### The Ram vs. the He-Goat = Christ vs. Satan

The very animals encode the Great Controversy:

- The **ram** (connected to sacrifice, to the shepherd, to the Messiah) = Christ's side
- The **he-goat** (connected to devils, to the scapegoat of Leviticus 16) = Satan's side
- East vs. West: the ram pushes from the east; the goat comes from the west. Eastern/Hebrew/Biblical mindset loses to Western/Hellenistic/philosophical mindset. Theology loses to philosophy.

### The Ram vs. the Little Horn — Directional Diagrams (vss. 4,9)

**The Ram (vs. 4):**

```
                    northward

     westward           the Ram

                    southward
```

The ram pushes in three directions from its position at Shushan (east).

**The Little Horn (vs. 9):**

```
                  the Little Horn

toward the                    toward the
glorious (land)               east

                  toward the south
```

The little horn waxes great in three directions — but NOT northward. It comes FROM the north.

### The Wilderness Camp and Four Standards

The directions map to the design of the wilderness camp of Israel:

```
                  camp of Dan (north)

camp of Ephraim       The sanctuary       camp of Judah
    (west)                                    (east)

                  camp of Reuben (south)
```

- **Numbers 2:1-31** — "Every man of the children of Israel shall pitch by his own standard, with the ensign of their father's house: far off about the tabernacle of the congregation shall they pitch."
  - East: camp of Judah (vs. 3)
  - South: camp of Reuben (vs. 10)
  - West: camp of Ephraim (vs. 18)
  - North: camp of Dan (vs. 25)

**According to Jewish tradition, the four standards:**

```
                    eagle (north)

     bull (west)      The sanctuary      lion (east)

                     man (south)
```

The little horn = eagle (north/Dan) attacking toward the other three camps:

- South = camp of Reuben = **man**
- East = camp of Judah = **lion**
- West/"glorious land" = camp of Ephraim = **bull**
- The little horn comes from the north = camp of Dan = **eagle**
- The little horn (eagle/Rome) attacks the other three camps (directions).

### "Little Horn" vs. "King of Fierce Countenance" (vss. 9,23)

The Hebrew words translated "of fierce countenance" in the interpretation (vs. 23) are the **same Hebrew words** occurring in:

- **Deuteronomy 28:49-50** — "The Lord shall bring a nation against thee from far, from the end of the earth, as swift as **the eagle** flieth; a nation whose tongue thou shalt not understand; A nation of **fierce countenance**, which shall not regard the person of the old, nor shew favour to the young."
- God prophesied the coming destruction by the Romans. The eagle = Rome's well-known symbol. Daniel 9:27's "wing of abominations" = an eagle's wing = Rome.

### "Captain of the Host" (vs. 11) = Christ

- **Joshua 5:13-6:1** — The "captain of the Lord's host" appears to Joshua with drawn sword: "Loose thy shoe from off thy foot; for the place whereon thou standest is holy." Joshua is told the same thing as Moses: "the place... is holy."
- **Exodus 3:2-5** — The "angel of the Lord" in the burning bush: "put off thy shoes from off thy feet, for the place whereon thou standest is holy ground."
- Same person: God the Son, the pre-incarnate Jesus, the captain of the Lord's host.
- **Malachi 3:1** — "the messenger (angel) of the covenant" = Jesus. "The Lord, whom ye seek, shall suddenly come to his temple, even **the messenger (angel) of the covenant**."
- The Hebrew word for "messenger" in Malachi and "angel" in Exodus is the same: מַלְאָךְ mal'ak (Strong's #4397), meaning _messenger_.
- **Genesis 48:15-16** — "And he blessed Joseph, and said, **God** (Elohim), before whom my fathers Abraham and Isaac did walk, **the God** (Elohim) which fed me all my life long unto this day, **The Angel which redeemed me** from all evil, bless the lads." Jacob calls Yahweh both "the God" and "the Angel" — the same person.

### The 2300 Evening Morning (vs. 14)

- "Unto two thousand and three hundred evening morning; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."
- "Evening and morning" = the daily cycle of the sanctuary service.
- The sanctuary shall be **cleansed** = the Day of Atonement (Leviticus 16).

### "Broken Without Hand" (vss. 25-26)

- **vs. 25** — "He shall stand up against the Prince of princes; but he shall be broken without hand."
- **Daniel 2:45** — "The stone was cut out of the mountain without hands."
- **Daniel 7:9-11** — The judgment scene leads to the beast's destruction.
- **Daniel 8:14** — "The evening and the morning."
- "Broken without hand," "without hands," and "the evening and the morning" all refer to **the Judgment**.

## Day of Atonement Framework

| Daniel 8 Vision                          | Leviticus 16                                        |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Dead ram and living he-goat              | Sacrificial ram and goat of Azazel (Lev 16:5,21)    |
| The sanctuary trampled                   | The sanctuary cleansed on Day of Atonement          |
| 2300 evening morning: sanctuary cleansed | Day of Atonement: annual cleansing of the sanctuary |

## Novel Findings

1. **Ram = Christ-type; He-goat = Satan-type** — encoded in the animals themselves via Leviticus 17:7, Matthew 25, and Isaiah 45:1.
2. **The little horn's three directions** map to three camps of Israel. The little horn (eagle/north/Rome) attacks the other three.
3. **"Captain of the host"** is the pre-incarnate Christ — same figure that appeared to Joshua and Moses.
4. **"Broken without hand" = "cut without hands" = "evening and morning"** — three expressions for the same event: the judgment.
5. **The entire chapter is Day of Atonement imagery** — ram, goat, sanctuary cleansing.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **2300 Days:** The prophetic time period leading to the cleansing of the sanctuary = the beginning of the investigative judgment (1844).
- **Sanctuary:** The sanctuary that is cleansed is the heavenly sanctuary where Christ ministers as High Priest.
- **Investigative Judgment:** The cleansing of the sanctuary IS the judgment.
- **Great Controversy:** Encoded in the very animals of the vision — Christ (ram) vs. Satan (goat).
- **Sabbath:** The little horn takes away "the daily" and casts down the sanctuary — attacks on God's worship system.

## Teaching Flow

1. The vision: Daniel at Shushan. A ram pushing in three directions — invincible.
2. A he-goat from the west — destroys the ram. Great horn broken, four stand up.
3. A little horn — waxes great toward the south, east, and glorious land. Identify the directions with the camps.
4. What do the animals mean? Ram = Cyrus = God's anointed = Christ-type. Goat = devils (Lev 17:7) = Satan-type.
5. The great controversy is in the animals themselves.
6. The little horn: takes away the daily, casts down the sanctuary, tramples the host.
7. "How long?" — 2300 evening morning. Then the sanctuary is cleansed.
8. What is the cleansing? The Day of Atonement. The judgment. (Leviticus 16)
9. Connect "broken without hand" to Daniel 2's stone and Daniel 7's judgment.
