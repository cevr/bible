## Layer A: The Gospels and Revelation 4:7

| Gospel of Matthew | Gospel of Mark               | Gospel of Luke | Gospel of John  |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------- | -------------- | --------------- |
| **Lion** (King)   | **Calf** (Sacrifice/Servant) | **Man** (Man)  | **Eagle** (God) |

- **Revelation 4:7** — "the first beast was like a lion, and the second beast like a calf, and the third beast had a face as a man, and the fourth beast was like a flying eagle."
- Matthew: mentions birth, wisemen search for a **king**, conflict with **king** Herod, genealogy = line of **kings**.
- Mark: NO mention of birth, fewest parables, fewest sermons — a servant's gospel.
- Luke: mentions birth with **shepherds** and **stable**, circumcision, dedication — like **any man**.
- John: NO mention of birth, introduced as **the Word/God**, called **the I AM**.
- Three earthly creatures (lion, calf, man) = three synoptic Gospels. One heavenly creature (eagle) = John.

## Layer B: Acts of the Apostles and Revelation 4:5

- Acts = actually "Acts of the **Holy Spirit**"
- **Acts 2:1-4** — "And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind, and it filled all the house where they were sitting. And there appeared unto them **cloven tongues like as of fire**, and it sat upon each of them. And they were all **filled with the Holy Ghost**, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance."
- **Acts 4:31** — "And when they had prayed, the place was shaken where they were assembled together; and they were all **filled with the Holy Ghost**, and they spake the word of God with boldness."
- **Acts 10:44-46** — "While Peter yet spake these words, **the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word**. And they of the circumcision which believed were astonished... because that on the Gentiles also was poured out the gift of the Holy Ghost. For they heard them speak with tongues, and magnify God."
- **Revelation 4:5** — "seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God."
- As God is threefold (past, present, future), the Holy Spirit is sevenfold in Revelation — occupying the seven periods of the church (Ephesus through Laodicea).

## Layer D: Hebrews and Revelation 1 — Christ Our High Priest (THE CENTER)

### Hebrews

- **Hebrews 2:17** — "Wherefore in all things it behoved him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be **a merciful and faithful high priest** in things pertaining to God, to make reconciliation for the sins of the people."
- **Hebrews 3:1** — "consider the Apostle and **High Priest** of our profession, Christ Jesus."
- **Hebrews 4:4,9-10** — "he spake in a certain place of **the seventh day**... There remaineth therefore **a Sabbath rest** [σαββατισμός, sabbatismos, #4521] to the people of God. For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his."
- **Hebrews 4:12-13** — "the **word of God** is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any **two-edged sword**... all things are **naked and opened unto the eyes of him** with whom we have to do."
- **Hebrews 4:14** — "we have a great **high priest**, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God."
- **Hebrews 5:5-6,10** — "Christ... Thou art a **priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec**."
- **Hebrews 6:20-7:6,11,15-17,21,24** — Extended discussion of Melchizedek priesthood: "**an unchangeable priesthood**."
- **Hebrews 8:1** — "We have such an **high priest**, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens."
- **Hebrews 9:11** — "Christ being come an **high priest** of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle."
- **Hebrews 10:19-21** — "boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through **the veil, that is to say, his flesh**."

### Revelation 1:10-16

- "I was in the Spirit on **the Lord's day**" (the Sabbath)
- "Seven golden candlesticks"
- "One like unto the Son of man, **clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle**" (high-priestly garments)
- "His eyes were as a flame of fire"
- "Out of his mouth went **a sharp two-edged sword**" (= Hebrews 4:12)

### Revelation 19:11-16 — The Same Christ

- **Revelation 19:11-16** — "And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called Faithful and True... His **eyes** were as a **flame of fire**... and his name is called **The Word of God**... And out of his mouth goeth **a sharp sword**, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall **rule them with a rod of iron**... And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, **KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS**."
- This is the same Christ described in Hebrews 4:12-13 (Word of God, two-edged sword, all things naked before His eyes) and Revelation 1:14-16 (eyes as flame of fire, sharp sword from mouth).

### The Center of the New Testament

Both Hebrews and Revelation 1 describe: Christ as High Priest, the Sabbath, the Word of God as a two-edged sword, all things naked and open before His eyes. **This is the heart of the New Testament: Christ as our High Priest ministering in the heavenly sanctuary.**

## Layer E: 2 Peter 1 and Revelation

- **2 Peter 1:19** — "We have also a more sure word of **prophecy**; whereunto ye do well that ye take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place, until the day dawn, and **the day star** arise in your hearts."
- **Revelation 22:16** — "I **Jesus**... am **the bright and morning star**."
- **The point of prophecy, according to 2 Peter 1:19, is to place the Day Star or Morning Star, Jesus Christ, in your heart.**

## Layer G: 1 John, 2 John, 3 John

- **1-3 John** = "John x 3"
- John is independently symbolic of God's last-day messengers to the world (the 144,000).
- x3 = the triple, referencing 7x3 or 777 — the Sabbath-keeping character.
- This says the 144,000 are centrally important to the New Testament church.

## Novel Findings

1. **The entire New Testament is a chiasm** with Christ as High Priest (Hebrews + Revelation 1) at the center.
2. **Sabbatismos** (Hebrews 4:9) — the Greek word explicitly preserves a Sabbath rest for God's people, at the structural center of the New Testament.
3. **Colossians = Laodicea** — Colossians 4:16 proves it. The sixth of Paul's churches matches the sixth of Revelation's churches (but in reversed pairing with Philadelphia).
4. **1-3 John = John x 3 = 777** — a reference to the 144,000's Sabbath-keeping character.
5. **2 Peter 1:19** — the point of all prophecy is to place Jesus (the Morning Star) in your heart.
6. **The Sermon on the Mount = the Sermon from the Judgment** — parallels the Ark and the Most Holy Place.
7. **John's Gospel structurally centers on the Sabbath conflict** — the Jews killed Jesus over Sabbath healing.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Christ's High Priesthood:** The structural center of the New Testament.
- **Sabbath:** Sabbatismos in Hebrews 4:9; "the Lord's day" in Revelation 1:10; Jesus' Sabbath healings in John.
- **Sanctuary:** The Sermon on the Mount parallels the sanctuary; Christ ministers in the heavenly sanctuary (Hebrews).
- **144,000:** 1-3 John = 777 = the Sabbath-keeping remnant.
- **Investigative Judgment:** The cleansing of the temple (John 2) and the Sabbath test — all professed Christians must pass through the judgment.
