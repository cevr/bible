## Theme

"Progressive Abominations in God's Temple" — Four escalating stages of apostasy within the sanctuary itself, each moving closer to the inner sanctuary, culminating in sun worship.

## The Four Abominations

### Progressive Structure

The phrase **"turn thee yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations"** appears three times (vss. 6, 13, 15), marking four escalating stages:

```
Stage 1   vss. 3-6      Image of jealousy at the gate (toward the north)
           "turn thee yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations"
Stage 2   vss. 7-12     70 elders with incense, creeping things and idols on walls
           "turn thee yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations"
Stage 3   vss. 13-14    Women weeping for Tammuz (toward the north)
           "turn thee yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations"
Stage 4   vss. 15-16    25 men worshipping the sun toward the east
```

### Key Texts

#### Stage 1: Image of Jealousy (vss. 3-6)

- **vs. 3** — "The spirit lifted me up... to the door of the inner gate that looketh **toward the north**; where was the seat of the image of jealousy, which provoketh to jealousy."
- **vs. 5** — "Then said he unto me, Son of man, lift up thine eyes now the way **toward the north**. So I lifted up mine eyes the way toward the north, and behold northward at the gate of the altar this image of jealousy in the entry."
- **vs. 6** — "He said furthermore unto me, Son of man, seest thou what they do? even the great abominations that the house of Israel committeth here, that I should go far off from my sanctuary? but **turn thee yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations**."

#### Stage 2: 70 Elders with Idols (vss. 7-12)

- **vss. 9-10** — "Go in, and behold the wicked abominations that they do here. So I went in and saw; and behold every form of creeping things, and abominable beasts, and all the idols of the house of Israel, pourtrayed upon the wall round about."
- **vs. 11** — "And there stood before them **seventy men of the ancients of the house of Israel, and in the midst of them stood Jaazaniah the son of Shaphan**, with every man his censer in his hand; and a thick cloud of incense went up."
- **vs. 12** — "Then said he unto me, Son of man, hast thou seen what the ancients of the house of Israel do in the dark, every man in the chambers of his imagery? for they say, The Lord seeth us not; the Lord hath forsaken the earth."

#### Stage 3: Women Weeping for Tammuz (vss. 13-14)

- **vs. 14** — "Then he brought me to the door of the gate of the Lord's house which was **toward the north**; and, behold, there sat women weeping for Tammuz."
- Tammuz = a pagan fertility god. Mourning for Tammuz was a Babylonian religious practice imported into the Lord's temple.

#### Stage 4: Sun Worship (vss. 15-16)

- **vs. 16** — "And he brought me into the inner court of the Lord's house, and, behold, at the door of the temple of the Lord, between the porch and the altar, were about **five and twenty men**, with their **backs toward the temple of the Lord**, and their **faces toward the east**; and they **worshipped the sun toward the east**."
- The worst abomination: leaders of Israel turn their backs on God's sanctuary and worship the sun.

### God's Response (vss. 17-18)

- **vss. 17-18** — "Is it a light thing to the house of Judah that they commit the abominations which they commit here? for they have filled the land with violence, and have returned to provoke me to anger: and, lo, they put the branch to their nose. Therefore will I also deal in fury: mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity: and though they cry in mine ears with a loud voice, yet will I not hear them."

## The Directional Pattern

Each stage moves **toward the north** — deeper into the sanctuary:

- The image of jealousy is at the gate, looking north (vs. 5)
- The 70 elders are inside the walls (vss. 7-10)
- The women weeping for Tammuz are at the gate of the Lord's house, toward the north (vs. 14)
- The 25 men are at the inner court, between the porch and the altar (vs. 16)

The abominations progress inward — from the outer gate to the inner court. The closer to the Holy of Holies, the greater the abomination.

## Sun Worship as the Climax

The final and worst abomination is **sun worship**:

- 25 men turn their backs on the temple (reject God)
- Face east (toward the rising sun)
- Worship the sun

This connects directly to the Sabbath/Sunday issue:

- The seventh-day Sabbath = worship of the Creator
- Sun-day = worship redirected toward the sun
- The progression in Ezekiel 8 shows how apostasy escalates until God's own people worship the sun with their backs turned to His sanctuary.

## Novel Findings

1. **Four escalating stages of abomination** — each progressively closer to the inner sanctuary, each worse than the last, marked by the threefold "greater abominations" refrain.
2. **Sun worship is the final abomination** — the worst offense is not the idols, not the pagan mourning, but worshipping the sun with backs turned to God's temple.
3. **The northward direction** — the abominations face/move toward the north (the direction of God in the compass framework), a counterfeit approach to the divine.
4. **70 elders** — the leadership of Israel is complicit. Apostasy is top-down.
5. **"The Lord seeth us not"** (vs. 12) — the elders believe God is absent. They practice abominations in secret, not realizing God sees everything.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Sabbath vs. Sunday:** Sun worship (Stage 4) = the ultimate abomination. The Sabbath/Sunday issue is not trivial — it is the climax of apostasy.
- **Sanctuary:** The abominations occur within God's own temple. Apostasy begins inside the church, not outside.
- **Investigative Judgment:** God investigates each stage — He brings Ezekiel to see what is happening. The judgment begins at the house of God (1 Peter 4:17).
- **Church and State:** The 25 men (likely the 24 priestly courses + the high priest) use their religious authority in the service of false worship.
- **Great Controversy:** The pattern moves from subtle idolatry (an image) to full-blown sun worship — the gradual corruption of truth.

## Teaching Flow

1. Setting: Ezekiel is transported in vision to the temple in Jerusalem. God says, "I will show you what they are doing."
2. Stage 1: The image of jealousy at the north gate. Idolatry at the entrance.
3. "Turn again — you will see greater abominations."
4. Stage 2: 70 elders in the dark with incense and idol-covered walls. Leadership corruption. "The Lord seeth us not."
5. "Turn again — greater abominations."
6. Stage 3: Women weeping for Tammuz. Pagan worship imported into the temple.
7. "Turn again — greater abominations than these."
8. Stage 4: 25 men between the porch and altar, backs to the temple, worshipping the sun toward the east.
9. The climax: sun worship. Backs turned to God. The worst abomination in all four stages.
10. Connect to the Sabbath/Sunday issue: the final test is about worship direction. Who do you face — the Creator or the sun?
11. Close with God's response (vss. 17-18): fury without pity. This is why the judgment comes.
