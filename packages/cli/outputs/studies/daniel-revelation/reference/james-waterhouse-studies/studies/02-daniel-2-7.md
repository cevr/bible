## Why This Section Exists

Daniel is divided by language: ch. 1 in Hebrew, chs. 2-7 in Aramaic, chs. 8-12 in Hebrew. The Aramaic section is also arranged **out of chronological order** (ch. 7 belongs between chs. 4 and 5). Both the language switch and the reordering are deliberate structural signals.

## The Four-Sided Nature of Christ in Daniel 2-12

| Daniel chs. 2-7                      | ch. 8 (+9)                   | ch. 10        | ch. 12 (+11)             |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------- | ------------- | ------------------------ |
| Call to war with the Lion of Babylon | Slain Ram                    | Man in Linen  | Man in Linen between Two |
| **Lion (King)**                      | **Calf (Sacrifice/Servant)** | **Man (Man)** | **Eagle (God)**          |

- **Revelation 4:6-7** — the four living creatures around the throne: lion, calf, man, flying eagle.
- **chs. 2-7, "Lion of Babylon"**: the lion of Babylon is the usurped kingdom of Christ. Prophecy is given so we can fight for souls to take back the kingdom for its rightful owner, Jesus Christ.

## Key Principle: Beast = King = Kingdom

- **Daniel 7:17** — "These great beasts, which are four, are four kings."
- **Daniel 7:23** — "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom."
- **Daniel 2:37-39** — "Thou, O king, art a king of kings: for the God of heaven hath given thee a kingdom... thou art this head of gold... after thee shall arise another kingdom."
- In Hebrew thought, king and kingdom are interchangeable. The greatest king represents the kingdom.

## "Fall of Babylon" — King = Kingdom Applied

- **Daniel 4** — How can the fall of Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon, be equated with the fall of Babylon?
- Because in the Hebrew mindset, king and kingdom are interchangeable.
- The king is also represented as a tree (Daniel 4; Ezekiel 31 — the Assyrian was a cedar in Lebanon).
- **Ezekiel 31:3-12** — "Behold, the Assyrian was a **cedar in Lebanon** with fair branches, and with a shadowing shroud, and of an high stature; and his top was among the thick boughs... his height was exalted above all the trees of the field... all the fowls of heaven made their nests in his boughs... all great nations dwelt under his shadow... nor any tree in the **garden of God** was like unto him in his beauty... all the **trees of Eden**, that were in the garden of God, **envied him**... I have therefore delivered him into the hand of the mighty one of the nations... **strangers, the terrible of the nations, have cut him off**."

## Novel Findings

1. **The deliberate reordering** — ch. 7 placed after ch. 6 instead of between chs. 4 and 5, creating the A-B-C-C'-B'-A' chiasm. This proves intentional literary design.
2. **The three-step pattern** — Prophecy → Persecution → Fall of Babylon is the engine of the great controversy. This pattern repeats in every era of church history.
3. **The four-sided Christ** across Daniel 2-12 — each major section of Daniel presents Christ from a different angle, matching the four living creatures.
4. **Babylon as the statue's name** — the entire statue in Daniel 2 IS Babylon in different phases. The head names the whole.

## Doctrinal Connections

- **Great Controversy:** The structure itself teaches the pattern — truth proclaimed leads to persecution, which leads to Babylon's fall.
- **Sabbath:** The persecution of Daniel 3 and 6 involves worship/prayer — the issue is always worship and loyalty to God vs. the state's demands.
- **Church and State:** The Babylonian system always involves a king/state enforcing worship. This is the template for the mark of the beast.
- **Second Coming:** Babylon falls. This is the certain outcome. We are living between Persecution (B') and the Fall (C'/A').

## Teaching Flow

1. Show the language structure: Hebrew-Aramaic-Hebrew. Ask why.
2. Show the chronological reordering of ch. 7. Ask why.
3. Reveal the chiastic structure: A-B-C-C'-B'-A'.
4. Draw out the message: Prophecy → Persecution → Fall of Babylon.
5. Show that this pattern repeats in every age (early church, Reformation, end times).
6. Show the four-sided Christ across Daniel 2-12.
7. Conclude: Prophecy is a weapon. God gave it to us so we could fight effectively in the great controversy.
