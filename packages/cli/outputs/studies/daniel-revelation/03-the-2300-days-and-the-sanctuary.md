# Study 3: The 2300 Days and the Sanctuary — Daniel 8-9

**Daniel sees a third vision — a ram, a goat, and a horn that waxes "exceeding great" until it strikes heaven itself. At the end, two holy ones talk over his head: "How long?" The answer: "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed." Daniel faints without understanding it. Years later Gabriel returns — not with a new vision, but to finish the old one. And he hands Daniel the most specific mathematical prophecy in the Bible: a timeline that pinpoints the Messiah's baptism, the Messiah's death, and the year the heavenly judgment would open. This is the chapter where prophecy becomes dateable.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 8 (entire chapter)
- Daniel 9 (entire chapter)
- Leviticus 16 (the Day of Atonement)
- Numbers 14:34
- Ezekiel 4:6

## A Vision with the Clock Added

Dan 8 is dated "in the third year of the reign of king Belshazzar" (Dan 8:1) — about 551 BC, two years after Dan 7. The vocabulary shifts. The recapitulation continues. But one new element makes this chapter stand apart from everything before it: **a number**.

- The same empires reappear, identified by name this time
  - The ram with two horns = "the kings of Media and Persia" (Dan 8:20)
  - The rough goat = "the king of Grecia" (Dan 8:21)
  - The great horn between the goat's eyes = "the first king" (Alexander the Great — Dan 8:21)
  - The four horns that replace the great horn = "four kingdoms... but not in his power" (Dan 8:22) — the Hellenistic four-way split after Alexander
- What Dan 2 and Dan 7 left to historical fit, Dan 8 names outright
- [→] Recapitulation is not vague. Each pass names more. `[⟲]` Beasts/kingdoms thread: Dan 2 metals → Dan 7 beasts → Dan 8 ram/goat (now named).

[DYK🔎] Babylon is missing from Dan 8. It is not forgotten — it is _about to be past_. Dan 8 is given in Belshazzar's third year, roughly a decade before Babylon falls (539 BC). Gabriel picks up the prophecy _from where Daniel is standing_. The vision walks forward from Medo-Persia because, for Daniel, Babylon is already ending.

## The Dan 8-11 Macro-Parallel

Before we dig into the details, notice the architecture. Dan 8 through Dan 11 are distinct dated visions, but they form a macro-parallel — each covers overlapping ground, and Daniel 9 sits between them explaining the _time_ element Daniel 8 left hanging.

- Dan 8 (551 BC) describes the ram, goat, little horn, attack on the sanctuary, 2300 days, and Daniel fainting without understanding the time
- Dan 9 (538 BC) is Daniel's prayer plus Gabriel's explanation of _the time_ — the 70 weeks cut off from the 2300 days
- Dan 10-12 (536 BC) is Daniel's final vision: the heavenly Man, a detailed walk through Persia, Greece, the four-way split, Rome's rise, and the final crisis (ending at "the glorious holy mountain" — Dan 11:45, often understood as Mount Zion)

When you lay Dan 8 and Dan 10-11 side by side, the broad pattern recapitulates:

```
Dan 8        ram and goat → little horn → sanctuary attack → 2300 days → "shut up the vision" (Dan 8:26)
  Dan 9                                       DANIEL'S PRAYER + the 70 weeks
Dan 10-11    Persia and Grecia → four-way split → Rome → final crisis → "the glorious holy mountain" (Dan 11:45)
```

- [→] Two consequences for the reading:
  1. Dan 9's 70 weeks are not a parenthesis. They are the _interpretive heart_ of the Dan 8 timeline. The 70 weeks give Dan 8's 2300-day count its starting reference.
  2. Dan 8 and Dan 10-11 are meant to be read together. Dan 11's "glorious holy mountain" ending answers back to Dan 8's ram stamped down. We will use this in Study 5.

## The Ram, the Goat, and the Little Horn

Dan 8 walks from Medo-Persia through the rise of a power that would attack the sanctuary itself.

- **The ram — Medo-Persia** (Dan 8:3-4, 20)
  - "Two horns" — one higher than the other, "the higher came up last" = Persia dominating the earlier coalition with Media
  - "Pushing westward, and northward, and southward" — the three directions of Persian expansion (Babylon west, Lydia north, Egypt south)
  - "No beasts might stand before him" — unchallenged in its era

- **The goat — Greece** (Dan 8:5-8, 21-22)
  - Comes "from the west on the face of the whole earth, and touched not the ground" — the speed of Alexander's conquest (roughly 334-323 BC, the entire Persian Empire taken in a decade)
  - "A notable horn between his eyes" = Alexander himself (Dan 8:21)
  - "Smote the ram, and brake his two horns" — Alexander defeats Darius III at Issus (333 BC) and Gaugamela (331 BC)
  - "The great horn was broken; and for it came up four notable ones" — after Alexander's sudden death at age 32, his empire divided among four of his generals: Cassander (Macedonia), Lysimachus (Thrace), Seleucus (Syria/east), Ptolemy (Egypt) — the four-way split also seen in Dan 7's leopard with four heads

- **The little horn — rising out of the Hellenistic world, growing into something bigger**
  - "Out of one of them came forth a little horn, which waxed exceeding great, toward the south, and toward the east, and toward the pleasant land" (Dan 8:9)
  - Same directions as the ram _plus_ "the pleasant land" (Israel) — a later power that inherited Greek territory and moved in the same directions
  - The only power that fits historically: Rome, which entered the Hellenistic world from the west, absorbed Greece into its empire (Pydna, 168 BC), and eventually controlled all three directions plus Israel
  - This is the _same_ little horn as Dan 7, seen through Dan 8's sanctuary lens. Dan 7 emphasized the beast's persecution; Dan 8 emphasizes its attack on the sanctuary

- **What the horn _does_** (Dan 8:10-13)
  - "It waxed great, even to the host of heaven; and it cast down some of the host and of the stars to the ground, and stamped upon them" (v. 10)
  - "Yea, he magnified himself even to the prince of the host, and by him the daily was taken away, and the place of his sanctuary was cast down" (v. 11)
  - "An host was given him against the daily by reason of transgression, and it cast down the truth to the ground; and it practised, and prospered" (v. 12)
  - Four targets: the host of heaven (God's people), the prince of the host (Christ as High Priest), the daily (the continual priestly ministry), and the sanctuary itself (the place where that ministry is conducted)
  - [→] This is a different attack than Dan 7's. Dan 7's little horn "made war with the saints." Dan 8's little horn goes after _the sanctuary and its service_. Both true. Different zoom.

[DYK🔎] "The daily" in Dan 8:11-13 (Hebrew _tamid_) literally means "the continual." Translators have added the word "sacrifice" in italics or brackets — but the Hebrew word _tamid_ is not specifically the daily _sacrifice_. It covers the ongoing priestly ministry as a whole: the morning and evening offerings, the incense, the shewbread, the lamps. When the little horn "takes away the daily," it is disrupting Christ's continual high-priestly ministry — not just one ritual. The KJV italics flag this (the word "sacrifice" is supplied, not in the Hebrew). We will see in Study 4 how the little horn substituted a counterfeit continual ministry on earth for Christ's real continual ministry in heaven.

## "How Long?" — 2300 Days

The vision reaches its climax with a dialogue Daniel overhears between two heavenly beings.

- **The question** (Dan 8:13)
  - "How long shall be the vision concerning the daily, and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot?"
  - The question covers the full scope: the attack on the daily, the desolating power, and the time that all of this continues

- **The answer** (Dan 8:14)
  - "And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
  - Hebrew literally: "evenings and mornings" — a Genesis 1 echo of creation days. 2300 of them.

- **Two options for reading 2300 "evenings and mornings"**
  1. Halve it — 1150 literal days (some commentators split the figure because evening+morning = one day/night cycle)
  2. Read it straight — 2300 prophetic days = 2300 years (by the year-day principle)
  - The straight reading is strongly supported on multiple grounds: (a) the Hebrew "evening and morning" phrase in Gen 1 counts one full day, not half-days; (b) the prophecy concerns multi-millennial powers (Medo-Persia to end-time), which 1150 literal days cannot fit; (c) Dan 9's 70 weeks _share the same starting point_ and only fit as 490 years — which, within this reading, requires the 2300 to be years too

- `[KEY🔑]` **The year-day principle**
  - "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, each day for a year, shall ye bear your iniquities, even forty years" (Num 14:34)
  - "I have appointed thee each day for a year" (Eze 4:6)
  - God states the rule twice, in two different contexts, to two different prophets. In apocalyptic time-prophecy, one prophetic day = one literal year.
  - Applied: 2300 days = 2300 years; 1260 days = 1260 years; 70 weeks (490 days) = 490 years.
  - This is the rule the Bible gives itself for its own time prophecies. Without it, Dan + Rev do not fit history. With it, they line up.

- **Daniel faints**
  - "I Daniel fainted, and was sick certain days; afterward I rose up, and did the king's business; and I was astonished at the vision, but none understood it" (Dan 8:27)
  - Gabriel has told Daniel _what_ would happen. But the _when_ was only a number hanging in the air — 2300 days with no starting point. Daniel does not know when the count begins. Neither does the reader.
  - The vision is left unfinished. For about a decade.

## Daniel's Prayer — The Structural Heart

Dan 9 opens with Daniel reading Jeremiah's scroll during Darius the Mede's first year (~538 BC, right after Babylon's fall). Jeremiah promised seventy years of captivity (Jer 25:11; 29:10); Daniel counts, realizes the time is nearly up, and does what the prophet does: he prays.

- **What Daniel prays**
  - Not for himself. For "thy city Jerusalem" and "thy sanctuary that is desolate" (Dan 9:16-17)
  - Confesses the nation's sin ("we have sinned... we have rebelled" — Dan 9:5) rather than distancing himself from it
  - Appeals "not... for our righteousnesses, but for thy great mercies" (Dan 9:18)
  - Ends urgently: "O Lord, hear; O Lord, forgive; O Lord, hearken and do; defer not" (Dan 9:19)

- **Why this prayer is the structural center of Dan 8-11**
  - Dan 8 ended with Daniel fainting over a vision he could not interpret
  - Ten years later, reading Jeremiah, Daniel prays about the fate of his city and sanctuary
  - Gabriel is dispatched _while_ Daniel is praying (Dan 9:21-23)
  - The answer is not about Jeremiah's 70 years — it is about the _2300 days_ from Dan 8
  - "Understand the matter, and consider _the vision_" (Dan 9:23) — which vision? The one from two years earlier that Gabriel left unfinished
  - [→] Dan 9 is Gabriel completing Dan 8. The prayer pulled the interpretation through. That is why the prayer is at the center of the structure.

[Q] **"If Dan 8 and Dan 9 are one prophecy, why are they two chapters?"**
→ Because the chapter divisions were added in the 13th century AD by readers, not by Daniel. The text flows straight from Dan 8's unanswered "2300 days" to Dan 9's explanation. Original Hebrew scrolls had no chapter markers. Read it as one movement.

## The 70 Weeks

- **The announcement** (Dan 9:24)
  - "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, and to make an end of sins, and to make reconciliation for iniquity, and to bring in everlasting righteousness, and to seal up the vision and prophecy, and to anoint the most Holy"
  - The word translated "determined" (Hebrew _chathak_) carries the sense "to cut off," and is rare in the Hebrew Bible — this is its only occurrence. Historicists have long read this as "cut off _from_" something larger; within this reading, the 70 weeks are the first segment of the 2300 days Daniel has been puzzling over since the previous vision. This is an inference from the broader context of Dan 8-9, not an explicit "cut off from" phrase in Dan 9:24 itself.

- **The math**
  - 70 "weeks" × 7 days per week = 490 prophetic days = 490 years (year-day)
  - Six purposes given in Dan 9:24 — three about ending sin, three about establishing righteousness and anointing the sanctuary. All six fulfilled by Christ's ministry and cross.

- **The starting point** (Dan 9:25)
  - "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks"
  - The starting event: a decree _to restore and to build Jerusalem_
  - Scripture names multiple Persian decrees about Jerusalem:
    - Cyrus (~538 BC, Ezra 1) — rebuilt the _temple_, not the city
    - Darius (~519 BC, Ezra 6) — reaffirmed Cyrus, still temple-focused
    - **Artaxerxes (457 BC, Ezra 7)** — rebuilt civil authority, restored judges and magistrates, gave autonomy to Jerusalem itself. This is the first decree fitting "restore and build Jerusalem."
    - Artaxerxes again (444 BC, Nehemiah 2) — walls-only authorization given to Nehemiah
  - The 457 BC decree (Ezra 7:12-26) is the only one that matches the language of Dan 9:25

- **The milestones**

  | Date            | Event                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Source        |
  | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------- |
  | 457 BC (autumn) | Decree of Artaxerxes goes into effect                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | Ezra 7        |
  | 408 BC          | End of the first 7 weeks (49 years) — "the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troublous times" (Dan 9:25). Roughly: walls/infrastructure restoration complete                                                                                      | Historical    |
  | AD 27 (autumn)  | End of the 69th week (7 + 62 = 69 weeks = 483 years). "Messiah the Prince" appears. Jesus is baptized and anointed by the Spirit — His public messianic ministry begins                                                                                            | Luke 3:21-22  |
  | AD 31 (spring)  | In the "midst" of the 70th week, "Messiah shall be cut off, but not for himself" (Dan 9:26). Jesus is crucified. The veil is torn; sacrifices end in heaven's reckoning                                                                                            | Matt 27:50-51 |
  | AD 34           | Close of the 70 weeks. Stephen's death (Acts 7), scattering beyond Jerusalem (Acts 8:1-4); the mission breaks beyond Judaism, with explicit Gentile admission unfolding in Acts 10. Verifiable by walking Acts backward from a dated Roman inscription — see below | Acts 7-8      |

- **Why the AD 34 date is verifiable — not just stipulated**
  - AD 34 is the prophetic answer to 457 BC + 490 years. But it is also reachable by an _independent_ path from the New Testament itself, using dated Roman governors as anchor points
  - **The Gallio inscription (Delphi, discovered 1905)** fixes the proconsulship of Gallio — named as proconsul of Achaia in Acts 18:12-17 — to **AD 51-52**. This is an externally dated Roman inscription, not a Christian source
  - **Walk backward through Paul's chronology:**
    - Paul appears before Gallio in Corinth after staying there 18 months (Acts 18:11-12) → Paul arrives in Corinth ~AD 50
    - Corinth comes during Paul's second missionary journey, which begins after the Jerusalem Council (Acts 15) → Council ~AD 49
    - Paul in Gal 2:1 says he went to Jerusalem "fourteen years" after an earlier visit; Gal 1:18 says the first post-conversion visit was "three years" after his conversion. Total span from conversion to the Council: roughly 14-17 years
    - Council ~AD 49 minus 14-17 years = **Paul's conversion ~AD 34-35**
  - Stephen's death (Acts 7) happens immediately before Paul's conversion (Acts 7:58-8:3; 9:1-19) — Acts reads the transition tight, within months, not years
  - **Result:** Stephen's martyrdom = **~AD 34**, reached by arithmetic backward from a dated Roman inscription
  - **Corroborating anchor — Pontius Pilate:** Josephus (_Antiquities_ 18.4.1-2) records Pilate's recall to Rome in AD 36. Stephen's death must precede Pilate's removal to fit Acts' sequencing. That places Stephen ≤ AD 36. Consistent.
  - [→] Two independent chains land on AD 34. One runs _forward_ from Artaxerxes' decree through the year-day principle (457 BC + 490 years). The other runs _backward_ from the Gallio inscription through Pauline chronology in Acts and Galatians. Prophecy and history converge on the same year. That is what makes it an anchor, not an assertion.

- **Why the AD 27 baptism verification is striking**
  - Dan 9:25 gives 7 + 62 = 69 weeks (483 years) from the decree to "Messiah the Prince"
  - 457 BC + 483 years = AD 27 (the BC/AD transition skips a year zero, so the raw arithmetic already accounts for this: –457 + 483 = +27)
  - Luke 3:1 dates Jesus' baptism to "the fifteenth year of the reign of Tiberius Caesar." Scholars debate whether this counts from Tiberius' sole reign (AD 14) or from his co-regency with Augustus (c. AD 11-12); the co-regency / provincial / inclusive reckoning places the fifteenth year in autumn AD 27
  - "Messiah" (Heb. _mashiyach_) literally means "anointed one." Christ is anointed with the Holy Spirit at His baptism (Matt 3:16; Acts 10:38) — His public messianic ministry begins at that moment
  - [→] This is not interpretive gymnastics. The math: 457 BC + 483 years = AD 27. On one standard reckoning, Luke dates Jesus' public ministry to that very year, anointed at baptism. The prophecy was written 550+ years earlier. It specified the year.

[DYK🔎] The **Gallio inscription** at Delphi (a letter from Emperor Claudius fragmentarily preserved on stone, discovered in 1905) is the single most important external anchor for New Testament chronology. It names Gallio as proconsul of Achaia and dates his term to AD 51-52 — a Roman date fixed by Claudius' regnal reckoning, independent of any Christian source. Acts 18:12-17 places Paul before Gallio during that term. From there you can walk both forward (to the later missionary journeys and epistles) and backward (to Paul's conversion ~AD 34-35, and therefore Stephen's martyrdom at ~AD 34). The entire framework of Acts and the Pauline epistles is externally datable from this single Greek stone fragment. Two independent chains — prophetic math (457 BC + 490) and Roman epigraphy (Delphi → Acts → Paul → Stephen) — land on the same year: AD 34.

## The 2300 Days Reach to 1844

- **Same starting point** — the decree of 457 BC (historicist reading: the 70 weeks are "cut off" from the 2300 days, so they share the beginning)
- **2300 years from 457 BC:**
  - Simple arithmetic: –457 + 2300 = +1843; crossing BC/AD skips a year zero, which the BC/AD convention already handles, so 457 BC autumn + 2300 years lands in AD 1844 autumn
  - Cross-check through the 70 weeks: 457 BC + 490 years = AD 34 (end of 70 weeks); AD 34 + the remaining 1810 years = AD 1844
  - Ezra 7:9 gives the journey's timing (first-month departure from Babylon, fifth-month arrival at Jerusalem, 457 BC); historicist chronologists (Froom, Hasel, and others) argue the decree's effective enforcement falls in the autumn seventh month. On that reading, the 2300-day count, starting in the autumn of 457 BC, terminates on the Day of Atonement, autumn 1844 (traditionally calculated to October 22, 1844)

- **What happens in 1844?**
  - Dan 8:14 says: "then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
  - The cleansing of the sanctuary is the Hebrew Day of Atonement (Lev 16:30, 33 — _yom kippur_). That is the one day each year when the earthly sanctuary was "cleansed."
  - But the earthly sanctuary had been destroyed in AD 70. The Jewish sacrificial system had ceased. What sanctuary is cleansed in 1844?
  - The _heavenly_ one. "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man" (Heb 8:1-2)
  - Heb 9:23-24 makes it explicit: "It was therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these [earthly sacrifices]; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these. For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us"
  - In 1844, Christ's high-priestly work entered its final, most-holy phase — the heavenly Day of Atonement, which Rev 14:6-7 will announce to the world as "the hour of his judgment"
  - [→] 1844 is not the Second Coming. It is the beginning of the heavenly judgment — what Dan 7 described as the courtroom opening, what Dan 8 described as the sanctuary being cleansed, what Rev 14 will announce as the hour of judgment come

[DYK🔎] The first half of the nineteenth century saw a broad Advent awakening across continents — William Miller in New York State preaching the 2300-day timeline and the 1844 terminus; Joseph Wolff traveling through the Middle East and Central Asia announcing an imminent advent; Manuel Lacunza (a Chilean Jesuit writing in European exile, d. 1801) producing _The Coming of Messiah in Glory and Majesty_, which Edward Irving translated into English in 1827 and promoted in Britain before his own death in 1834. The movements differed in exact dates, texts, and methods, but a recovered interest in Daniel's time prophecies and the nearness of Christ's coming was genuinely global. When October 22, 1844 came and went without Christ returning to earth, the Millerites in particular were shattered — the "Great Disappointment." Only after further study of Heb 9 did the Seventh-day Adventist pioneers conclude the sanctuary being cleansed was _heaven's_, not earth's. Historicists have read Rev 10's "sweet in the mouth, bitter in the belly" as Rev's own commentary on this experience. We will walk through Rev 10 in Study 11.

## The Anchor Dates

Historicists have long held out a short list of dates the timeline should be judged by. Not every date on every historicist chart deserves the same confidence level.

- **Anchor dates within the historicist/Adventist chronology — historically testable:**
  - 457 BC — Artaxerxes' seventh-year decree (Ezra 7:7, 9)
  - AD 27 (autumn) — Jesus' baptism begins His public ministry (Luke 3:1, 21-22)
  - AD 31 (spring) — the crucifixion, "midst of the week" (critical scholarship also discusses AD 30 and AD 33; the 31 date is the traditional historicist fit for Dan 9:27)
  - AD 34 — traditional historicist close of the 70 weeks
  - AD 1844 (autumn) — end of the 2300-day count; heavenly Day of Atonement begins

- **Secondary dates carry less weight:**
  - AD 538, AD 1798 (1260 years) — solid historical correlations for the papal phase, but the exact day-counting is less certain than the anchor dates
  - AD 508, AD 1843 (1290 and 1335 days of Dan 12) — held by many historicists; the fit is real but the secondary calculations carry less than absolute force

- [→] Hold what the text requires with conviction. Hold what the text allows with openness. Refuse to defend what the text does not demand. This is how historicists earn a hearing.

## Six Purposes of the 70 Weeks — All Fulfilled in Christ

Dan 9:24 lists six things the 70 weeks would accomplish. Run the list, and the six point to one Person.

| Purpose (Dan 9:24)                   | Fulfilled in Christ                                                                               |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Finish the transgression"           | Christ bore the nation's (and the world's) transgression on the cross — Isa 53:5-6, 1 Pet 2:24    |
| "Make an end of sins"                | "Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many" (Heb 9:28)                                     |
| "Make reconciliation for iniquity"   | "God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself" (2 Cor 5:19)                              |
| "Bring in everlasting righteousness" | "This is his name whereby he shall be called, THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS" (Jer 23:6); Rom 3:21-22 |
| "Seal up the vision and prophecy"    | Christ's fulfillment of prophecy sealed (authenticated) the predictive word — Luke 24:44          |
| "Anoint the most Holy"               | Christ's ascension inaugurates His priestly ministry in the heavenly sanctuary — Heb 9:11-12, 24  |

- [→] All six fit one Person. None of them fit a future individual antichrist or a rebuilt third temple. The 70 weeks are about the first coming, not the second.

## Key Takeaways

- Dan 8 is the third recapitulation — same sequence, now with the _sanctuary_ in focus and a _time prophecy_ attached
- Ram = Medo-Persia, Goat = Greece (both named in the text), four horns = Alexander's successors, little horn = Rome in its pagan and papal phases
- `[KEY🔑]` **Year-day principle** (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6) — in apocalyptic time prophecy, one prophetic day = one literal year
- `[⟲]` **Sanctuary thread** — Dan 8:11-14 is the first link. It continues through Heb 8-9 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 15:5-8
- The 2300 days are best read straight (as 2300 prophetic years), not halved
- Dan 9's 70 weeks are "cut off" (_chathak_) from the 2300 days; they share the starting point
- Starting point: Artaxerxes' decree, 457 BC (Ezra 7); 69 weeks (483 years) land on AD 27 (Jesus' baptism); "midst of the week" lands on AD 31 (crucifixion); 70 weeks end AD 34 (Stephen's martyrdom) — independently datable by walking Acts backward from the Gallio inscription (AD 51-52)
- 2300 years from 457 BC = 1844, the beginning of the heavenly Day of Atonement
- The sanctuary being cleansed is _heaven's_, not earth's — Heb 8:1-2; 9:23-24 make this unavoidable
- All six purposes of the 70 weeks (Dan 9:24) are fulfilled in Christ's first coming
- Anchor dates (457 BC, AD 27, AD 31, AD 34, 1844) are historically testable; secondary dates should be held with the confidence they earn

## Next: Study 4 — The Sanctuary Key

Dan 8:14 says the sanctuary will be cleansed. We have dated that event to 1844. But what _is_ the sanctuary, and why does its cleansing matter? Study 4 steps back from the timeline and takes a single chapter — the whole book of Leviticus 16 compressed into a study — to show how the earthly sanctuary was a three-phase model of Christ's work: the cross (courtyard), ongoing intercession (holy place), and the final judgment (most holy place). Dan 8:14 meets Rev 14:7. The Day of Atonement meets the hour of judgment. This is the key that unlocks the rest of the series.
