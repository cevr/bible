# Study 16: Babylon Exposed and Fallen — Revelation 17-18

**The second angel declared Babylon fallen. Now Rev 17-18 shows us the what, the who, and the how. A woman rides a scarlet-colored beast. She wears purple and scarlet, is decked with gold and precious stones, and holds a cup full of abominations. On her forehead is a name: MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH. She is "drunken with the blood of the saints." John, who has seen beasts and plagues and dragons, writes that he "wondered with great admiration." Then the angel explains. Seven heads = seven mountains; "the waters are peoples"; the ten horns will turn on the woman and destroy her. Rev 18 follows with the full verdict: "Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become the habitation of devils." God calls His people: "Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins." One hour and the whole system collapses — merchants weep, kings mourn, and heaven rejoices. Study 16 is where Rev identifies the beast system completely and shows how it ends.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 17 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 18 (entire chapter)
- Jeremiah 51 (OT Babylon's fall — the background)
- Isaiah 47 (the fall of the "virgin daughter of Babylon")

## The Harlot on the Scarlet Beast — Rev 17

The scene opens with one of the seven angels who had the seven plagues inviting John to see "the judgment of the great whore."

- **The setting** (Rev 17:1-3)
  - "Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters"
  - "With whom the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication"
  - "He carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness" — in contrast to the "mountain great and high" (Rev 21:10) where John will later be taken to see the _New Jerusalem_. Two cities, two destinations, two women — Rev sets up the contrast deliberately

- **The woman's appearance** (Rev 17:4-6)
  - "Arrayed in purple and scarlet colour, and decked with gold and precious stones and pearls"
  - "Having a golden cup in her hand full of abominations and filthiness of her fornication"
  - "Upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH"
  - "Drunken with the blood of the saints, and with the blood of the martyrs of Jesus"

- **Contrast with the Rev 12 woman**
  - Rev 12 woman: clothed with the sun (Christ's righteousness), moon under her feet (OT types fulfilled), crown of twelve stars (apostles and patriarchs)
  - Rev 17 woman: clothed in purple and scarlet (imperial and ecclesiastical colors), golden cup of abominations, forehead name of blasphemous mystery, drunken with saints' blood
  - One woman is the bride; one is the harlot. One speaks for Christ; one counterfeits Him

## Identifying Babylon

- **"Upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT"**
  - _Mystery_ — not hidden by accident but by deliberate design. Babylon operates behind a veil of religious language while carrying out its actual agenda
  - _Babylon_ — the OT city that destroyed Solomon's temple and carried God's people captive; Scripture's enduring symbol of anti-God imperial religion (Gen 11, Isa 47, Jer 50-51, Dan 1-5)
  - _Mother of Harlots_ — she is not the only harlot; she has _daughters_. The end-time system includes multiple religious entities that trace their character back to her
  - _Abominations of the earth_ — the worst of the world's religious corruption

- **Seven heads = seven mountains** (Rev 17:9-10)
  - "Here is the mind which hath wisdom. The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth"
  - Historical identification: Rome, the city built on seven hills. Roman writers before or near John's era — including Virgil (_Aeneid_ 6.782-783), Horace (_Carmen Saeculare_), and Pliny the Elder (_Natural History_ 3.66-67) — associated Rome with its seven heights; "the city on seven hills" was common currency in the 1st century
  - The woman _sits on_ the seven mountains — she is headquartered in Rome. Combined with Rev 13's sea beast identification (the papal power centered in Rome), the picture is consistent

- **"There are seven kings"** (Rev 17:10)
  - "Five are fallen, and one is, and the other is not yet come"
  - Historicist reading: the seven represent consecutive world-powers that have carried or will carry the Babylonian spirit — Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece are "fallen" when John writes; Rome "is" (the sixth); the "other" that "is not yet come" is the papal power that would follow imperial Rome. (Alternative interpretations identify seven Roman emperors or seven phases of Rome; the historicist reading fits the broader framework of Dan 2, Dan 7, and Rev 13)

- **"The beast... was, and is not, and yet is"** (Rev 17:8, 11)
  - The beast's wound-and-healing cycle, seen from Rev 13's angle
  - Historicist reading: the papal power _was_ (538-1798), _is not_ (the wound phase, 1798 onward), _and yet is_ (the healing and eventual return to persecuting authority)

- **Waters are peoples** (Rev 17:15)
  - "The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues"
  - Babylon's power rests on the populations who have received her wine. Her support is global. Her reach is cross-cultural

- **The ten horns turn on the woman** (Rev 17:16-17)
  - "The ten horns which thou sawest upon the beast, these shall hate the whore, and shall make her desolate and naked, and shall eat her flesh, and burn her with fire"
  - "God hath put in their hearts to fulfil his will, and to agree, and give their kingdom unto the beast, until the words of God shall be fulfilled"
  - The same political powers that supported Babylon finally turn against her in the final moments — a self-destructive coalition when judgment comes
  - Historicist parallel: like the ancient overthrow of Babylon by the Medo-Persians (Dan 5), the modern fall of Babylon happens through a coalition of powers turning on what they had once carried

- [→] Within the historicist framework, Babylon is identified coherently: a religious system, headquartered in Rome, global in reach, responsible for the persecution of saints, and ultimately destroyed by the very political powers that had supported her. The picture fits Rev 13's sea beast, Rev 14:8's fallen Babylon, and Dan 7's little horn — though Rev 17:9-11 remains one of Revelation's more contested passages, and historicist interpreters differ on some details of the seven-king sequence.

## Mother and Daughters

Rev 17:5 calls Babylon "the _mother_ of harlots." A mother implies daughters.

- **In the historicist/SDA application, the full Babylonian system in the final crisis**
  - **The mother** = the papal Roman Catholic system (Rev 13:1-10's sea beast)
  - **The daughters** = the religious bodies that share her essential character — specifically, apostate Protestantism that has retained her key error (Sunday observance received on her authority) and her methods (union with state power for enforcement)
  - **A third element** — spiritism / occult revival — Rev 16:13-14 will name three unclean spirits that proceed from the dragon, beast, and false prophet; the combined system in the end is trinitarian-false

- **Rev 13's earth beast connection**
  - Rev 13's two-horned lamb-beast "causeth the earth and them which dwell therein to worship the first beast" (Rev 13:12) and makes "an image to the beast"
  - The image is the _pattern_ — apostate Protestantism that reconstructs the mother's church-state enforcement model, and thereby becomes a "daughter" of the harlot
  - The combined system — mother + daughters + spiritism — is what the third angel's warning covers

- [→] Rev 18's call to "come out of her, my people" is not only a call out of the Roman Catholic Church. It is a call out of the whole Babylonian system — including Protestant bodies that have adopted the mother's authority pattern. Many faithful Christians remain inside these bodies; God calls them out before the final judgment falls.

## Rev 18 — The Fall

Rev 18 is the formal proclamation of Babylon's destruction.

- **The announcement** (Rev 18:1-3)
  - "And after these things I saw another angel come down from heaven, having great power; and the earth was lightened with his glory"
  - "He cried mightily with a strong voice, saying, Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit, and a cage of every unclean and hateful bird"
  - "For all nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication... and the merchants of the earth are waxed rich through the abundance of her delicacies"

- **The "fourth angel"?**
  - This angel is not numbered in the three-angels sequence of Rev 14, but historicist/SDA readers have often read Rev 18:1-3 as an amplification of the second angel's message, repeated with _additional_ power in the final crisis
  - "The earth was lightened with his glory" = the final proclamation carries unusual visibility and power; the gospel's last worldwide witness before probation closes
  - Some interpreters call this the "loud cry" — the final phase of the three angels' messages before the end

- **"Come out of her, my people"** (Rev 18:4)
  - "I heard another voice from heaven, saying, Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues"
  - The explicit acknowledgment that God has _people inside Babylon_ at the moment of her fall
  - The call is to come out _before_ the plagues. Not after. The call precedes the judgment
  - The historical pattern: OT Babylon's fall (Dan 5) was preceded by exhortations to God's exiled people to leave (Jer 50:8; 51:6, 45). The pattern repeats at the end

- **The sudden fall** (Rev 18:8-10)
  - "Therefore shall her plagues come in one day, death, and mourning, and famine; and she shall be utterly burned with fire"
  - "The kings of the earth... shall bewail her, and lament for her, when they shall see the smoke of her burning"
  - "Alas, alas, that great city Babylon, that mighty city! for in one hour is thy judgment come"
  - The fall is not a slow decline. When it comes, it is swift — "one day," "one hour"

- **The merchants weep** (Rev 18:11-19)
  - Rev 18 includes a long lament from the earth's merchants who had grown rich trading with Babylon. The list of their cargoes (Rev 18:12-13) ends with "slaves, and souls of men" — the final commodity of the system is human souls
  - The economic interests of the nations had become dependent on Babylon's commerce. Her fall is their economic catastrophe
  - Historicist reading: the global religious-political-economic system that has grown up around the Babylonian religious authority collapses together — the system is so intertwined that its religious fall also cracks its economic foundation

- **The martyrs' blood finally avenged** (Rev 18:20, 24)
  - "Rejoice over her, thou heaven, and ye holy apostles and prophets; for God hath avenged you on her"
  - "In her was found the blood of prophets, and of saints, and of all that were slain upon the earth"
  - The fifth seal martyrs' cry "How long?" (Rev 6:10) has now been fully answered
  - The judgment is not vengeance from the martyrs; it is God's vindication of them

- [→] Babylon's fall is swift, global, and economic as much as religious. The system that bought and sold human souls is bought out of existence in one hour. Heaven, apostles, prophets — the cast of the whole history — rejoice together.

## The Throw-the-Stone Picture (Rev 18:21)

- "A mighty angel took up a stone like a great millstone, and cast it into the sea, saying, Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all"
- The OT parallel: Jer 51:63-64 — Jeremiah wrote out the Babylon prophecy, tied a stone to it, and threw it into the Euphrates, saying "thus shall Babylon sink, and shall not rise"
- Rev 18:21 completes what Jer 51 foreshadowed: the final Babylon falls as the prototype did — suddenly, by God's judgment, never to rise again
- Six times in Rev 18 the phrase "no more at all" appears (vv. 14, 21, 22 [twice], 23 [twice]). The finality is emphatic: her music, her craftsmen, her light, her commerce — all gone forever

- `[⟲]` **Babylon thread:** Dan 2, 4-5 (literal Babylon) → Rev 14:8 (first fall-announcement) → Rev 16:19 (great Babylon comes into remembrance) → Rev 17-18 (full exposure and destruction) → Rev 19:1-3 (heaven's hallelujah at her fall). Rev 17-18 is the thread's exposure moment.

## Two Cities

Rev deliberately contrasts two cities through chapters 17-22:

| Babylon (Rev 17-18)                                  | New Jerusalem (Rev 21-22)                         |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Harlot                                               | Bride                                             |
| Drunken with saints' blood                           | Wipes every tear                                  |
| Purple, scarlet, precious stones, pearls — adornment | Precious stones, pearls — ornament (Rev 21:18-21) |
| Cup full of abominations                             | Pure river of water of life                       |
| Mother of harlots                                    | Bride, the Lamb's wife (Rev 21:9)                 |
| Babylon's name of mystery on her forehead (Rev 17:5) | God's name in her people's foreheads (Rev 22:4)   |
| "Found no more"                                      | "The former things are passed away"               |
| Destroyed in one hour                                | Stands forever                                    |
| Fornication with kings of the earth                  | Marriage with the Lamb                            |

The two cities are the two destinations. Every person is moving toward one of them. Rev 18:4's "Come out of her" is the pivot verse.

- [→] The next three studies (17-19) walk from the close of probation (Rev 15-16) through the Second Coming (Rev 19) and the millennium with its final judgment (Rev 20). By the end of Study 20 the book is ready to close with the two-city contrast fully resolved: Babylon destroyed, New Jerusalem descending.

## Key Takeaways

- Babylon is identified clearly in Rev 17: "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS" (v. 5), headquartered on seven mountains (v. 9 — Rome), global in reach, drunken with saints' blood
- The contrast with the Rev 12 woman is deliberate: two women, two cities, two destinations
- Babylon is not a single entity but a _system_: the mother (papal Rome), the daughters (apostate Protestantism that retains her pattern), and spiritism/occultism (Rev 16:13-14)
- The ten horns (Rev 17:16-17) ultimately turn against the harlot — a self-destructive political coalition
- Rev 18:1-3's mighty angel (so-called "fourth angel" in Adventist vocabulary, though not numbered in the text) with "great power" and glory lightening the earth is often identified with the final "loud cry" — the intensified version of the three angels' messages before probation closes
- Rev 18:4 — "Come out of her, my people" — is God's explicit acknowledgment that He has faithful children inside Babylon and His call to them before the plagues fall
- The fall is sudden: "in one day," "in one hour" (Rev 18:8, 10, 17, 19)
- The merchants' lament (Rev 18:11-19) ends with "slaves, and souls of men" — the final commodity of the system is human souls
- The martyrs' "how long?" of Rev 6:10 is finally answered in Rev 18:20, 24 — Babylon's destruction is God's vindication of His slain
- `[⟲]` Babylon thread: Rev 17-18 is the system's full exposure and destruction, the culmination of the biblical Babylon motif

## Next: Study 17 — Probation's Close and the Seven Last Plagues (Revelation 15-16)

Before Babylon falls visibly, there is an invisible event: probation closes. "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still" (Rev 22:11). The heavenly sanctuary is filled with the smoke of God's glory "so that no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled" (Rev 15:8). The High Priest stands alone; no intercessor remains between God and humanity; every case has been decided. Then the seven plagues begin: sores, blood, burning sun, darkness, Euphrates dried, Armageddon, the seventh bowl — "It is done." Study 17 is the most solemn chapter in Scripture.
