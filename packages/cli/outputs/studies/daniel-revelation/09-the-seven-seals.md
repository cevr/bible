# Study 9: The Seven Seals — Revelation 6-7

**The Lamb begins to break the seals, and history unfolds. Four horsemen ride in sequence — white, red, black, pale — tracing the spiritual condition of the church from the apostolic era through the long medieval decline. Under the fifth seal, martyrs cry "How long?" The sixth seal opens on signs in sun, moon, and stars that historicists identify with the Lisbon earthquake (1755), the Dark Day (1780), and the Leonid meteor storm (1833) — the very signs Jesus predicted in Matt 24. Then the scene pauses. Four angels hold back the winds. An angel ascending from the east commands them to wait "till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads." A hundred forty-four thousand are sealed. Then a great multitude no one can number appears before the throne. Study 9 opens Revelation's second recapitulation cycle — the same span as the seven churches, but this time through the lens of external history and the sealing of the remnant.**

## A Second Cycle, A New Angle

Rev 4-5 set the throne room. Rev 6 opens the first seal. The Lamb, having received the book (Rev 5:7), now administers history one seal at a time.

- **What the seals are**
  - A seven-part sequence covering the same historical span as the seven churches (Rev 1-3), from the apostolic era to the end
  - Where the letters showed the church's _internal spiritual condition_, the seals show the _external historical forces_ shaping the church: conquest, violence, famine, death, persecution, cosmic upheaval, silence
  - `[⟲]` Second recapitulation cycle in Revelation. Churches → Seals. Same history, new angle.

- **A pattern worth noticing** (Rev 6:1-2, 6:3, 6:5, 6:7)
  - The first four seals each open with a "voice" from one of the four living creatures saying "Come and see"
  - Each of the four creatures (Rev 4's lion/calf/man/eagle) announces one of the four horsemen (white/red/black/pale) in turn
  - Four-part unity: the first four seals form one unit (the horsemen); the last three (fifth seal, sixth seal, seventh seal) are separate in character
  - [→] 4 + 3 is Revelation's default cadence for its seven-part sequences (seven seals = 4+3; seven trumpets = 4+3; seven plagues = 4+3). The first four are a set; the last three develop differently.

## The Four Horsemen

The horseman imagery is drawn from Zech 1:7-11 and Zech 6:1-8, where God's horsemen patrol the earth. In Revelation, these are not enemy forces — they are agents the Lamb permits to ride, each representing a phase of the church's historical experience.

### The White Horse — Apostolic Victory (Rev 6:1-2)

- "A white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer"
- **White** = purity and victory; the rider wears a _stephanos_ (victor's crown)
- **Bow** = an ancient weapon of conquest, often associated with royal authority (Ps 45:4-5)
- Historicist reading: the apostolic era (~AD 31-100), the gospel going forth in purity and power. The "conquering" is evangelistic, not military. Historicists often connect this rider with Christ's gospel conquest; Rev 19:11 gives the unmistakable climactic white-horse Rider (sword, many diadems, explicit titles) — the first seal's rider foreshadows, Rev 19's rider completes
- [→] The first seal launches history with the gospel's initial victory. The church goes out strong. What happens next shows why.

### The Red Horse — Persecution (Rev 6:3-4)

- "There went out another horse that was red: and power was given to him that sat thereon to take peace from the earth, and that they should kill one another: and there was given unto him a great sword"
- **Red** = blood
- **Sword** = instrument of violence
- Historicist reading: the era of Roman imperial persecution (~AD 100-313). Ten waves of persecution drew Christian blood across two centuries
- This horse rides in the same era as Smyrna's "ten days" of tribulation (Rev 2:10)
- [→] The gospel's purity provokes resistance. The sword answers the bow.

### The Black Horse — Famine and Compromise (Rev 6:5-6)

- "A black horse; and he that sat on him had a pair of balances in his hand"
- "A measure of wheat for a penny, and three measures of barley for a penny; and see thou hurt not the oil and the wine"
- **Black** = mourning, scarcity, darkness
- **Balances** = weighing, rationing
- The grain prices (a day's wage for a day's food) indicate famine conditions, but oil and wine are protected
- Historicist reading: the era of the church's compromise with empire (~AD 313-538). After Constantine, the church gained wealth and political favor — but spiritual "famine" followed: the truth was weighed out carefully, rationed, while outward ceremony remained plentiful (historicists have read the protected oil and wine as preserved sacramental ritual or sometimes as the preserved Spirit/Word among the remnant — the general picture of rationed truth is stronger than any single sub-symbol)
- [→] The darkening is not primarily economic. It is doctrinal. Truth becomes scarce while religion stays well-fed.

### The Pale Horse — Spiritual Death (Rev 6:7-8)

- "A pale horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell followed with him"
- "Power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with sword, and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth"
- **Pale** (Greek _chlōros_) = a sickly yellow-green, the color of a corpse
- The rider is _named_ — the only named horseman — Death
- Historicist reading: the medieval era (~AD 538-1517). The long period when spiritual death reached its widest scope — the Dark Ages, the inquisitions, the suppression of Scripture, the mass spiritual malnutrition of most of Christendom. "Hell followed with him" (Greek _Hades_ = the grave) — what Death starts, the grave completes
- "The fourth part of the earth" — a fractional scope (not total destruction), within which Death's tools are sword, hunger, disease, and "the beasts of the earth"
- [→] With the pale horse, the first-four set closes. The cycle has walked from apostolic purity to medieval death. Same ground the seven churches traced (Ephesus → Thyatira), from a different angle.

## The Fifth Seal — The Martyrs' Cry

With the four horsemen, the scene shifts entirely.

- **The scene** (Rev 6:9-11)
  - "I saw under the altar the souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held"
  - "They cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?"
  - "White robes were given unto every one of them; and it was said unto them, that they should rest yet for a little season, until their fellowservants also and their brethren, that should be killed as they were, should be fulfilled"

- **"Under the altar"**
  - The brazen altar in the earthly sanctuary was where sacrifices were killed. The blood of the sacrifice ran down and was collected under the altar (Lev 4:7)
  - The martyrs "under the altar" = those whose blood has been poured out, like the sacrificial blood, for God's word and testimony
  - Their prayer is not for personal vengeance. It is for _vindication_ — the universe waiting for God to publicly judge the powers that shed righteous blood

- **"How long?"** — the same question Daniel's heavenly beings asked in Dan 8:13, and the same question the saints will echo in Rev 16:5-7 as the plagues begin
- **"Rest yet for a little season"** — the answer is not "now." The rest of the prophetic cycle has to complete. More martyrs are coming (the Reformation-era persecutions, the final-crisis martyrs of Rev 13:15)
- `[→]` The martyrs are given white robes _now_ (Rev 6:11) but vindication _later_. The verdict is secured; the sentence is pending. This is what the investigative judgment will eventually confirm (Study 3's 1844 and Dan 7's courtroom) — the fifth seal is the martyrs' side of the judgment question, paired with the `[⟲]` Judgment thread running through the throne-room scenes (Dan 7 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12)

[DYK🔎] The martyrs' location "under the altar" matches Lev 4:7's instruction for the sin offering: "The priest shall pour all the blood of the bullock at the bottom of the altar." Their bodies are not literally stored beneath a heavenly altar — the image is sacrificial: their lives were poured out as an offering, and God has registered every drop. Paul uses the same image of himself in Phil 2:17 ("if I be offered upon the sacrifice and service of your faith") and 2 Tim 4:6 ("I am now ready to be offered"). Martyrdom, in Revelation, is not defeat. It is priestly offering.

## The Sixth Seal — The Signs of the End

The fifth seal ended with "rest yet for a little season." The sixth seal breaks that rest with cosmic signs.

- **The signs** (Rev 6:12-14)
  - "A great earthquake"
  - "The sun became black as sackcloth of hair"
  - "The moon became as blood"
  - "The stars of heaven fell unto the earth, even as a fig tree casteth her untimely figs"
  - "The heaven departed as a scroll when it is rolled together"
  - "Every mountain and island were moved out of their places"

- **The Jesus-and-prophet precedent**
  - "Immediately after the tribulation of those days shall the sun be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven" (Matt 24:29)
  - "The sun shall be turned into darkness, and the moon into blood, before the great and the terrible day of the LORD come" (Joel 2:31)
  - Jesus gave the same sign list, placing it _after_ "the tribulation of those days" (the long medieval persecution) and _before_ His coming

- **Historicist anchors** — signs that historicists tie to the sixth seal, in sequence
  - **November 1, 1755 — the Lisbon earthquake** — estimated magnitude 8.5-9.0; one of Europe's most destructive earthquakes and tsunamis; tens of thousands killed; felt across Western Europe and North Africa; the shock and the later tsunami reshaped Enlightenment Europe's conversation about Providence and natural evil
  - **May 19, 1780 — the Dark Day of New England** — an unusual midday darkness over much of the northeastern American colonies; darkness lasted from late morning into the night, strong enough that birds went to roost and candles were needed indoors; modern dendrochronology traces the physical cause to extensive Canadian wildfire smoke combined with heavy fog, but contemporary observers (and historicist preachers since) read it as a providentially significant sign answering Matt 24:29
  - **The following night, May 19-20, 1780 — the moon "as blood"** — the moon appeared bloody-red through the lingering smoke and haze
  - **November 13, 1833 — the great Leonid meteor storm** — one of the most spectacular meteor storms ever recorded over North America; observers described stars "falling like snow," with rates estimated in the tens of thousands per hour visible across the eastern United States; contemporary accounts, religious and secular, described the scene in biblical language
  - [→] The sequence of dates (1755, 1780, 1833) falls within Rev's era of Philadelphia (the revival/Advent-awakening church, Study 7). Lisbon and the Dark Day preceded William Miller's public preaching (which began in 1831); the 1833 meteor storm fell _within_ the Advent awakening window and became the single most cited sign in early Millerite preaching. Whether or not one accepts the specific historicist dating, the fact that these three events occurred in this sequence, within ~80 years, in a form answering Matt 24's list, is striking

- **Earthquakes, mountains moved, sky rolled back**
  - "The heaven departed as a scroll" (Rev 6:14) — this element of the sixth seal is _not_ yet fulfilled; it describes the final visible appearing of Christ (cf. 2 Pet 3:10). The earlier signs (1755, 1780, 1833) are historicist anchors for the _beginning_ of the sixth seal; the rest (scroll-sky, every mountain moved, final terror) awaits the actual Second Coming
  - Historicist reading: the sixth seal is the longest of the seven. It opens with the 18th-19th century signs, continues across the Advent awakening era, and closes at the Second Coming itself. We are living inside the sixth seal

- **The terror of the lost** (Rev 6:15-17)
  - "The kings of the earth, and the great men, and the rich men, and the chief captains, and the mighty men, and every bondman, and every free man, hid themselves in the dens and in the rocks of the mountains"
  - "Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne, and from the wrath of the Lamb"
  - "The great day of his wrath is come; and who shall be able to stand?"
  - The sixth seal ends with the most universal terror in Scripture — every social class, at the unveiling of the Lamb's face. The question "who shall be able to stand?" is the setup for chapter 7's answer

## The Interlude — Sealing Before the Winds

Between the sixth seal and the seventh, Rev inserts an interlude. The winds are held back. The servants of God are sealed.

- **Four angels holding the four winds** (Rev 7:1-3)
  - "Four angels standing on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds of the earth, that the wind should not blow on the earth, nor on the sea, nor on any tree"
  - Wind in Scripture = strife, war, destructive force (Jer 25:32-33; 49:36; Dan 7:2 showed the four winds striving on the great sea, bringing the four kingdoms up)
  - The angels are _restraining_ the final storm

- **The sealing angel** (Rev 7:2-3)
  - "Another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God"
  - "From the east" — the direction of sunrise; Christ coming (Mal 4:2); the glory (Eze 43:2)
  - "Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads"
  - The sealing must complete before the winds are released

- **Ezekiel's precedent** (Eze 9:1-6)
  - Before Jerusalem's destruction, God sent a messenger with an "inkhorn" to "set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations" (Eze 9:4)
  - The destroying angels followed, with the command: "Come not near any man upon whom is the mark" (Eze 9:6)
  - Same structural sequence: sealing first, judgment second

- **What is the seal?**
  - "The seal of the living God" (Rev 7:2)
  - Placed in the forehead — the seat of reason and decision. (Eze 9's mark is also forehead-only; the hand/forehead contrast enters later with Rev 13:16's beast-mark, not here)
  - Historicist reading: the seal of God is a settled relationship with God, evidenced outwardly by Sabbath observance (the fourth commandment uniquely contains God's name, title, and territory — the three elements of a seal). This is the setup for Study 15's Mark-and-Seal study; we will develop the argument there
  - `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread:** Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. Rev 7 is link two.

- **The 144,000**
  - "An hundred and forty and four thousand of all the tribes of the children of Israel" (Rev 7:4)
  - 12 × 12 × 1000 — the symbolic fullness of God's people. 12 = tribes/apostles (Rev 21:12-14); squared = totality; times 1000 = scale
  - Tribal list in Rev 7:5-8: Judah is named first (not Reuben), Dan is absent, Ephraim is absent, Levi is included, and both Joseph and Manasseh appear. The list is deliberately non-standard — no OT tribal list looks like this. The irregularities mark the 144,000 as a spiritual, not genealogical, Israel: "all the tribes of the children of Israel" stands for the full remnant of the covenant-keeping people in the final crisis, headed by Judah (from whom the Lion/Lamb comes, Rev 5:5)
  - The number is often understood as symbolic of the sealed remnant at the close of probation — a specific, named, knowable group who have taken the final stand

[Q] **"Are the 144,000 literal or symbolic? Literal Jews, symbolic remnant, or representative firstfruits?"**
→ Three readings are held across Christian history:
(a) _Literal ethnic Jewish_: a literal 144,000 from genealogical Israel, sealed in an end-time Jewish revival. Dispensational reading. Textual cost: Rev 7's tribal list is non-standard (Dan omitted, Joseph replacing Ephraim, Judah first) — an unlikely list for literal genealogical purposes
(b) _Symbolic remnant_: the 144,000 = the full number of God's end-time faithful, with the number itself symbolic (12×12×1000 = covenant completeness × scale). The 144,000 and the great multitude are the same group, viewed from two angles: sealed on earth (7:1-8), then glorified in heaven (7:9-17)
(c) _Representative firstfruits_: the 144,000 is a specific group of end-time saints who stand through the final crisis, and the great multitude is the wider circle of all the redeemed of every age. The 144,000 are described as "firstfruits unto God" in Rev 14:4
Historicist/SDA reading usually favors some combination of (b) and (c): the composition is spiritual Israel (not genealogical), the number communicates completeness rather than a headcount ceiling, and the 144,000 plausibly refers to the last-generation remnant who live through the winds about to blow (Rev 7:3). The great multitude (7:9ff.) names the wider circle. Options (b) and (c) differ mainly on whether the two groups are identical or nested. Either way, the sealing in the forehead (7:3) is the defining mark, and the contrast is with those who bear the beast's mark (Rev 13:16 — the contrast we develop in Study 15).

## The Great Multitude

After the 144,000 are sealed, John sees what his vision has been building toward.

- **The scene** (Rev 7:9-10)
  - "A great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations, and kindreds, and people, and tongues, stood before the throne, and before the Lamb"
  - "Clothed with white robes, and palms in their hands"
  - "Salvation to our God which sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb"

- **Who are they?** (Rev 7:13-14)
  - "These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb"
  - "Great tribulation" = the full suffering of God's people across history (not only one end-time episode)
  - _Washed their robes in the blood of the Lamb_ — the paradox that only Christianity dares: cleanness from blood. The Lamb's sacrifice, not their own, is what makes their robes white

- **Their future** (Rev 7:15-17)
  - "Serve him day and night in his temple" — priestly service in the heavenly sanctuary continues in glory
  - "Shall hunger no more, neither thirst any more; neither shall the sun light on them, nor any heat" — the deprivations of the great tribulation reversed
  - "The Lamb which is in the midst of the throne shall feed them, and shall lead them unto living fountains of waters" — the Shepherd image (Ps 23; John 10), with the Lamb Himself as shepherd. Lamb _and_ Shepherd. The paradox deepens.
  - "God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes" — the first mention of this promise in Revelation; it will be repeated at Rev 21:4

- [→] The sixth seal's question ("who shall be able to stand?") is answered in Rev 7. _These_ stand. The sealed and the redeemed multitude — named by tribe and unnumbered from every nation — stand before the Lamb. The seventh seal (Rev 8:1) follows: silence in heaven for half an hour. Heaven pauses. The reader is meant to pause too.

## Key Takeaways

- The seven seals are Revelation's _second_ recapitulation cycle (after the churches), covering the same historical span through the lens of external forces on the church
- The first four seals follow a 4+3 pattern: four horsemen announced by the four living creatures, then three distinct seals
- **White horse** = apostolic purity and gospel victory; **Red** = persecution era; **Black** = era of doctrinal compromise under imperial church; **Pale (chlōros)** = medieval spiritual death
- **Fifth seal** = martyrs' cry "How long?" — vindication is secured (white robes given) but delayed (rest yet a little season); the martyrs' side of the judgment question the `[⟲]` Judgment thread will answer in Rev 11, 14, and 20
- **Sixth seal** = cosmic signs announcing Christ's coming; historicists identify Lisbon (1755), Dark Day (1780), and the Leonid meteor storm (1833) as the opening signs; we live inside the sixth seal until the Second Coming
- **Interlude (Rev 7:1-8)** = four angels hold the winds until the sealing is complete; parallels Eze 9's mark-before-judgment
- The seal of God is placed in the _forehead_ (reason), not the hand (Study 15 will detail the mark/seal contrast)
- **The 144,000** = the sealed end-time remnant standing at the close of probation; spiritual Israel, drawn from every nation
- **The great multitude** = the full redeemed of every age, out of "great tribulation," robes washed in the Lamb's blood
- `[⟲]` Remnant/seal thread: Rev 7 is link two (after Dan 12:1)

The seventh seal opens with silence — then seven angels are given seven trumpets. Trumpets in Scripture announce battle, convocation, and judgment.
