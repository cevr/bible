# Revelation 16 — The Seven Vials of Wrath

**Rev 15 closed with the temple filled with smoke, no man able to enter, and the seven angels equipped with the seven golden vials. Rev 16 opens with "a great voice out of the temple" — the only voice still speaking, since probation has closed — saying "Go your ways, and pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth." Twenty-one verses describe the seven plagues that follow: noisome sores on those who took the mark; the sea turned to blood; rivers and fountains turned to blood with the altar declaring "true and righteous are thy judgments"; the sun scorching men who blaspheme and repent not; darkness on the beast's throne; the Euphrates dried up to prepare the way of the kings of the east, while three frog-like spirits gather the world to Armageddon; and finally the seventh vial poured into the air with the great voice from the throne saying "It is done." Cities fall, islands flee, mountains are not found, and a hail of talent-weight stones falls on men. Rev 16 is the chapter of God's "filled-up" wrath actually pouring out — the answer to the fifth-seal cry, the antitype of the Egyptian plagues, and the final unmasking of Babylon before her ruin in Rev 17-18.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 16 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 15 (the preparation — vials handed off, temple closed)
- Exodus 7-12 (the ten plagues of Egypt — the pattern for Rev 16)
- Exodus 9:8-11 (boils — pattern for first plague)
- Exodus 7:14-25 (Nile turned to blood — pattern for second/third plagues)
- Exodus 10:21-23 (darkness over Egypt — pattern for fifth plague)
- Exodus 9:13-35 (hail of fire — pattern for seventh plague)
- Isaiah 44:27-45:1; Jeremiah 50:38; 51:36 (the dry-Euphrates / Cyrus pattern for the sixth plague)
- Joshua 5:13-15; 6 (Jericho's fall after the camp marched in silence — Armageddon's antitype)

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 16 Sits

Rev 15:5-8 opened the temple of testimony, equipped the seven angels, handed off the seven vials, filled the temple with smoke, and closed the door. **Rev 16:1 picks up immediately:** the great voice from the closed temple commands the angels to pour. **The whole chapter is the seven vials in sequence.** Then Rev 17-18 will detail Babylon's destruction in close-up, Rev 19 will bring the white-horse Rider, and Rev 20 will deal with Satan and the millennium. Rev 16 is the answer to "how long?" (Rev 6:10), to "in them is filled up the wrath of God" (Rev 15:1), and to every prayer-bowl of Rev 5:8 and Rev 8:3-5.

- **The chapter has two halves and a center.** Plagues 1-4 fall on natural elements (earth, sea, rivers, sun). Plagues 5-7 fall on the beast's political-religious order (his throne, his river, his city). The midpoint (vv. 5-7) inserts heaven's verdict: "Thou art righteous, O Lord... true and righteous are thy judgments."
- **The pattern is Exodus.** Each plague has an Egyptian-plague antitype (sores ↔ Ex 9:8-11; sea-blood ↔ Ex 7:14-25; rivers-blood ↔ same; scorching sun ↔ Ex 10:21-23 darkness inverted; darkness ↔ Ex 10:21-23; Euphrates ↔ Red Sea / Jordan dry-passage type; hail ↔ Ex 9:13-35). **The exodus typology was established in Rev 15 with the song of Moses; Rev 16 makes it explicit.**
- **The plagues are not redemptive.** Two explicit "repented not" notices appear in vv. 9 and 11; v. 21 gives the third impenitence notice as blasphemy under hail. **The plagues did not change a single character; they revealed every character.** Probation closed in Rev 15:8; Rev 22:11's character-fixing decree is in force. Those who hated God before still hate Him; those who loved Him before still love Him.
- **Armageddon is the chapter's climax-before-climax.** Rev 16:13-16 names the gathering — three frog-spirits from dragon, beast, and false prophet — to "the battle of that great day of God Almighty." The seventh plague (vv. 17-21) then resolves it: "It is done." Rev 19's white-horse Rider executes the resolution.
- **"It is done" (v. 17) deliberately echoes _tetelestai_ at the cross.** Different verb (Greek _gegonen_ — "it has happened/come to pass"), same finality. **At the cross redemption was finished; at the seventh plague judgment is finished.** Two endings; one God; one consistent character.

[KEY🔑] **Rev 16 is the chapter where the wrath of God — gathering since Eden, deliberated through the investigative judgment of Dan 7:9-10 / Rev 14:7, "filled up" in Rev 15:1 — actually pours out.** The plagues are not arbitrary; they are God's righteous answer to (a) the martyrs' "how long?" cry of Rev 6:10, (b) the sealed remnant's deliverance from the closing crisis, and (c) Babylon's two-thousand-year cup of fornication and blood. The chapter is structured to vindicate God's character mid-stream: at the third plague the altar speaks ("true and righteous are thy judgments"). **By Rev 16:21, the universe is silenced. Babylon falls. Christ comes.**

## Rev 16:1 — Pour Out the Vials

> "And I heard a great voice out of the temple saying to the seven angels, Go your ways, and pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth." (Rev 16:1)

- "**A great voice out of the temple**" — Greek _phōnēs megalēs ek tou naou_. **The temple John is hearing from is the temple Rev 15:8 just closed.** No man can enter; one voice still speaks. **It is the voice of the One whose mediation has finished — Christ as Judge, no longer as High Priest interceding.**
- "**Go your ways**" — Greek _hypagete_. The same word Jesus used in Mt 8:13 ("Go thy way; and as thou hast believed, so be it done unto thee") and Mt 26:18. **A simple imperative of dispatch.** The angels do not deliberate; they execute.
- "**Pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth**" — Greek _ekcheate tas hepta phialas tou thymou tou theou eis tēn gēn_. **_Phialē_** — bowl, the same vessel as Rev 5:8's prayer-bowls of saints' prayers. **The bowls that held the saints' prayers now hold the answering wrath.** Rev 8:3-5's golden censer of prayers became fire cast to earth introducing the trumpets; Rev 16:1's bowls of wrath become plagues poured on earth introducing the end.
- **One voice; seven angels; seven vials; one earth.** The whole earth-system — natural and political — is the target.

[⟲] **The prayer-bowl-becomes-wrath-bowl thread runs the whole book.** Rev 5:8 (golden vials full of odours, the prayers of saints). Rev 6:10 (the fifth-seal martyrs cry "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood?"). Rev 8:3-5 (the angel offers incense with the prayers of saints; takes the censer, fills it with fire from the altar, casts it to earth — voices, thunderings, lightnings, earthquake). Rev 15:7 (one of the four living creatures gives the seven golden vials full of the wrath of God to the seven angels). **Rev 16:1 — pour them out.** The vessels are the same; the contents have changed phase: the saints' prayers, which were _odours_ in Rev 5:8, are now _wrath_ poured out in Rev 16:1. **Heaven does not have two bowl-supplies — one for prayers and one for judgment.** The same prayers that asked "how long?" are the prayers that answer "now."

## Rev 16:2 — First Vial: Noisome Sores

> "And the first went, and poured out his vial upon the earth; and there fell a noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast, and upon them which worshipped his image." (Rev 16:2)

- "**A noisome and grievous sore**" — Greek _helkos kakon kai ponēron_. **_Helkos_** — ulcer, festering wound (LXX uses it for the boils of Ex 9:9-11 on the magicians and Egyptians). **A literal echo of the sixth Egyptian plague.** The boils in Egypt fell on every man, but specifically humiliated the magicians who could not stand before Moses. The Rev 16:2 sore falls specifically on those with the mark.
- "**Upon the men which had the mark of the beast**" — Greek _epi tous anthrōpous tous echontas to charagma tou thēriou_. **The plague is targeted.** It does not fall indiscriminately. **The sealed are not afflicted; the marked are.** This is the structural counterpart to Eze 9:6 ("come not near any man upon whom is the mark") and Ex 12:13 ("when I see the blood, I will pass over you").
- "**And them which worshipped his image**" — the same two-marked group named by Rev 13:15 (the image), Rev 13:16-17 (the mark), Rev 14:9-11 (the third angel's warning). **The third angel warned that whoever takes the mark "shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God"** (Rev 14:10); Rev 16:2 is that wine beginning to be poured.

[CTRF] **The mark of the beast versus the seal of God — first targeted-plague pair.** Rev 13:16-17 stamps the mark in the forehead or right hand; Rev 7:3 / Rev 14:1 seals the 144,000 in the foreheads with the Father's name. Rev 16:2 makes the distinction visible at the level of the body itself: those with the mark develop a festering sore; those with the seal do not. **The chapter that began the mark-versus-seal dichotomy (Rev 13) is now answered by the chapter that exposes its physical cost (Rev 16).** The sore on the marked is not cosmetic; it is a public, painful inscription on the same flesh that took the mark. **The choice was internal in Rev 13; it becomes external in Rev 16:2.**

## Rev 16:3 — Second Vial: Sea Becomes Blood

> "And the second angel poured out his vial upon the sea; and it became as the blood of a dead man: and every living soul died in the sea." (Rev 16:3)

- "**As the blood of a dead man**" — Greek _hōs nekrou_. **Not flowing blood — putrefying blood.** The blood of a corpse, congealed and stinking. **Worse than Ex 7:20-21's Nile-turned-blood**, where fish died and stank. The intensification is structural: Egyptian plagues = partial; Revelation plagues = complete.
- "**Every living soul died in the sea**" — Greek _pasa psychē zōēs apethanen_. **Not a localized sea — the whole sea-domain;** every living soul in the sea dies. **The dragon's recruiting-pool dies** (Rev 13:1's sea-beast came up out of this sea). The fourth trumpet (Rev 8:8-9) had killed a third of sea life; the second vial finishes what the trumpet began.

## Rev 16:4-7 — Third Vial: Rivers and Fountains, and the Altar's Verdict

> "And the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood. And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus. For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy. And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments." (Rev 16:4-7)

- "**Rivers and fountains of waters**" — drinking water specifically. **Sea was already dead from v. 3; now the freshwater supply turns to blood.** No water to drink. **Compare Rev 8:10-11 (Wormwood):** the third trumpet had embittered a third of the rivers; the third vial finishes the work.
- "**The angel of the waters**" — Greek _tou angelou tōn hydatōn_. **An angel with stewardship over water itself**, declaring the verdict on the plague that affects his domain. **Heaven gives no plague without heaven's own witnesses standing up to declare it just.**
- "**Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus**" — the great-controversy formula. The very angel whose realm was just turned to blood declares God _righteous_ for doing it. **No angelic creature accuses God of cruelty; every angelic creature vindicates God's judgment.**
- "**They have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy**" — the lex talionis. **The same lips that drank the cup of Babylon's fornication (Rev 17:2, 4) and that made her drunken with the blood of saints (Rev 17:6) now drink blood as their water.** Babylon shed blood; Babylon receives blood.
- "**Another out of the altar say**" — Greek _allou ek tou thysiastēriou_. **The altar itself speaks** — the altar under which the fifth-seal martyrs cried "how long?" (Rev 6:9). **Their cry is now answered;** the altar acknowledges the answer. **"Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments."**

[⟲] **The fifth-seal martyrs' cry comes to its answer in Rev 16:7.** Rev 6:9-11 (the souls under the altar — "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?"). Rev 8:3-5 (the censer of prayers thrown to earth as fire — the trumpets begin). Rev 14:18 (the angel from the altar gives the order for the vintage harvest). **Rev 16:7 — the altar speaks: "true and righteous are thy judgments."** The altar that received the martyrs' blood now declares the verdict on those who shed it. **The altar's voice closes the loop the fifth seal opened.**

## Rev 16:8-9 — Fourth Vial: Scorching Sun

> "And the fourth angel poured out his vial upon the sun; and power was given unto him to scorch men with fire. And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory." (Rev 16:8-9)

- "**Power was given unto him to scorch men with fire**" — Greek _kaumatisai tous anthrōpous en pyri_. **The sun's life-giving function inverted into a death-giving one.** Compare Mal 4:1 — "the day cometh, that shall burn as an oven" — and Mal 4:2's contrasting "Sun of righteousness... with healing in his wings." **The unsealed get the burning sun; the sealed get the healing Sun.**
- "**Blasphemed the name of God**" — they know whose plague it is. **Notice: they recognize "the name of God, which hath power over these plagues."** The plagues do not produce confusion about authorship. They produce blasphemy. **The unrepentant heart, given undeniable evidence of God's power, blasphemes rather than bows.**
- "**They repented not to give him glory**" — the first of two explicit "repented not" notices (here and v. 11), with v. 21 completing the impenitence pattern by blasphemy under hail. **The first angel of Rev 14:7 had said "Fear God, and give glory to him."** The unsealed of Rev 16:9 will not give Him glory. **The plagues confirm the verdict probation already fixed.**

## Rev 16:10-11 — Fifth Vial: Darkness on the Beast's Throne

> "And the fifth angel poured out his vial upon the seat of the beast; and his kingdom was full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain, and blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores, and repented not of their deeds." (Rev 16:10-11)

- "**Upon the seat of the beast**" — Greek _epi ton thronon tou thēriou_. **The throne**. The fifth vial does not fall on a place or a people; it falls on the beast's seat of authority. Rev 13:2 said the dragon gave the sea-beast his "seat" (_thronon_). **Now that throne goes dark.**
- "**His kingdom was full of darkness**" — direct echo of Ex 10:21-23, the ninth Egyptian plague. **In Egypt the darkness fell on Egypt while the children of Israel had light in their dwellings.** The structural inversion holds: the unsealed grope in darkness; the sealed walk in light. **Babylon's whole religio-political system loses its light source.**
- "**They gnawed their tongues for pain**" — Greek _emasōnto tas glōssas autōn ek tou ponou_. **The tongues that blasphemed in v. 9 now gnaw themselves in pain.** The instrument of blasphemy becomes the seat of suffering. **Yet still, "blasphemed the God of heaven... and repented not."** Pain does not produce repentance; only the Spirit does, and the Spirit is no longer striving (cf. Gen 6:3).

## Rev 16:12 — Sixth Vial: The Euphrates Dried Up

> "And the sixth angel poured out his vial upon the great river Euphrates; and the water thereof was dried up, that the way of the kings of the east might be prepared." (Rev 16:12)

- "**The great river Euphrates**" — same river as Rev 9:14 (sixth trumpet — the four angels bound at the great river Euphrates loosed). **In the OT, the Euphrates is the eastern boundary of the promised land (Gen 15:18) and the river of Babylon.** Babylon literally sat on the Euphrates; her city was watered by it.
- "**The water thereof was dried up**" — Greek _exēranthē to hydōr autou_. **Historical pattern:** Cyrus the Persian took Babylon in 539 BC by diverting the Euphrates and walking under the gates (Dan 5; Isa 44:27-45:1; Jer 50:38; 51:36). **Symbolic interpretation (Rev 17:15):** "the waters... are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." **The drying-up of Babylon's river = the withdrawal of her popular support.** Just before her ruin, the multitudes that supported her abandon her.
- "**The kings of the east**" — Greek _hoi basileis hoi apo anatolēs hēliou_, "the kings from the rising of the sun." **In Cyrus's time, "from the east" was Cyrus's Persian armies coming to break Babylon (Isa 46:11 calls Cyrus a "ravenous bird from the east").** In Revelation's symbolic system, the sealing angel comes "from the east" (Rev 7:2), the Sun of Righteousness rises in the east (Mal 4:2), and Christ's return is from the east (Mt 24:27). **The "kings of the east" are the Christ-led forces of deliverance,** for whom Babylon's collapsing supporters clear the way.

[DYK🔎] **The Cyrus-Babylon parallel is the structural backbone of the sixth vial.** In 539 BC, Belshazzar's Babylon fell to Cyrus the Persian in one night. Belshazzar had committed sacrilege against the temple vessels (Dan 5:1-4). The handwriting on the wall — _MENE MENE TEKEL UPHARSIN_ — declared his kingdom numbered, weighed, and divided (Dan 5:25-28). That same night, Cyrus's army diverted the Euphrates upstream so the river-bed at Babylon ran low; his troops walked under the city's water-gates and took Babylon while Belshazzar feasted. Isaiah, writing nearly two centuries earlier, had named Cyrus by name (Isa 44:28; 45:1) and said the LORD would "dry up thy rivers" (Isa 44:27). Jeremiah said her waters would be "dried up" (Jer 50:38; 51:36). **Rev 16:12 deliberately reuses every element of that historical fall as the antitype:** Babylon (now Rev 17's "Mystery, Babylon the great"), her river dried up (her popular support withdrawn), the kings from the east (Christ's deliverance-forces) entering. **Cyrus was a type of Christ; the historical fall of Babylon is the rehearsal for Rev 17-18's final fall.** The first-coming-of-Cyrus pattern (deliverer from the east) becomes the second-coming-of-Christ pattern (deliverer from the east). The Euphrates does not need to dry up literally; the political-religious river of Babylon's support has to dry up. By the seventh vial it has.

## Rev 16:13-14 — Three Frog-Spirits Gathering to Armageddon

> "And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet. For they are the spirits of devils, working miracles, which go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the whole world, to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty." (Rev 16:13-14)

- "**Three unclean spirits like frogs**" — Greek _pneumata tria akatharta hōs batrachoi_. **Frogs are unclean (Lev 11:10) and were the second Egyptian plague (Ex 8:1-15).** In Egypt the frogs invaded the houses, ovens, and bedchambers — total infestation. **Pharaoh's magicians could produce frogs but could not stop them.** The frog-spirits of Rev 16:13 carry the same sense: counterfeit power that infests but cannot stop the true plagues.
- "**Out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet**" — Greek _ek tou stomatos_, three times. **The unholy trinity speaks the lie.** Rev 12:9 named the dragon (Satan); Rev 13:1-10 the sea-beast (papal Rome); Rev 13:11-17 the earth-beast / false prophet (apostate Protestantism allied with civil power). **Their three mouths speak one deception** — gathered now into a single end-time campaign.
- "**Working miracles**" — Greek _poiounta sēmeia_, "doing signs." **Same word as Rev 13:13's earth-beast that "doeth great wonders."** The signs of the unholy trinity are not metaphor; they are literal supernatural manifestations designed to deceive the world's leaders. **Mt 24:24's "false Christs and false prophets, and shall shew great signs and wonders" reaches its fulfillment here.**
- "**To gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty**" — Greek _eis ton polemon tēs hēmeras ekeinēs tēs megalēs tou theou tou pantokratoros_. **Note: the day belongs to God, but the gathering is the unholy trinity's.** They mobilize the world's powers; they cannot mobilize the day. **They march to a battle whose date and outcome they did not set.**

[CTRF] **The unholy trinity versus the Holy Trinity — the eleventh and final counterfeit pair.** The Father is paralleled by the dragon, the Son by the sea-beast (whose authority is given by the dragon, Rev 13:2, just as the Son's is given by the Father, Jn 5:22-23), and the Spirit by the earth-beast / false prophet, who directs worship to the beast (Rev 13:12) just as the Spirit directs worship to Christ (Jn 15:26). **Rev 16:13 is the decisive gathering scene where dragon, beast, and false prophet are named together as the counterfeit trinity** (cf. Rev 20:10 where the same three meet their final judgment). Three unholy mouths; three frog-spirits; one campaign of deception. The holy Trinity's final mission is to gather a redeemed people to Christ; the unholy trinity's final mission is to gather a deluded world to Armageddon. **The chapter that began the parody (Rev 13) names its counterfeit-trinity climax here.**

## Rev 16:15 — Behold, I Come as a Thief

> "Behold, I come as a thief. Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments, lest he walk naked, and they see his shame." (Rev 16:15)

- **An interruption.** The narrative had been "the unholy trinity gathers the kings of the earth to Armageddon." Suddenly Christ's voice cuts in. **The third of Revelation's seven beatitudes** (Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14). **Inserted between v. 14's gathering and v. 16's name "Armageddon."**
- "**I come as a thief**" — Greek _erchomai hōs kleptēs_. **Same image as Mt 24:43, 1 Th 5:2, 2 Pet 3:10.** Christ's coming during the closing crisis is unexpected to the gathered world. **They are still mobilizing for battle when He arrives.**
- "**Keepeth his garments**" — Greek _tērōn ta himatia autou_. **Compare Rev 3:18 (Laodicea's "white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed") and Rev 19:8 ("the fine linen is the righteousness of saints").** The garments are character — Christ's righteousness imputed and imparted. **To "keep" them is to maintain unbroken faith through the closing crisis.**
- "**Lest he walk naked, and they see his shame**" — naked = unprepared, character exposed (cf. Adam and Eve's nakedness after sin, Gen 3:7). **The closing-crisis pressure will expose every false profession; only those clothed in Christ's righteousness will stand.**

## Rev 16:16 — Armageddon

> "And he gathered them together into a place called in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon." (Rev 16:16)

- "**In the Hebrew tongue Armageddon**" — Greek _Harmagedōn_, transliterating Hebrew _har-Megiddo_, "Mount Megiddo." **Megiddo is the strategic pass guarding the Jezreel Valley** — the site of decisive OT battles: Deborah and Barak vs. Sisera (Judg 4-5), Gideon vs. Midian (Judg 7), Saul's death at Gilboa near the same plain (1 Sam 31), Ahaziah's death (2 Kgs 9:27), and Josiah's death (2 Kgs 23:29-30). **The valley became Hebrew shorthand for decisive covenant-history conflict.**
- **There is no literal "Mount Megiddo."** Megiddo is a tell on a plain. **The "mount" is symbolic** — combining the Megiddo tradition of decisive battle with the OT's "mountain of the LORD" (Mt. Zion). **Armageddon is the final cosmic confrontation between the Lamb and the dragon; not a Middle-Eastern regional war.**
- **The unholy trinity does the gathering; the place is named in Hebrew.** John signals: Armageddon is on the ground of God's covenant history. **The dragon's coalition has marched onto God's chosen battleground;** the outcome will be Rev 19's Faithful and True on the white horse.

## Rev 16:17-21 — Seventh Vial: It Is Done

> "And the seventh angel poured out his vial into the air; and there came a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done. And there were voices, and thunders, and lightnings; and there was a great earthquake, such as was not since men were upon the earth, so mighty an earthquake, and so great. And the great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell: and great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath. And every island fled away, and the mountains were not found. And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, every stone about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great." (Rev 16:17-21)

- "**Into the air**" — Greek _eis ton aera_. **The first six vials were poured on earth, sea, rivers, sun, throne, and Euphrates — concrete domains.** The seventh is poured on the air itself. **The atmosphere of the great controversy.** Eph 2:2 calls Satan "the prince of the power of the air." **The seventh vial unseats him from his last domain.**
- "**It is done**" — Greek _gegonen_. **A different verb from the cross's _tetelestai_ (Jn 19:30) but the same finality.** _Tetelestai_ = "it has been brought to its end" (perfect passive of _teleō_, finishing the work). _Gegonen_ = "it has come to pass / it has happened" (perfect of _ginomai_, the becoming-event). **Both are perfect-tense divine declarations; one is spoken at Calvary, the other from the temple-throne; both close a chapter of redemptive history.** At Calvary mercy's work was done; at the seventh vial wrath's work is done.
- "**Voices, and thunders, and lightnings... a great earthquake**" — the Sinai-storm signature. **Rev 4:5 (lightnings, thunderings, voices proceed out of the throne); Rev 8:5 (after the censer is cast down — voices, thunderings, lightnings, earthquake); Rev 11:19 (after the temple opens — lightnings, voices, thunderings, earthquake, great hail); Rev 16:18-21 (the climactic full set: voices, thunders, lightnings, earthquake greater than any since men were on earth, hail of talent-weight stones).** The crescendo is structural: the storm-signature has been building from Rev 4 to Rev 16.
- "**The great city was divided into three parts**" — Greek _hē polis hē megalē_. **The same "great city" of Rev 11:8, Rev 14:8, Rev 17:18, Rev 18:10, 16, 18, 21.** Babylon. **Divided into three parts** — its three components (dragon, beast, false prophet, vv. 13-14) split from each other. **The unholy trinity's coalition disintegrates under the seventh plague.**
- "**Great Babylon came in remembrance before God**" — Greek _emnēsthē enōpion tou theou_. **God remembers Babylon.** Compare the same verb in Lk 23:42 ("Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom") — but here remembered for judgment, not mercy. **Probation closed in Rev 15:8; the books of judgment are now read aloud.**
- "**The cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath**" — Greek _to potērion tou oinou tou thymou tēs orgēs autou_. **Both _thymos_ and _orgē_ in one phrase.** The wrath that was filled up (Rev 15:1) and the indignation that was paired with it (Rev 14:10) come together in one cup. **The cup of fornication in Rev 17 will become the cup of wrath here.**
- "**Every island fled away, and the mountains were not found**" — global geographical convulsion. **The same imagery as Rev 6:14 (the sixth seal — every mountain and island moved out of their places).** The sixth seal anticipated this; the seventh vial completes it.
- "**Hail... about the weight of a talent**" — a talent is approximately 30-50 kg / 60-100 lb. **One stone could destroy a building.** Compare Ex 9:13-35 (the seventh Egyptian plague — hail mingled with fire); Josh 10:11 (the LORD cast hailstones from heaven on the Amorites); Eze 38:22 (judgment-hail on Gog). **The seventh vial's hail is the antitype of all OT judgment-hail.**
- "**Men blasphemed God... for the plague thereof was exceeding great**" — the third impenitence notice (vv. 9, 11 explicitly say "repented not"; v. 21 completes the pattern by blasphemy under hail). **Even under literal hailstones from heaven, the unsealed blaspheme rather than bow.** This is the verdict the closing crisis reveals: not that God did not give every chance, but that the hardened heart, given every chance, hardens further.

[Q] **The seventh vial declares "It is done." Yet Rev 17-19 still detail Babylon's destruction and Christ's coming. What is the relationship?** Rev 16 presents the plagues thematically as a complete sequence ending with "It is done"; Rev 17-19 then expand specific aspects in close-up: Rev 17 is the close-up of Babylon's identity and her relationship to the kings; Rev 18 is the close-up of Babylon's economic-religious fall and heaven's call to come out of her; Rev 19 is the close-up of the white-horse Rider executing the seventh-vial verdict. **"It is done" is heaven's verdict; Rev 17-19 are the cinematic detail.** This is the same pattern Rev 8-9 and Rev 10-11 used for the trumpets, where the seventh trumpet's "the kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord" (Rev 11:15) is then expanded across Rev 12-22. **Revelation moves by recapitulation, not strict chronology** — the seventh element of each sequence opens out into the next sequence's exposition.

## After the Verses — What Rev 16 Settles

- **The wrath of God is real, righteous, and finished.** The plagues are not arbitrary; they are the answer to Rev 6:10's "how long?" cry, the antitype of the Egyptian plagues, and the public vindication of God's judgment of Babylon. The altar itself declares "true and righteous" (v. 7). **No charge of cruelty stands;** every angelic creature concurs.
- **Probation does not move during the plagues.** Two explicit "repented not" notices (vv. 9, 11) and a third blasphemy-under-hail notice (v. 21). **The plagues do not produce conversion; they produce blasphemy.** The character-fixing of Rev 22:11 holds: the unjust still unjust, the righteous still righteous. Rev 16 confirms the verdict probation already fixed.
- **The sealed are protected.** The first vial falls specifically on those with the mark (v. 2); the targeted plagues target by spiritual allegiance, not geography. **The structural parallel is Ex 12:13** — "when I see the blood, I will pass over you" — and **Eze 9:4-6** — "set a mark upon the foreheads... come not near any man upon whom is the mark." Rev 7's seal is the antitypical Passover-blood and antitypical Eze-9-mark.
- **Armageddon is cosmic, not regional.** The unholy trinity (dragon, beast, false prophet) gathers the kings of the whole world (v. 14) to a battle "in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon" (v. 16). The Hebrew name signals: this is on God's covenant ground, against God's covenant people, with God Himself as the opposing combatant. **Rev 19's white-horse Rider resolves it, not human armies.**
- **"It is done" is the cross-cry's twin.** _Tetelestai_ and _gegonen_ — different verbs, both perfect-tense, both spoken with divine finality, both ending a redemptive era. **The cross finished mercy's work for the believer; the seventh vial finishes wrath's work against final-impenitent Babylon.** Two endings, one God, one consistent character.
- **Babylon is named, divided, and remembered.** Verse 19 is the verdict Rev 17-18 will then unfold in close-up. **The seventh vial makes the judgment a cosmic, universal fact;** Rev 17-18 will trace its outworking on Babylon's specific institutions.

[→] **Cross-anchor.** The fifth-seal cry (Rev 6:9-11), the third angel's warning (Rev 14:9-11), and the seventh vial (Rev 16:17-21) form one arc: **martyrs cry → mercy warns → wrath answers**. Hold them together; do not isolate any one.

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 16 is the wrath of God, "filled up" in Rev 15:1, actually pouring out
- The bowls (_phialai_) of v. 1 are the same vessels as Rev 5:8's prayer-bowls — the saints' "how long?" prayers return as the answering plagues
- Plague 1 falls specifically on those with the mark (v. 2) — the targeting is by spiritual allegiance, paralleling Ex 12:13 and Eze 9:4-6
- The altar itself declares "true and righteous are thy judgments" (v. 7) — no creature accuses God of cruelty; every creature vindicates Him
- Two explicit "repented not" notices (vv. 9, 11) and a third blasphemy-under-hail notice (v. 21) — the plagues do not change character; they reveal it
- The sixth vial dries up Babylon's Euphrates (v. 12) — her popular support withdraws — paralleling Cyrus's 539 BC fall of historical Babylon (Isa 44:27-45:1; Jer 50:38; 51:36)
- The unholy trinity (dragon, beast, false prophet) is named together openly only in Rev 16:13-14 — three frog-spirits gathering the world to Armageddon
- Armageddon (v. 16) = _har-Megiddo_, the symbolic mountain of the decisive covenant-history battle; cosmic, not regional
- "Behold, I come as a thief" (v. 15) inserted into the Armageddon gathering — Christ's return is the resolution of Armageddon
- Seventh vial's "It is done" (Greek _gegonen_) is the perfect-tense twin of the cross's _tetelestai_ — different verbs, same finality, different ministrations of one consistent God
- Rev 16:19's "great Babylon came in remembrance before God" is the verdict Rev 17-18 will then unfold in close-up
