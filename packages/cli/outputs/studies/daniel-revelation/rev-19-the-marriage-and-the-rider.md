# Revelation 19 — The Marriage of the Lamb and the White-Horse Rider

**Rev 18 ended with the millstone cast into the sea and Babylon found no more. Rev 19 opens with heaven's answer: four "alleluias" in seven verses, the only Hallelujah-chorus in the New Testament. The great voice of much people in heaven praises God for the just judgment of the great whore. The twenty-four elders and the four living creatures fall down and worship: "Amen; Alleluia." A voice from the throne calls all His servants — small and great — to praise. Then the multitude voice answers: "Alleluia: for the Lord God omnipotent reigneth. Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready." She is arrayed in fine linen, clean and white — the righteousness of saints. The fourth of Revelation's seven beatitudes is pronounced: "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb." Then heaven opens. A white horse appears. The Rider is "Faithful and True"; His eyes flame of fire; on His head many crowns; on His thigh a name: KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS. The armies of heaven follow on white horses. A sharp sword goes out of His mouth. He treads the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God. An angel in the sun calls the fowls to the supper of the great God. The beast and the false prophet are cast alive into the lake of fire; their armies are slain by the sword from His mouth; the fowls are filled with their flesh. Rev 19 is the chapter where the Lamb that was slain becomes the King who reigns — and the verdict of Rev 17:14 is executed on the field.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 19 (entire chapter)
- Revelation 17-18 (Babylon's identity and fall — the chapters Rev 19 answers)
- Revelation 5:6-13 (the Lamb in the throne room — the same Lamb who now rides)
- Revelation 6:2 (the first-seal white horse — superficial parallel; Rev 19 reveals the true Rider)
- Daniel 7:13-14 (the Son of Man receiving dominion — fulfilled in Rev 19's KING OF KINGS)
- Isaiah 63:1-6 (the lone winepress-treader from Edom — direct source for Rev 19:13, 15)
- Joel 3:13 (the harvest is ripe; the press is full)
- Ezekiel 39:17-20 (the supper of the LORD on Israel's mountains — pattern for Rev 19:17-18)
- Psalm 45:3-5 (the Messianic warrior-king — bow, arrows, sword, riding prosperously)
- Matthew 22:1-14 (the parable of the marriage feast — wedding garment required)

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 19 Sits

Rev 19 is the answer-chapter to Rev 17-18. **Rev 17 unveiled the harlot; Rev 18 announced her fall; Rev 19 brings heaven's hallelujah and the Lamb's wedding-and-victory.** The chapter has two halves: vv. 1-10 (the marriage-supper scene) and vv. 11-21 (the white-horse Rider scene). Both halves answer Rev 13-18 in different registers — the marriage answers the harlot; the Rider answers the beast.

- **Four "alleluias" in seven verses** (vv. 1, 3, 4, 6) — the only Hallelujah-chorus in the entire New Testament. The word _hallēlouia_ (transliteration of Hebrew _halelu-yah_, "praise YAH") appears exactly four times in the NT, all in Rev 19:1-6. **Heaven knows only one tune at the moment of Babylon's fall.**
- **The marriage of the Lamb is announced after Babylon falls** (vv. 7-9). The harlot of Rev 17 is destroyed; the bride of Rev 21 is now ready. **The harlot must fall before the bride can be presented.** The chapter is structured around the contrast Rev 17 and Rev 21 frame.
- **The bride's clothing is "the righteousness of saints"** (v. 8). Greek _ta dikaiōmata tōn hagiōn_ — the righteous acts/judgments of the saints. **Compare Rev 15:4** ("thy righteous judgments are made manifest" — _ta dikaiōmata sou ephanerōthēsan_). Same Greek word; different referent. **The character that has been formed during the closing crisis is the wedding garment.**
- **The Rider's name "no man knew, but he himself"** (v. 12) is the hidden identity, while "KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS" (v. 16) is the public title. **The same Person; the inner mystery and the outer office.**
- **The beast and the false prophet are cast alive into the lake of fire** (v. 20). Two of the three members of the unholy trinity (Rev 16:13) meet their judgment here. The dragon waits until Rev 20.
- **The chapter is the verdict Rev 17:14 promised executed.** Rev 17:14 — "These shall make war with the Lamb, and the Lamb shall overcome them: for he is Lord of lords, and King of kings." Rev 19:11-21 — the war is fought; the Lamb overcomes; the title is written on His thigh.

[KEY🔑] **Rev 19 is the chapter where the Lamb that was slain becomes the King who reigns.** From Rev 5:6 ("a Lamb as it had been slain") through Rev 7:17 ("the Lamb which is in the midst of the throne shall feed them") through Rev 13:8 ("the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world") through Rev 17:14 ("the Lamb shall overcome them") — the title "Lamb" runs through Revelation as the cross-vocabulary of the worthy-One. **In Rev 19 the Lamb's wedding is announced and the Lamb's army rides.** The same Person who was slain at Calvary is the Person on the white horse. **The wedding-supper of v. 9 and the supper-of-the-great-God of v. 17 are not parallel: they are inverse.** The first is the saints feasting at the table; the second is the fowls feasting on the corpses of those who refused. **The Lamb's wedding is the saints' supper; the Rider's coming is the wicked's supper — for the wicked, by the fowls. Rev 19 is the chapter that polarizes the universe at the moment of the Second Coming.**

## Rev 19:1 — Alleluia: Salvation, Glory, Honour, Power

> "And after these things I heard a great voice of much people in heaven, saying, Alleluia; Salvation, and glory, and honour, and power, unto the Lord our God:" (Rev 19:1)

- "**After these things**" — Greek _meta tauta_. **Same scene-shift formula as Rev 18:1.** Rev 18 announced and executed Babylon's fall on earth; Rev 19 opens with heaven's response.
- "**A great voice of much people in heaven**" — Greek _phōnēn megalēn ochlou pollou en tō ouranō_. **Compare Rev 7:9** ("a great multitude... before the throne") — the redeemed company. **The voice is plural and great; the joy is universal.**
- "**Alleluia**" — Greek _hallēlouia_, transliterating Hebrew _halelu-yah_, "praise YAH" / "praise the LORD." **The first of four Alleluias** in Rev 19:1, 3, 4, 6 — the only NT occurrences. **The OT Psalter uses _halelu-yah_ in the Hallelujah-psalms; in the listed set several are closing doxologies rather than headings (e.g., Pss 104, 105, 115, 116), while Ps 106, 111-113, 135, 146-150 open with "Praise ye the LORD" in KJV;** the Hallel psalms (113-118, 136) sung at Passover. **The _hallel_ that Israel sang at the Passover supper is what heaven sings at the antitypical Passover deliverance.**
- "**Salvation, and glory, and honour, and power**" — Greek _hē sōtēria kai hē doxa kai hē timē kai hē dynamis_. **Four-fold ascription** — compare Rev 5:12 (seven-fold "worthy is the Lamb") and Rev 7:12 (seven-fold "blessing, and glory, and wisdom"). **The four-fold pattern uses fewer terms because the focus is sharper:** salvation has come; glory belongs to God; honor is due to His name; power has been demonstrated.

## Rev 19:2 — True and Righteous Are His Judgments

> "For true and righteous are his judgments: for he hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and hath avenged the blood of his servants at her hand." (Rev 19:2)

- "**True and righteous are his judgments**" — Greek _alēthinai kai dikaiai hai kriseis autou_. **Same verdict-phrase as Rev 16:7** ("true and righteous are thy judgments" — spoken from the altar at the third vial); **related to Rev 15:3** ("just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints"). **Three-fold occurrence** in the closing chapters: at the third vial, at the song of Moses-and-the-Lamb, and at the post-Babylon Hallelujah. **Heaven's verdict on God's judgment is unanimous and repeated.**
- "**He hath judged the great whore**" — Greek _ekrinen tēn pornēn tēn megalēn_. **Same designation as Rev 17:1.** The chapter that opened the harlot's identity (Rev 17) closes the chapter on her judgment (Rev 19). The Greek aorist _ekrinen_ — "he has judged" — declares the verdict completed.
- "**Did corrupt the earth with her fornication**" — Greek _ephtheiren tēn gēn en tē porneia autēs_. **_Phtheirō_** = ruin, destroy, corrupt morally (cf. 1 Cor 3:17 — "if any man defile the temple of God, him shall God destroy"). **Babylon's _porneia_ is not a private sin; it is global moral ruin.**
- "**Hath avenged the blood of his servants at her hand**" — Greek _ekdikēsen to haima tōn doulōn autou ek cheiros autēs_. **Direct answer to Rev 6:10** (fifth-seal cry: "How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?"). **The wait of Rev 6:11 is over;** the avenging has come.

[⟲] **The fifth-seal cry comes to its full closure in Rev 19:2.** Rev 6:9-11 (the souls under the altar — "How long?"). Rev 8:3-5 (the prayer-incense / fire-cast-to-earth — the trumpets begin as answer). Rev 14:18 (the angel from the altar gives the order for the vintage harvest). Rev 16:7 (the altar speaks: "true and righteous are thy judgments"). Rev 18:20 ("Rejoice over her, thou heaven... God hath avenged you on her" — _ekrinen ho theos to krima hymōn ex autēs_). **Rev 19:2 — the universal Hallelujah declares the avenging _ekdikēsen_ as accomplished fact.** The thread that began at the fifth seal closes with the post-Babylon Hallelujah. **The martyrs' "how long?" is finally answered "now."**

## Rev 19:3-4 — Her Smoke for Ever and Ever; Amen; Alleluia

> "And again they said, Alleluia. And her smoke rose up for ever and ever. And the four and twenty elders and the four beasts fell down and worshipped God that sat on the throne, saying, Amen; Alleluia." (Rev 19:3-4)

- "**Her smoke rose up for ever and ever**" — Greek _ho kapnos autēs anabainei eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn_. **The same _eis aiōnas aiōnōn_ language Rev 14:11 used** of the smoke of the torment of those who worshipped the beast. **Compare Isa 34:9-10** (Edom's smoke goes up forever) and **Jude 7** (Sodom and Gomorrha set forth as an example, suffering "the vengeance of eternal fire"). **The "for ever" of v. 3 = the permanence of the verdict's RESULT, not eternal conscious burning.** Sodom's smoke is no longer rising literally; what is permanent is the destruction. **Rev 19:3 declares the same permanence for Babylon's destruction.**
- "**The four and twenty elders and the four beasts fell down and worshipped**" — same throne-room company as Rev 4-5. **The heavenly representatives that received the sealed scroll in Rev 5 now worship at its full unsealing.** **The whole arc from Rev 4-5 (the scroll given to the Lamb) through Rev 6-19 (its seven seals opened, its content executed) closes with the original throne-room company saying "Amen."**
- "**Amen; Alleluia**" — Greek _amēn hallēlouia_. **The third Alleluia.** Hebrew _amēn_ ("verily, so be it") plus _halelu-yah_ ("praise YAH"). **Pure Hebrew worship-language at the moment of cosmic vindication.**

## Rev 19:5-6 — Voice from the Throne; the Multitude Voice

> "And a voice came out of the throne, saying, Praise our God, all ye his servants, and ye that fear him, both small and great. And I heard as it were the voice of a great multitude, and as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of mighty thunderings, saying, Alleluia: for the Lord God omnipotent reigneth." (Rev 19:5-6)

- "**A voice came out of the throne**" — a single voice, not the multitude. **The throne speaks to its servants, calling them to praise.** Compare Ps 134:1, 135:1; the voice gives the call to worship in the OT-Psalter manner.
- "**Praise our God, all ye his servants, and ye that fear him**" — same two-fold designation as Ps 115:11; 118:4. **"Servants" + "those that fear him"** = the covenant community (servants by belonging) + the wider company of God-fearers (proselytes / those drawn to YHWH).
- "**Both small and great**" — Greek _hoi mikroi kai hoi megaloi_. **Same phrase as Rev 11:18, 13:16, 19:18, 20:12.** Including Rev 19:5 itself, this is a five-fold occurrence in Revelation's closing chapters; the universal-classification phrase.
- "**The voice of a great multitude, and as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of mighty thunderings**" — three comparisons. **Compare Rev 1:15** (Christ's voice "as the sound of many waters"); **Eze 43:2** ("his voice was like a noise of many waters"); **Rev 14:2** ("a voice from heaven, as the voice of many waters, and as the voice of a great thunder"). **The same vocabulary used for Christ's voice is used for the saints' voice** — a vast company speaking with one voice.
- "**The Lord God omnipotent reigneth**" — Greek _ebasileusen kyrios ho theos ho pantokratōr_. **Aorist _ebasileusen_** — has begun to reign / has reigned. **The great-controversy verdict:** God reigns. The dispute over the throne is settled. **Compare Rev 11:17** (seventh trumpet — "thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned"). **The seventh trumpet's announcement is now the post-Babylon Hallelujah's chorus.**

## Rev 19:7-8 — The Marriage of the Lamb; the Wife Made Herself Ready

> "Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready. And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints." (Rev 19:7-8)

- "**The marriage of the Lamb is come**" — Greek _ēlthen ho gamos tou arniou_. **Aorist _ēlthen_** — has come / is arrived. **The Lamb-bridegroom motif** runs from Jn 3:29 (John the Baptist — "he that hath the bride is the bridegroom") through 2 Cor 11:2 (Paul's "espoused you to one husband") through Eph 5:25-32 (Christ and the church) **to the consummation here.**
- "**His wife hath made herself ready**" — Greek _hē gynē autou hētoimasen heautēn_. **The bride is active, not passive.** She has prepared herself for the wedding. **Compare the parable of the ten virgins** (Mt 25:1-13) — the wise virgins prepared their lamps; the foolish did not.
- "**Arrayed in fine linen, clean and white**" — Greek _byssinon lampron katharon_. **_Byssos_** — fine linen, the priestly garment of Lev 16:4 and the Day of Atonement. **The bride wears Day-of-Atonement priestly white** because the Day of Atonement has just closed. **Same garment as Rev 19:14's heavenly armies.**
- "**For the fine linen is the righteousness of saints**" — Greek _to gar byssinon ta dikaiōmata tōn hagiōn estin_. **_Dikaiōmata_** — righteous deeds, righteous acts/judgments (cf. Rev 15:4 — "thy righteous judgments [_dikaiōmata_] are made manifest"). **The bride's clothing is the saints' righteous-acts that flowed from imputed righteousness during the closing crisis.** Compare Mt 22:11-13 (the man without the wedding garment is cast out) — only Christ's righteousness, manifested as character-formed-during-the-closing-crisis, qualifies. **The wedding garment is not earned; it is "granted"** (_edothē autē_ — was given to her). Yet she has "made herself ready" — synergy of grace and response.

[CTRF] **The bride of Rev 19 vs. the harlot of Rev 17 — the contrast at the wedding moment.** The harlot wore purple, scarlet, and gold (Rev 17:4) — colors of imperial-ecclesiastical ostentation. The bride wears _byssinon lampron katharon_ — bright, clean, fine linen — the priestly Day-of-Atonement white (Lev 16:4). The harlot's adornment was external display; the bride's adornment is _ta dikaiōmata tōn hagiōn_ — character flowing from imputed righteousness. **The harlot self-adorned with what the merchants of the earth supplied (Rev 18:12-13); the bride is adorned with what was "granted" by heaven (_edothē autē_) and "made ready" by her response.** The harlot rode a beast (Rev 17:3); the bride is the Lamb's. The harlot was "drunken with the blood of the saints" (Rev 17:6); the bride is the saints. **The chapter that named the bride is the chapter that consummated the harlot's destruction** — the two are deliberately frame-paired. Rev 19:7-8 must be read against Rev 17:4-6.

## Rev 19:9 — Blessed Are They Called to the Marriage Supper

> "And he saith unto me, Write, Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb. And he saith unto me, These are the true sayings of God." (Rev 19:9)

- "**Write, Blessed are they which are called**" — the **fourth of Revelation's seven beatitudes** (Rev 1:3, 14:13, 16:15, 19:9, 20:6, 22:7, 22:14). **Compare Mt 22:1-14** — Jesus's parable of the king who made a marriage for his son, the called who refused, the highways-and-hedges call, and the wedding-garment requirement.
- "**Called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb**" — Greek _hoi eis to deipnon tou gamou tou arniou keklēmenoi_. **The marriage-supper imagery** is Christ's own (Mt 22; Lk 14:15-24; Mt 25). **The call is to the supper, not just to the kingdom-as-such.** Salvation is presented as the privilege of sharing the Lamb's table at His wedding feast.
- "**These are the true sayings of God**" — Greek _houtoi hoi logoi alēthinoi tou theou eisin_. **A formal certification.** Compare Rev 21:5 ("these words are true and faithful") and Rev 22:6 ("these sayings are faithful and true"). **The angel's certification anchors the marriage-supper promise** as inerrant divine speech.

## Rev 19:10 — Worship God; the Testimony of Jesus Is the Spirit of Prophecy

> "And I fell at his feet to worship him. And he said unto me, See thou do it not: I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus: worship God: for the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy." (Rev 19:10)

- "**I fell at his feet to worship him**" — John's instinct is to worship the angel. **The angel's response** is identical to Rev 22:9 ("see thou do it not... worship God"). **Two-fold rebuke:** Revelation contains two separate moments where John tries to worship an angel and is corrected. The pattern is structural — the chapter that climaxes with the white-horse Rider does not begin with creature-worship.
- "**I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus**" — Greek _syndoulos sou kai tōn adelphōn sou tōn echontōn tēn martyrian Iēsou_. **The angel ranks himself with John and with the persecuted church** — fellow-servants under the same Lord. **The "testimony of Jesus" connects to Rev 12:17** (the dragon's wrath against the remnant which keep the commandments of God and have the testimony of Jesus Christ).
- "**Worship God**" — Greek _tō theō proskynēson_. **The chapter's worship-axis re-asserted.** Worship belongs only to God. **The chapter that closes with the wicked who worshiped the beast cast into the lake (v. 20) cannot tolerate creature-worship in its opening half.**
- "**The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy**" — Greek _hē gar martyria Iēsou estin to pneuma tēs prophēteias_. **A crucial definitional sentence.** The Greek genitive _Iēsou_ ("of Jesus") can be read as objective ("testimony about Jesus") or subjective ("testimony from Jesus"); both are operative. **The testimony Jesus gives through His prophets is the Spirit's prophetic activity in the church** — including the closing-crisis revival of the prophetic gift in the remnant. **Rev 12:17 + Rev 19:10 give the two-fold mark of the remnant:** they keep the commandments AND have the testimony of Jesus = the spirit of prophecy.

[→] **Cross-anchor: the testimony/witness thread.** Rev 1:2, 9 (John's situation — exiled "for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ"). Rev 6:9 (fifth-seal martyrs "for the testimony which they held"). Rev 12:17 (remnant keep the commandments AND have the testimony of Jesus). Rev 19:10 (the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy). Rev 20:4 (the beheaded for the witness of Jesus). **Five related anchor-points in Revelation;** the thread runs the whole book.

## Rev 19:11 — Heaven Opened, the White-Horse Rider

> "And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war." (Rev 19:11)

- "**Heaven opened**" — Greek _ēneōgmenon ton ouranon_. **Compare Eze 1:1** ("the heavens were opened, and I saw visions of God"); **Mt 3:16** (Jesus's baptism — "the heavens were opened unto him"); **Acts 7:56** (Stephen — "I see the heavens opened, and the Son of man standing on the right hand of God"). **Rev 19:11 is the opened-heaven of the Second Coming.**
- "**A white horse**" — Greek _hippos leukos_. **Same color as the first-seal horse of Rev 6:2.** The differences: in Rev 6 the rider has a bow and a stephanos-crown and goes forth conquering; in Rev 19 the Rider has many diadems, a sharp sword from His mouth, and is named Faithful and True / KING OF KINGS / Word of God. **The first-seal white horse is the Christian-era gospel mission going forth conquering;** the Rev 19:11 white horse is Christ Himself coming to consummate the conquest. **The historicist tradition reads Rev 6:2's white-horse rider as the apostolic gospel age, not Christ; Rev 19:11 is unambiguously Christ.**
- "**Called Faithful and True**" — Greek _kaloumenos pistos kai alēthinos_. **Same titles as Rev 3:14** (Christ to Laodicea — "the faithful and true witness"). **The Person who certified the testimony to the seven churches is the Person on the horse.**
- "**In righteousness he doth judge and make war**" — Greek _en dikaiosynē krinei kai polemei_. **Compare Isa 11:3-5** (the Branch from Jesse — "with righteousness shall he judge the poor... he shall smite the earth with the rod of his mouth, and with the breath of his lips shall he slay the wicked"). **Rev 19:11's Rider is the Isa-11 Branch consummating the judgment-and-war from righteousness.**

## Rev 19:12 — Eyes Flame of Fire, Many Crowns, Hidden Name

> "His eyes were as a flame of fire, and on his head were many crowns; and he had a name written, that no man knew, but he himself." (Rev 19:12)

- "**His eyes were as a flame of fire**" — Greek _hoi de ophthalmoi autou hōs phlox pyros_. **Same description as Rev 1:14 and Rev 2:18** (Christ to Thyatira). **Penetrating, judging eyes** that see through every disguise.
- "**Many crowns**" — Greek _diadēmata polla_. **_Diadēmata_** — kingly diadems (not _stephanoi_ = victor-wreaths). **The dragon had seven diadems on his heads (Rev 12:3); the sea-beast had ten diadems on his horns (Rev 13:1); the Rider has _many_ diadems** — more than the dragon, more than the beast, an indefinite plurality marking total kingly authority.
- "**A name written, that no man knew, but he himself**" — Greek _onoma gegrammenon ho oudeis oiden ei mē autos_. **A hidden name.** Compare Rev 2:17 ("a new name written, which no man knoweth saving he that receiveth it"). **The depth of Christ's identity exceeds any creature's full knowledge.** The next verses give what is revealed; the hidden name preserves what is His alone.

## Rev 19:13 — Vesture Dipped in Blood; the Word of God

> "And he was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God." (Rev 19:13)

- "**Clothed with a vesture dipped in blood**" — Greek _himation bebammenon haimati_. **Direct echo of Isa 63:1-3** ("Who is this that cometh from Edom, with dyed garments from Bozrah?... Wherefore art thou red in thine apparel, and thy garments like him that treadeth in the winefat?"). **The blood is on the vesture before the battle of v. 19** — meaning either (a) the vesture is dipped in His own blood from Calvary, or (b) the blood is the vintage-blood already trodden in Rev 14:20. **The historicist tradition has read both layers as operative:** the same Cross-blood that paid for redemption is now the vintage-blood that judges the rejection of redemption. **The blood that saved is the blood that judges.**
- "**His name is called The Word of God**" — Greek _to onoma autou ho logos tou theou_. **Same title as Jn 1:1** ("In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God") and **1 Jn 1:1** ("the Word of life"). **The Logos who created at Gen 1 is the Logos who consummates at Rev 19.** The Word that spoke creation into being is the Word that speaks judgment into final form. **The sword from His mouth (v. 15) is the verbal weapon — the Word's own speech is the instrument of judgment.**

## Rev 19:14 — The Armies of Heaven on White Horses

> "And the armies which were in heaven followed him upon white horses, clothed in fine linen, white and clean." (Rev 19:14)

- "**The armies which were in heaven**" — Greek _ta strateumata en tō ouranō_. **The Lord's hosts.** Could include angels and/or the redeemed; the language fits both. **Compare Mt 26:53** (Jesus could call twelve legions of angels) and **Jude 14-15** (Enoch's prophecy — "the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints, to execute judgment").
- "**Followed him upon white horses**" — Greek _ēkolouthei autō eph' hippois leukois_. **The army's horse-color matches the Rider's.** They follow His pattern.
- "**Clothed in fine linen, white and clean**" — Greek _byssinon leukon katharon_. **Same _byssinon_ as the bride's clothing in v. 8.** The bride and the heavenly armies wear the same garment. **The marriage company and the Rider's army share the wedding-clothing.** The same righteousness that prepared the bride for the wedding now arms her for the campaign.

## Rev 19:15 — Sharp Sword, Rod of Iron, the Winepress

> "And out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall rule them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God." (Rev 19:15)

- "**Out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword**" — Greek _rhomphaia oxeia_. **Same sword-from-the-mouth motif as Rev 1:16; Rev 1:16 adds "twoedged."** **The verbal weapon.** The sword does not come from His hand; it comes from His mouth. **Compare Isa 49:2** ("he hath made my mouth like a sharp sword") and **Heb 4:12** ("the word of God is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged sword"). **The Logos's speech is the instrument of final judgment.**
- "**Smite the nations**" — Greek _patassē ta ethnē_. **Compare Isa 11:4** ("with the rod of his mouth... shall he slay the wicked").
- "**Rule them with a rod of iron**" — Greek _poimanei autous en rhabdō sidērā_. **Greek follows the LXX wording of Ps 2:9** ("rule/shepherd with a rod of iron"); the KJV/Hebrew wording is "break them with a rod of iron; dash them in pieces like a potter's vessel." **Ps 2 is the Messianic-king psalm**, and Rev 19:15 invokes it as fulfilled. **Compare Rev 12:5; Rev 2:27** (same _rhabdō sidērā_ phrase).
- "**Treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God**" — Greek _patei tēn lēnon tou oinou tou thymou tēs orgēs tou theou tou pantokratoros_. **Both _thymos_ and _orgē_ in one phrase** (cf. Rev 16:19 — "the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath"). **Direct echo of Rev 14:19-20** (the angel cast the vintage into "the great winepress of the wrath of God"; "the winepress was trodden without the city, and blood came out of the winepress"). **Rev 14:20's "without the city" sets the location; Rev 19:15 names the Treader.** Compare **Isa 63:3** ("I have trodden the winepress alone; and of the people there was none with me") — Christ alone treads the winepress. **The vintage of Rev 14 is finally trodden in Rev 19.**

## Rev 19:16 — KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS

> "And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS." (Rev 19:16)

- "**On his vesture and on his thigh**" — Greek _epi to himation kai epi ton mēron autou_. **Two locations:** the outer garment (visible to all approaching) and the thigh (visible when riding). **No matter how the Rider is seen — on foot or mounted — the title is readable.**
- "**KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS**" — Greek _BASILEUS BASILEŌN KAI KYRIOS KYRIŌN_. **Same titles as Rev 17:14** (in reversed order: "Lord of lords, and King of kings") and **1 Tim 6:15** (Paul's doxology — "the King of kings, and Lord of lords"). **The "Lord of lords" half of the title is a Yahweh-title in Deut 10:17** ("the LORD your God is God of gods, and Lord of lords"). **Rev 17:14 named the title; Rev 19:16 inscribes it.**

---

## Rev 19:17-18 — The Supper of the Great God

> "And I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the fowls that fly in the midst of heaven, Come and gather yourselves together unto the supper of the great God; That ye may eat the flesh of kings, and the flesh of captains, and the flesh of mighty men, and the flesh of horses, and of them that sit on them, and the flesh of all men, both free and bond, both small and great." (Rev 19:17-18)

- "**An angel standing in the sun**" — Greek _hena angelon hestōta en tō hēliō_. **An angel positioned in the sun's full glare.** No shadow; no concealment. The herald's announcement is universal and inescapable.
- "**Cried with a loud voice**" — Greek _ekraxen phōnē megalē_. **Same loud-voice motif as Rev 14:7, 14:9, 18:2.**
- "**To all the fowls that fly in the midst of heaven**" — Greek _tois orneois tois petomenois en mesouranēmati_. **_Mesouranēma_** — mid-heaven (cf. Rev 8:13, 14:6 — same word for the announcing angel's flight-position). **The herald-position is the same; the audience is now the carrion-birds.**
- "**The supper of the great God**" — Greek _to deipnon to mega tou theou_. **The inverse of v. 9's marriage supper.** The bride feasts at the Lamb's table; the fowls feast on the wicked's flesh. **One supper for the saints; one supper from the saints' enemies.**
- "**The flesh of kings, and... captains, and... mighty men, and... horses, and... of them that sit on them, and... of all men, both free and bond, both small and great**" — six categories, climaxing in the universal small-and-great. **Direct echo of Eze 39:17-20** (the supper of the LORD on Israel's mountains — "ye shall eat the flesh of the mighty, and drink the blood of the princes of the earth... ye shall be filled at my table with horses and chariots, with mighty men, and with all men of war"). **Rev 19:17-18 is the eschatological version of Eze 39's Gog-supper.**

---

## Rev 19:19-20 — The Beast and False Prophet Cast Into the Lake of Fire

> "And I saw the beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together to make war against him that sat on the horse, and against his army. And the beast was taken, and with him the false prophet that wrought miracles before him, with which he deceived them that had received the mark of the beast, and them that worshipped his image. These both were cast alive into a lake of fire burning with brimstone." (Rev 19:19-20)

- "**The beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together**" — Greek _synēgmena poiēsai polemon_. **Same gathering-language as Rev 16:14, 16** (Armageddon — "to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty... into a place called in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon"). **Rev 19:19 is the Armageddon-engagement.** The frog-spirits succeeded in their mission — the kings are gathered. The Rider arrives at the moment of full assembly.
- "**The beast was taken, and with him the false prophet**" — Greek _epiasthē to thērion kai met' autou ho pseudoprophētēs_. **Two of the unholy trinity (Rev 16:13)** taken at once. **The false prophet** is identified explicitly with the earth-beast of Rev 13:11-17 by the description: "wrought miracles before him, with which he deceived them that had received the mark of the beast." **This is the structural verification that the false prophet = the earth-beast / two-horned beast** of Rev 13. **The dragon (Satan) waits until Rev 20:10** for his judgment.
- "**Cast alive into a lake of fire burning with brimstone**" — Greek _zōntes eis tēn limnēn tou pyros tēn kaiomenēn en theiō_. **First mention of "the lake of fire" in Revelation.** Rev 20:10, 14, 15; 21:8 will use the same image. **"Brimstone"** (sulfur) recalls **Sodom and Gomorrah** (Gen 19:24 — "the LORD rained upon Sodom and upon Gomorrah brimstone and fire"). **The Sodom precedent frames the lake's nature** — judgment-fire that consumes, not eternal-conscious-burning that preserves the consumed. **The beast and the false prophet meet the same destruction Sodom met,** scaled to the closing-crisis institutions they were.

---

## Rev 19:21 — The Remnant Slain by the Sword from His Mouth

> "And the remnant were slain with the sword of him that sat upon the horse, which sword proceeded out of his mouth: and all the fowls were filled with their flesh." (Rev 19:21)

- "**The remnant**" — Greek _hoi loipoi_. **Note:** this is the same lemma _loipos_ used in Rev 12:17, but a different inflection and referent: Rev 12:17 has the persecuted remnant of the woman's seed; Rev 19:21 has _hoi loipoi_ ("the rest, the remainder") of the wicked army — those left after the beast and false prophet are taken. The KJV translation "remnant" is potentially confusing; the meaning here is "the rest" / "the remainder" of the gathered armies.
- "**Slain with the sword... which sword proceeded out of his mouth**" — Greek _hoi loipoi apektanthēsan en tē rhomphaia tou kathēmenou epi tou hippou_. **The same sword from v. 15.** The Word's speech destroys what the Word's speech could have saved. **Compare 2 Th 2:8** ("whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth, and shall destroy with the brightness of his coming"). **The Second Coming destroys the wicked by the brightness of His arrival — the Word's manifestation = the wicked's death.**
- "**All the fowls were filled with their flesh**" — Greek _kai panta ta ornea echortasthēsan ek tōn sarkōn autōn_. **The supper of v. 17 is consumed.** The fowls are sated. The chapter that opened with the marriage supper closes with the supper of the great God consumed. **Two suppers in one chapter; opposite tables; opposite feasters.**

## After the Verses — What Rev 19 Settles

- **Heaven sings only Hallelujah at Babylon's fall.** Four "alleluias" in seven verses (vv. 1, 3, 4, 6) — the only Hallelujah-chorus in the entire NT. **No mourning over Babylon in heaven; no hand-wringing over the lost; no second-thought regret.** The verdict is just; the joy is unanimous. Whoever weeps with the kings/merchants/sailors of Rev 18 is on the wrong side of Rev 19.
- **The marriage of the Lamb is consummated.** The bride has made herself ready (v. 7); she is granted fine linen (v. 8 — _byssinon lampron katharon_, the priestly Day-of-Atonement white); the fine linen is the righteous-deeds of the saints (the character formed during the closing crisis). **The wedding garment is granted AND prepared — grace AND response.**
- **The bride is the inverse of the harlot.** Rev 19:7-8 must be read against Rev 17:4-6. Same chapter-pair angel; same kind of contrast as Rev 17 vs. Rev 21. **The bride wears what the harlot did not — the priestly white that signifies cleansed character.**
- **The white-horse Rider is Christ in royal-judicial mode.** "Faithful and True" (the Laodicean title from Rev 3:14); eyes a flame of fire (the priestly-judicial seeing of Rev 1:14, 2:18); many diadems (more than dragon's seven, more than beast's ten); vesture dipped in blood (Cross-blood + vintage-blood, both layers operative); name "The Word of God" (the Logos who created and now consummates); KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS (the title Rev 17:14 named, now inscribed).
- **The first-seal white horse and the Rev 19 white horse are not the same scene.** The first-seal rider has a bow and a victor-stephanos and goes forth conquering through the Christian-era gospel mission; the Rev 19 Rider has many diadems, a sword from His mouth, and is named Christ Himself coming for the consummation. **Historicist tradition: Rev 6:2 = the apostolic gospel age; Rev 19:11 = the Second Coming.**
- **Two of the unholy trinity meet their judgment.** The beast and the false prophet are cast alive into the lake of fire (v. 20). The dragon waits until Rev 20:10. **The lake of fire's brimstone recalls Sodom's destruction** (Gen 19:24) — judgment-fire that consumes, not eternal-conscious-burning that preserves the consumed.
- **The Logos's speech is the weapon.** The sword from the Rider's mouth (v. 15, 21) destroys the wicked. **No human warrior fights this battle; the Word's own speech finishes the gathered armies.** Compare 2 Th 2:8 (the Lord consumes the wicked by the spirit of His mouth and the brightness of His coming).
- **The chapter ends with the Armageddon-engagement resolved.** Rev 16:14, 16's gathering is met in Rev 19:19's Rider arrival. The same armies that the frog-spirits gathered are slain by the Word's speech. **Armageddon is not won by armies; it is won by the Word.**

## Key Takeaways

- Four "alleluias" in seven verses (vv. 1, 3, 4, 6) — the only Hallelujah-chorus in the entire NT
- The fifth-seal cry "how long?" of Rev 6:10 is finally answered "now" in Rev 19:2's "hath avenged the blood of his servants"
- The bride's wedding garment is "fine linen, clean and white" = the righteous-deeds (_dikaiōmata_) of the saints — character formed during the closing crisis (cf. Rev 15:4's same Greek root for God's "righteous judgments made manifest")
- The bride is the inverse of the harlot — same chapter-pair frame as Rev 17 vs. Rev 21
- Fourth of Revelation's seven beatitudes: "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb" (v. 9)
- "The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy" (v. 10) defines the second mark of the remnant of Rev 12:17 — they have the testimony of Jesus = the Spirit's prophetic activity
- The Rev 19:11 white-horse Rider is Christ in royal-judicial mode, NOT the same scene as the Rev 6:2 first-seal white horse (apostolic gospel age vs. the Second Coming)
- Many crowns (_diadēmata polla_) on the Rider's head — more than the dragon's seven, more than the beast's ten; total kingly authority
- "Vesture dipped in blood" (v. 13) — both Calvary-blood and vintage-blood layers operative; the blood that saved is the blood that judges
- "The Word of God" (v. 13) — the Logos who created at Gen 1 is the Logos who consummates at Rev 19; the sword from His mouth is His verbal weapon
- KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS (v. 16) — the Rev 17:14 title now inscribed; the Yahweh-title of Deut 10:17
- Two suppers in one chapter: the marriage supper of the Lamb (v. 9, saints feasting) and the supper of the great God (vv. 17-18, fowls feasting on the wicked's flesh) — the chapter polarizes the universe at the Second Coming
- The beast and false prophet cast alive into the lake of fire (v. 20) — first mention of the lake; brimstone-Sodom precedent (Gen 19:24)
- The false prophet = the earth-beast of Rev 13:11-17 (verified by v. 20's description: wrought miracles, deceived those with the mark)
- "The remnant" of v. 21 (_hoi loipoi_) = the remainder of the wicked army, NOT the remnant of Rev 12:17

## Next

Rev 20 takes the reader past the Second Coming into the millennium. An angel comes down with a great chain and casts Satan into the bottomless pit for a thousand years. The saints reign with Christ during this time; the rest of the dead live not again until the thousand years are finished. At the end Satan is loosed, gathers Gog and Magog, surrounds the camp of the saints; fire comes down from God and devours them. The devil is cast into the lake of fire where the beast and the false prophet are, "tormented day and night for ever and ever." Then a great white throne, the books opened, the dead small and great standing before God. Death and hell give up their dead. Each is judged according to his works. Death and hell are cast into the lake of fire — "this is the second death." Whosoever was not found written in the book of life is cast into the lake of fire. Rev 20 is the chapter where the great controversy reaches its forensic conclusion.
