## The Seven Beatitudes of Revelation

Revelation contains _seven_ beatitudes — a deliberate sevenfold blessing, scattered across the book in strategic places.

- **1. Blessed is he that readeth** (Rev 1:3)
  - "Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time is at hand"
  - The book's opening blessing: on readers, hearers, and keepers
  - Three actions: _read, hear, keep_. The beatitude is progressive — the blessing deepens as reading turns to hearing and hearing turns to obeying

- **2. Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth** (Rev 14:13)
  - "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours; and their works do follow them"
  - Positioned immediately after the third angel's warning (Rev 14:9-12). The blessing is for those who remain faithful _through_ the final crisis and, if necessary, die for the faith
  - The saints rest; their works _follow_ — not precede as justifying merit, but follow as the fruit of faithful lives

- **3. Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments** (Rev 16:15)
  - "Behold, I come as a thief. Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments, lest he walk naked, and they see his shame"
  - Inserted into the plague sequence (between plagues six and seven). The blessing is for those who remain watchful during the final hours — who _keep their garments_ (the righteousness of saints, Rev 19:8) rather than losing them when pressure intensifies

- **4. Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb** (Rev 19:9)
  - "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb"
  - The wedding blessing. The bride has made herself ready; those called are blessed
  - Positioned at the Second Coming's prologue — for those about to be united with Christ

- **5. Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection** (Rev 20:6)
  - "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death hath no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years"
  - The millennium blessing. Those in the first resurrection are safe from the second death, serve as priests, and reign with Christ

- **6. Blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book** (Rev 22:7)
  - "Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book"
  - The closing chapter's first beatitude. _Keepeth_ — the same word as Rev 1:3. The blessing is for those who have not only read and heard but _kept_ what they heard

- **7. Blessed are they that do his commandments** (Rev 22:14)
  - "Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city"
  - The final beatitude. The right to the tree of life (the one Gen 3 barred) is the inheritance of those who do the commandments. Note: _do_ His commandments, not merely profess faith; and the "right" is not a _wage_ earned but an _access granted_ to those whose character Christ has formed

- [→] The seven beatitudes mark a path. The reader is blessed (1); the faithful unto death are blessed (2); the watchful are blessed (3); the called to the supper are blessed (4); the first-resurrection saints are blessed (5); the keepers of the prophecy and of the commandments are blessed (6, 7). Each beatitude is a different phase of the journey the book describes. Read them as the book's own spiritual map: the progression from picking up the book to entering the city.

## The Three Invitations to "Come"

Revelation closes with an accelerating call.

- **"The Spirit and the bride say, Come"** (Rev 22:17, first _Come_)
  - The Spirit of God and the church together issue the call
  - This is the church's primary activity in the final hours: calling people to come to Christ

- **"And let him that heareth say, Come"** (Rev 22:17, second _Come_)
  - Those who have already heard become callers themselves
  - Evangelism is a multiplication: every person who hears and responds becomes a new voice of invitation

- **"And let him that is athirst come"** (Rev 22:17, third _Come_)
  - The thirsty are invited to come themselves — to the water of life, freely
  - "Whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely" — the offer is unrestricted

- **The progression is deliberate**
  - From initiators (the Spirit and the bride), through messengers (those who have heard), to seekers (the thirsty). The call expands outward, including more voices at each step, until everyone who wants to can come

- [→] Rev 22:17 is Revelation's gospel invitation. The whole book — prophecy, judgment, plagues, cosmic war, new creation — resolves in this verse. _Come._

## The Two Destinies

- **The tree of life** (Rev 22:2, 14)
  - "On either side of the river, was there the tree of life" (22:2)
  - "Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city" (22:14 — KJV; some critical-text editions read "wash their robes" in place of "do his commandments," but the beatitude stands either way)
  - The tree of life is the destiny of the overcomer. The corresponding warning at Rev 22:19 names the book of life: "If any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life, and out of the holy city"

- **The lake of fire** (Rev 20:14-15; 21:8)
  - "Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death"
  - "The fearful, and unbelieving... shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death"
  - The lake of fire is the destiny of the refuser

- **No third option**
  - Every person who has ever lived ends at one of these two destinations
  - The choice is not a decision made at death or at the judgment; it is a choice made over a lifetime, confirmed at the moment of probation's close, and executed at one of the two resurrections
  - Revelation's work is to make the choice clear _now_, so that the reader can choose in the light of evidence — not in the confusion of a crisis yet to come

## The Cosmic Trial Resolved

The Way Home series' opening study (Way Home Study 1) traced the cosmic trial — the great controversy over God's character. Revelation's 21 studies have now answered every charge.

- **Charge A (Satan's accusation about God's creatures):** "They only obey for reward. Take away the reward, and they will abandon God."
  - **Answer:** The sealed remnant (Rev 7:3-4; 14:1-5, 12) keep God's commandments under the beast's pressure (Rev 13:15-17 — no buying, selling, death). When the reward is taken away and only loss remains, they hold to God. The charge is answered on earth, in history, by the last generation
  - The "patience of the saints" (Rev 14:12) IS the verdict: loyalty that does not depend on receiving anything

- **Charge B (Satan's accusation about God's law):** "The law cannot be kept. You made demands that no creature can meet."
  - **Answer:** The remnant keep all ten commandments (including the Sabbath that most Christendom has set aside). They prove — not in their own strength but in Christ's (Rev 12:11 — "They overcame him by the blood of the Lamb") — that God's law IS keepable by those who receive His grace
  - The law's keepability is demonstrated in the _worst_ conditions of human history. That is the decisive vindication of God's government

- **Justice-vindication coda (Way Home Study 1's original Charge 4, about God's judgment):** "If God destroys the wicked, He is a tyrant no better than the devil."
  - **Answer:** The saints see every record during the millennium (Rev 20:4). Every decision is reviewed. When the fire finally descends (Rev 20:9), it is not surprise but consent: "True and righteous are thy judgments" (Rev 16:7; 19:2). The universe — including those about to be destroyed (Phil 2:10-11) — acknowledges God's justice before the second death executes

- [→] The cosmic trial is not left hanging at the end of Revelation. It is explicitly resolved. God's character is vindicated. Satan's charges collapse. The universe, at the end of the millennium, can say with Paul: "there shall in no wise enter into [the New Jerusalem] any thing that defileth" (Rev 21:27) — because the universe is safe forever

## Threads Recap

Through the 20 studies you have seen each of the seven canonical `[⟲]` threads appear, disappear, and return across the prophecies. Briefly summarized:

- **`[⟲]` Judgment thread:**
  Dan 7:9-14 → Rev 4-5 → Rev 11:15-19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 20:11-12
  _From the Ancient of Days' court opening (Dan 7) to the great white throne closing the books (Rev 20). Rev 19:11-16 is the public execution of the judgment; the books remain the thread's final link._

- **`[⟲]` 1260-year thread:**
  Dan 7:25 → Rev 11:2-3 → Rev 12:6, 14 → Rev 13:5
  _The same medieval-persecution period traced from multiple angles: the little horn, the outer court, the sackcloth witnesses, the wilderness woman, the beast's authority. AD 538 to 1798. (Dan 12:7 is the registry's associated reference for the sealed-book scene.)_

- **`[⟲]` Sanctuary thread:**
  Dan 8:11-14 → Heb 8-9 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 15:5-8
  _The heavenly sanctuary and its priestly ministry; Rev 21:22 is the eschatological fulfillment (no temple in the city because God and the Lamb are the temple)._

- **`[⟲]` Beasts/kingdoms thread:**
  Dan 2 metals → Dan 7 beasts → Dan 8 ram/goat → Rev 13 composite → Rev 17 harlot
  _World powers from Babylon to the final Babylonian system, traced through increasing resolution._

- **`[⟲]` Remnant/seal thread:**
  Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4
  _God's people identified, sealed, marked out, and finally seeing God's face with His name written in their foreheads._

- **`[⟲]` Babylon thread:**
  Dan 2, 4-5 (literal Babylon) → Rev 14:8 (fall announced) → Rev 17-18 (spiritual Babylon fully exposed and destroyed)
  _Scripture's enduring symbol of anti-God imperial religion — from Nebuchadnezzar's city to the final harlot-system._

- **`[⟲]` Michael thread:**
  Dan 10:13 → Dan 10:21 → Dan 12:1 → Jude 9 → Rev 12:7
  _The Warrior-Prince of the saints — in the historicist/SDA reading, the pre-incarnate Christ — standing up for His people at every decisive moment of prophetic history. Five named textual appearances._

[DYK🔎] You have _not_ been asked to memorize these threads. They have been pointed out as they appeared, so that by now they are part of your furniture — not as isolated facts to recite but as recognizable patterns you notice when you re-read the text. That is the mature fruit of the historicist method: not clever decoding but the ability to see how God's prophetic word speaks with one voice across centuries.

## What the Reader Does Now

Rev 1:3's blessing was on three actions: _read, hear, keep_.

- **Read** — you have done the reading. Twenty-one studies have walked Daniel and Revelation together from Nebuchadnezzar's dream to the new Jerusalem. You have seen the architecture, the chronology, the threads, the identifications
- **Hear** — you have heard what Revelation says, not what others say Revelation says. You have traced the prophetic math, the sanctuary pattern, the mark-and-seal logic. You have been given the text's own voice
- **Keep** — the third action is the one that has not yet happened. Keeping is the test

- **What does keeping look like?**
  - Accepting the everlasting gospel (Rev 14:6) — salvation by grace alone through faith in Christ alone
  - Worshiping the Creator (Rev 14:7) — on the day He appointed for that purpose, not on the day the beast substituted
  - Coming out of Babylon (Rev 18:4) — making a conscious decision to separate from religious systems that have accepted the beast's authority pattern
  - Keeping the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus (Rev 14:12) — whole-Bible obedience, rooted in Christ's finished work
  - Having the testimony of Jesus (Rev 19:10) — receiving and carrying forward the prophetic witness to Christ's present ministry and coming return
  - Watching and keeping your garments (Rev 16:15) — daily discipleship, daily choice, daily trust

- [→] The book has taught you. The question is not whether you now _understand_ Revelation. It is whether what you understand has become the shape of your life.

## The Final Appeal

"He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus." (Rev 22:20)

- **Christ's last recorded words**
  - "Surely I come quickly" — a promise, unconditional, without date but without doubt
  - No postponement; no escape clause; no spiritualized near-coming. A literal, physical, visible return

- **The church's last recorded response**
  - "Even so, come, Lord Jesus" — one of the earliest Christian prayers/formulas (the Aramaic _Maranatha_ = "Our Lord, come" appears at 1 Cor 16:22, preserved untranslated by Paul)
  - Not a demand, not a threat; a welcome. The bride saying _yes_ to the Bridegroom
  - Every generation of faithful Christians has prayed this prayer. The last generation prays it knowing it will be answered

- **The benediction** (Rev 22:21)
  - "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen"
  - The Bible's final benediction is _grace_
  - Every threat, every beast, every plague, every fire — and the Bible still closes with _grace_. The final note is mercy, not wrath

## Key Takeaways

- Revelation contains _seven_ beatitudes (Rev 1:3; 14:13; 16:15; 19:9; 20:6; 22:7; 22:14) — a deliberate sevenfold blessing marking the progression from reader to citizen of the New Jerusalem
- Three explicit "Comes" in Rev 22:17 (Spirit+bride; hearer; thirsty) expand into a universal offer of the water of life
- Two destinies: tree of life (Rev 22:14) or lake of fire (Rev 20:14-15; 21:8). No third option
- The cosmic trial's charges have been explicitly answered: loyalty under pressure (Rev 14:12), law-keeping in Christ's power, justice publicly vindicated during the millennium
- Seven threads have been traced across the 20 studies: Judgment, 1260-year, Sanctuary, Beasts/kingdoms, Remnant/seal, Babylon, Michael
- Rev 1:3's blessing is on _read, hear, keep_ — the third is the test still open for each reader
- "What keeping looks like": gospel acceptance; Creator-worship on His day; coming out of Babylon; commandment-keeping in Christ's grace; testimony of Jesus; daily watching
- Christ's last recorded words: "Surely I come quickly"
- The last recorded human response in Scripture: "Even so, come, Lord Jesus"
- The Bible's final benediction: _grace_
