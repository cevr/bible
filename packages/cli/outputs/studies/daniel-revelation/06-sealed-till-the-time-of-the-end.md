# Study 6: Sealed Till the Time of the End — Daniel 12

**Thirteen verses. Michael stands up. A time of trouble unlike any in history. A resurrection — partial, strangely split. Two numbers given nowhere else in Scripture: 1290 and 1335 days. And a book, sealed "to the time of the end," that will be opened again in Revelation 10. Daniel is old. This is his final vision. He does not understand the time. He asks. And the man clothed in linen answers him three times, each time more definitely. Then He tells the aged prophet: "Go thy way, Daniel... thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days." Daniel's book closes here. Revelation will pick up the thread.**

## Prep Reading

- Daniel 12 (entire chapter)
- Matthew 27:52-53
- Revelation 10 (preview of where the sealed book reopens)
- Revelation 12:7-9
- 1 Thessalonians 4:16
- Isaiah 26:19

## Michael Stands Up

Dan 11 ended with the willful king falling between the seas at the glorious holy mountain, with none to help him. Dan 12 picks up immediately — no new date, no new setting. It is the same vision continuing.

- **"And at that time shall Michael stand up"** (Dan 12:1)
  - "At that time" ties Dan 12 directly to Dan 11:45 — the fall of the northern power is the signal for Michael's standing up
  - "Michael" — we met Him in Dan 10 as the one who came to help the angelic messenger; now He acts personally. Historicist reading: the pre-incarnate Christ (Study 5)
  - "Stand up" (Heb. _amad_) in this context signals a change of posture from ministry to action. Within the Dan 12 sequence, Michael's standing up triggers the time of trouble and the deliverance — it is not more intercession but the move _beyond_ intercession to executive deliverance. Christ's priestly/intercessory ministry is grounded in Heb 7:25, 8:1-2, 9:24; Heb 10:12's "sat down" marks the enthronement of the completed sacrifice
  - [→] Read against Lev 16 (the high priest emerging from the Most Holy Place when atonement is complete) and Rev 22:11 ("he that is unjust, let him be unjust still... he that is righteous, let him be righteous still" — probation fixed), Michael's standing up in Dan 12:1 marks the close of the mediatorial phase. Rev 19:11-16 pictures what follows: Christ rides forth. The sanctuary is empty. The judgment's verdicts are executed.
  - `[⟲]` **Michael thread:** Dan 10:13 → Dan 10:21 → Dan 12:1 (this link) → Jude 9 → Rev 12:7. The Warrior-Prince's third textual appearance — and the decisive one, where He _stands up_ to close His mediatorial phase.

- **"A time of trouble, such as never was"** (Dan 12:1)
  - The phrase is unmatched in Scripture — nothing before, nothing up to that point, equals it
  - Jesus echoes this verse directly: "For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be" (Matt 24:21). Matt 24 has both a near-term reference (AD 70) and a far-term reference (the end); historicists read Jesus' unmatched-tribulation language as reaching beyond AD 70 to the final crisis — Dan 12:1's "such as never was" belongs with the far horizon
  - This is the period Rev 16 describes as the seven last plagues, and Jer 30:7 calls "the time of Jacob's trouble"
  - [→] The time of trouble follows Michael's standing up. The order matters. Probation closes _before_ the trouble begins. The sealed saints (Rev 7:1-3) go through the trouble, but they are already sealed when it starts.

- **"Thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book"** (Dan 12:1)
  - Book = the book of life (Ex 32:32-33; Mal 3:16-17; Rev 20:12-15)
  - Deliverance is by name, not by category — each one found in the book
  - `[⟲]` **Remnant/seal thread:** Dan 12:1 → Rev 7:1-4 → Rev 12:17 → Rev 14:1, 12 → Rev 22:4. The named and sealed remnant thread, beginning here.

## A Partial Resurrection

Dan 12:2 is one of the clearest OT statements of bodily resurrection — and also one of the strangest.

- **The text**
  - "And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt" (Dan 12:2)
  - _Many_ — not all. This is key. The general resurrection of all the dead comes later (Rev 20:5-6, 12-13)
  - _Both_ righteous and wicked in the same resurrection — but the wicked to "shame and everlasting contempt"

- **Historicist reading: a special resurrection at Christ's return**
  - Most in this tradition connect Dan 12:2 with Rev 1:7 — "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and _they also which pierced him_"
  - Those who pierced Christ are long dead by the Second Coming. For them to see Him return requires a special resurrection _before_ the general resurrection. Dan 12:2 accounts for this: some righteous are raised to meet the Lord, some wicked are raised to witness His appearing.
  - Matt 27:52-53 gives a precedent: at the crucifixion, "many bodies of the saints which slept arose, and came out of the graves after his resurrection, and went into the holy city, and appeared unto many." A _partial_ resurrection of saints on a specific prophetic occasion.
  - [→] Historicist interpreters infer: at the Second Coming, before the general resurrection of Rev 20, a particular group is raised — some righteous (including saints who preached through persecution) and some wicked (including those complicit in Christ's death, fulfilling Rev 1:7's "they also which pierced him"). The text itself names the two destinies ("everlasting life" / "shame and everlasting contempt") but does not itemize which individuals fall in which group — that is interpretive extrapolation, not an exegetical given.

- **Alternative reading:** some interpreters read Dan 12:2 as a Hebrew idiom for the full resurrection, with "many" standing in for "the multitude." This is a possible reading of the Hebrew but does not account for Rev 1:7's requirement of an earlier resurrection of Christ's piercers

- **Either way**, Dan 12:2 is a clear OT witness to:
  - Bodily resurrection of both righteous and wicked (cf. John 5:28-29; Acts 24:15)
  - Two eternal destinies (eternal life / shame and contempt)
  - God's power to raise the dead — stated before Easter, against Sadducee skepticism (Matt 22:29-32)

## Those Who Are Wise

- **The wise shine** (Dan 12:3)
  - "They that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever"
  - The wise = _maskilim_ in Hebrew — those who understand, who teach, who turn others to God
  - Two groups: those who are wise (inward understanding), and those who turn many to righteousness (outward fruit). The same people doing both things.
  - Rev 14 will use this image: "a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand" (Rev 14:1) — the sealed ones who understood and turned many
  - [→] This is not reserved for professionals. "Many" are wise, and their wisdom is measured by how many they bring to righteousness. That is the exam.

- **Why wisdom matters at the end** (Dan 12:10)
  - "Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand"
  - _Understanding_ prophetically is a moral condition, not just an intellectual one. The wicked are not simply ignorant — they are unable to understand, because they refuse to obey (cf. John 7:17 — "If any man will do his will, he shall know of the doctrine")
  - [→] The same text stands before two readers. One reads and is purified. The other reads and confirms his own rebellion. The difference is not the text.

## "Shut Up the Words"

- **The instruction** (Dan 12:4)
  - "But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end"
  - "Seal" (Heb. _chatham_) = to close, ratify, authenticate — as a sealed document whose contents are kept from general reading until authorized
  - _The time of the end_ — not the end itself, but the era leading up to it

- **"Many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased"** (Dan 12:4)
  - Historical readings have ranged widely: increased travel and education in the modern era (19th century forward); or specifically, prophetic students "running to and fro" through the Scriptures in search of understanding once the seal lifts
  - The latter fits the immediate context: the book itself is what has been sealed; the ones running to and fro are those searching it out
  - [→] The prophecy that closes Daniel's book is its own commentary on what happens when the book reopens — a generation will seek its meaning, and knowledge will increase. Historicists identify the early 19th-century Advent awakening (Miller, Wolff, Lacunza, Irving — Study 3) with exactly this. Once the seal breaks, the running to and fro begins.

## "How Long?"

Dan 12:5-7 is the most formal oath in the Bible outside of God's covenant with Abraham.

- **The scene** (Dan 12:5-6)
  - Daniel sees two others — "the one on this side of the bank of the river, and the other on that side"
  - The man clothed in linen (same figure from Dan 10, Day of Atonement high priest) stands "upon the waters of the river"
  - One of the two asks: "How long shall it be to the end of these wonders?"

- **The oath** (Dan 12:7)
  - "He held up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven" — a double-handed oath, the most solemn form, used elsewhere only by God Himself (cf. Deut 32:40)
  - "And sware by him that liveth for ever that it shall be for a time, times, and an half"
  - "And when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people, all these things shall be finished"

- **What is being sworn to?**
  - "A time, times, and an half" = three-and-a-half times = 1260 days = 1260 years (by the year-day principle, Study 3)
  - `[⟲]` **1260 years thread:** Dan 7:25 (first) → Dan 12:7 (oath) → Rev 11:2, 11:3, 12:6, 12:14, 13:5. Seven witnesses now — Dan 12:7 is the formal confirmation under oath of the period first given in Dan 7:25
  - [→] The two-handed, sworn-by-the-Eternal oath is God personally ratifying the 1260-year period. The same period Dan 7 mentioned casually is here set under the strongest possible guarantee. This is not a number historicists invented. It is a number God Himself sealed with an oath.

- **"To scatter the power of the holy people"**
  - The 1260 years is defined as the period during which the papal power "scatters" (breaks, disperses) the authority of God's people
  - Historically: AD 538 (Justinian's decree recognizing the papal primacy in the West, the Ostrogoths driven out of Rome) → AD 1798 (French general Berthier captures Pope Pius VI and removes him from Rome). Dan 7's "wear out the saints" meets Dan 12's "scatter the power of the holy people" at this exact span.

## 1290 and 1335 Days

These two numbers appear nowhere else in Scripture.

- **The 1290** (Dan 12:11)
  - "And from the time that the daily shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days"
  - 1290 prophetic days = 1290 years (year-day)
  - Starting point: when the daily is taken away and the abomination set up — tied to Dan 8:11-13, Dan 11:31. Historicists have traditionally placed this start at **AD 508** — the period of Clovis' Catholic military consolidation in the West, specifically the defeat of Arian opposition (the Visigoths at Vouillé, 507; his recognition by Emperor Anastasius soon after), marking a step in the rise of the papal-political fusion. 508 + 1290 = AD 1798, the same terminus as the 1260
  - The 30-year difference (1290 vs. 1260) = two different starting points for related events. The 1260 starts at AD 538 (papal supremacy formal); the 1290 starts at AD 508 (the transition phase that prepared it)

- **The 1335** (Dan 12:12)
  - "Blessed is he that waiteth, and cometh to the thousand three hundred and five and thirty days"
  - 1335 - 1290 = 45-year extension. 45 years from AD 1798 = **AD 1843**
  - Historicist reading: the Advent awakening (which preached the end in 1843/1844) is the "blessed" movement Dan 12:12 describes — those who waited through the 1335 and reached the era of the sealed book's opening
  - The "blessing" is for those who reach this moment, whether they understand every detail or not. The Millerites' 1843-1844 calculations were imperfect (they miscalculated by one year initially), but their _waiting on the prophecy_ put them in the blessed position Dan 12:12 named

- **Caveat:** These secondary dates (AD 508, AD 1798, AD 1843) are held less tightly than the 70-weeks anchor dates (Study 3). They represent the best historicist synthesis, not decrees of Scripture. We hold what the text requires — a period of papal dominance ending around the turn of the 19th century, followed by an Advent awakening — with confidence; the precise year-anchors are held with the confidence they have earned.

[DYK🔎] The three numbers 1260, 1290, 1335 all converge on the same window — late 18th to mid-19th century. The 1260 (Dan 7:25; Rev 12, 13) ends at 1798 with Napoleon's general Berthier deposing Pius VI. The 1290 (Dan 12:11) ends at the same year, 1798, starting 30 years earlier at AD 508. The 1335 (Dan 12:12) ends at 1843, pointing to the Advent awakening that preceded 1844's heavenly Day of Atonement. Three independent prophetic numbers converging on the same historical moment is not an accident of interpretation. It is the prophecy insisting on its own identification of the era of its unsealing.

## The Aged Prophet's Last Question — And the Answer

- **"I heard, but I understood not"** (Dan 12:8)
  - Daniel is honest. Ninety years old. A lifetime of visions behind him. And he still does not grasp the timing.
  - "O my Lord, what shall be the end of these things?" — not _when_ (he has three numbers), but _what_ — what does the end look like?

- **"Go thy way, Daniel"** (Dan 12:9)
  - "The words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end"
  - Daniel will not live to see the unsealing. He will rest. He will wait. The book goes to other readers — generations later — who will open it when the time comes.
  - [→] The prophet serving God across seventy years in Babylon does not get the answer. The grace here is not in the information; it is in the trust. Daniel dies without fully understanding, and God tells him that is fine. The book will open when it should.

- **"But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days"** (Dan 12:13)
  - Three movements: _go_, _rest_, _stand_. Life, death, resurrection.
  - "Stand in thy lot" = receive your portion in the inheritance, at the final distribution of the kingdom
  - [→] Daniel's closing promise is not that he will understand every prophetic detail. It is that he will _be there_ when the kingdom is given. The same promise covers every faithful reader who closes the book without every question answered.

## Bridging to Revelation

Dan 12 does not close the prophetic story. It hands the thread forward. Every image in the final verses will be picked up by John.

## The Inverse Commands

- **Dan 12:4** — "Shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end"
- **Rev 22:10** — "Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand"

Two books, two commands, two eras.

- Daniel's book is sealed until the time of the end
- Revelation is _commanded_ open, from the moment it is given, because the time is at hand
- The sealing of one matches the opening of the other. When the seal lifts on Daniel (at "the time of the end"), Revelation is already open and waiting
- [→] Historicist reading: the unsealing of Daniel happens precisely when Revelation's "little book open in the angel's hand" (Rev 10:2) is presented to the church — i.e., the Advent awakening leading to 1844. From that moment forward, Daniel and Revelation are meant to be read together, as one book, in the final generation. Which is what this series is attempting.

## Key Takeaways

- Dan 12 picks up directly from Dan 11:45 — Michael stands up, the final hinge begins
- Michael's "standing up" = the close of Christ's mediatorial/intercessory priestly work (cf. Heb 10:12's "sat down" implies standing is the opposite move)
- The "time of trouble, such as never was" (Dan 12:1) is echoed by Jesus in Matt 24:21 — historicists read His unmatched-tribulation wording as reaching past AD 70 to the final crisis
- Dan 12:2's partial resurrection "some... some" fits with Rev 1:7's requirement that Christ's piercers see Him return — a special resurrection at the Second Coming, before the general one of Rev 20
- The wise = those who understand _and_ turn many to righteousness (Dan 12:3); understanding prophecy is a moral condition (Dan 12:10)
- The 1260-year period is here sworn under God's most solemn oath (Dan 12:7) — the seventh witness in the `[⟲]` 1260-years chain
- The 1290 (Dan 12:11) starts at AD 508; the 1335 (Dan 12:12) ends at AD 1843 (Advent awakening); held as historicist synthesis, with the confidence they earn
- Daniel dies without fully understanding (Dan 12:8-9, 13); the book is sealed to his era but opens at the time of the end
- Dan 12:4's sealing + Rev 22:10's "seal not" = inverse commands across two eras; the final generation gets both books unsealed together
