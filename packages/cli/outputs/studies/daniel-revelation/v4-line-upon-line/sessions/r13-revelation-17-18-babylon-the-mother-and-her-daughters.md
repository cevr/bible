# Revelation 17-18 — Babylon the Mother and Her Daughters

**The same God who handed us the dictionary at Daniel's "great sea" now opens the casebook
on the harlot who sits on those waters — and every label is read off the page: waters ARE
peoples, seven heads ARE governments, ten horns ARE kings, the woman IS "that great city."
Babylon is not one fallen church but a family — the mother on her scarlet beast and her
Protestant daughters — and against her the last call of God thunders: "Come out of her, my
people."**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Revelation 17 hands
> us four entries in its own words — waters, woman, seven heads, ten horns — and we read
> each one off the page, line upon line.

---

## The setup — the casebook of the church, parallel to Daniel's casebook of the nations

- John is carried into the wilderness and shown **a woman on a scarlet beast** (Rev 17:3)
  — the angel promises to show _"the judgment of the great whore"_ (Rev 17:1)
- [→] Revelation 17 is **ECCLESIASTICAL history** — the complement to Daniel's **NATIONAL**
  history. Daniel drew the nations; John draws the church. Read together, one is the
  complement of the other.

> "The prophet Daniel wrote the history of the world from the standpoint of nations... the
> history presented to John on the isle of Patmos was primarily an ecclesiastical history."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 289_

- [→] frame the chapter's task: before the woman means anything, **the symbols must** — and
  the Bible defines its own.

---

## [KEY🔑] First entry: the WATERS the harlot sits on = peoples, nations, tongues

The angel points to the waters where the whore sitteth (Rev 17:1, 15) — and Scripture
defines the symbol outright:

> "And he saith unto me, The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are
> peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." (Revelation 17:15)

- [KEY🔑] **waters = peoples** — the verse hands us the dictionary, no guessing.
- ★ **THE PAYOFF MOMENT:** this is the very verse the reader has leaned on the whole series.
  Back at Daniel 7's "great sea" (D07), back at Revelation 13's beast "from the sea" (R10),
  we used Rev 17:15 to decode it. **NOW we stand at the verse itself and read it in its
  home.**

> "In verse 15 we have a plain definition of the Scripture symbol of waters; they denote
> peoples, multitudes, nations, and tongues."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 662_

- [→] so the harlot "sitting on many waters" = **a religious power riding on, and
  controlling, the multitudes of earth.**

---

## [KEY🔑] The WOMAN = "that great city" — and the harlot is the OPPOSITE of the pure woman

The woman is arrayed in purple and scarlet, named in her forehead MYSTERY, BABYLON THE
GREAT (Rev 17:4-5). And the angel defines her too:

> "And the woman which thou sawest is that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the
> earth." (Revelation 17:18)

- [KEY🔑] **woman = "that great city"** that reigns over the kings — the verse defines its
  own symbol.
- [⟲] **WEAVE — same or different?** This harlot is **NOT** the same woman as Revelation 12
  (R09). She is the **ANTITHESIS**. A pure woman = the church of Christ; a prostitute =
  that church turned from her husband to commit adultery with the kings of earth.
  **DIFFERENT figures — true church vs. apostate church.**

> "A pure woman represents the church of Christ; a prostitute represents this church when
> it turns from its lawful husband, and commits adultery with the kings of the earth."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 291_

- [→] the seven churches (Rev 2-3) traced the decline from the chaste virgin to the harlot —
  paganism walked bodily into the church (SSP 292).

> "That this apostate woman, as presented in this chapter, is a symbol of the Roman
> Catholic Church, is generally believed by Protestants... with the wine of her
> fornication, or her false doctrines, the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 657_

---

## [→] The woman ON the beast — church distinguished from state

- [→] this prophecy is **sharper than the others on Rome** because it distinguishes church
  from state: the woman (the church) **rides** the scarlet beast (the civil power) she
  controls and guides to her own ends.

> "We here have the woman, the church, seated upon a scarlet-colored beast, the civil
> power, by which she is upheld, and which she controls and guides to her own ends, as a
> rider controls the animal upon which he is seated."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 657_

- [→] SSP 296-297 dates the mounting: between **A.D. 533** (Justinian's decree) and **538**
  (the last rival power removed in Italy), "the woman mounted the beast."

---

## [⟲] The scarlet BEAST that carries her = the persecuting power re-met

The beast is scarlet, full of names of blasphemy, with seven heads and ten horns (Rev
17:3). Note: Rev 17:3 **describes** the beast — it does not define it. Its identity is the
historicist reading the guides establish, so we carry it as a **weave**, not a bare key.

- [⟲] **WEAVE — same or different?** **SAME power.** The scarlet beast of Revelation 17 is
  the same Roman power as the sea-beast of Revelation 13 (R10) and the fourth beast of
  Daniel 7 (D07) — traced continuously, not recapitulated as a new beast.

> "The beast had seven heads and ten horns, identifying it with the government of the
> Western Roman Empire, described in the thirteenth chapter of Revelation and in the
> seventh chapter of Daniel."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 296_

- [⟲] Daniel 7:8, 20-21 — the blasphemous, saint-warring little-horn power **IS** the power
  that carries Babylon. _"Full of names of blasphemy"_ (Rev 17:3) echoes Dan 7:25's _"great
  words against the most High."_

> "A wild beast, like the one thus introduced, is the symbol of an oppressive and
> persecuting power..."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 659_

---

## [→] The beast "that was, is not, and yet is" = three phases of Rome

> "The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit,
> and go into perdition... and yet is." (Revelation 17:8)

This is an overlay reading — we read it through the guides, who name the three faces:

> "Thus Rome in its pagan form was a persecuting power in its relation to the people of God,
> during which time it constituted the beast that was... and during a brief period, while
> this transition was going on, it lost its ferocious and persecuting character, and then
> it could be said of the beast that it was not. Time passed on, and it degenerated into
> popery, and again assumed its bloodthirsty and oppressive character, and then it
> constituted the beast that 'yet is.'"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 659_

And the prophetic witness names the transformation plainly:

> "during the papacy, the seventh, it disappeared to all outward appearances, but was
> nevertheless the controlling power; for the papacy is baptized paganism."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 298_

- [→] resolve: **same power, three faces** — the persecuting principle never died, it
  changed clothes.

---

## [KEY🔑] The SEVEN HEADS = seven mountains / forms of government

The angel defines the heads in his own words:

> "And here is the mind which hath wisdom. The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the
> woman sitteth." (Revelation 17:9)

- [KEY🔑] **the verse itself names the heads as mountains** — and the Bible elsewhere uses
  "mountains" for governments (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Zechariah, per SSP 296).
- [→] OVERLAY layer (the guides read the mountains as the seven forms of Roman government):
  kingly, consular, decemvirate, dictatorial, triumvirate, imperial, and papal.

> "The seven forms of government that have existed in the Roman empire are usually
> enumerated as follows: (1) kingly; (2) consular; (3) decemvirate; (4) dictatorial; (5)
> triumvirate; (6) imperial; and (7) papal... while the last, which is usually denominated
> the seventh, is in reality the eighth."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 659_

[DYK🔎] The verse hands us "mountains"; Scripture supplies "mountains = governments." We
read the label first, then history fills the seven seats.

---

## [KEY🔑] The TEN HORNS = ten kings — the SAME ten of Daniel 7, who turn on the harlot

Again the angel defines his own symbol:

> "And the ten horns which thou sawest are ten kings, which have received no kingdom as yet;
> but receive power as kings one hour with the beast." (Revelation 17:12)

- [KEY🔑] **ten horns = ten kings** — defined in the text.
- [⟲] **WEAVE — same or different?** **SAME** as Daniel 7:7-8's ten horns = the ten
  kingdoms of divided Rome (= the iron-and-clay of Dan 2:42-44). DAR explicitly
  cross-references Daniel 7:8; SSP ties them to the divisions formed before A.D. 476.

> "On this subject, see remarks on Daniel 7:8, where they are shown to represent the ten
> kingdoms that arose out of the Roman empire."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 660_

> "These are the ten divisions of the Roman Empire, prophesied of in the eighth chapter of
> Revelation, and symbolized by the mixture of iron and clay in the image of Daniel
> 2:42-44... The ten divisions were formed before a. d. 476."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 296_

- [⟲] **the FUTURE dimension the verse adds:** these same horn-powers _"shall hate the
  whore, and shall make her desolate and naked... and burn her with fire"_ (Rev 17:16) —
  the kingdoms that once **UPHELD** the papacy finally **turn and destroy** her.

> "This language must refer to the past, when the kingdoms of Europe were unanimous in
> giving their support to the papacy... the treatment which these kingdoms are finally to
> bestow upon the papacy, is expressed in verse 16, where it is said that they shall hate
> the harlot, make her desolate and naked, eat her flesh, and burn her with fire."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 661_

## [→] The 1260 years — the woman rode the beast, drunk with the blood of saints

- [→] OVERLAY: for the 1260 years (A.D. 538-1798) the whore controlled the nations of
  Europe from her seven-hilled capital.

> "For twelve hundred and sixty years the whore, from her capital at Rome, the seven hilled
> city, controlled the nations of Europe... The woman (was) drunken with the blood of the
> saints, and with the blood of the martyrs of Jesus."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 293-294_

- [⟲] **WEAVE — same or different?** **SAME span** as Daniel 7:25 (time, times, dividing of
  time), Revelation 12:6/14, Revelation 13:5 (42 months) — **one period, many wordings.**
  The reader has met this 1260 at R08, R09, R10.
- [→] this dates the woman's reign on the beast — **538 to 1798** — the same span the whole
  series has tracked.

## [KEY🔑/→] Babylon is a FAMILY — the mother and her harlot daughters

The woman is named **"MOTHER OF HARLOTS"** (Rev 17:5) — the title itself proves she is not
alone:

> "This woman is explicitly called Babylon. Is Rome, then, Babylon, to the exclusion of all
> other religious bodies? — No, from the fact that she is called the mother of harlots, as
> already noticed, which shows that there are other independent religious organizations that
> constitute the apostate daughters, and belong to the same great family."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 658_

- [⟲] **WEAVE — same or different?** **RECAPITULATED.** Revelation 17 ("Babylon the Mother")
  = Roman Catholicism primarily; Revelation 18 ("Babylon the Daughters") = apostate
  Protestantism primarily. The **NAME** carries across both chapters; the **EMPHASIS**
  shifts from the mother institution to her Protestant offspring.

> "This chapter has, naturally, more especial reference to the old mother, or Catholic
> Babylon. The next chapter, if we mistake not, deals with the character and destiny of
> another great branch of Babylon, the harlot daughters."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 662_

> "By her daughters must be symbolized churches that cling to her doctrines and traditions,
> and follow her example of sacrificing the truth and the approval of God, in order to form
> an unlawful alliance with the world... it cannot refer to the Roman Church alone, for that
> church has been in a fallen condition for many centuries."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 382_

- [→] the daughters started in reform, ran well, then fenced themselves with creeds and
  failed to keep pace with advancing light (DAR 664) — **Babylon = confusion** (Babel), not
  mere reproach (DAR 666).

## [→] The FALL of Babylon is MORAL, not literal — completed by Spiritualism

> "And he cried mightily with a strong voice, saying, Babylon the great is fallen, is
> fallen, and is become the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit..."
> (Revelation 18:2)

This is an overlay reading — the guides show the fall is moral, not yet literal:

> "The fall of Babylon here spoken of cannot be literal destruction... The fall is
> therefore a moral one; for the result of it is that Babylon becomes the habitation of
> devils, and the hold of every foul spirit, and a cage of every unclean and hateful bird."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 663_

- [→] the moral fall is accomplished in the daughters by their rejection of the three
  angels' messages (R11) after 1844, and **COMPLETED** by the infiltration of Spiritualism.

> "The churches that reject the message of the judgment and the Saviour's second coming,
> reject the Spirit of God, and are given over to the control of evil spirits, a miracle
> working power."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 309_

> "As the teachings of spiritualism are accepted by the churches, the restraint imposed
> upon the carnal heart is removed... the influence of evil angels will be felt in the
> churches."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 603_

- [→] the daughters form the **"image of the beast"** by using the civil arm to persecute
  (DAR 673) — the family resemblance to the mother.
- [DYK🔎] sorcery / Spiritualism is named as **Babylon's leading crime** (DAR 679).

## [→] The loud cry — a fifth messenger; the earth lightened with glory

> "And after these things I saw another angel come down from heaven, having great power; and
> the earth was lightened with his glory." (Revelation 18:1)

An overlay reading — the guides identify this descending angel as the **"loud cry,"** a
final amplification of the third angel's message. DAR now counts **FIVE** celestial
messengers engaged in the last reformation (the three angels of Rev 14 + the angel of Rev
18:1 + the "voice" of Rev 18:4):

> "We now have five celestial messengers expressly mentioned as engaged in this last
> religious reformation. These are the first, second, and third angels of chapter 14;
> fourth, the angel of verse one of this chapter; and fifth, the agency indicated by the
> 'voice' of verse 4, now before us."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 671_

> "Then I saw another mighty angel commissioned to descend to the earth, to unite his voice
> with the third angel, and give power and force to his message... the earth was lightened
> with his glory."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 277_

- [→] DAR 674: the first part of Revelation 18 has special reference to the religious
  oppression to be developed in the United States.

## [→] "Come out of her, my people" — the final call before the plagues

> "And I heard another voice from heaven, saying, Come out of her, my people, that ye be not
> partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues." (Revelation 18:4)

This is a **CALL / command**, not a self-defining symbol verse — so we carry it as a
teaching connector, with the guides supplying its force:

> "As the angels took Lot by the hands, and hurried him out of Sodom, bidding him not to
> look back, so angels will hurry the sincere and true-hearted out of Babylon, for her
> destruction cometh like the burning of Sodom."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 311_

> "But God still has a people in Babylon; and before the visitation of His judgments these
> faithful ones must be called out... These announcements, uniting with the third angel's
> message, constitute the final warning to be given to the inhabitants of the earth."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 604_

> "Come out of her, My people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not
> of her plagues... This message seemed to be an addition to the third message."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 277_

- [→] GC 390: Revelation 18 is the **last message that will ever be given to the world** — it
  WILL accomplish its work.
- [⟲] this answers the plagues of R12: God's people are called out **BEFORE** the plagues
  fall — because Babylon's moral fall precedes her literal destruction (DAR 663).

[Q] If the angels take the faithful **by the hand** out of a city already condemned, the
only question left is whether you let your hand be taken.

## Appeal

- The God who labeled His own symbols — **waters ARE peoples, heads ARE governments, horns
  ARE kings, the woman IS that great city** — is the God who now hands you the dictionary's
  most personal entry: _"my people."_
- Babylon is not a building to flee but a fellowship of error to leave. The call is not
  "come out of a place" but **"come out of a position"** — out of confusion, out of the
  creeds that fenced out advancing light.
- The angels take you **BY THE HAND**, as they took Lot out of Sodom (SSP 311). _"Come out
  of her, my people"_ (Rev 18:4). The destruction comes like the burning of Sodom — **do
  not look back.**
- The verdict against the harlot is already written (Rev 17:16; 18:8). The only question is
  which side of the line you stand on when the loud cry sounds. **STAND WITH THE LAMB**, not
  with the kings who drank her wine.

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: BABYLON = a FAMILY — the mother (Rome, Rev 17) on her scarlet
beast, and her daughters (apostate Protestantism, Rev 18) — judged together.** The
waters-key (Rev 17:15) the series leaned on from Daniel 7's "great sea" is now **shown in
its own home**; the scarlet beast is the same Dan 7 / Rev 13 power re-met; the ten horns
are the same Dan 7 ten, now turning to destroy the harlot they once carried. Hold it: the
harlot's destroyers (Rev 17:16) and the plagues (R12) set the stage for the rider who
executes the verdict next.
