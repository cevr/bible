# Revelation 21-22 — The New Jerusalem: the Tree and River of Life

**The book that opened in a garden with a guarded tree of life closes in a city with that
very same tree — its guard withdrawn. At the gate the Bible defines its own symbols one
last time: the bride is the city, the temple is God Himself, and the Alpha is the Omega.
Eden and the new earth clasp hands, and the everlasting kingdom Daniel saw in stone and in
judgment finally arrives — line upon line.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Watch Revelation hand
> us the last entries in the dictionary — bride, temple, river, tree — and watch the whole
> chain land inside the city, not outside the gate.

---

## The setup — the first heaven and the first earth passed away

Two visions fold into one session: Revelation 21 (the holy city descends) and Revelation
22 (the river, the tree, the closing testimony).

> "And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were
> passed away; and there was no more sea." (Revelation 21:1)

- [→] Haskell sets the creation-to-restoration frame: at creation the waters were held
  beneath and the fire was pent within — and both were kept in store for the last day, the
  elements of the old earth's destruction:

> "At creation, the waters filled the earth and there were no rains; but the ground was
> watered from beneath, by a mist which arose... since that time, there has been fire within
> the earth,—the elements of its own destruction, pent up, awaiting the command of Jehovah,
> to perform their appointed work."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 339_

- [→] this is the **destination** Daniel and Revelation have walked toward — not an
  afterthought, but the place the whole chain was headed. Dan 2's stone, Dan 7's everlasting
  kingdom, the sanctuary cleansed in Dan 8 — every thread points here.

[DYK🔎] The book spends two chapters here for a reason: the prophecy that drew the rise and
fall of four empires now draws the **one kingdom that does not fall.** The walk ends where
it always meant to end.

## [→] The third heaven is now literal — paradise and the tree of life

Before the city descends, fix where it is coming **from.** DAR names three heavens, and the
third is no metaphor (a guide overlay, not a verse-key — DAR reasons it from 2 Cor 12:2 and
Rev 2:7):

> "The Bible certainly recognizes three heavens in the present constitution of things;
> namely, the first, or atmospheric heaven, which the fowls of the air inhabit; the second,
> the planetary heaven, the region of the sun, moon, and stars; and the third, high above the
> others, where paradise and the tree of life are found."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 702_

- the **third heaven** — the throne, paradise, the tree of life — is about to come **down to
  earth.** The destination is not vague; it is a place
- the verse describes the move:

> "And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared
> as a bride adorned for her husband." (Revelation 21:2)

- [→] note for the walk: this is **not** a [KEY🔑]. The verse _describes_ the city
  descending — it does not say "X is Y." The Eden-restored reading is the theological frame
  the guides carry; it is set up here and **paid off** at the river and the tree.

## [KEY🔑] The bride, the Lamb's wife — the angel shows John a CITY

Now a verse that **does** define its own symbol. John is promised one thing and shown
another — and the swap is the dictionary entry:

> "Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife. And he carried me away in the
> spirit to a great and high mountain, and shewed me that great city, the holy Jerusalem,
> descending out of heaven from God." (Revelation 21:9-10)

[KEY🔑] **The bride IS the city.** The angel promised the bride and showed the New
Jerusalem — the verse hands us its own definition. The angel does not deceive:

> "The angel told John distinctly that he would show him the bride, the Lamb's wife; and we
> may be sure that he did not practice upon him a piece of deception, but fulfilled his
> promise to the very letter; ... all that he did show him was the New Jerusalem."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 706_

### [⟲] Rev 19:7-9 — the WEAVE where the risk concentrates (DIFFERENT instance)

Here is where careless reading goes wrong. Revelation 19 also speaks of a "wife" — but it
is **not the same referent.** Resolve it: same word, two scenes, two parties.

> "...the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his **wife** hath made herself ready... Blessed
> are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb." (Revelation 19:7-9)

- **Rev 19** calls the righteousness-clothed church the **wife**, and the redeemed the
  **guests** at the marriage supper
- **Rev 21** calls the **CITY** the bride
- [⟲] **DIFFERENT instances — not a contradiction.** DAR resolves it cleanly: the city above
  is the mother, the church are her children, and carrying out the figure, the city is the
  bride and we the guests:

> "Christ is called the Father of his people (Isaiah 9:6); the Jerusalem above is called our
> mother, and we are called the children; and, carrying out the figure of a marriage, Christ
> is represented as the Bridegroom, the city as the bride, and we, the church, as the guests.
> There is no confusion of parties here."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 708_

- the prophetic eye confirms the same parties — guests cannot also be the bride:

> "...the virgins that go out to meet the bridegroom are a symbol of the church. In the
> Revelation the people of God are said to be the guests at the marriage supper. Revelation
> 19:9. If guests, they cannot be represented also as the bride."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 426_

[→] So: Christ the Bridegroom, the **city** the bride, the **church** the guests. The same
word means two things in two scenes — read the scene, and the confusion dissolves.

## [KEY🔑] No temple — the mediatorial work forever past

Another verse defines its own symbol — by telling us what the temple **is** once the city
rests on earth:

> "And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it."
> (Revelation 21:22)

[KEY🔑] **God and the Lamb ARE the temple.** The verse states it outright. And DAR reads
why the building is gone:

> "With the temple is connected the idea of sacrifices and a mediatorial work; but when the
> city is located upon the earth, there will be no such work to be performed. Sacrifices and
> offerings, and all mediatorial work based thereon, will be forever past; hence there will be
> no need of the outward symbol of such work."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 714_

### [⟲] Rev 4-5 / Dan 8:14 — the sanctuary thread closes here

This is where a thread the series has carried since Daniel 8 lands.

- [⟲] the heavenly sanctuary shown in **Rev 4-5**, and the cleansing **dated by Dan 8:14**,
  reach their terminus: the ministry that began in the holy places **ends** — because the
  saved no longer need a mediator
- **SAME throne, SAME sanctuary** — relocated to earth, its mediatorial work complete. The
  cleansing Daniel dated is finished; the Mediator's work is "forever past."

[→] No temple is not a loss — it is the **proof** the work is done. The veil that stood
between is gone because the separation it answered is gone.

## [KEY🔑] The second death and the Lamb's book of life — who enters, who does not

Two verses, each defining a symbol in its own words. First the verdict on the lost — and the
text supplies the meaning right in the sentence:

> "But the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable, and murderers, and whoremongers,
> and sorcerers, and idolaters, and all liars, shall have their part in the lake which
> burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death." (Revelation 21:8)

[KEY🔑] **the lake of fire IS the second death** — the verse defines its own symbol in-text.
No guessing; the words "which is" hand us the entry.

Then the condition of entry — contingent on a register:

> "And there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth... but they which are
> written in the Lamb's book of life." (Revelation 21:27)

### [⟲] Rev 3:5 / 13:8 / 17:8 / 20:12,15 — the SAME book of life

The Lamb's book of life is not new here. Trace it: **the SAME register across every
appearance.**

- **Rev 3:5** — first promised: _"I will not blot out his name out of the book of life"_
- **Rev 13:8 / 17:8** — the names **not** written in it worship the beast
- **Rev 20:12,15** — opened at the millennial judgment; whoever is not found written is cast
  into the lake of fire
- [⟲] **SAME book, every time.** Its final contents were settled by the investigative
  judgment of **Rev 20 — before the city descends in Rev 21.** Entry is not decided at the
  gate; it was decided in the court.

[→] Haskell ties those contents to the close of probation — which is the very next section.

## [→] "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still" — probation closes before He comes

Revelation 22 opens its closing testimony with an irrevocable fiat — each character fixed
"still," and the advent following at once:

> "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy
> still: and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him
> be holy still. And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me, to give every man
> according as his work shall be." (Revelation 22:11-12)

- [→] **OVERLAY** (not a [KEY🔑]) — DAR reads the verse as the close of probation, before the
  coming:

> "Verse 11 proves that probation closes, and the cases of all are unalterably fixed, before
> the coming of Christ; for in the very next verse Christ says, 'Behold, I come quickly.'"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 720_

- [→] **OVERLAY** — Haskell ties the moment to 1844 and the end of the 2300 days:

> "In 1844 prophetic time closed; this was the end of the twenty-three hundred days of
> Daniel 8:14."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 358_

### [⟲] Dan 8:14 — the 1844 / 2300 payoff

- [⟲] the close-of-probation fiat is the **far end** of the investigative judgment Daniel
  dated. The 2300 days reached their start of the judgment in 1844; **when that judgment
  finishes, Christ speaks the words of Rev 22:11**, and the book of life of Rev 21:27 is
  sealed by this verdict
- the reward is brought **with** Him (v.12) — no probation after the advent

[→] The "still" of v.11 and the "quickly" of v.12 sit side by side on purpose: when the
cases are fixed, He comes. The two verses are one event.

## [KEY🔑] The river of life — and its source is God

Now the closing scene of the whole Bible. A verse that describes its own source:

> "And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the
> throne of God and of the Lamb." (Revelation 22:1)

[KEY🔑] the river's source is **self-described** — it proceeds out of the throne of God and
of the Lamb.

- [→] DAR draws the physical layout — throne, street, river, tree, one picture:

> "The glorious throne of God at the head of this broad way, or avenue; out of that throne the
> river of life, flowing lengthwise through the center of the street; and the tree of life
> growing on either side, forming a high and magnificent arch over that majestic stream."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 716_

- [→] Haskell names the source and traces it back to Eden:

> "The source of this river is God,—the fountain, or head waters of all truth; and flowing
> from Him, who is infinite and eternal, it signifies the spread of truth through the earth.
> In Eden that water typified Christ."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 351_

- [→] **OVERLAY** — the broad street runs from the throne toward the four gates, giving the
  whole 375-mile-square city access to the avenue and the river (DAR 717; sourced overlay)
- the prophetic eye confirms the picture:

> "There the heavenly Shepherd leads His flock to fountains of living waters... There are
> ever-flowing streams, clear as crystal, and beside them waving trees cast their shadows upon
> the paths prepared for the ransomed of the Lord."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 675_

## [KEY🔑] The tree of life — ONE tree, both banks, the SAME tree from Eden

> "In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the tree of
> life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the
> leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations." (Revelation 22:2)

How can **one** tree stand on **both** sides of the river? DAR reasons it straight from the
text:

> "But how can the tree of life be but one tree, and still be on either side of the river?...
> From Genesis to Revelation it is spoken of as but one — the tree of life... To be at once on
> both sides of the river, it must have more than one trunk, in which case it must be united
> at the top or in its upper branches, in order to form but one tree."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 717_

### [⟲] Gen 2:9 / 3:24 / Rev 2:7 — the SAME tree, traced (the title weave)

This is the title weave, and the verdict is **SAME instance.** Trace it:

- **Gen 2:9** — the tree of life stands in the midst of Eden
- **Gen 3:24** — after the fall, a flaming sword guards the way to it. The tree is fenced off
- **Rev 2:7** — overcomers are **PROMISED** _"to eat of the tree of life, which is in the
  midst of the paradise of God"_ — already in heaven, awaiting them
- **Rev 22:2** — the **FULFILLMENT** on the new earth

Haskell supplies the chain that links garden to city — the **same** Eden tree, transported
and restored:

> "Before the destruction of the earth by water, the garden was transported to heaven."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 351_

> "The real tree of Eden was transported to heaven... It will blossom in reality in the new
> earth, bearing its fruit every month... All the war and strife of nations have come
> because man ate not of the fruit of the tree of life."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 352_

And the tree's deepest meaning — it is a person:

> "Christ is the tree of life, the bread of life and the water of life."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 353_

### [⟲] EW 17 — the SAME picture confirmed in vision

The prophetic eye saw exactly what DAR reasoned from the verse — two trunks, one tree:

> "On one side of the river was a trunk of a tree, and a trunk on the other side of the
> river... I looked again, and saw that they were united at the top in one tree. So it was the
> tree of life on either side of the river of life."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 17_

[→] DAR reasoned it from the words; Haskell traced it from Eden; the vision matches both.
**One tree, both banks, the same tree from the garden — its flaming-sword guard withdrawn.**

## [→] The monthly fruit and the monthly worship — Isaiah 66:23

The tree _"yielded her fruit every month"_ (Rev 22:2) — and that detail answers an Old
Testament promise.

- [→] **OVERLAY** (not a [KEY🔑]) — DAR ties the monthly fruit to Isaiah's monthly worship:

> "This fact throws light upon the declaration in Isaiah 66:23, that all flesh shall come up
> 'from one new moon to another' to worship before the Lord of hosts... The redeemed come up
> to the holy city from month to month to partake of the fruit of the tree of life."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 718_

- [→] note: the lexicon (_hhodesh_ = "month," the LXX "from month to month") is **auxiliary,
  not load-bearing.** The reading stands on Isa 66:23 + Rev 22:2 read together; the Hebrew
  only confirms what the two verses already say.

[DYK🔎] The redeemed do not visit the tree once — they come up to the city **month by
month**, the worship and the fruit on the same rhythm. The new earth keeps an appointment.

## [→] No more curse — Eden and the new earth clasp hands

> "And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it;
> and his servants shall serve him." (Revelation 22:3)

The curse of Genesis 3 is reversed. The guides carry the Eden-restored frame:

- [→] **OVERLAY** — Haskell: the restored earth is not merely Eden again, but Eden plus six
  thousand years of what would have been:

> "...for six thousand years he has reigned on the earth. When the earth is restored, it will
> not be as it was in the beginning, but far more beautiful. It will be as it would have been
> in the same length of time, if sin had never entered."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 347_

- [→] **OVERLAY** — the city rests on the precise site of old Jerusalem; the Mount of Olives
  parts asunder (Zech 14:4). What sin robbed, Christ restores (SSP 340; sourced overlay)

### [⟲] Rev 22:13 — Alpha and Omega

Here is the in-text [KEY🔑] near this scene — the verse that defines its own symbol:

> "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last." (Revelation
> 22:13)

[KEY🔑] **Alpha IS Omega** — first and last named in the same breath. And Haskell reads the
whole arc of redemption in it:

> "Eden and the new earth clasp hands in the expression so oft repeated in the book of
> Revelation, 'I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last.'"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 359_

- the first thing (Eden) and the last thing (the new earth) **meet** in the One who is First
  and Last
- the prophetic eye confirms the full restoration:

> "All that was lost by sin has been restored... God's original purpose in the creation of
> the earth is fulfilled as it is made the eternal abode of the redeemed."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 674_

[→] note: "no more curse = Eden restored" is carried here as a **[→] guide-overlay frame**
plus the in-text **[KEY🔑] of Alpha/Omega (22:13)** — not as a bare key on 22:3. The Eden
idea is preserved, and sourced.

## [→] The commandments and the tree of life — joined from Eden to the gate

> "Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life,
> and may enter in through the gates into the city." (Revelation 22:14)

- [→] **OVERLAY** — DAR: the commandments are the Father's ten of Sinai, blessed near the
  very close of the book:

> "Thus in the closing chapter of the word of God, and near the very close of the last
> testimony which the faithful and true Witness there left for his people, he solemnly
> pronounces a blessing upon those who keep the commandments of God."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 721_

- [→] **OVERLAY** — Haskell: commandments and tree were placed **together** at the very
  beginning:

> "In Eden, at the beginning, the commandments and the tree of life were placed together."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 360_

### [⟲] Rev 14:12 / Rev 12:17 — the SAME remnant mark

- [⟲] the commandment-keeping that marked the saints through the three angels (_"here are
  they that keep the commandments of God,"_ Rev 14:12, R11) and marked the remnant (Rev
  12:17, R09) is the **very thing** that gives _"right to the tree of life"_ here — **SAME
  test, now rewarded.** The mark that made them a target on earth is their passport at the
  gate.

The prophetic eye confirms both halves:

> "And the revelator, half a century after the crucifixion, pronounces a blessing upon them
> 'that do His commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in
> through the gates into the city.'"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 466_

> "All, all who keep the commandments of God, will enter in through the gates into the city
> and have right to the tree of life."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 51_

[→] From Eden to the gate, the commandments and the tree stand together. The whole
controversy was ever whether eternal life comes by obedience or by some other means — and
the gate answers it.
