# Revelation 7 — The Sealing

**Before the last storm of strife is loosed, God halts the wind to write His name — His
Sabbath, His character — on the foreheads of His people. Revelation 7 is the parenthesis
of mercy thrown into the sixth seal: the last work done for the saints before they are
translated.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Watch Revelation 7
> hand us the key — "circumcision was a sign and a seal," "the Sabbath is a sign," "the
> Father's name is in the forehead" — line upon line.

---

## Where this chapter stands — a parenthesis inside the sixth seal

The sixth seal was breaking open in chapter 6: the stars fell (6:13), and the heaven was
about to depart as a scroll (6:14). Then the action **pauses**. Chapter 7 is not a new
sequence — it is thrown in **between those two verses**, to tell us one more thing about
the sixth seal. **We stand right here, now.**

- [→] **Rev 7 = a parenthesis inside the sixth seal** — between the falling stars (6:13)
  and the heavens rolling up as a scroll (6:14). This is the historicist placement DAR and
  Haskell both draw — not a verse defining itself — so it carries a [→], not a [KEY🔑].

> "The whole of chapter 7 is therefore thrown in here parenthetically... for the purpose of
> stating additional particulars concerning the sixth seal... It comes in, therefore,
> between the 13th and 14th verses of Revelation 6; but there, as already shown, is just
> where we now stand."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 435_

[→] DAR and Haskell agree sharply on the placement — quote both. Haskell sets the same
parenthesis between the very same two verses:

> "The seventh chapter of the book of Revelation continues the description of events taking
> place under the sixth seal... This distress of nations follows the falling of the
> stars... it places that chapter, when considered chronologically, between the thirteenth
> and fourteenth verses of the sixth chapter of Revelation."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 127_

[→] Mark the stakes: the sealing must **finish** before the heavens roll up. There is no
place for this work after that verse. **The pause is mercy with a deadline.**

---

## The four winds = strife — held by four angels

The walk opens at four angels on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds
**"that the wind should not blow on the earth."** First — what is a wind? Scripture does
not say in a single sentence "winds are strife," so this is the canon-wide symbolic
reading the guides draw, anchored in how the wind always moves in prophecy.

> "...the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea." (Daniel 7:2)

> "...a great whirlwind shall be raised up from the coasts of the earth." (Jeremiah 25:32)

- [→] **winds = political commotion, strife, and war** — the guide-drawn symbol, anchored
  in Daniel 7:2 (the four winds strive on the sea of peoples) and Jeremiah 25:32 (the
  whirlwind of judgment). No single verse says "winds are war," so this carries the [→]
  with its establishing DAR quote, not a bare [KEY🔑].

[⟲] **Rev 7 four winds vs. Daniel 7:2 four winds = RECAPITULATED.** Same symbol class —
winds = strife — but **a different epoch**. In Dan 7 the contending powers produce the
rise of empires (Babylon, Persia, Greece, Rome). In Rev 7 the same symbol is bent to the
**final** period: all the world's strife held back until the sealing is done. Do **not**
blur the empire-raising winds of Daniel with the end-time restraint of Revelation — same
key, different lock.

[→] The verse gives the symbol; DAR gives the picture of the held storm — the four literal
angels restraining the strife:

> "Winds, in the Bible, symbolize political commotion, strife, and war. Daniel 7:2;
> Jeremiah 25:32. The four winds, held by four angels... must denote all the elements of
> strife and commotion that exist in the world; and when they are all loosed... it will
> constitute the great whirlwind."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 436_

[→] Haskell reads the same restraint, and points to a distress of nations unlike any
before it:

> "Winds symbolize war or contention... at a certain period of time, there should be a
> distress of nations differing from all previous international troubles."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 128_

[DYK🔎] Haskell pins the restraint to history — the 1848 revolutions, and the sudden,
unaccountable calm that followed, just as the sealing message went out:

> "The four angels had been stationed on the earth to hold the winds of strife till the
> servants of God could be sealed... the sealing work is now going on."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 132_

## The angel from the east — the seal of the living God

A fifth angel ascends from the east, **the seal of the living God** in his hand, and cries
to the four: **"Hurt not the earth... till we have sealed the servants of our God in their
foreheads."** (Rev 7:2-3). Who is this angel, and what is his work?

- [→] **angel from the east = a divine messenger of Sabbath reform** — DAR's historicist
  overlay. The verse does not name the angel; DAR identifies the movement:

> "...the angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God, is a divine
> messenger in charge of a work of reform to be carried on among men in reference to the
> Sabbath of the fourth commandment."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 443_

- [→] **synchronization — this angel = the third angel of Revelation 14.** The Rev 7
  sealing work and the third angel's message are one and the same movement:

> "The angel with the seal of the living God, mentioned in chapter 7, is therefore the same
> as the third angel of chapter 14."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 444_

[→] The prophetic eye confirms the restraint-scene — the merciful eye of Jesus on the
unsealed remnant, and the commission to hold the winds:

> "...another angel was commissioned to fly swiftly to the four angels and bid them hold,
> until the servants of God were sealed with the seal of the living God in their
> foreheads."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 38_

[→] And EGW ties the **timing of the close** — the winds hold until Jesus' sanctuary work
is finished, then the plagues fall (this chains forward to the sanctuary and plagues
sessions):

> "...the four angels would hold the four winds until Jesus' work was done in the
> sanctuary, and then will come the seven last plagues."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 36_

[→] **present application** — the sealing is not merely future. It is the work now in
hand:

> "This points out the work we have now to do."
> — _EGW, Testimonies for the Church, vol. 5, p. 717_

## [KEY🔑] What the seal is — the Father's name, the Sabbath, in the forehead

What is written in the forehead? We let the verses answer, one at a time.

> "...and with him an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in
> their foreheads." (Revelation 14:1)

> "And he received the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith..."
> (Romans 4:11)

> "...my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it is a sign between me and you throughout your
> generations; that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you." (Exodus 31:13)

- [KEY🔑] **the Father's name is written in the forehead** — Rev 14:1 says it outright; the
  verse defines the verse.
- [KEY🔑] **sign = seal = token are synonyms** — Rom 4:11 calls circumcision "a seal" that
  was a "sign." Scripture's own synonymy.
- [KEY🔑] **the Sabbath is the God-given sign** — Exod 31:13: a sign "that ye may know that
  I am the LORD that doth sanctify you." These are the verse-defined keys.

[→] Now the **overlay**: the seal **is the Sabbath, the fourth commandment**, and the
Father's name and His sign converge there. That convergence is the DAR/Haskell reading —
never a bare [KEY🔑], so it carries the [→] with its establishing quotes:

> "...the fourth commandment constitutes the seal of the law of God, or the seal of God...
> [it is] the seal of his law in both dispensations."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, pp. 440, 441_

> "This is good proof that the 'seal of the living God,' and the 'Father's name,' are used
> synonymously."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 441_

[→] Haskell drives it home with the **three marks of a seal** — name, authority, dominion —
all present in the fourth commandment and nowhere else in the ten:

> "...the seal then is the knowledge of the true God, and this, Jehovah has placed in His
> Sabbath."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 133_

> "...the three specifications of a seal: first, the name,-the Lord thy God, Jehovah;
> second, the authority,-Creator; third, the extent of territory,-the heavens and earth.
> Take away this command from the decalogue, and it would contain no seal."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 135_

[→] And the prophetic eye confirms — the fourth commandment alone bears both the Lawgiver's
name and title (this chains forward to the mark-of-the-beast session):

> "The seal of God's law is found in the fourth commandment. This only, of all the ten,
> brings to view both the name and the title of the Lawgiver."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 452_

> "The Sabbath is the sign of God; it is the seal of His law... the seal or mark of the
> beast... is a counterfeit Sabbath on the day of the sun."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy (1888), p. 691_

[⟲] **Rev 7:3 forehead-mark vs. Ezekiel 9:4 mark = RECAPITULATED.** Ezekiel 9:4 — _"set a
mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry"_ — is the **OT type**; Rev 7's
sealing is the **antitype**, the final fulfillment. Same symbol (a protective divine mark
on the forehead), **different epoch**. Type and antitype, not the same event — and DAR
draws that very parallel (DAR 442).

[⟲] **Rev 7 seal vs. Exodus 31:13 / Ezekiel 20:12 sign = SAME.** The Sabbath-sign of both
testaments is **the very same seal** placed in the foreheads of Rev 7 — not a different
one. Hold the line: SAME (Exod 31's Sabbath-sign) is not the same as RECAPITULATED (Ezek
9's protective mark). One is identical; the other is type-and-antitype.

## The 144,000 — spiritual Israel, sealed by character

Now the count: **144,000 sealed, twelve thousand of every tribe of the children of
Israel** (Rev 7:4-8). Who are these tribes?

- [→] **the twelve tribes = spiritual Israel, not literal ethnic descent** — DAR's overlay,
  reading James 5 as addressed to the last generation of Christians:

> "...the last generation of Christians, the Christians of our own day; and he calls them
> the twelve tribes scattered abroad."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 446_

[→] Haskell makes the basis explicit — **character**, not bloodline — and reads the tribal
names as character-names (Gentiles grafted into the Jewish root, Rom 11):

> "Character alone is the basis of the sealing work... the Gentiles, like branches from a
> wild olive tree, were... grafted into the Jewish root... The one hundred and forty-four
> thousand are divided into classes called by the names of the twelve tribes of Israel.
> These are character names."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 136_

[→] The prophetic eye: the seal is a **character**, not a mere label — the image of Jesus
fully reflected:

> "Those who receive the seal of the living God and are protected in the time of trouble
> must reflect the image of Jesus fully."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 71_

[→] And the urgency that sets up the Appeal:

> "The sealing time is very short, and will soon be over. Now is the time, while the four
> angels are holding the four winds, to make our calling and election sure."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 58_

[⟲] **144,000 of Rev 7 vs. 144,000 of Rev 14 = SAME.** Not two companies, not recapitulated
instances — **the same 144,000**, sealed here, carried forward to the Rev 14 vision:

> "...the same company who in Revelation 7 have the seal... are brought to view again in
> Revelation 14:1."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 441_

## The great multitude before the throne — and why it is NOT the 144,000

The scene shifts: **"a great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations, and
kindreds, and people, and tongues,"** before the throne, in white robes, palms in hand
(Rev 7:9). Who are these?

- [→] **the great multitude = the saved of every age, raised at the Second Coming** — DAR's
  overlay, marking the sealing as the **last** work before translation:

> "This vast throng are undoubtedly the saved out of every nation... raised from the dead
> at the second coming of Christ, showing that the sealing is the last work accomplished
> for the people of God prior to translation."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 448_

[⟲] **Great multitude (Rev 7:9) vs. 144,000 (Rev 7:4) = DIFFERENT.** This is the single
most error-prone point in the chapter — guard it. The **great multitude** is ALL the
redeemed of every age, raised at the Second Coming (the full harvest, including the
redeemed dead). The **144,000** are a special company **within** that multitude — the
living, **translated without seeing death.** Two distinct groups: the 144,000 are a
**subset**, not identical to, the great multitude. The prophetic eye confirms they are
"translated from among the living":

> "These, having been translated from the earth, from among the living, are counted as the
> first fruits unto God and to the Lamb..."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 648_

[→] Do **not** blur this DIFFERENT group with the SAME-instance 144,000 of Rev 14. A
subset is not the whole, and the whole is not a recapitulation.

[→] Note the worship: the multitude and the angels fall before the throne, ascribing
salvation to God (Rev 7:10-12) — **the harvest home that the sealing makes possible.**

## [KEY🔑] The elder's question — they that came out of great tribulation

An elder asks: **"What are these which are arrayed in white robes? and whence came they?"**
(Rev 7:13). The verse answers its own question.

> "...These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes, and
> made them white in the blood of the Lamb." (Revelation 7:14)

- [KEY🔑] **the verse answers itself** — "These are they which came out of great
  tribulation." No outside meaning needed; Scripture is its own expositor.

[→] Haskell reads the elder as pointing past the multitude to the **special company** — the
little band that suffered most:

> "...as if again to call attention to the little company who have suffered most, one
> elder, pointing to the one hundred and forty-four thousand, said, What are these..."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 139_

[→] DAR locates the 144,000 in the great time of trouble — and here is where Daniel 12:1
actually lives: as **supporting prose**, not a structural weave:

> "...pass through the great time of trouble such as never was since there was a nation.
> Daniel 12:1... They stand without a mediator through the terrific scenes of the seven
> last plagues."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 450_

[→] The prophetic eye weaves Rev 7:14 with Rev 14:1-5 as the same company — delivered,
their robes washed white:

> "These, having been translated from the earth, from among the living, are counted as the
> first fruits unto God and to the Lamb... they have stood without an intercessor through
> the final outpouring of God's judgments. But they have been delivered, for they have
> washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 648_

[→] Haskell names the **destiny** — the sealed company become the throne's bodyguard, the
vacancy left by Lucifer's rebellion filled:

> "The place once occupied by Satan and his angels, will be filled by the hundred and
> forty-four thousand, when they gather at last about the throne, where they serve God day
> and night in His temple."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 140_

[→] And the glorified state — the seal seen in glory, the new name written:

> "The 144,000 were all sealed and perfectly united. On their foreheads was written, God,
> New Jerusalem, and a glorious star containing Jesus' new name."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 15_

[Q] The same chapter that shows the winds held back also shows the throng standing in white
before the throne. If God halted the storm of the whole world to finish one work in you,
whose name do you want written in your forehead before the heavens roll up?

## Appeal

- The sealing time is **very short, and will soon be over** (EW 58). The seal is not a stamp
  on the careless — it is _"the image of Jesus fully"_ reflected in the soul (EW 71), God's
  name and His Sabbath written where the world cannot see but **heaven reads it.**
- _"Now is the time, while the four angels are holding the four winds, to make our calling
  and election sure."_ (EW 58). The wind is held **for this.** When it is loosed, the work
  is closed.
- Receive the seal: **keep the Sabbath of the Creator** as the sign that He alone is your
  God and Sanctifier (Exod 31:13). That is the Father's name in the forehead (Rev 14:1) —
  the mark heaven is waiting to write on you now.

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: the seal = the Father's name, the Sabbath sign, in the forehead.**
This is the parenthesis inside the sixth seal we opened with (DAR 435) — the winds of
strife (Dan 7:2) are the **same class of symbol** we met in Daniel's beasts-from-the-sea,
now **restrained** for one last work. The seal is the Sabbath sign of Exodus 31:13 and the
protective mark of Ezekiel 9 — Scripture its own expositor, line upon line. Hold it; the
mark-of-the-beast session will need it.
