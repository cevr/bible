# Revelation 12 — The Woman, the Dragon, and the Wilderness

**Heaven opens on a woman and a dragon — and for once we do not have to argue about
the dragon, because the verse names him to his face: _"that old serpent, the Devil,
and Satan"_ (Rev 12:9). With that one verse defining itself, the whole great controversy
clicks into focus: the pure church travailing, fleeing, and outliving the 1260 — the
same 1260 the reader has already dated twice — until her last-generation remnant stands
keeping the commandments and holding the testimony of Jesus.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Where the verse
> defines its own word, that is a [KEY🔑]; where the guides read history onto the picture,
> that is a [→] overlay carrying the guide's quote. Watch Revelation 12 hand us its own
> dictionary entry — _dragon = Satan_ — line upon line.

---

## The setup — a great wonder in heaven, and the controversy laid bare

- Rev 12 opens **"a great wonder in heaven"** (Rev 12:1) — two figures, a woman and a
  dragon, drawn against the sky but acting out their drama on earth
  > "the same heaven where the woman and the dragon were seen at first; but they were
  > actors in scenes that took place here upon the earth."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 513_
- [→] this is the **heart of the book**: the long war between Christ's church and Satan,
  told as one continuous panorama. Haskell frames the whole chapter as **"the mountain
  peaks, in the history of the Christian church"** — three peaks of one story

> "Only the mountain peaks, in the history of the Christian church, are revealed in this
> view. There is the glorious rising of the sun; then, a lapse of over five hundred years.
> The days of papal tyranny and persecution are shown when the 'woman' was in the
> wilderness for twelve hundred and sixty years; and the last peak is when the sun again
> shines upon the Remnant church in all its splendor."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 217_

- [→] tell the reader the route in advance: **birth/first advent → war resolved at the
  cross → the wilderness 1260 → earth helps the woman → the remnant.** The chapter is
  **not** chronological end-to-end; it **recapitulates** — a flag we plant now and cash at
  the war-in-heaven section.

## The woman clothed with the sun = the true church (decoded by Scripture)

> "a woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and upon her head a crown
> of twelve stars." (Revelation 12:1)

- [→] **NOT a bare [KEY🔑]:** Rev 12 does not define "woman" in-text. It is decoded **by
  parity of reasoning** from the canon — a corrupt woman = a corrupt church (Eze 23; Rev
  17), therefore a **pure** woman = the **true church.** This is the DAR/Haskell historicist
  overlay, and it carries the guide quote.

> "'A woman,' the true church. A corrupt woman is used to represent an apostate or corrupt
> church. Ezekiel 23:2-4; Revelation 17:3-6, 15, 18. By parity of reasoning, a pure woman,
> as in this instance, would represent the true church."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 509_

Decode her clothing in place (all DAR 509.4-509.6):

- **sun** = the light and glory of the gospel dispensation
- **moon under her feet** = the Mosaic dispensation now passing, shining only with borrowed
  light
- **crown of twelve stars** = the twelve apostles

Haskell weaves the same picture — the moon passing, the sun rising:

> "It is the closing of one era, the age of types and shadows, which, like the moon,
> reflect the light of the true. The moon is under the feet of the church, and the glorious
> sunrising of a new day is ushered in."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 212_

- [CTRF] flag forward, do not resolve here: this **pure** woman is the Bible's own
  counterpart to the **corrupt** woman of Rev 17 ("that great city," Babylon, Rev 17:18) —
  a **different** symbol the series meets at R13. DAR 509.3 sets the pair up explicitly;
  do not blur them.

## The man child = Christ; the dragon (vv. 3-4) = pagan Rome

> "a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns... [stood] before the woman...
> to devour her child as soon as it was born... and she brought forth a man child, who
> was to rule all nations with a rod of iron: and her child was caught up unto God, and
> to his throne." (Revelation 12:3-5)

- [→] **man child = Jesus Christ**; "caught up unto God, and to his throne" = the
  **ascension** (overlay, DAR 511.1-512.1; SSP 216.2). The **rod of iron** (Ps 2:9) fixes
  it — only Christ is given to rule all nations.
- [→] **CRITICAL DISTINCTION — this dragon is NOT yet "Satan personally."** The great red
  dragon of vv. 3-4 (seven heads, ten horns, seven crowns) = **PAGAN ROME**, the power that
  tried to kill the child at birth. This is the historicist overlay and carries DAR's quote —
  it is decidedly **not** a [KEY🔑]. We meet the verse that **does** define the dragon (v. 9)
  one section down, and we must **not** collapse the two.

> "having fixed the chronology of the prophecy at the time when he was born into this
> world, it will be easy to find the power symbolized by the dragon... No formal answer to
> this question need be given to any one who has read how Herod, in a fiendish effort to
> destroy the infant Jesus, sent forth and slew all the children in Bethlehem... But who
> was Herod? — A Roman governor. From Rome, Herod derived his power. Rome ruled at that
> time over all the world (Luke 2:1), and was therefore the responsible party."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, pp. 511-512_

Haskell confirms the dragon = the Roman Empire as Satan's chosen instrument:

> "it was the opposing power of Satan embodied in the ruling monarchy of earth-the empire
> of Rome, with Caesar Augustus at its head... the seven heads with the ten horns represent
> the Roman Empire, in which the devil dwelt."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 214_

- [DYK🔎] the dragon of vv. 3-4 is **Satan-working-through-Rome** (Satan is the inspiring
  agent), but the symbol drawn here — seven heads, ten horns, seven crowns — is the
  **empire**, not Satan's person. DAR 513.3 makes the line razor-sharp in the next section.

## [KEY🔑] The great dragon (v. 9) = the Devil and Satan — and the war in heaven is the FIRST ADVENT

Now the verse defines its **own** symbol — the one true [KEY🔑] of the chapter's dragon
thread:

> "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which
> deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out
> with him." (Revelation 12:9)

- [KEY🔑] **dragon (v. 9) = the Devil and Satan** — defined in-text, no overlay needed. The
  Bible hands us the dictionary entry, exactly as Rev 1:20 (candlesticks = churches) and
  Rev 17:15 (waters = peoples) do for their visions.
- [→] but DAR draws the **sharpest line in the chapter** — the v. 9 dragon (Satan personally)
  is **not** the same picture as the vv. 3-4 dragon (pagan Rome):

> "this is not the same as the dragon of verses 3 and 4. That was a great red dragon, with
> seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns... It would be most grotesque to try to apply
> this to Satan personally... But all these features are very appropriate as applied to
> pagan Rome."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 513_

- hold **both** readings without confusing them: the **same** word "dragon" carries a
  **symbol** (Rome, vv. 3-4) and a **named person** (Satan, v. 9).

[⟲] **RECAPITULATION VERDICT:** vv. 1-6 ran down to 1798; v. 7 carries us **back** to the
first advent. This "war in heaven" is **not** the pre-creation expulsion of Lucifer (a
**different** casting-out); it is **Christ's victory over Satan at the cross.**

> "In the 7th verse it is equally plain that we are carried back into previous ages... To
> the time first introduced in the chapter, — the days of the first advent... Evidently to
> the commencement of Christ's ministry here upon earth."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 513_

[⟲] **Michael = Christ — a canon weave, NOT a verse-self-key.** Rev 12:7 does not define
"Michael" in-text; the name is decoded by **reaching across the canon** (Jude 9; 1 Thess
4:16; John 5:28, 29). Same-vs-different verdict: the Michael who fights here is the **same
person** as the Christ of the Gospels — one Being under his title as "the archangel," the
Captain of the host — not a separate created angel. DAR carries the establishing references:

> "To prove that Michael is Christ, see Jude 9; 1 Thessalonians 4:16; John 5:28, 29."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 513_

Haskell anchors the war's resolution at Calvary — and ties Rev 12:10's "Now is come
salvation" straight to "It is finished":

> "At the cross, the fate of Satan was forever sealed... the hour that seemed the darkest,
> was, for the universe, the hour of greatest victory."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 219_

And the prophetic eye seals DAR's and Haskell's placement — the casting-out of Rev 12 is the
**cross**, not Eden-before-time:

> "When the last steps of Christ's humiliation were to be taken... He said... 'The prince
> of this world is judged.' Now shall he be cast out... He knew that when He should exclaim,
> 'It is finished,' all heaven would triumph... the knell of Satan's empire would then be
> sounded."
> — _EGW, The Desire of Ages, p. 679_

## The woman flees into the wilderness = the true church hidden under the papacy, A.D. 538

> "And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that
> they should feed her there a thousand two hundred and threescore days." (Revelation 12:6;
> cf. Rev 12:13-14)

- [→] overlay (carries the guide quote, not a bare KEY): the flight = the true church
  driven into obscurity when the papacy is established, **A.D. 538** — fed by God's word
  through the long persecution.

> "And the church fled into the wilderness at the time the papacy was established, in 538,
> where it was nourished by the word of God and the ministration of angels during the long,
> dark, and bloody rule of that power, 1260 years."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 512_

Haskell dates and weaves it — note the Rev 11 link he draws himself:

> "The papacy was established at Rome in 538 a.d. for twelve hundred and sixty years,-the
> 'thousand two hundred and three score days' of Revelation 12:6, the 'time, and times, and
> half a time' of Revelation 12:14. It was the period during which the 'two witnesses' of
> the eleventh chapter of Revelation prophesied in sackcloth."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 221_

The prophetic eye confirms — the woman = the church of Christ, and 538 begins the 1260:

> "In the sixth century the papacy had become firmly established... Paganism had given place
> to the papacy... And now began the 1260 years of papal oppression... 'The woman fled into
> the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there a
> thousand two hundred and three-score days.'"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 54_

## [⟲] The 1260 met a THIRD time — the wilderness span IS the little-horn span the reader already dated

> "where she is nourished for a time, and times, and half a time, from the face of the
> serpent." (Revelation 12:14) — the very phrase of Daniel 7:25.

- [→] decode the span where the text raises it: a **time** = 1 year (360 days); **times** =
  2 years (720 days); **half a time** = ½ year (180 days) → **1260 days**; and being
  symbolic, 1260 days = **1260 LITERAL YEARS** by the year-day principle (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6).

> "The mention of the period during which the woman is nourished in the wilderness as 'a
> time and times and half a time,' the exact phraseology used in Daniel 7:25, furnishes a key
> for the explanation of the latter passage; for the very same period is called in verse 6
> of Revelation 12, 'a thousand two hundred and threescore days.'... this, being symbolic,
> signifies 1260 literal years."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 517_

- [⟲] **SAME-instance verdict:** this is **not** a new period. Rev 12:6's 1260 days = Rev
  12:14's time/times/half a time = Dan 7:25's identical phrase = Rev 13:5's 42 months (R10,
  next session) — **ONE span, four wordings: papal supremacy 538-1798.** The ★ of this
  session: the 1260 re-met for the **THIRD time** (R08 the witnesses, R09 the woman, R10 the
  42 months), now anchored to the **woman.**
- [⟲] reach back to Daniel for the reader who walked the spine: "a time, times, and half a
  time" here = Dan 7:25's "time and times and the dividing of time" (the little horn's lease)
  and Dan 12:7's "time, times, and an half." The reader already **DATED** this 538-1798 at
  D07; Revelation is **restating** it, not recapitulating a different clock.

The prophetic eye gathers all the wordings into one span:

> "The forty and two months are the same as the 'time and times and the dividing of time,'
> three years and a half, or 1260 days, of Daniel 7... This period... began with the
> supremacy of the papacy, A.D. 538, and terminated in 1798. At that time the pope was made
> captive by the French army..."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 439_

- [DYK🔎] optional Rev 11 tie (Haskell and EGW both make it): the same 1260 is when the two
  witnesses "prophesy a thousand two hundred and threescore days, clothed in sackcloth"
  (Rev 11:3) — _"In the darkest times there were faithful men who loved God's word"_ (GC p.
  267). The witnesses in sackcloth and the woman in the wilderness are the **same period,
  two pictures** (R08 weave).

## The flood and the earth — the serpent's false doctrines vs. the Reformation

> "And the serpent cast out of his mouth water as a flood after the woman, that he might
> cause her to be carried away of the flood. And the earth helped the woman, and the earth
> opened her mouth, and swallowed up the flood." (Revelation 12:15-16)

- [→] overlay (guide quote required): **flood from the serpent's mouth** = the papacy's
  false doctrines and the flood of persecution it hurled at the church.

> "The serpent cast out of his mouth water as a flood to carry away the church. By its false
> doctrines the papacy had so corrupted all nations as to have control absolutely... of the
> civil power. Through it Satan could hurl a mighty flood of persecution against the church
> in every direction... From fifty to one hundred million were carried away by the flood;
> but the church was not entirely swallowed up; the days were shortened for the elect's sake.
> Matthew 24:22."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 518_

- [→] **earth helping the woman** = the Protestant Reformation of the sixteenth century (and
  later the open lands and nations that absorbed papal fury):

> "The Reformation of the sixteenth century began its work. God raised up the noble Luther
> and his colaborers to expose the true character of the papacy, and break the power with
> which superstition had enslaved the minds of the people."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 518_

Haskell weaves the Reformation forward to America and the last message:

> "The power of the Reformation is still felt in the earth; and the nations of Western
> Europe, together with the people of the United States, have the privilege of giving the
> last messages of the Gospel of Christ to the world."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 222_

> "And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her
> seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ."
> (Revelation 12:17)

- [→] **DIFFERENT-instance verdict:** the remnant is **not** merely the wilderness church
  carried forward — it is a historically **specific** group, the **LAST GENERATION**,
  distinguished by two end-time marks the wilderness church lacked.

> "no generation but the last can truthfully be represented by the remnant... This remnant is
> characterized by their keeping of the commandments of God, and having the testimony of

> "In Revelation 19:10, the testimony of Jesus is defined to be the spirit of prophecy."

Haskell names **both** marks:

> "There are two characteristics of the Remnant; they keep the commandments of God... In the

## Three faces of one dragon — pagan Rome, papal Rome, and the two-horned beast

- [→] pull the chapter together: **Satan is the inspiring agent**, but he works through
  **three successive earthly powers**, all called "the dragon":

> "three powers are made use of by the Devil to carry out his work, and hence are all spoken
> of as the dragon, he being the inspiring agent in them all. These are, (1) pagan Rome; (2)
> papal Rome; (3) the two-horned beast, our own government under the control of apostate
> Protestantism."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 519_

- [⟲] **SAME-instance bridge to R10:** the Rev 12 dragon and the Rev 13 leopard-beast
  **both** carry seven heads and ten horns because **both are Rome** — one in its **pagan**
  phase, one in its **papal** phase.

> "By the dragon of the previous chapter, and the beast first introduced in this, we have
> the Roman power as a whole brought to view in its two phases, pagan and papal; hence these
> two symbols have each the seven heads and ten horns."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 521_

- [⟲] **same-vs-different guard:** the ten horns of the Rev 12 dragon = the ten kingdoms of
  divided Western Rome (Dan 2 feet / Dan 7 ten horns) — **NOT** the end-time ten of Rev
  17:12 ("received no kingdom as yet... one hour with the beast"), which is a **different**
  ten met at R13. Do not conflate.
- [→] this sets the rails for R10: the dragon hands the beast "his power, and his seat, and
  great authority" (Rev 13:2) — **pagan Rome passing the scepter to papal Rome**, the same
  power changing its religious character.
