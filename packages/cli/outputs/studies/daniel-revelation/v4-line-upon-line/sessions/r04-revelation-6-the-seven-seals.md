# Revelation 6 — The Seven Seals

**The Lamb who alone could open the book (R03) now breaks the seals — and the church's
own history unrolls in order: a white horse of apostolic purity fading red, then black,
then deathly pale, until the martyrs cry "How long?" and the sixth seal answers with
signs you can date. The seals are not a guess. They are the church's life read off the
page, line upon line, all the way to "the great day of His wrath is come."**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. The horse the Bible
> defines for us (Zech 6:5); the colors and the periods the guides read off church
> history. Watch which is which, seal by seal.

---

## [→] The setup — the Lamb opens what only He could open

- carry R03 forward: in Rev 5 **only the Lamb** was found worthy to take the seven-sealed
  book; now He begins to break the seals
  - _"And I saw when the Lamb opened one of the seals, and I heard, as it were the noise
    of thunder, one of the four beasts saying, Come and see."_ (Revelation 6:1)
  - [→] the book opens **because the Lamb opens it** — the worthiness of R03 is now the
    motion of R04
- [→] establish the **scope** before any horse runs: the seven seals are the religious
  history of the **church**, from the apostolic era to the close of probation — **not** a
  pre-Constantine fragment, and **not** the same series as the trumpets
- [→] this is the historicist overlay — attach the guide quote, no bare key:

> "The trumpets denote a series of events which transpire contemporaneously with the
> events of the seals, but of an entirely different character... The seals denote events
> of a religious character, and contain the history of the church from the opening of the
> Christian era to the coming of Christ."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 402_

- [→] DAR and Haskell agree on the span — Haskell reaches the same scope:

> "The seals, opened, reveal the life of the church, the child of God; and beginning at
> the birth of Christianity, the seals extend to the end of time."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 109_

[→] So frame the whole session: we are **not** decoding cryptic symbols one by one. We
are watching the church's own condition **change color, seal by seal**.

## [KEY🔑] First entry: the HORSE = a spirit going forth from before the Lord

Before the first horse means anything, the horse itself must — and the Bible defines it.
Daniel-2's discipline again: take the symbol where Scripture labels it.

> "And the angel answered and said unto me, These are the four spirits of the heavens,
> which go forth from standing before the Lord of all the earth." (Zechariah 6:5)

- **horse = a spirit going forth from before the Lord** — divine activity moving in the
  earth, the church carried by it (Zech 6:5, Scripture defining its own symbol, cross-book)
- [→] Haskell reaches for the very same key:

> "Zechariah was told that horses symbolized the 'spirits of the heavens, which go forth
> from standing before the Lord of all the earth.' God's Spirit is seeking for those who
> will give it full control in their lives, and the Apostolic Church was blessed with a
> double portion of the Spirit. The horse upon which it rode was white, representing the
> simple faith and trust of those who accepted the baptism of the Spirit in its purity."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 109-110_

[DYK🔎] Note for the walk: **Zech 6:5 is the one genuine in-the-Bible key behind the
horses.** The **colors** and the **periods** that follow are the DAR/Haskell historicist
overlay — never bare verse-keys. Watch the line: the horse is Scripture's; the dating is
the guides'.

## [→] First seal — the WHITE horse: the gospel's apostolic purity (1st century)

> "And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was
> given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer." (Revelation 6:2)

- [→] **white = purity of faith**; **crown + conquering = the zeal and triumph of the
  gospel in the first century** — the DAR/Haskell reading, not a bare key
- [→] DAR fixes the color and the era:

> "The first symbol, a white horse, and the rider who bears a bow, and to whom a crown is
> given, and who goes forth conquering and to conquer, is a fit emblem of the triumphs of
> the gospel in the first century of this dispensation. The whiteness of the horse denotes
> the purity of faith in that age."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 403_

- [→] Haskell reads the same horse as the **Apostolic Church**, Spirit-empowered, victory
  written on every move:

> "A crown denotes victory. A crown was given to him that sat on the horse, and he went
> forth 'conquering, and to conquer.'... Victory was written upon every move of the
> disciples."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 111_

### [⟲] WEAVE — the white horse of Rev 6:2 is NOT the white horse of Rev 19:11

Two white horses, two **different** riders — do not blur them:

> "And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called
> Faithful and True... and he was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is
> called The Word of God." (Revelation 19:11-13)

- [⟲] **Rev 6:2** = the **gospel** going forth in apostolic purity — the rider has **one**
  crown _given_, and a bow
- [⟲] **Rev 19:11** = **Christ Himself** — "Faithful and True," **many** crowns, vesture
  dipped in blood, named "The Word of God"
- [→] same color, **entirely distinct identities.** The first seal is the church carried
  by the Spirit; the white horse of chapter 19 is the King returning. Never merge them.

## [→] Second seal — the RED horse: purity corrupted, peace taken from the earth

> "And there went out another horse that was red: and power was given to him that sat
> thereon to take peace from the earth, and that they should kill one another: and there
> was given unto him a great sword." (Revelation 6:4)

- [→] **red = the original purity beginning to be corrupted**; church-state alliance
  sought; commotion and bloodshed — DAR/Haskell overlay, attach the quote
- [→] DAR stresses the **internal** turn — corruption and the reach for secular power:

> "If the whiteness of the first horse denoted the purity of the gospel in the period
> which that symbol covers, the redness of the second horse would signify that in this
> period that original purity began to be corrupted... The ecclesiastical power sought the
> alliance of the secular. Troubles and commotions were the result. The spirit of this
> period perhaps reached its climax as we come down to the days of Constantine, the first
> so-called Christian emperor, whose conversion to Christianity is dated by Mosheim in A.
> D. 323."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 404_

- [→] Haskell stresses the **external** blow — pagan persecution, the church led as a
  lamb to the slaughter, and maps it to the **Smyrna** age:

> "Peace was taken from the earth; blood was shed upon the right hand and upon the left,
> and the saints were led as a lamb to the slaughter... This carries us through the period
> known as the triumph of paganism, corresponding to the Smyrna church."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 112_

[DYK🔎] Two emphases, one color: DAR flags **internal corruption / church-state alliance
toward Constantine**; Haskell flags **pagan persecution (Smyrna)**. Both read the same
thing — the **loss of the white horse's purity**.

## [→] Third seal — the BLACK horse: darkest errors; balances, oil and wine

> "And when he had opened the third seal... lo a black horse; and he that sat on him had a
> pair of balances in his hand. And I heard a voice... say, A measure of wheat for a
> penny, and three measures of barley for a penny; and see thou hurt not the oil and the
> wine." (Revelation 6:5-6)

- [→] **black (the opposite of white) = a period of great moral darkness and superstition
  in the church** — DAR/Haskell overlay, not a bare key
- [→] DAR sets the color and the era — Constantine to the establishment of the papacy
  (A.D. 538):

> "What a contrast in color between this symbol and the first one: A black horse — the
> very opposite of white! A period of great darkness and moral corruption in the church
> must be denoted by this symbol... The time that intervened between the reign of
> Constantine and the establishment of the papacy in A. D. 538 may be justly noted as the
> time when the darkest errors and grossest superstitions sprung up in the church."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 405_

- [→] **the balances = union of religious and civil JUDICIAL power**, from Constantine
  through Justinian's grant to the bishop of Rome — DAR quotes **Miller** by name:

> "The balances denoted that religion and civil power would be united in the person who
> would administer the executive power in the government, and that he would claim the
> judicial authority both in church and state. This was true among the Roman emperors from
> the days of Constantine until the reign of Justinian, when he gave the same judicial
> power to the bishop of Rome."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 407 (quoting Miller's Lectures, p. 181)_

- [→] **oil and wine = the graces of the Spirit (faith and love)** God preserves even amid
  worldly corruption — DAR, Miller again:

> "The Oil and Wine. — These 'denote the graces of the Spirit, faith and love, and there
> was great danger of hurting these, under the influence of so much of a worldly spirit.
> And it is well attested by all historians that the prosperity of the church in this age
> produced the corruptions which finally terminated in the falling away, and the setting
> up of the anti-Christian abominations.'"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 407 (Miller, Id.)_

- [→] Haskell reads the same horse as Satan's **change of tactic** — from open persecution
  to **flattery** — and the church set up as its own **judge** (the balances):

> "What Satan could not do by persecution, he accomplished by flattery... It is strange
> that, when men lose the Spirit of God, they at once become self-appointed judges of
> other men... This was the period when Christianity was replaced by the papacy, and man
> was exalted as vicegerent of God on earth."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 114-115_

[DYK🔎] **This is the only seal where the guides quote Miller by name.** Flag the
DAR/Miller pairing on the **balances** (judicial church-state union) and the **oil and
wine** (the graces God preserves).

## [KEY🔑] Fourth seal — the PALE horse: the rider is NAMED Death, and Hell follows

> "And I looked, and behold a pale horse: and his name that sat on him was Death, and Hell
> followed with him. And power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to
> kill with sword, and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth."
> (Revelation 6:8)

- [KEY🔑] **the verse defines its OWN symbols:** the rider **is named Death**; **Hell**
  (Hades, the grave) **follows** — Scripture labels the symbol _in-text_ (Rev 6:8). No
  guessing; the verse hands us the names.
- [→] now the historicist **period** is layered on top of the named rider: the **pale**
  (unnatural, blighted) color = the **papacy in its unrebuked, persecuting rule, A.D. 538
  onward** — DAR:

> "The color of this horse is remarkable... a pale color is unnatural. The original word
> denotes the 'pale or yellowish color' that is seen in blighted or sickly plants... It
> must refer to the time in which the papacy bore its unrebuked, unrestrained, and
> persecuting rule, commencing about A. D. 538."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 408_

- [→] Death's power "over the fourth part of the earth" = the papacy's **jurisdiction**;
  the martyrs cry from beneath its altar — DAR:

> "By the fourth part of the earth is doubtless meant the territory over which this power
> had jurisdiction; while the words sword, hunger, death... and beasts of the earth, are
> figures denoting the means by which it has put to death its martyrs, fifty million of
> whom, according to the lowest estimate, call for vengeance from beneath its bloody
> altar."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 408_

- [→] Haskell reads the pale horse as the **greatest departure** from the spirit of truth
  — and names a death **worse** than the sword:

> "The pale horse was an indication of still greater departure from the spirit of truth
> than the black one. Thousands have been put to death by the sword, by starvation, and by
> wild beasts; and what is worse than killing the body, many more have suffered spiritual
> death because of the hiding of the Word of Life."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 116_

[CHAIN] **Hold this thread.** "Fifty million martyrs" and "A.D. 538" tie the pale-horse
papacy straight to the **little horn of D07** (Dan 7:25, **538–1798**) — the **same**
persecuting power, now seen as Death on a pale horse. The fifth and sixth seals will hang
on it.

## [KEY🔑] Fifth seal — the SOULS under the altar cry "How long?"

> "And when he had opened the fifth seal, I saw under the altar the souls of them that were
> slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held: and they cried with a
> loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our
> blood on them that dwell on the earth?" (Revelation 6:9-10)

- [KEY🔑] **the verse defines its own symbol:** the souls **ARE** "them that were slain for
  the word of God, and for the testimony which they held" — Scripture names the company
  _in-text_ (Rev 6:9): **the papal-era martyrs.** Not a riddle; a label.
- [→] the **period** is the overlay: the fifth seal opens at the **Reformation**, following
  the pale-horse persecution — DAR:

> "Following the period of the papal persecution, the time covered by this seal would
> commence when the Reformation began to undermine the antichristian papal fabric, and
> restrain the persecuting power of the Romish Church."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 409_

- [→] the **white robes given = public VINDICATION** of the martyrs' defamed names, as the
  Reformation exposed Rome's corruptions — DAR:

> "But the Reformation began its work... the corruptions of the papal abominations were
> fully exposed... while the martyrs were vindicated from all the aspersions under which
> that anti-Christian church had sought to bury them... White robes were thus given unto
> every one of them."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 413_

- [→] the souls' **cry is personification** — like Abel's blood, the stone in the wall, the
  laborers' hire — **not** proof of conscious disembodied existence — DAR (the digest's
  overlayReading, so a [→], not a bare key):

> "Thus the blood of Abel is said to have cried to God from the ground. Genesis 4:9, 10.
> The stone cried out of the wall, and the beam out of the timber answered it. Habakkuk
> 2:11. The hire of the laborers kept back by fraud cried, and the cry entered into the
> ears of the Lord of sabaoth. James 5:4. So the souls mentioned in our text could cry,
> and not thereby be proved to be conscious."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 411_

- [→] Haskell reads the **altar = the EARTH** — where Christ's blood was shed and the
  martyrs' bodies lie buried, their names in the Book of Life:

> "God does not forget those who have suffered for His name, but their names are written in
> the Book of Life... Christ left the courts of heaven, and the earth became the altar
> where His blood was shed... so the earth has drunk the blood of martyrs, and their
> bodies lie buried in its bosom."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 117_

### [⟲] WEAVE — the fifth-seal souls are RECAPITULATED, not a new company

- [⟲] DAR ties these same martyrs to those **raised in Rev 20:4-6** — one company, two
  modes:

> "They certainly were not alive when John saw them under the fifth seal; for he again
> brings to view the same company, in almost the same language, and assures us that the
> first time they live after their martyrdom, is at the resurrection of the just.
> Revelation 20:4-6."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 412_

- [→] they are **NOT** the 144,000 of Rev 7 (held to R05). **Here** they lie dead beneath
  the earthly altar; **later** they are raised at Christ's return — **same company, two
  modes.**

### [⟲] WEAVE — "How long ... dost thou not judge?" is the JUDGMENT CRY

The cry is not free-floating despair — it reaches straight to the **courtroom** the reader
already met in D07 and the **cleansing** dated in D09:

> "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit... the

> "...Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."

- [⟲] the martyrs' "How long?" is **answered** by the investigative judgment of **Dan

- [→] the seals **raise** the very expectation the sanctuary thread **satisfies.** The

## [→] Sixth seal — the language turns LITERAL; signs you can date

> "And I beheld when he had opened the sixth seal, and, lo, there was a great earthquake;
> and the sun became black as sackcloth of hair, and the moon became as blood; and the
> stars of heaven fell unto the earth..." (Revelation 6:12-13)

- [→] between the fifth and sixth seals the language **shifts from highly figurative to
  strictly literal** — this is the DAR interpretive judgment (the overlay), not a verse
  defining its own symbol, so it is a [→] carrying the guide quote, not a bare key:

> "Between the fifth and sixth seals there seems to be a sudden and entire change in the
> language, from the highly figurative to the strictly literal."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 414_

- [→] the sixth seal also **differs** from the seals before it: it marks **prophetic TIME**
  rather than the condition of the church, and covers history to the end of time — Haskell:

> "The sixth seal covers history until the end of time... It differs from the first four
> seals, by showing events which mark prophetic time, rather than by showing the condition
> of the church."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 119_

Now read the three signs straight off history — each one **dated**:

- **earthquake = the Lisbon earthquake, Nov 1, 1755** — DAR and Haskell:
  > "Other earthquakes may have been as severe in particular localities, but no other one
  > of which we have any record, combining so great an extent with such a degree of
  > severity, has ever been felt on this earth. It certainly supplies all the conditions
  > necessary to constitute it a fitting event to mark the opening of the seal."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 418_
  > "At the beginning of the sixth seal, a mighty earthquake shook the earth. This doubtless
  > refers to the earthquake of 1755, felt with greatest severity at Lisbon, Portugal, and
  > known in history as the Lisbon earthquake."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 120_
- **sun black / moon as blood = the Dark Day of May 19, 1780** (New England) — DAR:
  > "The Darkening of the Sun. — Following the earthquake, it is announced that 'the sun
  > became black as sackcloth of hair.' This portion of the prediction has also been
  > fulfilled. Into a detailed account of the wonderful darkening of the sun, May 19, 1780,
  > we need not here enter."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 419_
- **stars fell = the meteoric shower of Nov 13, 1833** — DAR:

  > "And the Stars of Heaven Fell. — The voice of history still is, Fulfilled!... We refer
  > to the great meteoric shower of Nov. 13, 1833."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 422_

- [→] EGW is the confirming witness that **dates each sign**:

> "In fulfillment of this prophecy there occurred, in the year 1755, the most terrible
> earthquake that has ever been recorded. Though commonly known as the earthquake of
> Lisbon, it extended to the greater part of Europe, Africa, and America."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 304_

> "Twenty-five years later appeared the next sign mentioned in the prophecy — the darkening
> of the sun and moon... 'In those days, after that tribulation, the sun shall be darkened,
> and the moon shall not give her light.'... On the 19th of May, 1780, this prophecy was
> fulfilled."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 306_

> "In 1833... the last of the signs appeared which were promised by the Saviour as tokens
> of His second advent... This prophecy received a striking and impressive fulfillment in
> the great meteoric shower of November 13, 1833. That was the most extensive and wonderful
> display of falling stars which has ever been recorded."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 333_

The signs are not free-floating dates. DAR and EGW **bound** them by the 1260-year
