# Revelation 15-16 — The Seven Last Plagues

**The same wrath the third angel warned of (Rev 14:9-10) is now poured out — and the
Bible itself tells us WHEN: not while Christ pleads, but after the temple fills with
smoke and no one can enter. "Wrath without mixture" is not a mood; it is a calendar.
When mercy's ministration closes, the plagues fall line upon line — literal, future,
on the mark-of-the-beast class the reader already knows — and Daniel 8:14's cleansing
reaches its end.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Two of these plagues'
> symbols the verse defines outright (the frog-spirits, the dragon); the rest the guide
> overlays decode — and each carries its establishing quote. Watch the passage hand us
> the dictionary, and watch every weave resolve same-vs-different.

---

## The setup — heaven pauses before the storm

- John sees _"another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven
  last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God"_ (Rev 15:1) — the verse's OWN
  words: the wrath is **"filled up,"** brought to its full measure
- [→] the **WHEN** of this fullness is the guide overlay, not the verse's bare claim. Carry
  the establishing word:

> "This chapter introduces the seven last plagues, a manifestation of Heaven's unmingled
> wrath, and the fullness of its measure, for the last generation of the wicked. The work
> of mercy is then forever past."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 638_

- [→] Haskell sets the **preparation scene** before any plague falls:

> "Christ casts to the earth the censer which He holds in His hand. He lays aside the
> garments of His priesthood, and passes from the temple. Probation is closed... the glory
> of God bursts forth in all its grandeur, until His train fills the temple."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 267_

- This is **one vision**: Rev 15 (preparation) then Rev 16 (the seven vials, in order). We
  walk it as it stands.

## [⟲] The sea of glass and the victors — same sea John already saw

- _"them that had gotten the victory over the beast... stand on the sea of glass, having
  the harps of God"_ (Rev 15:2); they sing _"the song of Moses... and the song of the
  Lamb"_ (Rev 15:3)

> "And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the
> victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his
> name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God." (Revelation 15:2)

### [⟲] SAME sea — Rev 15:2 = Rev 4:6

- [⟲] the sea of glass here is the **same sea of glass already shown at Rev 4:6** — before
  the throne in heaven (we stood there at R03). Same location, same object; the **"mingled
  with fire"** is the added glory of this eschatological moment, **not a different sea**.
- [→] Haskell sees the sealed company here as an **anticipatory vision** — shown safe
  BEFORE the plagues fall:

> "John sees the same company... standing, as they will stand on the sea of glass, when the
> seven last plagues have been poured out. Lest it should seem that these were lost in the
> terror of the plagues... the prophet sees beyond the time of trouble."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 269_

- [→] **the song of Moses** ties forward to the Egypt-parallel the plagues will follow — the
  redeemed sing as Israel sang after the Red Sea deliverance. Hold the Egypt thread; it
  runs through the whole chapter.

## [→] The temple filled with smoke — why these are "wrath WITHOUT mixture"

This is the doctrinal heart of the session. The verse paints the scene; the overlay tells
us what the scene **means**.

> "And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no
> man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were
> fulfilled." (Revelation 15:8)

- the verse describes the **smoke** and the **locked temple**; it does NOT itself say
  "probation closed." That reading is the guide OVERLAY — carry it with its establishing
  word:

> "these plagues fall after the close of the ministration in the sanctuary... no man —
> οὐδεὶς (oudeis), no one... can enter therein. This shows that the work of mercy is
> closed... hence they are manifestations of the wrath of God without any mixture of mercy."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 638_

- [DYK🔎] the Greek is auxiliary only — the English **"no man"** already yields it. No point
  hangs on the lexicon.
- [→] DAR sharpens the mechanism — **Christ steps down from His mediation:**

> "the temple is filled with smoke... no being can enter into the temple... Christ is then
> no longer a mediator; mercy, which has long stayed the hand of vengeance, pleads no more."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 643_

- [→] EGW confirming witness — the **order** of events spelled out:

> "the wrath of God could not come until Jesus had finished His work in the most holy place,
> laid off His priestly attire, and clothed Himself with the garments of vengeance. Then
> Jesus will step out from between the Father and man... and then the seven last plagues
> will be poured out."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 36_

### [⟲] SANCTUARY payoff — the smoke is the END of Daniel 8:14's cleansing

- [⟲] **WEAVE to Dan 8:14** — the sanctuary's cleansing ministry the reader dated to **1844**
  here reaches its **END**. The smoke-filled temple is the close of that very ministration.
  (★ SANCTUARY payoff)
- [⟲] **WEAVE to Dan 12:1** — EGW's own language ties this moment to Daniel 12:1: _"the
  time of trouble, such as never was, had not yet commenced"_; _"Michael had not stood up"_
  (EW 36). The plagues fall **AFTER Michael stands.**
- [→] EGW draws the unmixed/mingled line explicitly:

> "All the judgments upon men, prior to the close of probation, have been mingled with
> mercy... but in the final judgment, wrath is poured out unmixed with mercy."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 628_

[→] That is why these are **wrath WITHOUT mixture.** Every earlier judgment in history left
a door open. These do not. The smoke is the locked door.

## [→] The plagues are literal, and they are future — the Egypt parallel

Not figurative. Not past. Not centuries ago. The guides settle all three at once.

- [→] **literal** — DAR:

> "There is no apparent reason why this should not be regarded as strictly literal. These
> plagues are almost identical with those which God inflicted upon the Egyptians... the
> literality of which is seldom, if ever, called in question."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 644_

- [→] EGW seconds the Egypt parallel:

> "The plagues upon Egypt when God was about to deliver Israel were similar in character to
> those more terrible and extensive judgments which are to fall upon the world just before
> the final deliverance of God's people."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 627_

- [→] **not universal in extent** (until the last one) — EGW:

> "These plagues are not universal, or the inhabitants of the earth would be wholly cut off."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 628_

- [→] **their duration** — Haskell applies the year-day key to "one day":

> the angels "come to the earth one at a time... Her Plagues [shall] come in one day... or
> one year of literal time"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 274_

- [DYK🔎] the year-day principle (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6) applied to **"one day"** — the plagues
  run within **one year** of literal time. One generation, not centuries.

## [→] First plague — the noisome sore falls ONLY on the mark-of-the-beast class

The very first vial is the **chronological anchor** of the whole sequence — it lands on a
class the reader has already met.

> "And the first went, and poured out his vial upon the earth; and there fell a noisome and
> grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast, and upon them which worshipped
> his image." (Revelation 16:2)

- [→] DAR makes the timing **conclusive:**

> "it is poured out upon those who have the mark of the beast... the identical work against
> which the third angel warns us. This is conclusive proof that these judgments are not
> poured out till after this angel closes his work, and that the very class who hear his
> warning, and reject it, are the ones to receive the first drops."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 641_

### [⟲] SAME beast — Rev 16:2 = Rev 13's papacy

- [⟲] **"the mark of the beast"** here is the **SAME beast of Rev 13** (the papacy) the
  reader met at R10, tied back through the **third angel's message** of Rev 14:9-10 from R11.
  **Not a new power.**
- [→] and the sealed are **shielded** — Haskell:

> "Before the first angel... left the temple, all men had been divided into two classes,—
> those who are sealed with the seal of the living God, and those who worship the beast...
> The grievous sores come upon those having the mark of the beast."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 276_

- [→] OVERLAY — the sealed saints' **immunity** through all seven plagues is typified by
  Israel's separation from Egypt after the first plagues:

> "As the Lord put a division between Israel and the Egyptians after the first three plagues
> had fallen upon the land of the Pharaohs, so in the time of trouble He says, 'Come, My
> people, enter thou into thy chambers, and shut thy doors about thee...'"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 277_

- [→] **the mark of the beast vs. the seal of God** — the two classes re-met from R05 /
  R10. The first plague does not _create_ the line; it falls along a line already drawn.

## [→] Plagues two through five — the wicked harden like Pharaoh

The next four vials fall, and still the verdict repeats: _"they repented not"_ /
_"blasphemed God."_

- **sea to blood** (Rev 16:3), **rivers to blood** (Rev 16:4), **the sun scorches with
  fire** (Rev 16:8-9), **darkness on the beast's seat** (Rev 16:10-11) — and through it all,
  no repentance
- [→] the unrepentance is **Pharaoh's hardening replayed** — Haskell names the pattern:

> "men of science... will doubtless offer scientific reasons for the disease on land and
> sea. The magicians of Egypt first imitated the wonders... and when they could no longer do
> that, they gave a reason for each miracle, assigning some natural cause... and Pharaoh
> hardened his heart."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 278_

- [→] **the same generation throughout:** under the fifth plague men still bear the **sores
  of the first** (Rev 16:11, _"their pains and their sores"_). That single detail rules out
  any centuries-long past fulfillment — **one generation, one year.**

## [→] Sixth plague — the Euphrates dried, and the gathering to Armageddon

> "And the sixth angel poured out his vial upon the great river Euphrates; and the water
> thereof was dried up, that the way of the kings of the east might be prepared."
> (Revelation 16:12)

- [→] OVERLAY — the **Euphrates = the Ottoman/Turkish power:**

> "it must be understood figuratively as symbolizing the power holding possession of the
> territory watered by that river, which is the Ottoman, or Turkish, empire."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 648_

### [⟲] SAME referent as Rev 9:14 — difference is DEGREE

- [⟲] the Euphrates symbolized the **Turkish power at the sixth trumpet** too (Rev 9:14, met
  at R06). **SAME referent** — the difference is **DEGREE:** the sixth trumpet **began**
  Ottoman decline, the sixth plague **completes** its dissolution. Not a new power; the same
  power, at its end.
- [→] Haskell joins the two readings and names the battle-place:

> "The Turkish power designated as the River Euphrates, which has separated between the East
> and the West, gives way; and... the armies of the earth... meet in the valley of
> Jehoshaphat,—the ancient meeting place... known in the Hebrew as Megiddo, and in Greek as
> Armageddon. The word itself means 'the place of the troops.'"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 284_

- the gathering is to _"the battle of that great day of God Almighty"_ (Rev 16:14); _"he
  gathered them together into a place called in the Hebrew tongue Armageddon"_ (Rev 16:16)
- [→] **Armageddon = Megiddo / valley of Jehoshaphat** is the guide OVERLAY (SSP 284), not
  the verse's own definition. The verse names the place; the guide decodes it.

## [KEY🔑] The three unclean spirits like frogs — the verse defines them

Here the verse itself hands us the dictionary — the **one place** in this passage where a
symbol defines its own meaning in-text.

> "And I saw three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out
> of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet. For they are the
> spirits of devils, working miracles, which go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the
> whole world, to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty."
> (Revelation 16:13-14)

- [KEY🔑] the verse **defines its own symbol** — the three unclean spirits **ARE "the
  spirits of devils"** (Rev 16:14). A true in-text dictionary entry.
- [KEY🔑] the **dragon** is defined in-text too:

> "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which
> deceiveth the whole world." (Revelation 12:9)

- [KEY🔑] the personal referent of the dragon is **Satan himself** — named outright by the
  verse (Rev 12:9), exactly the key we forged for the dragon earlier.

### [⟲] SAME dragon, added dimension

- [⟲] in Rev 16:13 the dragon is **one of three SOURCES** of unclean spirits. The personal
  referent **stays Satan** (Rev 12:9); the guide overlay adds the **institutional vehicle** —
  consistent with Rev 13:2 (the beast received its seat **FROM** the dragon). Same dragon,
  new dimension; not a different referent.
- [→] OVERLAY — the three sources = three **religious divisions** — DAR:

> "the dragon, the beast, and the false prophet, or Paganism, Catholicism, and
> Protestantism"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 650_

- [→] Haskell amplifies the threefold union and the Armageddon gathering:

> "Paganism (the dragon), the papacy (the beast), and fallen Protestantism (the false
> prophet), join hands... the nations gather for the great battle of Armageddon."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 283_

- [→] EGW confirming witness on the frog-spirits' work:

> "I saw three unclean spirits like frogs... they are the spirits of devils, working
> miracles, which go forth unto the kings of the earth... to gather them to the battle...
> the whole world will be swept into the ranks of this delusion."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 561_

## [→] Seventh plague, and the great voice — "It is done"

> "And the seventh angel poured out his vial into the air; and there came a great voice out
> of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done." (Revelation 16:17)

- [→] OVERLAY — this plague is **UNIVERSAL,** unlike the local ones:

> "this one is poured out into the air. The air envelops the whole earth... It will be
> universal. The very air will be deadly."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 653_

> "And the great city was divided into three parts... and great Babylon came in remembrance
> before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath."
> (Revelation 16:19)

### [⟲] RECAPITULATED Babylon — Rev 16:19 → Rev 17-18

- [⟲] Rev 16:19 introduces **"great Babylon"** coming in remembrance — and she receives her
  **full exposition in Rev 17-18 (R13).** This is the **SAME Babylon** announced fallen in
  Rev 14:8 (R11), now receiving the **final cup** — a **recapitulation, NOT a different
  city.** (Teaser for R13.)
- [→] OVERLAY — Babylon's **threefold nature** = the city _"divided into three parts"_ —
  Haskell:

> "Babylon... comes in remembrance before God in her threefold nature, as paganism, papacy,
> and apostate Protestantism, and is made to drink of the wine of the wrath of God."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 288_

- [→] DAR — the **close of human history:**

> "It is done! Thus all is finished. The cup of human guilt has been filled up. The last
> soul has availed itself of the plan of salvation. The books are closed. The number of the
> saved is completed. The final period is placed to this world's history."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 656_

- [→] EGW confirming witness — the seventh plague is also the moment of the saints'
  **deliverance:**

> "the voice of God like the sound of many waters, saying: It is done. Revelation 16:17"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 636_

### [⟲] The 1260 — BACKGROUND here, not recapitulated

- [⟲] Rev 15-16 **never mentions the 1260.** The plagues are **POST-1260** — they fall after
  the third angel's message, which follows the 538→1798 supremacy. **Do NOT blur:** no
  time-prophecy span runs inside this passage. The 1260 is concluded background, not a
  symbol re-met here.

## Appeal

- The God who drew the empires twice and dated the 1260 to the day is the same God who has
  set a **calendar for mercy itself.** "Wrath without mixture" is not cruelty — it is the
  day mercy's door closes because the last soul has chosen: _"The last soul has availed
  itself of the plan of salvation"_ (DAR 656).
- The first plague divides the whole world into **two classes already settled BEFORE the
  angel leaves the temple** (SSP 276) — the sealed and the marked. The plagues do not
  **create** the line; they **reveal** it.
- [Q] If the temple fills with smoke and no one can enter, the time to enter is **NOW,**
  while Christ still pleads. On which side of the seal does this plague find you? **Take your
  stand with the company already seen safe on the sea of glass** (Rev 15:2; SSP 269).

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: the seven last plagues = the unmixed wrath of Rev 14:10, poured
out AFTER the close of probation** (the temple filled with smoke, Rev 15:8) — literal,
future, one year, on the mark-of-the-beast class the reader already met. The Euphrates =
the Ottoman power re-met from Rev 9:14; Armageddon = the threefold union's final gathering;
Babylon's cup recapitulates forward to Rev 17-18.

[CHAIN] — **★ SANCTUARY payoff:** the smoke-filled temple is the END of the cleansing
ministry **Daniel 8:14** raised and **Rev 14:7** proclaimed; the close of probation answers
Dan 8:14. Hold it — Rev 17-18 will now show **WHO** Babylon is.
