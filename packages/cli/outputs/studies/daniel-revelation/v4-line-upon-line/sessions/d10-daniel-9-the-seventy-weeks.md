# Daniel 9 — The Seventy Weeks

**The angel who ran out of strength in Daniel 8 comes back to finish — and his very
first word, "determined," means CUT OFF. Seventy weeks are cut off from the 2300, the
same key-discipline now hands us a date, and the Bible does the math: Messiah at the 69
weeks, the cross at the midst, Israel's probation closing at 490 — and the 2300 landing
in 1844.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel 9 does not
> start a new prophecy — it finishes Daniel 8. Watch one Hebrew word ("cut off") forge a
> dated chain, line upon line.

---

## [⟲] The setup — Gabriel comes back to finish Daniel 8

- Daniel 8 ended with one thing **un-explained** — the **2300 days** (Dan 8:27,
  _"I was astonished at the vision, but none understood it"_). The **TIME** was the
  unfinished business.
- [⟲] **SAME messenger, SAME unfinished mission.** In Daniel 9 the same angel returns:

  > "Yea, whiles I was speaking in prayer, even the man Gabriel, whom I had seen in the
  > vision at the beginning... touched me." (Daniel 9:21)
  - this points **straight back to Dan 8**, where Gabriel was charged _"make this man to
    understand the vision"_ (Dan 8:16) and ran out of strength before he reached the 2300.

  > "The manner in which Gabriel introduces himself on this occasion, shows that he has
  > come to complete some unfulfilled mission. This can be nothing less than to carry out
  > the instruction to make this man 'understand the vision,' as recorded in chapter 8. 'I
  > am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding.'"
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 188_

- [⟲] **SAME period at issue.** Daniel 9 introduces **NO new prophecy** — Gabriel resumes
  Dan 8:14's 2300 days. Resolve the sanctuary distinction up front: Daniel was grieving
  the **earthly** temple, but the 2300-day vision concerns the **heavenly** sanctuary —
  distinct sanctuaries, one continuous period (DAR 187.1).
- [→] So Daniel 9 is the **answer key** to Daniel 8. Whatever Gabriel says next about the
  70 weeks is a **comment ON the 2300.** And the prophetic eye confirms it:

  > "for the express purpose of explaining to him the point which he had failed to
  > understand in the vision of the eighth chapter... unto two thousand and three hundred
  > days."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 326_

## [→] First word: "determined" = CUT OFF

The first word the angel speaks of the 70 weeks is the door to the whole chain. The
guides establish its force — this is the DAR/EGW expositional reading (not a bare
verse-key; the equivalence rests on the Hebrew, which is auxiliary, never load-bearing):

> "Seventy weeks are **determined** upon thy people and upon thy holy city." (Daniel 9:24)

- [→] **OVERLAY** (carries the guide): **"determined" (Heb. nehhtak) means "cut off"** —
  and a thing can only be cut off **FROM** something larger. The link is forced by
  context: the one period just mentioned (Dan 8:14) is the **2300 days.**

  > "there is no period from which the seventy weeks could be cut off but the 2300 days of
  > the previous vision... Cut off from what? — The 2300 days, most assuredly."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 192_

- [DYK🔎] **Hebrew is auxiliary, not load-bearing.** Gesenius glosses _nehhtak_
  "properly, to cut off; tropically, to divide; and so to determine, to decree" (DAR
  192.2). The point does not hinge on the lexicon — DAR and EGW carry it; the Hebrew only
  confirms what the guides establish.
- [→] EGW confirms the cut and the consequence — **this is the hinge of the whole
  session:**

  > "the seventy weeks must therefore be a part of the 2300 days, and the two periods must
  > begin together."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 326_
  - **70 weeks = the FIRST 490 years of the 2300**, both starting at the same point. Mark
    it: once the 490 is placed, the remainder of the 2300 places itself.

## The six things the 70 weeks must accomplish (Dan 9:24)

Gabriel names six works the 490 years will finish. Walk the clauses in order:

> "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the
> transgression, and to make an end of sins, and to make reconciliation for iniquity, and
> to bring in everlasting righteousness, and to seal up the vision and prophecy, and to
> anoint the most Holy." (Daniel 9:24)

- [→] DAR reads them straight off the cross and Israel's history (DAR 193.3):
  - **finish the transgression** = Israel filling the cup at the rejection / crucifixion
  - **make an end of sins** = sin-offerings ended at Calvary
  - **make reconciliation** = the sacrificial death of the Son of God
  - **everlasting righteousness** = Christ's sinless life
  - **vision and prophecy sealed** = tested-and-proved by these very events
- [→] Haskell weighs the verse — one verse, a whole nation's future:

  > "The entire future history of the Jews as a nation is contained in this one verse. No
  > other history ever crowded so much into so few words... the exact date for the
  > beginning of the work of Christ; the time allotted Israel as a nation for repentance."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 122_

[Q] One verse holds Israel's whole probation **and** the cross. If God dated mercy this
precisely once, does He improvise with anything?

## [→] "To anoint the most holy" = the heavenly sanctuary

The sixth clause is the one most easily misread as "anoint the Messiah." Decode it where
the walk reaches it — but as **OVERLAY**, carrying the guide quote, not a bare verse-key:

- [→] **"the most Holy" = the heavenly sanctuary**, not Christ Himself:

  > "The words translated 'most holy' are... (kodesh kodashim), the 'holy of holies,' an
  > expression which, according to Gesenius, applies to the most holy place in the
  > sanctuary, and which in no instance is applied to a person, unless this passage be an
  > exception."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 212_
  - so "to anoint the most holy" = the anointing of the **heavenly sanctuary** at the
    start of Christ's high-priestly ministry there.

- [⟲] this is the **Dan 8:14 sanctuary thread** (D09) re-surfacing: the 70 weeks **END**
  by pointing at the very sanctuary whose **CLEANSING** the 2300 days will reach. The
  first link of the chain (anointing, AD 27) and the last (cleansing, 1844) **bracket
  Christ's heavenly ministry.**
- [DYK🔎] flag the distinction the weave already settled: Daniel's **earthly** temple
  grief (the sanctuary he prayed about) vs. the **heavenly** sanctuary anointed here —
  different sanctuaries, **one** prophetic period.

## [→] The anchor: "the commandment to restore and build Jerusalem" = 457 BC

The cut needs a date. Dan 9:25 names the starting gun:

> "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth of the **commandment to
> restore and to build Jerusalem** unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and
> threescore and two weeks." (Daniel 9:25)

- [→] **OVERLAY** (not a verse-key): the commandment = the decree to **Ezra**,
  Artaxerxes' 7th year, **457 BC** (Ezra 7) — the only decree that restored the Jewish
  state civil, judicial, **AND** ecclesiastical:

  > "[empowered Ezra] to ordain laws, set magistrates and judges... to restore the Jewish
  > state, civil and ecclesiastical."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 198_

- [→] Haskell counts the **same** decree by its provisions:

  > "the third decree concerning the rebuilding of Jerusalem... arranged for the
  > appointment of magistrates and judges in Palestine from among the Jews themselves."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 126_

- [→] EGW reconciles the three-king decree (Cyrus / Darius / Artaxerxes) into one
  completed commandment:

  > "These three kings, in originating, reaffirming, and completing the decree, brought it
  > to the perfection required by the prophecy... Taking 457 B.C., the time when the decree
  > was completed."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 326_
  - and PK 698.1 fixes when it "went into effect in the autumn of 457 B.C."

- [DYK🔎] the date is **not a guess.** Ptolemy's canon places Artaxerxes' 7th year at 457
  BC, "demonstrated by the concurrent agreement of more than twenty eclipses" (DAR 205.2;
  SDP 131.1). Astronomy nails the anchor.

## The structure of the 70 weeks: 7 + 62 + 1 (Dan 9:25-27)

The verse itself divides the period: "seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks" to
Messiah (v25), and v27 adds the final "one week."

- [→] Haskell lays out the three blocks:

  > "Seven weeks for building the walls and streets of Jerusalem; threescore and two (62)
  > weeks till the work of Messiah; and one week... devoted to the confirmation of the
  > covenant."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 123_

- **7 + 62 + 1 = 70.** The seven weeks (49 yrs, **457 → 408 BC**) = rebuilding the city
  (DAR 201.1).
- [→] year-day reminder: 70 weeks = 490 prophetic days = **490 years**; the 69 = **483.**
  Carry it forward.

## [KEY🔑] The year-day principle — a day for a year

Before any date can be claimed, justify the scale. The Bible defines its own measure in
its own words — this is the one true verse-self-definition the chain rests on, reached by
cross-reference to where Scripture states the rule outright:

> "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, **each day
> for a year**, shall ye bear your iniquities." (Numbers 14:34)

> "...I have appointed thee **each day for a year**." (Ezekiel 4:6)

- [KEY🔑] a prophetic day = a literal **year**; a prophetic year = 360 days. EGW states
  it plainly as the rule of decoding:

  > "A day in prophecy stands for a year. See Numbers 14:34; Ezekiel 4:6."
  > — _EGW, The Desire of Ages, p. 233_

- [⟲] **SAME principle the reader already used** at Dan 7:25 (the 1260) and Dan 8:14 (the
  2300). Not a new tool — the same key. The proof is that "the prophecies have actually
  been fulfilled on this principle" (DAR 197.3).
- [→] So **69 weeks = 483 years**; **70 weeks = 490 years**; and the **2300 = 2300
  years.** The arithmetic is now licensed.

## [→] The 70th week: confirm the covenant; the midst = the cross, AD 31

> "And he shall **confirm the covenant** with many for one week: and **in the midst of the
> week** he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease." (Daniel 9:27)

- [→] **"confirm the covenant... one week"** = the final **7 years (AD 27-34)** the gospel
  went specially to the Jews — 3.5 yrs by Christ, 3.5 by the apostles:

  > "For seven years after the Saviour entered on His ministry, the gospel was to be
  > preached especially to the Jews; for three and a half years by Christ Himself; and
  > afterward by the apostles."
  > — _EGW, The Desire of Ages, p. 233_

- [→] **"midst of the week"** = spring **AD 31** (AD 27 + 3.5): the **crucifixion.** The
  veil rent, type met antitype:

  > "the inner veil of the temple was rent from top to bottom... sacrifice and oblation
  > were forever done away."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 130_

  > "In the spring of A.D. 31, Christ the true sacrifice was offered on Calvary."
  > — _EGW, The Desire of Ages, p. 233_

- [→] DAR ties the ceasing to the cross — "there they did virtually come to an end" (DAR
  202.1) — and fixes the spring AD 31 date by "thirteen credible authorities," reckoning
  3.5 back and 3.5 forward from the midst (DAR 204.3).

## [⟲] Two princes, one chapter — do NOT blur them (Dan 9:25 vs 9:26)

Here is where the interpretive risk concentrates: **adjacent verses both say "prince," and
they are DIFFERENT actors.** This is the worked-error the whole method exists to avoid.

> "Messiah the Prince" (Daniel 9:25) — vs. — "the people of **the prince that shall
> come** shall destroy the city and the sanctuary" (Daniel 9:26)

- [⟲] **Dan 9:25 "Messiah THE Prince" = Christ**, the Anointed Redeemer.
- [⟲] **Dan 9:26 "the prince that shall come" = the ROMAN military power (Titus)**, whose
  people destroy the city and the sanctuary:

  > "[the prophet] speaks of the soon-following destruction of Jerusalem by the Roman
  > power; and finally of the destruction of that power itself."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 211_

- [→] **Never collapse the anointed Redeemer into the general of the desolating army.**
  One prince **saves**; the other **sacks.** Same word, opposite men — the discipline the
  whole series teaches (cf. Dan 2 feet = Dan 7 horns vs. Dan 2 toes = Rev 17 ten: same vs.
  different).
- [→] historical landing: _"Messiah [shall be] cut off, but not for himself"_ (Dan 9:26)
  at the cross **AD 31**; then Jerusalem destroyed by Rome **AD 70** (DAR 211.2).

## [CHAIN] The master arithmetic — 490 cut from 2300 lands the rest in 1844

Now the payoff the whole session was built for. The 70 weeks were **CUT OFF** ("determined"
= cut off, section 2) from the 2300 — so once the 490 is placed, the remainder **places
itself.**

- [→] the math, plainly: **2300 − 490 = 1810** years still to run after the 70 weeks end.
  The 490 ended autumn AD 34; **AD 34 + 1810 = autumn 1844:**

  > "to A.D. 34, autumn, add 1810, and we have the autumn of A.D. 1844."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 206_

  > "Eighteen hundred and ten years remained, 2300-490 = 1810... we have the year 1844
  > A.D.... It was the investigative judgment."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 131_

- [→] EGW seals the whole arch:

  > "The seventy weeks—490 days—having been cut off from the 2300, there were 1810 days
  > remaining... From A.D. 34, 1810 years extend to 1844. Consequently the 2300 days of
  > Daniel 8:14 terminate in 1844."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 328_

- [⟲] this is the **Dan 8:14 sanctuary / 2300 thread** (D08-D09) finally **DATED.** Daniel
  9 did not start a new prophecy — it gave the 2300 its **anchor.** _"The sanctuary shall
  be cleansed"_ (Dan 8:14) now has an address: **1844.** (Pays off in Revelation: R07
  little book / R11 the judgment hour.)
