# Revelation 11 — The Two Witnesses

**The Word of God is its own witness — and the same Bible that named a 1260-year
sackcloth in Daniel now shows it kept, killed, and raised on history's own calendar.
Two witnesses, one Word, Old Testament and New; obscured 538–1798, slain three and a
half years in atheist France, then lifted higher than ever. We do not assert it — we
read the dates off the page, and the chapter closes by throwing the temple doors open
on 1844.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Revelation 11
> names its own figure (olive trees / candlesticks) and points us to Zechariah to
> decode it — then the chapter walks the Word through fire and out the other side, line
> upon line.

## [KEY🔑] The verse names its own figure — two olive trees, two candlesticks

The chapter introduces the witnesses, then immediately hands us the symbol — and points
us **outward** to decode it:

> "And I will give power unto my two witnesses, and they shall prophesy a thousand two
> hundred and threescore days, clothed in sackcloth. These are the two olive trees, and
> the two candlesticks standing before the God of the earth." (Revelation 11:3-4)

- [KEY🔑] this is the **only in-text self-definition** in the chapter: the verse tells
  us the witnesses **ARE the two olive trees and the two candlesticks** (Rev 11:4)
- but keep the key honest — the verse hands us the **figure** (olive trees /
  candlesticks) and points us to its source. Rev 11:4 quotes **Zechariah's vision word
  for word.**

**[⟲] Reach to Zechariah 4 — the Bible's own dictionary entry for this symbol:**

> "...a candlestick all of gold... and two olive trees by it, one upon the right side
> of the bowl, and the other upon the left side thereof... Then he answered and spake
> unto me, saying, This is the word of the LORD unto Zerubbabel, saying, Not by might,
> nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the LORD of hosts... Then answered I, and said
> unto him, What are these two olive trees upon the right side of the candlestick and
> upon the left side thereof?" (Zechariah 4:2-6, 11)

- [⟲] the golden candlestick fed by two olive trees — _"Not by might, nor by power, but
  by my spirit"_ — is the source Rev 11:4 reaches back to. The verse defines the
  **figure**; the next section establishes **who the figure is.**

## [→] Who the witnesses ARE — the Old and New Testaments

The identification **"two witnesses = OT and NT"** is the historicist reading drawn
**from Zechariah 4** — not the bare words of Rev 11. It is an overlay, so it **carries
the establishing guide quotes:**

- **why the Word:** _"in the mouth of two witnesses every word is established"_; written
  testimony is stronger than oral; the OT — _"they are they which testify of me"_; the
  NT records the works that bear witness
- [→] two witnesses, one Word — the Bible testifying of itself in two voices that agree

> "the two olive trees are taken to represent the word of God... These declarations and
> considerations are sufficient to sustain the conclusion that the Old and New
> Testaments... are Christ's two witnesses."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 499_

> "The 'two witnesses' are the Old and the New Testaments. In the mouth of two witnesses
> every word is established. The Old Testament told of the God, who strove to live in
> man; the New Testament told of the God, who had lived in the human form, and the two
> agree."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 197_

- [DYK🔎] the candlestick is the **church**; the golden oil that lights it flows **only**
  through the two olive trees. Cut that connection — sever the church from the Word — and
  you get the darkness the next section dates:

> "By the prophet Zechariah, the church is represented as a golden candlestick having
> seven branches, each bearing aloft a light for the world. These seven branches receive
> their oil from a single bowl, and the oil for this bowl is supplied by two olive trees,
> one on either side."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 197_

> "When connection with the living trees is severed, spiritual death is the result...
> the twelve hundred and sixty years of darkness, brought about by severing the
> connection between the church and the two witnesses."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 198_

And the prophetic eye confirms the same identity:

> "The two witnesses represent the Scriptures of the Old and the New Testament. Both are
> important testimonies to the origin and perpetuity of the law of God... point forward
> to a Saviour to come... tell of a Saviour who has come."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 267_
