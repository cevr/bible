# Revelation 19 — The Triumph of the Saints

**The same future the STONE smashed and the COURT condemned is now executed in person:
heaven sings over fallen Babylon, the bride is made ready, and the Rider — KING OF KINGS —
comes to do what the prophecies promised. Every symbol here is read off the page: the
bride's linen is named, the testimony of Jesus is named, the Rider's name is named. The
overlay only tells us WHEN — and it is the second coming.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Revelation 19 hands us
> three labels in its own words — fine linen, testimony of Jesus, the Word of God — and the
> guides supply only the WHEN. Watch the verdict of Daniel become a Rider, line upon line.

---

## The setup — the song over a fallen Babylon

- Rev 19 continues Rev 18: the redeemed strike up the **song of triumph** as great Babylon
  is destroyed
- [→] this is **not a new scene out of nowhere** — the harlot of R13 (Rev 17-18) has just
  fallen; ch. 19 opens with **heaven's verdict ON that fall**
- the song is sung _"in connection with the second coming of Christ at the commencement of
  the thousand years"_ — the redeemed sing on **victor harps** when they behold the complete
  destruction of the system of opposition to God

> "CONTINUING the subject of chapter 18, the apostle here introduces the song of triumph
> which the redeemed saints strike up on victor harps, when they behold the complete
> destruction of that great system of opposition to God and his true worship comprehended in
> great Babylon. This destruction takes place, and this song is sung, in connection with the
> second coming of Christ at the commencement of the thousand years."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 680_

> "And he 'heard a great voice of much people in heaven,' the greatest chorus the universe
> ever heard; that in which all voices unite in singing, 'Alleluia; salvation, and glory, and
> honor, and power, unto the Lord our God.'"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 316_

[→] Frame the chapter's shape: it moves from the **FALL of the false woman** (vv.1-3)
straight to the **MARRIAGE of the true woman** (vv.7-9) — **two women, one chapter.** Keep
them apart; the whole interpretive risk of this chapter is blurring them.

## [→] "Her smoke rose up for ever and ever" (Rev 19:3) — limited language, not eternal torment

> "And again they said, Alleluia. And her smoke rose up for ever and ever." (Revelation 19:3)

- [→] this is the historicist **overlay**, NOT a self-defining verse — so it carries the
  **guide quote**, not a KEY
- the expression _"smoke going up forever"_ is used **three times** in Scripture (Isa 34 of
  Idumea, Rev 14 of the beast's worshipers, Rev 19 of Babylon) — all of them point to the
  **same time**: the destruction visited on this earth at the second advent

> "Three times this expression of smoke going up forever is used in the Bible: once here in
> Isaiah 34, of the land of Idumea as a figure of the earth; in Revelation 14 (which see), of
> the worshipers of the beast and his image; and again in the chapter we are now considering,
> referring to the destruction of great Babylon; and all of them apply to the very same time,
> and describe the same scenes; namely, the destruction visited upon this earth, the
> worshipers of the beast, and all the pomp of great Babylon, at the second advent of our Lord
> and Saviour."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 680_

- **Scripture-its-own-expositor move:** read _"for ever"_ by its **own** biblical usage (Isa 34) — it signifies **utter, final, irreversible destruction**, not eternal conscious
  torment. The smoke is the mark of sin **gone**, not sin endlessly burning.

> "When the last trace of sin was gone, and the smoke of the final burning had ascended up
> forever and ever, they burst forth in unrestrained accents, saying, 'True and righteous are
> His judgments.'"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 317_

[DYK🔎] Same phrase, three passages, one event — the Bible defines its own "for ever." The
smoke marks **the universe's relief**, the moment sin is blotted out.

## [KEY🔑] The bride's FINE LINEN = the righteousness of saints (Rev 19:8)

> "Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come,
> and his wife hath made herself ready. And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in
> fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints."
> (Revelation 19:7-8)

- [KEY🔑] the verse defines its **OWN** symbol, in-text: _"for the fine linen is the
  righteousness of saints"_ (Rev 19:8) — we do not interpret it, we **read the label**
- this is the **FIRST self-defining symbol** of the chapter — **fine linen = the
  righteousness of saints**

> "The redeemed are clothed in the wedding garments, linen clean and white, which is the
> righteousness of Christ worn by the saints."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 318_

### [⟲] The bride's linen vs. the harlot's purple and scarlet — a DIFFERENT garment

The chapter's danger is reading the bride as if she were the harlot just destroyed. The
**garments** keep them apart:

> "And the woman was arrayed in purple and scarlet colour, and decked with gold and precious
> stones and pearls..." (Revelation 17:4)

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT instance:** the bride's **fine linen** (Rev 19:8) vs. the harlot's
  **purple and scarlet** (Rev 17:4) — **contrasting symbols, not the same.** The bride is
  clothed in **pure white righteousness**; the harlot in **corrupt false-religious
  authority.** One garment is granted from heaven; the other is worn by Babylon.

[→] First key handed us, first weave resolved: the true woman is **not** the false woman.
Hold the two apart for the rest of the chapter.

## [→] The marriage of the Lamb — the bride is the New Jerusalem; the church is the GUESTS

> "...the marriage of the Lamb is come... And he saith unto me, Write, Blessed are they
> which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb." (Revelation 19:7, 9)

- [→] **OVERLAY** (not a KEY — the verse does not define _"bride" = New Jerusalem_ in-text;
  the **guides** establish it):

> "The Lamb's wife is the New Jerusalem which is above... The marriage of the Lamb is his
> reception of this city. When he receives this city, he receives it as the glory and
> metropolis of his kingdom; hence with it he receives his kingdom, and the throne of his
> father David."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 682_

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT woman:** the bride of Rev 19:7 is **NOT** the harlot of Rev 17 just
  destroyed. **Two different women in the same chapter** — the **holy city** vs. **great
  Babylon.**

And the prophetic eye locks the same reading — and adds a guardrail: if the church are the
**guests**, they cannot also be the **bride**:

> "The marriage represents the reception by Christ of His kingdom. The Holy City, the New
> Jerusalem, which is the capital and representative of the kingdom, is called 'the bride, the
> Lamb's wife.'... In the Revelation the people of God are said to be the guests at the
> marriage supper. Revelation 19:9. If guests, they cannot be represented also as the bride."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 426_

### [⟲] Weave back to Daniel 7 — the marriage IS the reception of the kingdom

- [⟲] **SAME event, two pictures:** Christ _"came to the Ancient of days"_ (Dan 7:13) and
  _"there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom"_ (Dan 7:14) — the **reception of
  the kingdom** is the **marriage of Christ**

> "This is the marriage of Christ, and the work of the sealing angel is to put a sign upon
> those of the last generation who are prepared for the wedding supper... Probation closes when
> the last guest has accepted the invitation."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 315_

- [→] **probation closes when the last guest accepts** — the close of probation flagged
  here is the hinge the whole series has built toward

And the supper itself is the New Testament feasting promise, kept:

> "Many are the allusions to this marriage supper in the New Testament... It is the time when
> we shall drink the fruit of the vine new with our Redeemer in his heavenly kingdom. Matthew
> 26:29; Mark 14:25; Luke 22:18. It is the time when we shall sit at his table in the kingdom
> (Luke 22:30), and he will gird himself and come forth and serve us. Luke 12:37."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 683_

> "...the redeemed with the Redeemer shall sit down to the marriage supper of the Lamb."
> — _EGW, The Desire of Ages, p. 151_

## [KEY🔑] The TESTIMONY OF JESUS = the spirit of prophecy (Rev 19:10)

> "And I fell at his feet to worship him. And he said unto me, See thou do it not: I am thy
> fellowservant, and of thy brethren that have the testimony of Jesus: worship God: for the
> testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy." (Revelation 19:10)

- [KEY🔑] the verse defines its **OWN** symbol, in-text: **testimony of Jesus = the spirit
  of prophecy** — the **SECOND self-defining key** of the chapter
- the verse cross-locks to Rev 22:9, where the same angel names the same brethren — **the
  prophets:**

> "In Revelation 22:9, he repeats the same in substance, as follows: 'I am thy fellow servant,
> and of thy brethren the prophets.' From the comparison we see the force of the expression,
> 'The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy.'"
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 143_

[DYK🔎] The angel **refuses worship** — a guardrail against creature-worship, in the very
chapter that is about **WHO is truly King.** Worship God only. The label tells us what the
testimony of Jesus is; the refusal tells us where worship belongs.

## [KEY🔑] The Rider on the white horse — "his name is called The Word of God" (Rev 19:13)

> "And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and he that sat upon him was called
> Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war... And he was clothed
> with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God." (Revelation
> 19:11-13)

- [KEY🔑] the verse **names the Rider IN-TEXT:** _"his name is called The Word of God"_ (Rev
  19:13) — the **THIRD self-defining symbol.** This, not the overlay, is the KEY: we read the
  name off the page.

[→] **OVERLAY — WHO and WHEN:** the guides supply the timing — this is **Christ at His
literal second coming**, riding to battle:

> "With verse 11 a new scene is introduced. We are here carried back to the second coming of
> Christ, this time under the symbol of a warrior riding forth to battle... His vesture is
> dipped in blood. (See a description of the same scene in Isaiah 63:1-4.) The armies of
> heaven, the angels of God, follow him."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 684_

> "At the head of the forces, rode the Commander in chief of all the hosts of heaven. He was
> clothed in a vesture dipped in blood... He was seated on a pure white horse, a sign of
> royalty. On His head He wore many crowns in token of the victories won. To His devoted
> followers, the name of the Commander was 'Faithful and True.'... but aside from these letters
> He had a name known only to Himself and the Father."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 319_

The contrast that makes the scene land — the gentlest becomes the warrior:

> "For the first time in all ages, Christ goes from heaven as a warrior... For six thousand
> years He has been the gentlest of the gentle... But when He comes... His eyes flash flames
> of fire... out of His mouth goeth a sharp sword."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 320_

And the prophetic eye saw the same Rider, crowned and flaming:

> "A crown of glory rested upon His holy brow. Upon His vesture and thigh was a name written,
> King of kings, and Lord of lords... His eyes were as a flame of fire."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 286_

## [→] KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS (Rev 19:16) — the priest has become the King

> "And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF
> LORDS." (Revelation 19:16)

- [→] **OVERLAY** (the title's **MEANING** is established by the guides, not self-defined):

> "Christ has at this time closed his mediatorial work, and laid off his priestly robes for
> kingly attire; for he has on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, King of kings and
> Lord of lords."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 685_

- this is the transition the **whole series has built toward: priest → King.** It connects to
  the close of probation flagged at SSP 315 (the marriage section) — **mediation is OVER.**

> "Jesus rides forth as a mighty conqueror. Not now a 'Man of Sorrows,' to drink the bitter
> cup of shame and woe, He comes, victor in heaven and earth, to judge the living and the
> dead. 'Faithful and True,' 'in righteousness He doth judge and make war.' And 'the armies
> which were in heaven' (Revelation 19:11, 14) follow Him... 'And He hath on His vesture and on
> His thigh a name written, King of kings, and Lord of lords.'"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 640_

- [⟲] **CHAIN echo (thematic, NOT a sourced weave):** the **everlasting kingdom awarded in
  the Dan 7 courtroom** (Dan 7:14) is here **WORN as a title** — the verdict made visible.
  (Note: the often-cited Dan 2:44 stone = rider link is **not** sourced in this digest, so we
  keep the Daniel 7 connection only as a **thematic chain**, not a guide-backed [⟲].)

## [→] The beast and the false prophet cast alive into the lake of fire (Rev 19:19-20)

> "And I saw the beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together to
> make war against him that sat on the horse, and against his army. And the beast was taken,
> and with him the false prophet that wrought miracles before him... These both were cast
> alive into a lake of fire burning with brimstone." (Revelation 19:19-20)

- [→] **OVERLAY — the false prophet IDENTIFIED** (a guide reading, not a self-defining KEY):

> "The false prophet is the one that works miracles before the beast. This proves him to be
> identical with the two-horned beast of chapter 13... The fact that these are cast alive into
> the lake of fire, shows that these powers will not pass away and be succeeded by others, but
> be living powers at the second advent of Christ."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 685_

### [⟲] Three SAME-instance weaves — this is the continuous career of ONE power

- [⟲] **SAME beast:** this beast is the **same papacy tracked from Daniel** — _"its overthrow
  is emphatically predicted... notably in Daniel 7:11."_ Not a different beast — the **climax
  of one continuous career.**

> "The papacy has long been in the field, and has come to the closing scenes in its career.
> And its overthrow is emphatically predicted in other prophecies than the one now before us,
> notably in Daniel 7:11, in which the prophet says that he beheld till the beast was slain,
> and his body destroyed and given to the burning flame. And this followed close upon the
> utterance of great words which the horn spake, which words were doubtless heard in the decree
> of papal infallibility in the great ecumenical council of 1870."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 686_

- [⟲] **SAME false prophet:** DAR 685 explicitly equates the false prophet with the
  **two-horned beast of Rev 13** — _"the same work, for the very same purpose."_ Same actor,
  same miracles, same deception about the mark. **Not a new figure.**
- [⟲] **SAME 1260:** the beast cast in here is the **same horn** that _"made war with the
  saints"_ for **42 months** (Rev 13:5 = Dan 7:25 = Rev 12:6/14). The 1260 powers that
  **oppressed** the saints are the same powers that **perish** at the advent.

> "The beast in Europe and the false prophet in the United States, having blended their
> forces... fall before Him who sits on the white horse... these both are cast alive into the
> lake of fire."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 321_

## [⟲] The lake of fire (Rev 19:20) — the FIRST revelation, not the last

The lake of fire opens **twice** in Revelation — and they must not be blurred:

- [⟲] **RECAPITULATED instance:** the lake of fire of **Rev 19:20** vs. **Rev 20:10-15** —
  **same instrument, two chronological stages.** The beast and false prophet go in **at the
  second advent** (Rev 19:20); the **full, final** use comes at the **close of the 1000
  years** (Rev 20:10-15). Do **NOT** blur them.

> "The great and final display of the 'winepress of God's wrath,' and also of 'the lake of
> fire,' occurs at the end of the thousand years, as described in chapter 20... But the
> destruction of the living wicked at the second coming of Christ, at the beginning of the
> thousand years, furnishes a scene on a smaller scale, similar, in both these respects, to
> what takes place at the close of that period."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 685_

> "This is the first revelation of the lake of fire."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 321_

[→] **Teaser into R15:** the lake of fire opened here is opened **AGAIN** at the millennium's
end — **recapitulation, the series' governing move.** Hold the first stage; R15 needs it.

## [→] Two suppers — a feast of death vs. the marriage supper of the Lamb (Rev 19:9, 17-18)

The chapter sets **two suppers** side by side — Haskell's chapter title is literally **"The
Two Suppers."**

> "And I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the
> fowls that fly in the midst of heaven, Come and gather yourselves together unto the supper
> of the great God; That ye may eat the flesh of kings, and the flesh of captains... and the
> flesh of all men, both free and bond, both small and great." (Revelation 19:17-18)

> "It is over. Those who sought to slay the truth,—men from every kindred, representing all
> classes, lie dead, slain by the Word which they rejected. And while Christ returns to heaven
> with the redeemed, the fowls of the heavens devour the bodies of the slain. This is one
> supper,—a feast of death. What a contrast to the marriage supper of the Lamb!"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 323_

[→] **The structural payoff:** the chapter that **OPENED** with the marriage supper of the
Lamb (v.9, the redeemed) **CLOSES** with the supper of the great God (vv.17-18, the wicked) —
**two tables, two destinies, decided by one Rider.**

## Appeal

- The God who drew this future in the **STONE** (Dan 2:44) and condemned the beast in the
  **COURT** (Dan 7:11, 26) now **executes the verdict in person** — the Rider whose name is
  _The Word of God_, **KING OF KINGS.**
- There are **two suppers, and only two:** the **marriage supper of the Lamb** for the bride
  clothed in the righteousness of saints, or the **supper of the great God** for those slain
  by the Word they rejected (SSP 323). **There is no third table.**
- Christ has **laid off the priestly robe for kingly attire** (DAR 685) — mediation has an
  end; probation closes _"when the last guest has accepted the invitation"_ (SSP 315). The
  wedding garment of fine linen (Rev 19:8) is **granted, not earned** — but it must be **PUT
  ON now.**
- [Q] **Which supper is your name written for?** The bride _"hath made herself ready"_ (Rev
  19:7) — readiness is a **present decision**, not a future scramble. Take your place at the
  table of the One who returns as King.

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: the STONE and the COURT, executed.** The Rider on the white horse =
Christ at His literal second coming (DAR 684), and the beast cast into the lake of fire = the
SAME papacy slain in Dan 7:11 (DAR 686). The Daniel 7 judgment-verdict and the everlasting
kingdom (Dan 7:14, worn as **KING OF KINGS**) are now executed in history. Carry forward the
**three self-defined keys** this chapter handed us — fine linen = righteousness of saints (Rev
19:8), testimony of Jesus = spirit of prophecy (Rev 19:10), the Rider's name = The Word of God
(Rev 19:13) — and the **recapitulation flag:** the lake of fire opened here (Rev 19:20) is the
FIRST revelation; it opens again at the millennium's end (Rev 20:10-15). R15 needs it.
