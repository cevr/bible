# Daniel 11 — The Kings of the North and South

**Daniel said it plainly: this prophecy is given "mostly in plain language," not figures
and symbols. So we do not decode this chapter — we WATCH it. Fix ONE geographic key off
the page (north and south, reckoned from Palestine), and the whole sweep reads itself:
Persia, Greece, Rome, the papacy, down to the time of the end.**

> The rule of the road: most of Daniel is symbol we decode; Daniel 11 is plain speech we
> watch. Set one compass — north and south — and follow the king of the north line upon
> line, all the way to the verse still ahead of us.

---

## The setup — a prophecy in plain language, not symbols

- Years after Dan 8's symbolic ram and goat, the angel now gives Daniel **the same
  future in plain speech** — kings, marriages, wars, taxes, named to the event (Dan 11)
- [→] **the hermeneutical key for the whole session:** this chapter is **not** a
  symbol-vision to be decoded; it is **plain prediction to be watched** against history

> "We now enter upon a prophecy of future events, clothed not in figures and symbols, as
> in the visions of chapters 2, 7, and 8, but given mostly in plain language. Many of the
> signal events of the world's history, from the days of Daniel to the end of the world,
> are here brought to view."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 222_

- [→] **consequence for method:** because the language is plain, almost nothing here is a
  [KEY🔑] — no verse says "X are Y." The work is to **fix ONE geographic key, then let
  history confirm.** Watch for the difference from every chapter before this one.
- [⟲] this is the **same vision begun in Dan 10** — Gabriel still speaking; the angel's
  ministry of the opening verse reaches back into Daniel 10's unseen war (Michael, Dan
  11:1). The prophetic eye confirms the angel-ministry:

> "The reign of Darius was honored of God. To him was sent the angel Gabriel, 'to confirm
> and to strengthen him.' Daniel 11:1."
> — _EGW, Prophets and Kings, p. 556_

[DYK🔎] Dan 11:1 ties back to the curtain pulled aside in Dan 10 — heavenly beings at
work behind earthly thrones. The kings march below; the real contest is above.

## [→] The one key to fix first — north and south = territory, reckoned from Palestine

Two titles run the whole chapter. The verses **name** them but do **not define** them — so
this is a [→] guide-overlay, never a bare [KEY🔑]:

> "And the king of the south shall be strong..." (Daniel 11:5)

> "...the king's daughter of the south shall come to the king of the north to make an
> agreement..." (Daniel 11:6)

- [→] **the governing rule, sourced to DAR:** the title is **geographic**, reckoned from
  **Palestine**, the prophet's homeland. Whoever holds the **northern** territory **is**
  the king of the north; whoever holds the **southern** **is** the king of the south — for
  as long as they hold it.

> "...whatever power at any time should occupy the territory which at first constituted
> the kingdom of the north, that power, so long as it occupied that territory, would be
> the king of the north; and whatever power should occupy that which at first constituted
> the kingdom of the south, that power would so long be the king of the south."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 224_

- [→] this single rule is **why the chapter never switches hermeneutics:** the actors
  change, the compass-poles do not. **Egypt = south; Syria** (the Seleucid territory,
  including Thrace and Asia Minor) **= north** — both reckoned from the glorious land
  between them.
- [Q] If the title follows the territory, then at every verse there is only one question:
  **who holds the northern ground now?** Hold that question — it carries us to v.45.

## The two divisions — Seleucid Syria (north) vs. Ptolemaic Egypt (south), vv. 5-13

- vv. 5-13 walk the wars of Alexander's successors: **Ptolemaic Egypt** and **Seleucid
  Syria** — the literal first occupants of south and north
- [→] **why the northern line, not the southern, is the dominant one** — sourced to
  Haskell. The northern (Greco-Syriac) division carried forward the work of the
  leopard/goat and produced the little horn of Dan 8; the south merely "rises and opposes":

> "The kingdom of Alexander resolved itself into two divisions, a northern and a
> southern... It was the northern division which carried forward the work of the
> prophecy... and it was from the northern division that the little horn of Daniel 8
> proceeded... Nevertheless there will be throughout the ages until the end of time a
> strength rising from the south and opposing the northern power."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 193_

- [→] Haskell's structural key sets up everything ahead: **follow the king of the
  NORTH**; the south is the persistent opposer. This is why Rome, then the papacy, then
  Turkey all inherit the northern title — the line never breaks, the occupant changes.

## [→] Rome enters — the fourth beast steps onto the stage, vv. 14-22

The prophecy's first sight of Rome arrives quietly — "robbers of thy people" (v. 14):

> "...the introduction... of the mightiest kingdom of the earth, and at the same time the
> greatest enemy which the people of God ever had to meet... Daniel had seen this kingdom
> before. In the vision of the seventh chapter, Rome was represented as a beast too
> terrible to name."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 200_

- [⟲] **weave (same referent):** this Rome is the **same fourth beast of Dan 7** — "too
  terrible to name" — and the **iron legs of Dan 2.** Not a new power; the reader already
  met it twice. Recapitulation, identical referent, third picture.
- [→] **v. 16 — Rome stands in "the glorious land"** = Pompey's conquest of Jerusalem,
  B.C. 63 (overlay reading, DAR 234). Note: "glorious land" is **named** in v.16 but not
  self-defined — carry the guide quote.
- [→] **vv. 19-22 — the three Caesars:** Julius (stumbles and falls), Augustus (the
  "raiser of taxes"), Tiberius (the "vile person" of v.21) — overlay reading, DAR 244.

## [⟲] The prince of the covenant = Christ crucified, v. 22

In the middle of the kings'-wars narrative, one figure is **named but not defined** — the
definition comes by weave to Daniel 9:

> "...yea, also the prince of the covenant." (Daniel 11:22)

- [⟲] **weave (same — internal to Daniel):** "prince of the covenant" (Dan 11:22) =
  "**Messiah the Prince**" who "confirm[s] the covenant" (Dan 9:25-27). **One individual,
  one event** — Christ cut off under Tiberius. No recapitulation, no second referent.

> "'The prince of the covenant' unquestionably refers to Jesus Christ, 'the Messiah the
> Prince,' who was to 'confirm the covenant' one week with his people. Daniel 9:25-27...
> namely, the cutting off of the Prince of the covenant, or, in other words, the death of
> our Lord Jesus Christ."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 243_

- [→] the **cross sits at the heart** of the plain-prophecy chapter — even the
  kings'-wars narrative pauses to mark Calvary. The prophecy of empires bows for a moment
  at one death.

## [→] The hinge — from kingdoms to the MYSTERY OF INIQUITY, vv. 23-30

- [→] **structural hinge in Haskell's reading:** from v.23 the prophecy stops tracking
  governments-as-governments and starts tracking a **POWER swaying the governments** — the
  "mystery of iniquity" already at work in Paul's day:

> "In Paul's day... that power was at work. Hitherto the history... dealt with earthly
> kingdoms, but from this time on history handles this 'mystery of iniquity' which worked
> through the various governments. The distinction between the kingdoms of the north and
> the south remains as it was in the past, but we pass from governments as governments to
> a power which is swaying these governments."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 222_

Paul names this power for us — the lens that reads the verses ahead:

> "...the mystery of iniquity doth already work... that man of sin... so that he as God
> sitteth in the temple of God... This is Paul's description of the mystery of iniquity,
> the fourth beast of the vision of Daniel seven."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 221_

- [⟲] **weave (same):** the "mystery of iniquity" = the **little horn of Dan 7** =
  Paul's "**man of sin**" (2 Thess 2). **Same power, three names**; not a new actor.
- [→] **v. 24 "even for a time"** = a prophetic time (360 years), B.C. 31 (Actium) to A.D.
  330 (the seat of empire moves to Constantinople) — overlay reading, DAR 249.

## [→] The abomination set up = the papacy, A.D. 538 — the "daily" first removed, vv. 30-31

The verse names the act in-text; the **identification** is the guide overlay:

> "...take away the daily sacrifice, and they shall place the abomination that maketh
> desolate." (Daniel 11:31)

- [→] **two sequential desolating powers, not one** — sourced to DAR. "Sacrifice" is a
  supplied word; read "**desolation**." The "**daily**" = **paganism**; the "**abomination
  of desolation**" = the **papacy** that succeeds it:

> "...sacrifice is a word erroneously supplied; that it should be desolation... The
> 'daily' desolation was paganism, the 'abomination of desolation' is the papacy."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 255_

- [⟲] **weave (DIFFERENT — guard the verbal trap):** the "abomination" of Dan 11:31 is
  **papal** (begins A.D. 538). Christ's "abomination of desolation" in Matt 24:15 points
  back to Dan 9 / **Jerusalem's destruction.** Same phrase, **different referent,
  different fulfillment** — do not blur them.
- [→] **the staging:** 508 the last pagan resistance falls, the "daily" is taken away; 538
  the way opens for the papacy:

> "...the way was clear for the papacy to sit enthroned in Rome... the harvest of the seed
> sown in the days of Constantine was reaped in the reign of Justinian."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 233_

- [→] **the mechanism:** Justinian's decree A.D. 533 declaring the bishop of Rome "chief
  of the whole ecclesiastical body" (DAR 259); the Gothic horn plucked up in 538:

> "...this must therefore be taken as the year when this abomination was placed, or set
> up... the point from which to date the predicted 1260 years."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 262_

## [⟲] The 1260 years of papal supremacy — the SAME span, multiple wordings, vv. 32-35

- vv. 33-35 the "many days" of persecution = the **1260 years** (538–1798):

> "...is called, 'a time, times, and the dividing of time;' 'a time, times, and a half;'
> 'a thousand two hundred and threescore days;' and 'forty and two months.' It is the 1260
> years of papal supremacy."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 263_

- [⟲] **weave (SAME — the series' anchor weave, re-met from D07):** Dan 11:33 = Dan 7:25
  = Rev 12:6,14 = Rev 13:5. **Four wordings, ONE span of 1260 years** — not four periods,
  not recapitulations of different events. The same **538–1798**.
- [→] Haskell ties the dates of two great prophecies together:

> "...as 457 B.C.... marks the beginning of... the 2300 days of Daniel 8:14[,] the
> latter, which witnessed the setting up of the papacy [538 A.D.], is the date from which
> to reckon... the twelve hundred and sixty days of Daniel 7:25. It is to the beginning of
> this period, the year 538, that the thirty-first verse of Daniel 11 brings the history."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 235_

- [→] and the character of the era, in one line:

> "The noontide of the papacy was the world's moral midnight."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 237_

And the prophetic eye confirms the same span, by the same dates, named in both Daniel and
Revelation:

> "In the sixth century the papacy had become firmly established... Paganism had given
> place to the papacy... And now began the 1260 years of papal oppression foretold in the
> prophecies of Daniel and the Revelation. Daniel 7:25; Revelation 13:5-7."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 54_

## [→] "The time of the end" begins 1798 — papal supremacy falls, v. 35

"Time of the end" is **named, not self-dated**; the dating is the guide overlay:

> "...even to the time of the end: because it is yet for a time appointed." (Daniel 11:35)

- [→] the 1260 closes when papal civil supremacy is **taken away** — **1798**, the French
  make the pope a prisoner. This taking-away **marks the start of "the time of the end"**:

> "...this taking away of the papal supremacy would mark the commencement of the period
> here called the time of the end... the time of the end commenced in 1798; for there...
> the papacy was overthrown by the French, and has never since been able to wield the
> power it before possessed."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 264_

The prophetic eye confirms the terminus to the year:

> "The 1260 years of papal supremacy began in A.D. 538, and would therefore terminate in 1798. At that time a French army entered Rome and made the pope a prisoner, and he died
> in exile... the papal hierarchy has never since been able to wield the power which it
> before possessed."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 266_

- [→] and the "mystery of iniquity" = "man of sin" = papacy identification, with the same
  1798 terminus, is confirmed by EGW at GC 356 — Paul's, Daniel's, and John's power are
  the one power.

## [→] "The king" who magnifies himself above every god = atheistic France, vv. 36-39

A new actor steps onto the stage — and the verse hands us a **three-feature test**:

> "And the king shall do according to his will... magnify himself above every god...
> speak marvellous things against the God of gods..." (Daniel 11:36)

- [→] **a NEW actor, not the papacy continued** — DAR's three-feature test: (1) it
  assumes character near the commencement of the time of the end (1798); (2) a **wilful**
  power; (3) an **atheistical** power. Only Revolutionary France fits all three:

> "Three peculiar features must appear in the power which fulfils this prophecy: (1) It
> must assume the character... near the commencement of the time of the end...; (2) it
> must be a wilful power; (3) it must be an atheistical power... A revolution exactly
> answering to this description did take place in France..."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 265_

> "France stands apart in the world's history as the single state which, by the decree of
> her legislative assembly, pronounced that there was no God..."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 241_

The prophetic eye confirms the fulfillment, almost word for word:

> "This prophecy has received a most exact and striking fulfillment in the history of
> France. During the Revolution, in 1793... France stands apart in the world's history as
> the single state which, by the decree of her Legislative Assembly, pronounced that there
> was no God."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 269_

[DYK🔎] The standing corpus — DAR, Haskell, and EGW alike — is **unanimous** that v.36 is
atheistic France. Three witnesses, one verdict; the test is the verse's own, the answer is
history's.

## [→] The time of the end — king of south = Egypt, king of north = Turkey, v. 40

Now apply the key fixed up top to the time-of-the-end occupants:

> "...the king of the south shall push at him: and the king of the north shall come
> against him like a whirlwind..." (Daniel 11:40)

- [→] **apply the KEY:** same northern/southern territory, new occupants. **South =
  Egypt** (still, by common agreement); **North = Ottoman Turkey**, which has held the
  original Seleucid/Thracian ground for 400 years:

> "We have met with nothing to indicate that we are to look to any localities for these
> powers other than those which shortly after the death of Alexander, constituted
> respectively the southern and northern divisions... Egypt is still... the king of the
> south, while the territory which at first constituted the king of the north, has been
> for the past four hundred years wholly included within the dominions of the sultan of
> Turkey."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 273_

- [⟲] **weave (RECAPITULATED):** the king of the north in vv.40-45 is **not** a different
  character from vv.5-13, **nor** a spiritualized papacy. The geographic key (DAR 224)
  makes **Turkey** the king of the north because Turkey holds the northern ground. **The
  title migrates with the territory** — same geopolitical role, new occupant; a
  recapitulation, not a new symbol and not a switch to spiritual geography.
- [→] **the 1798 confirmation** — the triangular war fixes the date:

> "...the king of the north (Turkey) came against him (France) in the same year that the
> king of the south (Egypt) 'pushed,' and both 'at the time of the end;' which is another
> conclusive proof that the year 1798 is the year which begins that period."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 275_

> "At the time of the end (1798) the kings of the north and the south again contended...
> Constantinople is recognized by all nations as the key to both Asia and Europe. In the
> time of the end, history will again center about this city."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 245_

## [→] The unfulfilled verse — v. 45, and the cue for Michael to stand

> "And he shall plant the tabernacles of his palace between the seas in the glorious holy
> mountain; yet he shall come to his end, and none shall help him." (Daniel 11:45)

- [→] every verse to v.44 has been **wrought into history**; **v.45 alone remains
  future** — the same northern power (Turkey) is to plant its palace "between the seas in
  the glorious holy mountain" (Jerusalem), then fall with none to help. This is the **one
  place the prophecy is genuinely "noted, not over-claimed":**

> "We have now traced the prophecy of the 11th of Daniel down, step by step... It has all
> been wrought out into history except this last verse... no power has yet performed the
> acts here described. But it is to be fulfilled... by that power which has been
> continuously the subject of the prophecy from the 40th verse down to this 45th verse."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 281_

- [⟲] **weave (same):** "the glorious land / holy mountain" across **v.16, v.41, v.45** =
  one continuous referent, **Palestine / Jerusalem.** Rome stood in it (v.16), Turkey
  enters it (v.41) and ends there (v.45) — the same ground, three occupants, one map.
- [→] Dan 11:45's fall is **back-to-back with Dan 12:1** — "at that time shall Michael
  stand up." The willful king's end is the cue for the resurrection.
