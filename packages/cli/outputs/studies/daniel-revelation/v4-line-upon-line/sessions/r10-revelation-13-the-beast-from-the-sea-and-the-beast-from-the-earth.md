# Revelation 13 — The Beast from the Sea and the Beast from the Earth

**The dragon hands his seat to a beast that IS Daniel's four beasts fused into one — and the Bible itself wrote that cross-reference (Rev 13:2). This is the spine's central re-meeting: every key forged in Daniel now snaps shut on one power, and Revelation goes further than Daniel ever did — a second beast, an IMAGE, and a MARK. We do not guess one symbol. We read the labels off the page.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel forged the keys (beast = kingdom, the 1260, the little-horn marks). Revelation 13 turns every one of them — line upon line — and adds three the prophet never reached.

---

## The setup — from the woman to the powers that hunt her

- Rev 12 was the **bird's-eye view of the CHURCH**; Rev 13 turns the camera onto the **NATIONS** that are the chief actors in the controversy — same ground, new focus

> "The twelfth chapter of Revelation is a bird's-eye view of the church... The thirteenth chapter bears more directly on the nations which are the chief actors in the Great Controversy"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 224_

- [→] the dragon of ch.12 does **not vanish** — he now WORKS THROUGH the beast he is about to empower (Rev 13:2, 4). The persecutor changes mask, not master
- the chapter introduces **THREE powers in order**: the dragon (already known), the **sea-beast** (Rev 13:1-10), the **earth-beast** (Rev 13:11-18). We decode each as we reach it.

---

## [KEY🔑] First label: the SEA = peoples, nations, tongues

John stands on the sand and a beast rises **"up out of the sea"** (Rev 13:1). Before the beast can mean anything, the water must — and the Bible defines its own symbol:

> "The waters which thou sawest... are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." (Revelation 17:15)

- [KEY🔑] **sea / waters = peoples, multitudes, nations, tongues** — the verse defines its own symbol. This is the series' load-bearing waters-key.

> "THE sea is a symbol of peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues. Revelation 17:15. A beast is the Bible symbol of an unrighteous nation, or power..."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 520_

- and winds on the sea = political commotion, civil strife, revolution
- [→] so a beast rising out of the sea = a power arising amid the **CROWDED, populous, strife-torn Old World**. Hold the contrast: the **EARTH** (Rev 13:11) will mean the opposite — empty, unoccupied territory. (Set up here, paid off later.)
- [⟲ Dan 7:2-3] this is the **SAME key** the reader has carried since Daniel 7's "great sea" — Rev 17:15 is the exact verse used there. Same symbol, same meaning, whole canon.

## [→] The leopard-beast = the papacy — and the Bible wrote the cross-reference

Rev 13:1-2 describes the beast. Who is it? This is not a verse self-definition — it is the historicist reading, and it must carry its guide quote:

> "We now come to the leopard beast of chapter 13. What does that symbolize?... The Roman empire... this beast symbolizes Rome in its professedly Christian form"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 523_

- [→] **papal Rome** — the dragon's successor, Rome in its professedly Christian form
- not the civil power alone — the persecuting **union** of civil and ecclesiastical:

> "differs from the dragon only in that he presents a different religious aspect. Hence it would be altogether wrong to affirm that it denotes simply the Roman civil power"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 523_

- who persecutes the true church? Mark the test:

> "It is a false or apostate church... governments do not persecute people on account of their religion, unless under the control of some opposite and hostile system of religion"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 522_

And the prophetic eye names the same power, tying it straight back to Daniel 7:

> "This symbol, as most Protestants have believed, represents the papacy, which succeeded to the power and seat and authority once held by the ancient Roman empire... This prophecy, which is nearly identical with the description of the little horn of Daniel 7, unquestionably points to the papacy"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 439_

- [⟲ Rev 12:9 — KEY🔑 the dragon = Satan, defined in-text last session] when Rev 13:2 says "the dragon gave him his power, and his seat," Satan — working through pagan Rome as his agent — **transfers the seat to papal Rome**. Same persecutor, new vessel.

## [⟲] THE WEAVE — Daniel's four beasts re-assembled into ONE

★ **The central re-meeting of the whole series.** Revelation did NOT invent a new beast — it **COPIED Daniel 7's key on purpose.**

> "And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion..." (Revelation 13:2)

- [⟲ Dan 7:3-7] **leopard (Greece) + bear (Medo-Persia) + lion (Babylon)** — Daniel's four world-kingdoms, named in **REVERSE**, now form the body of Revelation's **one** beast. The composite is deliberate: papal Rome inherits the territory, traits, and idolatry of every empire before it.
- [⟲ Dan 7:8, 25] the beast's **"mouth speaking great things and blasphemies"** = the little horn's mouth, word for word — same blasphemy, same war on saints. This is the SAME-power link: the leopard-beast **IS** the little horn of Daniel 7 — one power, two symbol-systems.

> "the symbols here used, are the same which were given to Daniel, and were interpreted for that prophet by Gabriel... four beasts, or kingdoms... Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome. Babylon was the lion, the king of beasts"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 225_

- the two symbols — Daniel's little horn and Revelation's leopard-beast — name **the same identical power**: same time, same territory, same character, same work, same fate.
- [CHAIN recall] the link forged at D07 — **beast = kingdom, little horn = papacy, dated 538-1798** — now snaps shut. R10 is where Daniel 7 was always headed.

[Q] If Daniel's little horn IS Revelation's beast, then everything proven about the little horn is already proven about this beast **before we read another verse.** Who else on earth could fit?

## [KEY🔑] The 42 months = the SAME 1260 the reader already dated

> "...a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue forty and two months." (Revelation 13:5)

- [KEY🔑] Scripture's own arithmetic, year-day principle (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6): **42 months × 30 days = 1260 prophetic days = 1260 literal years.** 538 + 1260 = **1798.** NOT a new period — the **fourth wording** of one span.

> "Power was given to the little horn to continue for a time, times, and the dividing of time, or 1260 years. Daniel 7:25. To this beast also power was given for forty-two months, or 1260 years. Revelation 13:5"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 524_

The dates, read off history:

> "From 538 a.d. may be reckoned that absolute power which lasted for forty and two prophetic months... He opened his mouth in blasphemy against God"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 230_

- **538** — Justinian's decree (533) became effective when the Ostrogoths, the last opposing power, were overthrown (SSP 229.1)

[⟲ Dan 7:25 = Rev 12:6, 14 = Rev 13:5] the **SAME span, four ways**: Dan 7:25 "time, times, dividing"; Rev 12:6 "1260 days"; Rev 12:14 "time, times, half"; Rev 13:5 "42 months" — **ONE historical period, A.D. 538-1798, four angles.** Never blur it into a different span.

> "has been viewed from the standpoint of the church of God, from that of the false or apostate church, and from the civil side as well... The noontide of the papacy was the world's moral midnight"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 233_

## [→] The deadly wound — given 1798, healed after

> "...one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed." (Revelation 13:3)

- [→] whatever prophecy says of a symbol applies only while that symbol represents the power:

> "The head that was wounded to death was the papal head"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 525_

> "It was inflicted when the pope was taken prisoner by Berthier, the French general... in 1798. Stripped of his power... Pius VI, died in exile... But the deadly wound was healed when the papacy was re-established... March 14, 1800"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 525_

And the prophetic eye fixes the same date:

> "The infliction of the deadly wound points to the downfall of the papacy in 1798. After this, says the prophet, his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 578_

- [⟲ D07 / R08 / R09] **1798** is the SAME terminus the reader has now hit four times — the 1260's end, the witnesses, the wilderness woman, and now the wounded head. The clock ran out exactly when the prophecy said.

## [→] The SECOND beast — from the EARTH, lamb-horns, dragon-voice

> "...another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon." (Revelation 13:11)

A **NEW power**, where Daniel's chain never reached. The earth/sea contrast set up earlier now pays off:

> "If the sea... denotes peoples, nations, and multitudes (Revelation 17:15), the earth would suggest, by contrast, a new and previously unoccupied territory"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 534_

> "the nation thus represented must arise in territory previously unoccupied and grow up gradually and peacefully... It could not, then, arise among the crowded and struggling nationalities of the Old World... It must be sought in the Western Continent"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 440_

- [→] analogy requires a great religious system still controlling the world:

> "still another great system of religion... the only remaining system which is exercising a controlling influence in the world to-day is Protestantism"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 527_

- the prophetic eye and the historicist guide agree: this earth-rising, lamb-horned power is the United States — the territory God reserved for the planting of truth:

> "God reserved the territory, afterwards known as the United States of America, for the planting of downtrodden truth... those seeking liberty of conscience, passed to the eastern shores of North America"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 235_

- the two lamb-horns = civil and religious liberty — the First Amendment (DAR 538.1)
- [⟲ Rev 12:16] the Reformation's twofold civil-and-ecclesiastical principle — **"The earth helped the woman"** — leads straight into this earth-beast:

> "America has already repudiated her first principles of liberty... The professed Protestant nation is imitating the papal power of Rome, thus forming the image to the beast"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 240_

## [→] The dragon-voice — the lamb that legislates persecution

The beast **LOOKS** like a lamb but **SPEAKS** like a dragon, and "exerciseth all the power of the first beast" (Rev 13:11-12):

> "The speaking of the nation is the action of its legislative and judicial authorities... it will give the lie to those liberal and peaceful principles... a development of the spirit of intolerance and persecution... in enforcing some observance which shall be an act of homage to the papacy"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 442_

- [→] the contradiction is the whole point: **lamb-horns (profession) vs. dragon-voice (practice).** A free, Protestant nation becoming the enforcer.

## [→] The IMAGE of the beast — a Protestant papacy

> "...saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast... that as many as would not worship the image of the beast should be killed." (Revelation 13:14-15)

- [→] to know the image we must know the papacy:

> "a church clothed with civil power... authority to punish all dissenters... What would be an image of the papacy? — Another ecclesiastical establishment clothed with similar power"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 546_

> "The image to the beast represents that form of apostate Protestantism which will be developed when the Protestant churches shall seek the aid of the civil power for the enforcement of their dogmas"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 445_

- [→] an IMAGE is a **likeness, not the original** — Protestant churches reproducing the papal pattern (church + state) on American soil. **The lamb builds the image of the leopard.**

## [CTRF] The MARK of the beast — the counterfeit of the SEAL

> "And he causeth all... to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads: And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark..." (Revelation 13:16-17)

- [CTRF the seal of God, R05] the **seal** = God's name/character in the FOREHEAD (Rev 7:3; 14:1); the **mark** is its deliberate counterfeit — **same location, opposite allegiance.** The Bible itself sets up the pair.

How is the question decided?

> "how is it to be determined which of these powers the people honor and worship? — It is determined by the law which they keep"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 553_

> "This change of the fourth commandment must therefore be the change to which the prophecy points, and Sunday-keeping must be the mark of the beast!"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 559_

> "the image to the beast repeats these acts, and passes laws enforcing the observance of its mark — the false Sabbath"
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 241_

The mark is received **knowingly, under compulsion — never ignorantly:**

> "when Sunday observance shall be enforced by law, and the world shall be enlightened concerning the obligation of the true Sabbath"
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 449_

- [⟲ Dan 7:25] the power that **"thought to change times and laws"** in Daniel is the power whose changed law is now the **test-mark.** The little horn's act and the beast's mark are one and the same deed.

## [KEY🔑] 666 — the number of a MAN

> "Let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast: for it is the number of a man; and his number is Six hundred threescore and six." (Revelation 13:18)

- [KEY🔑] the verse itself says **"the number of a man"** — a man, a title, a sum. The verse defines its own symbol.

> "the title which the pope applies to himself... Vicarius Filii Dei, Vicegerent of the Son of God. Taking the letters... which the Latins used as numerals... we have just 666"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 580_

- [DYK🔎] **Latin numerals only**, not Greek/Hebrew — auxiliary confirmation, never load-bearing. The identity already stands on the prior labels; 666 is the seal on a case already closed.
