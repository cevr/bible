# Revelation 2-3 — The Seven Churches

**Seven letters, seven churches — and the Bible itself hands us the dictionary: the
candlesticks ARE the churches (Rev 1:20). But the guides take it one step further: these
seven are not just seven first-century congregations, they are seven successive ages of
the one church, apostles to the second coming. Walk them in order and you have the whole
Christian era sketched in advance — and the 1260 you already dated at Daniel 7 falls
right inside it, lived through by the church called Thyatira.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Rev 1:20 defines its
> own symbol (candlesticks = churches); the guides then read the seven as seven ages —
> line upon line.

---

## The setup — seven letters that are really one sweep

- Rev 2-3 is **seven short letters**, each to a named church — Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamos,
  Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, Laodicea (Rev 1:11)
- [→] but they read as **more than seven first-century mailings**: each opens with Christ
  describing Himself from the Rev 1 vision, and the promises run to _"him that
  overcometh"_ at the very end
- [→] one continuous span, seven stages — the **church-age sweep** this session walks

> "The message to the seven churches covers a period in ecclesiastical history, extending
> from the time of Christ's first advent to His second coming."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 39_

---

## [KEY🔑] The candlesticks ARE the churches — the in-text key carried from Rev 1

The verse defines its own symbol. We do not assign meaning; we read the label off the
page, exactly as "beast = kingdom" in Daniel 7:

> "The seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches." (Revelation 1:20)

> "The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches." (Revelation 1:20)

- **candlesticks = churches**, **stars = angels of the churches** (their messengers /
  ministers) — Scripture handing us the dictionary
- [KEY🔑] this is the same move as Dan 7:17 ("beasts ARE kings") — the verse names its
  own meaning, so no guessing is needed

[→] And Christ walks **"in the midst"** of them (Rev 2:1) — the prophetic eye confirms
the picture:

> "Christ is spoken of as walking in the midst of the golden candlesticks. Thus is
> symbolized His relation to the churches... He observes their order, their piety, their
> devotion."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 586_

[DYK🔎] Haskell amplifies the beauty of it — Christ among the lampstands means the light
has never gone out:

> "the Divine Presence has never been withdrawn from the earth."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 39_

---

## [→] The overlay — seven churches = seven successive periods of the gospel church

[→] **This is NOT the verse's own words.** Rev 1:20 says candlesticks = churches, full
stop. That the seven churches are seven _ages_ is the historicist **overlay** the guides
establish — so it must carry the establishing quote, never a bare key:

> "should be regarded as prophetic, having their application to seven distinct periods
> covering the Christian age... this view is neither new nor local. Benson quotes Bishop
> Newton... that the seven epistles are prophetical of so many successive periods, or
> states, of the church, from the beginning to the conclusion of all."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 345_

> "The Spirit used each of those churches to represent a period in the history of the
> work of God on earth, the seven covering the time from the life of John to the closing
> events in the history of the world."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 31_

And the prophetic eye confirms the same reading:

> "The names of the seven churches are symbolic of the church in different periods of the
> Christian Era. The number 7 indicates completeness, and is symbolic of the fact that
> the messages extend to the end of time."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 585_

[DYK🔎] Why did some later expositors drop this prophetic reading? DAR names the reason:

> "the comparatively recent and unscriptural doctrine of the temporal millennium" — so
> "the more Scriptural view is made to yield to the more pleasing."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 346_

[→] Laodicea's lukewarm _last_ state will not fit a converted-world millennium — so the
prophetic seven-ages view was traded for a more comfortable one. We keep the prophetic
view, because the seventh church proves it.

## [→] Ephesus — the apostolic church (A.D. 27/31 to c. A.D. 100)

> "thou hast left thy first love... Remember therefore from whence thou art fallen, and
> repent, and do the first works." (Revelation 2:4-5)

- [→] period decode — the first church is the **apostolic age**:

> "The time covered by this first church, may be considered the period from the
> resurrection of Christ, to the close of the first century, or to the death of the last
> of the apostles."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 351_

And the prophetic eye confirms the same first-love cooling:

> the mystery of iniquity had begun, "many had lost their first love of gospel truth,"
> yet in "a message of infinite tenderness" God pleaded, "Remember... from whence thou
> art fallen, and repent, and do the first works."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 587_

[→] the pure first-century church, **already cooling** — the sweep begins.

## [→] Smyrna — the persecuted church (A.D. 100-323); ten days = ten years

> "ye shall have tribulation ten days: be thou faithful unto death, and I will give thee
> a crown of life." (Revelation 2:10)

- [→] the **year-day principle** (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6) applied — a prophetic day = a
  literal year:

> "As this message is prophetic, the time mentioned in it must also be regarded as
> prophetic, and would denote ten years. And it is a noticeable fact that the last and
> most bloody of the ten persecutions continued just ten years, under Diocletian, from
> A. D. 302 to A. D. 312."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 352_

- [→] period decode:

> "the date of the Smyrnian church would be A. D. 100-323."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 354_

[→] the **same year-day key the reader forged at Dan 7:25** (1 prophetic day = 1 literal
year) reappears in miniature — ten symbolic days = ten literal years of Diocletian's
fire.

## [→] Pergamos — the church wedded to the State (A.D. 323-538)

> "I know thy works, and where thou dwellest, even where Satan's seat is." (Revelation
> 2:13)

- [→] period decode — the word _Pergamos_ "signifies hight, elevation"; the period runs
  "from the days of Constantine... his professed conversion to Christianity, A. D. 323,
  to the establishment of the papacy, A. D. 538."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 354_

Haskell names what happened in this period — the church married the state:

> "The church between 312 and 538 a. d. joined hands with the civil power. It took of the
> wealth of the State, and asked for civil protection. Then it was that the spiritual
> sins of idolatry and fornication were introduced... It is fornication in the eyes of
> God when His people are wedded to any power save the arm of Omnipotence."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 57_

[DYK🔎] **Antipas** (Rev 2:13) decoded — not one man, but a class of witnesses:

> "It is supposed that Antipas was not an individual, but a class of men who opposed the
> power of the bishops, or popes... a combination of two words, anti, opposed, and papas,
> father, or pope."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 355_

### [⟲] The weave — "Satan's seat" here is NOT the dragon's seat of Rev 13:2

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT instance, same Satan.** Rev 2:13's "Satan's seat" = the seat of papal
  power _developing_ in Rome during Pergamos (323-538) — the early stage.
- Rev 13:2's _"his power, and his seat, and great authority"_ is the **same underlying
  Satan** seen later from the **beast's perspective** — same power, a **different stage**
  of the prophetic unfolding.
- [→] do not blur them: one power, two moments on the clock.

## [→] Thyatira — papal supremacy (A.D. 538-1798); Jezebel = the papacy

> "thou sufferest that woman Jezebel... to teach and to seduce my servants." (Revelation
> 2:20)

- [→] period decode — the church of Thyatira spans **the 1260 years**:

> "the most natural division to be assigned to the church of Thyatira would be the time
> of the continuance of this blasphemous power, through the 1260 years of its supremacy,
> or from A. D. 538 to A. D. 1798."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 358_

**Jezebel** decoded — a figure for papal abomination:

> "Jezebel is a figurative name, alluding to Ahab's wife, who slew the prophets of the
> Lord, led her husband into idolatry... A more striking figure could not have been used
> to denote the papal abominations."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 359_

Haskell ties Thyatira to the Dark Ages and the same prophetic span:

> "the history of Jezebel is a parable of the church history during the time, times, and
> half a time-the three and one half years of the papal supremacy, the period covered by
> the message to Thyatira... Europe had over a thousand years of darkness, known in all
> history as the Dark Ages."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 64_

## [⟲] The weave — Thyatira IS the 1260 you already dated at Daniel 7

This is the heart of the session's cross-reference. **The interpretive risk is here, so
the verdict must be stated plainly:**

- [⟲] this is Dan 7:25's _"a time and times and the dividing of time"_ = Rev 12:6's

- [→] the reader does **not re-date anything**: the little-horn supremacy dated at D07
  (538-1798) is the very era the church lived **through** as Thyatira. The church and the

[⟲] And **Jezebel the woman = RECAPITULATED, not identical, with Rev 17's mother of
harlots:**

- DAR 360.1 ties Rev 2:20's Jezebel to "the Romish Church under the symbol of a corrupt
  woman, the mother of harlots and abominations of the earth. (See chapters 17-19.)"

## [→] Philadelphia — the Advent movement (to autumn 1844); the open door

> "he that openeth, and no man shutteth... behold, I have set before thee an open door,
> and no man can shut it." (Revelation 3:7-8)

- [→] period decode — _Philadelphia_ means **brotherly love**:

> "signifies brotherly love, and expresses the position and spirit of those who received
> the Advent message up to the autumn of 1844... they left party names and party feelings
> behind... and pointed to the coming of the Son of man as the believer's true hope."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 366_

The **open door** decoded — a sanctuary event in 1844:

> "the time did come for this service to commence at the close of the 2300 days, in
> 1844... the opening being the opening of his ministration in the most holy place, and
> the shutting, its cessation in the first apartment, or holy place."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 367_

### [⟲] The weave — Philadelphia's door is NOT the door of Rev 4:1

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT door, different event.** Philadelphia's door (Rev 3:8) = Christ's
  transition into the **Most Holy Place** at the close of the 2300 days in 1844 — a
  **sanctuary event**.
- Rev 4:1's door = the **literary device** opening John's throne-room vision.
- [→] both point heavenward, but they are **different doors** — do not merge them.

[→] forward link: the **2300-day / 1844 sanctuary move** named here is the payoff coming
at **R03** (Rev 4-5, the heavenly sanctuary shown) and dated back at **Dan 8:14**.

## [→] Laodicea — the last church (1844 onward); the judging of the people

> "thou art neither cold nor hot... thou sayest, I am rich... and knowest not that thou
> art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked." (Revelation 3:15-17)

- [→] period decode — _Laodicea_ means **the judging of the people**:

> "Laodicea signifies the judging of the people... It reveals a period of judgment. It is
> the last stage of the church... while the great day of atonement is transpiring, and
> the investigative Judgment is going forward upon the house of God."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 371_

Haskell — the last church gathers the light of every age:

> "the Laodicean message covers ecclesiastical history to the very end of time. As many
> as I love, I rebuke and chasten: be zealous therefore, and repent."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 90_

> "Upon this last church-the remnant,-shine the accumulated rays of all past ages."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 88_

And the prophetic eye twice confirms the lukewarm last state:

> the words to Laodicea "describe their present condition perfectly... They are neither
> cold nor hot, but lukewarm" — and unless they "zealously repent and obtain gold tried
> in the fire... He will spew them out of His mouth."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 107_

> "The message of the True Witness finds the people of God in a sad deception, yet honest
> in that deception... The testimony, so cutting and severe, cannot be a mistake, for it
> is the True Witness who speaks."
> — _EGW, Testimonies for the Church, vol. 3, p. 252_

[→] the investigative-judgment reading ties to **Dan 7's courtroom** and **Dan 8:14's
cleansing** — Laodicea is the church living _during_ the judgment the reader watched
convene at D07.

## [→] One sweep, one Witness — the seven ages overlap into the present

The final periods do not stop dead; they **overlap** and reach the second coming:

> "The messages to Sardis and to Philadelphia, separately cover a period extending to the
> second coming of Christ; but in addition... that which is directed to Laodicea is also
> applicable... for the Laodicean message is given to the people at the time when the
> investigative judgment is in progress."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 86_

[→] And Thyatira's church-state crisis **recurs at the end** — recapitulation forward:

> "the history of Thyatira will have its counterpart in the last generation. The power of
> Jezebel will again be felt... The union of the church and state will be followed by
> laws compelling obedience to man-made laws."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 69_

[⟲] forward link: the church-state crisis of Thyatira recurs at the end — **Rev 13's
image and mark.**

DAR closes the seven messages with the Witness who misses nothing:

> "How pointed and searching their testimony!... It is as true with the last church as
> with the first, that all their works are known to Him who walks in the midst of the
> seven golden candlesticks. From his scrutinizing gaze nothing can be hidden."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 383_

And Haskell sums the whole sweep in a single sentence:

> "This line of prophecy follows the church from the purity of the first century, until
> it unites with the state and persecutes the true people of God, and finally emerges
> from the Dark Ages and separating from the world prepares to meet its Lord and Master
> in the clouds of heaven."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Seer of Patmos, p. 73_
