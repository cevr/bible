# Daniel 7 — Four Beasts and the Judgment

**The same future Daniel 2 drew in metal, Daniel 7 now draws in beasts — and once again the
Bible hands us its own dictionary: beast = kingdom (Dan 7:17, 23). We do not guess; we read
the label off the page. This is recapitulation — the same four kingdoms (Babylon,
Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome) "line upon line" — but it adds what Daniel 2 withheld: the
character of earthly governments as wild ravenous beasts in heaven's sight, the little horn
(papal Rome) rising AFTER the ten, the 1260-year span (538–1798), and above all the
chapter's DESTINATION — the heavenly courtroom where the Ancient of days sits, the books
open, and judgment is given to the saints. The ten horns of Dan 7:24 are the FEET of Dan
2:41 — the medieval breakup of Rome — never the toes. And Revelation deliberately re-draws
the whole key (Rev 13:2; 13:5; 12:6; 12:14): the cross-reference is the Bible's own.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel 2 taught us the
> key (metal = kingdom). Daniel 7 hands us the next entry (beast = kingdom) — line upon
> line — and then carries us all the way to the judgment.

## [KEY🔑] First entry: the great SEA = peoples; the four WINDS = strife

Before the beasts mean anything, the water must. Daniel does not define "sea" here — so we
reach to where the Bible does:

> "The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are peoples, and multitudes, and
> nations, and tongues." (Revelation 17:15)

- [KEY🔑] **sea / waters = peoples, nations, tongues** — defined outright by Scripture (Rev
  17:15)

> "The Bible definition of sea, or waters, when used as a symbol, is, peoples, and nations,
> and tongues. In proof of this, see Revelation 17:15, where it is expressly so declared."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 114_

- [→] beasts rising **"out of the sea"** = kingdoms rising out of the crowded, warring
  world; the **four winds striving** = strife and political commotion among the nations.
- [→] **EGW confirming tier** — the prophetic eye restates the exact Scripture-defined keys:

> "The great kingdoms that have ruled the world were presented to the prophet Daniel as
> beasts of prey, rising when 'the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea.'
> Daniel 7:2. In Revelation 17 an angel explained that waters represent 'peoples, and
> multitudes, and nations, and tongues.'... Winds are a symbol of strife. The four winds of
> heaven striving upon the great sea represent the terrible scenes of conquest and
> revolution by which kingdoms have attained to power."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 439_

## [KEY🔑] Second entry: BEAST = kingdom

We do not have to imagine what the four beasts mean. As in Daniel 2, the interpretation is
given **inside the vision**:

> "These great beasts, which are four, are four kings, which shall arise out of the earth."
> (Daniel 7:17)

And again, plainer, for the fourth:

> "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom upon earth, which shall be diverse from all
> kingdoms, and shall devour the whole earth, and shall tread it down, and break it in
> pieces." (Daniel 7:23)

- [KEY🔑] **beast = kingdom** — the angel says "kings... kingdom," Scripture handing us the
  dictionary, exactly as Dan 2:38-40 said "kingdom" four times. We are not interpreting an
  animal; we are reading a label.

> "The definition of the symbol of the four beasts is given to Daniel ere the close of the
> vision. Verse 17: 'These great beasts, which are four, are four kings which shall arise
> out of the earth.'"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 114_

- [→] Same key, second cut. Metal in chapter 2, beast in chapter 7 — **both defined by the
  text as kingdom.** Learn it once, it opens every vision ahead.

## The four beasts named in history — same four metals of Daniel 2

Scripture gives the meaning (four successive kingdoms); the four beasts simply re-draw the
four metals of Daniel 2. Haskell ties the two visions together at the first beast:

> "Four great beasts came up from the sea; that is, they arose into prominence from the
> midst of the multitudes of earth. Babylon, the first of these kingdoms, was represented to
> Nebuchadnezzar as the golden head of the great image. To Daniel the same power appeared as
> a lion, having eagle's wings..."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 90_

- **Lion (with eagle's wings) = Babylon** — the gold head, now winged
  - [⟲] the gold head of Dan 2 is the lion of Dan 7 — _same kingdom, two pictures_
    > "At first the lion had eagle's wings, denoting the rapidity with which Babylon extended
    > its conquests under Nebuchadnezzar..."
    > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 115_
- **Bear (raised on one side, three ribs) = Medo-Persia** — the silver breast
  > "The Medo-Persian kingdom was bloodthirsty and cruel in its nature, and is represented by
  > a bear. Darius was a Mede; and Cyrus, the leading general, a Persian."
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 91_
- **Leopard (four wings, four heads) = Greece** — the brass belly, now a swift four-winged
  leopard
  > "The Grecian kingdom, which followed the Medo-Persian, is compared to the sprightliness

  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 92_
- **Fourth beast (dreadful, no natural animal fits) = Rome** — the iron legs, now a beast so
  terrible no animal could carry its character
  > "...there was no animal with a character to represent its terrible nature; so a beast
  > without name..." [the fourth beast = Rome]
  > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 92_
  - [⟲] and DAR **binds the fourth beast to Daniel 2's iron** — the same kingdom, the same
    historicist slot:
    > "This beast, of course, corresponds to the fourth division of the great image — the legs
    > of iron."
    > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 118_

[DYK🔎] Same four kingdoms as Dan 2 — Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome — confirmed by Dan
5:28 (Medes and Persians) and Dan 8:21 (Grecia named outright). The Bible labels its own
symbols; history only supplies the dates.

## The little horn — defined by its marks, one verse at a time

Among the ten, a new power rises:

Daniel asks who it is. The angel answers with a string of identifying marks — and **each
mark is a Scripture, not a guess.** The verses fence it in until only one power on earth
fits.

> "And another shall rise after them; and he shall be diverse from the first, and he shall
> subdue three kings." (Daniel 7:24)

## [KEY🔑] The 1260 — "a time and times and the dividing of time"

Mark 7 is a time prophecy. Decode it where the text raises it:

> "...and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time."
> (Daniel 7:25)

- **a time = 1 year; times = 2 years; dividing of time = ½ year** → **3½ prophetic years**
- a prophetic year = 360 days → 3½ × 360 = **1260 days**
- the year-day principle (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6) → **1260 days = 1260 literal years**

The full derivation and dates, read off history:

> "A time, as we have seen from chapter 4:23, is one year... The ordinary Jewish year, which
> must be used as the basis of reckoning, contained three hundred and sixty days. Three years
> and a half contained twelve hundred and sixty days. As each day stands for a year, we have
> twelve hundred and sixty years for the continuation of the supremacy of this horn... For
> 538 + 1260 = 1798; and in the year 1798, Berthier, with a French army, entered Rome...
> took the pope prisoner, and for a time abolished the papacy."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 141_

- **538 — supremacy begins.** The third horn (Ostrogoths) is pulled, leaving the bishop of
  Rome in undisputed possession
  > "The Vandals fell before the victorious arms of Belisarius in 534; and the Goths,
  > retiring, left him in undisputed possession of Rome in 538."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 135_
  - cross-confirm Haskell on the full span:
    > "This time, three and one half years, or forty-two months, or twelve hundred and sixty
    > years... began in 538... It continued until 1798, when his dominion was taken away."
    > — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 97_
- **538 + 1260 = 1798 — supremacy ends.** Berthier's army takes the pope captive; the wound
  is given.

[→] **538 → 1798.** A 1260-year span the prophecy named in advance, opened and closed on
schedule. The arithmetic is plain; the history filled it in.

## [⟲] The weave — the Bible wrote the cross-reference

Now the move this whole series is built on. **Revelation deliberately re-draws Daniel 7.** We
are not importing a parallel — John copied Daniel's key on purpose.

**[⟲] Rev 13:2 — the three beasts re-assembled into one:**

> "And the beast which I saw was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a
> bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion: and the dragon gave him his power, and his
> seat, and great authority." (Revelation 13:2)

- leopard + bear + lion — **Daniel's beasts, named in reverse, fused into Revelation's one
  beast.** John is pointing straight back at Daniel 7. The Bible wrote the weave.

**[⟲] Rev 13:5 — the same 1260, restated as 42 months:**

> "...a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue
> forty and two months." (Revelation 13:5)

- "mouth speaking great things" = Dan 7:8, 25, word for word
- **42 months × 30 days = 1260 days** = Dan 7:25's "time, times, and dividing of time." Same
  span, second wording.

**[⟲] Rev 13:7 — the same war on the saints:**

> "And it was given unto him to make war with the saints, and to overcome them..."
> (Revelation 13:7)

- this is Dan 7:21, 25 — _"the same horn made war with the saints, and prevailed against
  them"_ (Dan 7:21). Daniel's little horn and Revelation's beast do the **same deed.**

**[⟲] Rev 12:6 + Rev 12:14 — the SAME 1260, a third and fourth way:**

> "And the woman fled into the wilderness... a thousand two hundred and threescore days."
> (Revelation 12:6)

> "...for a time, and times, and half a time..." (Revelation 12:14)

- Rev 12:6 says **1260 days**; Rev 12:14 says **time, times, half a time** — the very phrase
  of Dan 7:25.

[→] Count them: the **same 1260** appears four ways — "time, times, dividing of time" (Dan
7:25), "42 months" (Rev 13:5), "1260 days" (Rev 12:6), and "time, times, half a time" (Rev
12:14). **Four statements, one span.** And the prophetic eye states both the equivalence and
the dating outright:

> "The periods here mentioned—'forty and two months,' and 'a thousand two hundred and
> threescore days'—are the same, alike representing the time in which the church of Christ
> was to suffer oppression from Rome. The 1260 years of papal supremacy began in A.D. 538,
> and would therefore terminate in 1798... At that time a French army entered Rome and made
> the pope a prisoner, and he died in exile."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 266_

[→] The Bible cross-references itself; **EGW only confirms what the four texts already say.**
The weave is not ours; it is theirs.

## The judgment — the destination of the chapter

The little horn does not get the last word. The scene shifts to heaven:

> "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit, whose garment
> was white as snow... thousand thousands ministered unto him... the judgment was set, and
> the books were opened." (Daniel 7:9-10)

> "I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of
> heaven, and came to the Ancient of days... And there was given him dominion, and glory, and
> a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him..." (Daniel 7:13-14)

- **Ancient of days = God the Father**, taking the seat of judgment — and the work here is
  the investigative judgment, the books opened:

> "The 'Ancient of days,' God the Father, takes the throne of judgment... The closing up of
> the ministration of Christ, our great High Priest, in the heavenly sanctuary, is the work
> of judgment here introduced. It is an investigative judgment. The books are opened, and the
> cases of all come up for examination..."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 120_

- [→] **EGW confirming tier (Ancient of days):** the prophetic eye quotes Dan 7:9-10 as the
  foundational judgment text and names the Father as the One who presides:

> "I beheld," says the prophet Daniel, "till thrones were placed, and One that was Ancient of
> Days did sit: His raiment was white as snow, and the hair of His head like pure wool...
> thousand thousands ministered unto Him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before
> Him: the judgment was set, and the books were opened." Daniel 7:9, 10, R.V.
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 479_

> "The Ancient of Days is God the Father... It is He, the source of all being, and the
> fountain of all law, that is to preside in the judgment. And holy angels as ministers and
> witnesses... attend this great tribunal."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 479_

- [→] the **Son of man coming** (Dan 7:13) is **NOT the second advent** — it is Christ coming
  TO the Ancient of days IN HEAVEN. [⟲] **same-vs-different weave resolved:** this is heavenly
  enthronement, not descent to earth.

> "The scene here described is not the second advent of Christ to this earth, unless the
> Ancient of days is on this earth; for it is a coming to the Ancient of days... He receives
> the kingdom at the close of his priestly work in the sanctuary."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 122_

- [→] **EGW confirming tier — dating the enthronement to 1844:**

> "The coming of Christ here described is not His second coming to the earth. He comes to the
> Ancient of Days in heaven to receive dominion and glory and a kingdom... It is this coming,
> and not His second advent to the earth, that was foretold in prophecy to take place at the
> termination of the 2300 days in 1844... to perform the work of investigative judgment..."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 479_

And the verdict goes to the persecuted:

> "...the same horn made war with the saints, and prevailed against them; Until the Ancient
> of days came, and judgment was given to the saints of the most High; and the time came that
> the saints possessed the kingdom." (Daniel 7:21-22)

> "But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to
> destroy it unto the end." (Daniel 7:26)

- [→] the horn made war and **"prevailed"** — for a time. Then the court sits, the dominion
  is taken away, and the kingdom passes to the saints. This is the **stone of Dan 2:44**
  again — God's everlasting kingdom — now shown being **awarded in a courtroom** rather than
  smashing the image.
- [⟲] this judgment is the chapter's heart — Haskell's **"central theme"** (SDP 88). The
  2300-day cleansing of Dan 8:14 will **date** it:

> "In Daniel seven is the only description found in the Bible of the judgment scene announced
> by the first angel of Revelation fourteen... the twenty-three hundred days or literal years
> which began in the year 457 B.C... and expired in 1844, A.D."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 100_
