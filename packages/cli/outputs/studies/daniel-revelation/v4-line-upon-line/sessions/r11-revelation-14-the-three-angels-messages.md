# Revelation 14 — The Lamb on Zion and the Three Angels' Messages

**Chapter 13 left God's people under a death decree. Chapter 14 answers it: the very same
company stands victorious on Mount Zion, gathered there by a threefold message that is
Daniel's 2300-day judgment turned into a worldwide cry. The spine of the whole series and
the gospel meet here.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Revelation 14 defines
> only one symbol in its own words (the "women" of v.4) — everything else is read by the
> weave back to Daniel and Revelation already walked, line upon line.

---

## The same company, now on Zion — reading Rev 14 against Rev 13's cliff

- Chapter 13 closed on a cliff: God's people "a small and apparently weak and defenseless
  company," under a death decree to worship the image and take the mark
- Chapter 14 opens on the answer — the **same** company, no longer threatened but **standing
  victorious**:

> "And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and
> four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads." (Revelation 14:1)

> "Glance forward with the apostle to the very next scene in the program, and what do we
> behold? — The very same company standing on Mount Zion with the Lamb, — a victorious
> company, harping on symphonious harps their triumph in the court of heaven."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 583_

- [→] the next scene **answers the conflict.** The threatened remnant of 13 is the
  triumphant remnant of 14 — same people, the storm now behind them
- Haskell frames the whole controversy resolving in this one image — the Lamb who was
  slandered as a despot now seen for what He is:

> "After the strife and the turmoil of the great controversy... the One whom Lucifer had
> held before the eyes of the world as a cruel despot, stands a Lamb on Mount Zion...
> through six thousand years of conflict, it has come forth victorious with garments
> unsullied."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 246_

- [→] **the Mount Zion of v.1 = the heavenly Mount Zion** (Jerusalem above), not earthly
  Jerusalem; the harpers' voice is heard **from heaven** — a guide overlay, carried by DAR:
  > "On what Mount Zion does John see this company standing? — The Mount Zion above; for the
  > voice of harpers, which no doubt is uttered by these very ones, is heard from heaven."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 583_
- [→] **the 144,000 on Zion = the same company sealed in Rev 7** — the living saints
  translated at the second advent (DAR 583.1-583.4). This is a **guide overlay**, NOT a
  verse self-definition.

[DYK🔎] Lay the chapter's arc down for the walk: vv.1-5 (the 144,000), vv.6-7 (first
angel), v.8 (second angel), vv.9-12 (third angel), vv.13-20 (the two harvests). We follow
it bottom to top, exactly as Haskell does (SSP 246-265).

## [⟲] The Father's name in their foreheads — the seal re-met

Verse 1 gives the mark of this company — a name written where chapter 13's victims were to
take a different mark:

> "...having his Father's name written in their foreheads." (Revelation 14:1)

This is not a fresh symbol. We have met it — in Revelation 7's sealing:

> "...till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads." (Revelation 7:3)

- [⟲] **WEAVE (SAME instance):** the Father's name written in foreheads (Rev 14:1) = the
  **seal of God** of Rev 7:2-3 = the **Sabbath commandment**. DAR links Rev 14:1 directly
  back to Rev 7's sealing — **same** sealing work, two angles:
  > "An important key to an understanding of the seal of God is thus furnished us."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 584_
- [→] the seal of God = **the fourth commandment** — the only command of the decalogue
  carrying God's descriptive title and name (DAR 584.2). A guide overlay, carried here:
  > "The Sabbath commandment is the only one which has this; that is, that contains the
  > descriptive title which distinguishes the true God from all false gods... whoever keeps
  > this commandment has, consequently, the seal of the living God."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 584_
- [CTRF] the seal of God / the mark — fully walked at **R10** (Rev 13)
- [→] hold a deliberate contrast for later: this **forehead-name** (the seal) stands against
  the **forehead/hand mark** of v.9 — the two opposite seals of the final division. We pay
  it off at the third angel.

[DYK🔎] the confirming witness for the two seals is reserved for the third-angel section

## [KEY🔑] Not defiled with women; the firstfruits — the verse defines itself

Here, and only here in the chapter, the **verse hands us its own dictionary.** Verse 4
states what its symbols mean:

> "These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are virgins... These were
> redeemed from among men, being the firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb."
> (Revelation 14:4)

[KEY🔑] **This is the one true self-definition in Revelation 14.** "Women" and
"firstfruits" are decoded by the text and by Scripture — not by overlay.

Decode "women" by Scripture — woman = church:

> "A woman is in Scripture the symbol of a church, a virtuous woman representing a pure
> church, a corrupt woman an apostate church... they are not defiled with, or have no
> connection with, the fallen churches of the land."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 584_

Haskell corroborates straight from the canon:

> "The church is represented by a woman; and the relation of Christ to the true church, as
> the relation of the husband to his wife."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 249_

- [→] the picture is Isaiah 4:1 — _"seven women shall take hold of one man"_ — and the
  144,000 are "snatched as brands from the burning," the **firstfruits** of the harvest
- [⟲] **WEAVE (DIFFERENT instance — guard against blur):** the "women" of 14:4 are **NOT**
  the one pure woman of Rev 12, nor the single harlot of Rev 17. Three distinct women, one
  symbol-type (woman = church), three applications:
  - (1) **Rev 12 pure woman** = the true church
  - (2) **Rev 17 harlot** = apostate Rome / Babylon the Great
  - (3) **the "women" of 14:4** = apostate / fallen churches broadly (Babylon's daughters)
    > "...a chaste woman standing for a pure church, a vile woman for an impure or apostate
    > church."
    > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 603_
- [→] **the character determines the church.** Same symbol every time — pure or corrupt is
  read off the woman's character, not assigned. Never blur the three.

## The first angel — "the hour of his judgment is come" (vv. 6-7)

Now the message that gathers the 144,000 begins. First herald:

> "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to
> preach... saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his
> judgment is come." (Revelation 14:6-7)

- [→] the three angels are **SYMBOLIC** — a historicist overlay, carried by DAR:
  > "the preaching of the gospel has not been intrusted to literal angels; it has been
  > committed unto men... Each of these three angels, therefore, symbolizes a body of
  > religious teachers."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 586_
- [→] the first angel was fulfilled in the **1840-1844 Advent movement** — overlay, carried:
  > "It went forth with the power thus indicated between the years 1840-44... bringing us to
  > the end of the 2300 days, when the work of Judgment commenced as Christ began the work
  > of cleansing the sanctuary."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 600_
- Haskell ties this angel straight back to the mighty angel of Revelation 10 and the 2300
  days of Daniel:

> "the prophetic time to which he referred was the twenty-three hundred days of Daniel
> 8:14. The message was given between 1833 and 1844."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 252_

> "Christ entered the most holy place... and commenced the work of the investigative
> judgment."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 255_

- EGW confirming witness — angels behind the Miller movement, sounding the very words:

> "Angels of God accompanied William Miller... sounding far and near the cry, Fear God, and
> give glory to Him; for the hour of His judgment is come."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 232_

### [⟲] The spine payoff — Daniel 8:14 / Daniel 7:9-10 land here

> "...unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."
> (Daniel 8:14)

> "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit..." (Daniel 7:9)

- [⟲] **WEAVE (the spine):** the "hour of his judgment" = the **pre-advent investigative
  judgment** Daniel dated to 1844. The **cleansing of the sanctuary IS the judgment** this
  angel announces:
  > "It is this work of judgment, immediately preceding the second advent, that is announced
  > in the first angel's message of Revelation 14:7."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 352_
- [→] this is the destination of the whole arc. The 2300-day sanctuary spine — raised at Dan
  8 (R03), opened at the judgment scene of Dan 7 (R07) — **lands here**, made into a
  worldwide cry. The date Daniel set, Revelation proclaims.

## The second angel — "Babylon is fallen" (v. 8)

Second herald, drawing a line through the churches that reject the first:

> "And there followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great
> city..." (Revelation 14:8)

- [→] **Babylon defined etymologically** — overlay, carried by DAR:
  > "the word means confusion... used figuratively to designate the great symbolic city of
  > the book of Revelation."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 601_
  > "Babylon signifies the universal worldly church... a chaste woman standing for a pure
  > church, a vile woman for an impure or apostate church."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 603_
- [→] so Babylon is **not** the city of Rome nor the papacy alone — it is the universal
  worldly church, fallen
- Haskell — the second angel marked the moment the churches shut the door:

> "closed their doors against the everlasting Gospel; and ministers gave their flocks to
> drink of the wine of fornication, a mixture of truth and error."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 257_

- EGW confirms the same definition and the mother-and-daughters reading:

> "Babylon... signifies confusion... a virtuous woman representing a pure church, a vile
> woman an apostate church."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 381_

> "churches that... follow her example... the message... must apply to religious bodies
> that were once pure and have become corrupt... it cannot refer to the Roman Church alone."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 382_

- [→] the fall is a **MORAL fall** — progressive, and not yet complete — overlay, carried:
  > "The second angel's message... was first preached in the summer of 1844... but the
  > message of the second angel did not reach its complete fulfillment in 1844... they have
  > fallen lower and lower."
  > — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 389_
- [⟲] **WEAVE (RECAPITULATED instance):** the 14:8 announcement, the fuller Rev 17 harlot
  portrait, and the Rev 18 loud-cry repetition are **NOT three separate falls** but **ONE
  progressive reality** — same entity, recapitulated with rising force. The 14:8 announcement
  began 1844; the Rev 18 repetition is the loud cry near probation's close (EW 277.1;
  DAR 619.1).
- [→] **Next:** Rev 17-18 gives Babylon's full portrait — **R13**.

## The third angel — the mark, the wrath, and the Sabbath (vv. 9-11)

Third herald — the severest warning in all of Scripture:

> "If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in
> his hand, The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God..." (Revelation 14:9-10)

> "...the most fearful import. No severer threatening of divine wrath can be found in all
> the Bible."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 619_

- [→] **the beast = the Roman Catholic power; the mark = the change of the Sabbath** —
  overlay, carried:
  > "the mark of the beast is that institution which this power has set up as proof of its
  > authority... a change of the law of God... the seventh-day Sabbath... is torn from its
  > place in the decalogue, and a false and counterfeit Sabbath, the first day of the week,
  > is set up in its stead."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 624_

### [⟲] The beast is one power across three books

> "And I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea..."
> (Revelation 13:1)

- [⟲] **WEAVE (SAME instance):** the beast of 14:9 = the leopard-beast of **Rev 13:1** = the
  little horn / fourth beast of **Dan 7** — **ONE** historical power (papal Rome) across
  Daniel 7, Revelation 13, and Revelation 14 (DAR 524.2 / 524.8 point-by-point; DAR 624.2)
- [⟲] and the **42 months / 1260** (Rev 13:5 = Rev 12:6,14 = Dan 7:25) = the **SAME**
  538-1798 span behind this beast — already forged at D07/R10. Same power, same clock.

Haskell — the counter-sealing runs against the seal of God:

> "a counter sealing goes on at the same time... He who opposeth... puts his seal in the
> place of the seal of the King of heaven. The image to the beast enforces the observance
> of Sunday... instead of the Sabbath of the fourth commandment."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 259_

EGW — the third angel sent as Christ passed into the holiest:

> "a fearful warning, with the most terrible threatening ever borne to man."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 254_

And the mark is received only when the issue is made plain and forced:

> "Sunday observance shall be enforced by law, and the world shall be enlightened
> concerning the obligation of the true Sabbath."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 449_

- [→] **pay off the v.1 contrast** — Christendom divides into exactly two companies, two
  seals:
  > "Christendom will at last be divided into just two classes... those who are sealed with
  > the seal of the living God... and those who are sealed with the seal of the beast."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 628_

## The patience of the saints — "keep the commandments of God" (v. 12)

Between the warning and the harvest stands the company that holds fast:

> "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and
> the faith of Jesus." (Revelation 14:12)

- [→] overlay, carried — the third message is a time of patience; the keeping is the **ten
  commandments**, the Sabbath in vivid contrast with the counterfeit:
  > "The period of the third message is a time of patience... this waiting company are
  > keeping the commandments of God — the ten commandments, and the faith of Jesus... The
  > true Sabbath... is thus brought out in vivid contrast with the counterfeit Sabbath, the
  > mark of the beast."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 633_
- [→] the three messages are **CUMULATIVE** — not three dead steps that end, but one living
  threefold message going forward together:
  > "one does not cease when another is introduced... we now have three messages going forth
  > simultaneously, or, rather, a threefold message... the last one... being the leading
  > proclamation."
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 619_

EGW — the divine authority of the threefold message, the crown of Daniel's whole line:

> "I was shown three steps — the first, second, and third angels' messages... Woe to him

## The two harvests — the wheat and the winepress (vv. 13-20)

The chapter closes on a harvest field with **two reapings** — and the text marks them as
two events, at two times.

> "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth..." (Revelation 14:13)

- [→] these receive a **special resurrection** at the seventh plague, rising to join the
  living saints and be counted among the 144,000 (DAR 634.1; cf. Dan 12:2; Rev 1:7)

Haskell — Christ lays off the priestly robe for the kingly:

> "On leaving the temple... the Son of man exchanges His priestly garments for those of a
> king. The royal diadem is placed upon His brow... an angel crying... Thrust in Thy
> sickle, and reap: for the time is come for Thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is
> ripe."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 263_

**Verses 14-16 — the harvest of the wheat: the second advent, gathering the righteous:**

> "And I looked, and behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of
> man... and in his hand a sharp sickle." (Revelation 14:14)

> "earth beholds her King, seated upon [the cloud]. In the hand of the King, is the law of
> God, which is as a sharp twoedged sword, and the wicked fall before the brightness of His
> countenance. Those who are one with Christ, will be drawn upward."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 264_

**Verses 17-20 — the vine and the winepress: the FINAL destruction of the wicked:**

> "...and cast it into the great winepress of the wrath of God." (Revelation 14:19)

- [→] guard the distinction — overlay, carried:
  > "The last two angels have to do with the wicked... May it not be that the closing doom
  > of that class at the end of the thousand years is here presented... the righteous clothed
  > with immortality... the wicked perishing around the city at the time of its ultimate
  > location upon the earth?"
  > — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 636_
- [→] **the winepress = the FINAL destruction of the wicked outside the city at the end of
  the 1000 years** (Rev 20:9) — **NOT** the same event as the wheat-harvest second advent.
  Two reapings, two times: **wheat at the second advent (now); the vintage at the close of
  the millennium.**
- [⟲] forward link: Rev 19 (the rider treads "the winepress of the fierceness... of God" —
  **R14**) and Rev 20 (the wicked destroyed outside the city — **R15**).
