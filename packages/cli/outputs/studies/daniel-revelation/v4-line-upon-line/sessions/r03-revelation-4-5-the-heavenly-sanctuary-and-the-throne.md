# Revelation 4-5 — The Heavenly Sanctuary and the Throne

**The door Daniel could only point to is now thrown open: John walks straight into the
heavenly sanctuary and watches the slain Lamb take the sealed book. The sanctuary Daniel
8:14 promised would be cleansed is here SHOWN — throne, lamps, living creatures, and all —
and the Bible labels its own furniture, line upon line: the lamps ARE the seven Spirits,
the vials ARE the prayers of saints, the Lamb's seven horns ARE perfect power and perfect
wisdom.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel dated the
> sanctuary and pointed; Revelation 4-5 opens the door and SHOWS it. Watch the verse hand
> us the dictionary for its own furniture — and watch the guides locate every piece in the
> two-apartment temple, line upon line.

---

## The setup — a door opened, not heaven opened

- After the seven churches (R02), John hears the trumpet-voice again: **"Come up hither"**
  (Rev 4:1) — the same prophet, now lifted into the place the churches were addressed FROM
- [→] DAR's careful reading: John says a door was **OPENED IN heaven**, not _into_ heaven —
  not the sky split open (as for Stephen, _"I see the heavens opened,"_ Acts 7:56) but a
  **specific apartment in heaven disclosed to view**

> "someplace, or apartment, in heaven was opened before him... That this apartment which
> John saw open was the heavenly sanctuary, will plainly appear from other portions of the
> book."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 384_

- [→] as Christ rose in sympathy with the suffering Stephen, so the same Lord answers John —
  and invites him up to be shown _"things which must be hereafter"_

> "the yearning felt by John, touched the heart of Christ."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 92_

[→] Frame the whole session: **this is a SANCTUARY vision.** Everything John sees must be
read against the two-apartment temple, and the test of each item is simple — _does Scripture
or the guides locate it?_

## The throne and the rainbow — the plan of redemption written into heaven

> "And immediately I was in the spirit: and, behold, a throne was set in heaven, and one
> sat on the throne. And he that sat was to look upon like a jasper and a sardine stone:
> and there was a rainbow round about the throne, in sight like unto an emerald."
> (Revelation 4:2-3)

- [→] the rainbow round the throne = **mercy encircling judgment** — heaven itself drawn as
  the plan of salvation

> "Heaven, from whatever standpoint it may be viewed, presents the plan of Redemption...
> heaven reflects it in all its works."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 93_

- [→] establish the identity that holds the whole chapter: **"him that sat on the throne" =
  the Father** — confirmed when the Son enters separately in Rev 5 as the Lamb

> "the same view is still before the mind of the apostle."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 391_

[→] The rainbow anticipates the union of justice and mercy the sanctuary embodies — it sets
up the Most Holy weave that runs through the rest of Revelation.

---

## [→] The twenty-four elders — redeemed saints crowned in heaven

> "And round about the throne were four and twenty seats: and upon the seats I saw four and
> twenty elders sitting, clothed in white raiment; and they had on their heads crowns of
> gold." (Revelation 4:4)

- **NOT a [KEY🔑]** — no verse here defines the elders. This is the DAR/Haskell overlay, so
  it must carry the guide quote.
- [→] white raiment + victor crowns = the tokens of a finished fight — those who once trod
  this earthly pilgrimage, now crowned in advance of the great multitude

> "tokens both of a conflict completed and a victory gained... once trod, in common with all
> saints, this earthly pilgrimage, but have overcome; and for some good purpose, in advance
> of the great multitude of the redeemed, are wearing their victor crowns in the heavenly
> world."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 386_

- [→] who are they? — the firstfruits of the resurrection, men from every nation, redeemed
  by Christ's own blood

> "represent men from every kindred, tongue, and people, redeemed by the blood of Christ...
> who arose from the grave when Christ came from the tomb."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 96_

- [DYK🔎] the company offered as _"firstfruits"_ — those whose graves opened at the
  crucifixion (_"and the graves were opened; and many bodies of the saints which slept
  arose,"_ Matt 27:52-53), led captive at the ascension (_"he led captivity captive,"_ Eph
  4:8)
- [→] their **priestly work is deferred to Rev 5** — both guides note the elders are only
  _"mentioned in this connection"_ here; their ministry is described in chapter 5 (this sets
  up the golden-vials KEY)

## [⟲] The twenty-four THRONES — and the thrones of Daniel 7:9

The KJV reads "seats," but the Greek reads **θρόνοι — thrones** (Revised Version: _"round
about the throne were four and twenty thrones"_).

- [→] Greek is auxiliary here, not load-bearing: it only confirms the elders are
  **enthroned**, which the scene already shows

> "the four and twenty elders sitting... These are the same thrones."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 387_

**[⟲] SAME-instance weave:** these are the same thrones Daniel saw —

> "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit..." (Daniel 7:9)

- [⟲] **resolve the contradiction the reader carries from D07.** "Cast down" does NOT mean
  overturned — it means **placed / established** (the Eastern custom of casting down mats or
  divans for guests). Daniel 7:9 shows the thrones being **SET** when the judgment commences
  in the Most Holy; Rev 4 shows them already **occupied**. Same thrones, two moments.
- [⟲] **the sanctuary-court payoff begins here:** the throne-room Daniel watched the
  judgment convene in (Dan 7:9-10, _"the judgment was set, and the books were opened"_) is
  the very room John now stands **inside.**

## [KEY🔑] The seven lamps of fire = the seven Spirits of God

The verse defines its own symbol. We read the label off the page:

> "And out of the throne proceeded lightnings and thunderings and voices: and there were
> seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God."
> (Revelation 4:5)

[KEY🔑] **seven lamps of fire ARE the seven Spirits of God** — Scripture handing us the
dictionary for its own furniture, exactly as Rev 1:20 said _"the candlesticks... are the
seven churches."_

[→] **The sanctuary OVERLAY** (distinct from the KEY, so it carries the guide quote): the
seven lamps = the antitype of the golden candlestick of the earthly sanctuary — and that
candlestick stood in the **FIRST APARTMENT.**

> "it is good proof that he is looking into the first apartment of the sanctuary above."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 388_

- [→] confirming witness — the prophetic eye sees the same first-apartment furniture:

> "He beheld there 'seven lamps of fire burning before the throne'... the first apartment of
> the sanctuary in heaven... represented by the golden candlestick and the altar of incense
> in the sanctuary on earth."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 414_

- [→] **seven = completeness / perfection** — hold this phrase, "the seven Spirits of God":
  it will recur two verses into Rev 5 on the Lamb's horns and eyes. One symbol, two angles.

## [→] The sea of glass — and [⟲] the sea mingled with fire (Rev 15:2)

> "And before the throne there was a sea of glass like unto crystal..." (Revelation 4:6)

- **NOT a [KEY🔑]** — the verse does not define it. Treat it as a described feature of the
  sanctuary court.
- [⟲] **RECAPITULATED-instance weave** (guard against blurring this with a different
  instance): the SAME location, seen at two different times in two different conditions. Rev
  4:6 = its **present** state, transparent like crystal.
- [⟲] the same sea returns at the END of history — mingled with fire — when the redeemed
  stand victorious:

> "And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the
> victory over the beast, and over his image... stand on the sea of glass, having the harps
> of God. And they sing the song of Moses... and the song of the Lamb." (Revelation 15:2-3)

> "Upon the crystal sea before the throne, that sea of glass as it were mingled with fire,—so
> resplendent is it with the glory of God,—are gathered the company that have 'gotten the
> victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his
> name.' With the Lamb upon Mount Zion, 'having the harps of God,' they stand, the hundred
> and forty and four thousand that were redeemed from among men."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 648_

- [⟲] **flag the discipline:** same place, recapitulated at the eschatological moment — do
  **not** confuse the present crystal sea with the future fire-mingled scene. **One location,
  two times.**

## [⟲] The four living creatures — Ezekiel's creatures, and NOT the beasts of Rev 13

> "...and in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, were four beasts full of
> eyes before and behind." (Revelation 4:6)

- "beasts" is an unhappy KJV translation. **NOT a [KEY🔑]** (no verse defines them); the
  overlay reading carries the guide quote.
- [→] the Greek word here is **ζῷον — a living creature** — a different word entirely from
  the prophetic beasts later in the book:

> "denotes properly a living creature... The word is very different from θηρίον, used to
> designate the prophetic beasts in the 13th and following chapters."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 388_

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT-instance weave (critical):** the ζῷα of Rev 4-5 (redeemed beings round the
  throne) are **NOT** the θηρία of Rev 13 (political-religious powers). These must **never**
  be conflated — the reader who learned _"beast = kingdom"_ in D07 must not import it here.
- [⟲] **SAME-instance weave:** these are the same living creatures **Ezekiel** saw —

> "Ezekiel and John, one before Christ's advent, the other after, describe the same thing,
> showing that the New Testament is but the unfolding of the Old."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 98_

- [→] the four faces — lion, calf, man, eagle (Rev 4:7) — draw the fourfold character of God
  that Christ combined (and they foreshadow the Lion-then-Lamb of the next chapter):

> "four phases of the character of God... the Lion of the tribe of Judah... the slain lamb,
> and the priest... God in the heavens, and God on the cross."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, pp. 98-99_

## Revelation 5 — a new chapter, the same scene: the sealed book

- [→] **not a new vision** — _"the same view is still before the mind of the apostle"_ (DAR
  391). The Father still sits enthroned; now John sees the book in His right hand:

> "And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on
> the backside, sealed with seven seals." (Revelation 5:1)

- [→] decode the book — its meaning comes from the guide, not from an "X are Y" verse, so
  this is a **[→] overlay**, not a [KEY🔑]:

> "The book... contained a revelation of scenes that were to transpire in the history of the
> church to the end of time. Its being held in the right hand... may signify that a
> knowledge of the future rests with God alone, except so far as he sees fit to reveal it."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 391_

- [→] this book **IS the program of R04-R06** (the seals and trumpets): what the Lamb is
  about to unseal is the church's history the rest of Revelation narrates.

## [→] "Who is worthy?" — and John weeps

> "And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, Who is worthy to open the book,
> and to loose the seals thereof? And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the
> earth, was able to open the book... And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to
> open and to read the book." (Revelation 5:2-4)

- [→] the challenge is **not a reproof** but a summons to the whole universe — and John, as
  one of the fallen race, weeps:

> "It was not a reproof, but a call to all the universe of God, to witness anew the glory of
> the Son of man... a fresh unfolding of the plan of salvation."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 102_

- [Q] John, "a representative of the fallen race," weeps the question every sinner feels:
  _Must the work of earth cease? Was the sacrifice a failure?_
- [→] the dramatic hinge — **the worthiness question is answered only by redemption.** It
  sets up the Lion who is a Lamb.

## [→] The Lion of Judah who is a slain Lamb — Christ takes the book

> "...Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to
> open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof. And I beheld... in the midst of the
> throne... stood a Lamb as it had been slain..." (Revelation 5:5-6)

- **NOT a bare [KEY🔑]** — no verse says _"the Lamb IS Christ"_ in so many words (contrast
  Rev 1:20 _"candlesticks ARE churches"_). The identification is the canon-wide overlay (_"Behold
  the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world,"_ John 1:29), so it carries the
  guide quote.

> "In the center of all the glory, in the very presence of Life, before adoring angels and
> witnesses from earth, stood a Lamb, slain, its life-blood dripping from its veins."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 104_

- [→] and the Lamb takes the book — **because not even Christ does the work alone:**

> "He came and took the book out of the right hand of Him that sat upon the throne; for not
> even Christ could do the work alone. The power came from the Father. Father and Son unite
> in the work of Redemption."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 105_

- [→] the **Lion AND the Lamb are one Christ** — kingly conqueror precisely _by being_ the
  slain sacrifice. Ties back to the fourfold character (SSP 98) and forward to the King of
  Rev 14 and 19.

## [KEY🔑] The seven horns and seven eyes = perfect power and perfect wisdom

The verse defines its own symbol — the symbol the spec almost missed:

> "...a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven
> Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth." (Revelation 5:6)

[KEY🔑] **seven horns and seven eyes ARE the seven Spirits of God** — read straight off the
page.

> "Horns are symbols of power, eyes of wisdom; and seven is a number denoting completeness...
> perfect power and perfect wisdom inhere in the Lamb, through the operation of the Spirit of
> God, called the seven Spirits of God."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 395_

- [⟲] **SAME-symbol weave:** the "seven Spirits of God" here = the **seven lamps** of Rev
  4:5 — two verses defining **one** symbol from two angles (the fullness and perfection of
  the Holy Spirit). Same entity, same session: **the slain Lamb is full of the very same
  Spirit that burns before the throne.**

## [KEY🔑] The golden vials full of odours = the prayers of saints

The verse defines its own symbol — the strongest priestly-ministry payoff in the session:

> "...the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one
> of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints."
> (Revelation 5:8)

[KEY🔑] **golden vials full of odours ARE the prayers of saints** — a clean
verse-self-definition.

- [→] from this we learn the **employment** of the redeemed — they present the prayers of
  saints as incense:

> "the employment of those redeemed ones... This is a work of ministry such as pertains to
> priests."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 396_

- [→] like the high priest on the Day of Atonement offering much incense, the redeemed in
  heaven carry the prayers of penitent sinners before the Lamb:

> "When the high priest entered on the day of atonement into the Holy of Holies, he made his
> offering for the people with much incense... To-day in heaven those who once lived on
> earth... take the prayers offered by penitent sinners, and present them before the Lamb."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 106_

- [→] **confirming witness + SANCTUARY weave** — this is the antitypical daily ministration
  begun at the ascension, in the first apartment:

> "It was the work of the priest in the daily ministration to present before God the blood of
> the sin offering, also the incense which ascended with the prayers of Israel... the prayers
> of penitent believers."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 420_

## [⟲] The new song, the universal anthem — and ten thousand times ten thousand (Daniel 7:10)

> "And they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals
> thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood..." (Revelation 5:9)

> "...and the number of them was ten thousand times ten thousand, and thousands of thousands;
> Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the Lamb that was slain..." (Revelation 5:11-12)

> "from ten thousand times ten thousand of angel voices there rang the response: Worthy is
> the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honor,
> and glory, and blessing."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 108_

- [⟲] **SAME-instance weave:** _"ten thousand times ten thousand"_ (Rev 5:11) is the very
  expression of **Daniel 7:10** —

> "The abiding place of the King of kings, where thousand thousands minister unto Him, and
> ten thousand times ten thousand stand before Him (Daniel 7:10)."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 414_

- [⟲] the **SAME heavenly court** Daniel saw in his judgment vision and John sees in his
  sanctuary vision — **not two events, one throne-room.**
- [→] the concentric-circle picture: nearest the throne the living creatures, next the
  elders, then the multitude of angels surrounding the whole:

> "Round about the throne are those represented by the four living creatures. Next come the
> four and twenty elders. Then John views, surrounding the whole, a multitude of the
> heavenly angels."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 398_

## [→] "Every creature" — anticipation, not present fact

> "And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as
> are in the sea... heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, be unto him
> that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever." (Revelation 5:13)

- [→] DAR's discipline against flattening the chronology — this verse leaps ahead to the
  finish:

> "a declaration thrown in out of its chronological order... the time is anticipated when
> redemption is finished... the universe be freed from sin and sinners, and a universal song
> of adoration go up to God and the Lamb."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 399_

- [→] **guard the reader:** Rev 5:13 is NOT a snapshot of the present — sin and the little
  horn still rage in the chapters ahead. It is a prophetic **look-over** to the clean
  universe. The vision leaps to the finish, then the seals (R04) return us to the long
  history in between.

## Appeal

- The sanctuary Daniel could only date and point toward (_"then shall the sanctuary be
  cleansed,"_ Dan 8:14) is now thrown open before your eyes — a real temple, a real throne, a
  real Lamb still bearing the marks of the slaughter, ministering **your** prayers as golden
  incense before the Father.
- The worthiness question — _"Who is worthy?"_ — was answered by no power, no wisdom, no
  merit in heaven or earth, but only by **the blood of the slain Lamb.** The same Lamb holds
  the book of your future and is found worthy to open it.
- You were not left out of that throne-room: the twenty-four elders are _"men from every
  kindred, tongue, and people"_ — the redeemed are **already represented** before the throne.
  Cast your lot with the company that overcame, and let the Lamb who took the book take your
  case.
- And your prayers do not fall to the floor — they rise as incense, presented by priestly
  hands before the throne (Rev 5:8). **Pray as one whose petitions are carried into the
  heavenly sanctuary by the slain Lamb.**

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: the heavenly sanctuary is SHOWN.** Daniel pointed (Dan 8:14, the
sanctuary cleansed; Dan 7:9-10, thrones set and books opened). Revelation 4-5 SHOWS it —
the first-apartment furniture (seven lamps / candlestick, golden altar / incense), the
throne-court of Daniel 7, the same ten-thousand-times-ten-thousand host, and the Lamb
beginning His mediatorial ministry. Hold three keys forged here: **(1)** seven lamps / seven
horns-and-eyes = the seven Spirits of God (the full perfection of the Spirit); **(2)** golden
vials = the prayers of saints (the priestly incense ministry); **(3)** the sealed book = the
church's history to the end — the seals (R04) open that very book. And guard two
weave-verdicts: the living creatures (ζῷον) are **NOT** the beasts of Rev 13 (θηρίον) —
redeemed beings, not kingdoms; and the crystal sea of Rev 4 = the fire-mingled sea of Rev 15
— **one place at two times.**
