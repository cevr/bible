# Revelation 1 — The Opening Vision

**The book is named for what it does: it _reveals_. Its first chapter hands us the master
key — the glorified Son of man walking among seven candlesticks, holding seven stars. The
same conquered-death Christ of Daniel's vision, present and unwithdrawn with ALL His
people, from John's day to the end of time.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel handed us the
> dictionary; Revelation opens by naming itself "a revelation" and then defines its own
> symbols in verse 20 — line upon line.

---

## "The Revelation" — a book unsealed (Rev 1:1-3)

Open on the title itself. The very first word settles the argument:

> "The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants
> things which must shortly come to pass..." (Revelation 1:1)

- [→] **the name refutes the rumor.** A "revelation" is something _revealed_ — not sealed,
  not hidden. The title alone tells us this book is meant to be understood.

> "A revelation is something revealed, something clearly made known, not something hidden
> and concealed... The very title of the book, then, is a sufficient refutation of the
> popular opinion of to-day, that this book is among the hidden mysteries of God, and cannot
> be understood."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 324_

- [→] anchor it in the law: _"The secret things belong unto the LORD our God: but those
  things which are **revealed** belong unto us and to our children for ever"_ (Deut 29:29).
  A revealed thing belongs to **us**.

[DYK🔎] Verse 1 packs the whole frame into one line — title, author, object, manner, and
agent all named at once:

> "The first chapter of Revelation is an introduction to the entire book... the first verse
> is the key, not only to Revelation, but to every prophetic book in the Bible, showing how
> all prophecy is given."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 28_

And the prophetic eye says the same — this book was given to be opened, not shelved:

> "This revelation was given for the guidance and comfort of the church throughout the
> Christian dispensation... But God does not wish His people to regard the book thus. It is
> 'the revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto Him, to show unto His servants things
> which must shortly come to pass.'"
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 583_

- [→] so here is our method, set by the book's own name: if verse 1 calls itself a
  _revelation_, we read it **expecting to understand.** Scripture is its own expositor.

> "Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep
> those things which are written therein: for the time is at hand." (Revelation 1:3)

## [⟲] The unfolding of Emmanuel — the hinge from Daniel's Son of man (Rev 1:7-8)

Before the symbols, the Person. Christ describes Himself in His own words:

> "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him..." (Revelation 1:7)

> "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which
> was, and which is to come, the Almighty." (Revelation 1:8)

- [→] read these as **Christ's own self-witness** — Christ describing Christ. NOT decoded as
  a separate symbol: no verse defines "Alpha and Omega" as X = Y. It is the Almighty naming
  Himself, not an emblem awaiting a key.

This is the through-line for the whole series — the book is, from first to last, the story
of one Person:

> "To John, then, was laid open, or made manifest, the mystery of Emmanuel, the union of the
> divine and human, the Christ. The entire book of Revelation is an explanation of the
> divine life which God placed in the human mold... the history of the church is the history
> of Emmanuel,—the mystery of godliness."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 29_

### [⟲] WEAVE — the SAME Son of man as Daniel 7:13

The Son of man we are about to meet walking among the candlesticks (Rev 1:13) is **the same
Son of man** of Daniel's vision:

> "I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of
> heaven, and came to the Ancient of days..." (Daniel 7:13)

- [⟲] **verdict: SAME.** Not a recapitulated different figure — one Person, one continuous
  ministry. Daniel saw Him enthroned and judging; John sees Him tending His churches. Both
  visions place Him at His post-ascension priestly-kingly station.
- [→] guard the weave: do **not** blur this into a "different Christ" or a different period.
  Daniel's enthroned Son of man is John's Son of man among the lamps. This is the bridge from
  the Daniel arc straight into Revelation.

## "The Lord's day" — Christ Lord of the Sabbath (Rev 1:10)

> "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a
> trumpet." (Revelation 1:10)

- [→] the verse names a day singled out as **the Lord's** — but does not itself spell out
  _which_ day. That identification is the DAR overlay, so this is a **teaching connector**,
  not a [KEY🔑]. The verse hands us the title "the Lord's day"; the expositor supplies which
  day wears it.

> "There is in this dispensation one day distinguished above the other days of the week as
> the Lord's day... the apostle has given us, near the close of the first century, apostolic
> sanction for the observance of the only day which can be called the Lord's day, which is
> the seventh day of the week."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 341_

- [→] establish it by **Christ's own words** — Scripture on Scripture, no lexicon load-bearing:

> "For the Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day." (Matthew 12:8)

- [→] the Lord's day is the day of which **Christ is Lord.** And the same Son of man who is
  Lord of the Sabbath is the very One John is about to see in the midst of the candlesticks.
  The through-line holds: one Person, one Lord, one day He claims as His own.

## The seven churches — seven periods of the gospel age (Rev 1:11)

> "Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a
> book, and send it unto the seven churches which are in Asia..." (Revelation 1:11)

- [→] the verse names seven **literal** Asian churches. The historicist reading — seven =
  seven successive periods of the church age — is the DAR/Haskell **overlay**, a teaching
  connector carrying its guide quote, **not** a [KEY🔑]. The verse names the cities; the
  expositors read the ages.

First, why seven? The number is itself a sermon:

> "Seven, as used in the Scriptures, is a number denoting fulness and completeness... The
> whole gospel church in seven divisions, or periods, must be symbolized by them."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 329_

- [→] seven literal churches stand for the **whole** church — every age of it:

> "'the seven churches' are doubtless to be understood to mean not merely the seven literal
> churches of Asia which went by the names mentioned, but seven periods of the Christian
> church, from the days of the apostles to the close of probation."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 329_

[DYK🔎] The very **names** encode each period's character — DAR 329.2 reads the religious
features of each age out of the meaning of its church's name. We will walk that in R02.

And Haskell draws the same line — the seven span the entire history:

> "The Spirit used each of those churches to represent a period in the history of the work
> of God on earth, the seven covering the time from the life of John to the closing events
> in the history of the world."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 31_

The prophetic eye confirms it, completeness and reach together:

> "The names of the seven churches are symbolic of the church in different periods of the
> Christian Era. The number 7 indicates completeness, and is symbolic of the fact that the
> messages extend to the end of time."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 585_

[Q] If seven literal lamp-stands carry the whole church across all time — which age is the
last? The names run Ephesus → Smyrna → Pergamos → Thyatira → Sardis → Philadelphia →
Laodicea. We will walk them one age at a time — and the last one is our own.

## [⟲] The Son of man among the candlesticks — the same Jesus (Rev 1:12-13)

John turns to see the voice — and meets a Person:

> "And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden
> candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks one like unto the Son of man,
> clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle."
> (Revelation 1:12-13)

The voice is mighty as many waters — yet it is no stranger:

> "...but still, to John He was the same Jesus... Not now despised, mocked, and rejected, but
> standing in the midst of the seven candlesticks... clothed... in a garment of righteousness...
> girt about the loins with the golden girdle of truth... His head and His hairs were white
> like wool, as white as snow."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 35_

### [⟲] WEAVE — SAME Son of man as Daniel 7:13

> "...his head and his hairs were white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes were as a
> flame of fire." (Revelation 1:14)

- [⟲] **verdict: SAME.** The white head and hair echo Daniel's vision; DAR identifies this
  figure unambiguously as **Christ at His heavenly-sanctuary post.** Both visions locate Him
  in the sanctuary — one Person, not a recapitulated new figure.

### [⟲] WEAVE — Christ's angel = Gabriel of the Daniel arc

The same messenger who served Daniel carries the Revelation:

> "...who, in this work, can be his angel but he who was engaged with him in the former work,
> that is, the angel Gabriel?"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 325_

- [⟲] cf. _"there is none that holdeth with me in these things, but Michael your prince"_
  (Dan 10:21). The angel who made Daniel understand (Dan 8:16; 9:21) is the angel who signified
  the Revelation to John — **same Gabriel, both books.**
- [→] guard against blur: this is the **same Christ** across both books and the **same Gabriel**
  across both books. Continuity, not the recapitulation of some new entity.

## [KEY🔑] The candlesticks ARE the churches — and NOT the Mosaic menorah (Rev 1:20a)

Now the verse defines its own symbol — the one place a true KEY belongs:

> "The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden
> candlesticks. The seven... candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches."
> (Revelation 1:20)

- [KEY🔑] **candlestick = church.** The text says it outright. We are not interpreting a
  lamp-stand; we are reading a label, exactly as Daniel read "beast = kingdom."

Then the overlay layer, carried by the guide quotes — the scope is **all** churches, **all**
time:

> "The providential care and presence of the Lord are with, not a specified number of churches
> only, but all his people; not in the days of John merely, but through all time. 'Lo! I am
> with you alway.'"
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 343_

### [⟲] WEAVE — DIFFERENT from the single OT candlestick

Are these seven lamp-stands the old temple menorah? **No** — and the difference is the whole point:

> "These cannot be the antitype of the golden candlestick of the ancient typical temple
> service; for that was but one candlestick with seven branches... here are seven... the stands
> are so distinct, and so far separated... that the Son of man is seen walking about in the
> midst of them."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 342_

- [⟲] **verdict: DIFFERENT.** The Mosaic menorah (Ex 25, **one** stand, seven branches) is the
  **type**; the seven **separate** lamp-stands of Rev 1 are the **antitype** = the whole gospel
  church. Do not collapse them into one object — Christ walks _between_ them, so they must be
  distinct.

[DYK🔎] **This opens Revelation as a sanctuary book.** The candlesticks frame the entire
volume:

> "The book of Revelation is a revelation of Christ's work in the heavenly sanctuary. The first
> chapter presents Him walking in the midst of the seven candlesticks, guarding and directing
> His people."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 363_

> "The golden candlestick represented the church of God. It was of beaten work... it takes
> many trials and chastisements to eradicate pride, envy and covetousness from the people of
> God, and blend them into one complete church."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 367_

- [→] hold this frame: the sanctuary thread runs the whole book — the lamps here (ch. 1), the
  lamps before the throne (ch. 4), the incense altar (ch. 8), the ark (ch. 11). Revelation 1 sets
  the room we will walk through to the end.

And the prophetic eye shows the same Christ at the same work:

> "He is represented as walking up and down in the midst of His churches on the earth. With
> untiring wakefulness and unremitting vigilance, He watches to see whether the light of any
> of His sentinels is burning dim or going out."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 586_

## [KEY🔑] The seven stars ARE the angels of the churches; the keys of death in His hand (Rev 1:16, 18, 20b)

The second self-definition, in the same verse:

> "And he had in his right hand seven stars... The seven stars are the angels of the seven
> churches." (Revelation 1:16, 20)

- [KEY🔑] **star = angel (messenger) of the church.** The verse defines its own symbol — a true
  KEY. And this definition **rules** what follows: _"These things saith he that holdeth the
  seven stars in his right hand"_ (Rev 2:1). Defined once, the meaning governs every message.

Read the stars as the ministers / messengers / light-bearers of the churches:

> "Christ... held in His hand the seven stars or angels, which direct the work of the churches,
> and which are light-bearers from His throne... The church completes the work begun by Christ in
> the flesh... His life as recorded in the Revelation... is but a further unfolding of that same
> mystery of the incarnation,—the Emmanuel."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 38_

The prophetic eye gives the comfort — the stars are held by Omnipotence:

> "...no church faithful to its trust need fear coming to nought, for not a star that has the
> protection of Omnipotence can be plucked out of the hand of Christ."
> — _EGW, The Acts of the Apostles, p. 586_

[DYK🔎] Christ Himself defines both symbols in the chapter — EGW AA 585.2 marks Rev 1:18-20 as His
own words decoding the candlesticks and the stars. The interpreter is the Author.

And His hand holds more than the stars — it holds the keys of the grave:

> "I am he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the
> keys of hell and of death." (Revelation 1:18)

> "Death is a conquered tyrant... the key to his dark prison-house has been wrenched from his
> grasp, and is now held in the hands of a mightier than he... this one is the unchanging Friend
> and the pledged Redeemer of his people."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 343_

- [→] the One walking among the lamps is the One who died and rose — the keys of death are **in
  His hand.** The church's Keeper is the grave's Conqueror.

## Appeal

- The same glorified Son of man walks among His churches **today**, with untiring vigilance,
  watching every lamp (_AA 586_). He holds His messengers safe in His right hand — not a star
  under His protection can be plucked out (_AA 586_). He holds the keys of the grave itself
  (_DAR 343_).
- The Divine Presence has **never** been withdrawn:

> "Lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world... that promise, reverberating from age
> to age, has been the comfort and solace of each individual Christian, and of the church as a
> body."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 39_

- The One who tends seven lamps across seven ages is tending **yours.** Will you let the Son of
  man who walks among the candlesticks trim and fill **your** lamp?

[CHAIN] — **Link forged: candlestick = church, star = messenger (Rev 1:20) — and the Son of man
among the lamps is Daniel's Son of man (Dan 7:13), continued.** Daniel's enthroned, judging Christ
is now seen tending His people; the candlestick is the sanctuary **type** made **antitype** (SSP
363), opening the heavenly-sanctuary thread — ch. 4 lamps, ch. 8 incense, ch. 11 ark — we will
follow through the rest of the book. Hold it.
