# Daniel 8:14 — The Sanctuary: Which Sanctuary, What Cleansing?

**One verse fires a question the whole world has a stake in — "the sanctuary shall be cleansed" — and Daniel 8:14 will not let us guess the answer. Which sanctuary? What cleansing? We refuse to import an answer; we let Scripture hand us the earthly type (Lev 16), then watch Hebrews point the type to its heavenly antitype, and the 2300 days land the cleansing in 1844. The pioneers' first answer was wrong — and the Bible itself corrected it.**

> The rule of the road: a symbol means what Scripture says it means. Daniel 7 ended in a courtroom; Daniel 8 names the time and the place. Hold the question before reaching for the answer — line upon line.

---

## The hinge — one verse, a question with universal stakes

The whole session hangs on a single sentence. Put it under the lens whole:

> "And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed." (Daniel 8:14)

- [→] the verse raises **two** questions it does not yet answer: **WHICH** sanctuary, and **WHAT** cleansing? We hold the question before reaching for any answer — that is the discipline of the whole series
- the stake is not academic. DAR sets the weight before we move an inch:

> "Connected with the 2300 days is another subject of equal importance... namely, the sanctuary; and with this is also connected the subject of its cleansing... all the inhabitants of the earth... have a personal interest in that solemn work."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 160_

- [→] the cleansing is linked to the 2300 (the time-span of D08), and to **every** living soul. This is not a side-room of prophecy; it is the courtroom Daniel 7 opened.

[Q] If the cleansing of the sanctuary decides every case of the saved and lost, can any reader afford to leave "which sanctuary" to guesswork?

## [CTRF] The wrong answer first — and why it had to be corrected

Before we read the answer off the page, honor the pioneers who reached for it first. They had the **right method** and the **wrong object** — and that gap is the most instructive thing in the chapter.

- William Miller, following the year-day rule rightly, reached the **wrong object**: he thought the sanctuary was the **earth** (or the church), cleansed by **fire** at the Second Advent
- Miller's own reasoning, narrowing the candidates — and landing on the earth:

> "Which of these sanctuaries does Daniel mean...? Not the second, heaven, for that is not unclean... Then there are but two things more... and those are the EARTH and the CHURCH: when these are cleansed... will the entire sanctuary of God be cleansed."
> — _William Miller, Letter to Joshua V. Himes, on the Cleansing of the Sanctuary, p. 7-8_

- and so Miller expected the cleansing **at the advent**, not before it:

> "one short year more... and then he will cleanse his sanctuary, and make the place of his feet glorious"
> — _William Miller, Letter to Joshua V. Himes, on the Cleansing of the Sanctuary, p. 3_

- the Introduction to Early Writings records both the method and the mistake:

> "Miller accepted the generally received view, that in the Christian age the earth is the sanctuary... the cleansing of the sanctuary foretold in Daniel 8:14 represented the purification of the earth by fire at the second coming of Christ."
> — _Early Writings, Introduction, p. ix_

- [→] the **method** was right (Scripture its own expositor; year-day); the **object** was assumed, not read off the page. This session re-runs the question the right way — and the Bible itself supplies the correction (we close this counterfeit-pair at the end, EW 243.2)

## [KEY🔑] The sanctuary defined by Scripture — candidates narrowed to one

We do not vote on "which sanctuary." The Bible defines "the sanctuary" by its own words. First, the **earthly**, first-covenant sanctuary:

> "Then verily the first covenant had also ordinances of divine service, and a worldly sanctuary... a tabernacle made; the first, wherein was the candlestick, and the table, and the shewbread; which is called the sanctuary." (Hebrews 9:1-2)

And then it names a **second**, new-covenant sanctuary — in heaven:

> "...such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; A minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man." (Hebrews 8:1-2)

- [KEY🔑] Scripture names **exactly two** sanctuaries: the earthly (Heb 9:1-2) and the heavenly true tabernacle (Heb 8:1-2). That is the dictionary, handed to us.
- [→] Paul's language closes the door on every other candidate:

> "It forever annihilates the claims which are put forth in behalf of the earth, the land of Canaan, or the church, as the sanctuary."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 166_

- [→] one candidate remains — the heavenly true tabernacle:

> "...what Paul calls in Hebrews the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man... which he locates in the heavens... on the view here suggested rests our only hope of ever understanding this question."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 164_

- [→] so the **first** question is answered by the verses themselves, not by us: the sanctuary of Dan 8:14 is the **heavenly true tabernacle**.

## Type and antitype — the new covenant must have its own sanctuary

The earthly was never the end of the matter; it was the **figure** of the heavenly. Hebrews says so outright:

> "It was therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these... For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us." (Hebrews 9:23-24)

- [KEY🔑] the earthly = the **figure**; the heavenly = the **true**. The pattern points up.
- [→] the analogy itself demands a new-covenant sanctuary:

> "...is there no sanctuary which pertains to the second, or new covenant? There must be; otherwise the analogy is lacking... the new covenant has likewise its services and its sanctuary."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 168_

- Haskell frames the whole relation — three sanctuaries, type giving way to substance:

> "Three sanctuaries... the heavenly... the earthly sanctuary was a miniature model of the heavenly one... For more than fourteen hundred years, God designed that the service should be in the shadowy sanctuary. The time came when those following the shadow reached the substance."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 134_

- and Moses built from a pattern he had **seen**:

> "Before his wondering gaze was spread out the beauties of the heavenly sanctuary. Forty days the Lord communed with him, giving minute directions in regard to building a shadow of that heavenly structure upon the earth."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 138_

## [⟲] WEAVE — earthly vs. heavenly: type and antitype, not the same object

Here the interpretive risk concentrates. The earthly and the heavenly are **one institution** as type-and-antitype — but **two objects** in space and dispensation. Never blur them.

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT-instance verdict:** the earthly (Heb 9:1, first covenant) and the heavenly (Heb 8:2, new covenant) are the **same institution** as type-and-antitype, but **different objects** — one made with hands, one pitched by the Lord; one old-covenant, one new
- [→] and the time-span itself proves which one Dan 8:14 means:

> "...the earthly was to be cleansed each year; and we make the prophet utter nonsense, if we understand him as saying that at the end of 2300 days... an event should take place which was to occur regularly every year. The heavenly sanctuary is the one in which the decision of all cases is to be rendered."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 178_

- [→] the 2300 days cannot terminate in a **yearly earthly rite** — the time-span itself forces the **antitype**. The WHICH-sanctuary question is now closed by the verse's own arithmetic, not by assumption.

## [KEY🔑] What the cleansing IS — not fire, but the putting away of sin

Now the **second** question. As with the sanctuary, we let Scripture define "the cleansing." Leviticus 16 names it plainly:

> "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD." (Leviticus 16:30)

And Hebrews ties cleansing to **blood**:

> "And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission." (Hebrews 9:22)

- [KEY🔑] the cleansing is a cleansing from **SIN** (Lev 16:30); cleansing = **remission**, and remission requires **blood** (Heb 9:22)
- [→] DAR draws the conclusion the verses force:

> "Remission, then, that is, the putting away of sin, is the work to be done. The cleansing, therefore, is not physical cleansing, but a cleansing from sin."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 173_

- [→] the WHAT-cleansing question is answered: **not** the burning of the planet, but the judicial removal of recorded sin. Miller's fire-reading is overturned by the Bible's own definition.

## How sin got INTO the sanctuary — the earthly type read off Leviticus

If the cleansing removes sin, then sin must first have got **in**. The earthly type shows exactly how — the daily service carried sin **into** the sanctuary:

> "The sin of the individual was thus, by his confession, by the slaying of the victim, and by the ministry of the priest, transferred from himself to the sanctuary... thus the sanctuary continually became the receptacle of the sins of the congregation... The accumulated guilt was removed by a special service, which was called the cleansing of the sanctuary."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 174_

- [→] sin accumulated in the sanctuary **all year**; one day removed it. Haskell marks that day as the crown of the whole calendar:

> "In the autumn, on the tenth day of the seventh month, came the crowning service of the year. All other services were a preparation for this. Day by day the sins of the people had been transferred in type and shadow to the priest and the sanctuary, and once each year these were to be cleansed, and the sins forever removed."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 147_

- [→] so the cleansing answers a **real need** — the type explains **why** a sanctuary in heaven would need cleansing at all.

## [KEY🔑] The two goats — the Lord's goat (Christ) and the scapegoat (Azazel)

The Day of Atonement turns on **two goats**, and the Bible names them by lot:

> "And Aaron shall cast lots upon the two goats; one lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat." (Leviticus 16:8)

And the high priest, on that day, reached the **Most Holy** furniture:

> "...which had the golden censer, and the ark of the covenant overlaid round about with gold... And over it the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat..." (Hebrews 9:4-5)

- [KEY🔑] two goats, divided **by lot** — one for the LORD, one for the scapegoat (Lev 16:8); the day reaches the ark and the mercy seat (Heb 9:4-5)
- Haskell walks the rite:

> "...two goats were brought, lots were cast, and one was chosen for the Lord's goat, while the other, Azazel, the scapegoat, represented the evil one. The Lord's goat was slain. With its blood and the golden censer, the priest entered within the second veil."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 149_

- [→] the Lord's goat (slain, blood carried **in**) figures **Christ**; the scapegoat (sent away **alive**) is a **different** figure — resolved next.

## [⟲] WEAVE — the scapegoat is Satan, NOT Christ

This is the specific error the series must guard against. The two goats are **not** two pictures of one Savior.

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT-instance verdict:** the Lord's goat = **Christ** in His sacrificial/intercessory work; the scapegoat (Azazel) = **SATAN** — a different figure entirely. Reading Christ as the scapegoat is the error to refuse.
- [→] the antitype shows the scapegoat's true role:

> "...in the antitype, the Saviour, in the presence of the Father, the angels of God, and all the redeemed host, will lay the sins of the righteous upon the head of Satan, and a mighty angel will lead him away to the desolate earth, where he will remain a thousand years."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 151_

- [→] the **order** settles it: Satan bears the sins **only after** the High Priest has finished His work — bearing ultimate responsibility at the close of the investigative judgment, **not** as a sin-bearing savior. Christ already paid; Satan answers for instigating.
- [⟲] forward-thread: this scapegoat-led-to-the-desolate-earth is the **seed of the 1000 years** — we meet it again in Revelation (the millennium, Rev 20). Flagged here, not developed.

## The antitype — Christ our High Priest cleansing the heavenly sanctuary

Now lift the whole type into heaven. Since Christ ascended, sin has been transferred **in fact** to the heavenly sanctuary:

> "...so ever since Christ ascended to be our intercessor... the sins of all those who sincerely seek pardon through him, are transferred in fact to the heavenly sanctuary where he ministers."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 176_

- [→] **this** is why the heavenly needs cleansing — and the cleansing is Christ's **closing** work:

> "In the antitype, the cleansing of the sanctuary must be the closing work of Christ, our great High Priest... our High Priest, in like manner, enters into the most holy place to make a final end of his intercessory work in behalf of mankind."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 177_

- Haskell dates the antitypical Day of Atonement:

> "The period of the investigative judgment opened in 1844 when every character would be measured by the standard of God's law... The day of atonement was the type of the judgment."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 148_

- and names what is happening **now**:

> "Since 1844 the world has been living in the great antitypical day of atonement... We are living in the time when our great High Priest is cleansing the heavenly sanctuary, removing the sin records."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of Daniel the Prophet, p. 151_

- [⟲] **RECAPITULATED verdict:** same structure as the type (high priest enters the Most Holy, blood applied, sins removed, scapegoat sent away) — but now the antitype, universal and final

## Year-day applied — the 2300 days land the cleansing in 1844

Back to the time-symbol of the verse itself:

> "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed." (Daniel 8:14)

- [→] the time-symbol is **2300 days**. Apply the series' year-day rule (Num 14:34; Eze 4:6 — a prophetic day = a literal year): **2300 prophetic days = 2300 literal years** (guide/EGW overlay — carry the quote)
- the arithmetic, run by EGW:

> "The seventy weeks—490 days—having been cut off from the 2300, there were 1810 days remaining... From A.D. 34, 1810 years extend to 1844. Consequently the 2300 days of Daniel 8:14 terminate in 1844."

- [→] the precise antitypical date — the very Day of Atonement:

> "The tenth day of the seventh month, the great Day of Atonement, the time of the cleansing of the sanctuary, which in the year 1844 fell upon the twenty-second of October."

- [→] and the shift of place in 1844 — Christ entering the Most Holy:

> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 433_

## [⟲] WEAVE — the 2300 and the 70 weeks: ONE vision, one starting point

The 2300 needs a starting date — and Daniel 9 supplies it, because the two prophecies share one vision and one start.

- [⟲] **SAME-vision / SAME-start verdict:** the 70 weeks of Dan 9:24 are a **part** "cut off" from the 2300 days of Dan 8:14 — the same vision, the same starting point, the 70 weeks being the **first segment** of the longer period, NOT a separate prophecy and NOT coextensive with the 2300 (Heb. _chathak_ = severed)
- [→] the proof from the Hebrew and the context:

> "The word here translated 'determined,' literally signifies 'cut off.' Seventy weeks... are declared by the angel to be cut off... As the 2300 days was the only period of time mentioned in chapter eight, it must be the period from which the seventy weeks were cut off."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy (1888), p. 326_

- [→] forward pointer (not a disclosure of the answer): **Gabriel returns in Daniel 9** expressly to finish the explanation he could not complete in Daniel 8 — the 457 B.C. anchor that **dates** the 2300 is established there. We reach it next.
