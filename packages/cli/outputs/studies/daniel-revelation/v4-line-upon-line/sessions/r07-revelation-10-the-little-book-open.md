# Revelation 10 — The Little Book Open

**The same book Daniel was told to SEAL "till the time of the end" is now in the
angel's hand OPEN — and the Bible itself ties the knot. Daniel's sealed time-prophecy,
unsealed in Revelation 10, became the worldwide Advent cry of 1840–1844: sweet in the
mouth, bitter in the belly. Watch Revelation re-draw Daniel's own key, line upon line —
and watch a disappointment that prophecy itself foretold turn from wormwood back to
nourishment.**

> The rule of the road: where the Bible defines a symbol, we read the label; where the
> Bible seals a symbol, we stop. Watch Revelation 10 reach back to Daniel and open the
> book Daniel was told to shut — line upon line.

---

## The setup — a mighty angel comes down with something OPEN

- Years of empire-sweep behind us — R06's trumpets just closed; the **sixth trumpet
  ended in 1840** (SSP 187.1). Now a single **mighty angel** descends,
  clothed with a cloud, a rainbow on his head, his face as the sun, his feet as pillars
  of fire (Rev 10:1)
- The one detail the whole session turns on:

> "And he had in his hand a little book **open**: and he set his right foot upon the sea,
> and his left foot on the earth." (Revelation 10:2)

- **open** — not closed, not sealed. That single word forces a question the verse does
  not itself answer.
- [→] the rule of the road restated: **a book described as _open_ implies it was at some
  time _closed_.** The verse begs the question — WHICH book, and when was it sealed? We
  do not guess; we let Scripture point us to its own dictionary.
- [→] open the angel's **posture** too: one foot on the sea, one on the land (Rev 10:2) —
  a worldwide reach we will decode in place.

DAR draws the inference that frames the whole chapter:

> "He had in his hand a little book open. There is a necessary inference to be drawn from
> this language, which is, that this book was at some time closed up."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 488_

## [⟲] First weave: the OPEN little book = Daniel's SEALED book, now unsealed

The verse does not name the book — so we reach to where the Bible **did** name a sealed
book:

> "But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the
> end." (Daniel 12:4)

> "...for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end." (Daniel 12:9)

- [⟲] **SAME book, two moments** — sealed in Daniel, open in Revelation. There is only
  one sealed book in all of Scripture, and only one place it is ever opened:

> "There is no book spoken of as closed up and sealed except the book of Daniel's
> prophecy; and there is no account of the opening of that book, unless it be here in the
> 10th of Revelation."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 488_

- the **content-match** clinches it: Daniel's sealed words concern **TIME** — the 2300
  days (Dan 8:14), sealed at Dan 8:26 (_"shut thou up the vision"_) — and the Rev 10
  angel bases his whole proclamation on **TIME**. Same subject, same book.

Haskell is sharper still, naming exactly which sealed message:

> "Daniel's prophecy was preeminently a time message... 'shut up the words, and seal the
> book, even to the time of the end'... The period of time which Daniel sought to
> understand, was the two thousand three hundred days, at the end of which time, the
> sanctuary would be cleansed. This is the only sealed message of the Word."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 183_

- [→] note the discipline: this is a **[⟲] weave, not a [KEY🔑].** Rev 10:2 shows the
  book is open but **never names it.** The identification as Daniel's book is the DAR /
  Haskell weave off Dan 12:4 — so it carries the guide quote, not a verse self-label.
  Where the verse defines, we read the label; here the verse points, and Scripture
  elsewhere answers.

## [→] When does the angel cry? After 1798 — and the book itself dates him

The book was sealed only _"till the time of the end"_ — so when it appears **open**, the
angel must be proclaiming **in the time of the end** (after 1798). The seal is its own
calendar.

> "...the prophetic periods of Daniel, were not to be opened till the time of the end;
> and if this is the book which the angel had in his hand open, it follows that he
> proclaims his message... somewhere this side of the commencement of the time of the
> end."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 489_

- [→] Haskell fixes the moment **by the trumpets** — the sixth trumpet (the second woe)
  ended in 1840:

> "It was at the close of the second woe, in 1840, that this angel with the open book of
> Daniel, set one foot on the land and one on the sea."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 184_

- [→] note the interval: between the sixth and seventh trumpets is the _"quickly"_ of Rev
  11:14 — the window **1840–1844**, the precise span of the loud cry (SSP 187.1).

## [→] The sea-and-land posture = the WORLDWIDE Advent proclamation

The verse shows the posture (Rev 10:2) but does not gloss it — so this is a teaching
connector carrying the guide, **not** a verse-key.

> "And he set his right foot upon the sea, and his left foot on the earth." (Revelation
> 10:2)

- [→] DAR's overlay — one foot on sea, one on land = the **wide extent** of the
  proclamation:

> "The position of this angel, one foot upon the sea and the other on the land, denotes
> the wide extent of his proclamation by sea and by land... he has one foot upon the sea,
> from which we may infer that his message would cross the ocean... and this inference is
> strengthened by the fact that the Advent proclamation... did go to every missionary

Haskell paints the cry that woke the nations:

> "'he cried with a loud voice' like the roar of a lion in the forest, and this cry awoke
> men from their slumber, and startled nations. No man was too humble, no place too
> secluded; that voice penetrated everywhere."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 184_

[DYK🔎] The movement really was worldwide — one message, many lands, at once:

> "In America the movement was led by William Miller, in England, by Edward Irving; in
> Asia, by Joseph Wolff, a Christian Jew; in Sweden, where the laws prohibited adults

## [⟲] Second weave: the Rev 10 angel = the FIRST angel of Revelation 14

The mighty angel of Rev 10 is not a stranger — he is the **first angel of Revelation 14**
already on the move. DAR gives four identity marks that admit no question:

> "(1) They both have a special message to proclaim; (2) they both utter their
> proclamation with a loud voice; (3) they both use similar language, referring to the
> great Creator as the maker of heaven and earth, the sea, and the things that are
> therein; and (4) they both proclaim time, one swearing that time should be no more, and
> the other proclaiming that the hour of God's Judgment has come."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 490_

- [⟲] **SAME angel, same proclamation** — special message, loud voice, the Creator's own
  language, and a message of **time.** The cry of 1840–1844 was the "full and
  circumstantial accomplishment" of both (DAR 491.1).
- [CHAIN] forward-link planted: this angel hands us the **first angel's message** that
  R11 (Revelation 14) will detail in full. Hold it.

## [→] "Time no longer" (Rev 10:6) = PROPHETIC time closes — not eternity, not probation

The angel lifts his hand and swears:

> "And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever... that there should be **time no
> longer**." (Revelation 10:6)

The verse states the oath but does not define **which** time — so the gloss is the
guides', a [→], not a verse-key. DAR rules out the wrong readings precisely:

> "It must therefore mean prophetic time; for there is no other to which it can refer...
> no prophetic period should extend beyond this message; those that reach to the latest
> point would all close there."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 492_

- [→] it cannot mean **time-in-this-world** (the very next verse speaks of the days of
  the seventh angel, events still in the present state); it cannot mean **probationary
  time** (that runs till Christ closes His priestly work). It can only mean **prophetic
  time.**
- [⟲] **RECAPITULATED, not a new time-word.** This is NOT a fresh prophetic period — it
  is the declaration that the **last** of the existing periods now terminates. The 1260
  of Dan 7:25 = Rev 12:6/14 = Rev 13:5 already closed in the 538–1798 window; the
  **longest**, the **2300 days**, closes in the autumn of **1844.** Never blur this with
  a new period.

Miller names the witness for the whole period-stack:

> "The time when these things shall take place is also specified... unto 2300 days,
> (meaning years;) then shall the sanctuary be cleansed, after the anti-Christian beast
> has reigned her 'time, times, and a half'... after the church captivity in the
> wilderness, 'forty-two months'."
> — _William Miller, Miller's Works, Vol. 2, p. 6_

And Haskell pins the seventh trumpet to the close of prophetic time:

> "The close of the prophetic period was 1844... the seventh trumpet began to sound when
> prophetic time was at an end, that is, in 1844."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 187_

## [⟲] Third weave: "the mystery of God finished" (Rev 10:7) = the SANCTUARY payoff in 1844

The angel adds **what** the seventh trumpet brings:

> "But in the days of the voice of the seventh angel, when he shall begin to sound, the
> **mystery of God should be finished**, as he hath declared to his servants the
> prophets." (Revelation 10:7)

- [⟲] **SAME event, two sides** — earth-side (Rev 10: no more prophetic time) and
  heaven-side (Dan 8:14: the sanctuary cleansed). DAR locates the seventh trumpet's
  commencement "at the close of the prophetic periods in 1844" (DAR 493).

Haskell delivers the unsealing's true content — the sanctuary truth:

> "In 1844 the antitypical work of the day of atonement was begun in the heavenly
> sanctuary... Christ at that time passed within the veil... The investigative judgment
> was opened, and in the first case decided before the throne, the work of finishing the
> Gospel was begun."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 188_

The prophetic eye confirms the resolution — the mistake was never in the time:

> "Both the prophecy of Daniel 8:14... and the first angel's message... pointed to
> Christ's ministration in the most holy place, to the investigative judgment, and not to
> the coming of Christ... The mistake had not been in the reckoning of the prophetic
> periods, but in the event to take place at the end of the 2300 days."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 424_

★ **The TIME-PROPHECY / SANCTUARY payoff lands here.** The 2300/1844 the reader dated
back at D08–D09 is precisely what Daniel was told to seal — now unsealed, proclaimed, and
fulfilled.

## [KEY🔑] Sweet in the mouth, bitter in the belly (Rev 10:9-10) — the verse self-describes

This symbol the **verse itself** describes — Scripture names the experience in its own
words:

> "Take it, and eat it up; and it shall make thy belly bitter, but it shall be in thy
> mouth sweet as honey... it was in my mouth sweet as honey: and as soon as I had eaten
> it, my belly was bitter." (Revelation 10:9-10)

- [KEY🔑] **sweet, then bitter** — the verse defines the eating in its own words. No
  overlay needed for the experience itself; Scripture spells it out.
- [→] **what the sweet-then-bitter MEANT in history is the overlay** — carry the guide,
  do not read it off the verse: the joyful reception of the Advent message, then the
  bitter disappointment of October 1844.

DAR digests the disappointment back into nourishment:

> "the joy with which they received the message of Christ's immediate second coming, the
> honey-like sweetness of the precious truths then brought out, and the sadness and pain
> that followed, when at the appointed time in 1844 the Lord did not come... What had been
> so like honey to their taste, suddenly became like wormwood and gall. But those who had
> patience... soon learned that the mistake was only in the event, not in the time."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 495_

Haskell measures the depth of the bitterness:

> "No earthly inducement ever seemed so sweet as the message of His coming; no
> disappointment was ever so bitter as that experienced by the believers in the second
> coming of Christ."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 189_

- [→] keep the two apart: the **sweet/bitter eating** is verse-self-defined ([KEY🔑]);
  the **1844-disappointment meaning** is the guide overlay ([→]). One is the label, one
  is the history.

## [⟲] The tarrying was FORETOLD — and God's hand covered the mistake on purpose

The bitterness was not an accident of method. **Prophecy itself predicted a tarrying** —
doctrine wrote the history in advance:

> "...though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come..." (Habakkuk 2:3)

> "While the bridegroom tarried, they all slumbered and slept." (Matthew 25:5)

- [⟲] the tarrying of Hab 2:3 and Matt 25:5 = the very delay the believers met. The Word
  foretold the wait; the history filled it in.

And the test was **deliberate** — the prophetic eye says so plainly:

> "God designed to prove His people. His hand covered a mistake in the reckoning of the
> prophetic periods. Adventists did not discover the error, nor was it discovered by the
> most learned of their opponents."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 373_

The personal testimony adds the resolution — the hand removed, the figures cleared:

> "His hand covered a mistake in the reckoning of the prophetic periods... God designed
> that His people should meet with a disappointment... The hand of the Lord was removed
> from the figures, and the mistake was explained. They saw that the prophetic periods
> reached to 1844."
> — _EGW, Early Writings, p. 235-236_

- [→] the **sanctuary** was the key that turned wormwood back to honey:

> "The subject of the sanctuary was the key which unlocked the mystery of the
> disappointment of 1844... so did those now rejoice who had looked in faith for His
> second coming."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 423_

The definitive verdict, to settle the chain:

> "The computation of the prophetic periods on which that message was based, placing the
> close of the 2300 days in the autumn of 1844, stands without impeachment."
> — _EGW, The Great Controversy, p. 457_

## [⟲] The seven thunders (Rev 10:3-4) — a SEALED symbol that stands alone

Some symbols the Bible defines by **commanding silence.** When the angel cried and the
seven thunders answered, John was told **not** to write:

> "...and when he had cried, seven thunders uttered their voices. And when the seven
> thunders had uttered their voices, I was about to write: and I heard a voice from heaven
> saying unto me, **Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them
> not.**" (Revelation 10:3-4)

- [⟲] **DIFFERENT** — a unique symbol with **no** cross-referential parallel. There is no
  weave to make and no key to read; the text rules it sealed, and we honor the ruling.
- [→] DAR conjectures the thunders may stand for the sealed record of the Adventist
  disappointment experience — but this stays **conjecture**, never a firm key or weave.
  The seven thunders are honored exactly as the text leaves them: **sealed.**
- [→] method note: the discipline cuts **both ways** — where the Bible defines, we read
  the label; where the Bible seals, we stop. We do not force a meaning Scripture withheld.

## [⟲] Fourth weave: "Thou must prophesy again" (Rev 10:11) = the THIRD angel's message

After the disappointment, the commission is **renewed** — the movement that ate the bitter
book is sent out again:

> "And he said unto me, **Thou must prophesy again** before many peoples, and nations, and
> tongues, and kings." (Revelation 10:11)

- [⟲] **RECAPITULATED with expanded scope** — not a stand-alone. John, as representative
  of the church, receives a charge that DAR identifies as the third angel's message:

> "...a prophecy of the third angel's message, now... in process of fulfilment. Neither
> will this work be done in a corner."
> — _Uriah Smith, Daniel and the Revelation, p. 496_

Haskell agrees and names the text:

> "...the angel said, 'Thou must prophesy again, before many peoples...' This foretells
> the third message of Revelation 14:9-12, which will go to all the world, increasing as
> it goes, until it swells into a loud cry."
> — _S.N. Haskell, The Story of the Seer of Patmos, p. 190_

- [CHAIN] the hinge: the movement that ate the bitter book did **not** sink out of sight —
  it was commissioned to carry the **third angel's message.** That is R11 (Revelation 14).
