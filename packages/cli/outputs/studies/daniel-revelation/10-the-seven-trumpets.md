# Study 10: The Seven Trumpets — Revelation 8-9

**The seventh seal opens, and heaven falls silent. Then seven angels receive seven trumpets. Where the seals traced the church's own history, the trumpets announce God's judgments on the powers that persecuted her. Four trumpets in sequence sound the fall of Western Rome — Goths, Vandals, Huns, and the deposition of the last emperor in AD 476. A fifth trumpet brings smoke, locusts, and a five-month torment that historicist pioneers tied to the rise of Saracen and Ottoman power. A sixth trumpet releases an army of two hundred million and names a prophetic time — hour, day, month, and year — that Josiah Litch computed publicly, in print, and named August 11, 1840 as the endpoint before the event occurred. On that date, the Ottoman Empire was placed under European tutelage by the Convention of London's ultimatum. Millerites read the diplomatic event as a public fulfillment; the Advent movement's audience expanded dramatically afterward. Study 10 walks the trumpets honestly — the strongest cases, the debated cases, and the public apologetic test that shaped the historicist method as the pioneers moved toward 1844.**

## From the Seventh Seal to the Seven Trumpets

Rev 8 opens with a half-hour of silence. Then the scene shifts.

- **"Silence in heaven about the space of half an hour"** (Rev 8:1)
  - The loudest place in the universe — the throne where "holy, holy, holy" never stops (Rev 4:8) — goes quiet
  - In Jewish temple practice, the daily incense offering was accompanied by silence in the court (Zech 2:13; Hab 2:20). The half hour prefaces an act of priestly intercession
  - Historicist reading: a brief hush in the vision's narrative, marking the transition from one recapitulation cycle (the seals) to the next (the trumpets)

- **The angel at the altar of incense** (Rev 8:3-5)
  - "Another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne"
  - "The smoke of the incense, which came with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand"
  - Then — "The angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast it into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake"
  - [→] The prayers of the saints _ignite_ the trumpets. The fire that judges the earth comes from the same altar where their prayers were received. The martyrs' "how long?" in Rev 6:10 gets its first partial answer here: the prayers are poured back as judgment
  - `[⟲]` **Sanctuary thread:** the golden altar of incense is the Holy Place's inner altar — the same altar Study 4 examined in Moses' pattern and Heb 9's heavenly original. Rev 8:3-5 is the third link.

- **The seven trumpets in Scripture**
  - At Sinai, the trumpet announced God's own voice (Ex 19:16-19). In battle, the trumpet signaled war (Num 10:9). On the Day of Atonement of the Jubilee, the trumpet announced release (Lev 25:9). The feast of trumpets preceded the Day of Atonement (Lev 23:24 — the Jewish Rosh Hashanah)
  - Trumpets in Revelation carry all three senses: God's voice, the call to battle, and the announcement of a coming judgment. The seven trumpets are the _warning_ blasts of history; the seven plagues (Rev 15-16) are the blows themselves

- `[⟲]` **Third recapitulation cycle.** Churches (Rev 1-3, internal spiritual condition) → Seals (Rev 6-7, external forces on the church) → Trumpets (Rev 8-9, political-military judgments on the persecuting powers). Same history, third angle.

## The First Four Trumpets: The Fall of Western Rome

The first four trumpets form a unit — the 4+3 pattern we met in the seals. Each strikes a "third part" of some natural element: earth, sea, rivers, heavenly bodies. Historicist interpreters from the Reformation forward have read these four as the four great waves that brought down Western Rome.

- **The historical spine** — Western Rome's disintegration, AD 400-476, came through a specific sequence of invading powers that historians still use as the standard frame:
  1. The Visigoths under Alaric — sack of Rome AD 410
  2. The Vandals under Genseric — sack of Rome AD 455, destroying much of the city
  3. The Huns under Attila — the Scourge of God, the Catalaunian Plain AD 451, devastating Italy AD 452
  4. The Heruli under Odoacer — deposing the last Western emperor Romulus Augustulus AD 476, ending the Western Empire

- **First trumpet — hail and fire mingled with blood** (Rev 8:7)
  - "Hail and fire mingled with blood... cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up"
  - Historicist reading: the Visigothic invasions under Alaric (ca. AD 395-410). Hail = storms from the north; fire = the burning of Roman cities; blood = slaughter; "third part" = the Western portion of the empire
  - Uriah Smith's _Daniel and the Revelation_ collected the classical historians who used exactly this weather-storm imagery for Alaric's incursions

- **Second trumpet — a burning mountain cast into the sea** (Rev 8:8-9)
  - "A great mountain burning with fire was cast into the sea: and the third part of the sea became blood"
  - Historicist reading: the Vandal invasions under Genseric (ca. AD 429-477). The Vandals conquered North Africa, then raided the Mediterranean from the sea, plundering Rome for two weeks in 455. A great burning mountain cast into the _sea_ = a maritime warrior power, the Vandal fleet
  - Within this reading, the "third part of the sea" corresponds symbolically to the disruption of Roman Mediterranean shipping and commerce under Vandal naval dominance

- **Third trumpet — Wormwood** (Rev 8:10-11)
  - "There fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters"
  - "The name of the star is called Wormwood... many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter"
  - Historicist reading: the Huns under Attila (ca. AD 433-453). A falling star with "burning as it were a lamp" = a meteoric rise and fall; Attila's empire flared brilliantly and collapsed within decades. His invasions struck the rivers of Europe (the Rhine, the Danube, the Po), terrorizing the populations along those arteries
  - "Wormwood" (Greek _apsinthos_) = bitterness; Attila's reputation as "the Scourge of God" matches the name

- **Fourth trumpet — sun, moon, and stars smitten** (Rev 8:12)
  - "The third part of the sun was smitten, and the third part of the moon, and the third part of the stars; so as the third part of them was darkened, and the day shone not for a third part of it"
  - In Scripture, sun/moon/stars often stand for political luminaries (Gen 37:9-11; Isa 13:10; Joel 2:10). The darkening of a third = the extinguishing of Western Rome's ruling authorities
  - Historicist reading: Odoacer's deposition of Romulus Augustulus in AD 476, ending the Western line of emperors. The sun (the emperor), moon (senate), and stars (provincial rulers) of the Western empire were put out. The East continued, but the West was dark

- **The "third part" pattern** (Rev 8:7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12)
  - Each of the first four trumpets strikes a _third_ — partial, not total, judgment. The first four trumpets fall on the Western Roman Empire, leaving the Eastern Empire (Byzantine/Constantinople) intact for now
  - [→] The trumpets are judgments on the persecuting power that crucified Christ and martyred His people for three centuries. But the judgment is _measured_ — a third, not the whole. God's wrath is never vindictive.

- **The woe-announcement between the fourth and fifth trumpets** (Rev 8:13)
  - "Woe, woe, woe, to the inhabiters of the earth by reason of the other voices of the trumpet of the three angels, which are yet to sound!"
  - The last three trumpets are marked out as "woes" — qualitatively different, more intense, reaching the empire's eastern heartland
  - [→] The first four brought down Rome's western half. The next three will deal with what rose in her place: the Islamic expansion (trumpet 5), the Ottoman consolidation and decline (trumpet 6), and the final announcement of the kingdom (trumpet 7, Rev 11:15)

[DYK🔎] The four-trumpet Western Rome mapping is one of the most stable interpretations in the historicist tradition — held by Mede, Newton, Wesley, Clarke, Keith, Uriah Smith, and the SDA pioneers. The specifics of which trumpet maps to which invader vary slightly (some reverse Huns and Vandals), but the overall picture — four blows, AD 395-476, Western Rome dismantled — is broadly agreed. This is not the fragile edge of historicism. It is the tradition's load-bearing historical spine.

## The Fifth Trumpet: The Locusts from the Pit

The fifth trumpet opens a darker register. The imagery becomes strange — smoke, locusts, iron breastplates, king-locusts with human faces and scorpion tails. And a number: _five months_.

- **The scene** (Rev 9:1-11)
  - "I saw a star fall from heaven unto the earth: and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit"
  - "He opened the bottomless pit; and there arose a smoke out of the pit, as the smoke of a great furnace; and the sun and the air were darkened by reason of the smoke"
  - "There came out of the smoke locusts upon the earth: and unto them was given power, as the scorpions of the earth have power"
  - "They should not hurt the grass... neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads"
  - "To them it was given that they should not kill them, but that they should be tormented five months"
  - The locusts: "crowns like gold" on their heads, "faces as the faces of men," "hair as the hair of women," "teeth as the teeth of lions," "breastplates of iron," "the sound of their wings as the sound of chariots of many horses running to battle," and "tails like unto scorpions"
  - Their king: "the angel of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon, but in the Greek tongue hath his name Apollyon" — both names mean _Destroyer_

- **The classic historicist reading (Keith, Litch, Smith)**
  - The imagery has been read as covering first the 7th-century Arab/Saracen expansion and then the Ottoman Turkish consolidation of the same Near-Eastern territory. Litch's actual dated calculation is anchored on the Ottoman phase (1299-1449); the broader Saracen imagery provides the pictorial layer, the Ottoman phase provides the time-marker
  - "Not hurt the grass... neither any green thing" = the traditional Islamic rules of war (attributed to the early Caliphs) forbidding the destruction of trees, crops, and grass during campaigns (Alexander Keith, _The Signs of the Times, as Denoted by the Fulfilment of Historical Predictions_, 1832, cited these rules from Arab historians)
  - "Smoke from the pit" = the rapid spread of a doctrinal system from an unexpected source; "darkened sun and air" = the obscuring of the pure gospel across the former Christian East
  - "Crowns of gold" = the turbans of the Saracen/Ottoman leaders (the Greek _stephanoi_ is flexible enough to cover ornamental headgear)
  - "Faces of men / hair as the hair of women" = the Arabic tradition of warrior beards and long hair
  - "Breastplates of iron" = heavily armored cavalry
  - "Sound of many chariots" = the famed noise of Arab/Turkish cavalry charges
  - "Scorpion tails" = the cavalry archer shooting behind while retreating, the tactical signature of the Saracen and Turkish armies
  - "Torment, not kill" = the Ottoman policy of subjugation (the _dhimmi_ status) rather than outright extermination of conquered Christians

- **The "five months"** (Rev 9:5, 9:10)
  - "Five months" is repeated at v. 5 and v. 10 — emphasis on a single 150-day period, not two distinct phases
  - Applying the year-day principle (Study 3): five months × 30 days = 150 days = 150 literal years
  - Historicist calculation (Litch, _Prophetic Expositions_ 1838; Uriah Smith _Daniel and the Revelation_ 1867, expanded edition): from the traditional anchor of Osman I's first major campaign against Byzantine territory (dated by Gibbon, and followed by Litch, to July 27, 1299) to AD 1449, when the last Greek emperor Constantine XI took the throne only with Ottoman permission — 150 years marking Ottoman rise to effective dominance over the Byzantine East
  - Honesty note: the July 27, 1299 date is the traditional Gibbon/Litch/SDA anchor, not the strongest date mainstream Ottoman scholarship would choose (modern historians more often mark the Battle of Bapheus, July 27, 1302, as the first major datable Ottoman victory over Byzantine forces). The 1449 endpoint is well-documented. The 150-year period frames the Ottoman rise accurately in broad terms; the precise start-date is the historicist tradition's anchor, not a neutral historical consensus
  - The same 391-year-and-15-day period that governs the sixth trumpet will carry from 1449 forward to 1840 (see below) — the two trumpets' numbers are sequential, not overlapping

- **The star with the key** (Rev 9:1)
  - Views differ: some historicists (Smith) read this as Muhammad himself; others read it as a more general spiritual/political permission given to the rising power; others as Satan personally (matching Rev 12:9's "cast to earth")
  - The text does not name the star — the fact that it is _given_ the key suggests this is a power permitted (not sovereignly holding) its authority over the pit's torment
  - We do not need to resolve the exact identification to read the trumpet: a power rose from a previously "sealed" region (the Arabian peninsula, off the map of classical powers), carrying with it a darkening of the former Christian East and a five-month (150-year) torment

- [→] The fifth trumpet is an honest piece of the trumpets' argument — it takes the period of Islamic expansion and consolidation seriously as one of history's great events, and reads it within the prophetic frame. Not every detail of the historicist mapping has the same weight. But the broad claim — that the rise of Islamic power and its conquest of the former Christian Near East is a prophetic event Revelation 9 has in view — is a claim worth taking seriously.

[Q] **"Isn't it uncomfortable to read a prophecy as being about Islam?"**
→ It is, and it should be read with care. Four honest qualifications: (1) Revelation's fifth trumpet describes a _judgment_ — not on Muslims personally, but on the _formerly Christian territories_ that had received the gospel and then fallen into the "darkness" of doctrinal compromise (the black horse, Pergamos, the fourth trumpet's darkened sun). The Near East had been Christian for centuries before Islam arose. The judgment is on the _prior compromise_, not on the people who carried its consequences. (2) The historicist reading distinguishes the political rise of a power from the spiritual condition of individuals within it. (3) The text says the locusts were _restrained_ from total killing — the judgment is bounded. (4) Every prophetic mapping, honestly done, can be read with respect for the peoples involved. The point is not polemic. It is to read what Revelation actually says about the history it addresses.

## The Sixth Trumpet: The 391 Years and Josiah Litch

The sixth trumpet is where the historicist case is strongest, because it gives a date.

- **The scene** (Rev 9:13-21)
  - "A voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God... Loose the four angels which are bound in the great river Euphrates"
  - "The four angels were loosed, which were prepared for an hour, and a day, and a month, and a year, for to slay the third part of men"
  - "The number of the army of the horsemen were two hundred thousand thousand [two hundred million]"
  - Their breastplates "of fire, and of jacinth, and brimstone"
  - The horses: "the heads of the horses were as the heads of lions; and out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone"
  - Their power "in their mouth, and in their tails: for their tails were like unto serpents, and had heads, and with them they do hurt"
  - "The rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not" (Rev 9:20)

- **The great river Euphrates**
  - The Euphrates in OT prophecy is Babylon's river (Jer 51:63-64) and the boundary of the promised land (Gen 15:18) — in Rev, it marks the region of the enemies of God's people
  - Historicist reading: the four angels "bound" at the Euphrates = the Ottoman power, restrained for a period, then loosed
  - Constantinople (the New Rome) fell to the Ottomans in 1453 — the event this trumpet's "loosing" historically refers to

- **The "hour, day, month, and year" — the prophetic period** (Rev 9:15)
  - Each unit converted by the year-day principle:
    - 1 year = 360 days (Jewish prophetic year) = 360 years
    - 1 month = 30 days = 30 years
    - 1 day = 1 year
    - 1 hour = 1/24 of a day = 1/24 of a year ≈ 15 days
  - Total: 360 + 30 + 1 + (15 days) = **391 years and 15 days**

- **Josiah Litch's calculation** (_The Probability of the Second Coming of Christ About A.D. 1843_, 1838; revised and expanded in later Millerite publications)
  - Litch anchored the period (following Gibbon's chronology) to **July 27, 1299** — the traditional date of Osman I's first major campaign against Byzantine territory
  - 1299 + 391 years and 15 days = **August 11, 1840**
  - Litch published this prediction in 1838, two years in advance, naming the exact date. His book and articles circulated publicly
  - On July 15, 1840, Britain, Austria, Prussia, Russia, and the Ottoman Empire signed the Convention of London to settle the Egyptian crisis (Mehmet Ali's revolt against the Sultan). The European powers issued an ultimatum to Mehmet Ali, which — according to contemporary Millerite reports — was placed in Mehmet Ali's hands "about August 11, 1840." Litch and the Millerites read this diplomatic moment — when the Ottoman Empire's internal sovereignty became dependent on European arbitration — as the public fulfillment of the sixth trumpet's "loosing"
  - Contemporary secular reporting documented the diplomatic event (_Blackwood's Edinburgh Magazine_, 1840; _The Times_ of London); the _prophetic_ interpretation was Litch's, and the Millerite movement amplified it

- **What this meant for the Advent movement**
  - Litch was not an Adventist when he made the prediction — he was a Methodist minister reading historicist commentaries
  - When the date hit, the wider historicist world took notice. William Miller's audiences expanded dramatically. Thousands who had been skeptical of the year-day principle took it seriously after seeing it publicly verified
  - The effect on the Advent movement was structural: if the year-day principle worked for the 391-year period of the sixth trumpet, it would work for the 2300-day period of Dan 8-9 ending in 1844. The pioneers did not invent the method; Litch had demonstrated it
  - Historian Le Roy Froom documented this sequence in _Prophetic Faith of Our Fathers_, vol. 4

- **Honesty notes on the calculation**
  - The July 27, 1299 starting date comes from Gibbon's _Decline and Fall_ chronology and was carried by Litch into Adventist tradition; modern Ottoman historians more often date the first major Byzantine defeat to the Battle of Bapheus (July 27, 1302). The period's endpoint — the 1449 submission of the last Byzantine emperor — is well-documented
  - The 360-day prophetic year is well-documented in Jewish and biblical sources (Gen 7:11 and 8:3-4 give 150 days for 5 months = 30-day months; Dan 12:7's "time, times, and half a time" = 3.5 years = 1260 days in Rev 12:14 confirms the 360-day prophetic year)
  - The August 11, 1840 diplomatic event is documented in secular sources; Litch's prediction of it is published and dated before the event
  - What this case _is_: a public apologetic test for Millerite historicism — a predicted date, published two years in advance, where a major geopolitical event answering the prophetic description occurred on the named day. Millerite audiences grew dramatically after the event. What this case _is not_: a neutral verification by secular historians of the historicist method. Historians documented the diplomatic event; interpreting that event as the sixth trumpet's fulfillment was Litch's prophetic reading

- [→] The sixth trumpet was the moment historicist prediction met public date-fixing. Litch named a date two years in advance; the Ottoman Empire's sovereignty was publicly placed under European arbitration on that day; Millerite confidence in the prophetic method grew sharply. Whatever weight one gives the reading, the sixth trumpet is the place where the historicist tradition made its clearest falsifiable claim. The same interpretive framework — the year-day principle, the Jewish prophetic year, careful anchoring of start-dates — is the framework that produces 1844 in Dan 8:14.

## The Hardness That Followed

- **Rev 9:20-21** — the men who were "not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk: Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts."
- The trumpets ended, and the men did not repent
- The list of unrepented sins — idolatry, murder, sorcery, fornication, theft — is the same list Rev 21:8 and Rev 22:15 will name as the list of the excluded at the final judgment. The sixth trumpet announces that judgment is coming; the men's response announces that hearts have not turned
- [→] The trumpets end with the stage set for the seventh — but Rev interrupts with an interlude (Rev 10-11) before the seventh trumpet sounds. That interlude is where Daniel's sealed book opens.

## Key Takeaways

- The seven trumpets are Revelation's _third_ recapitulation cycle — political-military judgments on the persecuting powers, paralleling the church's experience under seals and churches
- The prayers of the saints (Rev 8:3-5) ignite the trumpets. The fire that judges comes from the same altar where their prayers were received
- **First four trumpets** = the fall of Western Rome (AD 395-476): Visigoths/Alaric, Vandals/Genseric, Huns/Attila, Heruli/Odoacer. Held by the broad historicist tradition (Mede, Newton, Wesley, SDA pioneers). The load-bearing spine.
- The first four are "a third part" each — partial judgment, not total
- "Woe, woe, woe" (Rev 8:13) marks the last three trumpets as a different intensity — reaching the Christian East
- **Fifth trumpet** (Rev 9:1-11) = the rise of Islamic/Ottoman power (5 months = 150 years, AD 1299-1449 by the historicist calculation). The imagery — armored cavalry, scorpion tails, torment-not-kill — reads the political rise within the prophetic frame
- **Sixth trumpet** (Rev 9:13-21) = the "hour, day, month, and year" = 391 years and 15 days. Josiah Litch computed this publicly, published in 1838, named August 11, 1840 as the endpoint. On that date the Ottoman Empire's sovereignty was placed under European arbitration via the Convention of London's ultimatum. This is the historicist method's most public apologetic test case
- The sixth trumpet's public date-setting gave the Advent movement confidence in the year-day method as it moved toward 1844
- The trumpets close with men unrepentant (Rev 9:20-21) — the stage set for the seventh trumpet's announcement of the kingdom, which will come after Rev 10-11's interlude

## Next: Study 11 — The Little Book Opened (Revelation 10-11)

Between the sixth and seventh trumpets, a mighty angel descends with a _little book open_ — the book Daniel was told to seal. John is told to eat it; it is sweet in the mouth, bitter in the belly. The Advent movement's 1844 hope and disappointment is in that sentence. Two witnesses prophesy in sackcloth 1260 days, are killed in "the great city," lie three and a half days, and rise — the Bible's Reformation and the French Revolution's rejection of it. Then the seventh trumpet sounds: "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord." Study 11 is where Daniel's sealed book finally opens.
