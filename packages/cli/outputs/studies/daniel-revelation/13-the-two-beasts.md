# Study 13: The Two Beasts — Revelation 13

**The dragon stands on the sand of the sea. He calls up a beast — leopard-bodied, bear-footed, lion-mouthed, seven-headed, ten-horned — a composite of every predatory kingdom Daniel saw. One of its heads is wounded as if unto death; then the wound begins to heal. A second beast comes up out of the earth with lamb-like horns, but speaks as a dragon. It makes the first beast's recovery its project, makes an image of it, and enforces a mark. A number is given: six hundred threescore and six. Rev 13 is the chapter most people avoid — and the chapter most Reformers considered the hinge of Revelation. Historicists have argued for five centuries that the chapter's descriptors converge on a specific identification. Study 13 walks that case honestly — neither sensationalist nor evasive — distinguishing what the tradition has documented from what must be stated as interpretation.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 13 (entire chapter)
- Daniel 7:1-8, 23-25 (the four beasts and the little horn — review)
- 2 Thessalonians 2:1-12 (the man of sin)

---

## Beasts in Prophecy: What the Genre Is Telling Us

Before we identify, we need to read.

- **Dan 7:23** — "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom upon the earth" — Scripture's own interpretive key: a _beast_ in apocalyptic prophecy = a _kingdom_ (a nation, state, political-religious system)
- **Sea and earth** — "Sea" in Rev 13:1 / 17:15 = "peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." Populated, crowded regions. "Earth" in Rev 13:11 = the opposite: sparsely populated regions, frontier territory
- **Animals as symbols** — composite animals (lion, bear, leopard, dragon) signal that the prophecy is dealing in _symbolic_ creatures, not zoology. The historicist, like the apostle Daniel, reads these as codes for historical powers

With this grammar established, Rev 13 becomes decipherable. Every feature points to something historically identifiable.

## The Sea Beast — Rev 13:1-10

- **The beast's emergence** (Rev 13:1)
  - "I stood upon the sand of the sea, and saw a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns, and upon his heads the name of blasphemy"
  - _Sea_ (populated regions) = this beast rises out of the crowded Mediterranean world, specifically Europe
  - _Seven heads, ten horns_ = same configuration as the dragon of Rev 12 (12:3) and the beasts of Dan 7. The sea beast inherits what the dragon worked through
  - _Ten crowns on horns_ — the ten horns are now crowned (the dragon had crowns on his heads, v. 12:3; the beast has crowns on his horns, v. 13:1). The political successor states of Rome each wear a crown
  - _Name of blasphemy_ upon the heads — titles claiming divine prerogative

- **The composite body** (Rev 13:2)
  - "The beast... was like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a bear, and his mouth as the mouth of a lion"
  - Daniel saw four beasts in sequence — lion (Babylon), bear (Medo-Persia), leopard (Greece), dragon/fourth beast (Rome). Rev 13 combines them _in reverse_ order: leopard-body, bear-feet, lion-mouth
  - The composite indicates this beast _inherits_ everything the four empires had — Greek philosophical framework, Persian administrative structure, Babylonian religious mysticism, and Roman political-military power — concentrated into one successor entity
  - "The dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority" (Rev 13:2b) — Rome's throne is _transferred_ to this beast. The pagan Roman emperors' seat in Rome was transferred to a new occupant after 476

- **The wounded head** (Rev 13:3)
  - "I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast"

- **The beast's characteristics across verses 4-10**
  - Receives worship: "they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast... saying, Who is like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?" (v. 4) — note the parody of Michael's name ("Who is like God?")
  - "A mouth speaking great things and blasphemies" (v. 5)
  - `[⟲]` "Power was given unto him to continue forty and two months" (v. 5) = 1260 days = the same medieval-persecution period Dan 7:25, Dan 12:7, Rev 11:2-3, Rev 12:6, 14 have traced. Seven textual references across six scene-units
  - "To make war with the saints, and to overcome them" (v. 7) — the same verdict Dan 7:25 gave of the little horn
  - "Power over all kindreds, and tongues, and nations" (v. 7)
  - "All that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world" (v. 8)
  - "He that leadeth into captivity shall go into captivity: he that killeth with the sword must be killed with the sword. Here is the patience and the faith of the saints" (v. 10)

## Identifying the Sea Beast

The historicist tradition — from Wycliffe (1384) through Luther, Calvin, Knox, Cranmer, and the Westminster Confession (1646), through Isaac Newton, Wesley, Adam Clarke, and the SDA pioneers — has identified this beast with remarkable consistency. The identification was the Reformation's shared conclusion.

- **Honesty frame before the table.** What follows is the historicist identification of the sea beast as the medieval papal system. It targets the _system's_ claims (altered law, usurpation of Christ's mediation, state-enforced persecution), not the sincerity of individual Roman Catholic believers — among whom have always been devoted followers of Christ. - **The Reformation's shared witness**
  - John Wycliffe (d. 1384) repeatedly identified the pope and the papal party with Antichrist in his tracts and treatises (_De Christo et suo adversario Antichristo_ and elsewhere)
  - Martin Luther (1520): "I am convinced that the papacy is the seat of the true and real Antichrist" (letter to Spalatin, February 24, 1520; similar statements across Luther's later writings)
  - John Calvin (_Institutes_, 4.7.25): "Some persons think us too severe and censorious when we call the Roman pontiff Antichrist. But those who are of this opinion do not consider that they bring the same charge of presumption against Paul himself, after whom we speak..."
  - The Westminster Confession (1646, 25.6): "There is no other Head of the Church, but the Lord Jesus Christ; nor can the Pope of Rome in any sense be head thereof; but is that Antichrist, that man of sin, and son of perdition, that exalteth himself, in the Church, against Christ, and all that is called God"
  - Isaac Newton's posthumously-published _Observations Upon the Prophecies of Daniel and the Apocalypse of St. John_ (1733) identified the little horn of Dan 7 with the Roman papal power and used a 1260-year period, though his own start-date was approximately AD 800 rather than 538. Newton could not have named 1798 as an endpoint in 1733; the 538-1798 bracket is the later historicist/SDA consensus (Litch, Miller, Uriah Smith), building on Newton's prior identification of the power without adopting his specific dates
  - The identification is _not_ an Adventist innovation. The identification of the papal system with the Rev 13 / Dan 7 power was the mainstream Reformation reading, carried by Wycliffe, Luther, Calvin, Knox, Cranmer, Newton, Wesley, and the Adventist pioneers over more than four centuries of study — though the specific 538-1798 date-bracket is the Adventist historicist refinement

- **Honesty note**
  - The historicist identification refers to the papal _system as an institution_, not to individual Roman Catholic Christians. Devout, faithful followers of Christ have been found in every Christian communion, including the Roman Catholic Church. The prophecy targets the _system's_ claim to alter God's law, persecute dissenters, and usurp Christ's mediatorial work — not the sincerity of individual believers
  - Modern Roman Catholicism has reformed in many respects since Vatican II; the deeper structural claims (papal infallibility, Marian doctrines, the confessional system, altered Sabbath) remain. The wound is healing

- [→] The sea beast is the medieval papal power, as the Reformation identified, as Daniel foresaw, as the 1260 years confirm. Its wound was inflicted in 1798. Its recovery is the setup for the final crisis.

[DYK🔎] The specific date AD 538 lines up with documented events: Justinian's 533 decree named the Bishop of Rome "head of all the holy churches," and practical ecclesiastical supremacy in the city of Rome consolidated after Belisarius's forces held Rome through the Ostrogothic siege (537-538). The 1798 endpoint is precisely documented: Napoleon's general Louis-Alexandre Berthier entered Rome about February 10, 1798; the Roman Republic was proclaimed; Pope Pius VI was deposed and arrested on February 15, 1798; he was removed to France, and he died there in 1799. 538 + 1260 = 1798. Historicist interpreters did not invent these dates; they observed that they match.

## The Earth Beast — Rev 13:11-17

- **Emergence** (Rev 13:11)
  - "I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon"
  - _Earth_ (vs. the first beast's _sea_) = sparsely populated, frontier territory — not the crowded old-world Mediterranean
  - Rises _as the first beast's wound is being inflicted_ — the earth beast is already ascending when the sea beast falls

- **"Two horns like a lamb" — "spake as a dragon"**
  - _Lamb_ = Christ-like appearance; _two horns_ without a crown = peaceful, non-imperial authority; _speaks as a dragon_ = underlying character of the dragon (Satan) despite appearances
  - A power that _looks_ like a Christian lamb (founded on Christian principles, religious liberty, peaceful form of government) but _will speak_ coercively in the final conflict

- **What the earth beast does** (Rev 13:12-17)
  - "Exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him"
  - "Causeth the earth and them which dwell therein to worship the first beast, whose deadly wound was healed"
  - "Doeth great wonders, so that he maketh fire come down from heaven"
  - "Deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by the means of those miracles which he had power to do in the sight of the beast"
  - "Saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast, which had the wound by a sword, and did live"
  - "Had power to give life unto the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak, and cause that as many as would not worship the image of the beast should be killed"
  - "Causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads: and that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name"

- **The United States as the historicist candidate**
  - Rose from a frontier territory (North America) — "earth"
  - Became a nation around the same time the first beast received its wound (1776 Declaration of Independence; 1788 Constitution ratified; 1798 is the first beast's wound). Timing matches
  - Two founding principles: _Protestantism_ (religious liberty against established churches) and _Republicanism_ (lamb-like political authority, not monarchic/imperial) — "two horns like a lamb"
  - The prophecy describes an _end-time_ shift: "speaks as a dragon" — the same nation, under crisis pressure, turns coercive against those who do not worship the first beast
  - This turn has not yet fully occurred; the prophecy describes what _will_ be done, not what has already been done

- **The image of the beast** (Rev 13:14-15)
  - _Image_ = an imitation, a likeness; not the thing itself
  - An _image of the beast_ = a system that imitates the first beast's mode — church-state union, enforced religious uniformity, suppression of dissent
  - Historicist reading: the earth beast will, at the end, lead other nations (including itself) to adopt the first beast's church-state pattern — the very pattern America was founded _against_
  - "Cause that as many as would not worship the image of the beast should be killed" = economic and ultimately legal enforcement against those who refuse to conform

- **The mark in the right hand or forehead** (Rev 13:16-17)
  - The mark is placed where the seal of God goes (Rev 7:3 — forehead; also now including the hand)
  - The hand/forehead contrast: _forehead_ = conviction, reasoned assent; _hand_ = action, compliance without conviction
  - The mark is _imposed_ by the earth beast in contrast to the seal, which is received in the forehead only (Rev 7)
  - What _the mark is_ will be developed in Study 15. The mark is not a barcode, microchip, or vaccine. It is a coerced sign of allegiance to the first beast's system of worship — specifically, the practice of the beast's altered sabbath (Sunday) over God's appointed Sabbath (seventh-day). We will develop this full argument in Study 15

- [→] The earth beast is the predicted end-time power that will lead the world — including nations formerly built on religious liberty — to enforce the first beast's restored authority. The United States is the most natural historical candidate. The prophecy's "speaks as a dragon" is the critical clause: the beast _will speak_ coercively, not has already.

## 666 — The Number of the Beast

Rev 13:18 gives the beast a number.

- **The text** (Rev 13:18)
  - "Here is wisdom. Let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast: for it is the number of a man; and his number is Six hundred threescore and six"

- **What the verse does NOT say**
  - It does NOT say the mark is the number 666
  - It does NOT say the number is a barcode, microchip, tattoo, or credit card
  - It does NOT say every person will have a literal three-digit code

- **What the verse DOES say**
  - The number identifies the _beast_ (not the mark)
  - It is "the number of a man" — a human being, a specific individual or office, is connected to the number
  - "Count" suggests calculation, specifically _gematria_ — the ancient practice of adding the numerical values of the letters in a name

- **The historicist calculation — "Vicarius Filii Dei"**
  - "Vicar of the Son of God" — a Latin phrase that appears in the medieval forged _Donation of Constantine_ (8th-9th century) and in later canon-law references. Its documentary status as an official papal title (e.g., on coronation tiaras) is disputed, and the SDA tradition's historical claims about the phrase have been contested back and forth since the 19th century. The phrase is attested in Catholic legal and literary contexts; its weight as a dogmatic title is weaker than that of "Vicar of Christ" (which is official, affirmed in _Lumen Gentium_, 1964)
  - Roman numerals in the title: V = 5, I = 1, C = 100, A = 0, R = 0, I = 1, U = 5 (V), S = 0. "VICARIVS" = 5 + 1 + 100 + 0 + 0 + 1 + 5 + 0 = 112
  - "FILII": F = 0, I = 1, L = 50, I = 1, I = 1 = 53
  - "DEI": D = 500, E = 0, I = 1 = 501
  - Total: 112 + 53 + 501 = **666**

- **Honesty note on the calculation**
  - The Vicarius Filii Dei calculation is the SDA historicist tradition's preferred answer. The title's documentary history is real but limited; its weight as a cornerstone argument is less than the cumulative identification of the beast system
  - Other historicist traditions have proposed alternative calculations: Irenaeus (_Against Heresies_ 5.30, c. AD 180) offered "Lateinos" (Greek for "Latin man") as a probable solution — while explicitly warning against hazardous certainty because multiple names can be made to sum to 666. Some interpreters have favored the Hebrew transliteration of "Nero Caesar" (which yields 666 and may explain the manuscript variant "616")
  - The cumulative historicist case does not depend on any single gematria calculation. The descriptors of Rev 13's beast (composite body, 1260 years, wound and healing, blasphemous titles, war against saints, worldwide authority) carry the identification; the number is one element, not the foundation

- **What the number does**
  - It adds one more identifier to a beast already identified by seven other characteristics (composite body, 1260 years, blasphemous titles, war against saints, wound and healing, worldwide worship, image-making)
  - The cumulative case is the argument; the number is its final seal, not its basis

- [→] The historicist case rests on the complete pattern, not on gematria alone. The main features of Rev 13 — composite body, 1260 years, wound and healing, blasphemous titles, war against the saints, worldwide authority — point to the papal system as the first beast, and to a modern church-state alliance (with the United States as the most natural historical candidate) as the second. The Reformation reached the broad identification from different angles, across four centuries; the specific date-brackets and modern application are the Adventist refinement of that inherited reading.

## Key Takeaways

- The sea beast (Rev 13:1-10) = the medieval papal system, identified by the cumulative witness of the historicist tradition: Wycliffe, Luther, Calvin, Knox, Newton, Wesley, the SDA pioneers, and the Westminster Confession
- The core descriptors converge on this identification: composite Daniel beasts; 42 months = 1260 years (AD 538-1798, the Adventist refinement of the broader Reformation period); blasphemous titles; war against saints; wounded head (1798) now healing
- The deadly wound: Napoleon's general Berthier deposed and arrested Pope Pius VI on February 15, 1798, dissolving the papal states. Contemporary European opinion broadly regarded the papacy as a finished political power. The 1929 Lateran Treaty restored papal sovereignty (Vatican City State), and the modern papacy's global moral-religious authority are read as the wound's healing
- The identification is of the _system_, not individual Roman Catholic believers
- The earth beast (Rev 13:11-17) = the end-time power that rises as the first beast falls; lamb-like appearance (Protestant + republican), dragon-like coercion when it speaks
- The United States is the most natural historical candidate: frontier territory, founded ~1776-1798, religious liberty and republican principles
- The earth beast _will_ make an image of the first beast — a church-state alliance — and enforce a mark; this is prophetic (future), not fully occurred
- The mark (Rev 13:16-17) is placed in the right hand or forehead; the seal of God (Rev 7:3) is placed in the forehead only; contrast developed in Study 15
- 666 (Rev 13:18) = the number of a man/office; historicist calculation: VICARIVS FILII DEI = 666 in Roman numerals. The cumulative identification case does not rest on this calculation alone
- The identification is the Reformation's considered judgment, reached independently by many interpreters across five centuries

## Next: Study 14 — The Three Angels' Messages (Revelation 14)

The beasts have been identified. Rev 14 gives heaven's response. Three angels fly through the midst of heaven proclaiming: (1) the everlasting gospel and the hour of judgment; (2) "Babylon is fallen, is fallen"; (3) a warning against the mark of the beast — the most solemn warning in Scripture. The chapter closes with the Second Coming in harvest imagery: two sickles, two harvests, the wheat and the vintage. Rev 14 is where God answers the dragon's final campaign. It is also Adventism's mission statement in its clearest form.
