# Revelation 17 — Mystery Babylon the Great

**Rev 16:19 declared "great Babylon came in remembrance before God." Rev 17 zooms in. One of the seven angels of the seven vials calls John aside: "Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters." The angel carries John in spirit into the wilderness. There he sees a woman riding a scarlet beast — the beast full of names of blasphemy, with seven heads and ten horns. The woman is arrayed in purple and scarlet, gilded with gold, precious stones, and pearls, holding a golden cup full of abominations and the filthiness of her fornication. On her forehead a name: MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH. She is drunken with the blood of the saints and of the martyrs of Jesus. John "wondered with great admiration." The angel then unfolds the mystery — seven heads = seven mountains and seven kings; the beast that "was, and is not, and yet is"; the ten horns that have not yet received kingdoms but will give their power to the beast for "one hour"; the war they make with the Lamb and the Lamb's victory; the waters as peoples; and the climactic moment when the ten horns turn on the whore and burn her with fire. Rev 17 is Revelation's open identification of Babylon — the system named, the symbols decoded, the destruction foretold.**

## Before the Verses — Where Rev 17 Sits

Rev 16 ended with Babylon "remembered before God" and given the cup of the wine of the fierceness of His wrath. **Rev 17 is the close-up.** The seventh-vial verdict ("the great city was divided into three parts") is now expanded in two chapters: Rev 17 unmasks Babylon's identity; Rev 18 describes her economic-religious collapse and heaven's call to come out of her. Rev 19 then closes the loop with the Lamb's wedding feast and the white-horse Rider. **Rev 17-18 are the answer to the second angel's "Babylon is fallen" announcement of Rev 14:8.**

- **The angel-introduction is a structural marker.** "There came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials" (Rev 17:1) is matched in Rev 21:9 — "There came unto me one of the seven angels which had the seven vials." **The same angel introduces both the harlot and the bride.** Two women; two cities; two destinies. The chapter is half of a deliberately matched pair.
- **The setting is the wilderness.** Rev 17:3 — "he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness." Compare Rev 12:6 — the woman (true church) flees _into the wilderness_ for 1260 days. **Rev 17's harlot is shown in the wilderness because the wilderness is where the true woman has been hidden** — and the harlot has set up the global counterfeit system in plain sight.
- **The chapter is essentially an angelic exposition.** John sees the vision (vv. 1-6); then the angel explains the symbols (vv. 7-18). **This is unusual in Revelation** — most visions are not directly decoded. Rev 17 is decoded because the identification is critical. Babylon must not be missed.
- **Babylon and the Lamb are headed to one battle.** Verse 14 — "These shall make war with the Lamb, and the Lamb shall overcome them." **The chapter is not just identification; it is courtroom and battlefield.** The whore's destruction is sure: by the ten horns first (v. 16), and then by the Lamb (v. 14, executed in Rev 19:11-21).

[KEY🔑] **Rev 17 is Revelation's open identification of Babylon.** No more parables. No more sealed mysteries. The angel literally says "I will tell thee the mystery of the woman" (v. 7). The woman is "that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth" (v. 18); she sits on seven mountains (v. 9); she rides the same scarlet beast that has the seven heads and ten horns of Rev 12-13; she is gilded with imperial purple and ecclesiastical scarlet; her cup is full of "abominations and filthiness of her fornication"; her forehead bears the name MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS; she is drunken with the blood of the saints and the martyrs of Jesus. **In John's day there was only one city built on seven hills that reigned over the kings of the earth: Rome.** In the post-Constantinian centuries that imperial seat became the seat of an ecclesiastical power that took the same title and the same throne. **The Reformers — Wycliffe, Tyndale, Luther, Calvin, Knox, the Westminster divines, Newton, Wesley — were unanimous that Rev 17 names papal Rome.** Rev 17:5's "mother of harlots" implies daughters: the system extends beyond a single church to every religious power that has adopted her pattern. **Rev 17 names the system; Rev 18:4 calls God's people out of it.**

## Rev 17:1-2 — Come See the Judgment of the Great Whore

> "And there came one of the seven angels which had the seven vials, and talked with me, saying unto me, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters: With whom the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication." (Rev 17:1-2)

- "**One of the seven angels which had the seven vials**" — Greek _heis ek tōn hepta angelōn tōn echontōn tas hepta phialas_. **The angel of the plague-judgment is also the angel of the harlot-exposition.** The chapter that exposes Babylon is given by the same angels who poured the wrath that brought her down. **Rev 21:9 will use the identical formula to introduce the bride** — the same angel; the matched pair; two women, two destinies.
- "**The great whore**" — Greek _tēs pornēs tēs megalēs_. **Whoredom / harlotry / _porneia_ is the OT's standard image for unfaithfulness in covenant** (Hos 2:1-13; Jer 3:8-9; Eze 16; Eze 23). **A harlot is not a pagan power — she is an unfaithful covenant-people-turned-system.** Babylon is presented as religious infidelity dressed up as religion.
- "**That sitteth upon many waters**" — Greek _tēs kathēmenēs epi hydatōn pollōn_. **Verse 15 will decode the symbol: "the waters... are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues."** The harlot is universal; she rides the global multitude. **Compare Jer 51:13** — "O thou that dwellest upon many waters, abundant in treasures, thine end is come" — the OT prophecy spoken to Babylon-on-the-Euphrates is the linguistic source for Rev 17:1.
- "**The kings of the earth have committed fornication**" — Greek _emporneusan_. **Political-religious adultery.** When the kings make alliances of state with the harlot's religion, both parties have committed _porneia_. **The OT pattern: Israel's kings and the high places** (1 Kgs 11; Eze 16:25-29).
- "**The inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication**" — the people are not innocent victims. **The wine of false doctrine and ecstatic submission has stupefied them.** Compare Jer 51:7 — "Babylon hath been a golden cup in the LORD'S hand, that made all the earth drunken: the nations have drunken of her wine; therefore the nations are mad."

## Rev 17:3 — A Woman on a Scarlet Beast in the Wilderness

> "So he carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness: and I saw a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns." (Rev 17:3)

- "**Carried me away in the spirit into the wilderness**" — Greek _en pneumati eis erēmon_. **John is taken in vision to the wilderness** — the place where, in Rev 12:6, the true woman (the faithful church) had fled for 1260 days of hiding. **The harlot is shown in the wilderness deliberately:** the place that hid the persecuted church is where the persecuting system is now exposed.
- "**A woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast**" — Greek _gynaika kathēmenēn epi thērion kokkinon_. **The woman _rides_ the beast.** The relationship is parasitic-symbiotic: a religious power riding a political beast it controls but does not _become_. **The same beast as Rev 13:1's sea-beast — seven heads, ten horns** — but here _scarlet_ rather than rising from the sea, indicating the persecuting / blood-stained nature of the political vehicle.
- "**Full of names of blasphemy**" — Greek _gemonta onomata blasphēmias_. **Names, plural.** Rev 13:1's sea-beast had _names_ of blasphemy (KJV) on its heads; Rev 17:3's beast has them on its body. **The blasphemous titles are inscribed all over the system:** vicar-of-Christ-claims, lord-of-heaven-and-earth claims, infallibility claims, every counterfeit-of-Christ name the system has assumed.
- **Seven heads, ten horns.** Same imagery as Rev 13:1 (sea-beast); same as Rev 12:3 (red dragon — but the dragon's diadems were on the heads, while Rev 13:1's beast has them on the horns and Rev 17:3's beast has none mentioned). **The scarlet beast is the political instrument the harlot rides** — the same continuum from Dan 7's four beasts through the dragon's pagan Rome through the sea-beast's papal Rome.

## Rev 17:4-5 — Purple and Scarlet, Gilded, Cup of Abominations, Forehead Name

> "And the woman was arrayed in purple and scarlet colour, and decked with gold and precious stones and pearls, having a golden cup in her hand full of abominations and filthiness of her fornication: And upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH." (Rev 17:4-5)

- "**Arrayed in purple and scarlet colour**" — Greek _peribeblēmenē porphyroun kai kokkinon_. **Purple = imperial sovereignty.** Scarlet = ecclesiastical vesture in the medieval-Roman system. **The woman wears both — political authority and religious office, fused.** Compare the priestly garments of the OT high priest, Ex 28 (blue, purple, and scarlet) — the sacred colors counterfeited.
- "**Decked with gold and precious stones and pearls**" — same materials as the priestly breastplate (Ex 28:17-20) and the New Jerusalem's foundations (Rev 21:19-21). **The harlot wears the bride's jewelry.** The counterfeit looks externally like the genuine.
- "**A golden cup in her hand full of abominations and filthiness of her fornication**" — Greek _potērion chrysoun gemon bdelygmatōn kai ta akatharta tēs porneias autēs_. **The same _bdelygmata_** ("abominations") **the LXX uses at Eze 8:10** for the creeping things and idols on the inner walls of the temple. **Babylon's cup is the same anatomy as Eze 8's defiled sanctuary** — pagan content carried in golden form. Compare Jer 51:7 — "Babylon hath been a golden cup in the LORD'S hand."
- "**Upon her forehead was a name written**" — Greek _epi to metōpon autēs onoma gegrammenon_. **Forehead = the seat of choice and conviction** (Rev 13:16's mark; Rev 14:1's seal; Rev 22:4's name). **The harlot bears her identity openly** — she is not in disguise to those who can read.
- "**MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT**" — _mystērion_ in NT usage = a divine truth that was hidden but is now revealed (Eph 3:3-6, Col 1:26-27). **The harlot's "mystery" is the inverse: a long-concealed religious-political fusion now openly named.** "Babylon the great" is the OT's archetypal city of self-deification (Gen 11; Dan 4:30) carried forward as a transferable system.
- "**THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH**" — _hē mētēr tōn pornōn_. **A mother implies daughters.** The end-time system is not a single denomination; the mother is the dominant counterfeit-religious power, and the daughters are the religious entities that have absorbed her pattern (post-Reformation drift back into Rome's framework — sacramentalism, ecclesiastical hierarchy displacing biblical authority, state-church alliance, Sunday worship inherited from her, the "abominations" of v. 4 multiplied through ecumenical compromise).

[CTRF] **The harlot of Rev 17 vs. the bride of Rev 21 — the tenth and tightest counterfeit pair.** The same angel introduces both: "one of the seven angels which had the seven vials" (Rev 17:1; Rev 21:9). Both women are richly adorned: Rev 17:4 (gold, precious stones, pearls) // Rev 21:18-21 (gold, precious stones, pearls). Both are identified by what is on the forehead: Rev 17:5 (MYSTERY, BABYLON) // Rev 22:4 ("his name shall be in their foreheads"). Both are global: the harlot sits on "many waters... peoples, multitudes, nations" (Rev 17:1, 15); the New Jerusalem's gates open to "the nations of them which are saved" (Rev 21:24). The harlot rides a beast (Rev 17:3); the bride is the Lamb's (Rev 21:9). The harlot is drunken with the blood of saints (Rev 17:6); the bride has had every tear wiped away (Rev 21:4). **The counterfeit is structurally precise: the harlot is what Babylon makes of a woman; the bride is what Christ makes of a city.** Rev 17 and Rev 21 are not two separate visions; they are the two halves of one diptych.

## Rev 17:6 — Drunken with the Blood of the Saints

> "And I saw the woman drunken with the blood of the saints, and with the blood of the martyrs of Jesus: and when I saw her, I wondered with great admiration." (Rev 17:6)

- "**Drunken with the blood of the saints**" — Greek _methyousan ek tou haimatos tōn hagiōn_. **The OT pattern: shedding blood as drinking it** (Ps 79:3; Eze 39:18). **The intoxicating substance of v. 2 was the wine of fornication; the intoxicating substance of v. 6 is the saints' blood.** The system is so used to martyr-killing that it has become drunk on the practice.
- "**The blood of the martyrs of Jesus**" — Greek _ek tou haimatos tōn martyrōn Iēsou_. **A second category** added to the saints — not a synonym. **The martyrs of Jesus** are those killed specifically for testimony to Christ. Compare Rev 12:17 (the remnant having "the testimony of Jesus"); Rev 6:9 (souls under the altar "for the testimony which they held"); Rev 1:9 / 19:10 (testimony of Jesus). **The 1260-year medieval persecution era (AD 538-1798) and beyond** — Albigenses, Waldenses, Lollards, Hussites, Reformers, Huguenots — saw enough martyred blood to drink the system drunk.
- "**I wondered with great admiration**" — Greek _ethaumasa thauma mega_. **John marvels with great marvel** — astonishment, not approval. **The same _thaumazō_ root used in Rev 13:3** for the world wondering after the beast, and the same _thaumaston_ family used in Rev 15:1 for the great-and-marvellous sign. **The harlot's appearance is staggering to John precisely because of the contradiction** — the church (a woman) drunk on the blood of the saints. The verb chosen is the verb of structural recognition, not affirmation.

[⟲] **The Babylon thread runs the whole canon and reaches its exposure-moment in Rev 17.** Gen 11:1-9 (the tower of Babel — the original self-deifying city). Dan 1-2 (Nebuchadnezzar's literal Babylon, the metallic-image-head). Dan 4 (Nebuchadnezzar humbled — "Babylon that I have built"). Dan 5 (Belshazzar's fall — historical Babylon dies). Isa 13-14, 47 (Babylon's prophesied judgment). Jer 50-51 (Babylon's destruction by Cyrus — "Babylon hath been a golden cup"). Rev 14:8 (the second angel: "Babylon is fallen, is fallen"). Rev 16:19 ("great Babylon came in remembrance before God"). **Rev 17 — the woman is named openly: MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT.** Rev 18 (her actual fall, "Come out of her, my people"). Rev 19:1-3 (heaven's hallelujah at her destruction). **The thread that began at Babel and ran through literal Babylon and crystallized as papal Rome reaches its full unmasking here.**

## Rev 17:7-8 — The Beast That Was, And Is Not

> "And the angel said unto me, Wherefore didst thou marvel? I will tell thee the mystery of the woman, and of the beast that carrieth her, which hath the seven heads and ten horns. The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition: and they that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is." (Rev 17:7-8)

- "**The beast that thou sawest was, and is not; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition**" — Greek _to thērion ho eides ēn kai ouk estin kai mellei anabainein ek tēs abyssou kai eis apōleian hypagei_. **A three-phase pattern: was → is not → ascends again.** The political beast that carried the harlot has had a historical existence (was), suffered an interruption (is not), and will rise from the abyss for a final outing (shall ascend) before going into perdition. **The historicist reading:** pagan-imperial Rome (was) → the deadly wound of Rev 13:3 at 1798 / breakup of imperial-style political structure (is not) → end-time resurgent beast-coalition that the harlot rides into the final crisis (yet is).
- "**Out of the bottomless pit**" — Greek _ek tēs abyssou_. **Same _abyssos_ as Rev 9:1-2 (locust-power), Rev 11:7 (the beast that ascends out of the bottomless pit and makes war on the two witnesses), and Rev 20:1-3 (Satan bound).** The beast's final form is openly demonic — it ascends from the abyss, not from the sea. **The fig-leaf of legitimate political authority is gone.**
- "**They that dwell on the earth shall wonder, whose names were not written in the book of life**" — Greek _thaumasthēsontai_, future passive. **The earth-dwellers will be amazed** — same root _thaumazō_ as Rev 13:3 ("all the world wondered after the beast"). **The unsealed are dazzled by the beast's resurgence;** the sealed are not. **The Lamb's book of life predates the foundation of the world** (cf. Rev 13:8) — the names of those who refuse to wonder were written from before creation.

[Q] **Who is the beast that "was, and is not, and yet is"?** Verse 11 calls it "the eighth, and is of the seven." The seven heads (v. 9) are "seven mountains" + "seven kings" (v. 10), of which "five are fallen, and one is, the other is not yet come." The historicist reading from John's vantage — Rev was given AD 95 — has been: five fallen world-empires (Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece), the one that "is" (imperial Rome), the one "not yet come" (post-Roman fragmented Europe under papal direction), and the eighth (the resurgent end-time beast that is "of the seven" because it is the same anti-God power in its final form, riding the harlot). Other historicist interpreters take the seven kings as the seven major heads of the persecuting power across history. **Rev 17:9-11 is one of Revelation's most-debated passages, and historicists have differed on the specific seven-king identification.** What is not debated within historicism: the woman is Rome (v. 18 — "that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth"); the beast is the political-imperial vehicle she rides; the eighth is the end-time resurgent form that goes into perdition at the Second Coming.

## Rev 17:9-11 — Seven Mountains, Seven Kings, an Eighth

> "And here is the mind which hath wisdom. The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth. And there are seven kings: five are fallen, and one is, and the other is not yet come; and when he cometh, he must continue a short space. And the beast that was, and is not, even he is the eighth, and is of the seven, and goeth into perdition." (Rev 17:9-11)

- "**Here is the mind which hath wisdom**" — Greek _hōde ho nous ho echōn sophian_. **Same construction as Rev 13:18** ("here is wisdom; let him that hath understanding count the number of the beast"). **Both interpretive markers stand on the same chapter-pair** — Rev 13 names the number (666); Rev 17 names the place (seven mountains). **Both are designed to make the identification unmistakable.**
- "**The seven heads are seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth**" — Greek _hepta orē eisin hopou hē gynē kathētai ep' autōn_. **In John's day, the city built on seven hills was Rome** (the _septem montes_ of Roman classical literature: Aventine, Caelian, Capitoline, Esquiline, Palatine, Quirinal, Viminal — a fact universally known to the Roman world). **No other ancient city claimed the title.** Combined with v. 18 ("that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth"), the identification is settled. **The Reformers — Wycliffe, Tyndale, Luther, Calvin, Knox, Newton, Wesley, the Westminster divines — were unanimous: Rev 17 names papal Rome.**
- "**And there are seven kings**" — the heads serve double duty. They are seven mountains (geographic / political seat) AND seven kings (a historical sequence). **The dual symbolism is built into the apocalyptic language.**
- "**Five are fallen, and one is, the other is not yet come**" — from John's vantage (AD 95), the historicist tradition has read these as world-empires that have persecuted God's people: **Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece** (the five fallen), **imperial Rome** (the one that "is" in John's day), and **the post-Roman / papal political phase** as the one "not yet come." **The "short space" of the seventh** corresponds to the final phase before the end-time resurgence.
- "**The beast... is the eighth, and is of the seven**" — Greek _kai autos ogdoos estin kai ek tōn hepta estin_. **The eighth is a mode, not a separate empire.** The end-time beast that the harlot rides is "of the seven" because it is the same anti-God power in its final form — gathering the legacies of Egypt's defiance, Babylon's idolatry, Greece's philosophy, and Rome's persecution into one resurgent end-time coalition.
- "**Goeth into perdition**" — Greek _eis apōleian hypagei_. **Same end as the Rev 13 beast and the Rev 19:20 beast.** The destination is fixed; the only question is who rides into perdition with him.

## Rev 17:12-14 — Ten Horns, One Hour, War with the Lamb

> "And the ten horns which thou sawest are ten kings, which have received no kingdom as yet; but receive power as kings one hour with the beast. These have one mind, and shall give their power and strength unto the beast. These shall make war with the Lamb, and the Lamb shall overcome them: for he is Lord of lords, and King of kings: and they that are with him are called, and chosen, and faithful." (Rev 17:12-14)

- "**The ten horns... are ten kings, which have received no kingdom as yet**" — same imagery as Dan 7:24 (the ten horns of the fourth beast = ten kings out of fragmented imperial Rome). **In the Rev 17 frame, however, the ten horns are an end-time alliance** — "have received no kingdom as yet." **They give their power to the beast for "one hour"** — an end-time political coalition that hands its sovereignty to the resurgent beast for a brief, intense moment.
- "**One hour**" — Greek _mian hōran_. **A short, climactic moment.** Same phrase as Rev 18:10, 17, 19 ("in one hour is thy judgment come... in one hour so great riches is come to nought... in one hour is she made desolate"). **The political coalition that empowers the beast and the system that collapses inhabit the same hour-frame.**
- "**These have one mind**" — Greek _mian gnōmēn echousin_. **Total ideological unity.** The end-time alliance is not a coalition of competing interests but a single-minded global power-bloc.
- "**Shall make war with the Lamb**" — same war as Rev 16:14 (Armageddon). **Rev 17:14 names the combatants** — beast + ten horns + dragon-coalition vs. the Lamb. Rev 19:11-21 will execute the engagement.
- "**The Lamb shall overcome them**" — Greek _to arnion nikēsei autous_. **The book's verdict.** The Lamb that was slain (Rev 5:6) is the Lamb that overcomes (Rev 17:14). **The strength of the verdict is the cross.** The worthy-to-open-the-scroll-because-slain Lamb is the same Lamb who now decides the final conflict.
- "**Lord of lords, and King of kings**" — Greek _kyrios kyriōn estin kai basileus basileōn_. **Same titles ascribed to Christ in Rev 19:16** ("KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS" written on His thigh and vesture). **The titles named in Rev 17:14 are written on the Rider in Rev 19:16** — the chapter that names them is the chapter that announces the Rider who carries them.
- "**They that are with him are called, and chosen, and faithful**" — Greek _klētoi kai eklektoi kai pistoi_. **A three-fold description of the Lamb's army.** Called = invited; chosen = elected; faithful = enduring. **Compare Rev 14:4** ("they... follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth"). The 144,000 / sealed remnant are the Lamb's company in the war.

## Rev 17:15 — Waters Are Peoples

> "And he saith unto me, The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues." (Rev 17:15)

- "**The waters... are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues**" — Greek _laoi kai ochloi eisin kai ethnē kai glōssai_. **An angelic decoder for one of Revelation's most important symbols.** Wherever "waters" appears in apocalyptic context, this is the lexicon: peoples-multitudes-nations-tongues — the four-fold formula of universal humanity (cf. Rev 5:9; 7:9; 13:7; 14:6).
- **This decoder unlocks Rev 16:12.** The drying-up of the Euphrates in the sixth vial is not a literal river running dry; it is the withdrawal of Babylon's popular support. **The harlot rides the global multitude until the multitude pulls out.** The decoder also unlocks Rev 17:1 ("sitteth on many waters") — the harlot's seat is global humanity itself.
- **The waters' withdrawal is the prerequisite for v. 16's destruction.** First the multitude pulls out (Rev 16:12); then the ten horns turn on her (Rev 17:16). Babylon falls when her base evaporates and her allies turn.

## Rev 17:16-17 — The Ten Horns Burn the Whore

> "And the ten horns which thou sawest upon the beast, these shall hate the whore, and shall make her desolate and naked, and shall eat her flesh, and burn her with fire. For God hath put in their hearts to fulfil his will, and to agree, and give their kingdom unto the beast, until the words of God shall be fulfilled." (Rev 17:16-17)

- "**These shall hate the whore**" — Greek _outoi misēsousin tēn pornēn_. **The political coalition that supported her turns on her.** A self-destructive collapse: the ten horns that gave the beast their power (v. 13) become the agents of the harlot's destruction. **History shows this pattern in miniature:** Henry VIII broke from Rome; the French Revolution turned on the Roman church; Napoleon imprisoned the pope. **Rev 17:16 is the eschatological version** — the political bloc that rode the harlot ultimately cannot survive its own contradictions and turns its violence on her.
- "**Shall make her desolate and naked, and shall eat her flesh, and burn her with fire**" — Greek _erēmōmenēn kai gymnēn... katakausousin en pyri_. **Five terms of total destruction.** Compare Lev 21:9 (the priest's harlot daughter — "she shall be burnt with fire") and Eze 16:39-41 (Jerusalem-as-harlot stripped, stoned, burnt). **The OT-pattern judgment for a harlot under covenant has come on Babylon.**
- "**God hath put in their hearts to fulfil his will**" — Greek _ho theos edōken eis tas kardias autōn poiēsai tēn gnōmēn autou_. **The same God who let the kings unite under the beast hardens the hands that destroy her.** Compare Pharaoh's hardened heart (Ex 9:12; 10:1) — God superintends the political will of the rebellious to bring His judgment to pass. **The horns think they are acting independently; God is turning their independence into the instrument of His justice.**
- "**Until the words of God shall be fulfilled**" — Greek _achri telesthēsontai hoi logoi tou theou_. **The horns' violence is bracketed by God's word.** The end-time political coalition serves a divine timeline whether they know it or not.

## Rev 17:18 — The Woman Is That Great City

> "And the woman which thou sawest is that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth." (Rev 17:18)

- "**That great city**" — Greek _hē polis hē megalē_. **Same designation as Rev 11:8 (Sodom and Egypt, where also our Lord was crucified — the persecuting world-system at the cross); Rev 14:8; Rev 16:19; Rev 18:10, 16, 18, 19, 21.** From Rev 14 onward, "the great city" becomes Revelation's consistent designation for Babylon (Rev 11:8's earlier symbolic use names the same persecuting principle in its first-century Jerusalem-Rome manifestation).
- "**Which reigneth over the kings of the earth**" — Greek _hē echousa basileian epi tōn basileōn tēs gēs_. **Present-tense reign at the time of writing.** **In John's day there was only one city built on seven hills that reigned over the kings of the earth: Rome.** The angel's identification is concrete and dated: at the moment Revelation was given (c. AD 95), the woman is Rome. The post-Constantinian transition then transferred the same throne, the same seven hills, and the same global authority from emperor to pontiff. **The continuity is geographical, institutional, and titular** — the title _Pontifex Maximus_ itself was inherited from the pagan Roman religious office.

## After the Verses — What Rev 17 Settles

- **Babylon is named.** Not generic evil; not a vague religious-political composite; but a specific identifiable system: a religious power riding a political-imperial beast, headquartered in the city of seven hills, drunken with the blood of the saints across centuries, ruling over the kings of the earth at the time of writing.
- **The "mystery" is precisely that Rev 17 unveils it.** _Mystērion_ in Pauline usage = the once-hidden truth now revealed. The harlot's mystery is no longer hidden; she is shown for what she is. **Rev 17 is the chapter that takes the cover off.**
- **The Reformation read this chapter unanimously.** Wycliffe, Tyndale, Luther, Calvin, Knox, the Westminster divines, Newton, Wesley — every Reformer and post-Reformation orthodox commentator identified the harlot as papal Rome. **The historicist reading is not a marginal SDA construction; it is the inheritance of the Reformation.** Modern futurist readings that postpone Rev 17 to a future literal-Iraq Babylon are a 19th-century rollback to pre-Reformation hermeneutics.
- **The mother has daughters.** Rev 17:5's _mētēr tōn pornōn_ implies a multi-entity system. Apostate Protestantism, having drifted back into the mother's framework on sacramentalism, hierarchy, state-alliance, and Sunday-keeping, is the fellow-harlot in the end-time crisis. The third angel's warning (Rev 14:9-11) goes to all of them.
- **The waters' withdrawal precedes the destruction.** Rev 16:12 (sixth vial) dries up the harlot's popular support; Rev 17:16 lets the ten horns burn her. **The sequence is: Spirit-withdrawal first (by the close of probation in Rev 15:8), then popular withdrawal (Rev 16:12 / Rev 17:15), then political destruction (Rev 17:16), then eschatological consummation (Rev 19:11-21).**
- **The Lamb wins.** Verse 14 — "the Lamb shall overcome them" — is the chapter's verdict. The strength of the verdict is the cross. The Lamb that was slain (Rev 5:6, 5:9) is the Lamb that overcomes (Rev 17:14). **No other power in the chapter speaks the final word.**

[→] **Cross-anchor.** Rev 17 (Babylon's identity) opens out into Rev 18 (Babylon's fall and "Come out of her, my people") and Rev 19 (the white-horse Rider's verdict). Hold the three chapters together; do not isolate Rev 17.

## Key Takeaways

- Rev 17 is Revelation's open identification of Babylon — the same angel who poured the seven vials now decodes the symbols
- The woman = a religious power; the beast she rides = the political-imperial vehicle. Religious-political fusion, not religion alone or politics alone
- Purple (imperial) + scarlet (ecclesiastical) on the same woman — political and religious authority fused
- Forehead name MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS — the mother implies daughters, a multi-entity end-time system
- "Drunken with the blood of the saints and martyrs of Jesus" — the system has martyred its critics across the 1260-year medieval era and beyond
- Seven heads = seven mountains (Rome, the city of seven hills) + seven kings (a historical sequence of persecuting empires)
- The eighth beast is "of the seven" — the end-time resurgent form that gathers the legacies of all prior persecuting empires
- Ten horns = end-time political alliance, "one mind," giving power to the beast for "one hour"
- "Waters are peoples, multitudes, nations, tongues" (v. 15) — the angelic decoder for one of Revelation's key symbols, also unlocks Rev 16:12's Euphrates
- The ten horns turn on the whore (v. 16) — the political coalition that supported her becomes the instrument of her destruction
- The woman is "that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth" (v. 18) — concrete identification as Rome at the time of writing
- The Reformation read this chapter unanimously as identifying papal Rome; modern futurist readings are a 19th-century rollback
- "The Lamb shall overcome them" (v. 14) — the cross is the strength of the chapter's verdict

## Next

Rev 18 follows immediately. Another mighty angel comes down from heaven, his glory lighting up the earth, and cries with a strong voice: "Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit." A second voice from heaven calls: "Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues." The chapter then describes Babylon's economic-religious collapse — kings, merchants, sailors weeping over her fall in three "alas, alas" laments — and heaven's command: "Reward her even as she rewarded you." Rev 18 is the call to leave the system and watch heaven settle her account.
