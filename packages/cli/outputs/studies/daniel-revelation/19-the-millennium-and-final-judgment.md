# Study 19: The Millennium and the Final Judgment — Revelation 20

**The wicked are slain by the brightness of Christ's coming. The righteous are caught up to meet Him in the air. The earth is emptied. And then — Rev 20 — an angel comes down with a great chain and the key of the bottomless pit. Satan, defeated at Armageddon, is bound "that he should deceive the nations no more: till the thousand years should be fulfilled." For a thousand years the saints are in heaven, reigning with Christ, and the judgment is given unto them. On earth, Satan and his angels walk a desolate planet that has become their prison. Then the thousand years end. The wicked are raised. Satan is loosed. The New Jerusalem descends, and the armies of the unrighteous surround the beloved city. Fire comes down from God. The second death is executed; death and hell are cast into the lake of fire. Study 19 is the end of sin's history — the final and complete cleansing of the universe.**

## Prep Reading

- Revelation 20 (entire chapter)
- Jeremiah 4:23-26 (the desolate earth)
- 1 Corinthians 6:2-3 (the saints judging angels)
- Malachi 4:1-3 (the wicked as ashes)

---

## Satan Bound

Rev 20 opens with the capture of the dragon.

- **The scene** (Rev 20:1-3)
  - "I saw an angel come down from heaven, having the key of the bottomless pit and a great chain in his hand"
  - "He laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years"
  - "And cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him, that he should deceive the nations no more, till the thousand years should be fulfilled"
  - "After that he must be loosed a little season"

- **The "chain" is symbolic**
  - Satan is a spirit (Eph 6:12) — no literal chain could bind him
  - The chain = a chain of _circumstances_. Satan is bound _by the circumstances_ that leave him with no one to deceive:
    - The wicked are all dead (killed at the Second Coming — Rev 19:21)
    - The righteous are all in heaven with Christ (raptured — 1 Thess 4:16-17)
    - The earth is emptied of living humans
    - With no population, Satan's deceptive activity has no audience; his work is _bound_ by the simple absence of anyone to deceive
  - Eze 28:12-19 traced Satan's downward arc from his first fall to his final extinction; the millennium is the period when he has nothing left to do but contemplate his history

- **"Bottomless pit"** (_abyssos_)
  - In the LXX, _abyssos_ corresponds to "the deep" of Gen 1:2 — the primordial waters over which God's Spirit moved before creation was formed
  - In Rev 11:7 and 17:8, the _abyssos_ was the source of the beast's power — the disordered realm from which evil emerges
  - Now Satan is locked inside it. The earth, reduced to chaos by God's Second Coming judgments (Jer 4:23-26's "without form, and void," echoing the pre-creation state of Gen 1:2), becomes his prison
  - No demons among the raised righteous; no humans among the fallen; Satan walks a dead planet alone with his angels for a thousand years

- [→] The millennium is not a golden age on earth. It is the exact opposite: a thousand-year period when Satan is cut off from his work and the earth lies in desolation, awaiting the final events. Evil is contained, but not yet destroyed. That destruction will come at the end of the thousand years.

## The Saints Reign

The scene shifts to heaven.

- **The saints on thrones** (Rev 20:4)
  - "I saw thrones, and they sat upon them, and judgment was given unto them"
  - "The souls of them that were beheaded for the witness of Jesus, and for the word of God, and which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image, neither had received his mark upon their foreheads, or in their hands"
  - "They lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years"

- **Where are the saints?**
  - John 14:1-3 — "I go to prepare a place for you. And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again, and receive you unto myself; that where I am, there ye may be also"
  - Christ's promise: He returns to take His people _where He is_ (heaven), not to where they are (earth)
  - 1 Thess 4:16-17 — caught up "together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord"
  - Rev 20:4's thrones are in heaven (the same throne room John has been seeing since Rev 4), not a restored earthly Jerusalem

- **"Judgment was given unto them"** (v. 4)
  - 1 Cor 6:2-3 — "Do ye not know that the saints shall judge the world? ... Know ye not that we shall judge angels?"
  - The saints participate in judgment, reviewing the records during the thousand years
  - _Why this judgment?_ Not to _decide_ who is saved — that was decided in the pre-Advent investigative judgment that began in 1844 and was executed at the Second Coming. Rather, the millennial judgment has a different purpose:
    - The saints see every record they might have questions about. "Why isn't my friend here? My relative? Person X whom I admired?"
    - Every case is reviewed, explained, understood
    - The saints emerge with every question about God's justice answered and God's character fully vindicated
    - When the wicked are raised and the lake of fire executed, there is no objection, no confusion, no lingering "but what about..." in the saints' hearts

- **First and second resurrection** (Rev 20:4-6)
  - "This is the first resurrection" (v. 5) — the righteous, raised at the Second Coming (1 Thess 4:16)
  - "The rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" (v. 5) — the wicked wait
  - "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death hath no power" (v. 6)

- [→] The millennium is a thousand years in which the saints, with Christ, understand everything — the whole history of sin, of the cross, of the cosmic trial. By the end, every question is answered. When the wicked are finally judged and destroyed, the universe can say with one voice: "just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints" (Rev 15:3)

## The Earth as Satan's Prison

Rev 20's bottomless pit is not a subterranean chamber. It is the earth itself, returned to chaos.

- **The pattern from the OT**
  - Jer 4:23-26 — "I beheld the earth, and, lo, it was without form, and void; and the heavens, and they had no light. I beheld the mountains, and, lo, they trembled, and all the hills moved lightly... I beheld, and, lo, there was no man, and all the birds of the heavens were fled. I beheld, and, lo, the fruitful place was a wilderness"
  - Immediate context: Judah's judgment; prophetically echoed in the post-Second-Coming desolation of the globe
  - Isa 24:1-12 — "Behold, the LORD maketh the earth empty, and maketh it waste, and turneth it upside down"
  - Isa 24:21-22 — "in that day... the high ones that are on high... shall be gathered together, as prisoners are gathered in the pit, and shall be shut up in the prison, and after many days shall they be visited" — a clear OT precedent for Rev 20's binding and final visitation

- **What the earth looks like during the millennium**
  - Cities destroyed (Rev 16:19)
  - Mountains not found, every island moved (Rev 16:20)
  - Every living person dead (Rev 19:21)
  - The heavens rolled up (Rev 6:14)
  - Satan walks this desolate wasteland with his angels for a thousand years, contemplating what he has done — and what he has lost (2 Pet 3:10's "earth also and the works that are therein shall be burned up" fits the _final_ fire at the millennium's end, when the cleansing flame consumes both the wicked and the old earth itself before Rev 21's new earth)

- [→] The millennium is not a lull between innings. It is judgment in process — Satan confronted, for a thousand years, with the full weight of his work

## The End of the Thousand Years

After a thousand years, the scene shifts again.

- **Satan loosed** (Rev 20:7)
  - "When the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be loosed out of his prison"
  - Why loosed? Not to give him a second chance. To make the final result of his character inescapably visible

- **The second resurrection** (Rev 20:5, 13)
  - "The rest of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" (v. 5)
  - "The sea gave up the dead which were in it; and death and hell delivered up the dead which were in them" (v. 13)
  - Every wicked person who ever lived is raised — from Cain onward. All together, all at once, at the millennium's end

- **The holy city descends** (Rev 21:2, in chronological sequence)
  - Though narrated later in Rev 21-22, the New Jerusalem descends to earth chronologically at the close of the millennium, in connection with the second resurrection and the final judgment
  - The saints are inside the city with Christ; the wicked are outside; the final confrontation unfolds

- **Gog and Magog — the final rebellion** (Rev 20:8-9)
  - "Satan shall be loosed out of his prison, And shall go out to deceive the nations which are in the four quarters of the earth, Gog and Magog, to gather them together to battle: the number of whom is as the sand of the sea"
  - _Gog and Magog_ (Eze 38-39) — the final coalition of nations against God's people
  - With the wicked resurrected and unreformed, Satan rallies them one last time for an assault on the holy city
  - The whole mass of unsaved humanity, led by Satan, surrounds the city
  - This fits the final universal acknowledgment of Phil 2:10-11: "at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of things in heaven, and things in earth, and things under the earth; And that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father"
  - Even Satan and the wicked, beholding the holy city and the judgment records, acknowledge God's justice. Not in repentance (too late) but in recognition — the final silence of every charge made against God throughout history

- **Fire from God** (Rev 20:9)
  - "Fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them"
  - Not an eternal-torment fire; a _devouring_ fire that executes the second death
  - Mal 4:1-3 — "The day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud, yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble... and it shall leave them neither root nor branch... they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet"
  - Psa 37:10, 20 — "Yet a little while, and the wicked shall not be... the wicked shall perish, and the enemies of the LORD shall be as the fat of lambs: they shall consume; into smoke shall they consume away"
  - 2 Pet 2:6 — "Turning the cities of Sodom and Gomorrha into ashes condemned them with an overthrow, making them an ensample unto those that after should live ungodly"
  - The fire destroys. It does not torture forever. The "eternal" in the biblical vocabulary of this judgment is the result's permanence, not the torment's duration

## The Great White Throne

Before the fire descends, the final judgment scene is rendered.

- **The throne** (Rev 20:11)
  - "I saw a great white throne, and him that sat on it, from whose face the earth and the heaven fled away; and there was found no place for them"
  - _White_ = purity; _great_ = supreme; from whose face _the earth and heaven fled_ — the judgment throne is so holy that the entire created order cannot stand before it
  - This is the final phase of the judgment that opened in Dan 7:9-14, continued through 1844's investigative judgment, was publicly executed at the Second Coming, and now closes here

- **The books opened** (Rev 20:12)
  - "I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God; and the books were opened: and another book was opened, which is the book of life: and the dead were judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works"
  - _The books_ — the records of every life (Dan 7:10's opened books)
  - _The book of life_ — the register of the saved (Phil 4:3; Dan 12:1; Rev 3:5; Rev 13:8; 17:8; 21:27)
  - The wicked are judged _out of the books_ — their own records convict them
  - _According to their works_ — not because works save (Eph 2:8-9) but because works reveal character. What each person was is now visible to every being in the universe

- `[⟲]` **Judgment thread final link:** Dan 7:10 ("thousand thousands ministered... the judgment was set, and the books were opened") → Rev 20:11-12 (the great white throne and the books opened). The same books from Daniel's vision now produce the universe's final reckoning

- **Death and hell into the lake of fire** (Rev 20:14)
  - "Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death"
  - _Death_ (thanatos) and _hell_ (hades — the grave, state of the dead) are _themselves_ destroyed
  - Sin's final two instruments — mortality and the grave — are no longer needed; they are consumed
  - Paul confirms: "The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death" (1 Cor 15:26); "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?" (1 Cor 15:55)

- **"Whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire"** (Rev 20:15)
  - The great white throne's final act: the cosmic sifting is complete
  - The wicked who had been raised in the second resurrection now die the second death
  - The universe is cleansed forever

## What the Lake of Fire IS and Is Not

- **Is**
  - The final execution of the second death (Rev 20:14)
  - The destruction of sin, death, and the grave (1 Cor 15:26, 54-57)
  - The consuming fire that leaves "neither root nor branch" (Mal 4:1)
  - The ashes under the righteous's feet (Mal 4:3)
  - _Permanent_ in its result — those who enter do not return (Isa 47:14 — "they shall not deliver themselves from the power of the flame")

- **Is NOT**
  - Eternal conscious torment for billions of years
  - A place where the wicked scream in agony forever while the saved watch
  - A continuing testimony to God's wrath that never ends
  - A justification for the doctrine of hell as traditionally portrayed in post-Augustine Christendom (Way Home Study 16's restoration arc and the conditional-immortality texts traced through the Way Home series develop the full biblical case against eternal conscious torment)

- **The "for ever and ever" language**
  - Rev 20:10 says the devil, beast, and false prophet are "tormented day and night for ever and ever"
  - The phrase is biblical idiom for lasting finality, read with the surrounding testimony: Rev 20:14 names the lake of fire itself "the second death"; Mal 4:1-3 has the wicked burned to ashes "neither root nor branch"; Isa 34:10 describes Edom's smoke rising "for ever" though Edom is not still smoking; Rev 14:11's smoke "ascendeth up for ever and ever" as the permanent witness of judgment. Taken together, "for ever and ever" names the judgment's permanence — the wicked are destroyed once and never return — not an infinite duration of conscious torment
  - The fire consumes, leaves ashes, and ends. The _result_ is eternal. The _burning_ is not

## Key Takeaways

- Satan is bound for a thousand years by circumstances (Rev 20:1-3) — the living are in heaven, the wicked dead, no one to deceive
- The earth becomes Satan's prison — a desolate wasteland he walks with his angels for a millennium (Jer 4:23-26; Isa 24:21-22)
- The saints reign with Christ in heaven, _not_ on earth (John 14:1-3; 1 Thess 4:16-17; Rev 20:4)
- "Judgment was given unto them" — the saints review every record during the millennium, so that every question about God's justice is answered before the final destruction
- First resurrection = the righteous, at the Second Coming. Second resurrection = the wicked, at the millennium's end
- At the end of the thousand years, Satan is loosed, the wicked raised, the New Jerusalem descends, and the armies of the unrighteous surround the city (Gog and Magog, Rev 20:8-9)
- Every knee bows (Phil 2:10-11) — even the wicked acknowledge God's justice before the fire falls
- Fire from God devours them (Rev 20:9; Mal 4:1-3) — the second death is execution, not eternal torment
- The Great White Throne (Rev 20:11-12) opens the books; the wicked are judged out of their own records
- Death and hell are cast into the lake of fire (Rev 20:14) — the last enemies destroyed (1 Cor 15:26)
- "For ever and ever" language = the permanence of the result, not infinite conscious torment
- `[⟲]` Judgment thread final link: Dan 7:10's books opened → Rev 20:11-12's great white throne

## Next: Study 20 — All Things New (Revelation 21-22)

Sin is ended. Death is ended. The lake of fire has consumed every enemy. The earth, cleansed by the fire that destroyed the wicked, is remade. "Behold, I make all things new." A new heaven, a new earth, the holy city descending out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. God dwells with men; no more tears, no more death, no more sorrow or crying or pain. The tree of life, last seen in Eden, is restored — and Adam's long exile ends. The final chapter is the arrival everything pointed toward.
