# Study 4: The Sanctuary Key

**Dan 8:14 said the sanctuary will be cleansed. Study 3 dated that event to 1844. But what _is_ the sanctuary — and why does Scripture make it the place where the entire plan of salvation unfolds? The earthly tabernacle Moses built was not decoration. It was a scale model, given on a mountain, of Christ's priestly work. Its three compartments traced the gospel: cross, intercession, judgment. Its one holy day, the Day of Atonement, pictured what Revelation will later announce as "the hour of his judgment." If you understand the sanctuary, every thread in Dan + Rev — the 1844, the little horn's attack on the daily, the three angels, the mark of the beast, the close of probation — has a coherent place to stand. Without it, those threads float.**

## Prep Reading

- Leviticus 16 (the Day of Atonement — entire chapter)
- Hebrews 8 (the priestly ministry in the heavenly sanctuary)
- Hebrews 9 (the two tabernacles compared)
- Hebrews 10:19-25
- Psalm 77:13
- Revelation 11:19; 14:6-7; 15:5-8

## "Thy Way, O God, Is in the Sanctuary"

Before we get to Leviticus, listen to Asaph. The psalm begins in crisis — "I cried unto God with my voice... my sore ran in the night" (Ps 77:1-2). Asaph asks whether God has forgotten mercy, whether His promises have failed. Then he turns the corner:

- "I will remember the works of the LORD: surely I will remember thy wonders of old" (Ps 77:11)
- "I will meditate also of all thy work" (Ps 77:12)
- "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary: who is so great a God as our God?" (Ps 77:13)

- [→] Asaph's answer to doubt is not theology-from-nowhere. It is the sanctuary. Where does God's character come clear? In the sanctuary service. Every question about God's justice, God's mercy, God's mission — the sanctuary shows the answer in physical form. "Thy way... is in the sanctuary."

That is why this study matters. Dan 8:14 did not pick the sanctuary arbitrarily. The sanctuary is _the_ place God uses to reveal Himself across the Old Testament. When Rev 11:19 opens heaven's temple, Rev 14 cries "the hour of his judgment," Rev 15 shows the tabernacle filled with smoke, and Rev 21 announces "the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple" — all of it lands on sanctuary soil. The closing book ends where Exodus 25 began.

## A Copy of the Real Thing

Moses did not design the tabernacle. He was _shown_ it.

- **Exodus 25:8-9, 40**
  - "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them"
  - "According to all that I shew thee, after the pattern of the tabernacle, and the pattern of all the instruments thereof, even so shall ye make it"
  - "See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount" (Heb 8:5 quoting Ex 25:40)
- **Hebrews 8:1-5**
  - "We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man"
  - "Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle"

- **The implication:**
  - There is a _real_ sanctuary in heaven — "the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man"
  - The earthly tabernacle Moses built was a scale model, a "shadow" (_skia_ in Greek = outline, silhouette) of that real one
  - Every piece of furniture, every garment, every ritual was a teaching model of something Christ would do in the real heavenly sanctuary
  - [→] You cannot understand the earthly sanctuary by staring at the earthly sanctuary. You understand it by watching what Christ does _above_, and then reading Leviticus as the blueprint that was given in advance to explain it.

- `[KEY🔑]` **The earthly sanctuary is a _pattern_ of the heavenly, not a replacement for it.** The book of Hebrews makes this the axis of its entire theology (Heb 8:1-5; 9:11-12, 23-24; 10:1). If we hold this distinction, the rest of Daniel and Revelation opens. If we collapse it, we are stuck looking for earthly temples to rebuild.

[DYK🔎] The Greek word Hebrews uses for the earthly sanctuary is _hypodeigma_ (Heb 8:5; 9:23) — "a specimen, a pattern," or "a copy." Paired with _skia_ ("shadow") it means: the earthly was never the point. The earthly told you what to look for in the heavenly. When Christ ascended (Heb 9:24), He did not enter "the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us." The figures had purpose for a time. The real thing has taken over.

## The Three Compartments

The earthly tabernacle had three zones, each with a different function and a different level of access.

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │                     OUTER COURTYARD                         │
   │                                                             │
   │     ┌─ Brazen altar ─┐         ┌─ Laver ─┐                  │
   │     │   (sacrifice)  │         │ (wash)  │                  │
   │     └────────────────┘         └─────────┘                  │
   │                                                             │
   │  ┌────────────────────────────┐ ┌──────────────┐            │
   │  │        HOLY PLACE          │ │ MOST HOLY    │            │
   │  │                            │ │              │            │
   │  │ Table of     Golden        │ │  Ark of the  │            │
   │  │ shewbread    candlestick   │ │  covenant    │            │
   │  │                            │ │  (mercy seat)│            │
   │  │    Altar of incense        │ │              │            │
   │  │     (before the veil)      │ │              │            │
   │  └────────────────────────────┘ └──────────────┘            │
   │                              ↑                              │
   │                      the second veil                        │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Compartment                                    | Furniture                                       | Function                | What it pictures                                                     |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Courtyard** (open to all Israel)             | Brazen altar, laver                             | Sacrifice and washing   | The cross: Christ's death, confession and cleansing                  |
| **Holy Place** (priests, daily)                | Lampstand, table of shewbread, altar of incense | Daily priestly ministry | Christ's ongoing intercession in heaven since the ascension          |
| **Most Holy Place** (high priest, once a year) | Ark of the covenant, mercy seat                 | Annual Day of Atonement | Final judgment — sins examined, records reviewed, covenant confirmed |

- **The gospel walk, in physical form:**
  1. A sinner brings a lamb to the **courtyard**, confesses, the lamb dies in his place — salvation by substitution
  2. The priests in the **Holy Place** trim the lamps, change the bread, burn incense, carry blood inward — Christ's continual intercession on behalf of forgiven sinners
  3. Once a year, on the Day of Atonement, the high priest enters the **Most Holy Place** with blood — the final cleansing that settles every case and readies the camp for the next year
- [→] Every sinner who followed the lamb through the gate walked the gospel in his feet. The architecture taught the order: cross first, then intercession, then judgment. Not two phases. Not one phase. Three.

[Q] **"Why three compartments instead of one altar?"**
→ Because salvation is not a single moment. The cross is the foundation, but it is not the whole work. After the lamb's death, sin was still ritually _transferred_ to the sanctuary (Lev 4:5-7) and had to be _cleansed out_ on a later day (Lev 16:30). The tabernacle's three zones teach: the cross is paid, but the _administration_ of salvation continues until the books are closed. The Holy Place is Christ's ongoing ministry (Heb 7:25 — "he ever liveth to make intercession"). The Most Holy is the final review. Three compartments, three phases, one gospel.

## The Daily — _Tamid_

From the moment the tabernacle was dedicated, the priests' main task was continuous: day by day, morning and evening, forever.

- **What _tamid_ (Hebrew: "continual") covered** — Ex 29-30, Lev 6, Num 28
  - The morning and evening burnt offerings (Ex 29:38-42)
  - The lamps burning before the Lord continually (Ex 27:20-21; Lev 24:2-4)
  - The shewbread set out continually (Ex 25:30; Lev 24:5-8)
  - Incense burned on the altar morning and evening (Ex 30:7-8)
  - Sin offerings for individuals bringing confession throughout the year (Lev 4)
- **Notice the word:** "continual" — _tamid_ — describes _all of it_, not just one ritual. This is the word Dan 8:11-13 uses when the little horn "takes away the daily"

- **The daily pictured Christ's _continual_ ministry in heaven**
  - "Christ... ever liveth to make intercession" (Heb 7:25)
  - "For if the blood of bulls and of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the purifying of the flesh: how much more shall the blood of Christ... purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?" (Heb 9:13-14)
  - The earthly lampstand, shewbread, and incense were _visual aids_ for a heavenly reality that would start at the cross and continue until the judgment
  - [→] When Dan 8:11 says the little horn "took away the daily," the target is not a set of Jewish rituals that had already ceased by the papal era. Christ's actual priestly ministry cannot be removed — He "ever liveth" (Heb 7:25). But the _access_ and _truth_ of that ministry can be obscured: substitute priests on earth, substitute intercession, substitute confessional booths, a substitute mediatrix. Dan 8:12 says "it cast down the truth to the ground" — the continual High Priest above is hidden behind a counterfeit continual on earth. (We will see this develop in Studies 13 and 15.)

## The Day of Atonement — _Yom Kippur_

Once a year everything else stopped.

- **The day** (Lev 16:29-31; 23:26-32)
  - "In the seventh month, on the tenth day of the month" (Lev 16:29) — the only day each year the Most Holy Place was entered
  - "A sabbath of rest unto you, and ye shall afflict your souls" (Lev 23:32) — fasting, confession, self-examination
  - "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD" (Lev 16:30)
  - A day of judgment for Israel: those who "afflicted" their souls were "cleansed"; those who refused were "cut off from among his people" (Lev 23:29)

- **The ritual** (Lev 16)

  | Step                                                                                                     | Text                                                                      | Meaning                                                         |
  | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
  | High priest bathes and puts on linen (Lev 16:4)                                                          | "He shall put on the holy linen coat..."                                  | Purity of the mediator                                          |
  | Two goats presented, lots cast — one for the LORD, one for Azazel (Lev 16:5-10)                          | "One lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat"               | Two destinies for sin: atonement vs. banishment                 |
  | The LORD's goat is killed as a sin offering (Lev 16:9, 15)                                               | "Then shall he kill the goat of the sin offering, that is for the people" | The Christ-type — sacrificed for the people                     |
  | Blood sprinkled before and on the mercy seat (Lev 16:14-16)                                              | "Sprinkle it upon the mercy seat, and before the mercy seat"              | Christ's blood applied in the Most Holy Place                   |
  | Atonement "made... for the holy place, because of the uncleanness of the children of Israel" (Lev 16:16) | —                                                                         | The sanctuary itself is cleansed from accumulated confessed sin |
  | High priest returns, confesses all the people's sins on the scapegoat (Lev 16:20-22)                     | "Confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel"           | Sins finally placed on their true originator — not the victim   |
  | The scapegoat is led by "a fit man" into "a land not inhabited" (Lev 16:21-22)                           | —                                                                         | Azazel/Satan bears away the guilt he caused, into isolation     |
  | People freed, camp cleansed, statute kept "for ever" (Lev 16:29-34)                                      | —                                                                         | The community walks into the next year free of confessed sin    |

- **Key move: the ritual _cleanses the sanctuary itself_** (Lev 16:16, 33)
  - Throughout the year, when Israelites confessed sins over their offerings in the courtyard, the sins were ritually _transferred_ into the sanctuary via the blood (Lev 4:5-7, 16-18)
  - The sanctuary accumulated sin-record across the year
  - On the Day of Atonement, the sanctuary was _cleansed_ — the records of confessed sin were finally dealt with
  - [→] This is what Dan 8:14 pictures: "unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be _cleansed_." Same verb conceptually as Lev 16. Same ceremony. Same function. Just executed in the heavenly sanctuary, at the end of a 2300-year timeline.

[DYK🔎] The scapegoat (Hebrew _Azazel_) is _not_ a Christ-symbol in the ritual itself. Christ's blood is pictured by the _first_ goat ("for the LORD" — Lev 16:9, 15), which is killed and whose blood enters the sanctuary. The scapegoat receives no sin-bearing role until _after_ atonement is complete (Lev 16:20-21); it bears away sins that have _already_ been reconciled, and it is led to "a land not inhabited" — a desolation picture, not a Calvary picture. Jewish apocalyptic sources, some early-Christian readers (Origen among them), and the historicist Adventist tradition have identified Azazel with Satan: the one who originated sin has its weight rolled back onto him when the judgment is finished. Christian interpretation is mixed — other traditions read both goats christologically — so this is a reading with substantial support, not a universal consensus. Rev 20 will show this picture literally: Satan bound in the desolated earth for a thousand years while the saints are in the city. The scapegoat reaches across the whole Bible.

## Dan 8:14 Meets Rev 14:7

Put the three key texts side by side.

- **Dan 8:14** — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
- **Lev 16:30** — "For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you"
- **Rev 14:6-7** — "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel... saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for _the hour of his judgment is come_"

- [→] Three witnesses, one event:
  - Lev 16 says: the cleansing is a judgment, done in the sanctuary, on a specific day
  - Dan 8 says: the cleansing arrives at the end of 2300 days
  - Rev 14 says: when the time comes, the world is told "the hour of his judgment is come"
- These are not three different events. They are three angles on the same event — the final phase of Christ's priestly ministry, which Dan dates, Lev pictures, and Rev announces.

- `[⟲]` **Sanctuary thread:** Lev 16 → Dan 8:11-14 → Heb 8-9 → Rev 11:19 → Rev 14:6-7 → Rev 15:5-8. Every link is built on the one before.
- `[⟲]` **Judgment thread:** Lev 16 judgment structure → Dan 7:9-14 courtroom → Rev 4-5 throne → Rev 11:15-19 temple opened → Rev 14:6-7 judgment announced → Rev 20:11-12 books opened. The sanctuary and the judgment are not two threads; they are the same thread named from two angles.

## What 1844 Is — and Is Not

In Study 3 we reached the date. Here we place what actually starts that year.

- **What 1844 _is_ (Heb 9:23-24; Rev 11:19; 14:6-7)**
  - The start of the heavenly Day of Atonement — Christ's entrance into the final phase of His priestly work
  - The beginning of an investigative judgment on the records of all who have claimed His name (cf. 1 Pet 4:17 — "judgment must begin at the house of God")
  - The prophetic event Rev 14:7 announces to the world as "the hour of his judgment"
  - The temple's Most Holy Place opened in heaven — "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament" (Rev 11:19)

- **What 1844 is _not_**
  - It is _not_ the Second Coming (Christ does not return to earth then)
  - It is _not_ Christ's first entrance into God's presence — Heb 9:24 places that at the ascension, c. AD 31. What starts in 1844 is the final judgment/cleansing phase of His already-ongoing priestly ministry, not the ministry itself
  - It is _not_ a "second chance at probation" starting then (probation opened at the cross, not 1844)
  - It is _not_ a new gospel — the gospel has always been Christ's blood; 1844 is a phase-change in the _administration_ of that gospel
  - It is _not_ primarily about fear for the believer — the High Priest appears "in the presence of God for us" (Heb 9:24). Presence. Advocacy. Not opposition.

[Q] **"Does an investigative judgment mean God does not already know who is His?"**
→ God does not need the investigation. The _universe_ does. The cosmic trial Satan opened (Job 1-2; Rev 12:10) requires a public accounting. When Dan 7:10 says "the judgment was set, and the books were opened" — the books are opened _before angels_. "Unto the principalities and powers in heavenly places might be known by the church the manifold wisdom of God" (Eph 3:10). The judgment is not for God's benefit. It vindicates His administration before every created being. That is why records matter. That is why advocacy matters. That is why the Lamb slain stands in the throne (Rev 5:6) — to be seen.

## Christ Our High Priest Right Now

This is not historical trivia. Hebrews ends its sanctuary exposition with a charge for the reader.

- **Hebrews 10:19-23**
  - "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus"
  - "By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"
  - "And having an high priest over the house of God"
  - "Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith"

- [→] Three practical consequences:
  1. **You have access.** The Most Holy Place, once entered by only one man once a year, is now accessible to every believer through Christ's blood
  2. **You have an advocate.** Not a distant judge. A High Priest who bore your flesh, faced your temptations, and now stands in heaven with the scars that guarantee He knows (Heb 4:14-16)
  3. **You have a timetable.** The Day of Atonement is _now_ — since 1844. The examination is underway. The appeal is to "draw near"

- If Study 3 gave us the date and this study gave us the architecture, what remains is: _live in it_. The sanctuary is not a museum. It is the room Christ is serving in right now.

## The Day of Atonement Frame Across Daniel

Before we leave the sanctuary, notice how saturated the second half of Daniel is with Day of Atonement imagery.

| Dan passage                                                                                           | Day of Atonement connection                                                    |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Dan 8:5-8 — he-goat killed the ram                                                                    | Lev 16:5 — ram and goat both feature in the Day of Atonement sacrifices        |
| Dan 8:14 — "then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"                                                     | Lev 16:30, 33 — the day the sanctuary is cleansed                              |
| Dan 10:5-6 — "a certain man clothed in linen"                                                         | Lev 16:4 — the high priest wears special linen garments for this day           |
| Dan 12:6-7 — "the man clothed in linen... which was upon the waters of the river," between two others | Lev 16:2; Ex 25:22 — God meets between the two cherubim in the Most Holy Place |
| Dan 12:1 — "Michael shall stand up" + "deliver every one that shall be found written in the book"     | Lev 16:16 — the day sins are blotted from the sanctuary records                |

- [→] Daniel 8-12 is _framed_ by Day of Atonement imagery — the animal set evokes the Day of Atonement service, the man-in-linen wears the high priest's Day of Atonement garments (Lev 16:4), and the cleansing of Dan 8:14 echoes Lev 16:30. The chapters also carry political/historical sequence material alongside the priestly frame, but the sanctuary and judgment motifs are deliberate and sustained. We will come back to the Man in Linen in Study 6 and see Him again in Rev 1 in Study 7.

## Key Takeaways

- "Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary" (Ps 77:13) — the sanctuary is Scripture's chosen vehicle for revealing how God saves
- `[KEY🔑]` The earthly sanctuary is a _pattern_ (_hypodeigma_) of the heavenly, not a replacement for it (Heb 8:1-5; 9:23-24)
- Three compartments = three phases of Christ's work: **courtyard** (cross), **Holy Place** (continual intercession), **Most Holy** (Day of Atonement / judgment)
- The _tamid_ (continual daily ministry) covered morning/evening offerings, lamps, shewbread, incense — together they pictured Christ's continual intercession; Dan 8:11's "taking away of the daily" targets that symbol
- The Day of Atonement cleansed _the sanctuary itself_ (Lev 16:16, 33) — the same verb concept Dan 8:14 uses: "then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
- Dan 8:14 + Lev 16:30 + Rev 14:6-7 describe one event from three angles; Rev 11:19 and Rev 15:5-8 complete the sanctuary thread
- 1844 is the opening of the heavenly Most Holy Place phase — not the Second Coming, not a new gospel, and not an event to dread for the believer
- The scapegoat (Azazel) is _not_ a Christ-symbol; it is Satan receiving back the guilt he originated, a picture Rev 20 will repeat literally
- Dan 8-12 is _framed_ by Day of Atonement imagery — animal set, linen garments, sanctuary cleansing, books of record; political sequence material runs alongside the priestly frame
