# 2026 Q2 W13 - June 20–26

## Into Eternity

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** The hope of heaven—seeing Jesus face-to-face, the New Jerusalem, and Christ’s invitation to “Come!”
**Present Truth Theme:** Eternity is the outcome of the sanctuary gospel: Christ’s righteousness **imputed** (altar), **imparted** (Holy Place), and **finally applied/sealed** in His closing Most Holy Place ministry—producing a people who keep the commandments of God by the **faith of Jesus** (Rev. 14:12).
**Central Focus:** Moving from escapism, anxious waiting, and self-effort into **abiding faith** that actually purifies the heart, reshapes relationships, and produces obedience as fruit—so we are fit for Christ’s presence now and forever.
**Key Texts:** Psalm 80:3, 19; 1 John 3:2–3; Revelation 21:3–4

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Many want heaven mainly as relief from pain. But what if the greatest test is not “Can I endure earth?” but “Am I safe to live in the unveiled presence of a holy God?”
  - [DQ]: _(Icebreaker):_ “When you picture eternity, what do you most long for—rest, reunion, reward, or relationship with Christ? Why?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson closes the quarter—“Into Eternity”—the hope of the city, the invitation “Come,” and the question: what kind of people will dwell there?
- Present Truth Bridge: The sanctuary teaches that heaven is not entered by sentiment; it is entered by a **real righteousness**—Christ’s righteousness received by faith and reproduced in the life.
  - [WB]: Title: `INTO ETERNITY = CHRIST’S RIGHTEOUSNESS NOW`
  - [WB]: Sanctuary roadmap: `ALTAR (pardon) -> HOLY (power) -> MOST HOLY (seal/ready)`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement—Christ’s Most Holy Place ministry aims to finish the work in a people (Rev. 14:12).
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 = Commandments + Faith of Jesus`
- Roadmap:
  - Psalm 80: “Cause Thy face to shine” = God’s initiative to restore.
  - 1 John 3:2–3: hope that purifies—how change actually happens.
  - Revelation 21–22: the city, the Bride, and the “Come” invitation as present-tense faith.

---

### II. Cause Thy Face to Shine: Justification That Restores (Psalm 80 + Priestly Blessing) (16-18 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Psalm 80 is a sanctuary prayer—God enthroned “between the cherubim” (Ps. 80:1) is pleaded with to **restore**. Restoration begins with being accepted in the Beloved, not with self-repair.

#### A. The shining face: grace first, then restoration (8-9 mins)

- Foundation Text 1: Psalm 80:3
  - Reading/Context: The refrain ties salvation to God’s shining face.
  - [WB]: `Ps 80:3 = RESTORE + SHINE = SAVED`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Salvation starts with God turning toward us in mercy; faith receives what grace initiates.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What are the two actions in the verse—what does God do, and what happens to the people?”
- Foundation Text 2: Numbers 6:24–26
  - Reading/Connection: The shining face is priestly blessing—sanctuary grace announced over unworthy people.
  - [WB]: `SHINE = GRACE -> PEACE`
  - The Faith Distinction: Legalism says, “When I’m better, God will shine.” The gospel says, “Because God shines in Christ, I can be restored.”
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you catch yourself bargaining with God—‘I’ll pray more, then You’ll accept me’?”
- Foundation Text 3: John 6:37
  - Reading/Challenge: Coming is the sinner’s only “qualification.”
  - [WB]: `COME -> NO CAST OUT`
  - Sanctuary connection: At the altar, the sinner brings nothing but need; Christ provides the sacrifice (imputed righteousness).
  - [EGW]: “The righteousness by which we are justified is imputed; the righteousness by which we are sanctified is imparted. The first is our title to heaven, the second is our fitness for heaven.” [EGW] (1SM 394.2).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Do you treat ‘coming to Christ’ as your first move after failure—or your last move after you’ve punished yourself with distance?”

- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: A child breaks something valuable and hides, rehearsing promises to “do better.” Faith runs to the Father because relationship is safety; then restoration happens.
  - The Practical Landing: Monday repentance is not self-fixing; it is immediate coming—confessing, believing acceptance in Christ, and letting Him restore what you cannot.
  - [DQ]: _(Personal honesty):_ “When you sin, what is your default—hide, perform, or come? What would ‘come at once’ look like this week?”

#### B. “Restore” is end-time language: Most Holy Place light without panic (8-9 mins)

- Foundation Text 4: Psalm 80:19
  - Reading/Context: The repeated refrain signals urgency and persistence.
  - [WB]: `Ps 80 refrain (3x) = URGENT REVIVAL`
  - Unpacking the Truth: In judgment-hour reality, God’s people plead for revival—not to earn salvation, but to be made ready.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “Why would God inspire a refrain—what does repetition do to faith and focus?”
- Foundation Text 5: 1 Peter 4:7
  - Reading/Connection: End-time sobriety is prayerful dependence, not anxious works.
  - [WB]: `END NEAR -> SOBER + PRAYERFUL`
  - Faith distinction: The nearness of the end should drive us to the High Priest, not to self-salvation projects.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What is the difference between ‘seriousness’ and ‘fear-driven striving’?”
- Foundation Text 6 (Promise/Power): Philippians 1:6
  - Reading/Challenge: The finisher is God, not you.
  - [WB]: `HE BEGAN -> HE FINISHES`
  - How to Receive: Consent to be saved; surrender known sin; claim His promise; keep coming when you fall—faith is dependence maintained.
  - [EGW]: “A revival of true godliness among us is the greatest and most urgent of all our needs.” [EGW] (1SM 121.1).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Name one area where you need God to ‘finish His work’—temper, appetite, entertainment, resentment, secret sin. What would surrender look like specifically?”

- [SN]: Trying to climb out of a pit by clawing the walls (effort) vs. grabbing the rope thrown down (faith). The rope is Christ’s merits and intercession.
- [WB]: Diagram: `PIT (me) <- ROPE (Christ) -> OUT (restored)`
- [DQ]: “In your recurring temptation, what is ‘clawing the walls,’ and what would ‘grabbing the rope’ be in real time (words you pray, action you take)?”

### III. Face-to-Face: Hope That Purifies—Imparted Righteousness by the Indwelling Christ (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Heaven is not merely a location; it is a society built on God’s character. The gospel’s aim is fitness for His presence—purity of heart produced by Christ within, received by faith.

#### A. “We shall be like Him”: the purpose of redemption (8-9 mins)

- Foundation Text 1: 1 John 3:2
  - Reading/Context: The vision of Christ is connected to likeness.
  - [WB]: `SEE HIM -> LIKE HIM`
  - Unpacking the Truth: The end-point is character transformation, not merely acquittal; likeness is promised to “children” who remain in relationship.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What two certainties does John give—what we are now, and what we will be?”
- Foundation Text 2: 1 John 3:3
  - Reading/Connection: Hope does something now—it purifies.
  - [WB]: `HOPE -> PURIFIES`
  - The Faith Distinction: Biblical purification is not self-scouring; it is cooperating with Christ’s purifying presence by abiding faith.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How is ‘hope’ different from ‘optimism’? What does hope do to behavior?”
- Foundation Text 3: Matthew 5:8
  - Reading/Challenge: “Pure in heart” = singleness of motive, God enthroned within.
  - [WB]: `PURE HEART -> SEE GOD`
  - [EGW]: “It is impossible for us, of ourselves, to escape from the pit of sin in which we are sunken… only by yielding up our will to Christ can we be delivered.” [EGW] (SC 18.1).
  - Practical mechanics of faith: Yield the will (choice) to Christ; trust His power (ability); act on His word (obedience as fruit).
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ “What ‘impure motive’ shows up most in you—pride, resentment, people-pleasing, control? How would yielding your will touch that today?”

- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: A mirror only reflects what it faces. If I face my failures, I reflect despair; if I face Christ by faith, I reflect His character.
  - [WB]: `BEHOLD -> BECOME`
  - Practical Landing: Start the day by intentionally “turning” the mind to Christ (Word/prayer/surrender), then in the moment of provocation pause and say, “Jesus, live Your patience in me now.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What is one predictable daily trigger (traffic, spouse, coworker, child) where you need ‘Christ’s patience in you’ rather than your patience for Him?”

#### B. Sealing readiness: His name in the forehead (7-8 mins)

- Foundation Text 4: Revelation 22:4
  - Reading/Connection: Seeing His face and bearing His name in the forehead.
  - [WB]: `FACE + NAME (mind/character)`
  - Sanctuary connection: Forehead language connects to sealing—settled into truth so we cannot be moved.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “What does it mean, practically, for God’s name (character) to be in your mind under pressure?”
- Foundation Text 5: Revelation 21:27
  - Reading/Challenge: Nothing that defiles enters—heaven’s moral reality.
  - [WB]: `NO DEFILEMENT ENTERS`
  - Faith distinction: The same Christ who pardons also cleanses. Cleansing is not moral polishing; it is a new heart maintained by abiding.
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Do you believe Christ can forgive you more easily than He can change you? What has shaped that belief?”
- Foundation Text 6 (Promise/Power): Titus 2:11–14
  - Reading/Connection: Grace trains and purifies a peculiar people.
  - [WB]: `GRACE -> TRAINS -> PURIFIES`
  - How it works Monday: In temptation, faith says, “Lord, You promised grace that teaches; I yield and trust You to supply the ‘No’ and the new desire.”
  - [EGW]: “Christ is waiting with longing desire for the manifestation of Himself in His church. When the character of Christ shall be perfectly reproduced in His people, then He will come to claim them as His own.” [EGW] (COL 69.1).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would it look like for grace to ‘train’ you this week—what practice will you stop, and what will you replace it with?”

- [SN]: A traveler invited into a palace doesn’t scrub himself with a toothbrush at the gate; he accepts the King’s provision for cleansing and then walks carefully inside.
- [WB]: `INVITATION -> CLEANSING PROVIDED -> FELLOWSHIP`
- [DQ]: “Where are you postponing coming to God because you think you must clean up first?”

### IV. Follow the Lamb: The Bride Made Ready—Commandments as Fruit of the Faith of Jesus (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: The quarter ends with a call to a prepared people. The sanctuary order is:
  - Altar: pardon (imputed righteousness).
  - Holy Place: power for obedience (imparted righteousness).
  - Most Holy Place: sealing readiness (final atonement work applied).
  - [WB]: `IMPUTED -> IMPARTED -> SEALED`
- Foundation Text 1: Revelation 14:12
  - The saints are described, not merely instructed: commandments of God + faith of Jesus.
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12`
  - [DQ]: “Which side do you tend to separate—commandments without faith, or faith without commandments? What is the Bible’s picture?”
- Foundation Text 2: Revelation 14:4–5
  - “Follow the Lamb” = present tense discipleship; “no guile” = integrity produced by Christ within.
  - [WB]: `FOLLOW THE LAMB (wherever)`
  - [DQ]: “Where is the Lamb ‘going’ in your life right now—what duty or surrender are you avoiding?”
- Foundation Text 3 (Invitation/Power): Revelation 22:17
  - “Come… take the water of life freely” = not earned; received.
  - [WB]: `COME + TAKE FREELY`
  - [EGW]: “If we are clothed with the righteousness of Christ and are filled with the joy of His indwelling Spirit, we shall not be able to hold our peace.” [EGW] (SC 77.3).
  - [DQ]: “If the water is ‘freely’ given, what keeps people from taking—pride, unbelief, love of sin, discouragement?”

- [SN]: Two ways to “prepare for heaven”: packing your own parachute (self-righteousness) vs. putting on the one provided (Christ’s righteousness). One fails under pressure; one holds.
- [WB]: `SELF -> FAILS` / `CHRIST -> HOLDS`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - [WB]: `COME (today) -> ABIDE -> OBEY (fruit)`
  - (Final Question): “What is the one area you will stop ‘trying harder’ in, and instead bring to Jesus daily as High Priest—asking Him to live His obedience in you?”

---

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: Eternity is the fruit of Christ’s sanctuary work—He pardons, He empowers, He seals. Heaven is for those who have learned to live by **faith**—Christ’s life in them—so the commandments become joy, not bondage.
- The Practical Takeaway: When we receive Christ by faith, **He does in us what we cannot do**—repentance becomes immediate coming, obedience becomes lived dependence, and hope becomes purification.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST IN YOU = FITNESS FOR HEAVEN`
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Bring the specific sin, fear, or relational rupture to Christ today; agree with His verdict; yield the will; believe He acts as High Priest now.
  - [WB]: `HE IS ABLE (Heb 7:25)`
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - Read Hebrews 7:25 (brief): He saves “to the uttermost” because He lives to intercede.
  - [EGW]: “To the omnipotent power of the Holy Spirit is committed the work of making effectual the blood of Christ.” [EGW] (DA 671.2).
  - [WB]: `Heb 7:25`
- Closing appeal question (for silent decision): “What will you bring—specifically—into the Most Holy Place by faith this week, believing Jesus will finish His work in you?”
