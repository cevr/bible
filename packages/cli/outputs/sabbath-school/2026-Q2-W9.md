# 2026 Q2 W9 - May 23–29

## Sin, the Gospel, and the Law

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Sin, the Gospel, and the Law  
**Present Truth Theme:** The law reveals sin, but only Christ’s sanctuary ministry provides forgiveness and heart-change—**righteousness by faith** establishes the law as Christ lives His obedience in us (the **faith of Jesus**).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from self-reliance (Samson Christianity) to abiding faith (Rock Christianity): Christ’s righteousness **imputed and imparted** breaking sin’s strongholds in daily life.  
**Key Texts:** Romans 3:20; Romans 3:28–31; Matthew 7:24–29

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: “If God’s law can’t forgive you, and your willpower can’t change you—what actually breaks sin’s power Monday morning?”
  - [DQ]: _(Quick share):_ “When you hear ‘law,’ do you feel mainly comfort, pressure, or guilt? Why?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson names sin honestly, then puts law and gospel together so we can be restored to God.
- Present Truth Bridge: The sanctuary shows the order: **sin exposed (law/mirror)** → **blood applied (justification)** → **presence/relationship restored (sanctification)** → **final cleansing (Most Holy Place)**.
  - [WB]: `LAW → CONVICTION → CHRIST (BLOOD) → NEW HEART → OBEDIENCE (FRUIT)`
- Why This Matters Now: We are living in the antitypical Day of Atonement; victory over sin is not moral optimism—it is Christ’s current High Priestly work received by faith.
  - [EGW]: “The law and the gospel are in perfect harmony. Each upholds the other… The sense of sin, urged home by the law, drives the sinner to the Saviour… He may claim the righteousness of Christ; for it is imparted to every repentant sinner.” [EGW] (AG 15).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Do you tend to run to Christ after conviction—or hide, explain, and delay?”
- Roadmap: We will trace 3 movements:
  - (1) Sin’s strategy and self-reliance (Samson)
  - (2) Law and gospel in harmony (Romans; Sermon on the Mount)
  - (3) Knowing-and-doing by the Word (Rock foundation)

### II. Sin’s Strongholds vs. Christ’s Indwelling Power (Samson to Jesus) (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: The lesson starts with distractions/temptations. The real issue isn’t just “bad habits”—it’s **self-dependence** versus **faith-union with Christ**.

#### A. Samson: Called, Gifted, Yet Defeated by Self-Trust (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Judges 14 (overview) and Judges 16:1, 4, 16-17
  - Reading/Context: Samson’s calling was real; his consecration was symbolic; but his heart was divided by appetite, lust, and presumption.
  - [WB]: `SAMSON = GIFTED + CALLED + UNGUARDED`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Gifts are not righteousness. Strength is not holiness. Sin becomes a **stronghold** where the will is slowly surrendered.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What do you notice about Samson’s pattern—sudden fall, or gradual drift?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** 1 Corinthians 10:12
  - Reading/Connection: “Let him who thinks he stands take heed lest he fall”—Samson is a warning against confidence in spiritual track-record.
  - [WB]: `SELF-CONFIDENCE → CARELESSNESS → CAPTIVITY`
  - The Faith Distinction: Self-effort says, “I’ve got this.” Faith says, “Lord, keep me; I cannot keep myself.”
  - [DQ]: _(Heart check):_ “Where are you most ‘sure’ you won’t fall—speech, appetite, media, money, relationships?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Matthew 6:33
  - Reading/Challenge: Seeking first the kingdom is not time-management advice; it is **lordship**—who rules the morning rules the day.
  - [WB]: `FIRST = PRIORITY + TRUST`
  - How to Receive: Faith begins with surrender of control: “Jesus, I consent to Your rule today; I receive Your life as mine.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What is one ‘not necessarily wrong’ distraction that is draining your spiritual life?”

- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A phone with 2% battery. It’s not “sinning” to use it—but if you never plug in, you will crash at the exact moment you need strength. Prayer/Word is not a hobby; it’s connection.
  - The Practical Landing: Don’t promise God “better behavior”; bring the **stronghold** to Christ: “Lord, this appetite/temper/scrolling owns me—apply Your blood, break its claim, live Your faith in me.”
  - [DQ]: _(Practical):_ “If you were to ‘plug in’ daily, what would you remove or reduce this week to make room?”

#### B. Jesus’ Way: Word + Prayer + Dependence (6-8 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 4:** Luke 5:16
  - Reading/Context: Jesus withdrew to pray—not because He was weak in character, but because He lived by dependence.
  - [WB]: `JESUS: WITHDREW → PRAYED → RETURNED WITH POWER`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Do you withdraw to God before pressure—or only after you’ve fallen?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Mark 1:35
  - Unpacking: Early seeking is a confession: “Father, You are my life.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would be a realistic ‘Mark 1:35’ moment for your household?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Revelation 3:14–22 (allusion from lesson)
  - Unpacking: Laodicea’s disease is self-sufficiency. Christ’s remedy is **gold tried in the fire (faith working by love)** and **eye salve (spiritual discernment)**—both received from Him.
  - [WB]: `LAODICEA = "I am rich" → CHRIST = "Buy of Me"`
  - [EGW]: “Faith is the living power that presses through every barrier, overrides all obstacles, and plants its banner in the heart of the enemy’s camp.” [EGW] (SD 202).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Where do you feel ‘in need of nothing’—and therefore pray least?”

### III. Law and Gospel in Perfect Harmony (Mirror, Cross, and Sanctuary) (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: The lesson’s core: the law defines sin, but cannot cure it. The gospel cures what the law diagnoses—without canceling the law.

#### A. The Law: Diagnosis (Conviction), Not Remedy (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Romans 3:20
  - Reading/Context: “By the law is the knowledge of sin”—law functions like a mirror.
  - [WB]: `LAW = MIRROR (REVEALS) NOT SOAP (CLEANSES)`
  - Sanctuary Link: In the courtyard, the sinner comes **guilty**; the altar shows that sin requires death; the law shows why.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What can a mirror never do for you, even if it’s perfectly clear?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** 1 John 3:4
  - Unpacking: Sin is lawlessness—rebellion against God’s character/government; it breaks relationship.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How does redefining sin as ‘mistakes’ change how we seek a Savior?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Psalm 51:5 (lesson allusion)
  - Unpacking: Sin is not only actions; it’s a bent nature. That’s why behavior-modification can’t finish the work.
  - [WB]: `SINS (acts) ← SIN (nature) → NEED NEW HEART`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Which is harder: stopping a behavior, or changing what you love? Why?”

- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A doctor’s scan reveals a tumor. You don’t hate the scan because it found it; you hate the tumor. The law is the scan—if we shoot the messenger, we die untreated.
  - Practical Landing: Welcome conviction as mercy. Don’t bargain with God. Bring the real disease into the light.

#### B. The Gospel: Remedy (Justification + New Birth) That Establishes the Law (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Romans 3:28
  - Reading/Context: Justification is “without the deeds of the law”—not lawlessness, but **a different basis**: Christ’s obedience and blood.
  - [WB]: `JUSTIFIED = DECLARED RIGHTEOUS IN CHRIST`
  - [DQ]: _(Clarity):_ “If God justifies the ungodly, what exactly is He crediting to them?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Romans 3:31
  - Unpacking: Faith does not void the law; it establishes it—because the law is fulfilled in a life where Christ reigns.
  - [EGW]: “The law reveals to man his sins, but it provides no remedy… The gospel of Christ alone can free him from the condemnation or the defilement of sin… In the new birth the heart is brought into harmony with God, as it is brought into accord with His law.” [EGW] (GC 467–468).
  - [WB]: `FAITH → NEW BIRTH → HARMONY WITH LAW`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Have you ever tried to use the law as a remedy? What did it produce in you—pride, despair, or both?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Philippians 3:9
  - Unpacking: “Not having mine own righteousness… but that which is through the faith of Christ.” This is the **faith of Jesus**—His perfect trust credited to us and reproduced in us.
  - [WB]: `FAITH OF CHRIST → RIGHTEOUSNESS FROM GOD`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “How would your prayer change if you believed Christ’s own faith is offered to you today?”
- **Receiving by Faith (mechanics, plainly stated):**
  - Confess specifically (name the sin/stronghold).
  - Consent to Christ’s ownership (stop defending, stop negotiating).
  - Believe God’s promise and act on it.
  - [EGW]: “You cannot atone for your past sins; you cannot change your heart… But God promises to do all this for you through Christ. You believe that promise… Do not wait to feel… but say, ‘I believe it; it is so… because God has promised.’” [EGW] (SC 51).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Where are you ‘waiting to feel’ forgiven or changed before you will believe?”

### IV. Knowing and Doing: Building on the Rock in the Investigative Judgment Hour (6-8 mins)

- Introduction to Section: The issue isn’t information; it’s foundation. In the judgment-hour message, God is producing a people whose obedience is **faith-made**, not self-made.

#### A. Hearing vs. Doing: Two Foundations (6-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Matthew 7:21
  - Reading/Context: Religious words without surrendered will = deception.
  - [WB]: `"LORD, LORD" ≠ SURRENDERED HEART`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What is a ‘Lord, Lord’ substitute for real surrender in our day?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Matthew 7:24–27
  - Unpacking: Same storm, different foundation. The Rock is Christ—His Word received as life, not as mere doctrine.
  - Sanctuary Link: In the Most Holy Place, God’s law is under the mercy seat—law and mercy meet in Christ’s intercession.
  - [WB]: `LAW (ARK) + MERCY SEAT = CHRIST OUR HIGH PRIEST`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What ‘storm’ reveals your real foundation—criticism, loneliness, financial pressure, temptation?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** John 8:32 and John 7:17 (from EGW notes)
  - Unpacking: Truth liberates when we are willing to do God’s will—light increases with obedience-by-faith.
  - [EGW]: “The means by which we can overcome the wicked one is that by which Christ overcame—the power of the word… Through faith in these promises, everyone may be delivered… Those who will consent to enter into covenant relation with the God of heaven are not left to the power of Satan or to the infirmity of their own nature.” [EGW] (DA 258–259).
  - [WB]: `CONSENT → COVENANT → DELIVERANCE`
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Where do you need to ‘consent’ rather than ‘try’?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two builders: one rushed to decorate; the other dug deep. Storms don’t create the foundation—they reveal it. Trials reveal whether we are resting in Christ’s righteousness or ours.
  - Practical Landing (Monday morning): When temptation hits, don’t debate with it. Turn immediately to Christ’s Word and High Priestly help: “Lord, I consent; live Your life in me now.”

---

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - The **law** names sin and reveals God’s character.
  - The **gospel** provides what law cannot: forgiveness, justification, and power for a new life.
  - The **sanctuary** shows the living reality: Christ applies His blood and writes His law in the heart—**righteousness by faith**.
- The Practical Takeaway: When we stop trying to use law-keeping to save ourselves and start trusting Christ to live in us, obedience becomes the **fruit of union**, not the price of acceptance.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST FOR ME (IMPUTED) + CHRIST IN ME (IMPARTED)`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one specific stronghold to Jesus as High Priest today: confess it, consent to His rule, and believe His promise.
  - [WB]: `“I CONSENT” + “HE IS ABLE”`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop _trying_ to fix in your own strength, and instead bring to Jesus for cleansing and power this week?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - **Bible:** Romans 8:4 (as cited in EGW excerpt): the righteousness of the law is fulfilled in those who walk after the Spirit.
  - [EGW]: “The law is the gospel embodied, and the gospel is the law unfolded.” [EGW] (COL 128).
  - [WB]: `ROM 8:4 — WALK BY THE SPIRIT`
