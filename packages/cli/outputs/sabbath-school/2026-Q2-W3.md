# 2026 Q2 W3 - April 11–17

## Pride Versus Humility

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Pride versus humility in our relationship with God and others  
**Present Truth Theme:** Humility is not self-hatred; it is **self-renounced faith** that consents to be saved, cleansed, and obey by **Christ’s righteousness**—revealed in the sanctuary as Christ’s ongoing ministry **for us** and **in us** (justification, sanctification, final atonement).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from self-exaltation and self-trust to **daily dependence** on Christ’s merits and Christ’s indwelling life—so pride is crucified and Christlike service becomes natural fruit.  
**Key Texts:** Luke 18:13–14; Hebrews 4:14–16; Philippians 2:5–8

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Pride is sneaky—often it sounds like “I’m fine,” “I know,” “I can handle it,” “I’m not like them.” Humility sounds like “Lord, I need You—right now.” Which voice do you hear most in your private thoughts?
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson contrasts pride and humility (Lucifer, Babel, Pharisee/tax collector, disciples, Moses, and supremely Christ).
- Present Truth Bridge: Pride is the religion of **self-salvation**; humility is the posture of **righteousness by faith**—receiving what Christ alone can provide.
  - [WB]: Title: `PRIDE (SELF) vs HUMILITY (CHRIST)`
  - [WB]: `Righteousness by Faith = Receive Christ’s merits + Receive Christ’s life`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement—Christ’s Most Holy Place ministry calls for heart-searching, repentance, and faith that clings to His blood and yields to His law written in the heart.
  - [WB]: `Most Holy Place → heart-searching → cleansing → restored image`
- Roadmap:
  - Section II: Pride’s DNA = “I will” (Lucifer/Babel/Laodicea spirit) vs the gospel posture of faith
  - Section III: Humility that justifies and transforms (publican → cross → mind of Christ)

---

### II. Pride’s Root: “I Will” Versus the Gospel Posture of Faith (16-18 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Pride is not mere confidence; it is **self as savior**—self enthroned. Humility is not weakness; it is **truthfulness** about our need and God’s sufficiency.

#### A. Lucifer, Babel, and Laodicea: Self on the Throne (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Isaiah 14:12–15
  - Reading/Context: The repeated “I will” reveals pride’s essence—self-exaltation into God’s place.
  - [WB]: `Isa 14:13-14 → “I will…” x5`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Pride is a counterfeit atonement—it tries to fix insecurity and emptiness by self-glory instead of by God’s verdict in Christ.
  - [EGW]: “Sin originated in self-seeking. Lucifer… desired to be first in heaven… Lucifer desired God’s power, but not His character.” [EGW] (FLB 68).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What repeated phrase shows the real spirit of pride in Isaiah 14?
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ Where does that same “I will” show up in church life—even in “good” religious activity?
- **Foundation Text 2:** Genesis 11:4, 9
  - Reading/Connection: Babel = “make a name” + “reach heaven”—salvation by human project.
  - [WB]: `Babel: “Make a name” → confusion`
  - The Faith Distinction: Pride builds a tower; faith enters a sanctuary. Pride says, “Let us…”; faith says, “Lord, be merciful…”
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ What was Babel really trying to accomplish spiritually?
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What are modern “towers” we build—reputation, control, ministry identity, perfectionism?
- **Foundation Text 3:** Revelation 3:17–20
  - Reading/Challenge: Laodicea’s pride is self-sufficiency (“I am rich… have need of nothing”) while Christ stands outside offering true riches.
  - [WB]: `Rev 3:17 → “need of nothing” (pride)`
  - [WB]: `Rev 3:20 → Jesus outside/inside`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Pride can be orthodox and active yet Christless; humility opens the door—**Christ in you** is the cure.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What does Laodicea think it has, and what does Jesus say it lacks?
  - [DQ]: _(Heart-check):_ In what area do you tend to live as if you “have need of nothing” (prayerlessness, impatience, reliance on habits, knowledge, position)?
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two singers: one prays, “Lord, help me bless,” the other, “Lord, help me impress.” Same hymn, different religion—**self** or **Christ**.
  - The Practical Landing: This week, watch for “name-making” impulses (defensiveness, boasting, silent resentment, needing the last word). That moment is a call to bring self to the cross.
  - [WB]: `Pride symptoms: defensiveness • comparison • control • contempt`

#### B. The Sanctuary Cure: Confession, Cleansing, and a New Heart (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** 1 John 1:9
  - Reading/Context: Confession is humility in action—agreeing with God about sin, trusting God about cleansing.
  - [WB]: `1 Jn 1:9 → forgive + cleanse`
  - Sanctuary Connection: Forgiveness = blood atonement (altar); cleansing = priestly application of the blood to the life (ongoing ministry).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What two actions does God promise in 1 John 1:9?
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ Why do we often want forgiveness but resist cleansing (the removal of the cherished sin)?
- **Foundation Text 5:** Psalm 51:10–12
  - Reading/Connection: Humility asks for what only God can do—**creation power** in the heart.
  - [WB]: `Ps 51:10 → “Create in me…”`
  - The Faith Distinction: Pride reforms behavior to preserve ego; faith surrenders the heart to be recreated.
  - [EGW]: “Pride and self-love resist the Spirit of God… In humility… ‘Create in me a clean heart’… we are ‘born again’… by the power of Christ.” [EGW] (FLB 137).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What’s one area where you need “create power,” not “try-harder power”?
- **Foundation Text 6:** Hebrews 4:14–16
  - Reading/Challenge: Our High Priest invites boldness—not bold self-confidence, but bold faith in His merits.
  - [WB]: `Heb 4:16 → come → mercy → grace → help`
  - How to Receive (practical mechanics of faith):
    - Name the sin plainly (no excuses)
    - Consent to God’s verdict (He is right)
    - Ask for mercy and cleansing
    - Thank Him for grace **before** feelings change (faith takes God at His word)
  - [DQ]: _(Practical):_ What does it look like to “come to the throne” at the exact moment pride flares (tone, facial expression, quick prayer, silence)?
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Pride is clenched fists; faith is open hands. God doesn’t fill what I refuse to open.
  - Monday morning practice: When corrected, pause and pray silently: “Jesus, I receive Your humility as my life right now; I surrender my image.”

### III. Humility That Justifies and Transforms: The Faith of Jesus Living in Us (16-18 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Humility is the doorway into justification and the pathway of sanctification. God’s goal is not merely “nicer people,” but the **image of Christ** reproduced—commandment-keeping as fruit, not self-made performance.

#### A. The Publican’s Prayer: Justification by Faith, Not Religious Performance (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Luke 18:9–14
  - Reading/Context: Two worshipers; one trusts his record, the other trusts God’s mercy.
  - [WB]: Two columns: `Pharisee: “I…”` | `Publican: “God…”`
  - Unpacking the Truth: The publican asks for propitiation—mercy grounded in sacrifice. He goes home justified because he renounced self-trust.
  - [EGW]: “It is only he who knows himself to be a sinner that Christ can save.” [EGW] (COL 158).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What does each man base his confidence on?
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ Which prayer sounds more like yours when you fail—excuses/bargaining, or “be merciful to me”?
- **Foundation Text 2:** Romans 3:23–26
  - Reading/Connection: Justification is God’s legal verdict based on Christ’s blood—humility accepts the verdict; pride argues.
  - [WB]: `Rom 3:24-26 → justified → blood → just & justifier`
  - Sanctuary Link: The cross (altar) is the only ground; the High Priest applies it (living intercession).
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ Why must justification be grounded outside of us—in Christ alone?
- **Foundation Text 3:** Galatians 2:20–21
  - Reading/Challenge: The justified life continues the same way it began—Christ living in me; no mixing of grace with self.
  - [WB]: `Gal 2:20 → “Christ liveth in me”`
  - The Faith of Jesus: Not merely my weak faith reaching up, but Christ’s own faithful life reproduced in the believer.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ Where are you tempted to “add law” as a way of earning (proving you’re serious) instead of trusting Christ to live obedience in you?
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A drowning man cannot argue about technique; he must stop thrashing and let the rescuer carry him. Faith is consenting to be carried.
  - Practical Landing: Confess specifically (not vaguely), then thank God for a clean standing in Christ; treat others that day as fellow sinners under mercy, not as rivals.

#### B. The Mind of Christ: Commandment Life as the Fruit of Indwelling Faith (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Philippians 2:3–8
  - Reading/Context: “Let this mind be in you…”—humility is Christ’s mindset implanted, not human niceness.
  - [WB]: `Phil 2 → “mind of Christ” → downward path`
  - Imparted Righteousness: Christ’s self-emptying becomes ours by the Spirit—He reproduces His character where pride once ruled.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ What part of Christ’s “downward path” is hardest for you—being overlooked, misunderstood, or serving when tired?
- **Foundation Text 5:** Luke 22:24–27
  - Reading/Connection: Greatness in Christ’s kingdom = serving; pride competes, humility serves.
  - [WB]: `Luke 22:27 → “I am among you as One who serves”`
  - [EGW]: “There is nothing so offensive to God or so dangerous to the human soul as pride and self-sufficiency.” [EGW] (COL 154).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What definition of greatness does Jesus give?
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ Where do you most “compete” (even subtly)—family, church roles, knowledge, spirituality?
- **Foundation Text 6:** Exodus 20:8–11; Hebrews 10:19–22; Revelation 14:12
  - Reading/Challenge:
    - Sabbath is the weekly cure for pride—resting from our works, trusting the Creator/Redeemer.
    - The sanctuary invitation is to draw near by Christ’s blood with a true heart.
    - The remnant description unites **commandments of God** with the **faith of Jesus**.
  - [WB]: `Sabbath = rest from self`
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 → Commandments + Faith of Jesus`
  - Commandments as Fruit: Obedience is faith working by love; not self-exaltation, but Christ’s life expressed in the will.
  - [DQ]: _(Deeper):_ How does Sabbath-keeping expose hidden pride (anxiety, control, identity in productivity)?
  - [DQ]: _(Practical):_ What would “the faith of Jesus” look like in you during one specific test this week (traffic, disrespect, loneliness, temptation online)?
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two “obediences”: one is a paycheck (to earn), the other is a love letter (because loved). Pride obeys to be seen; faith obeys because Christ is within.
  - Monday morning practice: Before a hard conversation, pray: “Jesus, live Your Philippians 2 humility in me; I choose to serve, not win.”

---

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Pride = self on the throne; it blocks grace and makes religion a tower of Babel.
  - Humility = coming as the publican, trusting Christ’s blood, receiving cleansing, and letting Christ’s mind live in us.
  - The sanctuary shows the process: sacrifice for guilt, priestly ministry for cleansing, final atonement for full restoration—received by faith.
- The Practical Takeaway: When you stop defending self and start beholding Christ, **His righteousness becomes yours**—peace for the conscience and power for a changed life.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST’S MERITS (imputed) + CHRIST’S LIFE (imparted)`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one concrete pride-symptom to Jesus as High Priest: defensiveness, comparison, control, contempt, craving recognition.
  - Pray (quietly, in your own words): “Lord, be merciful to me… cleanse me… put the mind of Christ in me.”
  - [WB]: `“Look up… it is safe to look up” (OHC 27)`
  - _(Final Question):_ What is the one area you will stop managing for your image, and instead bring to Christ for cleansing this week?
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - **Bible promise:** Philippians 1:6
  - [EGW]: “When temptations assail you… look, O look, to where with the eye of faith you last saw the light… His grace is sufficient to subdue sin.” [EGW] (AG 259).
  - [WB]: `Phil 1:6` / `Luke 14:11` / `“GRACE FLOWS DOWN”`
