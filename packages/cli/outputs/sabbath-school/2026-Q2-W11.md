# 2026 Q2 W11 - June 6–12

## Setbacks

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Setbacks—storms, delays, disappointments, sickness, and suffering that tempt us to doubt God’s love.  
**Present Truth Theme:** Setbacks expose our **self-trust** and drive us to Christ’s **Most Holy Place ministry**, where His righteousness is **imputed and imparted**—producing the **faith of Jesus** that keeps the commandments in trial.  
**Central Focus:** Moving from “Lord, do You not care?” to “My Redeemer lives”—learning to **receive** Christ’s peace, purity, and perseverance **by faith** in our daily battles.  
**Key Texts:** Hebrews 4:14–16; Romans 5:3–5; Revelation 14:12

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: When you hit a setback (health, money, family, spiritual failure), what’s your first inner sentence?
  - “I must fix this.”
  - “God is against me.”
  - “God doesn’t care.”
- Lesson Connection: This week traces setbacks through the storm (Mark 4), the sick woman (Mark 5), Job, Emmaus, and Romans 8.
- Present Truth Bridge: Setbacks are not just “hard seasons”—they reveal where we seek righteousness: **self**, or **Christ our High Priest**.
  - [WB]: Title: `SETBACKS → SELF? or SAVIOR?`
  - [WB]: Add: `Sanctuary = How God saves in real time`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement—Christ is finishing His work in the Most Holy Place; setbacks are often the crucible where self is stripped away and **faith becomes real**.
  - [WB]: `Most Holy Place = Present ministry (Heb 8:1-2)`
- Roadmap:
  - Storms: peace by surrender (Mark 4; John 14; Isa 26)
  - The touch of faith: receiving virtue, not casual religion (Mark 5; Rom 3; Heb 12)
  - Job and judgment: faith “without explanation,” Living Redeemer, throne of grace (Job 13; 19; Heb 4)
  - Emmaus: Christ in all Scripture; setbacks turned into mission endurance (Luke 24; Rom 8; Rev 14)

### II. The Storm Reveals the Heart: From “Do You Not Care?” to Christ’s Peace (14-16 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Setbacks often begin as a “storm.” The key issue is not wind and waves—it’s **what we believe about Christ’s heart** and **where we look for control**.

#### A. Jesus asleep at the stern: faith when God seems inactive (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Mark 4:35–41
  - Reading/Context: Jesus initiates the crossing; the storm is real; the disciples accuse His character: “Do You not care?”
  - [WB]: `Mark 4:38 = "Do You not care?"`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - The crisis revealed their theology: they believed in Jesus’ power, but doubted His love.
    - The storm didn’t mean Jesus wasn’t “driving the boat”—He was still Lord, even when seemingly silent.
    - Sanctuary bridge: true peace starts at the **altar**—reconciliation with God—before winds change (Romans 5:1).
  - [WB]: `Peace = reconciled (Rom 5:1)`
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ What exactly do the disciples accuse Jesus of in Mark 4:38—powerlessness, or lack of care?
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ In your last setback, what did you assume about God’s heart?
- **Foundation Text 2:** John 14:27
  - Reading/Connection: Jesus’ legacy is not explanations, but **His peace**—the same peace He had in conflict.
  - [WB]: `John 14:27 = "My peace"`
  - The Faith Distinction:
    - Self-effort says: “Peace comes when circumstances calm.”
    - Faith says: “Peace comes when the soul is reconciled and surrendered to Christ’s keeping.”
  - [EGW]: “Those who take Christ at His word, and surrender their souls to His keeping, their lives to His ordering, will find peace and quietude.” [EGW] (RC 278.6).
  - [WB]: `Surrender → Peace (RC 278.6)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ What is the difference between asking Jesus to change the storm and surrendering your life to His ordering?
- **Foundation Text 3 (Power/Promise):** Isaiah 26:3
  - Reading/Challenge: Perfect peace is connected to the mind being “stayed” on God—**fixation**, not occasional glance.
  - [WB]: `Isa 26:3 = Mind stayed → Peace`
  - How to Receive (practical mechanics of faith):
    - Name the fear, confess the accusation against God’s character, claim a promise, and thank Him before you feel relief.
    - Choose what your mind will “stay” on: Christ’s word, not the waves (Matthew 14:30–31 principle).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What “accusation thought” do you most often believe in a storm (about God or about yourself)?
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Like a child in the back seat panicking because they can’t see the road, forgetting the parent is driving. The issue isn’t the child’s skill—it’s trust in the driver.
  - The Practical Landing (Monday morning):
    - When anxiety spikes: don’t start “rowing harder” spiritually; go to the High Priest by prayer: “Lord, rule my mind—keep me stayed on You.”
  - [DQ]: _(Experience):_ When has a storm exposed that you were relying on control more than Christ?

#### B. Storms as sanctification: Christ’s peace quiets inward passions (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** 2 Corinthians 5:7
  - Unpacking: In setbacks, sight argues “God has left me.” Faith walks on God’s revealed character.
  - [WB]: `Sight vs Faith (2 Cor 5:7)`
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ What does it look like to “walk by faith” during a setback that does not change?
- **Foundation Text 5:** Romans 5:3–5
  - Unpacking:
    - Tribulation → perseverance → character → hope
    - The engine is not willpower; it is **love poured out by the Holy Spirit** (imparted righteousness in lived form).
    - Sanctuary bridge: Holy Place reality—Christ supplies oil (Spirit), bread (Word), and intercession (incense) for daily endurance.
  - [WB]: `Rom 5:3-5 chain`
  - [WB]: `Holy Place help: Word + Spirit + Intercession`
  - [EGW]: “Sin has destroyed our peace… The masterful passions of the heart no human power can control. We are as helpless here as were the disciples to quiet the raging storm.” [EGW] (RC 278.8).
  - [DQ]: _(Heart check):_ Where are you trying to control “masterful passions” by human power rather than Christ’s indwelling grace?
  - [DQ]: _(Simple):_ According to Romans 5:5, who pours love into the heart—you, or God?
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two approaches to anger: (1) “count to ten” while boiling inside; (2) go to Christ, confess, receive His meekness, and the heat actually lowers.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A thermostat vs a thermometer—self-effort measures the heat; Christ changes the climate within.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What passion is your “storm” right now—anger, appetite, lust, discouragement, resentment—and what would surrender look like today?

### III. The Touch of Faith: Receiving Christ’s Righteousness, Not Casual Religion (14-16 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Many “press” around Jesus (religion, information, attendance). But only faith **draws virtue**—the sanctuary word is **appropriation** (personally taking hold of Christ’s merits).

#### A. The woman with the issue of blood: faith that reaches (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Mark 5:25–34
  - Reading/Context: She is worse, broke, and weak—perfect picture of human inability.
  - [WB]: `Mark 5:34 = "Your faith has made you well"`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - The garment had no magic; faith connected her to Christ’s life.
    - This is justification in miniature: come empty, receive full; defiled, made clean (sanctuary cleansing logic).
  - [EGW]: “The Saviour could distinguish the touch of faith from the casual contact of the careless throng.” [EGW] (DA 344.2).
  - [WB]: `Touch of faith ≠ casual contact (DA 344.2)`
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ In what ways can we “press around Jesus” today without actually touching by faith?
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ What does a “casual contact” week of religion look like for you?
- **Foundation Text 2:** Matthew 11:28–29
  - Unpacking: Christ calls the weary to come—not to perform. The yoke is union, not burden.
  - [WB]: `Come → Take → Learn (Matt 11:28-29)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ What is the difference between “coming to Jesus” and “coming to religious duties”?
- **Foundation Text 3 (Power/Promise):** Hebrews 12:2
  - Unpacking: He is the Author/Finisher—faith begins and ends in Christ, not self.
  - [WB]: `Heb 12:2 = Author & Finisher`
  - How to Receive:
    - Faith is not trying to believe harder; it is **looking** harder—fixing attention on Christ’s sufficiency.
    - Practical: “Lord, I reach for You right now. I accept Your verdict over me (justified) and Your life within me (sanctified).”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What would change this week if you treated Christ as the Finisher, not merely the Helper?

#### B. “Without cause” grace: the Gospel in setbacks produces commandment-keeping faith (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Romans 3:24
  - Unpacking: Justified **freely**—grace is unearned; it ends boasting and self-hate.
  - [WB]: `Justified freely (Rom 3:24)`
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ Why is “freely” so hard for the human heart to accept?
- **Foundation Text 5:** Titus 3:5–7
  - Unpacking:
    - Not by works—by mercy; regeneration and renewing by the Holy Spirit.
    - This is the cleansing “laver” reality applied: new birth is God’s act, received by faith.
  - [WB]: `Not by works → by mercy (Titus 3:5)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ Where do you still relate to God “as wages,” not “as mercy”?
- **Foundation Text 6:** Revelation 14:12
  - Unpacking:
    - End-time people: commandments of God + faith of Jesus.
    - The commandments are the **fruit** of union with Christ; setbacks reveal whether we have **His faith** or merely our grit.
    - Sanctuary bridge: Most Holy Place—law in the ark, mercy seat above it—obedience only stands in mercy, through Christ’s intercession.
  - [WB]: `Ark: Law + Mercy Seat`
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 = Fruit, not root`
  - [EGW]: “We must feel our continual dependence upon God… and then depend wholly upon Him who has conquered the foe in our behalf.” [EGW] (TMK 304.2).
  - [DQ]: _(Personal honesty):_ In your present setback, what does “depend wholly” look like—what would you stop doing in self-strength?
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What commandment-area is most tested by your setback (Sabbath trust, truthfulness, purity, patience, coveting/fear)?
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A drowning person cannot “assist” the lifeguard—rescue begins when they stop fighting and let themselves be carried.
  - The Practical Landing:
    - In relapse/backsliding: don’t promise God improvement; confess, claim Christ’s merits, and receive His life for obedience.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What is one “casual touch” habit (devotions, sermons, discussions) that needs to become a deliberate grasp of Christ by faith?

### IV. Job and Emmaus: Setbacks, Judgment, and the Living Redeemer (8-10 mins) [*]

- Introduction to Section: The deepest setbacks are prolonged darkness. Job and Emmaus teach us: grace when we cannot explain, and Scripture that reveals Christ in the sanctuary of salvation history.

#### A. Job: faith that pierces the gloom and rests in the Redeemer (4-5 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Job 13:15
  - Unpacking: Faith is not denial; it is trust in God’s character when feelings collapse.
  - [WB]: `Job 13:15 = Trust without explanation`
  - [EGW]: “When depression settles upon the soul, it is no evidence that God has changed… Your faith must pierce the gloom.” [EGW] (OHC 324.2).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ What “cloud” are you mistaking as God changing toward you?
- **Foundation Text 2:** Job 19:25–27
  - Unpacking:
    - Job’s anchor is not circumstances but **a living Redeemer** and the resurrection horizon.
    - Judgment hope: not God hunting reasons to reject, but Christ revealing who has trusted His merits and allowed His work to cleanse the life.
  - [WB]: `My Redeemer lives`
  - [DQ]: _(Hope):_ How does resurrection hope change the way you interpret current pain?
- **Foundation Text 3:** Hebrews 4:14–16
  - Unpacking: Our High Priest invites bold coming for mercy and grace **in time of need**—this is sanctuary help for setbacks.
  - [WB]: `Heb 4:16 = "come… obtain… help"`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What would it look like to “come boldly” today—specific sin, specific wound, specific request?
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A court case where the Judge is also your Advocate and Elder Brother—same courtroom, totally different fear.
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ Do you avoid God in setback because you see Him as Prosecutor rather than High Priest?

#### B. Emmaus: Christ opens Scripture, and setbacks become mission (4-5 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Luke 24:25–27
  - Unpacking: Jesus interprets the setback through Scripture—showing suffering as part of redemption.
  - [WB]: `Christ in all Scripture (Luke 24)`
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ When discouraged, do you interpret your life through feelings—or through Scripture?
- **Foundation Text 5:** Luke 24:31–32
  - Unpacking: When Christ is seen in the Word, hearts burn; despair turns into movement.
  - [WB]: `Opened Word → burning heart`
  - [DQ]: _(Experience):_ What kind of Bible study actually warms your heart—data only, or Christ revealed?
- **Foundation Text 6:** Romans 8:28
  - Unpacking: “All things” work for good—not that all things are good, but God overrules for salvation.
  - [WB]: `Not all good; all overruled`
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ What “thing” in your life are you currently excluding from Romans 8:28?
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two people read the same medical report: one reads it as a sentence; the other as a summons to the Throne of Grace—same report, different High Priest.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A detour sign—what feels like “blocked” may be God routing you past self-reliance.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ What is one setback that could become a testimony if you let Christ interpret it through Scripture?

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Storms expose doubts about God’s care (Mark 4), but Christ offers **His peace** received by surrender (John 14; Isa 26).
  - Healing and victory come by the **touch of faith**, not casual religion (Mark 5; Heb 12).
  - Job teaches faith that pierces gloom and anchors in the Living Redeemer, coming boldly to the High Priest (Job 19; Heb 4).
  - Emmaus teaches Christ-centered Scripture that reframes setbacks into redemption history and mission (Luke 24; Rom 8).
- The Practical Takeaway:
  - Setbacks are not permission to strive harder in self, but an invitation to **come to the High Priest** and receive **Christ’s righteousness**—His peace, His purity, His perseverance—by faith.
  - [WB]: `STOP: self-saving → START: sanctuary-coming (Heb 4:16)`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one specific setback and one specific sin-response to Jesus today: fear, anger, discouragement, appetite, resentment, unbelief.
  - Practice a simple “sanctuary-coming” habit this week:
    - Confess (altar) → Ask cleansing (laver) → Claim promise (Holy Place bread/light) → Trust intercession (incense) → Rest in mercy over law (Most Holy Place).
  - [WB]: `Altar → Laver → Holy Place → Most Holy Place`
  - [WB]: `Ask → Trust → Thank (Rom 5:5; Heb 4:16)`
  - _(Final Question):_ What is the one area you will stop managing in self-strength and deliberately “touch Jesus” for this week?
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - The final generation message is not human grit, but **commandments kept through the faith of Jesus** (Rev 14:12)—a people who have learned, in storms, to live by His indwelling righteousness.
  - [EGW]: “From the depths of discouragement and despondency Job rose to the heights of implicit trust… ‘Though He slay me, yet will I trust in Him.’” [EGW] (PP 163.2).
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 + Job 19:25 = End-time faith`
