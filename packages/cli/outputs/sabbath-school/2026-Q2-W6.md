# 2026 Q2 W6 - May 2–8

## Prayer Warriors

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Learning prayer lessons from Daniel, Enoch, and Moses—faithful, habitual, intercessory prayer.  
**Present Truth Theme:** Prayer is faith’s hand receiving Christ’s merits in the heavenly sanctuary—bringing our need to our High Priest so His righteousness is **imputed and imparted**, producing commandments-keeping as fruit (“the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus”).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from self-reliance (trying to be spiritual) to **sanctuary-faith** (coming to Christ as High Priest) so His life becomes our obedience, courage, and intercession.  
**Key Texts:** Hebrews 4:14–16; Daniel 6:10; Revelation 14:12.

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Many of us pray most when we’re in trouble. But what if prayer is not mainly a crisis tool—what if it’s **how we receive Christ’s life** for Monday morning?
  - [DQ]: _(Simple):_ “When do you most naturally pray—crisis, routine, or joy?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson highlights “prayer warriors”—Daniel (crisis + gratitude), Enoch (walk with God), Moses (intercession).
- Present Truth Bridge: Prayer is not earning God’s help; it is **coming to the sanctuary by faith**—to receive what Christ has already provided.
  - [WB]: Title: `PRAYER WARRIORS = FAITH RECEIVING CHRIST`
  - [WB]: Mini-map: `Altar → Laver → Holy Place → Most Holy Place (High Priest)`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement—Christ ministering in the Most Holy Place. Prayer is how we cooperate with His cleansing, not by willpower but by faith.
  - [WB]: `Heb 4:16 — "come boldly... obtain mercy"`
- Roadmap:
  - Daniel 2: prayer that confesses God’s sovereignty and receives wisdom.
  - Daniel 6: prayer that refuses counterfeit worship and keeps allegiance.
  - Enoch/Moses: prayer as walking communion and priestly intercession.

---

### II. Daniel’s Prayer in Crisis: Receiving Wisdom, Not Proving Worth (15-18 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Daniel 2 shows prayer as **dependence**—God reveals what man cannot produce. This is righteousness by faith in practice: **God provides; we receive.**

#### A. The “Secret” and the Sanctuary Principle: Mercy Comes Down (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Daniel 2:18–19
  - Reading/Context: Death decree—no human solution. Daniel asks for “mercies from the God of heaven.”
  - [WB]: `Dan 2:18 — "seek mercies" (not merit)`
  - Unpacking the Truth: This is the gospel pattern—when there is no answer in us, we go outside ourselves to God’s mercy. In sanctuary language: we don’t bring our own righteousness; we bring our need to the Substitute and Priest.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What does Daniel ask for—skills, time, or **mercy**?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Hebrews 4:14–16
  - Reading/Connection: We have a High Priest; therefore we “come boldly” to obtain mercy and grace “to help in time of need.”
  - [WB]: Diagram: `Need → Throne of Grace → Mercy + Grace → Help`
  - The Faith Distinction: Self-effort says, “Let me fix myself, then I’ll pray.” Faith says, “Because I cannot, I come now—based on Christ’s merits.”
  - [EGW]: “Prayer is the opening of the heart to God as to a friend… Prayer does not bring God down to us, but brings us up to Him.” (SC 93–94).
  - [WB]: `Prayer lifts me to Him (not Him down to me)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you delay prayer until you feel ‘good enough’?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** John 16:23–24
  - Reading/Challenge: Asking “in My name” is not a phrase; it is relying on Christ’s worth, not ours—and expecting to receive.
  - [WB]: `IN JESUS’ NAME = on His merits`
  - How to Receive: Confess inability; ask specifically; trust His promise; act on light given.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would change if you prayed as if Christ’s name is truly sufficient?”

- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Like trying to pay a massive debt with pocket change—self-effort prays to “top up” what I lack; faith prays because **Another has paid**, and I’m coming to receive the benefits.
  - The Practical Landing: Monday morning temptation/discouragement—don’t promise God you’ll do better; come to the High Priest for “mercy” (for guilt) and “grace” (for power).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “In your experience, which is harder—receiving mercy for guilt, or receiving grace for change?”

#### B. Gratitude That Guards From Pride: God Gives “Wisdom and Power” (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Daniel 2:20–23
  - Reading/Context: Daniel blesses God immediately after the answer.
  - [WB]: `Dan 2:21 — "He changes times... removes kings"`
  - Unpacking the Truth: True prayer produces worship—God is sovereign; I am dependent. This is the opposite of Babylon’s religion (human technique).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What attributes of God does Daniel praise—control, wisdom, or Daniel’s intelligence?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Daniel 2:30
  - Reading/Connection: “Not because of any wisdom that I have…”
  - [WB]: `NOT ME → ALL HIM`
  - Commandments Connection: Obedience that springs from prayer is not self-exaltation (“I’m faithful like Daniel”); it is Christ-exaltation (“God delivered”).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When God answers prayer, what is your natural next step—testimony, pride, or forgetfulness?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** James 1:5–6
  - Reading/Challenge: Ask for wisdom in faith, not doubting—faith receives and then acts.
  - [WB]: `Ask → Believe → Receive → Act`
  - [EGW]: “The assurance is broad and unlimited… When our prayers seem not to be answered, we are to cling to the promise… ‘Ask, and it shall be given you.’” (SC 96).
  - [WB]: `"cling to the promise" (SC 96)`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where are you tempted to interpret delay as rejection, instead of training in faith?”
- [SN]: _Illustration:_ A child asks a wise father for something harmful; the father’s “no” is still love. Faith keeps trusting the Father’s character.
- [DQ]: _(Heart check):_ “What do your prayers reveal about the kind of Father you think God is?”

### III. Daniel 6, Enoch, and Moses: Prayer as Allegiance, Communion, and Intercession (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Daniel’s praying is not only crisis-based; it’s **covenant allegiance**. Enoch shows prayer as a walk. Moses shows prayer as **priestly intercession**—all pointing to Christ’s faith living in us.

#### A. Prayer as Worship Loyalty: “Toward Jerusalem” and the Sanctuary Hope (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Daniel 6:10–11
  - Reading/Context: Law forbids prayer; Daniel continues—kneeling, thanksgiving, supplication, toward Jerusalem.
  - [WB]: `Dan 6:10 — Habit + Humility + Hope`
  - Sanctuary Connection: Facing Jerusalem aligns prayer with God’s appointed atonement and priestly ministry—faith looking to Christ’s righteousness, not Babylon’s decrees.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What details show Daniel’s prayer is worship—not mere habit?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Revelation 14:12
  - Reading/Connection: End-time saints: “commandments of God” + “faith of Jesus.” Daniel is a living preview: loyalty to God over the state.
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 = Commandments + Faith of Jesus`
  - The Faith of Jesus: Not mere human grit; it is Christ’s steadfast trust in the Father reproduced in the believer by the Spirit.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What ‘decree’ pressures you today—social, family, workplace—to silence prayer or compromise?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Matthew 6:6
  - Reading/Challenge: Secret prayer as real communion, not performance.
  - [WB]: `Secret place → real power`
  - [EGW]: “We should… not neglect secret prayer, for this is the life of the soul… Calmly, yet fervently, will it reach out after God.” (SC 98–99).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What competes most with secret prayer—busyness, distraction, or unbelief?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two kinds of “prayer”: one is like a public speech to protect reputation; the other is like turning the key in a generator room—unseen, but it powers the whole house.
  - [WB]: `Performance prayer vs. Communion prayer`
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “If someone followed you for a week, what would they conclude your ‘real power source’ is?”

#### B. Enoch’s Walk: Prayer Without Ceasing as Abiding Faith (4-5 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 4:** Genesis 5:22–24
  - Reading/Context: “walked with God… and he was not.”
  - [WB]: `Walked with God = daily dependence`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Walking implies agreement (Amos 3:3). This is sanctification by faith—Christ’s presence shaping choices from the inside.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What does ‘walked’ suggest that ‘visited’ does not?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** 1 Thessalonians 5:17–18
  - Reading/Connection: “Pray without ceasing… in every thing give thanks”—the attitude of continual consent and gratitude.
  - [WB]: `Ceaseless prayer = continual consent`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What is one routine moment (car, sink, workplace doorway) you could turn into an ‘uplifted heart’ moment?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** John 15:4–5
  - Reading/Challenge: Abiding is the method of fruit-bearing; “without Me ye can do nothing.”
  - [WB]: `ABIDE → FRUIT (not strain)`
  - [EGW]: “Pray… as you go about your daily labor let your heart be often uplifted… Satan cannot overcome him whose heart is thus stayed upon God.” (SC 98–99).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you feel you ‘must’ produce fruit—when Jesus says it comes from abiding?”
- [SN]: _Illustration:_ Like a phone staying connected to the network—if it’s on airplane mode, it may look fine, but it cannot receive what it needs. Prayer is staying connected.

#### C. Moses’ Intercession: Love That Reflects the High Priest (4-5 mins)

- **Foundation Text 7:** Exodus 32:31–32
  - Reading/Context: Golden calf—Moses pleads for sinners.
  - [WB]: `Intercession = carrying others to God`
  - Sanctuary Connection: Intercession is priestly work—pointing to Christ who bears our sin and pleads His merits.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Do you mainly pray about your problems—or do you carry people by name?”
- **Foundation Text 8:** Exodus 33:15–18
  - Reading/Connection: “If Thy presence go not… show me Thy glory.”
  - [WB]: `Presence → Glory = Character`
  - Commandments Connection: God’s “glory” is His character (Exod. 34:6–7). Prayer is how that character is received—so commandments become **transcript lived out**, not a list attempted.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “If God answered one prayer this week—‘show me Thy glory’—what would change in your home?”
- **Foundation Text 9:** Colossians 3:13 (compare Matthew 5:44 if time)
  - Reading/Challenge: Forgiveness and love for enemies—humanly impossible; divinely imparted.
  - [WB]: `Impossible command → promised life in Christ`
  - [EGW]: “Jesus… became a suppliant… seeking… fresh supplies of strength… If the Saviour… felt the need of prayer, how much more… sinful mortals.” (SC 93–94).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Who is hardest for you to pray for—and what does that reveal about your need of Christ’s heart?”
- [SN]: _Illustration:_ Intercession is like carrying someone to the hospital when they can’t walk—prayer brings them to the only One who can heal.
- [WB]: `High Priest mindset: "Father, forgive..."`

---

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Daniel 2: prayer receives mercy/wisdom—God’s solution comes down.
  - Daniel 6: prayer is loyalty—faith of Jesus under pressure.
  - Enoch: prayer is abiding communion—walking with God.
  - Moses: prayer is priestly intercession—reflecting God’s forgiving character.
- The Practical Takeaway: When we pray by faith, we stop trying to produce righteousness and instead **receive Christ’s righteousness**—mercy for guilt and grace for change—so obedience becomes fruit.
  - [WB]: `MERCY (forgives) + GRACE (empowers) = OBEDIENCE (fruit)`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one named sin/struggle to Jesus as High Priest (Heb. 4:16): confess, ask, trust, and step forward in His promise.
  - [WB]: `COME BOLDLY (Heb 4:16) — because HE is worthy`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop trying to conquer in your own strength, and instead bring to Christ daily—morning, midday, evening?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - We are in the closing work; God is forming a people with “the faith of Jesus.” Prayer is how that faith becomes ours in experience.
  - [EGW]: “We may keep so near to God that in every unexpected trial our thoughts will turn to Him as naturally as the flower turns to the sun.” (SC 99–100).
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12` / `LOOKING UNTO JESUS (Heb 12:2)` / `"HE IS ABLE"`
