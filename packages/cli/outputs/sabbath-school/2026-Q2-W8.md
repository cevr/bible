# 2026 Q2 W8 - May 16–22

## Having Faith

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Faith—dealing with doubt, recognizing strong faith, and understanding “the faith of Jesus”  
**Present Truth Theme:** Faith is trusting Christ’s **living High-Priestly ministry** to give us His righteousness—**imputed** (pardon) and **imparted** (power)—so the commandments become the **fruit** of His life in us (Rev. 14:12).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from sign-seeking and self-effort into a daily, practical experience of **Christ’s faith living in us**—especially in discouragement, conflict, and besetting sin.  
**Key Texts:** Hebrews 10:19-23; Galatians 2:20; Revelation 14:12

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: When you feel spiritually “offline” (no feeling, no answers, no sign), what do you do—try harder, look for proof, or **draw near** by faith?
  - [DQ]: _(Simple):_ “What usually shakes your faith—suffering, unanswered prayer, temptation, or conflict with people?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson is “Having Faith”—doubt, strong faith, and “the faith of Jesus.”
- Present Truth Bridge: Faith is not a mood and not a merit badge—faith is the hand that takes hold of **Christ our High Priest**, who alone provides righteousness and power to obey.
  - [WB]: Title: `HAVING FAITH = DRAWING NEAR TO CHRIST (High Priest)`
- Why This Matters Now: Revelation’s last generation is described as those who keep “the commandments of God” and have “the faith of Jesus” (Rev. 14:12)—this is **sanctuary language**: a people living by Christ’s intercession, not their own strength.
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 = Commandments + Faith of Jesus`
- Roadmap:
  - Signs vs. surrender (Mark 8; John 20; Heb. 10)
  - What Jesus calls “great faith” (Matt. 15; Luke 7)
  - Justification and indwelling life (Rom. 3; Gal. 2)
  - “Faith of Jesus” in Gethsemane to our daily life (Matt. 26; Rev. 14)

---

### II. Faith that refuses sign-seeking and chooses surrender (15-18 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Unbelief often hides under a respectable cloak: “If God would just prove it, then I’d obey.” Jesus exposes that spirit—and offers something deeper: a heart changed by His ministry.

#### A. Signs, skepticism, and the need for spiritual renovation (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Mark 8:11, 12
  - Reading/Context: Religious leaders demand a sign after many evidences.
  - [WB]: `Mark 8:11-12 — “seeking a sign” / Jesus “sighs”`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Sign-seeking is often a subtle way to keep control—“God, meet my terms.” Faith yields to God’s terms.
  - [EGW]: “These signs were not what the Jews needed… What they needed was… **spiritual renovation**.” [EGW] (DA 406).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What was the Pharisees’ request, and what was Jesus’ emotional response?”
  - [DQ]: _(Heart-check):_ “Where do I demand ‘proof’ from God before I will trust Him in an area He already spoke clearly?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** John 20:29
  - Reading/Connection: Blessed are those who believe without seeing.
  - [WB]: `John 20:29 — “not seen… yet believed”`
  - The Faith Distinction: Faith is not anti-evidence; it is **submission** to what God has already shown (Scripture, providence, the Cross, the sanctuary).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What’s the difference between honest questions and testing God?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A person says, “Lord, if You fix my marriage / heal me / give me a job, then I’ll be faithful.” That is a bargain, not surrender. Faith says, “Because You gave Your Son, I surrender now—whether the outcome is immediate or not.”
  - The Practical Landing: In temptation or discouragement, stop negotiating with God. Bring the matter to Christ as High Priest: “Lord, I choose to trust Your word and Your character **before** I see change.”
  - [WB]: `Bargain faith vs. surrender faith`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What bargain do people commonly make with God—what’s a ‘sign’ you’ve asked for?”

#### B. Faith anchored in Christ’s word—safe from last-day counterfeit miracles (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 3:** Deuteronomy 8:2, 3
  - Reading/Context: God educates faith by remembrance and by His Word.
  - [WB]: `Deut 8:3 — “man lives… by every word”`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Faith grows by feeding on God’s Word, not on sensational proofs.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “Why would God allow hunger/testing to train His people?”
- **Foundation Text 4:** Matthew 24:24
  - Reading/Connection: False christs and signs that could deceive.
  - [WB]: `Matt 24:24 — miracles ≠ truth`
  - End-time clarity: The safest people are those living by Scripture and Christ’s intercession, not by thrill, fear, or spectacle.
  - [EGW]: “Satan… will perform actual miracles… in the last days…” [EGW] (Mar 207).
  - [DQ]: _(Practical):_ “What is more dangerous: no miracles, or miracles without Bible truth?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Hebrews 10:23
  - Reading/Challenge: Hold fast the confession—He is faithful.
  - [WB]: `Heb 10:23 — “He is faithful”`
  - Sanctuary connection: Our confidence is not in our grip but in **His hold**—the faithful High Priest.
  - [EGW]: “Nothing is… more invincible, than the soul that feels its nothingness and relies wholly on the merits of the Saviour.” [EGW] (MH 182).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “When you feel helpless, do you interpret that as failure—or as an invitation to rely wholly on Christ?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Two people face the same temptation. One says, “I must prove I’m strong” (self-trust). The other says, “Lord, I am weak; keep me now” (Christ-trust). One is watching self; the other is watching Christ.
  - [WB]: `Self-watch → defeat / Christ-watch → peace`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “In your week, what ‘miracle’ do you most crave—changed feelings, changed circumstances, or changed heart?”

### III. What Jesus calls “great faith”: taking Him at His word and letting Him live obedience in us (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Jesus “sees” faith—not as bravado, but as persevering trust. This is righteousness by faith in action: dependence that results in obedience, patience, and courage.

#### A. Great faith in outsiders: persistence, humility, and coming to the sanctuary by faith (8-9 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 1:** Matthew 15:21–28
  - Reading/Context: The Canaanite woman persists, clings, and receives.
  - [WB]: `Matt 15 — “Great is your faith” = cling to Christ`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Faith is not demanding; it is **humble persistence** that agrees with God’s judgment and still trusts His mercy.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What words reveal her humility? What words reveal her persistence?”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where are you tempted to quit praying because you feel unworthy?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Mark 9:24
  - Reading/Connection: “I believe; help my unbelief!”
  - [WB]: `Mark 9:24 — faith + struggle in one prayer`
  - Practical mechanics of faith: Bring both faith and unbelief to Jesus—don’t pretend. Confession is not weakness; it is the doorway to strength.
  - [EGW]: “The unbelief… has a bewitching power… dig up every root of unbelief… enthrone… faith and love.” [EGW] (FW 17).
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ “What ‘root’ unbelief shows up most in you—God’s love, God’s power, or God’s timing?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Hebrews 10:19-22
  - Reading/Connection: We enter “by the blood of Jesus”… “let us draw near… in full assurance of faith.”
  - [WB]: `Heb 10:19-22 — blood → access → draw near`
  - Sanctuary reality: Faith is coming—daily—through Christ’s merits (altar) into living fellowship with God (presence), not standing outside hoping to feel accepted.
  - [DQ]: _(Experience):_ “Do you ‘draw near’ after you feel worthy—or because the blood made a way?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Like a child holding a parent’s hand crossing a busy street—confidence is not in the child’s strength but in the parent’s grip. Faith is not self-confidence; it is **Christ-confidence**.
  - The Practical Landing: When guilt rises, don’t run from God—run to the altar (Cross) and to the laver (cleansing promise), then ask your High Priest to keep you through the day.
  - [WB]: Simple sanctuary flow: `ALTAR (pardon) → LAVER (cleanse) → HP (light/bread/incense) → MHP (finish)`
  - [DQ]: _(Monday-morning):_ “What would it look like to ‘draw near’ in the first 5 minutes after you wake up tomorrow?”

#### B. Faith that trusts authority: Christ’s word, Christ’s merit, Christ’s indwelling obedience (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Luke 7:1–10
  - Reading/Context: Centurion trusts Christ’s word at a distance.
  - [WB]: `Luke 7 — “Say the word” faith`
  - Unpacking the Truth: True faith rests on Christ’s authority, not on my worthiness or my feelings.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “What did the centurion understand about authority that many religious people miss?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Romans 3:23–26
  - Reading/Connection: Justification is God’s righteous act through Christ.
  - [WB]: `Rom 3:24-26 — justified freely / God just + justifier`
  - RBF clarity: The only righteousness that passes judgment is **Christ’s**—received by faith, never produced by self-effort.
  - [DQ]: _(Clarity):_ “What part is God’s work here, and what part is my response?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Galatians 2:20
  - Reading/Challenge: “I live… yet not I… Christ lives in me… by the faith of the Son of God.”
  - [WB]: `Gal 2:20 — “not I… Christ” / “faith of the Son”`
  - The “faith of Jesus” thread: Not merely my weak faith _in_ Him, but His faithful life—His trust in the Father—**reproduced in me**.
  - [EGW]: “We have an ever-living Advocate… ‘let us draw near… in full assurance of faith…’” [EGW] (LUH 321).
  - [DQ]: _(Honest contrast):_ “In your real habits, what looks more common—‘not I’ living, or ‘I will’ striving?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A “to-do gospel” says, “Obey so God will accept you.” The sanctuary gospel says, “Come accepted in the Beloved; then Christ writes His law in your heart.”
  - [WB]: `Acceptance → obedience (fruit)` and `Self-effort → pride/despair`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Which commandment becomes hardest when you feel insecure—and how would ‘not I, but Christ’ change your approach?”

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Faith refuses sign-seeking and yields to God’s Word (Mark 8:11, 12; John 20:29; Deut. 8:3).
  - Faith is drawing near through Christ’s blood and priesthood—altar to Most Holy Place—so obedience becomes fruit, not currency (Heb. 10:19-23; Rom. 3:24-26; Rev. 14:12).
  - The “faith of Jesus” is Christ’s own trusting dependence living in us (Gal. 2:20).
- The Practical Takeaway: When you stop trying to manufacture righteousness and instead **receive Christ**, His faith produces real obedience, real patience, and real victory in ordinary life.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST IN YOU = HIS FAITH → YOUR VICTORY`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one specific area of unbelief (fear, appetite, irritation, lust, bitterness, despair) to Jesus as High Priest: confess helplessness, ask, and then **act as if His word is true**.
  - [WB]: `“DRAW NEAR” (Heb 10:22-23)` and `“HE IS FAITHFUL” (Heb 10:23)`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop **trying** to fix in your own strength—and instead bring to Jesus daily, trusting His word?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - Close with Christ-centered confidence: He who began the work will finish it as we rely wholly on Him.
  - [EGW]: “Nothing is… more invincible, than the soul that feels its nothingness and relies wholly on the merits of the Saviour.” [EGW] (MH 182).
  - [WB]: `Heb 12:2 — Author & Finisher`
