# 2026 Q2 W10 - May 30–June 5

## Repentance and Forgiveness

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Repentance and forgiveness (Hosea 6; 1 John 1:9; Exodus 34; Matthew 22 wedding garment)  
**Present Truth Theme:** Repentance and forgiveness are **Christ’s gifts** from His sanctuary ministry—He both **forgives (imputed righteousness)** and **cleanses (imparted righteousness)**, writing the law in the heart by the **faith of Jesus**.  
**Central Focus:** Moving from **self-justifying, fig-leaf religion** to **honest confession and abiding faith**—so Christ actually changes our reactions, habits, and relationships.  
**Key Texts:** 1 John 1:9; Zechariah 3:1–4; Acts 3:19

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Have you ever apologized but secretly felt, “Yes, but they deserved it”? That’s the human heart—**half-sorry, half-self-justifying**.
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson uses Israel’s failure (golden calf), Hosea’s call to return, and Jesus’ wedding garment to teach repentance and forgiveness.
- Present Truth Bridge: Repentance isn’t our payment to get forgiven—repentance is **part of Christ’s saving work** in us, just like forgiveness is.
  - [WB]: `REPENTANCE & FORGIVENESS = CHRIST’S GIFTS (SANCTUARY REALITY)`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement—Christ is cleansing a people **by faith**, not producing polished hypocrisy (Dan. 8:14; Rev. 14:12).
- Roadmap:
  - Fig leaves vs. God’s robe (imputed righteousness)
  - Walking in the light (forgive + cleanse)
  - God’s character and covenant (law written in the heart)

### II. Fig Leaves vs. the Robe: What Repentance Really Is (15-18 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Repentance is not human self-repair; it is **returning to God** to be covered and changed—**altar truth** (pardon through blood) leading into **laver truth** (washing/cleansing).

#### A. The “fig-leaf instinct”: self-justification and self-covering (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Genesis 3:7, 21
  - Reading/Context: Sin immediately produced shame, hiding, and human-made covering; then **God made** covering through sacrifice.
  - [WB]: `GEN 3:7 = THEY MADE` / `GEN 3:21 = GOD MADE`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Our first instinct is always “manage my image.” God’s way is substitutionary sacrifice and divine covering—**the gospel in seed form** (blood before clothing).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What do Adam and Eve do first—confess or cover?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Isaiah 64:6; Isaiah 61:10
  - Reading/Connection: Our righteousness = filthy rags; God clothes us with salvation and righteousness.
  - [WB]: `FILTHY RAGS` vs `ROBE OF RIGHTEOUSNESS`
  - The Faith Distinction: Fig leaves = behavior modification without heart surrender. The robe = Christ’s righteousness received by faith, then lived out.
  - [EGW]: “[EGW] Repentance is as certainly a gift of Jesus Christ as is forgiveness of sins… Those whom God pardons He first makes penitent.” (TMK 109).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you see ‘fig leaves’ in religious life today—maybe even in yourself?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A child spills something, then hurriedly shoves a rug over it before mom comes. Looks better—but the mess is still there, and the smell remains.
  - The Practical Landing: Monday morning repentance isn’t “hide it better,” it’s “Lord, I agree with You—clean it at the root.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What is one ‘rug-over-the-spill’ area—an attitude, habit, or secret compromise—you tend to manage instead of confess?”
- **Foundation Text 3 (Promise/Power):** Acts 5:31
  - Reading/Challenge: Jesus is exalted “to give repentance… and forgiveness.”
  - [WB]: `JESUS GIVES: REPENTANCE + FORGIVENESS`
  - How to Receive: Come as you are—ask for repentance itself; stop waiting to “feel sorry enough.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “If repentance is a gift, what should we do when we feel cold, defensive, or numb?”

#### B. The sanctuary logic: forgiveness and cleansing are two real works (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Zechariah 3:1–4
  - Reading/Context: Joshua stands in filthy garments; Satan accuses; Christ commands, “Take away the filthy garments… I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee.”
  - [WB]: Diagram: `ACCUSED → FILTHY → CHRIST SPEAKS → CLEAN GARMENTS`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Justification is a verdict (“iniquity removed”), and it comes by Christ’s authority—not by Joshua’s promises.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “Who initiates the change of garments—Joshua or the Lord?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** 1 John 1:7, 9
  - Reading/Connection: Walk in the light → cleansing; confess → forgiveness **and** cleansing from all unrighteousness.
  - [WB]: `CONFESS → FORGIVE + CLEANSE`
  - The Faith Distinction: Many want pardon but not cleansing; or cleansing by willpower. God offers both, and both come **through Christ’s blood**.
  - [EGW]: “[EGW] The righteousness of Christ is not a cloak to cover unconfessed and unforsaken sin; it is a principle of life that transforms the character and controls the conduct.” (DA 555-556).
  - [DQ]: _(Heart check):_ “Which do you find harder to believe—‘He forgives me,’ or ‘He can cleanse/change me’?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Courtroom vs. hospital. Court declares you free (verdict); hospital treats the disease (healing). God does not choose one—He does both in Christ.
  - Practical Landing: When you fall, don’t run from God—run to your High Priest. Ask for pardon and for the Spirit to uproot the love of the sin.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would change this week if, after failure, your first move was toward Christ instead of away from Him?”

### III. Covenant Character: Forgiveness That Writes the Law in the Heart (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: The weekly lesson includes Exodus 34 (God’s character after the golden calf) and the wedding garment (Matt. 22). Present truth: God does not merely cancel penalty—He **restores His image**, producing commandment-keeping as fruit of Christ’s indwelling.

#### A. Exodus 34: God’s name (character) is the foundation of real repentance (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Exodus 34:5–7
  - Reading/Context: After Israel’s worst shame, God proclaims His name—merciful, gracious, forgiving, yet just.
  - [WB]: `EX 34:6-7 = GOD’S NAME/CHARACTER`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Repentance grows safest where God’s character is seen clearly—mercy that tells the truth, grace that changes the heart.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What traits does God emphasize first—His mercy or His wrath?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Romans 2:4
  - Reading/Connection: God’s goodness leads to repentance.
  - [WB]: `GOODNESS → REPENTANCE`
  - Faith vs. fear: Fear can produce confession-of-convenience; goodness produces **heart surrender**.
  - [EGW]: “[EGW] If we do not resist this drawing, we shall be led to the foot of the cross in repentance… Then the Spirit of God through faith produces a new life in the soul… the law of God is written in the mind and heart.” (DA 175).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When you think of repentance, do you picture God’s goodness drawing you—or God’s anger chasing you?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Psalm 103:8–12
  - Reading/Connection: Mercy described in relational language—He removes our sins “as far as the east is from the west.”
  - [WB]: `EAST ≠ WEST (REMOVAL, NOT COVER-UP)`
  - Sanctuary connection: God removes guilt (altar) and removes sin’s dominion (washing/cleansing), because He wants a clean people in judgment.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where do you still carry what God says He has ‘removed’—and how does that sabotage obedience?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A father finds a broken window. One approach: humiliation and threats; another: “Tell me the truth—then we’ll fix what’s broken together.” Only the second produces honesty next time.
  - Monday morning: When you fail, run toward God’s character (Ex. 34) instead of away; speak truth plainly; expect cleansing, not condemnation.
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Do you avoid prayer/Bible after failure? What does that reveal about what you believe God is like?”

#### B. The wedding garment: imputed righteousness received, imparted righteousness revealed (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Matthew 22:11–14
  - Reading/Context: The guest without a wedding garment is speechless—presence at the feast is not the same as being clothed.
  - [WB]: `WEDDING GARMENT = CHRIST’S RIGHTEOUSNESS`
  - Unpacking the Truth: The garment is not our improved fig leaves; it is Christ provided—received by faith, then worn as a lived reality.
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “Why is the man ‘speechless’? What excuse dies in the light of a provided garment?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Revelation 19:7–8
  - Reading/Connection: The bride is arrayed in fine linen; “the fine linen is the righteousness of saints.”
  - [WB]: `GIFTED LINEN → LIVED RIGHTEOUSNESS`
  - The Faith of Jesus: End-time righteousness is not independent performance; it is Christ’s life reproduced—His faith, His obedience, His patience (Rev. 14:12).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How do we keep ‘righteousness of saints’ from turning into self-righteousness?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Hebrews 10:19–22
  - Reading/Connection: Boldness to enter by the blood; hearts sprinkled (guilt dealt with) and bodies washed (cleansing).
  - [WB]: `SPRINKLED HEART + WASHED BODY (ALTAR + LAVER)`
  - Sanctuary mechanics made practical: We come by blood (no boasting), we stay by washing (ongoing surrender), we overcome by **abiding faith**.
  - [EGW]: “[EGW] The provision made is complete, and the eternal righteousness of Christ is placed to the account of every believing soul.” (TBLJ 382).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Which is harder for you: to come boldly (no shame) or to come honestly (no hiding)?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Borrowed uniform vs. new identity. A borrowed uniform only changes how you look; a new identity changes how you live. Christ gives both: acceptance now, transformation ongoing.
  - Monday morning: Before the day starts, claim the garment (acceptance), then ask Jesus to live His obedience in you in one concrete place: speech, appetite, purity, patience.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Name one moment this week you’ll consciously ‘put on the garment’ before speaking/acting.”

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Fig leaves = self-righteousness and hiding (Gen. 3).
  - The robe/garment = Christ’s righteousness **imputed and imparted** (Isa. 61; Matt. 22).
  - Repentance and forgiveness are gifts from our High Priest, received by faith, producing commandment-keeping as fruit (Acts 5:31; 1 John 1:9; Rev. 14:12).
- The Practical Takeaway: When the Spirit convicts, don’t bargain, justify, or delay—**come to Jesus**, confess, and trust Him to both **forgive and cleanse**, because He provides both.
  - [WB]: `STOP HIDING → START ABIDING`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one specific sin pattern, one relationship rupture, or one hidden compromise to Christ today—not to promise Him you’ll do better, but to **receive His robe** and His renovating power.
  - [WB]: `1 JOHN 1:9` / `HEB 10:22` / `CHRIST OUR HIGH PRIEST`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is one thing you will stop trying to manage with ‘fig leaves’ and instead confess openly to Jesus this week?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - Christ is finishing a work in the Most Holy Place; He will have a people who overcome **by faith**, not by boasting (Dan. 8:14; Rev. 14:12).
  - [EGW]: “[EGW] The righteousness of Christ is not a cloak to cover unconfessed and unforsaken sin; it is a principle of life that transforms the character and controls the conduct.” (DA 555-556).
  - [WB]: `PHIL 1:6` / `REV 14:12`
  - Close with Scripture hope: Philippians 1:6—He will finish what He started.
