# 2026 Q2 W12 - June 13–19

## Share Him

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Sharing Jesus—witnessing from love, character, and God’s presence (Matt. 28:18–20; 1 Pet. 3:8–15; Hosea 7; Zech. 10)  
**Present Truth Theme:** True witnessing is **Christ’s righteousness received by faith** (imputed and imparted) through our High Priest’s sanctuary ministry—so the **commandments (love)** are the fruit, and the **faith of Jesus** becomes our living testimony.  
**Central Focus:** Moving from **self-driven religion** (pressure, temper, fear, performance) to **sanctuary-shaped abiding** (confession, cleansing, indwelling love) that naturally gives a Christlike witness in real situations.  
**Key Texts:** Matthew 28:18–20; 1 Peter 3:15; Galatians 2:20

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: If someone met you in traffic, at work, or online this week—did they meet **Jesus**, or just your stress and tone?
- Lesson Connection: This week says “Share Him.” The question is not whether we should witness, but **what kind of life** makes witness believable.
- Present Truth Bridge: We cannot share what we do not have. The gospel is not, “Try harder to be nice so you can witness.” It is: **Come to Christ our High Priest—be cleansed—receive His life by faith—then you overflow.**
  - [WB]: `SHARE HIM = OVERFLOW` and `SELF → CHRIST IN ME`
- Why This Matters Now: We are in the antitypical Day of Atonement; Christ’s Most Holy Place ministry means God is finishing a work of **heart-cleansing and character restoration** in His people—by faith, not by human strain.
- Roadmap:
  - Christ’s authority and presence (Matt. 28)
  - A sanctified heart that speaks with meekness (1 Pet. 3)
  - The danger of warmed-over religion (Hosea 7) and the promise of latter rain restoration (Zech. 10)

---

### II. The Great Commission Through the Sanctuary Lens: Authority, Presence, and “All” (15-18 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Jesus does not send us out to perform for Him; He sends us out **with Him**. Only His **indwelling righteousness** can make the message credible.

#### A. Christ’s authority is redemptive: the Cross secured the right to save completely (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Matthew 28:18
  - Reading/Context: The risen Christ speaks after the Cross and resurrection: “All authority…”
  - [WB]: `Matt 28:18 = ALL authority (saving authority)`
  - Unpacking the Truth: His authority is not merely to command; it is authority to **redeem**, pardon, and restore—He has won the battle we could not win.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What is the setting of this authority—before the Cross, or after it? Why does that matter?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Hebrews 7:25
  - Reading/Connection: The same risen Christ is active now: “He ever lives to make intercession.”
  - [WB]: `Heb 7:25 = SAVES to the UTTERMOST (intercession)`
  - The Faith Distinction: Self-effort says, “I must be brave enough, kind enough, consistent enough.” Faith says, “My High Priest supplies what I lack while I consent and obey.”
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When you avoid witnessing, is it mainly fear of people—or unbelief in Christ’s present help for you?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Acts 1:8
  - Reading/Challenge: Power to witness is Spirit-given, not personality-based.
  - [WB]: `Acts 1:8 = POWER → WITNESS`
  - Sanctuary connection: The Spirit applies Christ’s merits—cleansing at the altar and washing at the laver (pardon and renewal), then empowering for service (Holy Place ministry).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would change if you asked specifically each morning: ‘Lord, give me the Spirit for one person today’?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: Trying to “witness” without abiding is like trying to run a phone all day without charging it—apps may be good, but the power dies.
  - The Practical Landing: Before speaking to others, come by faith: confess known sin, claim cleansing, ask for the Spirit’s love—then go in Christ’s authority.
- [WB]: Simple sanctuary flow: `ALTAR (pardon) → LAVER (cleansing) → POWER for mission`

#### B. “I am with you”: the faith of Jesus becomes our lived witness (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Matthew 28:19–20
  - Reading/Context: “Go… make disciples… teaching them to observe… and lo, I am with you always.”
  - [WB]: `GO → DISCIPLES → TEACH OBEY` and `WITH YOU ALWAYS`
  - Unpacking: Christ doesn’t merely give a task; He gives a **presence**. Discipleship is teaching people how to **abide** so obedience is faith-produced.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What promise in verse 20 removes the pressure of ‘making it happen’ yourself?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Galatians 2:20
  - Reading/Connection: “Christ lives in me… the life… I live by the faith of the Son of God.”
  - [WB]: `Gal 2:20 = CHRIST LIVES IN ME` and `FAITH OF JESUS`
  - The Faith Distinction: Not only my faith _in_ Jesus, but Jesus’ own faithful trust in the Father reproduced in me by the Spirit—this is present truth lived.
  - [EGW]: “We can never come into possession of this spirit by trying to love others. What is needed is the love of Christ in the heart. When self is merged in Christ, love springs forth spontaneously.” [EGW] (COL 384.1).
  - [WB]: `Not TRY → MERGED → LOVE springs`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where are you ‘trying to love’ (strain, irritation) instead of ‘merged in Christ’ (quiet steadiness)?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: Two people “represent” a company: one wears a borrowed uniform with no backing; the other is an authorized ambassador with full resources. Christ sends ambassadors with **His life within**.
  - The Practical Landing: When impatience rises (home, traffic, church board), pause: “Lord Jesus, live Your patience in me now.” That moment is part of “Share Him.”
- [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Name one recurring situation where your tone ruins your witness. What would ‘Christ lives in me’ look like there this week?”

### III. A Credible Witness: Commandment-Keeping Love as the Fruit of Righteousness by Faith (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Peter’s counsel is not “polish behavior so God can use you,” but **sanctify Christ in the heart** so the law of love is written within and the witness is weighty.

#### A. New covenant Christianity: the law written in the heart produces a different spirit (7-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** 1 Peter 3:8–9
  - Reading/Context: Unity, sympathy, humility; no retaliation; bless instead.
  - [WB]: `1 Pet 3:8-9 = HUMBLE • BLESS (no retaliation)`
  - Sanctuary connection: This is the fruit of cleansing and fellowship with God—self is brought to the altar; pride dies; love lives.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “Which word in Peter’s list exposes you most—sympathy, humility, or blessing those who wrong you?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Jeremiah 31:33–34
  - Reading/Connection: New covenant: “I will… write My law… I will forgive.”
  - [WB]: `NEW COVENANT = I WILL (God acts)` and `LAW IN HEART + FORGIVENESS`
  - The Faith Distinction: Old covenant spirit: “I promise, I will.” New covenant faith: “Lord, You promised—You do it in me.” Obedience becomes **promise-based**, not pressure-based.
  - [EGW]: “Do you believe that He saves you from sin, that He imputes to you His righteousness?… ‘I will… write [My law] in their hearts.’ He will create… a divine principle of holiness.” [EGW] (CT 52).
  - [WB]: `IMPUTED (pardon) + IMPARTED (principle of holiness)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Do you relate to God mainly by resolutions—or by claiming His ‘I will’ promises?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** John 13:34–35
  - Reading/Challenge: Love “as I have loved you” is the identifying mark.
  - [WB]: `John 13:35 = THE MARK (love)`
  - Practical mechanics of faith: In conflict, surrender self-justification; ask for Christ’s meekness; choose words that align with His commandment.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What must be crucified in you—sarcasm, silent treatment, harsh correction—so Christ can be seen?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: A stained-glass window is beautiful only when light shines through. Without light, it’s just dark shapes. The beauty is **Christ shining through surrendered flaws**.
  - The Practical Landing: Choose one relationship where you rehearse their wrongs. Bring it to Jesus: “Write Your law here; I cannot bless them naturally.”

#### B. Witness under pressure: sanctify Christ first, then speak with meekness (8-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** 1 Peter 3:14–15
  - Reading/Context: “Do not be afraid… sanctify the Lord… be ready… with meekness and fear.”
  - [WB]: `Sanctify Christ in heart → Ready to answer (meekness)`
  - Unpacking: The first “defense” is a heart where Christ is enthroned. Meekness is evidence of Another Life within.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What comes first—having arguments, or sanctifying Christ in the heart?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** 2 Corinthians 5:14
  - Reading/Connection: “The love of Christ compels us…”
  - [WB]: `LOVE compels ≠ GUILT drives`
  - The Faith Distinction: Guilt-driven witnessing is often sharp and short-lived. Love-compelled witness is steady, tender, courageous—because it is Christ’s love.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When you think of sharing faith, do you feel guilt-pressure or love-compulsion?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Revelation 12:11
  - Reading/Challenge: Overcome by the blood + testimony + not loving life unto death.
  - [WB]: `BLOOD + TESTIMONY + SURRENDER`
  - Sanctuary tie-in: The blood gives pardon; testimony is overflow; surrender is self on the altar daily—this is how fear of man is broken.
  - [EGW]: “God never compels the obedience of man. He leaves all free to choose whom they will serve.” [EGW] (PK 510).
  - [WB]: `No coercion (God’s method)`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where are we tempted to ‘force’—in family pressure, online debates, or church culture—and what would Christ’s way look like?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: A door can be opened with a key or kicked in. Kicking may get you inside but destroys trust. Christ’s key is truth spoken in love, Spirit-powered.
  - The Practical Landing: Plan one “gentle defense” this week: a short personal testimony, one Bible promise, and an offer to pray.

---

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Christ’s authority (Matt. 28:18) is the authority of the crucified-risen High Priest who saves to the uttermost (Heb. 7:25).
  - Witnessing is not performance; it is **overflow** of Christ’s righteousness by faith (Gal. 2:20).
  - The commandments appear as love—written on the heart (Jer. 31:33; 1 Pet. 3:8–9)—and defended with meekness (1 Pet. 3:15).
- The Practical Takeaway: When self is merged in Christ, **love springs forth spontaneously**—and “Share Him” becomes natural because Jesus is actually living in you.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST IN YOU → LOVE → SHARE HIM`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one concrete failure of witness (temper, silence, harshness, fear of man) to Christ as High Priest—confess, accept pardon, ask for His faith to operate in you, then act on the next right step.
  - [WB]: `CONFESS • CLAIM • CONSENT`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop trying to fix this week—and instead bring to Jesus for cleansing and indwelling victory?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - [EGW]: “It is when we are faithful in exalting the name of God that our impulses are under divine supervision, and we are enabled to develop spiritual and intellectual power.” [EGW] (AG 105).
  - [WB]: `Exalt His name → impulses supervised`
  - Close with hope: God will finish what He began in a people who keep the commandments of God and have the faith of Jesus (Rev. 14:12).
