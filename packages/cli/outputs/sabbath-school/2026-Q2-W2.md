# 2026 Q2 W2 - April 4–10

## To Know God

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Knowing God’s character (holy love) as revealed in Scripture and in Jesus (Immanuel)  
**Present Truth Theme:** Knowing God is not mere information—it is **covenant fellowship** that comes only through Christ’s **righteousness by faith**, as pictured in the sanctuary: we draw near by the blood (altar), are cleansed (laver), are sustained by Christ’s life (Holy Place), and stand in the judgment only through His intercession (Most Holy Place).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from distorted views of God (and self-powered religion) to **abiding trust**—letting Christ’s faith reproduce God’s character (commandment-keeping love) in daily life.  
**Key Texts:** John 17:3; Hebrews 10:19–22; Revelation 14:12

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Many say, “I believe in God,” yet live anxious, guilty, defensive, and harsh—what if the root problem is we don’t truly **know** Him as He is?
  - [DQ]: _(Warm-up):_ “When you hear ‘God is holy,’ do you feel drawn in—or pushed away? Why?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson is “To Know God”—His holiness, His love, His creative power, and His nearness in Jesus.
- Present Truth Bridge: Satan’s chief work is to **misrepresent God**, because if God is seen wrongly, the gospel becomes either **presumption** or **self-salvation**.
  - [WB]: `KNOW GOD → TRUST GOD → RECEIVE CHRIST’S RIGHTEOUSNESS`
- Why This Matters Now: The last gospel message is the revelation of God’s character of love, and it is to be seen **in a people** who keep “the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus” (Rev. 14:12) in the setting of Christ’s Most Holy Place ministry.
  - [WB]: Sketch: `Court → Holy → Most Holy` (label: `Justify → Sanctify → Judge/Seal`)
- Roadmap:
  - The original lie about God (Gen. 3)
  - The law vs. gospel: mirror vs. remedy (Rom. 3; John 3)
  - Drawing near by the blood and washing (Heb. 10; sanctuary)
  - Abiding love that casts out fear (1 John 4)

---

### II. Satan’s First Attack: Distorting God’s Character (Gen. 3) (14-16 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Before we can “know God,” we must detect the **counterfeit picture** Satan sells—because a false god produces false religion and powerless Christianity.

#### A. The lie: God is withholding; His law is oppression (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Genesis 3:1–5
  - Reading/Context: The first temptation is not mainly “eat fruit,” but **suspect God**.
  - [WB]: `3 darts: DOUBT (Word) → DISTRUST (Heart) → DISOBEY (Life)`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - “Has God indeed said?” = attack Scripture’s trustworthiness
    - “You will not surely die” = deny consequences/judgment
    - “God knows…” = God is portrayed as selfish/controlling
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What picture of God does Satan paint in these verses?”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where do you feel tempted to believe ‘God is holding out on me’—relationships, money, appetite, entertainment, sexuality, reputation?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** John 8:44
  - Reading/Connection: Jesus identifies Satan as liar and murderer—his lies kill trust, then kill obedience, then kill us.
  - [WB]: `False God → False worship → Bondage`
  - The Faith Distinction:
    - Legalism begins with a lie: “God is hard; I must prove myself.”
    - Faith begins with truth: “God is good; I can surrender.”
  - [EGW]: “From the opening of the great controversy it has been Satan’s purpose to misrepresent God’s character and to excite rebellion against His law.” (PP 338).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How does a distorted view of God produce either lawlessness (‘I can do anything’) or legalism (‘I must earn’)? Which side are you more prone to?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: A child convinced his father is unfair will either rebel openly or become a fearful people-pleaser—both are reactions to **mistrust**, not love.
  - Practical Landing: Monday temptation often comes as an inner sermon: “God won’t come through; handle it yourself.” Faith answers: “My Father is trustworthy; I will obey by relying on Him.”
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “What is one command you struggle with because you suspect God’s motives?”

#### B. God’s law reveals His character; the cross restores trust (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 3:** Romans 3:20
  - Reading/Context: The law diagnoses sin; it cannot cure it.
  - [WB]: `LAW = MIRROR (diagnose) | CHRIST = REMEDY (cure)`
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “If the law is a mirror, what happens when I try to ‘wash’ with the mirror—how does that feel spiritually?”
- **Foundation Text 4:** Romans 7:12
  - Reading/Connection: The commandment is holy/just/good—so the problem is not the law, but the sinner.
  - [WB]: `Problem = SINNER’S HEART (needs new birth)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How does Romans 7:12 protect us from believing ‘God’s law is the enemy’?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** John 3:16
  - Reading/Connection: God answers Satan’s lie with the cross: He is not withholding—He gave His Son.
  - [WB]: `CROSS = God’s heart unveiled`
  - [EGW]: “The law of God…is a revelation of the will and the character of its Author. God is love, and His law is love.” (GC 467).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “When convicted of sin, do you run from God or to Him? What does the cross teach you to do instead?”

### III. Knowing God Through the Sanctuary: Draw Near by Another’s Righteousness (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** “To know” God (John 17:3) is covenant closeness—but sinners cannot approach holiness except through Christ’s priestly ministry. The sanctuary is God’s visual explanation of **righteousness by faith**.

#### A. Holiness exposes us—and drives us to cleansing (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Leviticus 20:26
  - Reading/Context: God separates a people to reflect Him.
  - [WB]: `HOLY = belonging to God → reflecting God`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Holiness is not mere “strictness”; it is God’s life and character reproduced in human life.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What does God separate His people _from_ and _to_ in this verse?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Isaiah 6:5–7
  - Reading/Connection: Seeing God produces self-knowledge; cleansing comes from God’s provision (coal from altar imagery).
  - [WB]: `SEE GOD → SEE SELF → RECEIVE CLEANSING`
  - Sanctuary tie-in: The altar speaks of atonement provided outside of us; cleansing is given, not achieved.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When you feel unworthy, do you conclude ‘God can’t love me,’ or ‘God must cleanse me’? What’s the difference?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Psalm 24:3–4
  - Reading/Connection: “Who shall ascend…? clean hands, pure heart.” The requirement is real.
  - The Faith Distinction: The gospel does not lower the standard; it provides the **High Priest** who can make it true in us.
  - [WB]: `Standard unchanged | Provision = Christ`
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “Why is it good news that God’s standard is not lowered? What would it mean if it were?”

- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: A patient cannot perform his own heart surgery. The more serious the diagnosis, the more he must trust the Surgeon.
  - Practical Landing: Conviction is not God pushing you away; it is God directing you to the only place cleansing is found—Christ’s ministry.
  - [DQ]: _(Personal):_ “When you fail, what do you naturally do first—hide, promise, punish yourself, or come to Christ?”

#### B. “Draw near” by the blood: justification and cleansing in one living approach (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Hebrews 10:19–22
  - Reading/Challenge: “Boldness…by the blood…hearts sprinkled…washed with pure water…full assurance of faith.”
  - [WB]: Diagram: `Blood (altar) → Wash (laver) → Draw near (priestly access)`
  - Unpacking the Truth (simple, practical):
    - “By the blood” = **imputed righteousness** (accepted in Another)
    - “Sprinkled/washed” = **imparted cleansing** (new desires, new habits, new power)
    - “Full assurance of faith” = not confidence in self, but in Christ’s present ministry
  - [EGW]: “In the new birth the heart is brought into harmony with God, as it is brought into accord with His law.” (AG 20).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would ‘full assurance of faith’ look like the next time you fall—what would you do differently in the first 60 seconds?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** John 17:3
  - Reading/Connection: Eternal life is knowing God—relational, experiential knowledge through Christ.
  - [WB]: `Know = covenant fellowship (not facts only)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How can someone know doctrines well, yet not ‘know God’ in John 17:3?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: Many treat prayer like a performance review: “I’ve been good; now I can approach.” Hebrews says: approach **because of blood**, then be changed by His presence.
  - Practical Landing: Daily “draw near” is not a vague feeling—it is a chosen act: confess, agree with God, claim Christ’s merits, and expect real cleansing power.

---

### IV. Immanuel and the Faith of Jesus: God Near Enough to Re-create Us (6-8 mins) [*]

- **Introduction to Section:** God’s nearness climaxes in Jesus (Immanuel). The “faith of Jesus” (Rev. 14:12) is His own trusting obedience reproduced in us by the Spirit—this is imparted righteousness in daily life.

#### A. God near: from Creator-power to covenant-presence (3-4 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Genesis 2:7
  - Reading/Context: God forms and breathes—personal nearness to human weakness.
  - [WB]: `God breathes life → God sustains life`
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What does Genesis 2:7 teach about God’s closeness to humanity?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Acts 17:27–28
  - Reading/Connection: He is “not far”—“in Him we live.”
  - [WB]: `NOT FAR → present help`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “How would your response to anxiety change today if you believed God is truly near—not reluctant?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Matthew 1:23
  - Reading/Connection: Immanuel—God **with us**.
  - [WB]: `IMMANUEL = God WITH us`

#### B. The faith of Jesus lived in us: presence that produces obedience (3-4 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Revelation 14:12
  - Reading/Context: End-time identity: “commandments of God” + “faith of Jesus.”
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 = Obedience (fruit) + Faith of Jesus (root)`
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What happens to obedience when the ‘root’ is self-effort instead of the faith of Jesus?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Galatians 2:20
  - Reading/Connection: “Not I, but Christ…life…by the faith of the Son of God.”
  - The Faith Distinction: Not merely my willpower for Jesus, but Christ’s own life of trust reproduced in me.
  - [WB]: `NOT I → CHRIST | Faith OF Jesus → in me`
  - [EGW]: “The light of the knowledge of the glory of God is seen in the face of Jesus Christ.” (RC 15).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Name one temptation where you need to stop ‘trying to be strong’ and instead claim ‘Christ lives in me’—what would you do first?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: A guide in a blizzard doesn’t just shout directions from far away; he walks beside you and sets the pace. Christ’s righteousness is not mere advice—it is **His life shared**.
  - [DQ]: _(Heart):_ “Where are you living as if you are alone—then reacting in panic, control, or resentment?”

---

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Satan’s first lie was about God’s character; mistrust produces either rebellion or legalism.
  - God answers with the cross and the sanctuary: we draw near **by the blood**, and are cleansed as we **abide**.
  - The commandments are the transcript of God’s character, written in the heart by the new birth—**fruit**, not the root.
- The Practical Takeaway: When I truly know God in Christ, I stop trying to become acceptable and begin **trusting Jesus as my High Priest**, letting His love cast out fear and produce real obedience.
  - [WB]: `NOT: Try harder | BUT: Draw near → Abide → Obey`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one specific area (besetting sin, relationship fracture, secret fear) to Christ in the heavenly sanctuary—confess, consent, and trust His promise to cleanse.
  - [WB]: `Heb 10:22 “Draw near” | Gal 2:20 “Not I”`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop managing in self-strength, and instead bring—today—to Jesus ‘by the blood’?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - [EGW]: “The last message of mercy to be given to the world, is a revelation of His character of love.” (COL 415).
  - [WB]: `Rev. 14:12 — Commandments + Faith of Jesus`
