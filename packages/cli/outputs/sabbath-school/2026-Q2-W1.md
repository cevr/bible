# 2026 Q2 W1 - March 28–April 3

## Reality Check

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** A sober look at our Laodicean condition and Christ’s remedy: “abide in Me.”  
**Present Truth Theme:** Laodicea is an end-time sanctuary message: the True Witness exposes self-sufficiency and offers a **sanctuary exchange**—**our nakedness for Christ’s robe**, **our blindness for Spirit sight**, **our powerless religion for the faith of Jesus** living in us (imputed and imparted righteousness).  
**Central Focus:** Moving from self-reliance (trying harder) to **daily receiving** Christ’s indwelling life—so obedience becomes fruit, not a wage.  
**Key Texts:** Revelation 3:14–22; John 15:1–11; Zechariah 3:1–5

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: “If your spiritual life were a phone battery, what percent are you right now—and what do you do when it hits 5%?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson calls for a **reality check**—Laodicea (Rev. 3:14–22) and the remedy: **abide** (John 15).
- Present Truth Bridge: Laodicea is not mainly about “being nicer.” It is Christ telling His remnant: you can be **rich in truth** yet **poor in experience**—and experience comes only from **Christ’s righteousness received by faith**, applied through His **High Priestly ministry** now.
  - [WB]: Title: `REALITY CHECK = DIAGNOSIS + REMEDY`
- Why This Matters Now: We live in the antitypical Day of Atonement; the True Witness is preparing a people who keep the commandments **by the faith of Jesus** (Rev. 14:12).
  - [WB]: `Laodicea → Door → Supper → Throne (Rev 3:20-21)`
- Roadmap:
  - Diagnose: what lukewarmness really is (Rev. 3:14–17)
  - Exchange: “buy” without money—sanctuary clothing and sight (Rev. 3:18–19; Isa. 55:1)
  - Abide: how Christ’s life flows into us (John 15; Rom. 8)

### II. The Diagnosis: Lukewarm = Self-Sufficient Religion Without Christ’s Life (15-18 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Christ does not flatter His last-day church. He exposes the core problem: **confidence in self** and **lack of felt need**—a form of godliness without the power.

#### A. The True Witness reads our condition (8-9 mins)

- Foundation Text 1: Revelation 3:14–17
  - Reading/Context: Last of the seven churches—end-time condition; Christ speaks as “Amen…Faithful and True Witness.”
  - [WB]: `Rev 3:17 = "I am rich" vs. God's: poor/blind/naked`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - Lukewarmness is not atheism; it is **religion without dependence**.
    - Nakedness = lacking Christ’s righteousness; blindness = not seeing our need; poverty = no spiritual capital with God.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What words in Rev. 3:17 show self-sufficiency?”
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How can a person be ‘rich in truth’ yet ‘poor’ spiritually?”
- Foundation Text 2: John 15:4–5
  - Reading/Connection: Laodicea is the disconnected-branch condition—activity without life.
  - [WB]: `Without Me → NOTHING (Jn 15:5)`
  - The Faith Distinction:
    - Self-effort says: “I’ll produce fruit for God.”
    - Faith says: “I’ll abide; **His life** produces fruit.”
  - [EGW]: “Abiding in Christ means a constant receiving of His Spirit, a life of unreserved surrender to His service.” [EGW] (DA 676.2).
  - [DQ]: _(Interpretation):_ “What does ‘without Me you can do nothing’ mean in daily temptations, not just ‘big’ sins?”
- Illustration & Practical Application:
  - [SN]: A car looks complete in the driveway—until you realize it has **no fuel**. Laodicea is polished religion with an empty tank.
  - The Practical Landing: When devotion becomes a checkbox, prayerless choices multiply—sharp words at home, secret indulgence, numb conscience—then we label it “normal.”
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where do you most often ‘run on empty’—home, work, screen habits, thoughts, temper?”
- Foundation Text 3: Psalm 139:1–3
  - Reading/Challenge: He knows—not to shame, but to heal.
  - [WB]: `God knows → to restore, not merely expose`
  - How to Receive: Start by agreeing with God (confession)—stop defending ourselves.
  - [DQ]: _(Heart):_ “What is hardest to admit to God about your current condition?”

#### B. The law cannot be the root—only Christ can (7-8 mins)

- Foundation Text 4: Romans 7:18–19
  - Reading/Context: Sincere desire without delivering power.
  - [WB]: `Will = present / Power = absent (Rom 7)`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Laodicea often confuses **conviction** with **conversion power**—knowing the good, but not doing it.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you experience ‘to will is present’ but still fail?”
- Foundation Text 5: Romans 8:7–9
  - Reading/Connection: The carnal mind cannot submit; only the Spirit enables real obedience.
  - [WB]: `Spirit IN you → new mind (Rom 8:9)`
  - The Faith Distinction:
    - Moral resolve cannot change the mind.
    - The Spirit gives a new principle: Christ’s life within.
  - [SN]: Trying to keep the law without the Spirit is like trying to run a refrigerator by taping fruit to the outside—looks “fruitful,” but no inner power.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Which do you rely on more—willpower, routines, accountability—or the Spirit’s indwelling? How can you tell?”
- Foundation Text 6: 1 John 5:3
  - Reading/Context: Love makes obedience non-burdensome.
  - [WB]: `Love → Obedience (not burden)`
  - [EGW]: “Love of self excludes the love of Christ…The condition…is exactly represented by the message to the Laodicean church.” [EGW] (OHC 348.5).
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “When do God’s commands feel burdensome—and what does that reveal about connection to Christ?”

### III. The Remedy: The Sanctuary Exchange—Gold, White Raiment, Eyesalve (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Christ’s counsel is not “perform better,” but **come to Me and receive**—a sanctuary transaction: cleansing, clothing, and clear sight.

#### A. “Buy from Me” = receive by faith, not earn (8-9 mins)

- Foundation Text 1: Revelation 3:18–19
  - Reading/Context: Three gifts answer three needs—poverty, nakedness, blindness; love motivates rebuke.
  - [WB]: `Gold / White raiment / Eyesalve`
  - Unpacking the Truth (Sanctuary lens):
    - **White raiment**: Christ’s righteousness credited (justification) and Christ’s righteousness reproduced (sanctification).
    - **Eyesalve**: Spirit-given discernment—seeing sin as sin, Christ as sufficient.
    - **Gold tried in the fire**: genuine faith working by love, refined by trial.
  - [EGW]: “God is waiting to bestow the blessing of forgiveness…of the gifts of righteousness, upon all who will believe in His love.” [EGW] (TM 516.1).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “Which of the three remedies do you sense you resist most—and why?”
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How does knowing rebuke comes from love (Rev. 3:19) change how you hear it?”
- Foundation Text 2: Isaiah 55:1–3
  - Reading/Connection: Buying without money—faith receives what effort cannot purchase.
  - [WB]: `No money → Come, buy (Isa 55:1)`
  - The Faith Distinction: Repentance is not paying God back; it is **turning to receive**.
  - [SN]: A sick person doesn’t earn medicine by promising to be healthier. They **take** the remedy; health follows.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where do you still relate to God like a wage contract—‘I did, so You must’?”
- Foundation Text 3: Zechariah 3:1–5
  - Reading/Challenge: Filthy garments replaced—God’s act, not Joshua’s tailoring.
  - [WB]: `Filthy → Removed → Robe given`
  - How to Receive (simple faith mechanics):
    - Confess: “Lord, I am naked.”
    - Ask: “Clothe me in Christ.”
    - Accept: Believe His word over feelings and Satan’s accusations.
  - [DQ]: _(Honesty):_ “Do you struggle more with guilt (‘I can’t be forgiven’) or pride (‘I’m not that bad’)?”

#### B. Overcoming is union with the Overcomer (7-8 mins)

- Foundation Text 4: Revelation 3:20–21
  - Reading/Context: Door, supper, throne—fellowship leading to shared victory.
  - [WB]: `Knock → Open → Supper → Throne`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - The promise “as I overcame” means Christ shares His victory with the believer through union.
    - Overcoming is not independent heroism; it is **Christ living His obedient life in us**.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What does ‘open the door’ look like in a specific moment of temptation?”
- Foundation Text 5: Hebrews 4:14–16
  - Reading/Connection: Our present-tense High Priest invites bold coming for mercy and **help in time of need**.
  - [WB]: `High Priest NOW → Grace to help`
  - [EGW]: “As wax takes the impression of the seal, so the soul is to take the impression of the Spirit of God and retain the image of Christ.” [EGW] (Mar 241.3).
  - [SN]: A child learning to walk doesn’t negotiate distance; they keep reaching up for the father’s hand again and again. Faith is continual reaching, not one heroic stride.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “When you fall, do you run to the throne (Heb. 4:16) or away from it? Why?”
- Foundation Text 6: 2 Corinthians 3:18
  - Reading/Challenge: Transformation comes by beholding—sanctuary gaze: look to our ministering Christ.
  - [WB]: `Behold → Changed`
  - [DQ]: _(Heart):_ “What do you ‘behold’ daily (media, fears, irritations), and how is it shaping you?”

### IV. Abide: Christ’s Faith Living in You by the Spirit (8-10 mins) [*]

- Introduction to Section: Abiding is the lived experience of righteousness by faith—**constant receiving**—so the commandments become natural fruit.

#### A. What abiding is (and is not) (4-5 mins)

- Foundation Text 1: John 15:9–11
  - Reading/Context: “Abide in My love” leads to joy—not burned-out religion.
  - [WB]: `Abide → Love → Joy full`
  - Unpacking the Truth:
    - Abiding is not a mystical feeling; it is chosen dependence.
    - Obedience is the path of remaining in His love, not the price of earning it.
  - [EGW]: “Faith works by love and purifies the soul. Through faith the Holy Spirit finds access to the heart, and creates holiness therein.” [EGW] (YRP 77.2).
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What result does Jesus promise in John 15:11?”
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “How can obedience be ‘in His love’ instead of ‘to get His love’?”
- Foundation Text 2: Galatians 2:20
  - [WB]: `Not I…Christ lives in me`
  - The Faith of Jesus: The same trust Jesus exercised toward the Father becomes operative in us—He lives His life again in the believer.
  - [SN]: A glove can’t do “glove-works” by squeezing harder; it must be filled by a hand. The Christian life is Christ filling the life.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What repeating failure reveals you’re trying to ‘move the glove’ without the Hand?”

#### B. How abiding happens daily: ask, receive, walk (4-5 mins)

- Foundation Text 3: Luke 11:13
  - Reading/Context: The Father delights to give the Holy Spirit—ask daily.
  - [WB]: `Ask → Receive (Holy Spirit)`
  - [DQ]: _(Simple):_ “What does this verse say about God’s willingness?”
- Foundation Text 4: Romans 8:9–11
  - Reading/Connection: The indwelling Spirit is the presence of Christ and resurrection power for daily living.
  - [WB]: `Spirit dwells → life/power`
  - Practical mechanics (Monday morning):
    - Morning: “Lord, I surrender my will; I receive Your Spirit.”
    - In temptation: pause; claim a promise; choose to stay connected.
    - After failure: run to Christ, not from Him—reopen the channel.
  - [DQ]: _(Practical):_ “What would change this week if you asked specifically for the Spirit before the day’s hardest moment?”
- Foundation Text 5: Jeremiah 31:3–4
  - Reading/Challenge: Everlasting love draws; rebuilding is His work.
  - [WB]: `Love draws → God rebuilds`
  - [EGW]: “It is your privilege to trust in the love of Jesus for salvation…Take Christ at His word.” [EGW] (TM 517.1).
  - [DQ]: _(Heart):_ “Do you believe God is drawing you today—or merely tolerating you?”

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth:
  - Laodicea is Christ’s end-time love message: diagnosis (self-sufficiency) and remedy (receive His righteousness).
  - Sanctuary reality: our High Priest clothes, cleanses, gives sight, and then produces fruit through abiding.
- The Practical Takeaway: Stop trying to manufacture fruit; **choose to abide and receive the Spirit**—Christ’s faith and obedience become yours in lived experience.
  - [WB]: `EXCHANGE: my poverty → His riches; my nakedness → His robe`
- Call to Surrender/Receive:
  - Bring one concrete area (temper, lust, discouragement, prayerlessness, resentment) to Jesus as High Priest and say: “Lord, I open the door. Come in. Live Your life in me today.”
  - [WB]: `Rev 3:20 — OPEN THE DOOR`
  - (Final Question): “What is the one thing you will stop trying to fix in your own strength, and instead bring to Jesus to receive His life this week?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - Close with Hebrews 4:16 and Revelation 3:21—He who diagnoses is the One who heals and enthrones.
  - [WB]: `ABIDE = CONSTANT RECEIVING (DA 676)`
