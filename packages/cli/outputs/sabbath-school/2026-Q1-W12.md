# 2026 Q1 W12 - March 14–20

## Living With Each Other

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Household codes — principles for Christian relationships in family and work
**Present Truth Theme:** Christ's Indwelling Life: The Only Source of Christlike Relationships
**Central Focus:** Transformation from self-effort relationships to faith-surrendered love — Christ living His life of self-giving love through us in our closest relationships
**Key Texts:** Colossians 3:23-24; Ephesians 5:25; Galatians 2:20

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Why is it that the people we love most are often the ones we wound most deeply? Why do our closest relationships — marriage, family, work — expose our greatest failures?
- Lesson Connection: This week's lesson addresses household codes in Colossians 3:18–4:6 — instructions for wives, husbands, children, parents, servants, and masters. These are practical, biblical principles for daily life.
- Present Truth Bridge: But here is what we must see — Paul does not give us these instructions expecting we can fulfill them by willpower. Every command in this passage is impossible _apart from Christ living in us_. This is not about trying harder to be a better spouse or parent. This is about receiving Christ's own love, patience, and humility by faith.
  - [WB]: Write: **CHRIST IN YOU — THE ONLY HOPE OF CHRISTLIKE RELATIONSHIPS**
- Why This Matters Now: Christ is now ministering in the Most Holy Place, perfecting a people who will reflect His character in every relationship. The home is the proving ground of genuine faith. What we are at home is what we truly are.
- Roadmap: We will examine: (1) The impossible standard God sets for relationships, (2) Why self-effort always fails, (3) How Christ's indwelling life accomplishes what we cannot.

### II. The Divine Standard: Impossible for the Natural Heart (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Paul's household codes reveal a standard of love, submission, patience, and fairness that exposes our utter inability. These commands are not modified for sinners — they reflect Christ's own character.

#### A. The Call to Self-Giving Love (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Ephesians 5:25 — _"Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ also loved the church and gave Himself for her."_
  - Reading/Context: Paul does not say, "Love your wives as much as you can." He says, "As Christ loved the church." This is sacrificial, unconditional, death-to-self love.
  - [WB]: `Eph 5:25` — **AS CHRIST LOVED**
  - Unpacking the Truth: The standard is not human affection — it is Calvary love. Christ gave _Himself_. This means complete self-forgetfulness, seeking the other's highest good regardless of response.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What specific aspects of Christ's love for the church does this verse point to? What did 'giving Himself' involve?"
- **Foundation Text 2:** Colossians 3:18-19 — _"Wives, submit to your own husbands, as is fitting in the Lord. Husbands, love your wives and do not be bitter toward them."_
  - Reading/Connection: Notice the balance — submission "fitting in the Lord," love without bitterness. Both require dying to self.
  - [WB]: `Col 3:18-19` — **SUBMISSION / LOVE WITHOUT BITTERNESS**
  - The Impossible Demand: The Greek word for "bitter" (_pikrainō_) describes a tyrannical harshness. Husbands must love without resentment when unappreciated, without anger when frustrated. Wives must submit not to tyranny, but as unto the Lord — which requires trust in God above all.
  - [EGW]: "If the husband and wife would only continue to cultivate these attentions which nourish love, they would be happy in each other's society and would have a sanctifying influence upon their families." (HP 206.2)
  - [WB]: _"Attentions which nourish love"_
  - The Problem Exposed: Who can sustain self-forgetful love when we feel neglected? Who can avoid bitterness when wronged? The natural heart cannot.
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* 'Think honestly — when has bitterness, impatience, or self-protection crept into your closest relationships? What triggered it?'

- **Foundation Text 3:** Colossians 3:20-21 — _"Children, obey your parents in all things, for this is well pleasing to the Lord. Fathers, do not provoke your children, lest they become discouraged."_
  - Reading/Context: The parent-child relationship also demands supernatural grace — obedience from children, patient discipline from fathers.
  - [WB]: `Col 3:20-21` — **OBEDIENCE / DO NOT PROVOKE**
  - [EGW]: "Satan is well pleased when parents irritate their children by speaking harsh, angry words... They may be very wrong, but you cannot lead them to the right by losing patience with them." (RH, Jan 24, 1907)
  - The Standard Applied: Patient, consistent, loving discipline that does not provoke discouragement — this requires a calmness and wisdom beyond human nature.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Imagine a father after a long, frustrating day at work. His child disobeys for the third time. The natural response rises — sharp words, anger, harshness. The child shrinks back, discouraged. Now imagine that same moment if the father's heart were filled with Christ's patience, Christ's gentle firmness. The difference is not technique — it is _source_. Where does the patience come from?
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "Parents — or anyone who works with children — where do you find yourself most tempted to 'provoke'? What does that reveal about your own heart's need?"

#### B. The Workplace as Worship (6-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Colossians 3:22-24 — _"Bondservants, obey in all things your masters according to the flesh, not with eyeservice, as men-pleasers, but in sincerity of heart, fearing God. And whatever you do, do it heartily, as to the Lord and not to men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the reward of the inheritance; for you serve the Lord Christ."_
  - Reading/Context: Paul addresses servants, but the principle applies to all work relationships — employees, employers, anyone under authority.
  - [WB]: `Col 3:23-24` — **AS TO THE LORD**
  - The Sanctuary Perspective: Every act of service, done "as to the Lord," becomes worship. Our earthly tasks connect to the heavenly sanctuary where Christ ministers. We serve an unseen Master.
  - [WB]: **Work = Worship → SANCTUARY MINDSET**
  - The Transformation Needed: "In sincerity of heart" — not faking it for the boss, not half-hearted. This requires a heart change, not a behavior change.
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "How would your work attitude change if you truly saw your supervisor as representing Christ? Where do you struggle with 'eyeservice'?"
- **Foundation Text 5:** Colossians 4:1 — _"Masters, give your bondservants what is just and fair, knowing that you also have a Master in heaven."_
  - Reading/Connection: Those in authority are accountable to a higher authority. Justice and fairness must flow from the recognition that we ourselves are servants of Christ.
  - [WB]: `Col 4:1` — **YOU HAVE A MASTER IN HEAVEN**
  - [EGW]: "How fitting an illustration of the love of Christ toward repenting sinners! As the servant who had defrauded his master had nothing with which to make restitution, so sinners who have robbed God of years of service have no means of canceling their debt; Jesus interposes between them and the just wrath of God, and says, I will pay the debt." (TBLJ 377.5)
  - Present Truth Application: This is Righteousness by Faith applied to the workplace — we who have received undeserved grace must extend it. The employer who has been forgiven much must forgive much.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A Christian business owner faces an employee who has made a costly mistake — perhaps even a dishonest one. The natural response is harsh consequences, bitterness, distrust. But if that owner remembers his own debt to Christ, freely forgiven, he responds differently — still with consequences perhaps, but with grace, with an opportunity for restoration. That is Christ's character reproduced.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "Whether you are an employee or supervisor — what specific relationship at work is testing your faith right now? How does Christ's treatment of you inform how you should respond?"

### III. The Only Solution: Christ Living in You (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** We have seen the impossible standard. Now we must see the only solution — not self-improvement, but Christ's life replacing ours. This is the heart of Righteousness by Faith.

#### A. The Exchange: Self-Effort for Faith-Surrender (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Galatians 2:20 — _"I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself for me."_
  - Reading/Context: This is Paul's testimony — not just theology, but experience. The secret of his transformed life.
  - [WB]: `Gal 2:20` — **CHRIST LIVES IN ME**
  - The Faith Distinction: "I live by faith in the Son of God." This is the key. The Christian life is not imitation of Christ by effort, but reception of Christ by faith. His love, His patience, His humility — _imparted_ to us, lived through us.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What does Paul say is the source of his life? What role does 'faith' play in this verse?"
- **Foundation Text 2:** John 15:4-5 — _"Abide in Me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit of itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide in Me. I am the vine, you are the branches. He who abides in Me, and I in him, bears much fruit; for without Me you can do nothing."_
  - Reading/Connection: Jesus uses the vine and branch image to show our complete dependence. The branch does not _try_ to produce fruit — it receives life from the vine.
  - [WB]: Draw: **VINE → BRANCH → FRUIT** (label: _ABIDE = RECEIVE_)
  - [EGW]: "We must let Christ into our hearts and homes if we would walk in the light. Home should be made all that the word implies. It should be a little heaven upon earth, a place where the affections are cultivated instead of being studiously repressed." (3T 539.2)
  - [WB]: _"A little heaven upon earth"_
  - Practical Meaning of "Abide": To abide is to remain connected, to depend moment by moment, to cease striving in self and rest in Christ's sufficiency. It is _faith_, not _force_.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Think of two people trying to carry water across a desert. One carries a bucket and exhausts himself trying not to spill it — self-effort. The other is connected to a pipeline that flows continuously — abiding. The bucket-carrier grows weary and fails. The one connected to the source never runs dry. Which describes your approach to loving your family?
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "What is the difference between 'trying to be more patient' and 'abiding in Christ for His patience'? Can you describe a time you've experienced the difference?"

#### B. Practical Surrender: How Faith Works in Relationships (7-8 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 3:** Romans 8:26 — _"Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered."_
  - Reading/Context: The Holy Spirit's ministry is to bring Christ's life into our weakness. Even when we do not know how to respond rightly, the Spirit intercedes.
  - [WB]: `Rom 8:26` — **THE SPIRIT HELPS OUR WEAKNESS**
  - Sanctuary Connection: Christ is our High Priest in the heavenly sanctuary; the Spirit is His representative within us, applying His ministry to our daily needs. This is present truth — the Most Holy Place ministry reaching into our homes.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "According to this verse, what does the Spirit do when we are weak and don't know how to respond?"
- **Foundation Text 4:** Colossians 4:2 — _"Continue earnestly in prayer, being vigilant in it with thanksgiving."_
  - Reading/Connection: Prayer is the practical means of abiding. "Continue earnestly" — persistent, moment-by-moment dependence.
  - [WB]: `Col 4:2` — **PRAYER = ABIDING**
  - [EGW]: "We are to keep close to the Source of our strength day by day, and when the enemy comes in like a flood the Spirit of the Lord lifts up a standard for us against the enemy. The promise of God is sure, that strength shall be proportioned to our day." (GAG 261.4)
  - [WB]: _"Strength proportioned to our day"_
  - The Practical Landing: When your spouse frustrates you — pray in that moment, surrendering your right to be angry, asking Christ to love through you. When your child disobeys — pray before you speak, asking for Christ's patience. This is not mysticism; it is faith in action.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A wife feels the familiar irritation rising as her husband leaves his things scattered again. The old pattern is to complain, nag, or silently resent. The faith response: a quick, silent prayer — "Lord, I cannot love him rightly. Love him through me." And then, to her surprise, a gentle word comes instead of a sharp one. That is Christ's life, received by faith, lived out in the small moments.
- **Foundation Text 5:** Colossians 4:6 — _"Let your speech always be with grace, seasoned with salt, that you may know how you ought to answer each one."_
  - Reading/Connection: Grace-filled speech is a fruit of Christ's indwelling. "That you may know how you ought to answer" — the Holy Spirit gives wisdom in the moment.
  - [WB]: `Col 4:6` — **SPEECH = GRACE + SALT**
  - The Faith of Jesus Applied: The "faith of Jesus" (Rev 14:12) is not just our belief about Him, but His own quality of faith — perfect trust in the Father — living in us. When Jesus faced provocation, He entrusted Himself to the Father (1 Pet 2:23). That same faith can live in us, producing the same responses.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* 'Think of a specific recurring conflict or frustration in your home or workplace. What would it look like to bring that moment to Christ *before* it happens next, asking Him to live His response through you?'

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: The household codes of Colossians are not a list of duties to perform by willpower — they are a description of Christ's character, which can only be reproduced in us by His indwelling life. The sanctuary truth shows us that Christ is actively ministering for us right now in the Most Holy Place. The commandments are the transcript of His character — the very character He is seeking to write in our hearts. The faith of Jesus — His own quality of trust and dependence on the Father — is offered to us.
- The Practical Takeaway: The transformation of our relationships does not come by _trying_ to love, submit, or be patient. It comes by _receiving_ Christ's love, Christ's submission to the Father's will, Christ's patience — moment by moment, by faith.
  - [WB]: **NOT TRYING → RECEIVING / SELF-EFFORT → FAITH-SURRENDER**
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Will you stop trying to change in your own strength? Will you bring your hardest relationship — your marriage, your child, your difficult coworker — to Christ's sanctuary ministry today and say, "Lord, I cannot do this. Live Your life in me"?
  - [WB]: **"LORD, LIVE YOUR LIFE IN ME"**
  - _(Final Question):_ "What is one relationship where you will consciously surrender self-effort this week and practice moment-by-moment dependence on Christ's indwelling life?"
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope: God is finishing His work in His people. He is preparing a generation who will "keep the commandments of God and have the faith of Jesus" (Rev 14:12). This is not legalism — it is Christ fully reproduced in human vessels. And it begins in our homes, in our daily relationships, in the small moments no one sees.
  - [EGW]: "Christ and heavenly agencies are waiting to quicken your spiritual sensibilities, to renew your activities, and to teach you of the deep things of God." (HP 208.5)
  - [WB]: `Revelation 14:12` — **COMMANDMENTS OF GOD + FAITH OF JESUS**
