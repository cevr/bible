# 2026 Q2 W5 - April 25–May 1

## How to Study the Bible

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Practical ways to study the Word of God better (time, place, depth, sharing, delight).  
**Present Truth Theme:** The Bible is the voice of our High Priest in the heavenly sanctuary—Christ’s **righteousness received by faith** (imputed and imparted) becomes living obedience (“commandments of God”) as we **abide** in Him by the **faith of Jesus**.  
**Central Focus:** Moving from rushed, guilty “devotions” to a daily **abiding union** with Christ that actually changes Monday morning choices and reactions.  
**Key Texts:** John 15:1–8; Hebrews 4:14–16; Isaiah 55:10–11.

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Many of us read the Bible like a medicine label when we feel sick—fast, anxious, and mostly to quiet our conscience. What if Bible study is actually **eating** Christ, receiving His life?
  - [DQ]: _(Warm-up):_ “When you miss Bible time, what do you feel most—guilt, dryness, fear, or just distraction?”
- Lesson Connection: This week’s lesson gives practical counsel—time, place, deeper study, and sharing.
- Present Truth Bridge: The “how” only works when we grasp the “what”: Scripture is not mainly information; it is **Christ ministering His righteousness** to the soul from the heavenly sanctuary.
  - [WB]: Title: `ABIDE → RECEIVE → BEAR FRUIT (RBF)`
- Why This Matters Now: In the antitypical Day of Atonement, Christ’s Most Holy Place ministry calls for a people who overcome **by faith**, not by nervous striving (Rev. 14:12; Heb. 4:14–16).
- Roadmap: We will study (1) **Abiding** (John 15), (2) **The Word as lamp/bread** (Psalm 119; Matthew 4; John 6), (3) **The Word that accomplishes** (Isaiah 55) and how this produces commandment-keeping as fruit.

---

### II. Abiding: Bible Study as Union, Not Self-Improvement (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: The foundation of all true Bible study is not “try harder” but **abide deeper**—the sanctuary reality: we come to a living High Priest, not a dead textbook.
  - [WB]: Simple sanctuary path: `COURT (need) → HOLY (light/bread) → MOST HOLY (heart law)`
  - [DQ]: “When you open the Bible, are you mainly seeking information, or are you coming to a living Person? How can you tell?”

#### A. Abide and Bear Fruit (John 15:1–8) (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** John 15:4
  - Reading/Context: Jesus is on His way to Gethsemane—He teaches fruit-bearing at the moment of greatest pressure.
  - [WB]: `John 15:4 — “Abide” (μένω = remain)`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Fruit is not stapled on; it is grown from union. Commandment-keeping is **fruit**, not the root.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What is the one thing the branch must do in this verse—and what can it not do?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** John 15:5
  - Reading/Connection: “Without Me you can do nothing”—that includes religious “success.”
  - [WB]: Two columns: `Self-effort` vs. `Abiding faith`
  - The Faith Distinction: Self-effort says, “I will produce love/temperance/patience.” Faith says, “Lord, You are my life—live Your obedience in me.”
  - [EGW]: “Christ is our life… He is made unto us wisdom and righteousness and sanctification and redemption. He is our sufficiency… ‘I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me.’” (OHC 209).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “Where do you see yourself trying to ‘attach fruit’—especially in a commandment area—rather than abiding?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** John 15:7–8
  - Reading/Challenge: Abiding shapes desires (“ask what you will”) and glorifies God by real fruit.
  - [WB]: `Abide → Word in you → Ask → Fruit → Glory`
  - How to Receive: Abiding is practical surrender—open Bible, consent to God’s verdict, ask for Christ’s life, trust His promise, act in that trust.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “If God answered your prayers exactly as you’ve been praying, would your life show fruit—or just religious activity?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A phone on 1% battery kept in “low-power mode” all day vs. plugged in and operating normally. Many Christians live on spiritual “battery saver,” then wonder why they have no power.
  - The Practical Landing: Monday morning temptation (anger, lust, discouragement) is not mainly a willpower test; it’s a “connection” test—am I drawing life from Christ now?
  - [DQ]: “What is one ‘battery saver’ habit that looks spiritual but actually avoids living dependence (scrolling devotionals only, rushing, avoiding conviction)?”

#### B. Coming Boldly: Abiding Through Our High Priest (Hebrews 4:14–16; Mark 1:35; Psalm 27:8) (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Hebrews 4:14–16
  - Reading/Context: The command is not “clean yourself up then come,” but “come boldly” because of Christ’s priesthood.
  - [WB]: `Heb 4:16 — Mercy + Grace = Help (NOW)`
  - Sanctuary Connection: This is present truth Bible study—entering by faith into Christ’s _current_ ministry for real-time help against sin.
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What two things do we receive when we come—mercy for the past, grace for the present?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** Mark 1:35
  - Reading/Connection: Jesus sought solitary prayer _when ministry pressure was high_.
  - [WB]: `Pressure ↑ → Prayer ↑ (Mark 1:35)`
  - Faith of Jesus: Christ’s life shows what true faith does under pressure: it runs to the Father, not to self-sufficiency.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “When pressure rises, what rises in you—prayer, planning, irritation, or escape?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Psalm 27:8
  - Reading/Challenge: “Seek ye My face”—this is relational, not transactional.
  - [WB]: `Seek His FACE (presence) > His HAND (stuff)`
  - The Faith Distinction: Seeking is not persuading God; it is responding to His invitation, trusting His welcome in Christ.
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What would change this week if you treated Bible time as ‘seeking His face’ rather than ‘meeting a requirement’?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ If you only talk to your spouse “when you feel like it,” the relationship cools. Feelings often follow faithfulness, not the other way around.
  - [DQ]: “What is one realistic boundary (phone, bedtime, location) that would protect a daily ‘come boldly’ appointment?”

### III. The Word as Sanctuary Food and Lamp: How Righteousness Enters the Character (15-17 mins)

- Introduction to Section: Psalm 119 doesn’t treat God’s law as a ladder to climb but as light and life to walk by. The Word brings us to Christ, and Christ reproduces His obedience in us.
  - [WB]: Three movements: `Light (Lampstand) → Bread (Table) → Law written (Ark)`
  - [DQ]: “Do you experience God’s law mainly as condemnation or as a promise of what Christ will reproduce in you?”

#### A. The Word: Lamp for the Next Step, Not a Floodlight for Control (Psalm 119:105; Psalm 119:97–104; Psalm 119:111–112) (8-9 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Psalm 119:105
  - Reading/Context: A lamp gives enough light for the next step—faith-walking, not anxiety-control.
  - [WB]: Draw a small circle of light on a path: `Lamp → next step`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Many want “control-light”; God often gives “obedience-light.”
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “What does the lamp imagery imply about the pace of growth—instant or step-by-step?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Psalm 119:97–104
  - Reading/Connection: Love for God’s law produces discernment and hatred for the false way.
  - [WB]: `Love the Law → Discernment → Turn from false way`
  - Commandments as Fruit: The law is the transcript of Christ’s character; it becomes beautiful when we see it in Jesus and receive His life.
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What makes a believer actually begin to ‘love’ God’s law—fear, pride, or seeing Christ?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Psalm 119:111–112
  - Reading/Challenge: “Inclined my heart”—this is imparted righteousness: God bends the will by grace.
  - [WB]: `Incline my HEART → not just inform my mind`
  - [EGW]: “The Word of God is the living seed… after the Word has been prayerfully received, it is to be cherished, and practiced… first the blade, then the ear, then the full corn.” (EGW Notes, Sabbath Afternoon).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “What ‘next step’ obedience is God putting in your light right now—apology, restitution, media choices, Sabbath preparation?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ GPS rerouting. When you miss a turn, it doesn’t shame you; it recalculates and guides you back. Psalm 119 ends with ‘I have gone astray… seek Thy servant’ (Ps. 119:176).
  - Monday Morning: When you fail, don’t hide from the Word—run to it as the voice of the Shepherd correcting your path.
  - [DQ]: “When you sin, do you tend to avoid Scripture (shame) or run to it (faith)? What would boldness look like?”

#### B. Eating Christ: Word to Mind, Heart, and Habit (Matthew 4:4; John 6:53–57; Galatians 2:20; Revelation 3:20) (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Matthew 4:4
  - Reading/Context: Jesus defeats temptation by living on the Father’s Word—this is the **faith of Jesus** in action.
  - [WB]: `Temptation → “It is written” → Trust → Obey`
  - [DQ]: _(Observation):_ “In the wilderness, what did Jesus treat as ‘food’—feelings, circumstances, or the Word?”
- **Foundation Text 5:** John 6:53–57
  - Reading/Connection: “Eat… drink” = internalize Christ by faith—His life becomes ours.
  - [WB]: `Eat/Drink = Receive/Assimilate (by faith)`
  - [EGW]: “The words, ‘Give us this day our daily bread,’ refer… to spiritual food… When we believe and receive Christ’s word, we eat His flesh and drink His blood… Christ is formed, the hope of glory.” (OHC 209).
  - [DQ]: _(Reflection):_ “What is the difference between reading about bread and actually eating it spiritually?”
- **Foundation Text 6:** Galatians 2:20
  - Reading/Challenge: Christian living is Christ living in me—this is righteousness by faith made practical.
  - [WB]: `Not I… Christ in me = new motive power`
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Name one repeated sin where ‘trying’ has failed. What would it look like to bring that temptation to Christ and say, ‘Live Your life in me now’?”
- **Foundation Text 7:** Revelation 3:20
  - Reading/Connection: Christ stands at the door; _we open_; _He comes in and sups_—this is daily communion.
  - [WB]: `Open → He enters → Sup (fellowship) → Strength`
  - Practical Mechanics: Choose one passage; pray; write; claim; obey the next step; share—so the Word moves from page → mind → choices.
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Marinating vs. rinsing. A quick rinse leaves the inside unchanged; marinating changes the inside over time. Many “rinse” the Bible; abiding is letting the Word stay.
  - [DQ]: “What would ‘marinating’ look like for you practically—one verse carried all day, Scripture memory, praying the verse back to God?”

---

### IV. The Word That Accomplishes: Isaiah 55 and the End-Time Need (6-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: Bible study is how we **come boldly** to Christ our High Priest; the Word brings **imputed righteousness** (peace with God) and **imparted righteousness** (a changed life), producing the commandments as fruit through the faith of Jesus.
- **Foundation Text 1:** Isaiah 55:1–3
  - Invitation: Come without money—no earning, no bargaining.
  - [WB]: `NO MONEY → grace-only access`
  - [DQ]: “What do we subtly ‘pay with’—promises to do better, spiritual performance, emotional intensity?”
- **Foundation Text 2:** Isaiah 55:6–7
  - Challenge: Forsake wicked ways; return to the Lord who will “abundantly pardon.”
  - [WB]: `Return → Pardon → New way`
  - Practical Clarification: We forsake sin because we believe His pardon and power, not to purchase it.
  - [DQ]: “In your experience, what makes repentance hardest—love of sin, despair, or the belief that God won’t receive you?”
- **Foundation Text 3:** Isaiah 55:10–11
  - Promise: God’s Word accomplishes what He pleases—real transformation, not empty religion.
  - [WB]: `ISA 55:11 — WORD → ACCOMPLISHES`
  - [EGW]: “The Bible should never be studied without prayer… The Holy Spirit alone can… prevent us from wrestling truths difficult of comprehension.” (GC 599-600).
  - [DQ]: _(Application):_ “Where do you need God’s Word to ‘accomplish’ something in you—patience, purity, forgiveness, courage?”
- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Seed vs. stone. A stone you can polish externally; a seed has life that grows from within. God’s Word is seed—transforming from inside out.
  - [DQ]: “Where have you been ‘polishing the stone’ (image-management) instead of letting the seed grow (heart-change)?”
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Bring one specific bondage to Christ’s sanctuary ministry today (Heb. 4:16). Ask, believe, and step forward acting on His promise—not on your feelings.
  - [WB]: `CHRIST IN YOU → HIS OBEDIENCE AS FRUIT`
  - _(Final Question):_ “What is the one thing you will stop trying to fix with willpower, and instead bring to Jesus as High Priest—expecting Him to accomplish it by His Word?”
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - [EGW]: “Times that will try men’s souls are just before us… He whose life is united to Christ by hidden links will be kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation.” (HP 138).
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12 — Commandments of God + Faith of Jesus`

---

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: The Bible is the appointed means by which our High Priest brings us into living union with Himself—His righteousness counted for us and worked out in us—so the law is written in the heart and obedience becomes the fruit of faith.
- The Practical Takeaway: When we **abide**, we stop using the Bible to manage guilt and start receiving **Christ’s life**, and that life shows up in speech, appetite, relationships, purity, Sabbath keeping, and courage.
  - [WB]: `ABIDE (union) → RECEIVE (righteousness) → OBEY (fruit)`
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Not “do more,” but **receive more**—come honestly with your specific sin and weakness; consent to Christ’s verdict; ask for His life; trust His promise; take the next step.
  - [WB]: `“HE IS ABLE” (Heb 7:25)` and `“A NEW HEART” (Eze 36:26-27)`
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope: Christ will finish His work in all who keep coming by faith; the issue in the last days is not access, but dependence.
  - [WB]: `Phil 1:6` and `Rev 14:12`
